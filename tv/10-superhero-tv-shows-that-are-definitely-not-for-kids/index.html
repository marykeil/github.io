10 Superhero TV Shows That Are Definitely Not For Kids

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2400 words 12 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

R-rated superhero movies have paved the way for mature and boundary-pushing TV storytelling in the MCU, DCU, and other brands, allowing for social commentary and exploration of the human experience. Shows like Luke Cage, Gen V, Doom Patrol, Jessica Jones, and Peacemaker deliver mature and gritty narratives, tackling themes such as injustice, trauma, abuse, and generational issues. Harley Quinn, The Punisher, Daredevil, Invincible, and The Boys offer violent and gory content alongside complex characters and social commentary, challenging traditional notions of heroism and exploring dark aspects of society.

Literature Review

Recent years have seen a surge in more mature superhero content that pushes the boundaries to embrace all that is dark, weird, and gory.

As the superhero genre disengages from the traditional clean-cut hero versus villain narrative, it opens the door for more interesting, boundary-pushing stories in the MCU, DCU, and other brands. In film, R-rated superhero movies began a legacy that has been adopted and perfected by the long-form storytelling of television. Thanks to this removal of constraints, the superhero genre has produced some stellar and mature explorations of social commentary and the (super) human experience. Daredevil got the ball rolling as the first truly adult superhero series, providing a breath of fresh air from its toned-down, family-friendly Marvel counterparts. Since then, the genre has exploded, with both live-action and animated projects exploring unique angles of just what it means to live in a world of heroes and villains. Truly, these mature superhero shows are not for kids — but they are exceptional. 10 Luke Cage Provides Vital Social Commentary With Its Depiction Of Violence And Corruption Luke Cage De-Centers Whiteness And Explores Mature Themes

luke cage
TV-MA

Release Date September 30, 2016 Cast Frank Whaley , Theo Rossi , Mahershala Ali , Justin Swain , simone missick , Frankie Faison , Jaiden Kaine , Alfre Woodard , Darius Kaleb , Mike Colter , Kellen C Wingate , erik laray harvey Seasons 2 Streaming Service(s) Netflix , Disney Plus

Luke Cage is for mature audiences not because of violence — although the series has plenty of that — but because of the mature themes it grapples with. The former Carl Lucas was forced to take on the identity of Luke Cage (Mike Colter) after a false imprisonment, during which he was subjected to experimentation that gave him his powers. The injustice against the hero, like the later explored injustice against Isaiah Bradley (Carl Lumbly) in The Falcon and the Winter Soldier, rings too close to real history. The series is a love letter to Harlem and Black culture, and Cage’s often violent war against ruthless crime lord Cottonmouth (Mahershala Ali) is at its core a war for his community’s future. Although it doesn’t get quite as violent as its gritty counterpart, Daredevil, the show is a far cry from family-friendly. Overall, Luke Cage is a strong exploration of injustice, reluctant heroism, and Black identity.

9 Gen V Shows That The Kids Really Aren’t Okay The Series Mixes Competition, Hormones, And Superpowers In The Darkest Way

Gen V
TV-MA

Release Date September 29, 2023 Cast Lizzie Broadway , Jaz Sinclair , Chance Perdomo , Maddie Phillips , London Thor , Derek Luh , Asa Germann , Shelley Conn , Patrick Schwarzenegger Seasons 1 Streaming Service(s) Prime Video

This spin-off of The Boys takes the former’s legacy of gratuitous gore to the next level. Gen V explores young adult “supes” navigating their place in the world and pushing the limits of their morality as they do it. Placing young supes together in a competitive setting is a recipe for some truly violent and shocking moments from the very first episode. While the series does follow The Boys’ critique of society, Gen V lacks the sharp hatred of supes that defines its predecessor. Rather, it is a wholly human show, critiquing not just the darkest parts of humanity but the circumstances that push people to act darkly. Gen V is a shock-filled, deeply violent show that, at its core, is about the choice between self-sacrifice for the greater good and self-preservation at a steep cost.

8 Doom Patrol Uses Its R-Rating Creatively And Absurdly The Wacky Cast-Outs Of Doom Patrol Experience Just How Bizarre A World With Superpowers Can Be

Doom Patrol

Release Date February 15, 2019 Cast Matt Bomer , Joivan Wade , Diane Guerrero , April Bowlby , Alan Tudyk , Brendan Fraser , Timothy Dalton Seasons 4 Streaming Service(s) HBO Max

Doom Patrol is one of the oddest, most eccentric entries into the superhero genre. From carnivorous butts to sex ghosts, the show certainly uses its mature rating more creatively than any other. Exploring a band of misfit not-quite-heroes overcoming the traumas of their pasts, Doom Patrol somehow pulls off explorations of human suffering and healing while still having time for goats that swallow towns and battles in the white spaces of comic books. In fact, the wacky elements of the show go hand-in-hand with its handling of more mature topics. Mental health is at the forefront of this show, from the character Jane’s (Diane Guerrero) Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID) to Robotman’s (Brendan Fraser) depression and rage issues. Doom Patrol’s mature rating has a two-fold purpose; first, providing shock value and pushing boundaries of what a superpowered world truly implies, and second, exploring the intense themes of trauma and healing.

7 Jessica Jones Unflinchingly Confronts Mature Themes Of Abuse The Private Investigator Faces Her Abuser With An Entirely Different Kind Of Strength Than Her Superpower

Marvel’s Jessica Jones

Release Date November 20, 2015 Cast Krysten Ritter , David Tennant , Mike Colter , Rachael Taylor , wil traval , Erin Moriarty , Eka Darville , Carrie-Anne Moss , J.R. Ramirez , Terry Chen , Leah Gibson , Janet McTeer , Benjamin Walker , Sarita Choudhury , Jeremy Bobb Seasons 3 Streaming Service(s) Netflix , Disney+

Jessica Jones deals with some very difficult topics, particularly the aftermath of sexual assault and abuse in season 1. The titular P.I. is a deeply traumatized, reluctant hero with super strength. The show is interesting because it positions Jessica Jones (Krysten Ritter) as a former superhero whose horrific entanglement with her mind-controlling abuser, Kilgrave (David Tennant), led her to give up the heroics.

While the second and third seasons can’t compare to the brilliance of the first one, they offer further development of this complex character on her journey back to heroism. The show treats the audience to a protagonist who is foul-mouthed, cynical, unapproachable, and sometimes cruel. Jessica Jones isn’t always a hero — she’s not even always likable — but therein lies her appeal as a character.

6 Peacemaker Handles Generational Abuse With Violence And Irony This Anti-Hero Is A Misguided, Violent Jerk With Complex Intergenerational Trauma

Peacemaker

Release Date January 13, 2022 Cast Steve Agee , Christopher Heyerdahl , Jennifer Holland , Annie Chang , John Cena , Chukwudi Iwuji , Danielle Brooks , Robert Patrick , Lochlyn Munro , Chris Conrad Seasons 1 Streaming Service(s) HBO Max , Amazon Prime Video

DC’s Peacemaker is definitely a fun show, even as it handles darker themes of parental abuse. John Cena’s vainglorious anti-hero is willing to kill anyone in the name of “peace,” a contradiction that makes him compelling, funny, and counter-intuitively easy to root for. The show follows the character from his previous appearance in The Suicide Squad, as he is enlisted (along with his eagle sidekick, Eagly) to join a new task force to save the world. Peacemaker explores the complex man underneath the arrogant, problematic exterior. The show is certainly violent, given the fact that it’s led by a man who relishes bloodbaths in the morning, but it is also an off-beat, fun project with a lot of laughs. Ultimately, the undercurrent of insecurity and Peacemaker’s relationship with his father are the show’s most gripping storylines.

5 Harley Quinn Is Feminist, Fun, And Full Of Gore The Irreverent Animated Comedy Delivers An Empowered Villain Paving Her Own Way

Harley Quinn
TV-MA

Release Date November 29, 2019 Cast Kaley Cuoco , Lake Bell , Alan Tudyk , Ron Funches , Tony Hale , Jason Alexander Seasons 4 Streaming Service(s) Max

Be warned, despite its TV-14 rating, Harley Quinn is not a kids’ cartoon. The show is led by an unhinged Harley Quinn coming into her own as she seeks to be defined by her own villainy instead of by association with the Joker. The violence expected of a clown villain competing with the Joker is gory and gratuitous — and being animated doesn’t make the crunch of breaking bones or squirts of blood any less sickening. Nonetheless, this gratuitously violent nature of the show, along with its humor, makes it a shining example of superhero content that is both animated and adult. Harley Quinn is bold, feminist, queer, and just a lot of fun overall. In spite of all the violence, there’s something almost feel-good about the show.

4 The Punisher Delivers Violence And Grit In A Vengeful Crusade Frank Castle Is Not A Hero, But A Damaged And Violent Vigilante

The Punisher

Release Date November 17, 2017 Cast Deborah Ann Woll , Jon Bernthal Seasons 2 Streaming Service(s) Netflix

Jon Bernthal plays the tortured anti-hero Frank Castle to brutal perfection in The Punisher. The man who lost his entire family seeks vigilante justice that is more akin to vengeance, as he faces the criminal underworld with lethal conviction. Castle is no hero, just a deeply damaged man with the military skills to make his pain all criminals’ problem. The Punisher provides a deeper look into a haunted protagonist who is both sympathetic and absolutely alienating in his bloody crusade. As seen in the second season, Castle simply cannot distance himself from his role as The Punisher. He is dark yet charismatic, somewhere between a hero and a monster.

3 Daredevil Set A New Standard For Gritty Superhero TV The Gritty Series Launched The Incredibly Violent Kingpin

Daredevil

Release Date April 10, 2015 Cast Charlie Cox , Rosario Dawson , Elden Henson , Vincent D’Onofrio , Deborah Ann Woll , Jon Bernthal Seasons 3 Streaming Service(s) Netflix , Disney+

Amid the announcement of Daredevil: Born Again bringing back Charlie Cox’s beloved character, it can be easy to forget that Daredevil was, in fact, the first TV-MA superhero show made. The superhero leads a tormented double life, keeping up the pretense that he is simply an ordinary blind man. The show delivers some truly memorable violent moments, especially courtesy of Wilson Fisk’s (Vincent D’Onofrio) Kingpin. Daredevil is responsible for a lot more than it is given credit for. From setting the bar for an adult superhero show to spawning The Defenders and each hero’s own show, and now finally to the MCU’s first foray into truly mature storytelling, Daredevil began it all. Now, years later, it’s clear the character and original series are still quite important to the future of the MCU’s street-level stories.

2 Invincible Brings The Notions Of Heroism And Family Into Question The Ruthless Omni-Man Is Not The Hero His Son Believed Him To Be

invincible

Release Date March 26, 2021 Cast Mark Hamill , Gillian Jacobs , Jon Hamm , Mae Whitman , Steven Yeun , Walton Goggins , Ezra Miller , Jonathan Groff , Sandra Oh , Djimon Hounsou , Khary Payton , Seth Rogen , Jeffrey Donovan , Sonequa Martin-Green , Mahershala Ali , Malese Jow , J. K. Simmons , Clancy Brown , Zazie Beetz , Zachary Quinto , Jason Mantzoukas , Nicole Byer Seasons 1 Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

Invincible provides one of the best critical takes on the idea of heroism in superhero media today, with its powerful and disturbing villain, Omni-Man (J.K. Simmons). The animated series definitely does not shy away from blood and gore. Its true devastation, however, lies in the painful realization by protagonist Mark Grayson (Steven Yeun) that his father, Omni-Man, is not the hero he thought he was. Although animated, the gore is hard to stomach, made even worse by the emotional weight of Omni-Man’s actions. The painful culmination of the two in the season 1 finale is unbeatable, as Omni-Man turns his own son into a vehicle for genocide. It is good versus evil, father versus son, and hero versus villain taken to the most bloody extreme.

1 The Boys Conceives Of “Supes” That Are Anything But Heroes The Series Holds A Mirror To Some Of Society’s Darkest Proclivities

The Boys

Release Date July 26, 2019 Cast Elisabeth Shue , Jensen Ackles , Goran Visnjic , Jessie T. Usher , Chace Crawford , Dominique McElligott , Laz Alonso , Nathan Mitchell , Aya Cash , Colby Minifie , Karl Urban , Erin Moriarty , Karen Fukuhara , Jack Quaid , Antony Starr , claudia doumit , Tomer Capon Seasons 4 Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

The Boys is the template for gory deaths, shocking sequences, lustful villains, and plain over-the-top violence. All this violence would seem gratuitous if not for the poignant social commentary the show makes regarding the real world through the medium of superheroes. The Boys themselves are the underdogs facing insurmountable odds against a morally bankrupt society as they themselves contend with their flaws. The show is about more than getting covered in guts, superhero orgies, and climbing out of whales. Superheroes in The Boys are depraved, selfish, ill-adjusted beings whose access to power, fame, and money put them beyond redemption. The fact that the supes are managed by a corporation provides further commentary on capitalism, fame, and corruption through a much more brutal lense than anything traditionally seen in the MCU or DCU.
