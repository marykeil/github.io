10 TV Episodes Perfect To Watch On Valentine s Day

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

TV shows have featured memorable Valentine’s Day episodes, showcasing both romantic and disastrous moments for characters. Favorite shows like The Office, The Simpsons, 30 Rock, Futurama, Parks and Recreation, and Grey’s Anatomy have all tackled Valentine’s Day themes. These Valentine’s Day episodes offer a mix of romance, comedy, and relatable relationship moments, making them a great watch for the romantic period.

Literature Review

February 14th: the Feast of Saint Valentine. Whether Cupid’s arrow has struck or not, some Valentine’s Day-themed TV episodes are a perfect watch.

With Valentine’s Day approaching, there are plenty of brilliant TV episodes to curl up and watch, whether you’re spending the day with someone or not. Over the years, various TV shows have delved into the lives of their characters on the most romantic day of the year. Of course, a day of love and passion can result in a great episode, but for some of these characters, Valentine’s Day can be a disaster. Although Valentine’s Day isn’t to everyone’s tastes, with many finding the holiday to be more of a gimmick than anything else, this isn’t always the case in TV shows. With relationship story arcs being one of the most popular forms of plot in TV, especially will-they/won’t-they TV relationships, there’s always room for romance. Regardless of whether there’s a special someone around or not, these Valentine’s Day episodes are a great watch for the romantic period.

10 The Office: Valentine’s Day Season 2, Episode 16

The Office

Release Date March 24, 2005 Cast Mindy Kaling , Jenna Fischer , Kate Flannery , Ed Helms , Craig Robinson , Paul Lieberstein , Ellie Kemper , B.J. Novak , Angela Kinsey , Oscar Nunez , Rainn Wilson , Brian Baumgartner , Phyllis Smith , Leslie David Baker , Creed Bratton , Steve Carell , John Krasinski Seasons 9

The season 2 Valentine’s Day episode saw some lovely relationship moments for many characters in The Office, but unfortunately, not for all. Throughout the day, Phyllis (Smith) is showered with gifts, but poor Pam (Jenna Fischer), who has to ferry the gifts across the room to her every few minutes, fails to receive anything from Roy (David Denman). This episode is also a staple for Dwight (Rainn Wilson) and Angela’s (Kinsey) relationship, and despite dating in secret, they exchange gifts, where Dwight makes a statement by giving Angela a spare key to his home. Despite all the romance, this episode was still filled with plenty of laughs, especially with some of Michael’s (Steve Carell) more cringeworthy moments, as he believes he and Jan (Melora Hardin) are dating. Unfortunately, Michael doesn’t have the love-filled day of adventure with his boss like he hoped for, but he does get a kiss from her before departing. While not everyone had the greatest Valentine’s Day in Scranton, this wholesome episode was still plenty hilarious.

9 The Simpsons: I Love Lisa Season 4, Episode 15

The Simpsons

Release Date December 17, 1989 Cast Tress MacNeille , Julie Kavner , Harry Shearer , Pamela Hayden , Nancy Cartwright , Hank Azaria , Dan Castellaneta , Yeardley Smith Seasons 35

This episode is the source of The Simpsons’ famous “I choo-choo-choose you” reference

One of the best episodes of The Simpsons saw Lisa (Yeardley Smith) become the subject of Ralph’s (Nancy Cartwright) affections. This episode is the source of The Simpsons’ famous “I choo-choo-choose you” reference, as Ralph misinterprets Lisa’s pity as a crush. While Lisa tries to be as sensitive to Ralph’s feelings as possible, it goes badly when she grows frustrated with him and announces that she only gave him a Valentine’s Day card because she felt sorry for him. It’s hard not to feel sorry for Lisa, who is simply trying to be a good and approachable friend to Ralph, but her wording happens to be unfortunate. Luckily, the two make up by the end of the episode, with Lisa offering him another card that says “Let’s ‘Bee’ Friends,” in a rather sweet and wholesome moment between the two. While it may still be early for Lisa to find a significant other, considering the character has been the age of 8 for over 30 years now, her Valentine’s Day with Ralph was certainly good practice.

8 30 Rock: Anna Howard Shaw Day Season 4, Episode 3

30 Rock saw Liz Lemon (Tina Fey) take a different approach to Valentine’s Day. After years of failed attempts at romance, Liz decides to book her root canal on Valentine’s Day but fails to take into account that due to the anesthetic, she’ll need to be driven home, making her realize just how alone she truly is. Persevering anyway, and while heavily drugged up, Liz sees three of her major ex-boyfriends: Drew (Jon Hamm), Floyd (Jason Sudeikis), and Dennis (Dean Winters). While touched by their sentiment, Liz is unaware that their presence is actually her hallucination. The relationship between Liz and Jack in 30 Rock proves that platonic love is just as important as romantic.

Despite not having a partner, Liz does have someone to rely on, and Jack (Alec Baldwin) ends up collecting her. The relationship between Liz and Jack in 30 Rock proves that platonic love is just as important as romantic, especially on a day dedicated to such. Although Liz’s final relationship with Criss (James Marsden) was still a way off in this episode, it did see the first mention of Liz’s “future husband,” Wesley (Michael Sheen), and Jack’s support proved she wasn’t as alone as she believed.

7 Futurama: Put Your Head On My Shoulders Season 2, Episode 10

Futurama

Release Date March 28, 1999 Cast Katey Sagal , Billy West , Lauren Tom , Maurice LaMarche , David Herman , Tress MacNeille , John DiMaggio , Phil LaMarr Seasons 11

Although Fry (Billy West) and Leela (Katey Sagal) were clearly relationship goals in Futurama, the show’s best Valentine’s Day episode centers around the beginning of Fry’s relationship with Amy (Lauren Tom) instead. After a road trip to Mercury, the two have sex and return as a couple, but Fry panics that the relationship is going too fast. After crashing on the way for another date, Fry’s head ends up being fused to Amy’s shoulder in order to stay alive. Fry breaks things off when they return to Earth, and in retaliation, Amy makes Valentine’s Day plans with someone else. Considering Fry’s abrupt break-up, it seems more than fair that he would then have to be subjected to watching his ex on a date with another person. After his head is reattached to his own body at the episode, Fry experiences nerve damage that causes his left leg to jolt when he touches his neck, which seems like an appropriate punishment. Although no significant damage was made to Fry and Leela’s eventual relationship with the short-lived romance, the experience did teach Fry a lesson about dating.

6 Parks And Recreation: Galentine’s Day Season 2, Episode 16

Parks and Recreation

Release Date April 9, 2009 Cast Amy Poehler , Nick Offerman , ​Chris Pratt2 , Aubrey Plaza , Adam Scott , Rashida Jones , Retta , Jim O’Heir , Aziz Ansari , Rob Lowe Seasons 7

The Parks and Recreation episode “Galentine’s Day” is the perfect example of why Leslie Knope (Amy Poehler) is the ultimate friend. Despite dating Justin (Theroux), Leslie still makes sure to organize an all-female event for her friends, showering each of them with personalized gifts. Gathering at brunch with close friends, Leslie listens to the tale of her mother, Marlene (Pamela Reed), and a lifeguard she fell in love with in 1968. In typical Leslie style, she organizes for the two to reunite, and although her relationship with Justin falls apart, Leslie proves that everyone deserves a good Valentine’s Day.

5 Supernatural: My Bloody Valentine Season 5, Episode 14

Supernatural

Release Date September 13, 2005 Cast Jensen Ackles , Misha Collins , Jim Beaver , Jared Padalecki , Katie Cassidy , Lauren Cohan , Mark A. Sheppard , Mark Pellegrino , Alexander Calvert Seasons 15

The Supernatural episode “My Bloody Valentine” starts off with a ridiculously gorey scene, as the newly formed couple Russell (Andrew Halliwell) and Alice (Aili Storen) tear chunks out of each other, literally. Influenced by the Horseman Famine, Alice ends their date with a kiss, but quickly bites into Russell’s neck, leaving her roommate to contact Sam Winchester (Jared Padalecki) to investigate. While this image isn’t the most romantic, with the two dying at the hands of the Famine (James Otis), this episode delves into Cupid’s markings, explaining that the pair were destined for a union together.

4 Gravity Falls: The Love God Season 2, Episode 9

Gravity Falls

Release Date June 15, 2012 Cast Jason Ritter , Kristen Schaal Seasons 2

Mabel’s (Kristen Schaal) decision to steal a love potion from a real love god quickly goes awry in Gravity Falls, despite her commitment to take her matchmaking abilities to higher places. Trying to support Robbie (T.J. Miller) through his breakup with Wendy (Linda Cardellini), Mabel tries to set him up with someone new, but her commitment to help him causes chaos. Adding the stolen potion to the food Robbie and his date share, the rest of the group are vocal about their disappointment in her. Despite her meddling, this episode proves that Mabel’s true priority is caring for her friends’ best interests.

In a panic, Mabel tries to reverse her actions. Despite getting an anti-love potion to release Robbie from his new relationship with Tambry (Jessica DiCicco), she ultimately decides not to use it when she sees how happy he is. Despite her meddling, this episode proves that Mabel’s true priority is caring for her friends’ best interests, even if it means upsetting others in the process.

3 Grey’s Anatomy: All You Need Is Love Season 8, Episode 14

Grey’s Anatomy
Romance Drama

Release Date March 27, 2005 Seasons 19

The Grey’s Anatomy episode “All You Need Is Love” is a great slice-of-life story and sees Meredith (Ellen Pompeo) and Derek (Patrick Dempsey) try to enjoy a night to themselves without Zola. The pair struggle to find an opportunity to have sex as relatively new parents, with the show demonstrating a great example of real-life couples. While the staff of Grey Sloan are loved up, the patients in the ER are, too, with one young girl checking in with an allergic reaction to the chocolate from her Valentine. Despite being uncomfortable, Clementine (Shelby Zemanek) has a very adorable moment in which her Valentine, Nico (Aidan Andrews) leaves her a heartfelt note about his upset that she’s not with him. Like many a Grey’s Anatomy episode, there are plenty of medical dramatics. But Meredith and Derek ultimately get their time together at the end of the episode, resulting in a rather sweet moment in their relationship.

2 The O.C.: The Heartbreak Season 1, Episode 19

The O.C.

Release Date August 5, 2003 Cast Mischa Barton , Ben McKenzie , Peter Gallagher , Melinda Clarke Seasons 4

The O.C. saw a huge moment for Seth (Adam Brody) and Summer (Rachel Bilson), as the two lost their virginities to one another on Valentine’s Day. However, that’s not the only romance happening at Newport Beach, as Marissa (Mischa Barton) starts to dwell on whether her split from Ryan (Ben McKenzie) was the right choice or not. While there was plenty of anticipated drama in this episode, it’s Kirsten (Kelly Rowan) that makes it so enjoyable, especially because of her excitement over Valentine’s Day, her favorite holiday.

1 And Just Like That…: February 14th Season 2, Episode 16

And Just Like That…

Release Date December 9, 2021 Cast Sarah Jessica Parker , Cynthia Nixon , Kristin Davis , Mario Cantone , David Eigenberg , Willie Garson , Evan Handler , Sara Ramirez , Chris Noth , Sarita Choudhury Seasons 2

And Just Like That… saw an episode dedicated to Valentine’s Day, which was an interesting production choice, considering the franchise dedicates a lot of time toward its various relationships. However, this episode is a great example of the differences in all of the characters and how they approach romance. While Carrie (Sarah Jessica Parker) and Aidan (John Corbett) nervously reunite, Nya (Karen Pittman) is more than happy staying home alone and eating a chocolate souffle. Although “February 14th” shows that not everyone’s Valentine’s Day goes to plan, it does confirm that there is no right way to enjoy the day.
