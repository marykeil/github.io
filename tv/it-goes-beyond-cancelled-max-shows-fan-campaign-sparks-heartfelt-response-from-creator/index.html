It Goes Beyond” Cancelled Max Shows Fan Campaign Sparks Heartfelt Response From Creator

SUMMARY

Fans campaign to save the canceled Max comedy Our Flag Means Death with a Times Square billboard. The creator, David Jenkins, expresses gratitude and hope for a third season. HBO and Max chief executive Casey Bloys cites low numbers as the reason for canceling Our Flag Means Death, but expresses support for the show finding a new home if possible. Fans of other canceled shows have purchased billboards to save them as well, making Our Flag Means Death the latest in this trend.

Literature Review

The canceled Max comedy Our Flag Means Death gets a Times Square billboard as fans mobilize to save the show in a similar tactic used by other titles.

The canceled Max comedy Our Flag Means Death gets a Times Square billboard as fans mobilize to save the show. Debuting in March 2022 from creator David Jenkins, Our Flag Means Death is set in the early 18th century. The story focuses on the Golden Age of Piracy. In particular, it examines Stede (Rhys Darby) and his crew as they try to make a name for themselves as pirates, and then cross paths with Taika Waititi’s infamous Blackbeard. After debuting its second season late last year, Max announced that Our Flag Means Death would not be back for season 3.

Jenkins posted on Instagram to highlight the campaign efforts to revive Our Flag Means Death season 3, which mirror efforts to save Netflix shows like Warrior Nun.

Jenkins gives a particular shoutout to a billboard in Times Square, which highlights the push to save the Max comedy. He poses in front of it, writing, “This truly is the best fandom. I’m speechless. It’s totally humbling and just so damn sweet.”

The series creator also keeps hope alive, mentioning in the second post’s caption, “We’re all ready and standing by to make a third season,” tagging Netflix, Apple TV+, and Amazon MGM Studios.

Why Was Our Flag Means Death Cancelled (& Could It Return?)

While responding to HBO’s success at the recent 75th Emmy Awards, HBO and Max chief executive Casey Bloys was asked by The Hollywood Reporter about the decision to cancel Our Flag Means Death and the fact that an online petition to save the show has received nearly 75,000 signatures. Bloys responded that it’s not an easy call to cancel a show but mentioned that “the numbers weren’t there.” Given the fact that Max does not publicly disclose its viewership data, it’s unknown exactly how OMFD performed and how it compares to other Max Originals.

There was a note of optimism about the series, which also starred Ewen Bremner, Joel Fry, Samson Kayo, Nathan Foad, Vico Ortiz, Matthew Maher, Kristian Nairn, Con O’Neill, Guz Khan, David Fane, and Samba Schutte. Bloys mentioned that if Our Flag Means Death did find a new home, Max would be supportive of the move and do its part to make it happen. As of the time of this writing, though, there has been no indication that another streamer is interested in the show.

Max’s slate of original comedies has shrunk significantly since just the start of 2024. The streamer has canceled The Flight Attendant, Julia, and Rap Sh!t, along with Our Flag Means Death, bringing its number of live-action comedy originals to just three: The Sex Lives of College Girls, the Sex and the City sequel series And Just Like That… and Hacks. Nevertheless, those who enjoyed the series are doing their part to bring it back, and history has shown they might have a good chance of succeeding.
