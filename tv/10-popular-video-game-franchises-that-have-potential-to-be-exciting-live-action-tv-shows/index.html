10 Popular Video Game Franchises That Have Potential To Be Exciting Live-Action TV Shows

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Video game franchises are finding better success in live-action TV adaptations rather than movies, as seen with The Last of Us and Fallout. TV’s episodic format allows for better adaptation of long-running video games, such as Psychonauts and Animal Crossing, than condensing them into movies. Series like Kingdom Hearts and Cyberpunk 2077 would benefit from live-action TV adaptations, addressing flaws in storytelling and exploring complex themes.

Literature Review

From new hits to timeless classics, some of the most popular video games of their era would make for great live-action TV shows if done correctly.

Some famous video games would make for incredible live-action TV shows. The success of shows like HBO’s The Last of Us and the anticipation regarding titles like Amazon Studios’s Fallout are proving that many video game franchises can be adapted into live-action TV series. For years, gamers have clamored for Hollywood to properly adapt some of the medium’s most popular titles, yet time and again, such as live-action Super Mario Bros., Street Fighter, and Doom have resulted in awful films. Nevertheless, the shift from movie to TV adaptations of games has resulted in better projects making it to viewers’ screens, thus making a strong argument that perhaps the serialized or even episodic format is a much better fit for live-action video game adaptations than movies. TV’s structure of stretching stories as far as possible lends itself better to adapting long-running video games than condensing multiple titles into single movies or even trilogies. With The Last of Us’ 2023 Emmys win, and more TV adaptations coming, there are plenty of video games that would also benefit from live-action TV adaptations.

10 Psychonauts A Psychonauts TV series could teach science to children in unique ways.

Psychonauts is a 2005 video game that follows Razputin Aquato, a young psychic with incredible powers based on telepathy, telekinesis, etc., and his quest to liberate the minds of young children whose brains are being stolen at a camp for gifted children. Its engaging gameplay and complex puzzles, unique art direction, and well-written characters and story could work well in a TV format as a show could inspire children to develop a vested interest in science while also delivering compelling drama for older audiences. Furthermore, the success of a Psychonauts show could lead to an adaptation of Psychonauts 2.

9 Animal Crossing Animal Crossing in a live-action format could show children how to prepare for adulthood.

Animal Crossing sees the player take on the role of an unnamed human protagonist who finds themselves living among anthropomorphic beings and completing tasks such as buying a house, facilitating a garden, and performing various tasks to pay off the mortgage on said house. A live-action Animal Crossing TV show geared toward child audiences could effectively teach them important things like financial responsibility, the value of developing a strong work ethic, and learning how to coexist with people (or in the game’s case, animals) from different backgrounds in an educational yet fun and engaging way.

8 The Horizon series An adaptation of Horizon could present audiences with a compelling female lead.

Released in 2017, Horizon Zero Dawn follows a brave warrior named Aloy who embarks on a quest to uncover the mystery surrounding her origins in a post-apocalyptic United States while combating incredible machines based on prehistoric creatures along the way. Horizon Zero Dawn is acclaimed by critics and audiences for its unique, Role-Playing Game (RPG) approach to the open-world genre, and for its enthralling story that’s steeped in sincere messaging about technology’s growing influence on humanity. Fortunately, a Horizon TV show from Amazon Prime is currently in the works.

7 Kingdom Hearts A proper Kingdom Hearts TV series could partially rectify Disney’s lackluster live-action movies.

While there would no doubt be plenty of licensing issues that Disney would have to approve before a Kingdom Hearts TV series ever came to fruition, its creative reworking of many classic Disney characters, locations, and tales combined with the Japanese sensibilities of the Final Fantasy franchise would make for a truly original show. Furthermore, with Disney’s poorly received live-action movies in recent years, a Kingdom Hearts show could be exactly what the company needs to revitalize interest in Disney’s live-action remake/reboot initiative. Despite the overall positive reception of the Kingdom Hearts franchise and its global influence on the gaming industry, Kingdom Hearts’s narrative structure and overall storytelling have consistently been weak points for the beloved series. While Kingdom Hearts’s visuals are distinct and its gameplay addicting, the story is incredibly convoluted and the plotting nearly incomprehensible due to the sheer number of retcons, prequels, and remakes that Square Enix has released throughout the decades. A Kingdom Hearts show also stands to smooth out the series’ complicated story while also bringing in newer audiences.

6 Cyberpunk 2077 A Cyberpunk 2077 live-action series could work after the animated one.

Inspired by the aesthetics and philosophical musings prominently displayed in the Bladerunner series, Cyberpunk 2077 sees a mercenary named V navigate the treachery on full display in Night City in a story rife with complex messaging about technology, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and the growing concerns regarding AI’s influence on humanity. Although Cyberpunk: Edgerunners received the 2023 Anime of the Year award from Crunchyroll, there’s still potential for a live-action adaptation of Cyberpunk that can push the series’ themes even further, complete with cameo appearances from Keanu Reeves and Idris Elba.

5 Half-Life Half-Life could continue The Last of Us’ trend of presenting challenging themes to mature audiences.

The critically acclaimed 1998 science fiction first-person shooter (FPS) Half-Life centers around Dr. Gordon Freeman’s attempts at escaping a research facility after experiments regarding a teleportation device go disastrously awry. Renowned for its compelling story and characters, engaging FPS gameplay, and exploration of thought-provoking themes regarding the ethics of science’s impact on humanity when left unchecked, Half-Life’s attention to detail and incredible worldbuilding lends itself to a televised format. Furthermore, if adapted properly, a Half-Life TV series could serve as a satisfying conclusion to the beloved series, since there has yet to be a conclusive ending to the franchise.

4 Devil May Cry A Devil May Cry live-action series could improve the acclaimed franchise’s flat storytelling.

Capcom’s critically acclaimed fantasy action-adventure series, Devil May Cry, is recognized globally for its captivating gameplay, well-written characters, hard-hitting soundtrack, and intense visuals that greatly enhance gamers’ engagement with the demon-slaying fare on full display throughout each installment. Furthermore, the series’ protagonist, Dante, is among one of the most charismatic in gaming history, thus validating a live-action depiction of the demon slayer. Although a Devil May Cry anime is slated for a 2024 release, TV shows like Supernatural and Preacher have perfectly demonstrated a strong market for live-action shows centered around the occult.

With five entries in the series, a live-action TV adaptation of Devil May Cry already has plenty of material to work with. However, the most important factor that justifies a proper adaptation of the beloved franchise is that a Devil May Cry TV series can improve the source material’s writing. While the writing in the games is good enough, it’s decidedly the weakest aspect of each entry, sliding between generic action movie quality stakes at best, and bland, almost uninspired generic drama at worst. A proper adaptation can explore the characters and lore deeper than the games have time for.

3 Final Fantasy An anthology series covering various Final Fantasy games would be great.

The Final Fantasy franchise is beloved globally for its unique approach to incorporating Japanese sensibilities with world mythologies, and its distinct blend of engaging RPG gameplay with storylines perfectly appropriate for daytime TV melodramas. Furthermore, the Final Fantasy brand is so influential, that virtually every entry in the series has seen a reworking of its original game and/or characters in some respect, with the intersection between the iconic franchise and Kingdom Hearts being one of the most notable examples. Rife with games spanning decades, a Final Fantasy TV adaptation has the potential to redefine some of the game’s best moments. One of the best ways a live-action adaptation of Final Fantasy can accomplish this is by incorporating the Dissidia franchise’s approach to the series for a TV format. In the Dissidia games, characters, locations, weapons, summons, etc. coexist in each other’s games, thus resulting in some unique interactions between protagonists and antagonists. While the critically panned Final Fantasy: The Spirits Within and the mixed bag that is Final Fantasy: Advent Children have already taken stabs at developing the franchise for film audiences, a Final Fantasy TV show would be better suited to handling the vast source material.

2 The Elder Scrolls TV provides a much better chance to adapt The Elder Scrolls franchise.

While The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim is arguably the most popular entry in the Elder Scrolls series, the franchise has plenty of material that a live-action TV adaptation could cover. With the upcoming Fallout TV series on the way - an adaptation of another Bethesda Software property, audiences familiar with both video game franchises will have an opportunity to gauge the likeliness of an Elder Scrolls TV series based on its reception. Moreover, among many things, The Elder Scrolls franchise is respected globally for its dense worldbuilding, strong writing, and inspiring score that effectively places gamers into the series’ expansive worlds. From The Elder Scrolls: Arena to The Elder Scrolls Online and Legends, there’s decidedly far too much content from the source material that a movie or even a series of movies wouldn’t be capable of adapting properly. Furthermore, the open-world nature of the games and the ability for players to create their own characters would make it far too difficult for a movie to properly adapt so many distinct personalities and locations. An Elder Scrolls TV show works best because an original story can be crafted featuring different aspects of each game in the franchise per season.

1 Grand Theft Auto Grand Theft Auto’s massive scope and influence lends itself to a TV format.

Arguably the most popular video game franchise ever made, Grand Theft Auto (GTA) features games that allow players to commit acts of crime against civilians while also taking on missions that see each entry’s protagonist(s) engage with government conspiracies, gang wars, and even alien invasions. While a GTA show could essentially be a culmination of every crime drama ever written, it could distinguish itself by showing characters carjacking someone one moment before purchasing a tank and destroying a city the next. Due to GTA’s free-flow nature, a GTA TV show has the potential to reinvent the crime drama genre
