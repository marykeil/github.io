8 Denzel Washington Movies That Should ve Started A Franchise

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Denzel Washington’s movies have often had the potential for sequels, but he has focused primarily on standalone films throughout his career. Several of his action thrillers, such as Inside Man and The Bone Collector, could have easily become successful franchises, but attempts to make sequels have not come to fruition. While some of his movies, like The Little Things and Devil in a Blue Dress, hint at the possibility of sequels, they have never been developed into long-running franchises.

Literature Review

Denzel Washington has starred in some of the most entertaining crime thrillers of the last 30 years, but he rarely turns these movies into franchises.

Denzel Washington has been one of the most consistently entertaining actors of the last 30 years, but The Equalizer is his only movie franchise. Although he has starred in plenty of movies that could easily have spawned sequels, Denzel Washington hasn’t appeared in many franchises, preferring instead to focus on standalone movies. His upcoming role in Gladiator 2 will mark the first time he has ever joined another franchise after the first movie, but it’s unlikely he will make a habit of this based on his career history until this point. Washington’s movies are largely split between propulsive detective thrillers and more prestigious character-driven works. It wouldn’t make sense for many of his best films to become franchises, since they tell powerful self-contained stories, but some of his action thrillers seem perfect for sequels and spinoffs. There have been a few attempts over the years to get more sequels off the ground, but none of them have come to fruition. Even though many Denzel Washington movies end with the promise of more to come, they have never turned into long-running franchises.

Every Denzel Washington Movie, Ranked Worst To Best
With a career spanning over four decades, Denzel Washington has long become a household name, and here are all of his movies, ranked.

8 Inside Man (2006) Spike Lee tried to make a sequel to his heist thriller for years

Release Date March 24, 2006 Director Spike Lee Cast Willem Dafoe , Denzel Washington , Christopher Plummer , Clive Owen , Chiwetel Ejiofor , Jodie Foster

One of Denzel Washington’s movies with Spike Lee, Inside Man is a twisty, intelligent heist thriller set in New York. Denzel plays a detective who matches wits with a cunning thief, played by Clive Owen, with a bank full of hostages. Inside Man is full of interesting plot twists, and introducing Jodie Foster late in the game changes the direction of the entire story. Inside Man looked set for a sequel originally, but Spike Lee couldn’t secure funding. Owen’s character is a fascinating villain, and his dynamic with Washington could have easily generated another high-stakes thriller. A direct-to-video sequel, Inside Man: Most Wanted, was released in 2019. However, without Spike Lee or any of the film’s original actors, it is a sequel in name only.

7 The Little Things (2021) The central mystery in The Little Things is never definitively solved

Release Date January 29, 2021 Director John Lee Hancock Cast Sofia Vassilieva , Isabel Arraiza , Stephanie Erb , Denzel Washington , Kerry O’Malley , Rami Malek , Jason James Richter , Terry Kinney , Adam J. Harrington , Jared Leto , Tom Hughes , Chris Bauer , Natalie Morales , Michael Hyatt

Thanks to a chilling performance from Jared Leto, The Little Things provides a refreshing twist on Denzel Washington’s usual detective dramas. Washington plays Joe Deacon, a cop who must confront his failures when a serial killer from his past reemerges. Deacon teams up with a rookie, played by Rami Malek, and the two zero in an unsettling loner, Alfred Sparma. Deacon is an immoral cop who doesn’t hesitate to cross legal boundaries when he thinks he is in the right. The Little Things never confirms the true killer, leaving room for a potential sequel where the murders continue.

6 Devil In A Blue Dress (1995) The period detective drama teases a sequel right at the end

Devil in a Blue Dress is a stylish neo-noir thriller set in Los Angeles shortly after World War II. Washington and Don Cheadle play two old friends searching for a missing woman, although they find themselves constantly under threat from the police, racist gangs, and the mob. The ending of Devil in a Blue Dress seems like the perfect way to set up a franchise, with Washington’s character suggesting that he will set up a private detective business. Washington and Cheadle could have been an excellent duo solving more cases in the atmospheric post-war haze of L.A.

5 The Pelican Brief (1993) One of Washington’s finest performances could easily have had a sequel

While the plot is a compelling thrill ride, the chemistry between Washington and Roberts is just as important to the film’s success.

Based on a novel by John Grisham, The Pelican Brief pairs Denzel Washington with Julia Roberts in a propulsive race to uncover the corporate greed behind a string of high-profile assassinations. While the plot is a compelling thrill ride, the chemistry between Washington and Roberts is just as important to the film’s success. The Pelican Brief cuts out the romance that is in Grisham’s novel, but there’s still an underlying compatibility between the two leads, meaning that a sequel could further explore the nature of their relationship as they tackle another political conspiracy.

15 Underrated Denzel Washington Performances
Oscar winner Denzel Washington’s mainstream work is prolific. However, he has plenty of underrated roles under his belt many aren’t familiar with.

4 Training Day (2001) Reports of a prequel persist over 20 years later

Release Date October 5, 2001 Director Antoine Fuqua Cast Eva Mendes , Ethan Hawke , Denzel Washington , Scott Glenn , Cliff Curtis

Training Day showcases Denzel Washington’s ability to inject action thrillers with brilliant touches of humor. He stars alongside Ethan Hawke as a pair of narcotics officers in Los Angeles over the course of a single day. Denzel Washington’s performance as Alonzo Harris earned him the Academy Award for Best Actor, and his dynamic with Hawke makes the movie tick. Although a sequel would be impossible, there have been reports of a Training Day prequel movie for years, which could finally launch a franchise that could have begun over 20 years ago. 3 The Bone Collector (1999) Denzel Washington and Angelina Jolie’s mystery thriller is based on a novel with plenty of sequels

Release Date November 5, 1999 Director Phillip Noyce Cast Denzel Washington , Angelina Jolie , Queen Latifah , Leland Orser , Michael Rooker

Washington and Jolie uncovering a string of serial killers could have made for a fantastic mystery franchise.

Angelina Jolie takes on most of the action duties in The Bone Collector, with Denzel Washington confined to a bed as the quadriplegic criminologist Lincoln Rhyme. Washington and Jolie make a great team as they try to follow a perplexing trail of clues left by a sadistic serial killer. The Bone Collector is based on a novel by Jeffrey Deaver, and there are many more books featuring the same characters. Unfortunately, only the first one was adapted into a movie. Washington and Jolie uncovering a string of serial killers could have made for a fantastic mystery franchise.

2 Déjà Vu (2006) A sci-fi premise with much more room to explore

Denzel Washington doesn’t appear in many sci-fi movies, but Déjà Vu adds time travel to his more familiar role as an all-action detective. After a terrorist attack on a boat kills hundreds of people, Doug Carlin is enlisted by the FBI to join a top-secret government program with access to technology that can look back into the past. A franchise based on Doug Carlin and his team attempting to prevent further attacks after they had already happened could have been interesting, as it could have delved into time travel paradoxes and the ethics of altering the past.

1 Fallen (1998) The paranormal crime thriller could have been just the first chapter

Like Déjà Vu, Fallen adds a strange twist on a detective drama. Denzel Washington plays a homicide detective in Philadelphia who believes he is chasing a copycat killer, but his investigation leads him to suspect that a demon is possessing multiple people to carry out evil deeds. Fallen sometimes struggles to reconcile its two disparate genres, but it’s a unique premise that deserves further exploration. As always, Washington is a driving force behind the parts of the movie which work best. The ending suggests that the demon escapes unharmed, potentially setting up a paranormal mystery franchise.
