Curb Your Enthusiasm s Season 12 Premiere Forgets A Key Element Of The Show s 24-Year Success

Contents

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere starts hilariously with great gags and a surprising twist ending. The episode lacks the classic Curb storytelling trick of dovetailing storylines and ironic payoffs. Despite the missing storytelling element, the season 12 premiere is still a success, remaining funny and watchable in its 12th season.

Literature Review

Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere has gotten the final season off to a great start, but it’s missing a key ingredient from earlier seasons.

Warning: This article contains spoilers for Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 1.

Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere has gotten the final season off to a hilarious start with some great gags and a surprising twist ending, but it’s missing the secret ingredient that made the show so successful in its earlier seasons. In Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 1, “Atlanta,” Larry goes to Georgia for a paid appearance at a birthday party and ends up in trouble with the law. It’s a really funny episode, with some classic Curb moments, but something is missing.

From Irma singing the J.G. Wentworth jingle to the no-win situation of being served by a waiter who’s grieving the recent loss of his mother, Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere has plenty of laughs. The whole thing builds to the hysterical image of Larry’s parody mugshot over the end credits. But the episode’s storytelling, outlined by Larry David and Jeff Schaffer, is lacking one crucial element that made the series so iconic and acclaimed in previous years.

Curb Your Enthusiasm Season 12 Episode 1 Is Missing The Classic Curb Dovetailing

The first episode of Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12 has plenty of funny situations, but it doesn’t dovetail its storylines or pay off its setups like classic Curb. Scenes like Leon eating the birthday cake and the Brookie debate and the awkward silent walk to the hotel restaurant are certainly amusing, but they don’t lead anywhere, and the storylines aren’t intertwined. In its heyday, Curb Your Enthusiasm – and David’s previous show, Seinfeld – would set up an A-plot, a B-plot, and sometimes a C-plot, and then masterfully weave those storylines together for hilariously ironic payoffs.

Season 2, episode 7, “The Doll,” is a prime example. Larry is constantly drinking bottled water on his doctor’s orders and sells a sitcom pitch to ABC. At the president of ABC’s house, he jeopardizes the deal when he meets the president’s daughter and cuts her priceless doll’s hair. During this commotion, he steps away from the bathroom door he’s watching for Cheryl and someone walks in on her.

At the end of the episode, after Larry has replaced the doll, he has Cheryl watching the door while he uses the bathroom. Cheryl abandons her post out of revenge, allowing the ABC president’s daughter to find Larry in the bathroom. She thanks him for replacing her doll, goes to hug him, and feels “something hard in his pants” (his water bottle), forcing Larry to flee the scene. This is a perfect case of the dovetailing storylines and ironic payoffs missing from Curb’s season 12 premiere.

Curb Your Enthusiasm’s Season 12 Premiere Still Succeeds Without The Show’s Storytelling Trick

Despite not dovetailing the storylines or paying off every single setup, Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere is still a resounding success. Storytelling tricks can be a lot of fun, but above all, the most important thing is to be funny, and Curb’s season 12 premiere definitely succeeds at that. It’s rare that a sitcom is even still watchable in its 12th season, so Curb Your Enthusiasm deserves praise for still being as great as it is, even if its episodes aren’t quite as masterfully crafted as they were in the show’s groundbreaking early days.
