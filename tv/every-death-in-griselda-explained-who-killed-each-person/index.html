Every Death In Griselda Explained (& Who Killed Each Person)

2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Griselda Blanco was a notorious drug queenpin responsible for a staggering amount of bloodshed, including the deaths of enemies, allies, and even her own children. The Netflix series “Griselda” aims to authentically portray the story of Griselda Blanco, drawing from various accounts and witnesses of her brutal career and crimes. While some deaths in the series are based on real events and individuals, others are fictionalized for dramatic purposes, but they still represent the type of violent crimes that Griselda was known for.

Literature Review

Griselda Blanco climbed to the top of the Miami drug world through murder and brutality, and this is reflected in each episode of the Netflix series.

Netflix’s Griselda tells the story of the infamous Griselda Blanco, and nearly every episode features a death caused by the notoriously brutal queenpin. The miniseries picks up with Griselda’s move from Colombia to Miami, where she became a prominent figure in the drug wars of the 1970s and 1980s. Of course, to stand out in this male-dominated world, the “Godmother” had to prove herself to be a vicious and blood-thirty adversary. The result of this was an astounding amount of bloodshed and the deaths of Griselda’s enemies, allies, and even her own children. Sofía Vergara’s character in Griselda, the titular Griselda Blanco, is believed to have been just as dangerous in real life as she was depicted in the Netflix series. Showrunners Eric Newman and Andrés Baiz aimed to bring the queenpin’s story to the screen as authentically as possible, sifting through various accounts of Griselda’s career and crimes over the years from those who witnessed the brutal details. This means that episode by episode, Griselda sees the deaths of characters who are intended to represent the real-world victims of the woman’s career—with a few differences.

12 Alberto Bravo Is Shot & Killed By Griselda Episode 1, “Lady Comes to Town”

The first episode of Griselda reveals that the Godmother had murdered her own husband before fleeing Colombia. In the Netflix series version of events, Alberto had forced Griselda to sleep with his brother, Fernando, to wipe away his debts. When he accused his wife of enjoying it, a fight broke out between the pair that ended with Griselda shooting Alberto down. Though the reason that Griselda killed Alberto isn’t known, the queenpin indeed murdered her husband in Colombia.

11 Eddie “The Bird” Rancon & His Men Are Killed By Amilcar’s Men Episode 1, “Lady Come to Town”

Perhaps the most minor death of Griselda was Eddie “The Bird” Rancon in episode 1. The man was a rival of Amilcar and, therefore, one of the few people who would buy Griselda’s cocaine after the drug lord had her blacklisted. Just after Griselda was handed her sizeable envelope of cash, Amilcar’s men entered the club and shot down Eddie and his men. This drug lord seems to be one of the few Griselda characters with seemingly no ties to any real-world figures in Griselda’s life.

10 Fernando Bravo Is Shot & Killed By Dario Episode 2, “Rich White People”

In Griselda episode 2, “Rich White People,” Alberto’s brother, Fernando, manages to hunt Griselda down at her hotel in Miami. There, he attempted to rape and murder his sister-in-law to seek revenge for Alberto’s death. However, when Fernando ordered Dario Sepulveda to kill Griselda’s son, the assassin turned his gun on his boss instead. Though it is rumored that Dario met Griselda when he was hired to kill her, there is no available information about who had ordered this hit. So, it’s unclear if any of this ever resulted in the death of a man named Fernando Bravo.

9 A Murder Witness & His Wife Are Killed By Dario & Chucho Episode 3, “Mutiny”

After everything that had gone wrong between them, Griselda wanted to ally with Amilcar, which required further murders. A man had witnessed Amilcar committing murder, so Griselda offered to handle the man’s death herself. However, when Dario and Chucho found the witness, he was with his wife and infant son. Griselda ordered the murders carried out anyway, only sparing the baby. These tragic deaths are meant to represent the sort of horrible crimes Griselda ordered during her career, and this witness and his wife aren’t based on any specific figures.

8 German Panesso Is Killed By Dario & Others Episode 4, “Middle Management”

After Griselda’s partner, German Panesso, betrayed her to join the Ochoa brothers in Griselda, the Godmother ordered a devastating hit to take the man and his bodyguards down. Dario and the Marielitos shot up the Dadeland Mall, riddling Panesso and his men with holes. Netflix provided a reasonably accurate recreation of Panesso’s death in the famous Dadeland Mall Massacre, an event that is believed to have been ordered by Griselda. However, since there was no definitive proof of this, the Godmother never faced charges for the massacre.

7 Isa, Carla, & The Marielito Are Decapitated By Papo Meija Episode 4, “Middle Management”

In episode 4 of Griselda, the titular queenpin not only ordered Dario to shoot up the Dadeland Mall, but she also sent Chucho and the other Marielitos to bomb the home of Papo Meija. However, she didn’t realize until it was too late that her rival had survived. To get even, Papo sent men to Griselda’s second residence, where her friends, drug couriers Isa and Carla, were with a single Merielito bodyguard. The assassin decapitated all three and left their bodies on the property. Isa and Carla were fictional additions to Griselda, but that crime might have been based on some truth.

6 Griselda Orders A Hit On Papo Meija Episode 4, “Middle Management”

After Papo killed her friends, Griselda became determined to take the man and his associates down. She went to one of his known drug houses herself and, killed several of Papo’s top men, and ordered one of her followers to kill her rival with a machete at an airport. In Griselda, Papo died as a result of the attack, but in real life, he survived and was later sent to prison. Released in 2000, there are varying reports regarding whether Papo Meija eventually died or is still living in Colombia.

5 Arturo Mesa Is Killed By Rafa Salazar Episode 4, “Middle Management”

After the murder and revenge ping-pong game in Griselda episode 4, “Middle Management,” the killings between Griselda and the Ochoas finally came to a close with the latter’s decision to include the Godmother in their operation. However, as a way to tip the scales back in the Ochoas’ direction and prove that Griselda hadn’t “won,” the brothers ordered Rafa Salazar to execute Arturo Mesa in front of his boss and friend. Arturo is another fictional character in Griselda, but his death demonstrated how Griselda was on a slippery slope with the Ochoas.

4 Chucho Castro’s Son Johnny Is Killed By Rivi Ayala Episode 5, “Paradise Lost”

Perhaps the most significant death of Griselda is that of two-year-old Johnny Castro. The boy was killed when Griselda ordered that her bodyguard Chucho Castro be killed after he punched her son Dixon. Rivi attempted to carry out the deed through a drive-by shooting, but his bullets missed Chucho, instead hitting the man’s son. Tragically, this murder really happened and is one of the few that Griselda was convicted for. Johnny Castro’s death was the final straw for the Miami police department, and it is thought that Chucho played a significant role in finally bringing Griselda down.

3 Marta Ochoa Dies From An Overdose Episode 6, “Adios, Miami”

Marta Ochoa’s death at the end of Griselda is another that had a significant impact on the Godmother’s downfall, though this time, she wasn’t responsible. Marta, sister to the Ochoa brothers, had become good friends with Griselda, and the two smoked crack cocaine together to blow off steam after Johnny Castro’s death. Unfortunately, Marta overdosed, and Griselda knew that her brothers wouldn’t believe that this had been an accident. Netflix’s version of events here is again slightly different from reality, where it is believed that Griselda indeed did murder Marta herself.

2 Dario Is Assassinated At Griselda’s Orders Episode 6, “Adios, Miami”

As things began to fall apart for Griselda, her husband, Dario, made matters worse by taking their son and fleeing to Colombia. Though the Godmother pretended to attempt to convince Dario to come home, she instead ordered one of her connections in Medellin to murder her husband and bring her son Michael back to the States. This truly is how the real Dario Sepúlveda died, though it happened before Griselda was arrested in California. Griselda murdered all three of her husbands, which is why she became known as “The Black Widow.”

1 Dixon, Uber, and Osvaldo Are Assassinated By The Ochoas Episode 6, “Adios, Miami”

At the end of Griselda, the series goes through a time jump to the end of the Godmother’s prison sentence. Detective June Hawkins visited and broke the news to Griselda that her three oldest sons had been murdered. Dixon and Uber were killed during drug deals, and Osvaldo was shot down at a nightclub, all just before their mother was set to get out of prison and be reunited with them. Though it’s never confirmed, it’s assumed that Rafa and the Ochoas are waiting for this moment to exact their revenge for Marta’s death since it would hurt Griselda the most. In truth, only two of Griselda Blanco’s sons were killed while she was in prison, and both murders happened long before she was set to be released. Dixon and Michael both lived in Colombia when Griselda was deported, and both outlived their mother, who was assassinated in 2012 while leaving a Medellin butcher shop. The Godmother’s death wasn’t included in Griselda, and the Netflix series instead left off with the woman mourning the deaths of her children. Ultimately, this was a far more impactful way to wrap up so such a tragic and cautionary tale.
