SUMMARY

The first season of Percy Jackson and the Olympians has concluded, receiving critical praise and fan adoration. Dior Goodjohn, who plays Clarisse La Rue, discusses getting into her intense headspace and upcoming plot points. Goodjohn highlights the great chemistry among the cast and shares some humorous behind-the-scenes moments.

Literature Review

Actress Dior Goodjohn, who plays Ares’ daughter Clarisse in Percy Jackson and the Olympians, discusses her time spent working on the show this season.

Percy Jackson and the Olympians first season finale aired this week, bringing the eight-episode saga to an end for now. The Disney+ series has quickly risen to prominence since its premiere late last year, receiving critical praise and fan adoration for its faithfulness to Riordan’s books and excellent casting. The mythological series stars Walker Scobell, Leah Jeffries, and Aryan Simhadri as the lead trio alongside actors like Viking’s Adam Copeland, Will and Grace’s Megan Mullally, and Are You Afraid of the Dark?’s Dior Goodjohn.

The first season of the show follows Percy and friends trying to find who stole Zeus’ master bolt after the protagonist is blamed for the crime. They also must contend with life at Camp Half-Blood, a training facility for demigods, including Clarisse La Rue (Goodjohn), the fiery daughter of Ares (Copeland). Known for being a camp bully, Percy becomes Clarisse’s newest target after his arrival, and their bad blood only intensifies after Percy must fight her father, who declares him an “enemy for life.”

Screen Rant recently interviewed Dior Goodjohn to discuss getting into Clarisse’s intense headspace, exciting upcoming plot points in Percy Jackson & the Olympians, and what the cast is like behind the scenes.

Dior Goodjohn Talks Percy Jackson & The Olympians Season 1

Screen Rant: Your character as the daughter of Ares is super intense, which is awesome. What was it like trying to get into that really aggressive headspace as an actress, when you come off as very nice and not that way at all?

Dior Goodjohn: Okay, I’m going to be honest, it was way easier than it probably should have been. [Laughs] Slightly alarming, but getting into Clarisse - I think just knowing her front page, back page, like the back of my hand, definitely helped. The homework I did for her definitely helped. But I did have a couple songs that I would listen to get myself in the mind space of Clarisse. For example, the scene where you first meet me at Camp Half-Blood, where I bump into Walker [Scobell] and then push him, the song that was playing in my head for that, the vibe was “Clique” by Big Sean and Kanye and Jay-Z. That was that vibe. And then Clarisse’s whole story in general, for me, that’s “King for a Day” by Pierce The Veil. And then the fight scene, specifically, capture the flag, it’s called “Nate Growing Up” by Labrinth.

Nice.

Dior Goodjohn: Yeah, so those are my top three songs for her. Music, it puts you at a certain vibration. And so those were definitely my big three that I used to get into her aggressive state.

And your character has a lot of beef with Percy in the show.

Dior Goodjohn: True.

What was it like building that sort of onscreen antagonistic bully dynamic?

Dior Goodjohn: Again, because I knew how their story unfolded from the books, it was kind of just - and Walker is Percy, first of all, let me just say. That boy, on and off screen, is Percy Jackson. So he and Charlie [Bushnell] made it super easy to slip into character and get into it. But I think, again, just knowing how everything plays out beforehand and understanding the motivations, it just makes it so much easier to get into it. It was like magic really.

And what was it like filming the infamous bathroom scene for episode two?

Dior Goodjohn: It was so fun. I was so freezing cold, all day long, because I was wearing jeans, white jeans, so it was just wet and sticky, but it was so worth it. It put me in the character, in the vibe.

And did you get to spend any time with your onscreen dad, Adam Copeland, on set?

Dior Goodjohn: I didn’t. I didn’t even meet him. We were on set at different times. The first time I ever met him was on was at the premiere on the carpet, which I think is very fitting, because Ares really doesn’t even like his kids. [Laughs]

That’s true, he’s a pretty absent father, it’s kind of on brand. In future episodes, are there characters that you’d really like to get more onscreen interaction with, just to see their chemistry play out a little more?

Dior Goodjohn: Honestly, with everybody. I would love to get more onscreen time, one, with my dad, two, with Walker, with Leah [Jeffries]. She is such a phenomenal actress. Same thing with Aryan [Simhadri] and Walker. Everybody is so good. I’m learning so much from each of my castmates, just watching them work. I’d love to get more scenes in with Charlie. I’d love to get more scenes with Glynn [Turman] - he’s such a good Chiron - with Dionysus, with everybody. Everyone is so amazing and so good, and they all have their own specific styles and ways of approaching a scene that I just want to sit there and watch and just soak it all up and just see what everyone’s process is.

Yeah. And you mentioned really knowing the books well and how that was sort of helping you in the general acting process. I’m curious, in terms of storyline, if there’s a particular plotline in future books or even just a future book as a whole that you would be especially excited to see the series sort of catch up to in terms of Clarisse’s storyline.

Dior Goodjohn: I have said this once, and I will say it again, because I’m going to die on this hill: my most looked forward to - if that’s even correct grammar - one, having that convo with my dad, where he basically tells me, “You’re doing this quest, and you’re doing it alone. Or that’s it - you’re done, we’re done.” I am so, so excited to work with Adam for that. He is such a talented actor, and I’m so excited to see what he brings and to bounce off of one another. Oh my gosh, I’m so excited for that, specifically. And then, I’m excited for the scene where I go Super Saiyan and I kill the drakon and I have my whole, “Ah,” moment about Silena, and I’m just so excited. I can’t wait.

And the second to last episode aired this past week.

Dior Goodjohn: It did. Our final days are upon us.

As the series has come out week to week, what have been your favorite things to see fans reacting to?

Dior Goodjohn: Honestly, my favorite things to see the fans doing is them complaining about having to wait a week, because, girl, me too. [Laughs] It’s to the point where I’ve been forcing myself to stave off for one or two episodes so that I can watch two back to back. It’s like, “I’m right here with you, girl. I don’t want to wait either. But we get what we get. We don’t get upset.”

And are there any scenes from filming that really stick out to you as especially difficult or funny in a behind-the-scenes sort of way?

Dior Goodjohn: Whenever I hear the line that Andrew [Alvarez] says, where he’s like, “Luke is the best swordsman at camp,” I just remember - it’s an ongoing joke in our group chat, because it took so many takes. Because all day long, Andrew would just say, after he saw that line, he would just make the biggest joke out of it and all day long, just be like, “Luke is the strongest swordsman camp.” [Laughs] And so I just can’t stop thinking about that whenever I hear him say that. And he’s so funny too. Whenever he goes like, “Clarisse hunts in those woods. You know that,” he’s just so zesty, and he’s just funny. That’s just one thing. Yeah.

I love the idea of you guys having a group chat, where you’re all together making inside jokes. Is there any other sort of joke like that that really stands out to you from this season?

Dior Goodjohn: Let’s see here. It’s Aryan’s song too, Aryan’s like, [singing] “Consensus!” First of all, I feel like we don’t give Aryan his flowers enough. That boy can sing his butt off, and I’m not even being funny. He’s so good. I just now wanted to put that out there. He’s just amazing, aside from all the jokes that we make. But his Consensus song, whenever we see a video of Aryan back in the day, performing Rolling in the Deep, we’ll be like, “Grover trying to make everybody happy by singing Consensus.” [Laughs] That’s another joke that we have too. It’s so funny. And then total sidebar, not related to the show, but constantly Walker is sending pictures of somber M&M to the group chat, and I just find that hilarious.

Oh my gosh, I know exactly the image you’re talking about.

Dior Goodjohn: Yeah.

Just like with no context or anything.

Dior Goodjohn: No context in the middle of a conversation, just M&M. Yeah.

That’s so funny.

Dior Goodjohn: In mid convo. I’ll be like, “Hey guys, so do we want to all get on FaceTime?” And then he’ll be like: M&M, and leave the chat. [Laughs]

I might have to steal that bit. I really like that, actually.

Dior Goodjohn: Honestly, it’s a great way to exit without arousing any suspicion.

About Percy Jackson & The Olympians

Percy Jackson is on a dangerous quest. Outrunning monsters and outwitting gods, he must journey across America to return Zeus’ master bolt and stop an all-out war. After losing his mother, Percy is sheltered at Camp Half-Blood, a sanctuary for demigod children. He must prove himself and confront his origins once he discovers he too is a demigod, and will take off into the perils of pursuing enemies in search of the Underworld. With the help of his quest mates Annabeth and Grover, Percy’s journey will lead him closer to the answers he seeks: how to fit into a world where he feels out of place, if he’ll ever see his mother again, and if he can ever find out who he’s destined to be.
