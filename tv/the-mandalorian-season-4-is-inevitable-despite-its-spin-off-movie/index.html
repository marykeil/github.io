The Mandalorian Season 4 Is Inevitable, Despite Its Spin-Off Movie

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The fate of The Mandalorian season 4 has not been confirmed by Star Wars, but comments from a Lucasfilm executive suggest that it is inevitable, despite its spin-off movie. The Mandalorian continues to be the best-performing original series on Disney+, making it the flagship TV show of both the franchise and its streaming platform. Keeping the possibility of The Mandalorian season 4 alive indicates that it will not be replaced by The Mandalorian & Grogu, and that the show’s future is bright.

Literature Review

The Mandalorian season 4’s fate seemed grim after the announcement of its spin-off movie, but new comments from Lucasfilm have proven it’s inevitable.

After the announcement of The Mandalorian & Grogu spin-off movie, it seemed as if The Mandalorian season 4 would no longer be happening, but new comments from a Lucasfilm executive have proven the next season of the Star Wars TV show is inevitable. Creator Jon Favreau was developing The Mandalorian season 4 scripts back in May 2022, and talking about his progress on them confirmed season 4 long before season 3 was even released. The neat ending of The Mandalorian season 3 and the announcement of The Mandalorian & Grogu movie, however, made The Mandalorian season 4 seem unlikely.

Despite this, the fate of The Mandalorian season 4 still has yet to be sealed by Star Wars, and this isn’t an accident. Even after three seasons of The Mandalorian, the Star Wars TV show continues to be the best-performing original series on Disney+, proving it’s still the flagship TV show of both the franchise and its streaming platform. Star Wars, then, doesn’t want to eliminate the possibility of continuing Din Djarin’s and Grogu’s story on the small screen in addition to the big screen - and Lucasfilm’s own words about it confirm The Mandalorian season 4 is inevitable.

              The Mandalorian Season 4 Updates: Cast, Story, & Everything We Know   After The Mandalorian season 3’s conclusion in 2023, the story of The Mandalorian season 4 is shaping up to be one of the show’s biggest yet.

The Mandalorian Season 4 Is Being Teased By Lucasfilm

Dave Filoni has provided a cryptic update on The Mandalorian season 4 that emphasizes his, and thus Lucasfilm’s, strong desire to keep every option on the table. Rather than providing a solid response about the fate of season 4, Filoni teases that it shouldn’t be discounted, and that he won’t “commit to one thing.” While this certainly seems like the bare minimum of what Star Wars could say about The Mandalorian season 4, Filoni’s eagerness to continue teasing its fate speaks volumes about what will happen for the TV show’s next season.

Lucasfilm Has No Reason To Tease Season 4 If It’s Not Happening

If The Mandalorian season 4 truly was being replaced by The Mandalorian & Grogu, then Star Wars and Lucasfilm would have made that clear already. There would be no point in keeping the flame of hope alive for viewers who have been waiting for news on The Mandalorian season 4, as they would, if anything, want to emphasize the full shift of The Mandalorian’s story from the small screen to the big screen to direct viewers towards their next focus. Instead, Filoni has actively chosen to keep this flame of hope alive, and it’s this choice that makes season 4’s future inevitable.

              The Mandalorian Cast & Characters Guide   The Mandalorian has created a long list of new Star Wars characters and brought back some familiar ones, with an incredible cast to match.

The Mandalorian Season 4 Is Inevitable - & It’s A Great Thing For The Movie

Even with so many questions about it left unanswered, The Mandalorian season 4 is inevitable. The timeline of its release will be the biggest mystery, with The Mandalorian & Grogu set to begin production later this year, as it’s not yet clear whether Star Wars will want to release The Mandalorian season 4 before or after its spin-off movie. Still, these questions can be answered in time, and it doesn’t change the fact that The Mandalorian season 4 will be great for The Mandalorian & Grogu either way.

Given the fact The Mandalorian films within the Volume soundstage and its spin-off movie could likely do the same, it may be possible to film both projects simultaneously.

Whether it releases before or after the movie, The Mandalorian season 4 being attached to the spin-off movie will build a perfect bridge between the two projects. The popularity that both Din Djarin and Grogu have garnered on their own will no doubt draw in brand-new Star Wars viewers who simply wish to see the father-son duo on the big screen, and attaching The Mandalorian season 4 to the movie could cause those viewers to go to Disney+ to watch more of their story. The same principle applies in reverse, with season 4 viewers watching the movie for more content.

To have these projects work together simultaneously, then, is great for The Mandalorian & Grogu. It can enrich the movie even more, and it can also have an outlet to flesh out anything it can’t fit into its shorter movie runtime. At the same time, Star Wars will have to be careful if it employs this technique, as it could risk isolating the two different audiences if too many story aspects between the show and the movie rely on each other. Still, Star Wars can no doubt accomplish this, and it’s the smartest move for them as a whole.

Star Wars Is Making The Right Choice With The Mandalorian Season 4

Filoni has actively chosen to keep this flame of hope alive, and it’s this choice that makes season 4’s future inevitable.

Continuing The Mandalorian season 4 creates a win-win scenario for Star Wars; the franchise gets both a triumphant return to the movie theater and another season of its most successful TV show. Even with its mixed reviews, The Mandalorian season 3 was still Disney+’s biggest TV show in 2023, and there’s no doubt the love audiences have for Din Djarin and Grogu will make their movie a huge success at the box office. Keeping both season 4 and the movie, then, is the right choice for Lucasfilm to maximize their success with The Mandalorian.

This also allows Star Wars to make a more effective pivot for The Mandalorian sub-franchise as a whole. The Mandalorian season 3 was not the most ideal way for the Star Wars TV show to end its run, as its mixed reviews were certainly jarring compared to its highly-praised first two seasons. The Mandalorian season 4 would allow the TV show to earn its full redemption while also serving as a smooth transition either into or out of The Mandalorian & Grogu, and it would allow viewers to have better closure should season 4 serve as the TV show’s final season.

The Mandalorian season 4, then, is truly inevitable. Star Wars won’t let go of its most successful TV show just yet, and keeping the project alive only offers more benefits for its story, characters, and franchise as a whole, as well as for its viewers who crave at least one more episodic storytelling saga for Din Djarin and Grogu on the small screen. Filoni clearly understands this, hence why his cryptic words about The Mandalorian season 4 are now proving its bright future is inevitable.
