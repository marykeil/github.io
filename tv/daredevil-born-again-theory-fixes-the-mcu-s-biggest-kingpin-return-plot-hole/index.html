Daredevil Born Again Theory Fixes The MCU s Biggest Kingpin Return Plot Hole

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Kingpin’s Daredevil: Born Again return is somewhat of a plot hole after Daredevil season 3’s ending saw Matt defeat Fisk by threatening the latter’s wife, Vanessa. The plot hole of Kingpin’s return after his defeat can be explained by using one theory: Vanessa was a victim of Thanos’ snap, giving Fisk a reason to continue his criminal activities without worrying about Matt’s threat. The MCU’s depiction of the Blip in WandaVision provides a plausible explanation of how Fisk could protect Vanessa by finding her in the chaos of everyone returning, nullifying Matt’s threat and allowing the rivalry between Daredevil and Kingpin to continue.

Literature Review

A Marvel plot hole centering around Kingpin and Daredevil’s rivalry could be avoided thanks to one theory about a Marvel Cinematic Universe event.

A potential Daredevil: Born Again plot hole can be avoided thanks to one theory surrounding other Marvel Cinematic Universe events. Initially, Daredevil: Born Again was considered a reboot of the brand until the Netflix Defenders shows appeared on the MCU timeline on Disney+. This update led Vincent D’Onofrio, the actor responsible for playing Wilson Fisk/Kingpin, to confirm that Daredevil: Born Again’s MCU story is now being treated as a continuation of Netflix’s Defenders timeline.

2:16 Daredevil & The Defenders Complete Timeline: What Order To Watch The Netflix Marvel shows are on Disney+. With Daredevil: Born Again on the way, here’s the full watch order for The Defenders shows and The Punisher.

With this update, attention turned to how the upcoming Marvel TV show would follow on from the lingering plot threads left by Daredevil season 3. This speculation was followed by swift answers from Marvel Studios amid the production start of Daredevil: Born Again; Deborah Ann Woll, Elden Hensen, and Wilson Bethel were confirmed to return to the show as Karen Page, Foggy Nelson, and Benjamin Poindexter/Bullseye respectively, solidifying its status as a continuation of Daredevil. With one Kingpin plot hole left to resolve amid his and Daredevil’s MCU homecoming, one theory provides the perfect answer.

Daredevil Season 3 Explained How Daredevil Beat Kingpin

The plot hole in question concerns Kingpin’s return, more specifically how his criminal empire can continue after Daredevil season 3’s ending in which the titular vigilante figured out how to beat his nemesis. For three seasons, Matt Murdock struggled to overcome Fisk be it through the eyes of the law or via the methods of his superheroic alter ego. Eventually, Daredevil season 3 explored the possibility of Matt breaking his main rule and killing Kingpin, putting an end to his criminal activity for good.

Fisk’s fondness for Vanessa meant that he would never put her through that, willingly accepting his defeat at the hands of Matt Murdock/Daredevil.

That said, Daredevil season 3’s ending provided another way for Matt to overcome Kingpin. This involved Kingpin’s wife, Vanessa Fisk, the only person the crime lord would risk everything to keep safe. Matt told Fisk that he would uncover his crimes and have him arrested and that, should Kingpin ever attempt to escape, expose Matt’s secret identity, or try to harm Karen and Foggy, he would expose Vanessa’s part in Fisk’s crimes. Fisk’s fondness for Vanessa meant that he would never put her through that, willingly accepting his defeat at the hands of Matt Murdock/Daredevil.

This satisfyingly ended the feud between Daredevil and Kingpin in Netflix’s Daredevil. However, Kingpin’s return to the MCU with no ramifications causes somewhat of a plot hole based on Daredevil season 3’s ending. That said, this plot hole can be fixed through one specific Marvel theory.

Daredevil: Born Again Theory Explains That Vanessa Did Not Survive Thanos’ Snap

With Daredevil: Born Again and its three preceding seasons being canon to the MCU, they can be tied to Thanos’ snap from Avengers: Infinity War. This theory claims that Vanessa was a victim of Thanos’ snap which goes hand-in-hand with Echo’s confirmation that Matt and Fisk survived the event. If Vanessa is revealed as one of the many beings snapped by Thanos, it could explain the revival of Kingpin and Daredevil’s rivalry. Fisk would now have nothing to lose after Vanessa’s death, once more going after Matt, Karen, and Foggy.

Echo included a flashback to Maya Lopez’s involvement with Fisk’s empire, taking place in the five years between Avengers: Infinity War and Avengers: Endgame and confirming Daredevil and Kingpin’s survival of the snap.

The MCU Already Revealed How Fisk Could Protect Vanessa From Daredevil

Utilizing the Blip to explain how Kingpin continued his criminal activities makes a lot of sense, but only when taking into account what happened when Vanessa returned. In Avengers: Endgame, Hulk used the Infinity Stones to bring back the victims of Thanos’ first snap. Once Matt figured this out, he could have sent Fisk back to jail by once again targeting Vanessa and holding her life over Kingpin’s head. However, a second part of this MCU theory explains how Fisk could have protected Vanessa stemming from a Phase 4 property.

In 2021’s WandaVision, one of the most compelling scenes was the depiction of the Blip and how everyone returned from Thanos’ attack. This was shown through the eyes of Monica Rambeau when the character simply reformed in the same place she disappeared five years prior. The ensuing return of every other victim caused widespread chaos as shown by Monica’s experience in the hospital in WandaVision. Through this chaos, it can be theorized how Fisk kept Vanessa from Daredevil.

After the return of the snap victims, there would likely have been a race amid the chaos between Fisk and Daredevil to see who could reach Vanessa first. The explanation around the plot hole would simply be to have Fisk reach Vanessa and use his immeasurable resources to protect her. This would nullify Matt’s threat from the ending of Daredevil season 3 and explain how his and Fisk’s rivalry continued post-Avengers: Endgame.

Furthermore, the MCU has recently revealed another way in which Kingpin could avoid Daredevil’s threat to Vanessa. Daredevil: Born Again set photos revealed a new love interest for Matt Murdock, someone beyond the likes of Karen Page, Claire Temple, Jennifer Walters, and Elektra Natchios from his MCU past. Through this character, Kingpin could turn the tables on Matt Murdock in the upcoming show.

              Matt Murdock's New MCU Love Interest Revealed In Latest Daredevil: Born Again Set Photos   New Daredevil: Born Again set photos from the upcoming Disney+ series reveal Matt Murdock may have moved on from She-Hulk and Karen Page.

Given that Matt has previously threatened the love of Kingpin’s life, it is hard to imagine the crime lord not seeking revenge against this in particular. Kingpin also knows Matt’s secret identity meaning he would have little trouble tracking down his new love interest. There would be no better way to seek retribution against Matt’s threats than to target someone he now loves, warding off yet another threat to Vanessa and fixing the biggest plot hole surrounding Kingpin’s return in Daredevil: Born Again.
