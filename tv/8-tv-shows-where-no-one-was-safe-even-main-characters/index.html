8 TV Shows Where No One Was Safe (Even Main Characters)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

TV shows like Game of Thrones and The Walking Dead prove that no character is safe from death, adding suspense and unpredictability to the plot. Some non-violent shows, like Grey’s Anatomy, include brutal deaths to shock and engage viewers, subverting genre conventions. Characters like Tony Soprano and Walter White demonstrate the corrupting nature of power, leading to the deaths of many around them.

Literature Review

The death of a central character can be a shocking twist, but for some TV series, killing off central characters was a constant on the show.

This article contains descriptions of violence and death.

In TV shows, using a character’s death as an interesting plot point is common practice, but some shows kill off so many characters that the mystery isn’t if someone will die but who will. This adds a different kind of suspense and a satisfying payoff when a character manages to survive through the end of the show. When a show is well-written and has strong actors, it doesn’t matter if the characters might disappear at any moment, it’s still impossible not to care what happens to them. Some series have premises that naturally align with having multiple character deaths, even killing off main characters who have an enormous impact on the show. Crime dramas, post-apocalyptic narratives, and series with supernatural elements all fall under the category of TV that is expected to have plenty of deaths. However, some non-violent TV shows have brutal deaths and have become known for including random accidents that end their characters’ lives. This subversion of genre conventions is a unique way to make a show stand out among its peers. 10 Shocking TV Deaths That Proved No Character Was Safe
The deaths of Ned Stark, Glenn Rhee, Boone Carlyle, and other major characters established that no one is safe from death in their respective shows.

8 24 (2001 - 2010) Jack Bauer loses more than one person throughout the 24 hours the audience witnesses.

24 uses each season to tell the story of one day through twenty-four episodes, each an hour of real-time. Jack Bauer (Kiefer Sutherland) is a counter-terrorism agent who deals with only the biggest threats to the country. Due to this high-risk job, he’s constantly losing allies, friends, partners, and loved ones. As the series progressed, the plot lines and threats of violence only became more outlandish and were no longer confined to domestic attacks. This, combined with the format of the show, meant that it was only important that Bauer made it to the end.

7 The 100 (2014 - 2020) Each season puts a new spin on the apocalypse and introduces new threats.

The 100
TV-14

Release Date March 19, 2014 Seasons 7 Writers Jason Rothenberg , Mallory Kass Network The CW Showrunner Jason Rothenberg

Clarke (Eliza Taylor) finds herself the unlikely leader of a group of misfit teens who are sent down to Earth thousands of years in the future to see if it can sustain human life. She and her team quickly realize that they aren’t alone on the planet, and there are plenty more threats they face than the harsh environment. Although Clarke gives everything she has for the safety of those who follow her, she is rarely rewarded with anything other than loss. Lexa (Alycia Debnam-Carey) is a rival leader turned lover of Clarke, and her loss is one of the saddest deaths on The 100. However, Lexa’s death isn’t as shocking as Bellamy’s (Bob Morely) in season 7. Even though he and Clarke have been through so much together and consider themselves family, he becomes a threat to Clarke and another person she cares about, leading not only to his death but Clarke being the one to do it.

6 Breaking Bad (2008 - 2013) A science teacher turns into a notorious and violent drug kingpin.

Breaking Bad

Release Date January 20, 2008 Seasons 5 Writers Peter Gould , Gennifer Hutchison , Vince Gilligan , George Mastras , Moira Walley-Beckett , Sam Catlin , Thomas Schnauz Network AMC Streaming Service(s) Netflix Franchise(s) Breaking Bad Showrunner Vince Gilligan

When Walter White (Bryan Cranston) decides that he wants to start cooking meth and selling it to support his family after he dies, it’s obvious that it isn’t his family he’s looking out for. He’s attracted to power and soon proves that he’ll do anything to get it. None of the characters that Walt interacts with inside the drug world are savory types, but their deaths are often extreme. Outside his enemies, when he outright kills or allows characters like Mike (Jonathon Banks) and Jane (Krysten Ritter) to die, it shows how expendable he believes the people around him are.

5 The Walking Dead (2010 - 2022) It’s not just the zombies that the characters need to watch out for.

The Walking Dead

Release Date October 31, 2010 Seasons 11 Network AMC Streaming Service(s) Netflix , AMC Plus Franchise(s) The Walking Dead Showrunner Frank Darabont , Angela Kang , Scott M. Gimple , Glen Mazzara

In a zombie apocalypse, it’s expected that there are going to be plenty of casualties, but the longer the characters of The Walking Dead survive on the show, the harder it is to say goodbye. One of the cruelest of the series was when Glenn (Steven Yeun) seemed to have survived a vicious walker attack and then was murdered in front of the others by Negan (Jeffery Dean Morgan). As one of the most-loved characters on the show, seeing him die forced the conclusion that even a resurrection doesn’t mean a character is back for good.

4 Grey’s Anatomy (2005 - ) Tragedy, accidents, and illness all result in one of the highest body counts on TV.

Grey’s Anatomy

Release Date March 27, 2005 Seasons 19 Writers Shonda Rhimes , Julie Wong , Jen Klein , Tameson Duffy , Meg Marinis Network ABC Showrunner Meg Marinis

Some character deaths in Grey’s Anatomy were more believable than others, but all of them were well-timed and came at the most shocking moments possible. Although the series has garnered the reputation for killing off doctors and patients alike at any time, every death has served a purpose and been warranted, even if it wasn’t popular. The show has been careful to space out its dramatic and dangerous events, like the plane crash, the shooting, and grisly natural disasters. This way, they align with poignant character storylines as well. Despite the number of main characters who were written off by dying, many Grey’s Anatomy characters have cheated death as well. The more times they come close to dying and still make it out alive, the more it seems that these characters will make it out of the hospital intact. Unfortunately, surviving one incident doesn’t mean a character is immune to them all. Of all the shocking deaths in the show, Derek Shepherd’s (Patrick Dempsey) sudden death hit the hardest.

3 Lost (2004 - 2010) Though the characters weren’t dead the entire time, they were by the end of the series.

Lost
TV-14

Release Date September 22, 2004 Seasons 6 Writers Jeffrey Lieber , J.J. Abrams , Damon Lindelof Network ABC Showrunner Damon Lindelof , Carlton Cuse

Lost starts with a devastating plane crash where the survivors must find a way to sustain their lives on the mysterious island they landed on. From there, the show expands into an exploration of the characters’ lives, motivations, and fears while leaning more deeply into themes of magical realism. By season 6, the characters had died, escaped the island, and come back to it over many years. The brutal realities of survival situations were always at the forefront of the show, with many characters succumbing to their injuries in season 1 because of the lack of medical supplies. After Boon’s (Ian Somerhalder) death in season 1, it became clear that it wouldn’t just be the minor characters or extras who suffered harsh fates at the hands of the island. However, it wasn’t only the mystical powers of the island that led to these deaths, as many characters were killed by other people on the island, not just in accidents. Even John Locke (Terry O’Quinn), who had the utmost faith in the island’s power, is eventually turned into a puppet of the powers at work there. Lost starts with a devastating plane crash where the survivors must find a way to sustain their lives on the mysterious island they landed on.

2 The Sopranos (1999 - 2007) As Tony Soprano becomes increasingly corrupted by power, it leads to the death of many.

The Sopranos
TV-MA

Release Date January 10, 1999 Seasons 6 Writers David Chase Network HBO Max Streaming Service(s) Max Showrunner David Chase

Tony Soprano (James Gandolfini) is one of the most infamous antiheroes in TV history, and the deaths at his hand, or orchestrated by him, are numerous. Being the head of a crime family is a dangerous and violent job, and he slowly devolves into an even more brutal man with few redeeming qualities. No one close to Tony is safe, but a pivotal moment in the show comes during the murder of Sal Bonpensiero (Vincent Pastore). Tony isn’t the one to pull the trigger himself, but he wholeheartedly agrees to the murder when he finds out that Sal has become an FBI informant. Once he proves himself capable of killing a dear friend, it only pushes the boundaries of his already corrupt morality to the limit. The audience might be on Tony’s side when he kills the villains of the show, but it calls into question when Tony himself becomes the true villain. Of course, living like this has its consequences, and Tony faces the reality of his situation in the final moments of the series.

As a ruthless mob boss, Tony Soprano orders the deaths of many gangsters during The Sopranos, but he’s unafraid to take matters into his own hands.

1 Game Of Thrones (2011 - 2019) Though many character deaths are faithful to the source material, some come as a shock.

Game of Thrones

Created by George R.R. Martin TV Show(s) Game Of Thrones , House of the Dragon , Jon Snow

Season 1 of Game of Thrones drops the biggest bombshell that sets the tone for the rest of the series: Ned Stark’s (Sean Bean) death. Though already a cruel and violent series, Game of Thrones communicates that no one is safe especially the just and noble, in the land of Westeros. Part of what makes Game of Thrones popular is that even though it’s a fantasy series, the stakes and consequences feel real. Only 8 characters in Game of Thrones survived from beginning to end, but this only made it easier to root for the characters who refused to die.
