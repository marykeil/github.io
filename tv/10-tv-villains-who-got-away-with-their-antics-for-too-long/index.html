10 TV Villains Who Got Away With Their Antics For Too Long

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Shows with great hero/villain dynamics can drag out their villain arcs to make them more engrossing, even if it means stretching the bounds of rationality. Unplanned or poorly managed villain arcs can undermine the stakes of a show and make it less captivating for viewers. Some TV villains, like Joe from You or Halbrand from The Rings of Power, escape capture for far too long due to the inaction or ineptitude of characters around them.

Literature Review

Compelling TV villains make their shows much better, and this means writers are often reluctant to kill them off and risk the show losing its balance.

Some of the best TV villains can haunt a show’s hero for multiple seasons, even if they should logically have been brought to justice much sooner. TV shows with great hero/villain duos usually find a way to make these captivating dynamics last for a very long time, allowing them to squeeze all the dramatic potential possible out of their characters. This can mean that shows have to concoct illogical ways for their antagonists to escape harm time and time again. A compelling villain can be just as important to a show’s success as a relatable hero, so shows don’t want to discard characters too soon. Both comedy and drama shows can drag out villain arcs. Comedy relies on great chemistry between actors, and drama needs characters who clash with each other’s personalities. If writers can find a way to logically keep their villains going without being stopped, then it can make their rivalry with a protagonist much more engrossing. However, unplanned or poorly managed villain arcs will stretch the limits of rationality, and it will become clear to viewers that there are no real stakes in the show. Some TV villains evade capture for far too long, continuing their evil deeds without repercussions.

10 Great TV Villains Wasted By Their Shows A captivating villain is one of the best ways to make a TV series binge-worthy. Still, several famous TV shows have wasted great antagonists.

10 Mom Played by Tress MacNeille in Futurama

Release Date March 28, 1999 Cast Katey Sagal , Billy West , Lauren Tom , Maurice LaMarche , David Herman , Tress MacNeille , John DiMaggio , Phil LaMarr Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Prime Video

Mom tries to use her robot empire to take over the world in season 2 of Futurama, but when her plan is foiled, she is able to put back on her charming matronly persona and convince everyone that she’s nothing more than a harmless old grandmother. In public, she is sweet and innocent, which helps her keep her consistently evil schemes behind closed doors. Despite multiple attempts at world domination, Mom never faces any real repercussions. She’s too rich and powerful to be touched, and no police officer wants to be seen arresting a helpless old lady.

9 Homelander Played by Anthony Starr in The Boys

Release Date July 26, 2019 Cast Elisabeth Shue , Jensen Ackles , Goran Visnjic , Jessie T. Usher , Chace Crawford , Dominique McElligott , Laz Alonso , Nathan Mitchell , Aya Cash , Colby Minifie , Karl Urban , Erin Moriarty , Karen Fukuhara , Jack Quaid , Antony Starr , claudia doumit , Tomer Capon Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

In a world filled with immoral superheroes, none are more twisted or more powerful than Homelander. Butcher’s quest to take down the corrupt supe has met countless dead-ends, even when teaming up with the indestructible Soldier Boy and taking experimental doses of Compound-V to give himself powers. Aside from his remarkable powers, Homelander’s greatest strength is his pristine public image, meaning that nobody knows about his horrific crimes. Homelander looks set to enter the political sphere in The Boys season 4, potentially making him even harder to defeat, although the public should have turned on him long ago.

8 Team Rocket Played by several voice actors in several differnet Pokémon shows

Team Rocket are so clumsy and ineffective that they have a catchphrase for every time they are defeated: “Team Rocket’s blasting off again!”

Team Rocket are so clumsy and ineffective that they have a catchphrase for every time they are defeated: “Team Rocket’s blasting off again!” Still, for every time they blast off, they manage to return with some new shoddy disguises and another half-baked plan to steal Ash’s Pikachu. Pokémon hinted at a Jessie and James romance a few times, so maybe their pathetic criminal schemes are just a guise, so they can spend more time together on the company card. Team Rocket have only been allowed to continue their criminal enterprise for so long because they’re so harmless.

7 Joe Goldberg Played by Penn Badgley in You

Release Date September 9, 2018 Cast Elizabeth Lail , Michaela McManus , Ambyr Childers , Victoria Pedretti , Luca Padovan , Scott Speedman , Travis Van Winkle , Penn Badgley , Jenna Ortega , Shay Mitchell Streaming Service(s) Netflix

Joe should have been brought to justice many times, but he always manages to worm his way out of trouble. In the first season, his strange behavior around Beck almost gets him caught, especially when he breaks into her apartment and has to hide in her shower. She’s seconds away from seeing him, but she gets a phone call which distracts her. Even after Joe’s crimes escalate, some incredible luck and some inept police work combine to ensure he remains a free man. You season 5 will be the show’s last, so Joe could still be caught.

8 TV Villains Who Saved Their Shows While there are many reasons a TV show can be made better or even saved from doom, it’s not often expected to be a villain, but these 8 managed it.

6 The Daleks Played by many different voice actors and operators in Doctor Who

Release Date November 23, 1963 Cast Jenna Coleman , Jodie Whittaker , Alex Kingston , David Tennant , Matt Smith , Peter Capaldi Network BBC

The Daleks first appeared in Doctor Who in 1963, and they have been a formidable foe for every new Doctor ever since. The Daleks were born out of a fascistic desire for racial purity and the extermination of all life they deem to be inferior. They are driven only by hatred, with no mercy or any other emotions to impede their violent surge across the universe. The Doctor has had a few chances to win the Time War, but the Daleks always seem to come up with a new technological development just in time. Their war could rage on forever.

5 Sauron/ Halbrand Played by Charlie Vickers in The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power

Release Date September 1, 2022 Cast Morfydd Clark , Ismael Cruz Cordova , Charlie Vickers , Markella Kavenagh , Megan Richards , Sara Zwangobani , Daniel Weyman , Cynthia Addai-Robinson , Lenny Henry , Lloyd Owen , Nazanin Boniadi , Dylan Smith , Alex Tarrant , Tyroe Muhafidin , Robert Aramayo , Geoff Morrell , Thusitha Jayasundera , Maxine Cunliffe Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

Galadriel probably could have ascertained his evil intentions much earlier.

The first season of The Rings of Power probed at the mystery of Sauron’s origins, but in the end, the reveal was pretty predictable. Halbrand spends a lot of his time looking menacing and hinting at his rightful position as King of the Southlands. Galadriel probably could have ascertained his evil intentions much earlier, especially if she had researched the history of the Southlands sooner. Assuming that The Rings of Power doesn’t deviate too much from Tolkein’s narrative, Sauron will survive to build his kingdom in Mordor, although he could have been stopped.

4 Hannibal Lecter Played by Mads Mikkelsen in Hannibal

Release Date April 4, 2013 Cast Hugh Dancy , Caroline Dhavernas , Aaron Abrams , Mads Mikkelsen , Laurence Fishburne , Scott Thompson Streaming Service(s) Hulu

Hannibal Lecter uses his position within the FBI to keep tabs on the investigation into the Chesapeake Ripper, and whenever someone gets too close to him, he disposes of them. Hannibal’s brutal deaths suggest that sadistic serial killers are much more common in the show than in real life, but the FBI still should be able to sniff out the Chesapeake Ripper when he is hiding right under their noses. Hannibal goading Will into indulging his dark side should be another red flag, but he remains undetected, showing the FBI’s reluctance to suspect one of their own.

3 The Vulture Played by Dean Winters in Brooklyn Nine-Nine

Release Date September 17, 2013 Cast Stephanie Beatriz , Melissa Fumero , Andy Samberg , Andre Braugher , Joel McKinnon Miller , Dirk Blocker , Terry Crews , Chelsea Peretti , Joe Lo Truglio Streaming Service(s) Netflix , Peacock

Along with Madeline Wuntch and Doug Judy, The Vulture is one of the recurring characters who helps paint a picture of New York’s bloated, bureaucratic criminal justice system in Brooklyn Nine-Nine. The Vulture is a detective from the NYPD’s Major Crimes Unit, and he gets his nickname from his habit of swooping in and scavenging on interesting cases, snatching them away from Jake and the other detectives in the 99th precinct. The Vulture is in some of Brooklyn Nine-Nine’s best episodes, but he’s an obnoxious jerk who builds a career off of stealing other peoples’ work.

10 Funniest Sitcom Villains, Ranked Every story needs a bad guy, even in situational comedies, but the best villains in sitcoms stand out for being just as funny as the main cast.

2 Logan Roy Played by Brian Cox in Succession

Release Date June 3, 2018 Cast Hiam Abbass , Alan Ruck , Sarah Snook , Kieran Culkin , Natalie Gold , Nicholas Braun , J. Smith-Cameron , Matthew Macfadyen , Peter Friedman , Brian Cox , Arian Moayed , Jeremy Strong , Rob Yang , Dagmara Dominczyk Streaming Service(s) HBO Max

Most of the characters in Succession are villains in some way or another, but Logan Roy is still the most loathsome. As the CEO of Waystar, he is the man who everyone else must strive to impress, but it would have been much healthier for his children to stop fighting each other for his approval. There are a few moments in Succession which show the Roy family working well together without Logan’s influence, and Shiv, Roman and Kendall could all have stood up to him if only they didn’t fear being ousted from the company.

1 Zack Addy Played by Eric Millegan in Bones

Release Date September 13, 2005 Cast Emily Deschanel , T.J. Thyne , Michaela Conlin , John Francis Daley , David Boreanaz , Tamara Taylor Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Disney+

Zack works for the Jefferson Institute under the guidance of Dr. Brennan until he is arrested for assisting the serial killer known as the Gormogon. Like Brennan, Zack prizes logic above all else, and this leads to his downfall. Brennan has a major blind spot with Zack, failing to see the warning signs, primarily that Zack often struggles to appreciate the intrinsic value of human life. Zack is more misguided than truly evil, but Brennan and the rest of the team fail to get him the help that he needs, dismissing his detached persona as mere eccentricity.
