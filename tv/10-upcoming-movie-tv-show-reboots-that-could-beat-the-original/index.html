10 Upcoming Movie & TV Show Reboots That Could Beat The Original

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Reboots can improve the odds of success by tapping into built-in nostalgia while updating and improving previous entries. Mahershala Ali’s involvement in the Blade reboot, along with a multi-billion dollar franchise behind it, gives the project great potential to outshine the previous films. Greta Gerwig’s visionary directing style and ability to adapt stories in new and exciting ways makes her the perfect choice to helm a new Netflix adaptation of The Chronicles of Narnia.

Literature Review

Reboots for popular movies and TV shows give old franchises a new shot at becoming fresh and popular for the modern audience, overtaking the original.

The film and TV industry can be extremely fickle, especially when choosing new projects, but revisiting older established IPs in a reboot improves the odds of success. Many of the most popular movies and shows currently being released are part of a larger franchise or IP. However, studios need to continue to deliver fresh new stories alongside their growing franchises, and sometimes this can be achieved by looking further back. Reboots have been around for a long time with projects being granted new life with a fresh cast, or simply returning after a long hiatus. The benefit to rebooting a franchise or series is that there is built-in nostalgia for the project, but this can be a double-edged sword. Reboots need to get the right balance of not destroying what came before, but still updating and improving on previous entries. And with the right studio behind a project, it’s possible to even improve on the original.

10 Blade Mahershala Ali Takes The Lead

The Blade trilogy, starring Wesley Snipes, was released between 1998 and 2004. The series featured one of the earliest black superheroes in a film, after 1993’s The Meteor Man, and leaned into a much darker, grittier film than the light and fluffy superhero movies that came before. Blade is an anti-hero story, and while the early films enjoyed success enough to make a trilogy, the MCUs Blade has much deeper pockets and the opportunity to lead a whole supernatural branch of the MCU. The film has already experienced a number of roadblocks with delays and changes in the creative team, but star Mahershala Ali has remained a constant throughout. With two-time Oscar winner Ali in the lead role, and a multi-billion dollar franchise behind it, the reboot has great potential to outshine the previous films. Blade is also one of the most highly anticipated projects for the future of the MCU, as it is almost certain to shake up the formula and be a darker, more adult project.

Marvel Proved How To Make The MCU Blade Reboot Work 25 Years Ago Production on the MCU’s upcoming Blade reboot has been tumultuous, so Marvel Studios may turn to the past to learn some valuable lessons.

9 The Flinstones Where Is The Family From Bedrock Now?

The Flinstones is a beloved children’s animated TV show from the ’60s. While the original run was popular enough to endure through the decades and land it a reboot movie in 1994, the story has never been updated until now. The new series Bedrock, starring Elizabeth Banks as Pebbles, and Manny Jacinto as Bamm-Bamm is set to be a new adult-animated comedy series with the characters all grown up (via Deadline). Animated shows for adults have become increasingly popular and with a talented voice cast and a brand-new story, Bedrock could see the series move into the modern era.

8 Nosferatu A Truly Terrifying Vampire Tale

Nosferatu was first released in 1922, as a silent film that adapted the story of Bram Stoker’s Dracula without any of the sexiness. The central character became a popular iteration of the vampire, but aside from the 1979 remake, the character is long overdue a reboot. In a new project starring Bill Skarsgård, Lily-Rose Depp, Nicholas Hoult, Willem Dafoe, Aaron Taylor-Johnson, and Emma Corrin, this film features an incredibly promising cast. With Skarsgård’s previous portrayal of Pennywise in the It reboot, his skill for playing an ominous and fear-inducing character sets this project up to be a great success.

7 Married… With Children A New Animated Revival

Married… With Children was a hit show during its run in the ’80s and ’90s, and it’s set to make a comeback with the original stars reprising their roles. The new series will star the original cast in a new animated format aimed at adults (via Deadline). The original show enjoyed success in its 11-season run, but upon rewatching, the series does not fit with modern audience ideals. A reboot can not only bring the series up to date and in front of a new audience, but also improve the comedy from the original.

6 Red Sonja Comic Book Movies Are Proving Successful

In 1985, Red Sonja starring Arnold Schwarzenegger and Brigitte Nielsen was released and failed spectacularly, earning back less than $7 million of its $17 million budget (via The Numbers). Now, the film has an opportunity to find success with a brand-new cast, starring Matilda Lutz as the titular lead, and Robert Sheehan as the antagonist Dragan The Magnificent. The film is set to release in 2024 and is very likely to overtake and succeed its predecessor with the talented cast and crew attached.

Why The New Red Sonja Movie Is Right To Change Her Backstory The Red Sonja remake will change one major element of her backstory from the original movie for good, bringing it in-line with a comic book revamp.

5 Fantastic Four Marvel’s First Family Joins The MCU

The Fantastic Four are one of the earliest comic-book superhero teams, and the first to be created under the Marvel label. After being adapted and rebooted twice in 10 years, Marvel Studios is set to see the First Family join the MCU in 2025. One of the biggest gripes with earlier entries has been down to the portrayal of the team’s primary antagonist, Doctor Doom, who has yet to be fully realized on screen, but with Marvel’s skill at crafting incredible villains like Thanos and Kang, Doctor Doom could finally get his time to shine in the upcoming film.

4 Superman: Legacy James Gunn’s New DCU

The DCEU struggled to live up to expectations with the films feeling incoherent, rushed, and lacking a clear creative vision, but with James Gunn now helming DC, Superman: Legacy could ring in a new era. Superman is a hero that is hard to get right and, after the incredible performance of Christopher Reeve in the 70s and 80s, later films have failed to recapture the original magic. Considering Gunn’s track record with superhero movies and his love of the medium, the upcoming Superman: Legacy has the most promise for a true Superman story in years.

3 The Chronicles Of Narnia Greta Gerwig Set To Helm A New Netflix Narnia

The Chronicles of Narnia is a set of beloved children’s books by C.S. Lewis that was most recently adapted to film for Disney with three films released between 2005 and 2010. Greta Gerwig is set to take the reins and bring the world of Narnia to Netflix (via Cosmopolitan). Gerwig has repeatedly proven how capable and visionary a director she is with films like Barbie, Little Women, and Lady Bird. Her ability to adapt and deliver stories in a new and exciting way is spectacular and bringing that talent to the Chronicles series is exactly what it needs to succeed.

2 Starsky & Hutch A Female-Led Reboot Of The Buddy Cop Action Series

Starsky & Hutch was a popular series from 1975 to 1979, but the story could be served well with a reboot. The original show is unlikely to land with modern audiences, as seen from the mediocre performance of the 2004 remake starring Ben Stiller and Owen Wilson. Now the series is set to be revived with a female-led cast for a modern retelling that could give the story new life (via THR). While gender-swapped reboots have not enjoyed the most success, there have been a few, such as Evil Dead Rise and 13 Going on 30, which worked well.

8 Arnold Schwarzenegger Roles That Sylvester Stallone Would Have Done Better Two iconic movie stars, some Arnold Schwarzenegger roles are hard to imagine Sylvester Stallone would not have done at least as good or even better.

1 Cliffhanger Another Stallone Action Hero

Sylvester Stallone has been at the heart of several action franchises as the leading man, but one 1993 film, Cliffhanger, almost bankrupted the production company behind the film. Now, more than 20 years later, it’s getting the love it deserves with a brand-new sequel starring Stallone. The movie featured spectacular stunts and an action-packed story, and with a greater financial investment, it has the potential to breathe new life into the series.
