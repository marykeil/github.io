SUMMARY

Peacock offers a robust catalog of movies and TV shows, with new additions each month, making it one of the largest streaming collections available. Peacock has become a multifaceted platform, featuring live-streaming sports and striking lucrative deals with major leagues like MLB, Premiere League, and WWE. In February 2024, Peacock is adding over 100 new movies and TV shows, including romance movies, superhero films, Hallmark originals, and exclusive docuseries like “Kings from Queens: The Run DMC Story.”

Literature Review

NBC Universal’s streaming service Peacock offers a wide variety of movies, TV shows, and even live events, with much more arriving every month.

Peacock features a robust catalog of movies and TV shows, with even more coming to the streaming service in February 2024. Launched to great fanfare in 2020, Peacock changed the streaming game with its mixture of free and paid content, and the streamer was able to land top-tier shows like The Office because of its NBC affiliation. In the years since its debut, Peacock has further expanded its reach by including many hit films on its platform, and it offers one of the largest streaming collections available.

Like most streaming services, Peacock’s catalog isn’t stagnant, and each month the streamer adds dozens of new shows and movies to keep things fresh. Additionally, Peacock has also become a home for live-streaming sports, and their lucrative deals with the MLB, Premiere League, and World Wrestling Entertainment have made them a truly multifaceted platform. The best TV shows on Peacock represent the upper echelon of streaming entertainment, and the service has begun to create memorable series and movies that rival the original content of their competitors. In February 2024, more than 100 movies and TV shows are new to Peacock, and that’s excluding live sporting events.

For more on the movies and TV shows coming to other streaming platforms, check out what’s new on Paramount+ and new on Netflix.

With Valentine’s Day just around the corner, Peacock is embracing the romance with its library additions on February 1. It’s got a combination of genres when it comes to romance though, like the zombie movie Warm Bodies, the self-aware rom-com Isn’t It Romantic, and the more traditional romantic drama Sleepless in Seattle all coming to Peacock on February 1. There are also some Hallmark originals thrown in for good measure, like Marrying Mr. Darcy, which is inspired by the works of Jane Austen.

Romance isn’t the only genre coming to Peacock at the start of the month though. In the huge drop of library content, there are also the superhero genre Batman Begins movies, comedies like Zoolander, Hallmark whodunits like The Morning Show Mysteries, and historical dramas like Braveheart. There are also quite a few movies depicting the journeys of musicians.

The stories of musicians on Peacock will also include the new exclusive docuseries Kings from Queens: The Run DMC Story. Rev Run and DMC reunite in the docuseries to tell the story of how the group met and changed the music game.

February 2

Bosco (2024) Bros (2022)

February 2 sees two very different projects added to Peacock. One is a Peacock exclusive movie while the other is a movie that had a theatrical release in 2022.

2022’s Bros was a groundbreaking romantic comedy. It featured a gay storyline and a predominantly LGBTQ+ main cast. The story follows two men who don’t commit to relationships, but continually find themselves drawn to one another while they pursue their career dreams and fall in love. Starring Billy Eichner and Luke Macfarlane, the movie was a critical success but didn’t perform well a the box office. Streaming gives it another chance to find its audience.

Bosco stars Nikki Blonsky and Aubrey Joseph in a tale inspired by a true story. Joseph stars as the titular character, a man sentenced to 35 years in prison for attempted drug possession, who plans an escape in order to be there for his child’s first birthday. Blonsky stars as the woman who helps him.

February 3

Gilded Newport Mysteries: Murder At The Breakers (2024) Paging Mr. Darcy (2024)

A pair of Hallmark movies join Peacock on February 3. While Hallmark movies can be a part of Peacock’s library content, that isn’t the case for new releases. Because Hallmark will reair their new movies throughout the month, they are only available to stream on Peacock for three days before they are removed from the streamer and added back at a later date, so Hallmark fans will want to catch these two quickly.

The first is Gilded Newport Mysteries: Murder At The Breakers, which is inspired by a novel. Set in 1895 Rhode Island, a murder occurs at a ball, and one of the women attending begins an investigation of her own when her brother is arrested. The second is Paging Mr. Darcy which is part of Hallmark’s “Loveuary” themed movies. Each of the movies has a link to Jane Austen. In this case, the movie is set at a modern Jane Austen conference where a scholar forms a connection with the man playing Mr. Darcy.

February 6

Empire of Dirt (2013)

Empire of Dirt is a Canadian drama that made a big impression at the Toronto International Film Festival and the Canadian Screen Awards the year it was released, picking up several award nominations. It centers on a First Nations single mother who struggles to connect with her mother, but also her own daughter. The movie examines the complexities of mother-daughter relationships and gives much-needed representation to the screen.

February 8

Couple to Throuple (2024)

There is no shortage of reality shows on Peacock. The streamer is the home to such successful franchises as The Real Housewives and Below Deck. Couple to Throuple, however, is a new dating show for the streamer. Taking place at a tropical resort, the series follows four couples who are considering bringing a third person into their relationship. They have the chance to meet and spend time with single people who have been in polyamorous relationships in the past to see if that is something that will work for their relationship.

Couple to Throuple premieres on February 8, but new episodes will stream weekly on Peacock.

February 9

Ticket to Paradise pg-13 Release Date September 20, 2022 Director Ol Parker Runtime 104 minutes Writers Ol Parker

Ticket to Paradise is actually making a return to Peacock for those who might have missed it the first time around. The movie initially streamed on Peacock 45 days after its theatrical release as part of Universal’s dual release strategy following the COVID-19 pandemic.

The movie sees Julia Roberts and George Clooney reunite as exes who must be civil to one another when their daughter surprises them with the news that she’s getting married to someone she met while on vacation in Bali. They believe she’s making a mistake and set out to sabotage the wedding with increasingly ridiculous schemes.

February 10

Three-Body (2023)

Inspired by the novel The Three-Body Problem, this Chinese series went through a long development process to get to the screen. The development began in 2008 - the same year the 2006 serialized story was published as a book. It took until 2020 to begin to assemble a cast and production team.

The science fiction series follows a detective as he tracks a series of apparent suicides in the scientific community. He eventually finds, however, that his investigations might not be that straightforward as there is a connection to an alien world depicted in a video game as well.

Netflix also has its own adaptation called 3 Body Problem which is set to release on March 21, 2024.

February 11

Love & Jane (2024)

Part of Hallmark’s Loveuary-themed month, Love & Jane follows a woman who is a Jane Austen fan and hopeless romantic. When she wishes to speak to Jane Austen, she magically gets her wish. Starring Hallmark staples Allison Sweeney and Benjamin Ayres, Love & Jane brings Austen into the modern day in a unique way - but it will only be available to stream for 72 hours.

February 12

Respect PG-13 Release Date August 13, 2021 Director Liesl Tommy Runtime 145 minutes Writers Tracey Scott Wilson

A movie about the life of Aretha Franklin had been in development for nearly two decades before production began on it. Franklin herself asked Jennifer Hudson to portray her after seeing Hudson’s performances in the Dreamgirls movie and The Color Purple Broadway musical. Respect chronicles Franklin’s journey as a young girl growing up singing in church to becoming a musical superstar in the 1970s.

February 14

Fruitvale Station (2013)

Quite a few Michael B. Jordan movies are added to Peacock in February. That Awkward Moment joins the steamer on February 1, while Creed is added on February 15, in addition to Fruitvale Station on Valentine’s Day. Each of the movies shows off Jordan’s impressive acting range, from rom-com to racially charged drama.

February 15

Caillou (Season 1) Creed (2015) Vigil (Season 2)

In addition to Creed, there’s a children’s animated program and a British drama series being added to Peacock on February 15. Caillou was a popular children’s series in the 1990s that got a reboot in 2023 and now has come to the streamer. Vigil, on the other hand, is a modern British series that follows an investigator who uncovers a conspiracy when she’s tasked with mysteries related to the Royal Navy and Air Force.

February 16

Oppenheimer R Release Date July 21, 2023 Director Christopher Nolan Runtime 150 Minutes Writers Christopher Nolan

One of the biggest new movies on Peacock this month is Oppenheimer. The Christopher Nolan movie has earned praise from critics and audiences alike for its incredible cinematography and stacked cast that includes Mark Ruffalo, Robert Downey Jr., Cillian Murphy, Florence Pugh, and Emily Blunt. The movie has been taking award season by storm, so movie fans who haven’t been able to see it in theaters will want to stream it.

February 18

An American in Austen (2024)

Another Hallmark addition for Loveuary, this is another unique take on a Jane Austen story. Most of Hallmark’s Jane Austen content has strived to do something new with the writer’s previous ideas instead of giving audiences another remake. Here, an American finds her love life stalling because she believes no man can live up to Austen’s Mr. Darcy. She gets the chance to find out when she’s magically transported into Pride & Prejudice.

February 20

A Taste of Love (2024)

Though this Loveuary features mostly Jane Austen content for Hallmark, this Hallmark romance strays from that set-up. Instead, it features regular Hallmark leading lady Erin Cahill (former Pink Ranger for Power Rangers: Time Force for those of a certain age) as a chef. When she finds herself dissatisfied with her work and pigeonholed, she moves back to her hometown for a break and some inspiration. She gets both while reconnecting with her childhood sweetheart.

February 23

Squealer (2024)

This 2024 thriller is definitely not one for those interested in the Hallmark offerings on Peacock this month. Lionsgate’s movie is inspired by real events and Candian murderer Robert Pickton. It follows a detective and social worker who start investigating young women who have been disappearing, only to find themselves led to a pig farm with some very disturbing results.

February 24

CrimeTime: Freefall (2024)

For mystery fans who want something a little less gruesome than Squealer, there is a Hallmark Movies & Mysteries offering in CrimeTime: Freefall. An actress who has starred in a crime series decides to take up life in a small town, only to become embroiled in a real-life mystery. She uses what she knows of mysteries from her work on television to team up with a local detective.

February 25

Sense & Sensibility (2024)

Among Hallmark’s many Loveuary movies, the network did decide to do a straight adaptation of one of Austen’s works after celebrating the author all month. Sense & Sensibility brings the story of Marianne and Elinor’s search for love to the network, but this time, the cast is made up largely of actors of color, potentially giving new layers to the story.

February 29

L’il Stompers (Season 1)

Like Caillou, L’il Stompers is also a remake for Peacock. It’s about a young group of dinosaur friends who spend their time exploring and learning to help one another, and it’s definitely for the preschool set. The series already has a handful of episodes on Peacock, but the rest of season 1 will be added to the streamer to close out February.

Also on Peacock throughout the month are new episodes of Days of Our Lives streaming almost daily. New episodes of television shows from NBC-Universal networks will also stream the day after they air. That means the Law & Order franchise, the Chicago franchise, and more, will have new episodes throughout February on Peacock.

True crime fans will also enjoy Peacock’s Dateline 24/7 channel. While the channel typically streams random episodes of Dateline, it does theme some episode sets around holidays. The days leading up to Valentine’s Day will feature crimes related to romance. With marathons like this one as well as numerous sports events streaming live on Peacock throughout the month, there’s something for everyone in February.
