30 Coins Season 2 Ending Explained The Biggest Reveals In The Finale

2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The season 2 finale of 30 Coins answers major questions and sets up a thrilling and shocking future for the series. Christian Barbrow successfully summons the Eye of God and takes his team to a new Earth, but only 30 followers can join him. Father Vergara is given a chance to save the world by Lucifer and must set aside old hatreds and loves to fulfill his mission.

Literature Review

30 Coins season 2 sees the introduction of new characters into the series and the finale answers many questions, opening the door for season 3.

The 30 Coins season 2 finale answered many major questions and set up where the series goes next in a thrilling and shocking episode. 30 Coins is a Spanish-language TV series available for streaming on Max that premiered its first season in November 2020. The supernatural mystery horror show follows Father Manuel Vergara (Eduard Fernández), an exorcist and ex-convict who begins experiencing paranormal phenomena in the small Spanish town of Pedraza. While Netflix seems to have a stranglehold on international horror, 30 Coins shows other streamers have life in the genre.

Religion factors heavily into 30 Coins, the title being a reference to the payment Judas received for betraying Jesus in the Bible. Season 1 saw Vergara and his allies uncover a supernatural conspiracy and season 2 ratchets up the sci-fi with an impending apocalypse and the introduction of a callous billionaire, Christian Barbrow, played by The Holdovers star Paul Giamatti. Science fiction, religion, horror, and adventure all come together in 30 Coins season 2, episode 8, “The Eye of God” (El ojo de Dios), and key characters are set on new and dangerous paths.

Christian Barbrow Succeeds And Takes His Team To New Earth

Billionaire and cult leader Christian Barbrow has been cultivating power and followers all season, which ends with a demonic summoning ceremony. It’s been Christian’s intention to summon “The Eye of God” and through scientific research and studying ancient tomes and religious relics, he has acquired everything he needs for the ritual. Christian has built a trans-dimensional spacer ship that will take him and his followers to a new version of Earth once the Eye is summoned, protecting them from the apocalypse that will occur after. He fails to mention to his followers that only 30 of their number will fit.

He is nearly stopped by Antonio (Javier Bódalo) who finds the quantum supercomputer responsible for sending Christian the coordinates to this new Earth. However, Antonio and his team arrive too late and Christian obtains the last piece of information he needs before causing the end of this world. Christian and his small army unceremoniously slaughter all but 30 lucky followers, and he summons the Eye of God. This monstrous visage begins to destroy the Earth and Christian jumps through the trans-dimensional rift, both ending the world and saving some followers.

It’s never been made clear how exactly Christian knows everything he does. There are darkly comedic moments in 30 Coins season 2 when it’s hinted that Christian is completely unsure of what will happen when he summons the Eye of God. So it comes as a bit of a surprise when not only does his summoning work, but his spacecraft actually transports his followers safely to a new reality.

Elena Frees Paco From Mercedes’ Mind Control

In 30 Coins season 2, Elena (Megan Montaner) has been trying to snap her lover Paco (Miguel Ángel Silvestre) out of whatever trance he’s been under. Unbeknownst to Elena, Paco’s ex-wife, Mercedes (Macarena Gómez), has developed superpowers thanks to her possession of one of Judas’ coins. She has the ability to manipulate a person’s mind, and she has put Paco in a trance so that she and he can travel to a new reality and start a life together, with him as Mercedes’ puppet.

Elena sneaks onto the spacecraft and briefly fights Paco, who attempts to kill her, firmly under Mercedes’ control. As a last-ditch effort, Elena kisses Paco, which snaps him out of his brainwashing. 30 Coins borrows a lot of its storytelling from fairytales and the kiss that Elena uses to reawaken Paco may seem like a Deux ex machine, but is actually a clever reversal of the “true love’s kiss” trope seen in many fairytales where a dashing prince revives his unconscious lover.

Paco and Elena find that the spaceship is already headed toward the transdimensional rift, and they hide themselves in ancient space suits, praying they survive the jump. Mercedes is furious at losing her slave and vows to get Paco back, suggesting that even if Paco and Elena survive the trip, they will still have her to contend with.

Lucifer Encourages Father Vergara To Reverse The Apocalypse

Father Vergara has spent season 2 trying to redeem himself after being manipulated by Lucifer (Cosimo Fusco) and Cardinal Santoro (Manolo Solo) and even sent to Hell himself. 30 Coins season 2 sees Vergara raised back from the dead, looking the worse for wear but with all his mental faculties. Lucifer wants Santoro and Vergara to stop Christian and avoid the apocalypse, seeing that a world without people would be a world he has no power over. Santoro reneges on the deal, joining Christian; while Vergara fails to stop Christian, Lucifer offers him another chance.

Taking his favorite form of a businessman named Angelo, Lucifer tells Vergara not to give up hope and that there is still a way to save the world. Lucifer gives Vergara the power to jump dimensions, and he sends the priest through the rift to go after Christian and stop the apocalypse. When Vergara lands in the new reality, he enters a Pedraza that is flourishing, unlike the destroyed one he came from. He sees doubles of friends and allies in this new world and understands that saving his Earth will require setting aside old hatreds and loves.

Vergara discovered in season 1 that the Vatican was an immoral system that had only its own interests at heart. Lost and alone in season 2, Vergara only went along with Lucifer because their interests aligned. However, at the end of season 2, it appears that the only person to be truthful with Vergara is the Devil. It makes for an interesting lesson that the disgraced priest finds an ally in the most unlikely of sources. Everything he has previously believed in has proven to be false, and Vergara has to be willing to trust again to save the world.

This is Paul Giamatti’s second HBO series after his starring role as the titular character in the 2008 miniseries John Adams.

The Real Meaning Of 30 Coins Season 2’s Ending

30 Coins season 3 is poised to be a different show after the season 2 finale. Vergara finds himself in a place that is both familiar and completely new at the same time, and the trials he has gone through on Earth and in Hell have prepared him for whatever comes next. 30 Coins places a heavy emphasis on cosmic horror and the finale shows how dangerous dealing with these forces can be. Innocent people are killed, homes are destroyed, and a priest and the Devil find a common interest in protecting humanity in the 30 Coins season 2 finale.
