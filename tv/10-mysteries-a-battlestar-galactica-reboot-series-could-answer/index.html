10 Mysteries A Battlestar Galactica Reboot Series Could Answer

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The Battlestar Galactica reboot has the potential to clarify the identity and nature of the show’s concept of “God,” which was left ambiguous in the 2004 series. The reboot could finally provide an explanation for the return of Starbuck after her ship exploded, addressing one of the biggest mysteries from the previous series. The fate of the Centurions and the reason behind the disappearance of technology in the Battlestar Galactica universe are other unanswered questions that the reboot can potentially address.

Literature Review

Peacock’s upcoming Battlestar Galactica reboot series could answer massive questions and solve big mysteries from the 2004 reboot series.

The Battlestar Galactica reboot could finally answer many of the series’ biggest mysteries. In 2004, SyFy rebooted the 1978 series Battlestar Galactica, starting with a miniseries and developing into a four-season TV show. The reboot series is widely considered one of the best sci-fi shows of all time due to its impact on the genre. Thanks to streaming, the series has gained a wider fanbase in the years since going off the air. In 2019, Peacock provided confirmation of an upcoming Battlestar Galactica reboot for their streaming platform. This series could modernize the show for a whole new generation of audiences while exploring the lives of multiple characters from the 2004 reboot series. Additionally, depending on when in the timeline the Battlestar Galactica reboot is set, it could answer many lingering mysteries that haunt fans. Where to watch Battlestar Galactica

10 What Battlestar Galactica’s “God” Actually Is The Peacock Reboot Could Expand On The Religious Aspects Of Battlestar Galactica Season 4

Battlestar Galactica season 4 took a turn towards religion in a way that many fans didn’t appreciate or enjoy. By the divisive ending of Battlestar Galactica, the series confirmed that God exists and has a plan. However, the show never elaborates on this further. The reboot has the potential to clarify many aspects of “God.” Battlestar Galactica’s God could be a physical deity or a sentient energy. If this God is a physical deity, there are a variety of versions on Earth that they could align with. It’s not guaranteed that God is the one referred to in Abrahamic religions. Additionally, God could be something entirely new. In this case, God’s motivations, values, and personality can be explored further in the Battlestar Galactica reboot so that audiences can understand their role in the show’s universe.

9 How Starbuck Returned From The Dead (If She Did) This Massive Battlestar Galactica Season 4 Mystery Could Finally Be Solved

In Battlestar Galactica season 3, episode 17, “Maelstrom,” Starbuck’s ship explodes during a conflict with a Cylon vessel. However, three episodes later, she and her ship appear undamaged, proclaiming she’s working in the service of God. The show never specifies whether she’s alive, dead, an angel, a ghost, or even God themselves – the last of which has become the subject of speculation in recent fan theories. The Battlestar Galactica reboot needs to clarify how exactly Starbuck survives because it’s a highly debated topic from the 2004 reboot series. While Ronald D. Moore denied it on the official BSG podcast, Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica reboot could make the Starbuck Cylon theory canon, explaining how she survived the battle in “Maelstrom.” If she didn’t survive the crash, the writers also need to clarify this, explaining how she appeared in a corporeal form to the rest of the crew.

8 What Happened To The Freed Centurions After BSG? The Centurions Could Return To Destroy Humanity

The Humanoid Cylons free the “Red Stripes” – self-aware Cylon Centurions working with the colonial forces – after the Battle of the Colony. These Centurions are given control of the Cylon Rebels’ basestar and then jump into deep space on their own. Ellen Tigh and Admiral William Adama acknowledge when freeing them that the Centurions could someday come back to destroy humanity. The Centurions’ fate for Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica reboot. It’s possible they die off or start a civilization elsewhere. Alternatively, if they come back to attack civilization, they can serve as the source of conflict for the reboot.

7 How BSG’s Technology Disappeared The Missing Technology Creates A Massive Plothole That The Reboot Can Address

One of the more confusing aspects of Battlestar Galactica’s ending is how the Earth takes almost 150,000 years to develop technology after the Colonialists decide to stay there. While the Colonialists destroy their entire fleet in the sun, there’s no logical explanation given for why the Earth doesn’t possess evidence of the Colonialists’ life prior to settling there. The show doesn’t give an answer to why they can’t help technology develop faster on Earth or keep some of their prior technology. In order to solve this mystery, the Battlestar Galactica reboot can work an explanation into the plot or dialogue.

6 Why Is Love Required For Cylon-Human Reproduction? Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica Reboot Can Explain The Biology Of Humans And Cylons

The Cylons cannot reproduce with each other, so they turn to humans with whom they are physically compatible. This leads to one of the most horrific events in Battlestar Galactica. Cylons create farms where all female humans of childbearing age are forced under sedation to get pregnant through artificial insemination. They also allow volunteers to have sex with a Cylon in hopes of carrying a human-Cylon hybrid pregnancy to term. None of their experiments work, though, with the only human-Cylon child coming from sex in which both parties were in love. The Battlestar Galactica reboot needs to clarify some of the specifics relating to human-Cylon reproduction. If love is a genetic requirement, that would have likely passed on to Hera, the human-Cylon hybrid from whom all humans on Earth come. In which case, the show should explain why this isn’t still a requirement for humans 150,000 years in the future. If love isn’t genetically required, the Battlestar Galactica reboot needs to explain why none of the other pregnancies succeeded.

5 The Reason The Cylon Models Are Limited Battlestar Galactica Never Explains The Number Of Cylons

One thing that the Battlestar Galactica reboot needs to address is why exactly the Cylons limited the number of humanoid models. The series includes twelve Cylon models, excluding Daniel. This includes the eight numbered models – since the Sevens were wiped out – and the Final Five. They likely would have had more success with their mysterious plan if they had more operatives, making it confusing why they limited themselves. It’s been speculated that religiosity and superstition are the reasons why Battlestar Galactica only had twelve Cylon models. After all, there were thirteen Tribes of Kobol in the series. However, this is never confirmed within Battlestar Galactica, making it seem illogical that they’d limit themselves in such a way. Number One destroys the Sevens in Battlestar Galactica by poisoning their amniotic fluid and corrupting the genetic code.

4 Why Are All The Corners Cut Off Every Piece Of Paper? Props Became The Source Of One Of The Biggest Battlestar Galactica Mysteries

While it may seem minor in comparison to other unanswered questions in Battlestar Galactica, one of the biggest mysteries throughout the fandom is why every piece of paper has its corners cut off. The production team could have just left the mystery unaddressed, but executive producer Ronald Moore fanned the flames in a 2009 interview with National Geographic when he called it the “Da Vinci Code of Battlestar Galactica.” Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica reboot could make the fandom extremely happy by finally providing an explanation for the papers with the corners cut off.

3 The Identity Of The Y Chromosomal Adam With Whom Did Hera Have Children?

Battlestar Galactica establishes that Hera is the Mitochondrial Eve – the oldest matrilineal ancestor of all living humans on Earth. This raises the question of the identity of Y Chromosomal Adam – the oldest patrilineal ancestor of all living humans on Earth. Since Eve is the only human-Cylon baby known to exist, the identity of the Y Chromosomal Adam would probably be a human or Cylon with whom she reproduced. Alternatively, it could be a primitive human on Earth. The Battlestar Galactica reboot could address this mystery by making Hera the main character.

2 What Was The Cylons’ Plan In BSG? The Battlestar Galactica Reboot Could Fill In A Plothole

One of the harsh realities of watching Battlestar Galactica two decades later is that, despite what the opening credits advertised, the Cylons never had a plan. Ronald D. Moore even confirmed this at the 2017 ATX Television Festival. However, this is wildly unsatisfying for fans. Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica reboot could take inspiration from Rogue One’s role in the Star Wars franchise by introducing a well-thought-out retcon that finally gives the Cylons a plan. This would finally fulfill the promise made by the 2004 reboot’s opening credits.

1 The Source Of The Final Five’s Song (And Why Starbuck Could Hear It) Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica Reboot Can Explain The Final Five Song

In Battlestar Galactica, the Final Five begin hearing a fragmented song and lyrics that bring back their memories of being a Cylon. While they learn that Anders wrote it, they never learn the source of it in their heads. It’s later revealed that Starbuck also knows the song from when she was little, making her the only human to be capable of hearing the song. This raises two major questions that the reboot needs to address. Peacock’s Battlestar Galactica reboot needs to explain who or what sent out the signal of the Final Five’s song. This can’t be ignored like it was the first time around. Secondly, the show needs to explain why Starbuck could hear the song. If she isn’t a Cylon, as Moore asserts, then they need to provide an answer as to why she can hear it when other humans can’t.
