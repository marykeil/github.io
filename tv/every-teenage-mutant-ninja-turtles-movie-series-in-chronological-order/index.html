Every Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles Movie & Series (In Chronological Order)

SUMMARY

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem, a new animated movie, is set to be released in 2023, featuring a teenage version of the turtle heroes and an underground world of mutants. The Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles have a rich history, from their comic book origins to their numerous TV series and movies spanning over 35 years. The franchise includes classic adaptations like the 1990 live-action film and the popular 2003 animated series, as well as newer takes such as the 2014 and 2016 live-action movies and the 2018 animated series, Rise of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles.

Literature Review

Keeping track of all the TMNT movies in order can be tricky as the franchise has had over a dozen screen adaptations.

With Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem out this summer, now is a great time for fans to revisit all the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles movies in order. From the pages of comic books to the big screen, Earth’s favorite crime-fighting anthropomorphic turtles love only one thing more than ridding the world of scum: pizza. Named after famous painters from the Italian Renaissance, these four sewer-dwelling, skateboarding heroes have appeared in a multitude of TV series and films over the years. Michelangelo, Donatello, Raphael, and Leonardo all maintain distinct personalities, but they share the same origin story. After being exposed to a toxic mutagen underneath New York City, these normal reptiles quickly evolved into human-sized martial arts experts. With the help of their sensei Splinter and a journalist named April O’Neil, the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles keep the universe’s evil forces at bay. Thirty-five years after their first television appearance, there are many Teenage Mutant Nina Turtles movies and TV shows involving the heroes in a half-shell.

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: 15 Best Quotes From The Original Trilogy With the original trilogy of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles remaining as popular as ever, fans might want to check out some of their best quotes.

17 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (1987 - 1996) 10 Seasons, 193 Episodes

Release Date December 14, 1987 Cast Cam Clarke , Barry Gordon , Rob Paulsen , townsend coleman , James Avery , Pat Fraley , Jennifer Darling , Tony Jay , Peter Renaday , Renae Jacobs Seasons 10

Stream on Paramount+

The very first screen adaptation of the TMNT is this cartoon classic, a syndicated series that ran for 10 seasons and includes seven special broadcasts. The show was a classic Saturday morning cartoon that an entire generation of kids grew up loving and that offered a kid-friendly introduction to the Ninja Turtles. While it veers somewhat from its comic book source material, the show focuses on the TMNT’s relationship with April O’Neil, Splinter, and their mortal enemy, Shredder. The series predates the first live-action movie by three years.

16 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (1990) The First Live-Action Movie For The TMNT

Stream on Paramount+

Thanks to the popularity of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles cartoon and its correlated line of toys, Jim Henson’s Creature Shop brought the Turtles to the big screen with some TMNT live-action suits that recreated the classic heroes much to the thrill of young fans. This first film sees the TMNT crew crossing paths with Shredder and his Foot Clan for the first time while also meeting April. Despite its poor critical reception, the movie was a major box office success, becoming one of the most successful independent movies of all time.

15 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles II: The Secret Of The Ooze (1991) The 1991 Sequel To The Original TMNT Movie

Stream on Paramount+

The Secret of the Ooze capitalized on the success of the original and attempted to launch the live-action franchise. The sequel is less violent than its predecessor. Instead of weapons, the Turtles spend more time fighting using bare-fisted martial arts techniques. It also provides more backstory into the origins of the Turtles and Splinter while introducing Shredder’s nefarious associates, Tokka and Rahzar. Perhaps the charm of seeing the turtles brought to life was fading on audiences as the sequel was a step-down.

14 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles III (1993) The Final Release In The Original TMNT Movie Franchise

Stream on Paramount+

The third and final film in the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles’ original theatrical run. Like the previous movie, this sequel uses the All Effects Company for the Turtles instead of Jim Henson’s Creature Shop. However, the lower budget is evident with the designs of the turtles feeling a lot less convincing. Considered the worst in the original line of films, TMNT III follows the Turtles on a time-traveling trip to feudal Japan, where they rub elbows with samurai and make both new friends and enemies.

13 Mutant Turtles: Superman Legend (1996) 1 Season, 2 Episodes

Not available to stream

Released in conjunction with a new line of toys, Mutant Turtles is a two-part Japanese special. The episodes recast the Turtles as next-level superheroes fighting a troop of extra-powerful supervillains led by Shredder. They gain these additional abilities with the help of a spirit trapped inside a magical MutaStone.

TMNT: Shredder’s Real Life Origin Story Is Even Weirder Than The Comics The nemesis of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, Shredder is a formidable villain, but the inspiration for the character came from an unlikely place.

12 Ninja Turtles: The Next Mutation (1997 - 1998) 1 Season, 26 Episodes

Buy on Apple TV & Prime Video

With the first cartoon and the three live-action films under its belt, the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles returned to TV in 1997, this time for a live-action series. The Next Mutation picks up where the movies leave off. The TMNTs meet a female Turtle, Venus de Milo, as well as a new villain: Dragonlord. The low budget hampers a lot of the movies as the cheap costumes are distracting. However, it is a fun and overlooked addition to the legacy of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles that fans of the franchise will want to check out.

11 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2003 - 2009) 7 Seasons, 155 Episodes

Stream on Paramount+

Fox originally aired the second animated TMNT series, which ran for seven seasons. The cartoon’s style and plot are more in line with the original Mirage comics, ignoring many of the family-friendly changes in the films. The show deals with more adult themes, even venturing into science fiction and fantasy territory. Despite the tone change, the series continued to capture the spirit of these characters and the humor that audiences have come to expect. The result is a solid cartoon that served as a new generation’s introduction to this franchise.

10 TMNT (2007) The First Big-Budget TMNT Blockbuster Movie

Stream on Paramount+

The Turtles returned to the big screen with this CGI film, featuring voice work from some of some big names, including Chris Evans, Sarah Michelle Gellar, and Patrick Stewart. In TMNT, the Turtles have fallen out after defeating Shredder once and for all, but they join forces again in NYC where a new evil threatens humanity. The new updated look of the characters showed how long these characters had been around and that they were changing with the styles of the day. Full of action and campy one-liners, TMNT did very well in theaters.

10 Animal Superheroes Not From Marvel Or DC DC League Of Super-Pets is bringing animals superheroes back to the big screen, but plenty already exist outside of the Marvel and DC bubble.

9 Turtles Forever (2009) The TV Movie Based On The 2003 Animated Series

Rent on Apple TV & Prime Video

The legacy of the Ninja Turtles can certainly be seen by how many young fans through the years are drawn to the franchise and its success after decades in pop culture was acknowledged in this fun adventure. Turtles Forever is a made-for-TV movie that celebrates the 25th anniversary of the franchise. Produced by 4Kids Entertainment, it also serves as the finale for the 2003 Fox series. In the film, the Turtles discover they have doppelgångers, “imposters” who turn out to be the original 1987 gang. The two groups work together to understand how their worlds have combined as one enemy after another pursues them and their special mutagen. Long before multiverse movies like The Flash used legacy casting, this combining of timelines was a thrill for fans.

8 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2012 - 2017) 5 Seasons, 124 Episodes

Stream on Paramount+

After acquiring the rights to the Turtles, Nickelodeon launched this CGI series that lasted for five seasons. Designed to look like an anime, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles focuses more on the Kraang’s mutagen than other entries in the franchise; April O’Neil returns, but this time in teenage form. The next jump in animation style for the Ninja Turtles continued to prove that audiences were willing to follow them throughout all these new looks so long as the essence of what makes their characters great was maintained.

7 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014) The First Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles Summer Blockbuster

PG-13

Release Date August 8, 2014 Director Jonathan Liebesman Cast Megan Fox , Will Arnett , Jeremy Howard , Johnny Knoxville , Alan Ritchson , Noel Fisher , Tony Shalhoub , Tohoru Masamune , Minae Noji Runtime 101 Minutes

Stream on Prime Video After Michael Bay’s Transformers movies, he decided to produce a new take on another 1980s nostalgic property with the live-action reboot of Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. Megan Fox stars as journalist April O’Neil, who follows the Turtles through the sewers and streets of New York City. In another technological step that the characters have been through, the Turtles themselves are brought to life thanks to motion-capture technology. It was the first time the Ninja Turtles headlined a summer blockbuster.

6 Half-Shell Heroes: Blast To The Past (2015) An Animated Movie Released By Nickelodeon

Stream on Paramount+

A movie-length special released by Nickelodeon while its Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles series aired, the Turtles travel back to the Cretaceous period in Blast to the Past. Once there, they are forced to deal with new enemies from the days of dinosaurs. The film came in conjunction with a fresh line of dinosaur toys from Playmates. The cuter design of the characters is aimed more at younger audiences, returning the franchise to a family-friendly vibe that was not present in the Bay-produced live-action movies.

5 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out Of The Shadows (2016) The Sequel To The 2014 Live-Action TMNT Movie

PG-13

Release Date June 3, 2016 Director Dave Green Cast Stephen Amell , Megan Fox , Tyler Perry , Will Arnett , Laura Linney , Noel Fisher , Jeremy Howard , Pete Ploszek , Alan Ritchson , Brian Tee , Stephen Farrelly , Gary Anthony Williams , Brad Garrett , Tony Shalhoub Runtime 88 Minutes

Stream on Paramount+

A sequel to the 2014 TMNT movie, Michael Bay’s Turtles and Megan Fox return for another martial arts-inspired romp in Out of the Shadows. Shredder escapes custody and works with a mad scientist to take over the world. The sequel introduces a variety of elements from the franchise that were missing from the first movie, including Krang, Beebop, Rocksteady, and Baxter Stockman. This follow-up was a box-office bomb, prompting Nickelodeon Movies to scrap a third movie. To date, this is the last live-action Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles movie.

4 Rise Of The Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2018-2020) 2 Seasons, 39 Episodes

Animation

Release Date July 20, 2018 Cast Omar Benson Miller , Josh Brener Seasons 2

Stream on Paramount+

A lighter and more comedic series, Rise of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles is Nickelodeon’s second cartoon. Two seasons aired, and it reimagines the Turtles and their adventures in NYC. They gain new duds, new weapons, and new abilities while hanging around the normal cast of characters, such as Shredder, April, and Splinter. This feels like the Ninja Turtles attempting to cement itself more firmly in the superhero genre with choices, such as the new powers, that did not sit well with all fans of the franchise.

3 Batman Vs. Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2019)

Rent on Apple TV & Prime Video

Despite their strange origins, the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles have managed to grow into a massive fixture of pop culture to the extent that they have earned the right to share the screen with one of the most beloved heroes in pop culture. The only TMNT crossover, Batman vs. Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles sees the Caped Crusader requesting help from the Turtles in Gotham City. It turns out Shredder has joined forces with one of Batman’s classic villains, Ra’s al Ghul. Batgirl and Robin also make appearances in this direct-to-video feature.

2 Rise Of The Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: The Movie (2022) A Movie Based Off The 2018 TMNT Series

TV-Y7

Release Date August 5, 2022 Director andy suriano , Ant Ward Cast Ben Schwartz , Omar Benson Miller , Josh Brener , Kat Graham , Eric Bauza , Haley Joel Osment , Brandon Mychal Smith Runtime 82 minutes

Stream on Netflix Rise of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: The Movie arrived on Netflix to continue the story from the 2018 series of the same name. The movie will bring back the show’s cast and picks up two years after they defeated Shredder. This time around, the story follows Leonardo as the leader of the Turtles as he has to save the world from the alien villains called the Krang.

1 Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem (2023) The 2023 Theatrical Animated Movie From Seth Rogen

PG

Release Date August 4, 2023 Director Jeff Rowe Cast Nicolas Cantu , Micah Abbey , Shamon Brown Jr. , Brady Noon , Jackie Chan , Ayo Edebiri , Seth Rogen , John Cena Runtime 100 Minutes

Stream on Paramount+

A brand-new TMNT movie arrived in 2023 with Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem by Seth Rogen and Evan Goldberg. It is also notable for actually depicting the turtle heroes as teenagers. While fighting crime in New York City and meeting April O’Neil, the turtles discover an underground world of other mutants. Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem looks to be inspired by the animation style of Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse while also featuring a lot of fun casting like Jackie Chan as Splinter.
