Industry Season 2 Episode 3 Recap Harper Discovers A Lucrative Trade, Yasmin Fights With Her Father

SUMMARY

Industry season 2, episode 3 explores the theme of inclusion and exclusion, particularly in Harper’s storyline. The episode highlights the clash between traditional and new approaches, represented by Eric, Felim, and Jesse. Sobriety plays a significant role, as Kenny and Rocco bond over their shared struggles while Yasmin’s substance abuse isolates her further.

Literature Review

Industry season 2, episode 3 “The Fool” sees characters boxed out of the spaces they need to operate in and broken relationships creating problems.

Industry season 2, episode 3 “The Fool” sees many of the main characters dealing with crumbling relationships that are only now coming back to bite them. Feeling like a British cross between Succession and Billions, Industry is a BBC and HBO series that follows the employees of Pierpoint & Co, an investment company in London. An ensemble cast of young people looking to make their names in the industry and banking veterans who waver between lifting the new employees up and pushing them down rounds out an exciting, funny, and soapy primetime drama.

Season 2 of Industry premiered in August 2022 and shows the Pierpoint employees returning to offices after COVID-19. One of the series’ main characters, Harper, played by Myha’la Herrold of the cast of Dumb Money, meets a new character introduced this season, Jesse Bloom (Jay Duplass), a hedge fund manager who is now Harper’s main client. In episode 2, “The Giant Squid”, Harper and Eric (Ken Leung) get themselves caught between two major clients, Jesse and Felim Bichan (Andrew Buchanan). Season 2, episode 3 sees many characters having to contend with broken relationships and being in the out-group looking in.

Eric, Harper, Felim, And Jesse Go Pheasant Shooting In Wales

At the end of season 2, episode 2, Jesse has covered for Harper’s mistake by buying up the Rican healthcare stocks that were supposed to go to Felim. So now, Eric, Harper, Felim, and Jesse all go to a pheasant shooting weekend in Wales to celebrate Felim purchasing the stock from Jesse. Originally, Pierpoint only intended Felim to go, but thanks to Eric and Harper’s mistake, Jesse and his American contingent come along and the differences between American and British culture are mocked by both hedge fund managers as they compete against one another.

Harper and Jesse are woefully inadequate shooters. Jesse even accidentally shoots buckshot at Felim at one point. Worse, Harper finds herself increasingly sidelined as the weekend goes on. Despite Eric leveling with Harper for once when he asks her to help him get Jesse to sell his Rican stock to Felim, Harper finds herself kept out of meetings and sequestered to a small room during the pitch. Harper finally confronts Eric, only to have him turn on her, reminding her that she works for him and dashing any expectations she’s had about being brought further into the fold.

Yasmin’s Father Embarrasses Her But She Still Calls Him

Back in the Pierpoint offices, Yasmin (Marissa Abella), attempts to get her wealthy father, Charles (Adam Levy), to put their family money in the care of Pierpoint. She sits down with her dad and Celeste (Katrine De Candole), another Wealth Manager, to pitch him, only for her father to admit that he never intended to give his money to Pierpoint, humiliating Yasmin and wasting Celeste’s time. Charles simply was not interested in investing the money, but Yasmin takes it personally, and before he leaves the building, tearfully berates him for making her look terrible.

Yasmin and her father have always had a contentious relationship in Industry, but season 2, episode 3, makes for one of their more awkward scenes yet. Despite this, the end of the episode sees Yasmin, drunk and on drugs, calling her father’s voicemail, and telling him she misses him, not once but twice. When her plans are foiled at every step, Yasmin still has only one person to turn to, even if he disappoints her.

Rocco And Kenny Find Kinship In AA And Crypto

After failing with her father, Yasmin still hopes to appease Celeste, so she calls Rocco (Pasquale Esposito), the son of Celeste’s wealthy Italian client. Yasmin invites Rocco to dinner, but he only wants to talk about Crypto, a subject she’s unfamiliar with. Fortunately, her coworker (and source of anguish in the series) Kenny (Conor MacNeill) does know Crypto, and so she invites him to dinner as well. There, Kenny and Rocco notice they each have Alcoholics Anonymous tattoos, and the two quickly bond over their sobriety, while Yasmin sinks back drinking wine, unable to speak their language.

Yasmin knows over five languages, is talented at foreign exchange sales, has huge ambition, and comes from a very wealthy family that can finance her no matter her situation. Despite all these advantages, Yasmin has frequently found herself on the outside looking in, in Industry. Even when she sets up a situation for herself to succeed, she gets pushed out, unable to speak the language actually required. To add insult to injury, Celeste doesn’t appreciate Yasmin trying to help, seeing her bringing in Kenny as a net negative.

Yasmin And Robert Nearly Get Together

Yasmin fails twice, spectacularly, in Industry season 2, episode 3, and so she retreats to her coworker Robert’s (Harry Lawtey) house, pretending she’s searching for Harper. Robert lets her in, and they talk as Yasmin snorts cocaine and drinks. Robert, for his part, does not partake. The two bring up what it would have been like if they had slept together in the past, and for a moment, it appears that the episode is leading to that ending. Industry goes a different route, and the pair burst out laughing, content for now to be each other’s fair-weather friends.

Harper Discovers A Better Trade For Jesse

Despite being kept out of the important conversations during the pheasant hunt, Harper manages to learn one critical piece of information: Rican Healthcare will not be able to bring down costs and increase access to healthcare in the U.S., meaning that the U.K. market will never approve it. Rican is trying to conceal this so that the sale of the stocks from Jesse to Felim goes off without a hitch. Harper realizes that if Jesse keeps his shares and purchases a controlling stake in Rican, he will suffer a minor short-term loss and realize a major long-term one.

The next morning, Jesse never shows up to meet Felim, and, unknown to anyone but Jesse, Harper has returned to the Pierpoint offices. There, with Rishi’s (Sagar Radia) help, she executes the sale of a portion of Rican to Jesse, ensuring the American hedge fund manager now has the chance to be a real changemaker in U.S. and U.K. healthcare.

The Real Meaning Of Industry Season 2, Episode 3’s Ending

Industry season 2, episode 3 “The Fool” features many characters struggling to weave their way into conversations, relationships, and deals. The idea of this in-group vs out-group mentality is part of every storyline, no more so than in Harper’s, where the young analyst is left out of everything. It isn’t until she finds a helpful piece of information that she’s treated as useful. Felim and Eric’s ideas of tradition are also at odds with Jesse’s idea that nothing is sacred, showing a division between class, new money/old money, and culture.

Sobriety also factors heavily into this Industry episode. Kenny and Rocco quickly get along because of their sobriety and, at the same time, push Yasmin into the out-group. Yasmin frequently imbibes and takes drugs, and she is often the only one partaking. Her substance abuse may be her way of trying to loosen herself up to fit in with the movers and shakers of Industry, but she only further alienates herself.
