All 10 Times Grogu Uses The Force In The Mandalorian & Why

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Grogu demonstrates impressive Force abilities, including healing, levitating objects, and using Force push and pull for various reasons in The Mandalorian. His growing bond with Din Djarin drives him to use the Force to protect his friends and loved ones in dangerous situations. Grogu’s journey in The Mandalorian showcases his development as a Force-user, from small acts like stealing cookies to saving lives.

Literature Review

Grogu has proven he’s one of Star Wars’ most powerful Force users yet, and here are 10 times and reasons why he uses his powers in The Mandalorian.

Grogu used the Force to perform epic feats 10 times in The Mandalorian, and this is why. When Grogu was first introduced in The Mandalorian, he was nicknamed “Baby Yoda” by viewers. This nickname fits for many reasons, as he is not only the third canon member of Yoda’s rare and unknown species, but he is also a powerful Force-user. While he is still the equivalent of a preschooler and thus not nearly as practiced in the Force as Yoda, he still has impressive abilities that he shows off several times during the show. Grogu uses the Force in small ways fairly frequently throughout the show - for example, to push himself around in a spinning chair and steal cookies. However, there are a few times when he shows just how powerful he truly is. He usually has his most epic displays of power in emergencies. If Din Djarin in particular is in danger, Grogu uses his love and attachment to tap into the Force. Ever since Grogu returned from two years of training with Luke Skywalker, his adeptness with the Force has noticeably increased. Here’s every time Grogu has used the Force thus far, and why.

Grogu’s 9 Force Powers In Star Wars So Far
Grogu’s Force powers in The Mandalorian keep growing stronger every season. He’s used at least 9 different Force powers so far, with more to come.

10 Grogu Saves Din Djarin From The Mudhorn Season 1, Episode 2 “Chapter 2: The Child”

Force Power Used Force Levitate

In “Chapter 2: The Child,” a group of Jawas steal parts off of Din Djarin’s ship. This, of course, poses a significant problem for him and Grogu. After all, he just went back on his bounty-hunting deal and rescued Grogu. The two were on the run from some very dangerous people. As such, they go with Kuiil to try to get the parts back from the Jawas. The Jawas insist they will only return the parts if Din Djarin gets them a Mudhorn egg. With no other choice, he agrees. This, of course, leads to a furious mother Mudhorn. Mudhorns are powerful creatures. Similar to a rhinoceros with an exaggerated horn, they have tough skin, weigh an incredible amount, and are known to charge. Din Djarin is understandably losing the fight. Knocked to the ground with the Mudhorn about to crush him, Grogu reaches out to the Force and lifts it off the ground. This is a powerful moment because it shows the developing bond between the two. Grogu has just been rescued by Din Djarin, and he’s grateful for that, so when he sees Din Djarin is the one in trouble, he uses the Force to protect him.

9 Grogu Chokes Cara Dune For Arm Wrestling Din Djarin Season 1, Episode 7 “Chapter 7: The Reckoning”

Force Power Used Force Choke

When Din Djarin recruits former Rebel shock trooper Cara Dune, to help protect Grogu on Nevarro, the two of them engage in a friendly arm wrestling match. Grogu, however, doesn’t understand what’s happening. He believes that his (then not yet) adoptive father is in distress and that Cara Dune is trying to hurt him. In anger and fear, he Force-chokes Cara until his father explains that it’s okay. This moment was shocking because it was the first time Grogu had slipped up and used a dark side Force power. It was also interesting because, while his attachment to his father is what led him to slip up, it was his father who was able to pull him out of it and keep him from going too far. This moment shows Grogu’s power and that he suffers from the same temptation to the dark side at times that all Force-users do, but it also shows that Din Djarin is able to regulate him, and thus is a good mentor for him.

8 Grogu Chokes Moff Gideon’s Stormtroopers Season 2, Episode 6 “Chapter 14: The Tragedy”

Force Power Used Force Choke

When Moff Gideon manages to kidnap Grogu, he is furious. He tosses Moff Gideon’s stormtroopers angrily around his cell, Force-choking them. However, he seems to stop and contemplate for a split second about the ethics of what he’s doing - giving into the dark side - and releases the stormtroopers. This is a moment of growth for Grogu because, unlike with Cara when he had to be told to stop, he self-regulates. He realizes what he’s doing is wrong and chooses to turn from the dark side.

7 Grogu Heals Greef Karga Season 1, Episode 7 “Chapter 7: The Reckoning”

Force Power Used Force Heal

In “Chapter 7: The Reckoning,” Din Djarin and Grogu’s friend Greef Karga is scratched by a venomous reptavian while camping with Din Djarin, Grogu, Kuiil, and Cara on Nevarro, and Grogu saves his life using the Force. This is another example of Grogu accessing the Force in a more intense way to save someone he cares about. What’s remarkable about this is that, while he changes his mind and becomes their ally, up until that moment, Karga was planning on betraying Grogu and delivering him back to the Imperials - and Grogu’s act of kindness changes his mind.

6 Grogu Blocks A Flametrooper’s Flamethrower Season 1, Episode 8 “Chapter 8: Redemption”

Force Power Used Force Push & Pull

When IG-11, Cara Dune, Greef Karga, and Grogu are trapped by Moff Gideon and his troopers in a building on Nevarro, Gideon sends in a flametrooper to burn the building to the ground with them inside it. He hopes to recapture Grogu and eliminate his friends permanently. However, Grogu uses the Force to save all their lives, pushing the flamethrower’s flames back at the flametrooper. Once again, Grogu is most in touch with the Force when his friends are in danger, particularly when his father is just inches from death and unable to protect himself.

All 24 Episodes Of The Mandalorian, Ranked From Worst To Best
As Star Wars’ first live-action TV show, The Mandalorian is both historic and beloved. Here are all 24 of its episodes, ranked from worst to best.

5 Grogu Pushes Away Moff Gideon’s Weapon Season 3, Episode 8 “Chapter 24: The Return”

Force Power Used Force Push & Pull

In their final showdown with Moff Gideon on Mandalore, Grogu watches as Bo-Katan Kryze and Din Djarin fight with the Imperial warlord in his secret Imperial base. During the fight, Moff Gideon is disarmed. He reaches for his weapon, but Grogu uses the Force to push it off of a platform. This proves Grogu’s ability to use the Force in a fight without having to resort to dark side powers, and he only further demonstrates this later in the same fight.

4 Grogu Protects Din Djarin and Bo-Katan Kryze Season 3, Episode 8 “Chapter 24: The Return”

Force Power Used Force Push & Pull

This is arguably Grogu’s most impressive display of Force power yet. He protects himself, Bo-Katan, and Din Djarin from being consumed in the explosion that killed Moff Gideon by using a Force barrier. Once again, Grogu is moved to use the Force by a sense of love and protectiveness as opposed to fear and hatred.

3 Grogu Connects With The Force At The Ancient Temple On Tython Season 2, Episode 6 “Chapter 14: The Tragedy”

Force Power Used Force Meditation

During seasons 1 and 2, Din Djarin didn’t believe he was a fit parent for Grogu. He felt Grogu needed to be with his own kind - the Jedi. Under Ahsoka’s recommendation, he brings Grogu to the seeing stone in the ancient temple on the planet Tython. There, Grogu goes into deep meditation, reaching out to every Jedi in the galaxy. He is able to call Luke from planets away. In this case, the stones and the deeply Force-sensitive planet Tython itself enhance Grogu’s Force connection.

2 Grogu Jumps Over Ragnar Vizsla’s Head During Mandalorian Training Season 3, Episode 4 “Chapter 20: The Foundling”

Force Power Used Force Enhanced Athleticism

When the Mandalorian children are training, Din Djarin has Grogu join in. At first, Ragnar Vizsla is winning the fight. However, Din Djarin tells Grogu that it is okay to use his Force abilities, and encourages him to show them what he can do. He then uses the Force to jump over Ragnar’s head and uses his superior reflexes to win the duel. This is a very sweet moment because it shows Din Djarin helping Grogu to learn to use the Force voluntarily to win a fight. Before this, Grogu had struggled to use the Force on command. This shows both the progress he made with Luke and how his father’s encouragement continues to help him become more in tune with the Force.

1 Grogu Runs Around Moff Gideon’s Praetorian Guards Season 3, Episode 8 “Chapter 24: The Return”

Force Power Used Force Enhanced Athleticism

In the final fight with Moff Gideon, Grogu gets trapped alone in a room with Gideon’s Praetorian Guards. With his life at stake, he uses the Force to jump into the rafters. He flips and runs around the room, managing to keep out of their reach until his father is able to come to his aid. This is a both impressive and lighthearted display of Grogu’s Force ability, and it shows precisely how Grogu can now fend for himself in The Mandalorian, thanks to his brief training with Luke and his encouragement from Din Djarin.
