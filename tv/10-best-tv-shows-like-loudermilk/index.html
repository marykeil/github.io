10 Best TV Shows Like Loudermilk

SUMMARY

Shows like Loudermilk challenge viewers to root for difficult characters by showing that people are more than their mistakes and can change with support. Loudermilk’s acerbic protagonist, Sam Loudermilk, hides a caring heart behind layers of sarcasm and expletives, making him a complex and compelling character. The show tackles serious topics like addiction and uses sharp wit to navigate through the challenges of life, emphasizing the importance of relationships and empathy.

Literature Review

Sam Loudermilk might not be the most likable character on TV, but his curmudgeonly humor is frequently used in comedy-drama shows.

In recent years, shows like Loudermilk, which feature an acerbic protagonist with a troubled past, have become increasingly popular, leading many shows to follow the example of its unlikely hero’s journey. At first, watching Loudermilk leads a viewer to wonder why anyone would root for such difficult people with so few qualities to recommend them. However, as the show progresses, the answer becomes clear; a person is much more than their mistakes and anyone can change with the right support. The series aired for three seasons from 2017 to 2020 and followed the life of the titular character, Sam Loudermilk (Ron Livingston). He is a substance abuse counselor who is a recovering alcoholic himself and doesn’t take the traditional approach to helping others. Making inappropriate, rude, and angry comments to everyone around him, he has difficulty connecting with others and often prefers not to. However, it’s clear that underneath it all, he cares deeply about those in need and is hiding a heart behind layers of sarcasm and frequent expletives.

10 Curb Your Enthusiasm (2000 - 2024) Larry David blatantly disregards the social contract

Curb Your Enthusiasm
TV-MA Comedy

Release Date October 15, 2000 Cast Larry David , Jeff Garlin , Cheryl Hines , Susie Essman , J.B. Smoove Writers Jeff Schaffer , Larry David Network HBO Max Streaming Service(s) Max

Best known for his creation of the iconic sitcom, Seinfeld, Larry David stars in and created Curb Your Enthusiasm, which is a loosely autobiographical comedy that incorporates improv into many of its scenes. His character on the show is a heightened version of himself who refuses to follow the social conventions of any situation. This, of course, leads him to be widely disliked and frequently offend everyone around him. Despite David’s firm belief that he’s doing the right thing no matter what, the many guest stars on Curb all chafe against his cynical nature, much like Loudermilk and his co-stars.

9 Brockmire (2017 - 2020) A disgraced baseball announcer returns to the profession in the minor leagues

Hank Azaria carries Brockmire as Jim Brockmire, a disgraced former sports announcer who gets back in the saddle at a minor league baseball stadium he considers below him. However, the supporting cast is just as important to the dynamic of the show, juxtaposing Brockmire’s inflated sense of self with the grassroots nature of the minor league team. Brockmire, like Loudermilk, focuses on a man who is misunderstood but not irredeemable. He’s slowly brought out of his shell by unlikely friends and begins to understand that his status is not as important as his relationships.

8 Letterkenny (2016 - 2023) Small-town Canadians with quick wits and hilarious misadventures

Letterkenny
Sitcom Comedy

Release Date February 7, 2018 Cast Jared Keeso Writers Jared Keeso Streaming Service(s) Hulu Directors Jacob Tierney

Letterkenny has many funny quotes that revel in its unique brand of truly Canadian humor. The show takes place in a rural Canadian town full of residents who are stubborn in their own right, but not the stereotypical small-town characters that are frequently looked down upon on television. Shown through the fast-paced, quippy dialogue, the characters are intelligent and have nuanced opinions that rival those of characters in shows that take place in metropolitan areas. Like the recovering addicts in Loudermilk, the people of the town of Letterkenny are underestimated and assumed to be something they’re not. In response to this, both casts of characters are likely to lash out with harsh humor that puts them at odds with those around them. However, using this sharp wit at the right time and place is what sets them apart from others, and makes their lives so compelling to watch. Additionally, in the tight-knit community of the show, people can’t stay angry with each other for long and always end up going back to their usual banter.

7 Better Things (2016 - 2022) A single mother working in Los Angeles trying to reign in three daughters

Better Things
Comedy Drama

Release Date September 8, 2016 Cast Celia Imrie , Mikey Madison , Olivia Edward , Pamela Adlon , Hannah Alligood Seasons 5 Network FX Streaming Service(s) HBO Max , Apple TV+ , Hulu

Better Things was an important addition to the television landscape for how much it centered on mother-daughter relationships and the experience of womanhood across all stages of life. Most of the time, Sam (Pamela Adlon) is struggling to keep her head above water while trying to support her three daughters and her mother and find time to catch her breath. She isn’t shy about sharing how hard it is to be a single mom and copes with her daily stressors by lashing out at those around her with brutally honest opinions. Like Loudermilk, she’s far from a perfect person, but her relationship with her kids, or in Loudermilk’s case his sponsees, is what keeps her going. When told through the perspective of Sam or Loudermilk, the daily annoyances of life become all the most frustrating, and all the more hilarious. Both shows take on serious topics, like addiction, divorce, and money, and use them as tools to communicate hard truths about the world, as well as the empathy that people can have for each other.

6 The Kominsky Method (2018 - 2021) Two Hollywood professionals attempt to stay relevant in the vapid world of Hollywood

The Kominsky Method
Sitcom

Release Date November 16, 2018 Cast Michael Douglas , Alan Arkin Seasons 3 Story By Chuck Lorre Writers Chuck Lorre Streaming Service(s) Netflix Directors Chuck Lorre

Exploring both larger themes about the realities of aging in an industry that idolizes youth, and the ramifications of growing older, The Kominsky Method has plenty of honest scenes about aging. Feeling valued when entering the later years of life is a struggle, but that doesn’t mean there isn’t plenty of levity to be found in even the darkest of moments. Kominsky (Michael Douglas) isn’t a perfect person, but it’s his moments of vulnerability that show who he truly is. Like Loudermilk, the show is judicious in revealing the softer side of its characters, making those moments carry more weight.

5 Hacks (2021 - ) An aging comedy star passes on her knowledge to an ungrateful recipient

Hacks
Comedy Drama

Release Date May 13, 2021 Cast Jean Smart , Hannah Einbinder , Carl Clemons-Hopkins Seasons 2 Story By Lucia Aniello Streaming Service(s) Prime Video , HBO Max Directors Lucia Aniello

Pairing veteran comedic actress Jean Smart with burgeoning talent on the comedic scene of Hannah Einbinder, Megan Stalter, and Paul W. Downs is a perfect combination. Protagonists Deborah (Smart) and Ava (Einbinder) are two sides of the same coin at different points in life. Although their philosophies clash, their personalities and lack of filters mirror each other, making them the perfect people to see the truth about each other. Intergenerational friendship is touched upon in Loudermilk and each series uses the trope effectively to point out the problems of every generation, no matter how different they might think they are.

4 Kevin Can F**k Himself (2021 - 2022) A dark blend of sitcom and drama with plenty of meta-commentary

Kevin Can F**k Himself
Comedy Drama

Release Date June 13, 2021 Cast Brian Howe , Alex Bonifer , Mary Hollis Inboden , Eric Petersen , Raymond Lee , Annie Murphy Seasons 2 Streaming Service(s) Prime Video , AMC Plus

It’s well-known that sitcoms are rife with tropes, particularly misogynistic ones that haven’t aged well and have bled into the cultural perception of women. In Kevin Can F**k Himself, Allison (Annie Murphy) lives in a dual world. One that’s shot like a traditional sitcom and one that’s shot like a drama when planning her escape from the life she hates. Though Loudermilk and other dramedies are rarely as meta as the show, and it isn’t afraid of getting dark, they share the centering of a protagonist who refuses to capitulate to the standards set by an unfair world order.

3 You’re The Worst (2014 - 2019) Two equally unlikable characters try to form a lasting relationship

You’re the Worst
Sitcom

Release Date July 14, 2014 Cast Aya Cash , Chris Geere Seasons 5 Network FX Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Apple TV+ , Disney+

Neither Jimmy (Chris Geere) nor Gretchen (Aya Cash) have many redeeming qualities or much to recommend them. However, despite their selfish tendencies and disregard for the feelings of others, they find themselves inexplicably drawn to each other. It’s refreshing for each of them to be with someone just as bad as they are, and their behavior makes the other want to change and be better, while also incurring hilarious moments of complete humiliation. You’re The Worst takes a distinctly romantic spin on dislikable characters, while Loudermilk decenters romance in favor of platonic connections, but both develop their difficult characters similarly.

2 Single Drunk Female (2022 - 2023) A recovering alcoholic reevaluates her life and works to change

After being confronted with the destructive effects of her addiction, Samantha (Sofia Black-D’Elia) has to move back home and start over while going through the difficult process of recovery. Both Loudermilk and Single Drunk Female touch upon the topics of alcoholism and recovery, and like Loudermilk, Samantha doesn’t always have the best attitude about her lot in life. In both shows, though the characters act self-destructively, it’s impossible not to root for them and hope that they’ll get better while retaining their sense of humor about the situation.

1 Shrinking (2023 - ) All the rules of therapy are broken, but connections are built along the way

Shrinking
Comedy Drama

Release Date January 27, 2023 Cast Jason Segel , Harrison Ford , Jessica Williams , Christa Miller , Michael Urie Writers Brett Goldstein , Bill Lawrence , Jason Segel Streaming Service(s) Apple TV+ Directors Bill Lawrence

Harrison Ford gives a very good and different performance in Shrinking, as a therapist who has had enough with his clients and begins telling them the harsh truths he believes they need to hear. His analysis of his patients is biting, but it’s clear that honesty, no matter how hard to hear, can be the best policy. Additionally, as more is revealed about his personal life, the root of his anger is shown, making him a more sympathetic figure.
