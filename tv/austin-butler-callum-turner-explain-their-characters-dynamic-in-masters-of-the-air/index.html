Summary

Masters of the Air is a star-studded war drama series on Apple TV+ that follows a group of World War II pilots who face German fighters in a bomber called “The Flying Fortress.” The series is created by John Shiban and John Orloff, based on the book by Donald L. Miller, and features a talented cast including Austin Butler, Callum Turner, Anthony Boyle, and Barry Keoghan. The actors in Masters of the Air underwent rigorous training to bond and understand the honor and responsibility of portraying real-life heroes, while the dynamic between their characters is described as a strong bond forged through determination and sacrifice.

Masters of the Air is a companion to Band of Brothers and The Pacific from the same minds. The series follows the Bloddy Hundredth, which is the nickname for the 100th Bomb Group in the Eighth Air Force. This group of pilots faced unending German fighters while suffering major losses during the war and becoming an unforgettable part of history.

John Shiban and John Orloff created Masters of the Air based on Donal Miller’s book, Masters of the Air: America’s Bomber Boys Who Fought the Air War Against Nazi Germany. Masters of the Air features a star-studded cast including Austin Butler, Callum Turner, Anthony Boyle, Barry Keoghan, Nikolai Kinski, Stephen Campbell Moore, Sawyer Spielberg, and Isabel May. Gary Goetzman, Tom Hanks, and Steven Spielberg are executive producers.

Screen Rant interviewed Masters of the Air stars Austin Butler and Callum Scott. Turner shared how the training experience differed for Masters of the Air compared to the other period drama he starred in, The Boys in the Boat. Butler discussed honoring real-life heroes while the pair explained the dynamic between their characters.

Austin Butler & Callum Turner Talk Masters of the Air

Screen Rant: This is a brilliant show. It really puts you in the cockpit with the other pilots, and it does a great job of preserving the history of these missions that these men flew on. Callum, last time we spoke at the Boys in the Boat, you talked about the rigorous training and how that helped you build a bond between the cast. How did the training for the Master of Air compare?

Callum Turner: Completely different. We had this two-week boot camp at the beginning, which bonded us in a really beautiful way. The guys in real life, they had this thing called Crew Blue, and it’s the thing that keeps them together. When you’re up in the plane, you’re not just fighting for yourself, you’re fighting for your friend next to you, and you really can’t drop the ball and you feel the responsibility of everyone. There’s 10 people in that full every time they go up. That boot camp gave us that idea. Really, we embodied it everyone, because we understood the level of how honorable it was what these guys did, and the honor it was for us to be representing them.

Austin, a lot of these stories are real. Can you talk about the responsibility of telling these stories and honoring these heroes?

Austin Butler: Absolutely. I know all of us had this feeling of an incredible privilege and responsibility in bringing their stories to life with as much authenticity and care and passion as we possibly could because what they did is they’re the true superheroes. They saved the world, they made the world a better place, and they were so young, the bravery that they had is just remarkable. It’s really such a true, true honor to get to be a small part of bringing their stories to life.

Callum, would you describe the dynamic between John Egan and Gale Cleven and how the two of you built these relationships on screen?

Callum Turner: The thing that’s so beautiful about them, they’re yin and yang. They’re chalk and cheese. They both joined up before Pearl Harbor, and they have this thing inside of them that’s inherent, and they’re determined to stand up to evil in the face of adversity and put themselves on the line and sacrifice themselves for the greater good. That’s the thing that bonds them. They’re also the best pilots in the Hundred by a million miles until Rosie turns up. They just have this, sometimes you just love someone and they love each other. I felt that about Austin from the moment I met him. I just fell in love with him. We had the same thing. We had this real passion for this project and for our work and this determination to honor these guys in the best possible way and not to let them down. Art imitates life in a sense. We just hung out for 10 months straight. Johnny Egan was Cleven’s best man at his wedding. This was a bond that was unbreakable and for life. You were saying about the video, right? Austin Butler: Yeah. Callum Turner: Oh, his nephew was talking about. Austin Butler: Yeah. At the premiere here in Los Angeles the other day, Cleven’s nephew came and I got to speak to him for a long time and he was just such a sweet, sweet man. He told me stories, but one of the core things was he said, “I just remember my uncle always talking about how his best friend in the world was Johnny Egan.” That was so touching to me. Callum Turner: Yeah. They were beautiful together.
