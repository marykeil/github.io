10 Upcoming House Of The Dragon Moments That Will Destroy You

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Literature Review

House of the Dragon season 1 has already shown some emotionally brutal scenes. With war ensuing in season 2, there’s plenty more to come.

Season 1 already saw some heartwrenching moments, such as Viserys’ final words and Rhaenyra’s facial expression after learning of her son’s murder. House of the Dragon foreshadowing moments have already indicated that some of the most devastating book events are going to happen in the series. Tragic character deaths are a given, but the atrocities of the Dance of the Dragons will offer even more difficult material. If the constant heartbreak of Game of Thrones wasn’t enough, there’s plenty more to come in the hit prequel.

10 Blood & Cheese The season 2 premiere should have one of the show’s most gruesome moments.

The House of the Dragon season 2 trailer already offered a glimpse of one of the most brutal upcoming scenes. Audiences won’t have to wait long for Blood and Cheese, a pair of assassins sent by Daemon Targaryen to kill one of Aegon’s sons in response to Lucerys’ death in season 1. The season 2 premiere episode is titled “A Son for a Son,” indicating that the violent sequence may happen immediately, starting off the season with blood. Viewers may not feel much sympathy for Aegon, but he won’t be the target of the brutal assassination. The pair sneak into the Red Keep, enter Alicent’s chambers, bind and gag her, and kill her maid. But wait, there’s more. The assassins capture Helaena Targaryen and force her to choose between her three children, asking which she’d like them to kill, to which she chooses her youngest, Maelor. Instead, the men kill Prince Jaehaerys with a brutal beheading. It will undoubtedly be a disturbing opening to House of the Dragon season 2.

9 The Cargyll Twins Die Together The Cargyll twins are on opposite sides of the Dance, which won’t end well for either.

Another upcoming House of the Dragon season 2 event displayed in the trailer is the duel between Erryk and Arryk Cargyll. The twin Kingsguard members were introduced late in season 1 and shown to be taking opposite sides in the Dance of the Dragons. Arryk Cargyll, who serves Aegon II, will be sent undercover to Dragonstone as an assassin to kill Rhaenyra, with the plan being for him to impersonate his twin brother to get close to her. Of course, he’ll run into his brother first, resulting in a needless, bloody death for both of them.

8 Rhaenys Goes Down Fighting At Rook’s Rest Rhaenys and Meleys will die in battle, presumably in season 2.

Rhaenys Targaryen isn’t the most likable House of the Dragon character, but she’s undoubtedly one of the most respectable. The climactic battle of season 2 seems to be Rook’s Rest, which will see her and her dragon Meleys facing off with Aegon II and Aemond in the skies. Outmatched, Rhaenys goes down, but not without a fight. Seeing her death will be difficult, but the worst part may be the treatment of her and Meleys afterward, which House of the Dragon behind-the-scenes photos indicated might be pretty disrespectful.

7 Jace’s Death At The Gullet Rhaenyra will lose her eldest son to the Dance.

Jacaerys Velaryon becomes more prominent toward the end of season 1, growing to an age where he’s eager to help his mother win the war, solidifying himself as a future king. Unfortunately for him, Jace will never sit the Iron Throne, as he dies a brutal death at the Battle of the Gullet. This likely won’t happen in season 2, which will instead push him further as a likable protagonist with a budding friendship with Cregan Stark, whom he was sent to negotiate with in Winterfell. Not to mention, Rhaenyra losing another son will be absolutely heartbreaking.

6 The Storming Of The Dragonpit The Dance’s dragon death count will be heartbreaking.

Possibly the most gut-wrenching moment in Fire & Blood is the Storming of the Dragonpit, which sees a rioting mob rush the Dragonpit and murder five dragons. Rhaenyra losing sons is one thing, but losing her dragon Syrax will be as heartbreaking as the late Game of Thrones dragon deaths. House of the Dragon opens with Rhaenyra and Syrax, displaying the beautiful partnership Targaryens have with their dragons. Unfortunately, dragon deaths will be very frequent in the upcoming seasons.

5 Mysaria Is Marched Through The Streets Mysaria’s death is so brutal it might need to be changed for TV.

Mysaria is another character that isn’t very likable, though her death will surely build some sympathy for the character. In the riot of King’s Landing, Mysaria is captured by a group of knights and given one option for survival: march through the city naked while being whipped, and if she makes it to the gates alive, she’s allowed to leave. Simply put, she doesn’t make it. The series has already made changes from Fire & Blood, and it’s not a stretch to assume this could change, too, as it’s pretty brutal, even for House of the Dragon.

4 Helaena’s Death In King’s Landing The beloved queen is one of the most tragic HotD characters.

Helaena is easily the most endearing and likable of the Green faction characters. She’s a tragic character in George R.R. Martin’s universe, and as war ensues, she’ll garner deep sympathy from audiences. That will make it all the more difficult to see her death, as she takes her own life following Rhaenyra’s capture of King’s Landing. One of the few Targaryens loved by the people, the common folk revolted and demanded justice for the beloved queen in response to her death. This will undoubtedly be one of the most upsetting events in the future.

3 Maelor Targaryen Dies To The Mob Helaena’s baby doesn’t last long.

In the Blood & Cheese attack, Helaena chooses Maelor as the baby to die. While he doesn’t get killed then, he’s not long for the world either. In the chaos of mob riots in King’s Landing, Maelor will be seized by a mob of peasants. The depiction of this event in Fire & Blood is unclear, as different accounts of the events suggest different, gruesome ways that the baby suffered before dying. No matter which option House of the Dragon goes with, it won’t be pretty.

2 Rhaenyra’s Reign Is Written Out Of History Rhaenyra’s death has brutal thematic implications.

While she is a complex figure, Rhaenyra Targaryen has become an exciting protagonist for House of the Dragon, and many audiences will be rooting for her to win the Dance of the Dragons. While Aegon II doesn’t necessarily win either, he outlives his half-sister, whom he has executed by dragonfire. This event will still be far off, but her death will have grander thematic implications corresponding to House of the Dragon’s central ideas. Since Fire & Blood is a fictional history text, the TV series adaptation examines how events translate into history. Rhaenyra Targaryen will sit the Iron Throne for some time, but she’s not regarded in Westerosi history as a legitimate monarch. This is because after Aegon II kills her, he demands that she not be considered a proper ruler by legitimate records. Not only is this devastating for Rhaenyra, who seeks to break tradition by being the first female monarch, but it’s also upsetting thematically, knowing how real-world history has been shaped by often unjust victors.

1 Alicent Lives Out Her Days As A Recluse Alicent survives the Dance but lives in isolation.

Season 1 developed Alicent Hightower and Rhaenyra’s relationship with nuance and sensitivity. Alicent isn’t Cersei Lannister; she has compassion and love for people in her life, but circumstances have brought her against her childhood friend, resulting in her being painted as the villain in House of the Dragon. Unlike most characters, Alicent will actually survive the Dance of the Dragons, though outliving everyone she’s known to remember the atrocities of violence and war isn’t much better. Alicent will tragically live out her last year isolated, weeping and talking to herself.

House of the Dragon
Drama Action Adventure

Taking place about 172 years before the events of Game of Thrones, House of the Dragon tells the tale of the rise of the Targaryens, the only family of dragonlords to survive the Doom of Valyria. The popular HBO spinoff show first starred Milly Alcock and Emily Carey as Rhaenyra Targaryen and Alicent Hightower before they were replaced by Emma D’Arcy and Olivia Cooke, who play the older versions of the characters. Also starring in the series is Matt Smith (Prince Daemon Targaryen) and Paddy Considine as Rhaenyra’s father, King Viserys Targaryen.
