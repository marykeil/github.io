Willow Episode 5 Recap Jade Meets Someone From Her Past, Boorman Reunites With The Bone Reavers

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Willow episode 5, “Wildwood”, reveals the history and weaknesses of Boorman, adding depth to his character. The heroes are imprisoned in bone cages, leading to heartfelt conversations about their feelings and relationships. The episode ends with an ambush by trolls and an attack on the Bone Reaver’s camp, leaving the characters in danger and creating a cliffhanger ending.

Literature Review

Willow episode 5 finds Willow and the rest of the heroes hiding in the Wildwoods, where old friends are met and new secrets are revealed.

In Willow episode 5, “Wildwood”, the heroes enter a dangerous forest to find friends and foes. The TV show Willow is a sequel series of the self-contained fantasy movie of the same name. It premiered in November 2022, and earned an 84% on Rotten Tomatoes. Willow does an admirable job of balancing nostalgia for George Lucas’ 1988 film and exploring the magical world in novel ways. But despite good ratings and a dedicated fanbase, Disney+ ended up canceling the series after one season, and removing it from their library, much to Willow star Warwick Davis’ frustration.

Willow features a cast of new and returning characters who band together to save the world from the forces of evil. 20 years after the events of the movie, Elora Danan (Ellie Bamber), future Empress of Tir Asleen, finds help from a Nelwyn sorcerer, Willow Ufgood (Davis). By episode 5, “Wildwood”, Willow, Elora, Princess Kit Tanthalos (Ruby Cruz), Jade (Erin Kellyman), Prince Graydon (Tony Revolori), and Thraxus Boorman (Amar Chadha-Patel) are on the run from the monstrous Gales tracking them. At the behest of the Crone (Rosabell Laurenti Sellers), the Gales set out to kill everyone but Elora.

Boorman And The Heroes Hide In The Wildwood And Meet The Bone Reavers

On the run from the Gales, Willow attempts to hold them off with some sort of flamethrower. Even so, the Gales close in on the group, and their only choice is to go into the Wildwood, a forest so frightening even the monstrous trackers will not follow them. Once inside, the heroes quietly set up camp. Everyone is nervous, but Jade is particularly alert as the Bone Reavers who live in the swamp are responsible for killing her parents. As they sit around, Elora continues practicing with Willow’s wand and Graydon asks Boorman for advice about women, referring to Elora.

As everyone is tending to magic and feelings, the Bone Reavers get the drop on the camp. This is not the first time this group has appeared in Willow. In the series pilot, Bone Reavers chase down the heroes, though they are defeated. One of the Bone Reavers points out Boorman specifically and says that Boorman is supposed to be dead. That is not brought up again until episode 5, where Boorman is brought to the leader of the outlaw group, Scorpia (Adwoa Aboah), who also says that Boorman is supposed to be dead.

Boorman is revealed to have had a history with the Bone Reavers, fighting alongside some of them in the Dread Mines of Skellin, years before, where he supposedly died. Despite what Boorman says about valiantly fighting trolls to help his friends, the actual scenes show he cowardly ran away, leaving the Bone Reavers to fend for themselves. Boorman is one of the more powerful characters in Willow, but he’s also prone to embellishing, and learning that this great warrior has a history of fleeing from battle adds some depth to his character.

Willow, Elora, Graydon, And Kit Are Imprisoned

While Boorman and Jade are brought before the Bone Reaver leader, Willow, Graydon, Elora, and Kit are put in elevated bone cages, with Willow and Graydon in one, and Elora and Kit in another. Each captured pair has a heart-to-heart about the events that have happened over the course of the season. Graydon once again tries to pick an older man’s mind about women but Willow catches on and immediately shuts him down. Willow stresses that no matter Elora’s feelings for Graydon, if there even are any, her responsibility to fulfill her destiny precludes her from romantic relationships at all.

Elora and Kit also discuss romance. In previous episodes, the two have butted heads but, now forced to spend time together, they are forced to admit that, at this point, they are friends despite their issues. Elora questions Kit about having feelings for Jade, but Kit denies that she and Jade are in love. While Elora says she feels sorry for Jade for loving someone who doesn’t love her back, Kit grows discontented with the line of questioning. She stays sullen until Elora produces a wand she took from Willow, and blows a hole in their cage.

Back with Graydon and Willow, the unexpected arrival of Brownies, small fairy-like creatures, gives Willow a chance to escape. Rool (Kevin Pollack, reprising his role from the original movie), a Brownie, reappears. An old friend of Willow’s, Rool helps the two men escape imprisonment.

Jade Discovers She Has A Sister And Who Her Father Is

While Scorpia is interrogating Jade, she notices Jade’s tattoo and reveals that she has the same one. Scorpia recognizes that this is the mark of General Kael, who put it on his children,making Jade and Scorpia long-lost sisters. Initially excited, Jade’s happiness fades when she realizes her father was not killed by Bone Reavers, but was, in fact, the founder of the clan. Kael was killed by Madmaritgan in the Willow movie, which means Kit’s father was mortal enemies with Jade’s and killed him for it, a difficult fact to contend with.

Val Kilmer played Madmartigan in Willow (1988) and Pat Roach played General Kael. Neither of these actors appear in the TV show, but Kilmer appears in archival footage with his voice provided by his son, Jack Kilmer.

Scorpia Gives Willow And His Friends Truth Plums

When Willow, Kit, Graydon, and Elora sneak down to the Bone Reaper camp, they are surprised not only to find a celebration going on, but one that they are invited to. Glad to be reunited with Boorman, whom she previously had a relationship with, and Jade, her lost sister, Scorpia has freed the group as captives and invites them to the party, complete with a dance number. During the celebration, Scorpia offers the newcomers food which they readily accept, eating it before they realize they’ve just consumed “Truth Plums”, a fruit that makes the consumer tell the truth.

This leads to both funny and heartfelt scenes of the heroes admitting their true feelings and thoughts. Elora asks Graydon about a vision from his past she saw during an exorcism, and Graydon reveals that when he was a child, he was possessed and pushed his brother out of a tree, which led to him being shipped off in an arranged marriage. She asks him if that’s why he’s acted so strangely since leaving on this journey. Even under the effects of the Truth Plum, Graydon is unsure of who he is or what he stands for, and can’t answer.

Willow speaks to Graydon and reveals that he is no great sorcerer, despite his ambitions in the movie to become a powerful spellcaster, he reveals he hasn’t improved much since then. While somewhat disappointing, it also shows how Willow’s strength comes from his courage, tenacity, and character, rather than some learned magic. Jade and Kit talk, and Kit is forced to admit she knew Jade’s feelings for her but didn’t want to bring them up and break her heart.

Despite resentment, anger, and fears, the Truth Plums help them both realize that they do in fact love each other. Just as they are about to kiss, however, a band of trolls arrives and attacks the camp, ending episode 5, “Wildwood” on a cliffhanger.

The Real Meaning Of Willow Episode 5’s Ending

Willow episode 5, “Wildwood”, ends on a cliffhanger. Kit and Jade are ambushed by trolls and the Bone Reaver’s camp is similarly attacked. This episode sees characters sharing secrets and feelings and, despite the chaos at the beginning of the episode, it’s a quieter entry in Willow where many characters’ fears and worries are revealed. Of course, they are immediately thrust back into danger. It’s a potent lesson in the world of Willow that people should connect with friends and family whenever they can, because in a perilous land, they may only have a few chances.
