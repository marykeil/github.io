Star Wars The 20 Most Empowering Ahsoka Tano Quotes

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2700 words 13 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Ahsoka’s quotes highlight her boldness, empathy, and wisdom as a Jedi. She challenges traditional Jedi beliefs and stands up for what she believes in. Ahsoka’s journey shows her growth, resilience, and ability to inspire others.

Literature Review

Ahsoka Tano has come a long way from Jedi Padawan to receiving a live-action spinoff. Here are some of her most empowering quotes in Star Wars.

For over 15 years, Ahsoka Tano has been on one of the best character journeys in all of Star Wars’ forty-plus-year history, going from an immature Jedi Padawan to one of the most powerful, inspiring, and all-round excellent characters the franchise has ever seen. Across multiple Star Wars movies and TV shows, Ahsoka has had many memorable and inspiring quotes that highlight just how great she is. Whether animated or live-action, Ahsoka is consistently phenomenal and embodies what it is to be a Jedi (without even being a Jedi). Unbelievably, Ahsoka was not embraced by the Star Wars fandom when she was introduced, with some finding her unlikable or questioning the decision to give Anakin Skywalker a Padawan. However, Ahsoka has grown into one of the most beloved characters in the Star Wars franchise, appearing in several animated shows before receiving a live-action spinoff series. 20 Star Wars quotes from across the franchise stand out as the most empowering and showcase the best aspects of Ahsoka’s character.

Ahsoka Season 2: Story, Updates, Everything We Know Ahsoka season 2 has finally been confirmed by Star Wars, and the story is now in development. Here’s everything we know about the project.

“Wait! Just Because There Haven’t Been Any Survivors Before Doesn’t Mean There Won’t Be Any This Time.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 1, episode 2 “Rising Malevolence”

This quote from Ahsoka comes in early season one of The Clone Wars and is about Plo Koon, whose ship has been attacked by Separatists with little chance of survival. Ahsoka’s boldness shows how much she cares for Plo Koon and is willing to go the extra mile to help those in need. Even after the Jedi Council tells Anakin and Ahsoka not to look for survivors, Ahsoka firmly stands by her friend and is adamant that there is a chance he has survived.

“You Don’t Have To Look Tough To Be Tough.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 2, episode 17 “Bounty Hunters”

In the same vein as Yoda’s famous “size matters not” quote, Ahsoka gives inspirational advice to Seripas, a small alien hiding behind an intimidating droid-like figure. He may not be physically imposing without his suit, but he’s also willing to defend a village against pirates, which is far more meaningful to a Jedi. Ahsoka is a prime example that looks are not everything: she does not appear too intimidating, but she can beat 99% of the galaxy’s best fighters in combat.

“But I Did Realize Something. The Politics Of War Are Not As Black And White As I Once Thought They Were.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 3, episode 10 “Heroes on Both Sides”

Ahsoka, alongside Anakin, does not conform to the traditions of the Jedi Order in the same way as everybody else. If she is presented with information telling her that something is different from her beliefs, she will not let her pride get in the way and be stubborn like other Jedi. When she is shown that there are real, good people on both sides of the war, she believes it. She understands that while people are being manipulated or lied to, they are real people with beliefs.

“If There’s One Thing I’ve Learned From You, Master, It’s That Following Direct Orders Isn’t Always The Best Way To Solve A Problem.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 3, episode 18 “The Citadel”

In the excellent Citadel arc, Ahsoka comes along on a dangerous mission despite getting specific instructions not too from Anakin. When Anakin gets angry with Ahsoka, she decides to throw his teachings back at him, and in the end, Ahsoka is shown to be correct in coming along, as she is crucial to the survival of the group. Anakin may have had many flaws, but he always did what he thought was right, and this quote shows that Ahsoka has taken his best qualities to heart.

“You Don’t Have To Carry A Sword To Be Powerful. Some Leaders’ Strength Is Inspiring Greatness In Others.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 4, episode 2 “Gungan Attack”

The civil war on Mon Cala in season four of The Clone Wars presented Lee-Char, the planet’s leader, as a very inexperienced ruler and warrior. In typical Ahsoka fashion, she reassures Lee-Char that being a leader is not just about being a skilled warrior but also about inspiring one’s people. This is inspiration at its finest from Ahsoka, demonstrating her growth throughout the series.

“Maybe, But I Have To Sort This Out On My Own, Without The Council And Without You.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 5, episode 20 “The Wrong Jedi”

Ahsoka choosing to leave the Jedi Order was heartbreaking and an unbelievably strong move from Ahsoka. Seeing her leave Anakin behind, despite him being the only one to be on her side during her trial, was devastating. Ahsoka tells Anakin she must go her own way and carve her path outside the Jedi Order. It is an empowering moment for Ahsoka, knowing that she needs to stick with her beliefs and what she has experienced and go into a new world and a new chapter in her life, even if it is hard to do.

“You’ll Find I Have Many Qualities For You To Dislike.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 7, episode 10 “Phantom Apprentice”

The Siege of Mandalore arc is a perfect Star Wars story that surpasses some of the movies in quality, and as usual, Ahsoka shines throughout. In her epic duel with Maul, she says this quote in response to Maul commenting on her Obi-Wan-esque arrogance. Not only is Ahsoka not doubting the traits she shares with Kenobi, but she is proud that she has learned from him and Anakin, and most definitely does share qualities that make her former teacher so loved.

“The Republic Couldn’t Have Asked For Better Soldiers, Nor I A Better Friend.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 7, episode 11 “Shattered”

There is a somber, eerie atmosphere as the second to last episode of The Clone Wars gradually builds to Order 66, and before that, Ahsoka and Rex have a very touching moment. Ahsoka and Anakin, more than anyone, have a close relationship with the clones, and Ahsoka and Rex have one of the best friendships in Star Wars. This moment of appreciation from Ahsoka is very welcome and makes her ensuing fight with the clones more tragic.

“You’re A Good Soldier, Rex. So Is Every One Of Those Men Down There. They May Be Willing To Die, But I Am Not The One Who Is Going To Kill Them.” Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 7, episode 12 “Victory and Death”

In the finale, Jesse and the other clones on the cruiser wait for Ahsoka and Rex, but unlike the other Jedi during Order 66, Ahsoka refuses to kill them. She gets Rex to set his blasters to stun and never cuts down any clones with her saber, knocking them out with the Force instead. When Rex pleads with her, in tears, that his brothers will kill them, Ahsoka remains calm, recognizing that they are not themselves. This is a testament to Ahsoka’s personality, showing that she is a true Jedi at heart even after leaving the order.

“In My Experience, Just When You Think You Understand The Force, You Find Out How Little You Actually Know.” Star Wars Rebels season 2, episode 20 “The Mystery of Chopper Base”

The Jedi Code states, “There is no ignorance, there is knowledge,” but Dexter Jettster joked in Star Wars: Episode II - Attack of the Clones that Jedi should understand the difference between knowledge and wisdom. Ahsoka demonstrated this when she comforted Ezra, telling him that even the most knowledgeable Jedi are always learning more about the Force. Her admission shows her wisdom and how far she has come in her understanding of the Force, appreciating its intricacies.

“Then I Will Avenge His Death.” Star Wars Rebels season 2, episode 22 “Twilight of the Apprentice: Part 2”

The relationship between Anakin and Ahsoka is unlike any other master/apprentice relationship, with such love and closeness often missing from other Jedi. When they reunite in Rebels, neither are Jedi and are no longer master and apprentice, and it is one of the greatest moments in all of Star Wars. Ahsoka’s loyalty to Anakin shines through with her desire to avenge his death, even if it is not the Jedi way.

“I Am No Jedi.” Star Wars Rebels season 2, episode 22 “Twilight of the Apprentice: Part 2”

Of course, that is not the best quote from their iconic exchange in “Twilight of the Apprentice,” as Vader reminds her that vengeance is not the Jedi way while she asserts that she is no longer a Jedi. It is truly a chilling moment, with Ahsoka pulling out the white lightsabers and the battle about to take place. Ahsoka has no interest in being a Jedi; she understands their flaws and belongs on her own path, which is very much a good thing.

“I Won’t Leave You. Not This Time.” Star Wars Rebels Season 2, Episode 22 “Twilight of the Apprentice: Part 2”

For Ahsoka fans, Anakin fans, and The Clone Wars fans who loved Star Wars Rebels, there are few things in Star Wars more heartbreaking than the meeting between Ahsoka and Vader at the end of season two. This quote from Ahsoka to Anakin is painful because she loves her former master, big brother, and friend, who is now gone, corrupted by evil, and she still will not leave him. The “not this time” is salt in the wound, taking fans back to the day Ahsoka left the Jedi Council.

“They Already Suffer Under Your Rule. Surrender, Or Face The Consequences. You Have One Day To Decide.” The Mandalorian season 2, episode 5 “Chapter 13: The Jedi”

Ahsoka’s appearance in season two of The Mandalorian kicks off epically as we see her cutting down enemies in a forest before approaching Morgan Elsbeth. This quote reminds viewers that Ahsoka is all about helping people and being there to save those who need it. Despite the episode title, Ahsoka will always put doing the right thing above strictly adhering to the Jedi Code.

“I Like Firsts. Good Or Bad, They’re Always Memorable.” The Mandalorian season 2, episode 5 “Chapter 13: The Jedi”

Ahsoka’s appearance in The Mandalorian season 2 marked a long-awaited live-action debut for the beloved animated character, as she helps Din Djarin understand Grogu and what he should do to complete his mission. Always willing to take risks and fight for what’s right, this quote is a perfect line for Ahsoka, and it sums up her journey from appearing in the first animated Star Wars film to her first appearance in live-action. It’s an inspiring message that reminds viewers about the importance of living life to the fullest and not fearing the outcome.

“I Go Where I’m Needed” Ahsoka season 1, episode 1 “Part One: Master and Apprentice”

Ahsoka may have left the Jedi Order, but she never abandoned their principles, including the nomadic lifestyle necessary to help others. The way she casually tells Sabine that she travels the galaxy and never settles down for long demonstrates her selfless nature, thinking about the well-being of others rather than her happiness. While it may have caused a bridge between her and Sabine, it’s an inspiring attitude that embodies the core values of Star Wars.

“You’re Training The Body, But You Must Also Open Your Mind.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 3 “Part Three: Time to Fly”

The Jedi also teach that connecting with the Force requires more than physical endurance, something Ahsoka understands while training Sabine. Just as Yoda told Luke that he needed to unlearn what he thought was possible, Ahsoka encourages her student to open her mind, a necessary attitude for someone without much Force sensitivity. It illustrates how wise Ahsoka has become and is a simple but effective reinforcement of a classic Star Wars lesson.

“I Don’t Need Sabine To Be A Jedi. I Need Her To Be herself.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 3 “Part Three: Time to Fly”

However, Ahsoka is still not a Jedi, and her faith in Sabine as an individual is comforting. No matter how much she berates Sabine or claims to want her to change, the two have a bond and understand the other’s strengths. This quote is particularly empowering because it reminds viewers that one doesn’t have to hold a title to live a life of meaning.

“I Choose To Live.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 5 “Part Five: Shadow Warrior”

Although Ahsoka had a purpose in the first half of her spinoff, she had become too detached from the galaxy and herself, lacking the same optimism she once embodied. However, her confrontation with Anakin in the World Between Worlds forced her to face her past and embrace the future, proclaiming to Anakin that she chose to live. Apart from her choosing to survive, this quote represents a spiritual awakening within Ahsoka, an inspiring reminder of how anyone can make a better life at any time.

“Ezra Is Where He Needs To Be. And So Are We. It’s Time To Move On.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 8 “Part Eight: The Jedi, the Witch, and the Warlord”

One of Ahsoka’s last lines to date was also one of her most inspiring, assuring Sabine they were where they needed to be. Despite being trapped in another galaxy with no way to return, Ahsoka focuses on what they accomplished and the good they can still do on Peridea. It’s never easy to move on in life, but she embraces the reality of change like a true Jedi, making this one of Star Wars’ most empowering Ahsoka Tano quotes.
