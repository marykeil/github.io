10 This Is Us Moments That Made Fans Cry

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes 
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

This Is Us is known for its emotional storytelling and ability to evoke powerful emotions, often reducing viewers to tears. The show explores themes of grief and the profound impact of relationships on a person’s life, even after they’re gone. Many of the most heartbreaking moments in the show revolve around death, loss, and the complexities of family dynamics.

Literature Review

This Is Us has a reputation for delivering exceptionally emotional storytelling, and some scenes are almost guaranteed to make viewers cry.

This Is Us earned a reputation for its emotional storytelling over the course of its six-season run, and the NBC show often made viewers cry with its most memorable moments. The TV drama follows the Pearson family over the course of several decades, and it doesn’t shy away from topics like illness and death. Grief is a huge theme throughout This Is Us, and the series’ ability to jump around in time makes its losses that much more powerful. At its core, This Is Us is a story about relationships and the profound impact they have on a person’s life, even after they’re over. The bonds featured throughout the series make the characters more relatable, and they also make it easier to immerse oneself in their struggles. As such, This Is Us evokes plenty of powerful emotions, sometimes even reducing viewers to tears.

10 Rebecca & Jack Lose The Baby Season 1, Episode 1 - “Pilot”

This Is Us digs into the grief of losing someone long before Jack or Rebecca die. In fact, This Is Us’ pilot sees the couple losing one of their triplets during childbirth, the very event that prompts them to adopt Randall. But even having three newborns to care for doesn’t ease the pain of Rebecca and Jack’s loss, which is palpable during the first installment. After all the anxiety surrounding Rebecca’s high-risk birth, Jack’s insistence that everything will work out gets turned on its head. Milo Ventimiglia’s performance as Jack learns of the third baby’s fate is heartbreaking, and it’s enough to bring on the waterworks every time.

9 William Dying Of Cancer Season 1, Episode 16 - “Memphis”

This Is Us season 1 sees Randall tracking down his biological father and getting to know him. Unfortunately, their relationship is short-lived, as William dies of cancer not long after. This is heartbreaking, especially knowing that Randall will lose his adopted father at some point in the show. And William’s admission that he’s afraid to die makes this moment hit even harder, evoking sympathy for him and his son. Randall and William’s final moment together is easily one of This Is Us’ saddest moments — and one of the most likely to make viewers cry.

8 Kevin Comforting Randall During His Breakdown Season 1, Episode 15 - “Jack Pearson’s Son”

Many of This Is Us’ most heartbreaking moments revolve around a character’s death, but the season 1 scene that sees the weight of Randall’s problems hitting him all at once is a tearjerker for different reasons. While William’s terminal illness contributes to this breakdown, Randall also faces an overwhelming amount of anxiety surrounding his marriage and career. Most viewers have been there in some capacity, so seeing Randall cry it all out is both cathartic and emotional. Kevin running to his brother’s side is the cherry on top of the cake, and it’s so heartwarming that viewers might cry happy tears, too.

7 Kate Introducing Toby To Jack Season 1, Episode 5 - “The Game Plan”

“The Game Plan” centers on the Pearson family’s love of football, and after an entire storyline of Kate acting strange about Toby’s invitation to watch the game, she reveals that Jack is dead in This Is Us’ future timeline. The scene runs parallel to a past storyline of Rebecca and Jack deciding to have kids. And that makes it even more tragic when Kate pulls out Jack’s urn, which she typically watches the game with. To his credit, Toby takes the news well, a green flag for his and Kate’s early relationship. However, the complex mix of emotions during this moment is sure to leave some viewers misty-eyed.

6 The Group Therapy Session Season 2, Episode 5 - “The Fifth Wheel”

There are many moments from Kevin’s addiction storyline that will evoke tears from viewers, but the group therapy session the Pearsons partake in during his rehab stay is among the most hard-hitting. “The Fifth Wheel” sees the seemingly happy family finally airing their long-buried feelings, with Rebecca, Randall, and Kevin all saying hurtful things to one another. Rebecca’s final outburst about her kids abandoning her is heartbreaking from all ends, and it will cause viewers to cry along with her.

5 Deja’s Departure Season 2, Episode 10 - “Number Three”

Deja’s decision to leave the Pearsons and return to her biological mother is a tough one, and it hits Randall harder than anybody else. Deja’s departure, during which she says farewell to the family she’s come to love, is hard to watch. While it’s a positive moment for her character, it’s also a heartbreaking one for the other characters. Sterling K. Brown’s performance stands out especially, nailing home just how sorry Randall is to see Deja go. At this point in the series, it doesn’t seem like there will be a reunion, which makes Randall and Deja’s goodbye a devastating one.

4 Rebecca’s Family Meeting Season 6, Episode 7 - “Taboo”

In This Is Us season 6, episode 7, “Taboo,” Rebecca uses a family holiday to tell the kids how she wants her end-of-life care to unfold. During Thanksgiving, she sits the family down and tells them who she wants to be in charge of her care before giving them her final request: that they won’t allow her the progression of her Alzheimer’s disease negatively impact their own day-to-day lives. 3 Toby Tells Kate He Sees It Season 6, Episode 12 - “Katoby”

After going through the pain of Kate and Toby’s relationship woes and divorce alongside them, it’s a relief to see Kate finally moving on in This Is Us season 6. However, the initial break-up scenes between the pair are heartbreaking, especially when Toby tells Kate that he’ll never see the good in their divorce. All of this is tragic, but viewers are likely to cry happy tears when Toby calls Kate just before her wedding. He tells her he finally sees the bigger picture, marking a bittersweet moment that proves there’s always a light in the darkness — even if it doesn’t seem that way at first.

2 Rebecca Pearson’s Death Season 6, Episode 17 - “The Train”

By the time Rebecca’s death happens in This Is Us season 6, viewers already know it’s coming — but that doesn’t make it any easier to stomach. Rebecca finally succumbs to Alzheimer’s disease in This Is Us season 6, episode 17, “The Train.” Not only do viewers get emotional responses from the Pearson children, but they’re treated to an episode that sees a younger Rebecca struggling to move on. Jack returns to help her say goodbye to her life and children, and the episode’s message about love persisting even after death is one that will elicit tears every time.

1 Rebecca Finds Out About Jack’s Death Season 2, Episode 14 - “Super Bowl Sunday”

Jack’s death will forever be one of the saddest moments of This Is Us’ 106 episodes, and Rebecca’s reaction — excellently brought to life by Mandy Moore — makes it all the more tragic. Although viewers know Jack is going to die at some point during the show’s run, the suddenness of his passing speaks to the fragility of life, as does Rebecca’s complete disbelief. It feels as though Jack is perfectly fine one moment and gone the next, and that’s an accurate portrayal of what it is to lose someone.
