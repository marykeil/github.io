10 Best Episodes Of X-Men The Animated Series

SUMMARY

X-Men: The Animated Series is celebrated as one of the greatest animated shows ever, with captivating storytelling and themes. Episodes like “Night of the Sentinels” and “Beyond Good and Evil” showcased the show’s ability to blend action, social commentary, and character-driven storytelling. The revival series, X-Men ‘97, promises an exhilarating continuation with returning cast members, bringing back fan-favorite mutants and villains.

Literature Review

X-Men: The Animated Series is one of the greatest animated shows of all time, with several incredible episodes that hold up to modern scrutiny.

X-Men: The Animated Series has an impressive legacy of episodes that can still thrill Marvel audiences years later. The iconic series first aired from 1992-1997, depicting Marvel’s famed mutant team in a faithful retelling of X-Men’s most famous stories. Producing a total of 76 episodes, X-Men: The Animated Series is celebrated as one of the greatest animated television shows ever, with a string of spell-binding stories that perfectly captured the era’s X-Men team and themes. X-Men: The Animated Series released a litany of stories, many of which were split across two episodes. The show has proven so popular in hindsight, that Marvel is producing a sequel series titled X-Men ‘97. While not considered a part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe or the MCU timeline, Marvel’s X-Men ‘97 series promises an exhilarating continuation of the famed series, with numerous returning cast and crew members.

All 5 X-Men Animated Series Ranked Worst To Best The X-Men have been given some great animated incarnations, and they stack up closely to one another in terms of quality.

10 “Night Of The Sentinels” Was The Perfect Start To X-Men: The Animated Series X-Men Season 1, Episodes 1 & 2

“Night of the Sentinels” was the inaugural episode of X-Men: The Animated Series, proffering a captivating introduction to the iconic mutants’ world. It skillfully established the main themes of prejudice, acceptance, and the struggle for coexistence. The narrative revolved around the menacing Sentinels, robot enforcers designed to eradicate mutants. While not perfect, the episode is an enthralling adventure told from Jubilee’s perspective as she meets the X-Men team. It was the perfect introduction that showcased the central characters. As the X-Men confronted the oppressive Sentinels, the stage was set for a series that blended action, social commentary, and character-driven storytelling.

Why X-Men: The Animated Series Created A Brand New Mutant X-Men: The Animated Series lovingly created so many classic comic book stories - so why did it feature Morph, an original character?

9 “One Man’s Worth” Was A Powerful Yet Fun Time-Traveling Adventure X-Men Season 4, Episodes 3 & 4

“One Man’s Worth” was a thrilling time-travel episode that delved deeply into the X-Men: The Animated Series’ line-up and their respective characterizations while exploring a discrimination narrative. Inspired by the iconic Age of Apocalypse storyline, the episode unfolds as Bishop and Shard are sent back in time to prevent Professor Xavier from being executed. The episode masterfully navigated alternate realities and their impact on character destinies. Among the thrilling action, it touched upon the consequences of prejudice and the potential for redemption. This fun excursion through time demonstrated the series’ ability to balance complex narratives with compelling character arcs.

X-Men: The Animated Series’ Apocalypse Was Better Than The Movies X-Men: The Animated Series featured a great version of villain Apocalypse, especially when compared to the movie incarnation.

8 “Nightcrawler” Introduced The Beloved X-Men Member X-Men Season 4, Episode 8

“Nightcralwer” is a standout episode in X-Men: The Animated Series, diverging from the typical retro-futurist aesthetic by unfolding in a provincial German village with a Medieval design. This unique setting provided a distinctive backdrop for exploring the character Nightcrawler. The narrative transcends typical superhero fare, evolving into a morality tale that delves into notions of acceptance and self-discovery. As Nightcrawler encountered prejudice due to his demonic appearance, the episode poignantly addressed societal biases. The episode contrasts with others, demonstrating the versatile storytelling and the show’s ability to deliver thought-provoking action while deepening the audience’s understanding of complex characters.

7 “Beyond Good And Evil, Part 4: End And Beginning” Was The Thrilling Conclusion To A Jam-Packed Story X-Men Season 4, Episode 21

“Beyond Good and Evil, Part 4: End and Beginning” served as the enthralling culmination of an epic X-Men: The Animated Series saga. The final chapter featured several formidable X-Men foes: Mister Sinister, Apocalypse, and Magneto, with a central appearance from the fan-favorite hero, Cable. Originally intended as the series finale, the episode masterfully tied up numerous narrative threads, delivering closure to several character arcs. The episode’s conclusion neatly resolved the overarching conflict with several action-packed sequences. “Beyond Good and Evil” was the culmination of the various time-travel stories, proffering the final foray into the dystopian futures and Cable’s intense narrative.

6 “Time Fugitives” Was Cable’s First Major Story In X-Men: The Animated Series X-Men Season 2, Episodes 7 & 8

“Time Fugitives” was a significant episode that adapted the Legacy Virus storyline and served as the first proper introduction to Cable - though he had appeared sporadically previously. The story posed profound philosophical questions, as various future timelines time-travel to vanquish the deadly mutant plague. Further exploring X-Men: The Animated Series’ themes of discrimination and serving the greater good, the episode challenges the X-Men to make difficult decisions in the face of anti-mutant terrorism. With Cable finally being centered, “Time Fugitives” showcased the show’s ability to merge compelling storylines with social commentary, perfectly encapsulating the series’ major strength.

5 “The Cure” Adapted Angel’s Tragic Transformation Into Archangel X-Men Season 1, Episode 9

“The Cure” stands out as one of the most gripping episodes from X-Men: The Animated Series, adapting Angel’s heart-breaking transformation into Apocalypse’s fourth horseman, Death, aka Archangel. The story remains one of the show’s most iconic and compelling narratives. The episode skillfully portrayed the emotional and physical toll of Angel’s transformation, while weaving in notions of identity and choice. The Mutant Cure is a stalwart X-Men narrative that perfectly portrays mutant discrimination and ostracization. “The Cure” also featured a fascinating interplay between Apocalypse and Mystique, proffering a fresh dynamic, with a fun array of classic X-Men villains.

4 “The Final Decision” Featured A Thrilling Final Battle Against The Sentinels X-Men Season 1, Episode 13

“The Final Decision” featured a captivating final battle that saw the X-Men team up with Magneto to battle 10,000 Sentinels. This episode delivers a litany of iconic scenes, as the X-Men face Master Mold and the Sentinel program, highlighting the team’s resilience and formidable abilities. Beyond the action, “The Final Decision” delved into the complex dynamics of coexistence, leaving a lasting impact. The episode was a powerful conclusion to the first season of X-Men: The Animated Series, encapsulating the core themes. The episode blended thrilling superhero action with thought-provoking social commentary, making it a quintessential installment.

3 “The Phoenix Saga, Part V: Child Of Light” Was The Best Phoenix Adaptation Ever X-Men Season 3, Episode 7

In the climactic episode “The Phoenix Saga, Part V: Child Of Light,” the X-Men engage in a thrilling battle against the formidable Imperial Guard as they navigate Jean Grey’s transformation in Phoenix. The episode served as the culmination of the famed comic book storyline, delivering spectacular visuals and high-stakes confrontations. The animation neatly captured the essence of the Phoenix Saga, revealing the immense power and internal conflict of Jean Grey. The dynamic action sequences and emotional resonance underscore the gravity of the Phoenix’s emergence, leaving an indelible mark on X-Men: The Animated Series.

2 “The Dark Phoenix, Part V: The Fate Of The Phoenix” Was A Heartbreaking Finale To The Incredible Adaptation X-Men Season 3, Episode 17

“The Dark Phoenix Saga, Part V: The Fate of the Phoenix” delivered a heart-wrenching conclusion to the dual Phoenix sagas. Staying remarkably faithful to the source material, the episode portrayed the tragic fate of Jean Grey, as the X-Men battled to save her life from the Imperial Guard. The narrative captured the internal turmoil and sacrifice integral to the story. It represents the X-Men’s purest member being corrupted, which X-Men: The Animated Series perfectly captured through stunning visuals and fight sequences. The tragic ending cemented it as a poignant and faithful adaptation that is unmatched outside the comics.

1 “Days Of Future Past” Is The Best Story From X-Men: The Animated Series X-Men Season 1, Episodes 11 & 12

“Days of Future Past” is the absolute best episode of X-Men: The Animated Series and is often hailed as the best rendition of the iconic story. Introducing Bishop, it delved into the consequences of the X-Men’s mission failing in a gripping time-travel narrative. The episode seamlessly weaves the dystopian future with the X-Men’s present, offering dynamic action sequences and exploring different characters and their motives. As the mutants faced a bleak future, the episode masterfully captured the original storyline’s essence while injecting its distinctive facets. It remains the finest episode of the iconic X-Men: The Animated Series.

X-Men ‘97
X-Men ‘97 is the direct continuation of the popular 1990s animated series X-Men: The Animated Series. Taking up where the third season left off, Marvel’s revival brings back famous mutants such as Wolverine, Storm, Rogue, Gambit, Cyclops, Beast, Magneto, and Nightcrawler, who fight villains like Mr. Sinister, the Sentinels, and the Hellfire club.
