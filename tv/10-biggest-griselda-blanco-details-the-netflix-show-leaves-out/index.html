10 Biggest Griselda Blanco Details The Netflix Show Leaves Out

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Griselda Blanco’s childhood and early life were left out of the Netflix series, as there is much speculation and conflicting information about her past. The series did not include Griselda’s first husband, Carlos Trujillo, who she married as a teenager and had three children with. Netflix did not explore Griselda’s earlier career as a marijuana dealer in New York City before she became involved in the cocaine trade. Her connection with Pablo Escobar was also left out of the series.

Literature Review

Griselda had a long and violent career that spanned decades, so it was impossible for Netflix to include all the facts in the six-episode miniseries.

Netflix’s Griselda told its own version of the life of Griselda Blanco, but there are several key details that the show missed. Of course, the only person who could outline such a tale with certainty is Blanco herself, but since the notorious drug lord passed away in 2012, showrunners Andrés Baiz and Eric Newman had to depend on public record and witness accounts to outline the woman’s life. Throughout Griselda’s six episodes, Blanco’s arrival in Miami and eventual downfall were explored, but it would be impossible to include every known aspect of the Godmother’s story. Portrayed by actor Sofía Vergara in Griselda, Blanco has gone down in history as one of the most brutal drug lords of the Miami Drug Wars of the 1980s. The added fact that she was one of the only women of such influence in the business has made Griselda a figure of interest for decades, and various points of her life and career have been depicted in countless forms of media. Still, Griselda is the first to really dive into the nitty-gritty of the woman’s career—but what did Netflix miss?

10 Griselda’s Childhood Was Left Out Of The Netflix Series

Netflix’s Griselda mentioned that Blanco had been a prostitute when she was younger, but that is about all that was said about the woman’s past. This is likely because much of what is rumored to have taken place during Griselda’s childhood is speculation, with some claims having started with the Godmother herself while others she vehemently refuted. For example, Griselda always denied that she had been a prostitute as a young teenager in Medellin, Colombia, but supported the claim that she had kidnapped and murdered a boy when she was only 11.

9 Netflix Left Out Griselda’s First Husband Carlos Trujillo The real Griselda Blanco was married three times (& killed each husband).

Though Griselda included the lives and deaths of Griselda’s husbands, Alberto Bravo and Dario Sepulveda, she had been married to another man long before the events of the Netflix series. Griselda met Carlos Trujillo, who was a minor criminal and pimp, as a young teen, assumedly while she was working as a prostitute. Together, they had the three boys seen in the Netflix series: Dixon, Uber, and Osvaldo. Griselda and Carlos were married in 1964 and divorced in 1969. It is believed that Griselda ordered Carlos Trujillo murdered after she developed her empire, leading to the nickname “The Black Widow.”

8 Griselda Was A Marijuana Dealer In Queens Before Switching To Cocaine Netflix left out Griselda’s earlier career in New York City.

Griselda mentions that Blanco once lived in New York City with her second husband, Alberto Bravo, but little else is said of this time in her life. The couple operated out of Queens and sold predominantly marijuana in the early 1970s before switching to cocaine since it was far easier to smuggle into the country. This is when Griselda first began having women bring the drug into the US in the linings of their bras and underwear. Though business was booming for the Bravos in New York, Griselda and her husband were ultimately forced to return to Colombia to avoid incarceration.

7 Netflix Left Out Griselda & Pablo Escobar’s Connection Pablo Escobar was entirely left out of Netflix’s Griselda.

Netflix’s Griselda opens with a quote from Pablo Escobar: “The only man I was ever afraid of was a woman named Griselda Blanco.” Despite this detail, Escobar was never depicted in the miniseries. Once again, this is likely because there is very little information about what the real connection was between these two famed drug lords. Since they both found their roots in Medellin and worked in Miami around the same time, it can be assumed that they worked together, and Escobar’s quote certainly implies that they did. There’s even a common rumor that they were lovers. Though this famous quote is linked to Pablo Escobar, there is no evidence of when (or whether) he said these words.

6 Griselda Was Reportedly Not Remorseful Of Johnny Castro’s Death Netflix’s version of Griselda was highly bothered by the death of Chucho’s son.

Netflix’s Griselda portrayed the Godmother in a unique light compared to other media, and Vergara’s character frequently felt devastating remorse for what she did. It’s difficult to know for sure, but there have been various reports from Griselda’s followers that the Queen pin took extreme joy in violence. Most notably, when Jorge “Rivi” Ayala accidentally killed two-year-old Johnny Castro after being ordered to assassinate former bodyguard Chucho Castro, Griselda was, according to Rivi (via Vulture), happy that she had successfully exacted her revenge.

5 Griselda Was Supposed To Serve Three Consecutive 20-Year Sentences Netflix’s Griselda was only sentenced to seven years in prison.

In Griselda, Blanco was only sentenced to seven years in prison since the police couldn’t make any murder charges stick. This had a lot to do with Rivi’s sex scandal since it compromised his status as a witness. Though Rivi’s involvement with staff members at the DA’s office is factual, it didn’t allow Griselda to get away with murder entirely. She was successfully charged with the murder (second-degree) of two-year-old Johnny Castro, as well as a spattering of drug charges, and, through a plea deal, was expected to serve three 20-year sentences (via Today).

4 Griselda Was Released Early From Prison Because Of Health Issues The real Griselda was released after 19 years in prison after having a heart attack.

Though Griselda changed Blanco’s original sentence, it is true that she only wound up serving a surprisingly short amount of time in prison. The Godmother was arrested in 1985 and remained in prison for 19 years, but after suffering a series of heart attacks (often attributed to her lifelong smoking habit), she was released early in 2004. Griselda was immediately deported to Colombia, where she lived out the rest of her days.

What Happened To Jorge “Rivi” Ayala In Real Life After Griselda Netflix’s Griselda saw Jorge “Rivi” Ayala carry out many nefarious deeds, which earned him a place in prison, but what happened to the henchman next?

3 Two Of Griselda’s Sons Were Alive When She Was Freed From Prison Netflix saw all three of Griselda’s oldest sons killed before she could reunite with them.

A significant moment at the end of Griselda is the reveal that her three oldest sons had all been murdered just before the Godmother was set to be released from prison. This wasn’t entirely accurate since only Osvaldo and Uber were killed during Griselda’s time behind bars, and both as a result of their own involvement in the drug world. Dixon, however, reportedly outlived his mother (via Stylecaster) and lived in Medellin, Colombia, when Griselda returned home. He is now believed to be dead, with only Griselda’s youngest son, Michael, surviving his family’s business.

2 Griselda Is Believed To Have Continued To Run Her Empire From Behind Bars Netflix’s Griselda never made mention of the queenpin’s continued success in prison.

Griselda Blanco being sent to prison didn’t mean the end of her empire. It is believed that the woman continued to give orders from behind bars, with several more of her enemies dropping dead during her incarceration. Additionally, when Griselda was deported to Colombia following her 2004 release, she was supposedly still very well off and contributed large amounts of cash to the community. Additionally, Rivi Ayala was notably stabbed in prison in 2004 on the exact day that Griselda was released (via Bustle), indicating the Godmother’s continued influence.

1 Netflix Didn’t Depict The Details Of Griselda Blanco’s Death Griselda was assassinated by a shooter on a motorcycle in Medellin, Colombia.

Netflix’s Griselda ended with the titular Queenpin learning of the death of her sons and imagining that she was on a peaceful beach with her family. Though text stating that she was assassinated in 2012 in Medellin, Colombia, appeared over the scene, the moment Griselda was killed wasn’t depicted. This meant the loss of several details, such as the fact that Blanco was with her pregnant daughter-in-law when it happened, or that the assassin carried out the deed while driving by on a motorcyle—a style of execution originated by Griselda herself.
