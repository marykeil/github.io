Bryan Cranston s The Office Movie Idea Sounds So Much Better Than Current Reboot Plans

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Bryan Cranston suggested a The Office movie reunion during his appearance on the Office Ladies podcast. A movie reunion would be a better way to revisit beloved characters and provide updates on their lives without ruining the end of the original show. If a movie happens, Bryan Cranston’s experience as a director and his connection to the show would make him a great choice to helm the project.

Literature Review

Bryan Cranston has an idea for a The Office movie reunion, and it sounds arguably better than the recently revealed plans for a reboot show.

Bryan Cranston has an idea for a The Office movie reunion, and it sounds much better than the recently revealed reboot plans. A The Office reboot from Greg Daniels is reportedly in the works, although not much has been revealed about the project. This new The Office show would seemingly focus on new characters rather than on the Dunder Mifflin Scranton crew, which lines up with Daniels’ comments about potentially creating new stories set in that same world. Rebooting The Office will be difficult, but there is clearly a demand for more of the original characters from the US version.

Appearing in the Jenna Fischer and Angela Kinsey’s Office Ladies podcast, Cranston asked the Pam and Angela actors whether they would be interested in doing a The Office movie reunion instead of a reboot. Both hosts seemed open to the idea, and Cranston even joked about him being an extra in it. Compared to the current plans for a The Office reboot, a self-contained movie reunion featuring the cast of The Office US is arguably a better idea.

The Office US ran for 9 seasons.

Bryan Cranston’s Idea To Revive The Office As A Movie Is More Exciting Than A Full Reboot

While TV shows returning as movies is nothing new, Cranston’s idea to revive The Office as a standalone film would be the perfect way to revisit the series without ruining its legacy. As interesting as a new The Office show can be, it will not be the same thing as having names like Michael, Pam, and Jim back – which is not necessarily a bad thing. A long-form The Office reboot featuring the original cast would not work, but having those characters back for one final adventure would be very exciting.

Cranston: “So let’s say that there’s not a reboot series, but let’s say that there’s a movie — something where we can see where these people are – these people in the entire cast that we’re curious about.

Similar to how The Office’s finale saw the characters reuniting for the launch of the documentary, a The Office movie could see a new documentary about where the former Dunder Mifflin crew is now. This would be a very meta movie, as the story would essentially be a “where are they now” focused on the beloved The Office characters. Although some popular headcanons and fan theories would be proven wrong, seeing what happened to The Office characters several years after the release of the documentary could make for funny and emotional scenes.

Why A Movie Is A Better Idea For The Office’s Reboot

In terms of the original The Office cast, a movie reunion is a much better idea than a TV reboot. The actors, many of whom have shown reservations about coming back for a new series over the years, would only need to commit for the short term given the nature of the project. As such, even names like Steve Carell, who exited The Office in season 7, would perhaps be interested in returning for the reunion. The movie would allow audiences to get updates on their favorite characters’ lives without ruining the end of the original show.

The Office’s characters would not have to be back at Dunder Mifflin, nor would the movie have to recreate the same workplace environment of the original series. It can be argued that The Office characters became bigger than the premise of the show, which is why a movie reunion would not even have to be set at Dunder Mifflin to be funny or nostalgic. Naturally, the film would have to find a way to bring all those characters together for at least a couple of scenes, which could be easily solved through a new documentary.

If The Office Movie Happens, Bryan Cranston Needs To Direct

Bryan Cranston directed The Office season 9, episode 4, “Work Bus,” meaning the Breaking Bad star is very much part of the show’s family. Considering Cranston has experience behind the camera and already has his own idea for a The Office film, it would be fascinating to have a movie reunion directed by him. Any The Office reunion should be helmed by people who have a connection to or at least loved the show, as the focus of the film wouldn’t be to relaunch an IP but rather revisit a beloved set of characters.

With a new The Office show moving forward, it remains to be seen whether the original cast will ever reunite for a movie or series. Still, should a The Office US reunion ever happen, a standalone movie directed by someone who was involved with the TV show and whose focus would be on revisiting the original characters should be the best way to go. A The Office reboot with new characters does not mean that there will never be a reunion of the original actors, and there should be room for both ideas considering how successful the show continues to be.
