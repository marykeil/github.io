10 TV Shows That Perfectly Cast Beloved Book Characters

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2300 words 11 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Henry Cavill’s portrayal of Geralt in The Witcher perfectly captures the character’s essence and physique. Walker Scobell’s performance as Percy Jackson showcases the character’s growth and struggle from a child to a hero. Ben Barnes’s depiction of The Darkling in Shadow and Bone captures the character’s mysterious and manipulative nature.

Literature Review

Some incredible book-to-TV adaptation castings have perfectly captured the characters, from Henry Cavill’s Geralt to Penn Badgley’s Joe Goldberg.

Adapting novels to TV comes with several challenges, but one of the most important to get right is casting the beloved characters from the source material. Books create entire worlds and stories around fictional characters that people can become attached to. Upon reading a book, many people will begin to formulate ideas about their favorite characters and have an idea of how they look, how they sound, and any defining features and characteristics. Of course, these ideas are also shaped by the author and the narrative, but everyone’s perception will highlight different elements. So, when it comes to casting these beloved characters for a TV adaptation, there is a lot of weight hanging onto the decision. Fortunately, there are also incredible actors who love the source material or are exceptionally talented at emulating the characters and bringing them to life. Some book adaptations of beloved characters are absolutely spot on, and the actors appear to be the perfect person to take on the role.

10 Henry Cavill As Geralt The Witcher (2019 - Present)

The Witcher
Based on the novels by Polish author Andrzej Sapkowski and the video games, The Witcher follows the story of Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter who struggles to find his place in a world where people often prove more wicked than beasts. “Ciri” Cirilla is the Princess of Cintra, who has magical abilities and whose fate has been linked to Geralt’s. Yennefer of Vengerberg, a sorceress who trains to become a mage before deserting her duties and going solo, is also a main character in the Netflix series. The show’s storylines are intertwined but told throughout different timelines, all of which eventually catch up to Ciri’s, whose timeline of events is the most recent. Set on the Continent where men, monsters, and elves exist together, the characters all have separate journeys, though their destinies bring them into each others’ orbits.

Henry Cavill is notoriously a famous nerd, who is incredibly passionate about gaming, computers, and many other aspects of fandoms. This lived experience made him the ideal person for tackling the role of Geralt of Rivia from The Witcher novels written by Andrzej Sapkowski. Cavill was very familiar with the novels and games and thus, able to contribute ideas from the source material and really get a feel for the character as he was originally intended. In addition, Cavill is an absolute hunk and a man with an impressive physique which ticked boxes for the heroic figure he was playing.

9 Walker Scobell As Percy Jackson Percy Jackson And The Olympians (2023 - Present)

Percy Jackson & the Olympians
Based on the novel series by Rick Riordan Percy Jackson & the Olympians is an action-adventure fantasy television series created for Disney+. When Percy Jackson is framed for the theft of Zeus’ almighty thunderbolt, Percy must clear his name, all while harnessing the powers inherited by his father, Poseidon, at a camp created for demi-gods.

The best-selling book series by Rick Riordan combined popular Greek mythology with modern storytelling. The books were adapted for a series of Percy Jackson movies in 2010 starring Logan Lerman, but the movies didn’t entirely capture the struggle and youth of the titular character. Percy is just a child when he discovers his heritage, but with some help, he learns to slowly grow in confidence and accept his destiny to become a hero among gods. Scobell is an incredibly talented young actor who fits the bill perfectly, delivering a complex and layered performance while still being just a kid.

11 Book Adaptations To Get Second Chances As TV Shows
Whether or not the film adaptation was successful, novels like Percy Jackson and Interview with the Vampire have gotten second chances as TV shows.

8 Ben Barnes As The Darkling Shadow And Bone (2021 - 2023)

Shadow and Bone
Based on Leigh Bardugo’s bestselling Grishaverse novels, Netflix’s Shadow and Bone follows the story of Alina Starkov, an orphan and soldier who has released a power that could potentially set her country free. Instead, Alina joins an elite army of soldiers known as the Grisha as the Shadow Fold, an area of mysterious darkness that grows each year, threatening to envelop her world. Alina quickly learns that nothing is as it seems, and it will take more than the power of magic to make it through.

Ben Barnes is an English actor who rose to prominence for playing Prince Caspian in the Chronicles of Narnia films. Barnes is a talented actor who is able to perfectly emulate the dark, mysterious, and manipulative nature of The Darkling, without appearing inherently evil. The character certainly doesn’t view themselves as evil as they seek to change Ravka for the betterment of Grisha. The Darkling is powerful, elusive, and full of intrigue, as well as possessing a gift for getting his own way, and Barnes perfectly captures that in his performance.

7 Mads Mikkelsen As Hannibal Lecter Hannibal (2013 - 2015)

Hannibal
Created for TV by Bryan Fuller, Hannibal is a TV adaptation/reimagining of Thomas Harris’ Red Dragon novel. The show follows the exploits of FBI criminal profiler Will Graham (Hugh Dancy) and psychiatrist Dr. Hannibal Lecter (Mads Mikkelsen) and their perpetual game of cat and mouse that unfolds over the series. When a string of murders in Minnesota requires a brilliant mind to crack the case, Will Graham is called in to investigate - but due to the intimate and terrifying nature of the crimes, he is appointed Dr. Lecter to act as his psychiatrist. The truth of Dr. Lecter is that he is Will’s target - but to complicate matters, he’s the only person that can truly understand him.

Hannibal found a perfect casting when the original movies cast Anthony Hopkins, but when a new TV series was being made to explore the origins of the character and his relationship with Will Graham, the series perfectly cast its leading duo. Mads Mikkelsen is one of the best character actors in Hollywood with his natural talent for exploring and developing villains as incredibly realistic and visceral. However, he does his best work in the role of Hannibal, a brilliant, but deeply secretive man, who has a love for fine art, classical music, and excellent cuisine.

6 Gwendoline Christie As Brienne Of Tarth Game Of Thrones (2011 - 2019)

Game Of Thrones
Created by David Benioff and D.B. Weiss, Game of Thrones is a TV series based on the book “A Song of Ice of Fire” by George R. R. Martin. It tells the story of the ongoing battle between the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros - as they fight for control of the coveted Iron Throne. Friction between the houses leads to full-scale war. All while a very ancient evil awakens in the far north. Amidst the war, a neglected military order of misfits, the Night’s Watch, led by House Stark’s Jon Snow, is the first to encounter icy horrors that threaten all realms of men. The series premiered on HBO in the United States on April 17, 2011, and quickly became one of the biggest event series in the “Golden Age” of TV. Winner of 38 Primetime Emmy Awards, Game of Thrones has attracted record viewership on HBO and has a broad, active, international fan base.

Game of Thrones featured several actors who were perfect for their roles, but one that easily stands out for their portrayal was Gwendoline Christie in the role of Brienne of Tarth. Gwendoline may be a perfect physical match for the character of a broad and tall, powerful woman who could overpower most men with ease, but Christie goes further in capturing the personality and complexity of Brienne. Brienne faces regular struggles daily in a world ruled by men, and despite her size, she still suffers abuse by those who wish to fight her or fetishize her.

5 Tom Sturridge As Dream The Sandman (2022 - Present)

The Sandman
The Sandman is a Supernatural Horror and Superhero television series developed by Neil Gaiman, Allan Heinberg, and David S. Goyer. The series is based on the original Superhero Comic Book series created by Neil Gaiman and follows the fictional Lord Morpheus as he attempts to regain control of his realm.

The Sandman, a series of graphic novel stories by Neil Gaiman, finally received an on-screen adaptation in 2022. The series is a fantasy adventure, and many of the actors cast were perfect in their roles, but Tom Sturridge did an outstanding job as Dream. The character suffered through being trapped for decades by humans, before finally breaking free and seeking to restore his powers and his kingdom in the land of sleep. Sturridge does a great job of showing just enough emotion and expression in the right moments, to not diminish Dream’s status as a god, but still emulate his struggles.

10 TV Adaptations That Were Better Than The Source Material
While it is often said that the original is always the best, there are several television series that have exceeded their source material in quality.

4 Ruth Wilson As Mrs. Marisa Coulter His Dark Materials (2019 - 2022)

His Dark Materials
Based on author Philip Pullman’s beloved novel trilogy, His Dark Materials is a fantasy/drama television series that follows Lyra, a brave young woman from another world. Lyra’s quest to find her kidnapped friend leads her to uncover a sinister plot of a secret organization, encounter extraordinary beings, and protect dangerous secrets. Lyra will find that her life is inextricably linked with another as they discover they may be the ones to change the world.

The character of Mrs. Coulter in Philip Pullman’s popular book series is an integral part of the story and incredibly challenging as she displays different faces in different scenes to get her intended results. Coulter is sweet, kind, and unassuming when she wants to appear that way, but bubbling just below the surface is a woman who is powerful, intelligent, and calculating. She engineers others’ behaviors with precision and exactness, and Ruth Wilson perfectly encapsulates all of these qualities in a natural way that brings out the best of each side of the character from scene to scene.

3 Neil Patrick Harris As Count Olaf A Series Of Unfortunate Events (2017 - 2019)

Count Olaf is a character that is larger than life, and full of bravado, which makes it a perfect role for the equally charismatic Neil Patrick Harris. However, the character also possesses exceptional greed, cruelty, and a degree of naivety that makes him seem almost childlike. As quirky and zany as he is, his motivations are purely selfish, and he is willing to do whatever it takes to claim the Baudelaire fortune for himself. Harris does a great job playing the many faces of Olaf as he puts on several poorly conceived costumes and seeks to dupe the authorities.

2 Michael Sheen As Aziraphale Good Omens (2019 - Present)

Good Omens
Based on the bestselling novel by Neil Gaiman and Terry Pratchett, Good Omens follows an angel and demon as they attempt to defy the forces of Heaven and Hell in order to avert the apocalypse. With Pratchett and Gaiman’s funny and irreverent style shining through, Good Omens stars Michael Sheen and David Tennant as Aziraphale and Crowley, with a larger cast that includes John Hamm, Francis McDormand, and Michael McKean.

Another story from the mind of Neil Gaiman, Good Omens, is an intricate and delightfully twisted look at an impending apocalyptic event from the perspective of an angel and a demon. Both Michael Sheen and David Tennant perfectly capture Aziraphale and Crowley respectively, but Sheen’s development from a non-questioning angel to one that begins to exercise free will is beautifully captured and performed. Sheen hits all the right notes as the nervous angel who wishes to simply lead a happy life of choice, instead of bearing the crushing weight of fate.

1 Penn Badgley As Joe Goldberg You (2018 - 2024)

You
You is a drama-thriller series based on the novel by Caroline Kepnes that follows an obsessive and dangerous bookstore owner known as Joe Goldberg. The show sees Joe meeting women that he becomes transfixed with and goes to terrifying lengths to insert himself into their lives. To accomplish his goal, Joe will remove each obstacle - and person- in his way.

Caroline Kepnes’ series of books about the murderous obsessive stalker that is Joe Goldberg was perfectly emulated by Penn Badgley in You. The character is a delusional man who becomes attached and obsessed with women at an alarmingly fast pace and then does all he can to insert himself into their lives. However, despite his best efforts, the relationship usually sours, and Joe convinces himself that killing his beloved is the only answer. Badgley gives off all the charm and charisma of the character, while still showing his dark side and how close he always is to flipping a switch.
