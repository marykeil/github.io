10 Best Medieval War Movies, Ranked

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Medieval war movies depict physically taxing hand-to-hand combat and showcase the harsh circumstances of each soldier. The best medieval war movies provide a visceral and human experience, capturing the grand scale of medieval warfare. Despite recreating conflicts from hundreds of years ago, medieval war movies can still resonate with modern audiences through their intense emotional stories.

Literature Review

Most war movies tell stories from more recent conflicts, but nothing can rival the epic scale and brutal hand-to-hand combat of medieval warfare.

Most war movies focus on more recent conflicts, but the chaos and scale of medieval battle have made for some incredible cinematic experiences over the years. The Middle Ages stretched from the fall of Rome to the Renaissance period. This stretch of roughly 1000 years is remembered today as a time of extreme social inequality, religious dogma, and persistent conflict in Europe. Naturally, this set the stage for large-scale medieval warfare, and movies about medieval knights and royalty attempt to capture the grand scale of the era. In contrast to the more modern warfare depicted in most movies, medieval battles were far more physically taxing. Without guns, soldiers fought hand-to-hand or on horseback, often wearing extremely heavy suits of armor or chain mail. This gives medieval war movies a more grueling type of conflict to play with, and the best movies manage to convey both the broad expanse of the battlefield and the harsh circumstances of each individual man. Despite the fact that they recreate conflicts from hundreds of years ago, the best medieval war movies can be deeply visceral human experiences.

The 20 Best Medieval TV Shows, Ranked Medieval settings can make for interesting stories whether it’s Viking Europe or Kublai Khan’s Asia. Here are the best medieval TV shows available.

10 Kingdom Of Heaven (2005) Ridley Scott’s expansive epic tells the story of warring tribes in the Middle East

Ridley Scott has earned a reputation as the master of epic large-scale cinematic battles, and Kingdom of Heaven lays out his credentials. Orlando Bloom stars as Balian, a French blacksmith who becomes a key figure in the war over the Holy Land between different religious groups. There are many huge battles, including the Battle of Hattin in the scorching desert and the excruciating siege of Jerusalem, and they are all depicted on a huge scale with impressive practical effects. Despite the prominence of the religious setting, Kingdom of Heaven is more consumed by battles and romance than any attempt to preach. Ridley Scott released a Kingdom of Heaven director’s cut which massively improves upon the theatrical version.

9 Outlaw King (2018) A somewhat overlooked story of the Scottish War of Independence

Outlaw King follows the legendary 14th-century Scottish king Robert the Bruce during the Scottish War of Independence. Chris Pine leads an ensemble cast including Florence Pugh and Aaron Taylor-Johnson, but Outlaw King’s focus never strays far from its elaborately choreographed battles. All the political scheming, personal turmoil and jostling for power points toward the mud and blood of the Battle of Loudoun Hill, with an entire English cavalry plummeting into a pit of spikes, and men on both sides doing their best to yell instructions over a whirl of frenzied violence.

8 The King (2019) A regal performance from Timothée Chalamet helps reconcile The King’s disparate sources

Release Date November 1, 2019 Director David Michôd Cast Nick Wittman , Ivan Kaye , Ben Mendelsohn , Tom Glynn-Carney , Andrew Havill , Lily-Rose Depp , Joel Edgerton , Timothee Chalamet , Dean-Charles Chapman , Jack Bandeira , Thomasin McKenzie , Steven Elder , Sean Harris , Robert Pattinson , Edward Ashley

The battles are shot up close to communicate the frantic chaos of medieval warfare.

Timothée Chalamet stars as Henry V in The King, a movie which blends Shakespeare with historical sources on the Hundred Years War. Although The King takes some historical liberties, Chalamet delivers a powerful performance in the lead role as a young man who is forced to mature very quickly and lead his people into battle, and the battles themselves are shot up close to communicate the frantic chaos of medieval warfare. The soldiers in The King are exhausted and caked in mud, but their king fights valiantly alongside them nonetheless.

7 The Last Duel (2021) Based on a little-known chapter of French history, The Last Duel is extremely relevant to modern audiences

Release Date October 15, 2021 Director Ridley Scott Cast Adam Driver , Michael McElhatton , Harriet Walter , Ben Affleck , Matt Damon , Sam Hazeldine , Nathaniel Parker , jodie comer

Drawing inspiration from Akira Kurosawa’s Rashomon, The Last Duel tells a story from three conflicting perspectives. When the truth can’t be figured out, there is no recourse other than a duel to settle the matter. The Last Duel is based on a true story from French history, and it includes scenes from medieval conflicts like the Siege of Limoges. The Last Duel’s battle scenes are gritty and viscerally violent. Even though they aren’t the main focus of the movie, they provide a colorful backdrop describing the violence of the times, and the blood-soaked bond between the main characters.

6 The Northman (2022) Robert Eggers infuses brutal reality with perplexing mythology

Release Date April 22, 2022 Director Robert Eggers Cast Claes Bang , Ian Gerard Whyte , Nicole Kidman , Ingvar Eggert Sigurðsson , Kate Dickie , Olwen Fouéré , Katie Pattinson , Ethan Hawke , Gustav Lindh , Willem Dafoe , Doa Barney , Ralph Ineson , Ian Whyte , Bjork , Alexander Skarsgard , Hafþór Júlíus Björnsson , Anya Taylor-Joy , Murray McArthur

Vikings wreaked havoc all over Europe in the Middle Ages, but their legacy isn’t reflected in many movies. The Northman aims to redress this imbalance by adapting Shakespeare’s Hamlet with much more overt violence and a healthy dose of Norse mythology. The Northman doesn’t depict a historical war, but it draws from many different components of Viking history to portray bloody raids and disorganized outbursts of clan violence. Despite some brilliant practical effects and a unique supernatural tone, The Northman underperformed at the box office, meaning a sequel is unlikely.

5 Richard III (1955) One of Laurence Olivier’s Shakespearean adaptations showed how to depict medieval warfare on film

Laurence Olivier was a vital proponent of adapting Shakespeare’s works for the big screen. His Hamlet and Henry V are still among the best Shakespeare movies ever made, and Richard III is also highly respected. Olivier’s King Richard isn’t the charming silver-tongued gentleman he usually plays. Instead, he’s a violent brute willing to bend the entire world to his vision. There is venom in his delivery of the famous “Now is the winter of our discontent” speech, and this spills over into rage-filled battle sequences with frightened men and horses alike.

4 El Cid (1961) A historical epic from the Golden Age of Hollywood

In the 1950s and 1960s, Hollywood was consumed by large-scale historical epics. Classics like Ben-Hur and Spartacus focus on Ancient Rome, but there were plenty of medieval films being made too. El Cid stars Charlton Heston as the eponymous warlord from a region in modern-day Spain. It blends war with the conventions of Western movies and romances, resulting in an epic tale of conquest with a compelling personal story. El Cid captures how the ordered traditions of medieval warfare can quickly devolve into messy skirmishes to the death, making them seem much more modern.

3 Henry V (1989) Shakespeare’s historical play made more violent than ever before

Kenneth Branagh has directed and starred in plenty of Shakespeare adaptations, including comedies like Much Ado About Nothing and tragedies like Hamlet. Henry V is Branagh’s take on one of Shakespeare’s historical plays, and it heightens the scale of the battle sequences which never could have been achieved on stage. Branagh envisions The Chorus as a presenter of a historical documentary, but the violence is no less immersive, especially at the Battle of Agincourt. Branagh extrapolates an entire battle scene from the gaps between Shakespeare’s dialogue, with thunderous cavalry and volleys of arrows which fill the sky.

2 Ran (1985) Akira Kurosawa’s tale of jealousy and greed in feudal Japan

The medieval period usually refers specifically to European history, but Akira Kurosawa’s Ran deserves recognition as one of the greatest historical war movies ever. Taking inspiration from Shakespeare’s King Lear, Ran sees an aging warlord divide his empire among his three sons. Soon, their hunger for power corrupts them all, causing a familial rift which plays out on the battlefields of Japan. The delicate balance between large-scale battle scenes and moving personal stories makes Ran one of Akira Kurosawa’s best movies, and his last great masterpiece. The burning castle scene is an iconic finale, symbolizing the end of an era.

1 Braveheart (1995) Medieval battle has never looked so good on the big screen

Release Date March 14, 1995 Director Mel Gibson Cast Angus Macfadyen , Catherine McCormack , Patrick McGoohan , Mel Gibson , Sophie Marceau

Injecting the war with such personal stakes gives Braveheart an intense emotional core, and this makes the danger of battle much more resonant and palpable.

Although Braveheart’s historical accuracy has repeatedly been called into question ever since its release, there is no denying the movie’s incredible medieval battle sequences. Mel Gibson’s William Wallace is a tragic hero who becomes a reluctant leader of the Scots after his wife is killed by the English. Injecting the war with such personal stakes gives Braveheart an intense emotional core, and this makes the danger of battle much more resonant and palpable. For all its clashing swords and inspirational speeches, Braveheart is a deeply personal journey. This bridges the gap between the distant past and the modern era.
