6 Burning Questions About Ruby Sunday Doctor Who Season 14 Needs To Answer

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Ruby Sunday’s motivations for traveling with the Doctor are still a mystery, but she may be seeking answers about her past. The old lady next door, Mrs. Flood, may be connected to Ruby and could potentially be the same person. Ruby’s origins are completely unknown, and there are theories that she could be a Time Lord or even a paradox.

Literature Review

Ruby Sunday is going to be a major part of the upcoming season of Doctor Who as Ncuti Gatwa’s companion, but Ruby also has a mystery to be explored.

Ruby Sunday is the Doctor’s latest companion in Doctor Who, and like a few of her predecessors, the character appears to be shrouded in mystery. Doctor Who first began airing in 1963, with a premise that saw an alien traveling through time and space with a rotating cast of companions. In the 60 years since the show began, the Doctor has changed his face several times, but the series establishes these changes in the lore of the show. What is more mysterious and intriguing, is normally the companions the Doctor takes with him on his travels. The Doctor has picked up people from all around the universe, with aliens, robots, and humans all spending time on the TARDIS as a regular companion. However, the Doctor has a fondness for humans as they appear to be his most common traveling companions. In fact, it is often the human companions that give the Doctor the most excitement, as he unravels the mystery of their lives, such as “the impossible girl” Clara Oswald, “the most important woman,” Donna Noble, and now, the mysterious enigma that is Ruby Sunday.

6 Why Ruby Sunday? What Makes Her So Special

While the Doctor does appear to have had a string of companions who have majorly influenced and altered the universe, that isn’t always the case. Often, the Doctor’s companions are largely normal people with no stand-out features that make them all that different from anyone else. Aside from one thing that they all seem to have in common. The Doctor’s companions crave adventure beyond their familiar surroundings, and they are brave (or potentially silly) enough to jump into the blue box with the strange man who promises them all of time and space. Ruby Sunday appears to have been thrown into the Doctor’s path while he was enjoying some time on Earth, so the new season of Doctor Who will need to explore more of her motivations for traveling with the Doctor. At first glance, she appears to want answers about the past, much like Rose Tyler who wanted to travel back and meet her father who died when she was just a baby. But, Ruby may have some other motivations for joining the Doctor in the TARDIS and leaving her family behind.

Ruby Sunday’s Strange Parallel To Doctor Who’s First Companion Can Explain Her Identity Mystery
Millie Gibson’s Ruby Sunday isn’t the first Doctor Who character to have a clue-laden name, but her ties to a classic companion could be very strong.

5 Who Are Ruby Sunday’s Parents? Ruby Was Abandoned As A Baby

The Doctor Who Christmas special, “The Church on Ruby Road,” spent a lot of time on Ruby trying to find her parents. The episode opens with Ruby sitting down with popular TV presenter, Davina McCall, apparently recording an episode of Long Lost Family. Ruby clearly has no idea who her parents are and is seeking any means available to try and get answers about where she came from. Unfortunately, Davina is unable to get any clear answers and calls Ruby with the bad news later in the episode. This could be setting up a bigger mystery to explore in the coming season.

4 Is Mrs. Flood A Future Ms. Sunday? The Old Lady Next Door Knows More Than Meets The Eye

Mrs. Flood is another character who appears early on in the Christmas special when she is yelling at a neighbor about the unsightly and inconvenient blue police box that is blocking the path outside her home. She initially seems to be a kind and stubborn older woman, but after the episode, she breaks the fourth wall to address the audience and says “Never seen a TARDIS before?” This appears to contradict her earlier grievance and confusion at the blue box outside her home. However, in a show about time travel, events don’t always happen linearly. Mrs. Flood being confused at the beginning of the episode, and then appearing much more calm, and aware of what a TARDIS is could be a strong indicator that she is in fact someone who ended up traveling with the Doctor after the events of the episode. The obvious person which this then points to, would be Ruby Sunday. If Ruby went on to get married or just changed her name for any other reason, it could lead to Ruby and Mrs. Flood being the same person.

3 Is Ruby Sunday A Time Lord? Her Origins Are Completely Unknown

Russell T Davies has previously alluded to Ruby’s mystery being a “slow burn” and building over the coming seasons of the show (via Radio Times), but with Millie Gibson’s exit (via Screen Rant), there won’t be much time to waste unraveling her mystery. The show has been recording both the 14th and 15th seasons ahead of schedule as Davies hopes to create a more consistent pattern for the show to continue releasing new episodes every year, so there is a little time to explore Ruby’s character, but whatever mystery surrounds her character will need to be developed at pace.

Every Doctor Who Companion Who Lasted Only 1 Season
Despite fan concerns, Millie Gibson only doing one season of Doctor Who is a rule, rather than an exception, as these classic companions prove.

Another theory surrounding the character is that she could be a Time Lord. This could tie her to one of the earlier companions of the Doctor, such as Romana, or even the Doctor’s daughter Jenny. If this is the case, it would give the show a chance to swap out the actress, but keep using the character, as they could regenerate. Several of the most recent seasons have been unpacking more about the Doctor’s race of people, and the way that the regeneration phenomenon works, so introducing another Time Lord/Lady to explore these ideas with would make sense.

2 Is Ruby A Paradox? Was Ruby The Mystery Woman Who Left The Baby At The Church

One popular theory that has been reported by British news outlets is the possibility that Ruby is the hooded woman who leaves the baby Ruby outside the church (via Metro). If this is the case, it still leaves the mystery about who her mother is wide open, but it also creates a paradox for the show. When Rose met herself as a baby, coming into close contact with the child intensified the paradox, so Ruby carrying herself as a baby would be unusual. However, with Doctor Who, anything is possible, and it could be something the new season will explore.

1 Why is Ruby So Unlucky? She Seems To Be Surrounded By Bad Luck

Paradoxes in Doctor Who frequently lead to all sorts of unusual things happening, but most of the time, these things are disastrous and dangerous for anyone who comes close to the eye of the storm. If Ruby is a walking paradox, it would explain why people like Davina got injured, despite the goblins no longer being around her. However, if Ruby is a paradox, it also means that Doctor Who’s upcoming season will need to explore what it is that makes her a paradox, which could lead to some of the other above questions in a timely wimey circle.

Doctor Who
Adventure Mystery Sci-Fi

Originally premiered in 1963, Doctor Who is a sci-fi series that follows a powerful being known as a Time Lord, referred to as the Doctor. Using an interdimensional time-traveling ship known as the TARDIS, the Doctor travels time and space with various companions as they solve multiple problems and help avert catastrophe as much as they almost cause it. Though the Doctor is always the same character, they experience regenerations, allowing them to be recast every few seasons as a unique immortal being with new personality traits.
