Expats Episode 4 Recap 8 Story Reveals

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Expats episode 4 brings Margaret and Clarke closer as they are forced to communicate their pain and problems. Hilary’s mother’s visit highlights the lack of support Hilary received, making Margaret’s hurtful comments even worse. David’s past and guilt draw him to Mercy, showing why he took care of her after Gus’s disappearance.

Literature Review

Expats episode 4 forced Margaret, Mercy, and Hilary to long waits, leading them to revelations about their families’ feelings and their past.

Warning! SPOILERS about Expats episode 4 ahead. Amazon Prime Video’s Expats episode 4 contained various shocking reveals even without the episode being packed with action. With Hilary, Margaret, and Mercy being forced to wait by Expats episode 4’s events, some long-awaited conversations and other unexpected exchanges provided even more insights into the three women’s histories, with some revelations also exposing severely painful details from the past of secondary characters like David. Despite being mostly uneventful based on what actually happened in the episode, Expats episode 4 fostered the progression of multiple characters’ relationships through its long pauses, letting them use the available time to bond and reconnect. From the shocking revelation of how Mercy’s future will change due to the sudden pregnancy to the surprise arrival of Hilary’s mother, which evidently affected her peace due to her mother’s unkind comments, Expats’ central characters lived through various epiphanies. However, no revelation was more impactful than learning Clarke and Margaret’s trip to Shenzhen was unnecessary, as the boy the police had found on the mainland didn’t belong to Gus. The news caused opposite reactions in Margaret and Clarke, with the latter emotionally crumbling due to the heavy situation and the former more emboldened in her hope to find Gus.

8 Margaret & Clarke’s Trip To Shenzhen Forces Them To Communicate Margaret’s inability to cope pushed her to avoid her family

Being locked in a room forced Margaret and Clarke to finally face each other and talk, without any possibility of running. Margaret’s secret apartment in Kowloon served precisely that purpose, letting her feel her emotions away from Clarke, Daisy, and Philip. However, the lack of communication between the two also hid the challenges they both faced, Clarke with Daisy and Philip’s coping issues, and Margaret’s deep guilt in having to go on believing to be at fault, blaming herself for losing Gus. Margaret and Clarke’s trip to Shenzhen in Expats thus brought them together again, uniting them in their pain.

7 Her Mother’s Visit Shows Why Hilary Always Supported Margaret Margaret’s vicious words are made even worse by Hilary’s mother telling her the same

The unpleasant comments Hilary’s mother repeatedly hurled at Hilary were already upsetting on their own, but their nature also highlighted why a particularly vicious exchange between Margaret and Hilary hurt Hilary so much. Expats episode 4 highlighted how Hilary didn’t feel her life choices were supported by her mother. In turn, when someone came to Hilary with problems and asked for support like Margaret, Hilary never let them feel out of place, no matter Margaret’s extreme reactions.

Hilary’s mother’s comments against Hilary’s child-free status showed how much Margaret hurt Hilary when she persisted in asking for the key to Christopher’s apartment in Expats episode 3. Indeed, Margaret’s thoughtless comments about Hilary never knowing how Margaret felt because she wasn’t a mother wholly echoed the sentiment behind Hilary’s mother’s comments, weaponizing Hilary’s choices by reminding Hilary of what her mother and Margaret perceived she lacked, no matter what Hilary’s thoughts about it were. This confirmed Margaret truly hit Hilary where it hurt, making their possible future reconciliation more challenging in Expats.

6 David’s Family History Explains Why He Was Drawn To Mercy In The First Place He showed Mercy the compassion he could never have for himself

David’s childhood story revealed more than his difficult past, inadvertently explaining also why he thought of taking care of Mercy after Gus’s disappearance. Expats episode 4 revealing that David had a twin brother who suffered a spinal injury that paralyzed him undoubtedly offered an insight into David’s more general issues, showing why he rarely could face his problems headfirst. However, the tragic story and how his family reacted to it also highlighted how David could never forgive himself for initiating the fight that changed his brother’s life. Similarly, Mercy couldn’t forgive herself for losing Gus, and David recognized his own guilt in her. If Mercy’s mother and curses were problems in Expats for her before Gus’s disappearance, losing him brought her such guilt that she couldn’t feel compassion for herself. Recognizing his past situation in Mercy’s, David was drawn to her, more willing to show her the compassion he never could give himself.

5 Clarke & Margaret Blaming Each Other Makes Them Reconnect Their heated exchange was ugly, but it also brought out their honesty & compassion for each other

Margaret blaming Gus’s disappearance on Clarke because he wanted them to stay in Hong Kong and Clarke blaming it instead on Margaret leaving their son to Mercy was a nasty exchange, but it also finally got them to start a conversation. Up to that point, they walked on eggshells in front of each other, afraid of talking about the tragedy. By locking them in a room at a Shenzhen morgue, Expats episode 4 forced them to finally talk about their problems, making each other aware of them, thus fostering an understanding between Margaret and Clarke and their reconciliation.

4 Hilary’s Family Past Shows Why She Was Willing To Let David Go Her family’s lies & hurt meant she would never put herself through that with David

Being stuck in an elevator with her mother and her neighbor unveiled many stories from Hilary’s childhood in Expats episode 4. From her mother’s insistence on making Hilary’s life’s path similar to hers and her father’s shameful actions against their family, Hilary’s extreme attempts at saving her marriage with David made sense. However, her history was also the key that made Hilary let David go. After all, having lived through relentless attempts to keep a family together no matter what, Hilary knew how much suffering was involved in that, and she knew she never wanted to subject herself to that.

3 Clarke’s Heartbreaking Confession Helps Margaret Not Feel Alone In Her Loss His & Gus’s pizza story shows her how much Clarke is affected by Gus’s disappearance

Margaret and Clarke’s Shenzhen trip was naturally burdensome because of its objective, but it also finally let them see each other’s point of view. Up until Expats episode 4, Margaret focused almost solely on her pain of having lost her younger son and not knowing where he was. However, that completely hid from her mind that Clarke, Daisy, and Philip must have also felt something similar. Hearing Clarke’s emotional memory of his last meal with Gus being pizza finally showed Margaret that she wasn’t the only one suffering, and that thinking this made her oblivious to her remaining family’s pain.

2 David Supported Mercy Only Before Knowing For Sure About Her Pregnancy His conflicted reaction to the news makes less sense knowing he wanted kids with Hilary

While the bewilderment Mercy’s pregnancy news caused was reasonable, it didn’t explain why David seemed fully supportive of her at first. Before knowing for sure if she was pregnant, David seemed happy about it, albeit shocked, going even to the shop to buy pregnancy tests. However, knowing for sure prompted David’s uncertainty about it all, even though one of the problems he had with Hilary was his desire for children when she didn’t want any. David’s conflicted behavior isn’t coherent with his past desires or his initial reaction, making it possible he won’t be “the good guy” Mercy wishes him to be.

1 The Body Not Being Gus’s Risks Giving Clarke & Margaret Hope Having hope could be counterproductive & potentially hurt Daisy & Philip

Margaret’s fears she wouldn’t be able to recognize the body as Gus’s became true for Clarke, who collapsed after seeing it. However, learning the truth about the body not being Gus’s potentially undid all the good progress Margaret and Clarke made in relation to considering all the family’s feelings after Gus’s disappearance, not only Margaret’s. Expats episode 4’s final shot showed Margaret happy and hopeful, hinting she might not be willing to budge on suggestions about moving back to the US, even if that could potentially help Clarke, Daisy, and Philip.
