One Grogu Detail Proves Din Djarin Is A Better Mentor Than Luke - Even Without The Force

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Grogu’s decision to leave the Jedi and return to Din Djarin was for the better, as his father figure is a much better mentor for him than Luke Skywalker is. Grogu’s Force abilities are unlocked by his attachments and love for his father, showing that familial relationships can be a net good for Force-users. Unlike Luke Skywalker, who fears attachments and restricts Grogu’s potential, Din Djarin encourages and supports Grogu’s feelings and Force powers, making him a better mentor.

Literature Review

This one Grogu detail shows that the Mandalorian Din Djarin is a much better mentor than Luke Skywalker, despite his lack of connection to the Force.

There’s one Grogu detail that shows why The Mandalorian’s Din Djarin is a better mentor than Luke Skywalker, despite not being able to use the Force. The Mandalorian is unique among Star Wars shows for its focus on the father-son bond between a Jedi youngling and a Mandalorian bounty hunter. The relationship between the two is heartwarming, and it differs from traditional portrayals of Jedi and Force-users. It posits loving, familial relationships as a net good for Force-users rather than just a potential pull to the dark side.

In The Book of Boba Fett, Luke Skywalker gives Grogu an ultimatum: return to his father figure, Din Djarin, or train as a Jedi. Grogu chose to leave the Jedi and return to Din Djarin. Grogu is the first character in a Star Wars movie or TV show who left the Jedi Order over an attachment. However, the decision was for the better, as Grogu is much happier with Din Djarin than he was during the two years he trained under Luke Skywalker. At the end of The Mandalorian season 3, Din Djarin officially adopted Grogu as per the Mandalorian Code.

Grogu’s Force Potential Is Unlocked By Love

Almost every time Grogu uses his Force powers - and every time he makes a truly epic display of skill - is when he is protecting his father. While he is seen doing Force-assisted backflips with Luke in The Book of Boba Fett, and presumably did more impressive feats that weren’t shown on-screen, the times when he shows immense power are always centered around love. His potential as a light side Force-user is unlocked by his attachments.

This is unsurprising. People are often able to perform superhuman feats in emergencies - especially if they are trying to save someone they care about. Grogu being able to use his most extreme Force abilities more easily when his father’s life is threatened makes complete sense.

Luke Skywalker & The Jedi Refuse To Embrace That Love

While his Legends counterpart embraced love by marrying Mara Jade, canon Luke Skywalker followed the Jedi Order’s strict policy on attachments. He took it so far that he even briefly considered killing his nephew when he sensed the dark side in him. His ultimatum to Grogu stemmed from this same issue. While Luke obviously didn’t attempt to kill Grogu, he did fear the attachment Grogu had to his father. He made Grogu pick between training and going back to Din Djarin because he was afraid to help someone with attachments grow strong in the Force.

Numerous Star Wars projects, including the Obi-Wan Kenobi show, have shown that a Force-sensitive person’s connection to the Force can and does dwindle if not properly trained and exercised. Luke, then, wasn’t just giving Grogu a choice between being part of an elite club and returning to his father. He was trying to make Grogu pick between his ability to connect to the Force and his attachments.

However, Luke’s wooden view of attachments isn’t entirely his fault. He was trained by two Jedi Masters who were sticklers for the old ways. They did their best to ingrain in Luke the idea that attachments are dangerous and contrary to the Jedi Code. Luke, unfortunately, learned his lessons well.

Ironically, despite the Jedi’s insistence on casting out fear, this rule can only come out of fear. The Jedi reject attachments specifically because they believe that an inability to let go can lead one to fall to the dark side. Thus, they reject even the healthiest relationships out of fear. This rule is in part why Anakin Skywalker turned to the dark side. He couldn’t be honest about his relationship with Padmé or go to his Jedi mentors for help when he needed it, so in the end, he turned to the Sith Lord who offered him help.

Din Djarin Encourages Grogu To Embrace These Feelings & His Abilities

Unlike Luke, who forced Grogu to choose between being trained in the Force and returning to his father, Din Djarin encourages his son to embrace both his feelings. When Grogu reunites with Din Djarin, even though they are actively being chased, Grogu launches himself at his father and gives him a big hug. Rather than pushing him away, Din Djarin pats his back and tells Grogu that he missed him as well, before letting him know that they need to save catching up for later. This simple act shows that Din Djarin cares about and encourages Grogu’s feelings.

He also encourages Grogu to embrace and practice his Force powers. He is always supportive when Grogu uses - or attempts to use - the Force. He tells Grogu that he is capable, and makes an effort to get him to practice, even though Din Djarin himself isn’t Force-sensitive and doesn’t really know how it works. In The Mandalorian seaon 2, he plays Force catch with Grogu and the silver shifter knob, and in season 3, he encourages Grogu to “show them” his Force abilities during training. He wants Grogu to unlock his full potential, and doesn’t want him to feel restricted.

In The Mandalorian season 2, Din Djarin openly celebrates Grogu’s ability to use the Force, and he’s quick to reassure Grogu when he initially thinks Din is mad at him.

Din Djarin Has Always Been Grogu’s Perfect Mentor

Din Djarin sees Grogu as a whole person who needs love and family just like anyone else.

Ever since he took Grogu in, Din Djarin has been encouraging Grogu to be his whole self. When he has Grogu spar with the other Mandalorian children, he tells Grogu that it is okay, and to stay calm and use the Force. To Grogu, this means everything. His father’s supportive words and belief in his abilities help him to access the Force voluntarily, which allows him to win the fight. Grogu thrives under his father’s mentorship because his father understands something the Jedi don’t: having no attachments is unhealthy.

Din Djarin’s status as a foundling is part of the reason why he’s such a good mentor. Like Grogu, he lost his family young and was taken in by people who helped him reach his potential. He knows what it takes to help a child through the pain of losing a family. Luke should know how to do this as well, but his commitment to the old ways of the Jedi Order prevents him from doing so. On the contrary, Din Djarin sees Grogu as a whole person who needs love and family just like anyone else.

It will be interesting to see how Din Djarin and Din Grogu’s relationship continues to develop throughout The Mandalorian & Grogu and The Mandalorian season 4. While their bond has been steadily growing stronger since the first episode, Din Djarin only officially adopted Grogu at the end of season 3. Explicitly putting a label on their relationship is a big step forward for the two Mandalorians, and their adventures to come in the future of The Mandalorian will certainly show how this relationship strengthens them.
