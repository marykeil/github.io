Single s Inferno 8 Times Gwan-Hee Was A Walking Red Flag, Ranked

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Gwan-hee showed a lack of commitment and neglect towards his partner, Hye-Seon, in Single’s Inferno season 3. He led multiple women on, including Min-ji and Ha-jeong, creating love triangles and leaving them hanging. Gwan-hee’s behavior was toxic, including making chauvinistic remarks and dehumanizing his female castmates, potentially for entertainment purposes.

Literature Review

Basketball player Lee Gwan-hee’s toxic behavior during Single’s Inferno season 3 was polarizing. He showed several red flags throughout the season.

The infamous Netflix Korean dating show, Single’s Inferno season 3 ended with Lee Gwan-hee and Choi Hye-Seon leaving the island as a couple, but Gwan-hee’s behavior was a collection of red flags. Season 3 brought together 12 single contestants, evenly split between men and women, to endure the challenges of “Hell Island,” forge connections, compete for dates, and vie for various rewards, including the opportunity to stay at a luxurious hotel. In the initial matching session of Single’s Inferno season 3, professional basketball player Gwan-hee and student Hye-Seon picked each other. They enjoyed a delightful night in paradise together, but Gwan-hee’s interest extended to at least three other women as the season progressed. Despite this, Hye-Seon remained loyal to Gwan-hee. Ultimately, they chose each other again at the end of the season. They have since split up, but Hye-seon deserves better anyway.

8 Neglecting Hye-seon He Wasn’t As Committed

Single’s Inferno season 3 opened with Gwan-hee and Hye-seon choosing each other and spending an amazing night together in Paradise. Though Hye-seon stayed loyal to Gwan-hee throughout the rest of the season, he would not give her the same consideration. Gwan-hee was interested in no fewer than four women during his time on the island, and, though he chose Hye-seon in the end, he neglected her for most of the season.

7 Leading Min-Ji On He Misled Her

Though Gwan-hee was already interested in three different women, when new girl, Choi Min-ji, arrived halfway through the season, Gwan-hee was immediately interested in her too. She showed interest in Gwan-hee and even pulled him aside to talk, which was the start of one of Single’s Inferno season 3’s hottest love triangles. He implied to Min-ji that he was attracted to her. In fact, in an on-camera interview, Gwan-hee referred to Min-ji as the one he had been waiting for. He made Min-ji think she was the one for him, only to leave her hanging.

6 Leading Ha-Jeong On Another Love Triangle

Gwan-hee met office worker Yun Ha-jeong when the two camps combined during the second episode, in a shocking twist in the game. Gwan-hee and Ha-jeong hit it off and even had the season’s most entertaining banter and dynamic, even delighting the Single’s Inferno season 3 hosts. At one point during the season, Gwan-hee seemed to like Ha-jeong better than the other women he was considering, but in the end, he chose Hye-seon.

5 Keeping His Options Open Playing The Field

Not only was Single’s Inferno season 3 star Gwan-hee interested in Hye-seon, Min-ji, and Ha-jeong, but at one point, he even entertained the idea of dating model Kim Gyu-ri. This egregious move is indicative of Gwan-hee’s toxic arrogance and the sense of entitlement that permeated the entire season. He went back and forth between the women throughout the season, and it was never clear where any of them stood with him until the Single’s Inferno season 3 finale.

4 Ignoring Min-Ji Drove Her Over The Edge

When Hye-seon won a women’s challenge, she got to pick a man she wanted to take to Paradise and chose Gwan-hee. Min-ji was disappointed in placing third because she had also wanted to take Gwan-hee but had to settle for Park Min-kyu. Both couples shared a helicopter ride to Paradise, during which Min-ji repeatedly tried to get Gwan-hee’s attention. When he simply ignored her, she lost her cool and sobbed into Min-kyu’s shirt. Min-ji’s overly emotional behavior was inappropriate and made her look immature, but Gwan-hee’s treatment of Min-ji was cold. In the end, Min-ji chose Gwan-hee, but he chose Hye-seon.

3 Chauvinistic Remarks About Ha-Jeong “Push Her Down A Little”

Some of Gwan-hee’s most toxic behavior has taken place after filming Single’s Inferno season 3. According to Koreaboo, Gwan-hee recently said that when he first met Ha-jeong on the show, he got the sense that she would be a challenge and that he felt he had to “push her down a little.” He seemed to be implying that he needed to bring Ha-jeong down to earth because she was too strong-willed and needed to be tamed. This can have a chauvinistic undertone when used in reference to an outspoken woman like Ha-jeong.

2 In It For Entertainment Putting On A Show

The red flags were there from the beginning. During the first episode of Single’s Inferno season 3, Gwan-hee said his goal was to make this the most entertaining season yet. In hindsight, the statement could be implying that his behavior during season 3 was for show. Gwan-hee was arguably the most popular and entertaining cast member on the Island, but his behavior towards the women was inappropriate and unacceptable, regardless of his motivation. Since Gwan-hee and Hye-seon broke up after leaving the Inferno, fans may never know if his feelings were real or if he was faking it.

1 Dehumanizing His Female Castmates “This One, That One, And This One.”

During the fourth episode of Single’s Inferno season 3, the islanders all gathered around a campfire to talk and play games. During the gathering, Gwan-hee admitted that he was interested in Gyu-ri in addition to Hye-seon and Ha-jeong by pointing at each of the three women and referring to them as “this one, that one, and this one.” Since he knew the women’s names, there was no reason to refer to them in such dehumanizing terms. His fellow Single’s Inferno season 3 cast member Yun Ha-bin snapped at him to call him out, but Gwan-hee was entirely unashamed. Single’s Inferno season 3 is streaming on Netflix.
