10 Saddest House Episodes Where The Patient Dies

Literature Review

Despite being one of the world’s best doctors, Gregory House can’t save everyone, and when he and his team lose patients, it’s often deeply emotional.

House is one of the most intense medical dramas on TV, and in that same vein, the series features a fair number of episodes where things go wrong and patients pass away. House ran for eight seasons between 2004 and 2012, and in that time, House and his team saved many lives. The team consists of some of the brightest doctors on the planet and is led by the highly eccentric genius, Dr. Gregory House, played by Hugh Laurie. However, regardless of considerable medical prowess and skill, the team still manages to miss things sometimes and make mistakes. In episodes where characters die, the most common cause is simply the team failing to reach the correct diagnosis in time, but there have been other issues that lead to deaths that feel even more tragic. For a team of doctors, death is a part of the job, but when it comes as it does in the following episodes, it cuts just a little bit deeper and often leaves a scar.

10 Diagnosed Too Late House Season 7, Episode 14, “Recession Proof”

House is a brilliant doctor who often sees things that most others miss. But when House fails to pick up on clear indicators of a rare disorder which was made worse by the patient’s exposure to the cold, he appears almost numb. House reached a good place in his life during season 7, but this death appears to bring him back down to earth. While not every doctor can catch everything, this episode highlights fallibilities in the man who is full of flaws, but up to this point, has proven to be correct in most medical scenarios.

9 Chase’s Negligence House Season 2, Episode 8, “The Mistake”

By season 2, Dr. Robert Chase (Jesse Spencer) has quickly adopted a nihilistic and arrogant attitude that is not entirely unlike his mentor, House. “The Mistake” deals with the aftermath of a series of ill-informed choices and negligence on Chase’s part, leading to the death of a patient, and a medical hearing to investigate his actions. This marks a turning point for Chase, as he has to reevaluate what he is becoming and how his actions, or in some cases inactions, impact those he is administering care to.

House: 8 Most Difficult Decisions House & His Team Made Across All 8 Seasons House and his team have the difficult job of saving lives, and often whether or not someone is saved relies on them making a tough call.

8 Trapped Under A Collapsing Building House Season 6, Episode 22 “Help Me”

“Help Me” is the final episode of House season 6 and sees House deal with several of his demons while trying to save a patient in a tragic accident. When a crane collapses and injures dozens of people, House and his team are called out to offer support at the disaster site. House finds a woman who is pinned underneath a large pile of concrete that’s crushing her leg. House is desperate to save the woman and her leg and strongly opposes amputation in hopes that he can save her from a future impaired by a less-than-perfect body like his own. Ultimately, House can’t save everyone, and the woman dies as a result of another issue that would have killed her with or without amputation, and House is rendered powerless and imperfect. The helpless feeling that this episode renders and the constant race against a clock adds to the intensity and drama and create one of the most moving and emotional episodes of the series. House isn’t perfect, and despite his best efforts, he can’t save everyone.

7 One Baby Dies To Save Others House Season 1, Episode 4, “Maternity”

Early in the show’s run, the intensity and high stakes are laid out when a mysterious illness spreads in the delivery unit. When several babies with seemingly no connections between them come down with the same illness, House and the team work fast to diagnose and save the newborns. When several more babies become chronically ill, the team manages to narrow the diagnosis down to two possibilities with opposite treatments. To save as many children as possible, House makes the impossibly hard call to treat two of the kids for opposing illnesses, essentially guaranteeing one death to save the others.

6 An Incurable Diagnosis House Season 1, Episode 10, “Histories”

Just a few episodes later in House season 1, the team is presented with a homeless Jane Doe whose lack of a medical history makes treatment difficult. Foreman is uncharacteristically aggressive to the woman and presents a clear disdain and distrust, believing her to simply want food and shelter at the hospital’s expense. However, when the woman’s history is uncovered and her tragic past revealed, Foreman experiences growth and solidarity with her, but her diagnosis of rabies from sleeping rough means she is terminally ill with no chance of recovery.

7 Core Elements Of House That Were Set Up In The Pilot House is a medical procedural show like no other, but it has a very distinct format which is established from the very first episode of the series.

5 Foreman Makes A Bad Call House Season 3, Episode 20, “House Training”

Another case where Foreman acted brashly and without enough caution resulted in the imminent death of his patient. Team House frequently rushes into treatment without testing, but when Foreman decides to issue a radiation therapy that wipes out a woman’s immune system, he inadvertently causes her imminent death. Tragic as that story already is, it is made worse when Foreman confronts his mistake head-on and comes to see how much he has changed and where he is heading. Foreman needs to make big changes to ensure he doesn’t wind up like House.

4 A Delusional Mother And Her Dying Child House Season 2, Episode 22, “Forever”

When a mother suffers unexplained seizures, she ends up directly causing the death of her child. As House’s team works to diagnose her illness and understand why she has been behaving erratically, the child goes from bad to worse and eventually dies. The team is then able to diagnose her and offer treatment, but distraught by her actions, she refuses. This episode is made even sadder when her husband goes to her to try and convince her to get the treatment but instead asks her to tell their son he is sorry when she goes.

3 The Compassionate Mother’s Sacrifice House Season 7, Episode 5, “Unplanned Parenthood”

When a mother gives birth and both she and her newborn fall ill, “Unplanned Parenthood” follows the difficult relationship between the mother and her older daughter as House’s team works to save them. The team discovers that both the mother and child have cancer cells present, and the mother pushes to treat her child before taking care of her. Ultimately, the mother dies due to a blood clot, and her blood can be harvested to rescue her child, but the episode is more bitter than sweet with her tragic passing and her older child’s newfound responsibilities to raise the child.

2 Unexpected And Untimely Deaths House Season 5, Episode 20, “Simple Explanation”

Towards the end of House season 5, the team experiences a tragic and sudden loss when Dr. Lawrence Kutner (Kal Penn) commits suicide. Kutner was one of the more positive doctors on the team, and when his untimely death was revealed, it came as a total surprise. In the same episode, an elderly couple is brought into the hospital when the wife who has been caring for her ill husband suddenly falls ill. The episode sees House trying to determine some other cause of death and a bigger reason, but the reality is that everything is exactly as it looks.

6 Amazing House Characters That Deserved Better Many House, M.D. characters had to overcome a lot of obstacles on their way to success, but they never got to their happily-ever-after.

1 Amber’s Goodbye House Season 4, Episode 16, “Wilson’s Heart”

Amber was never a member of House’s team, although she was a part of the recruiting program that House used to hire for his department. She became a formidable character, highly intelligent and talented, and had a relationship with House’s best friend, Dr. James Wilson. The episode “Wilson’s Heart” sees House struggle to recover his memories, while Amber fights for her life and her best chance at survival is in a chronic condition. Amber dies in Wilson’s arms, and the episode is one of the saddest of the series, setting up events for much of the remainder of House.
