Literature Review

Over the years, Stephen King has created close to 50 unique fictional towns to set his gripping stories and they’re all connected in his universe.

Stephen King’s fictional towns form a rich and interconnected tapestry within his literary universe, with numerous stories set in each. While it can be easy to sink beneath the quicksand of even one of King’s hefty manuscripts, it’s mind-boggling to realize they all share the same universe. Characters, events, and subtle references link these towns, creating a cohesive narrative that spans multiple stories, reinforcing the idea that Stephen King’s macroverse is an intricately woven web where the horrors of one small town can influence the fates of others. The connections between these towns often go beyond mere geography, intertwining the lives of characters and the manifestation of supernatural forces. Shared elements such as the presence of the supernatural entity Pennywise the Clown, the Dark Tower references, and recurring character names create a sense of continuity across King’s works. Known as the Macroverse, King’s universe adds depth to the storytelling, where its rich lore teases this one immutable truth: that a rose is never just a rose.

49 Arnette, Texas Arnette is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances The Stand, The Monkey References The Tommyknockers, Desperation, 11/22/63
Arnette is ground zero for “Captain Trips”, the deadly superflu outbreak triggering societal collapse in Stephen King’s The Stand. It’s also the home of one of the novel’s central protagonists, Stuart Redman, who proves inexplicably immune. It’s also been referenced in numerous other books.

48 Braintree, Texas Braintree is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances The Stand
Despite its limited role, Braintree becomes significant in The Stand after Charles Campion’s death prompts a superflu investigation at its hospital. Campion’s body is examined by a coroner, who prompts the Braintree hospital to contact the CDC.

47 Caslin, Nebraska Caslin is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances The Stand
Caslin is the hometown of another protagonist from The Stand, Nick Andros. While the events in Caslin are relatively light in the novel, they shape the main character’s resilient nature as a deaf-mute who tragically lost his parents in a car accident.

46 Castle Rock, Maine Castle Rock is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Bag of Bones, The Dead Zone, Cujo, Needful Things, The Dark Half, Lisey’s Story, Elevation, Gwendy’s Magic Feather, Gwendy’s Final Task, The Body, The Sun Dog, Gwendy’s Button Box, It Grows On You, The Main in the Black Suit, Uncle Otto’s Truck, Premium Harmony References 11/22/63, Doctor Sleep, Creepshow, Gerald’s Game, Gramma, Graveyard Shift, It, Kingdom Hospital, Mrs. Todd’s Shortcut, Nona, Pet Sematary, One on One, Riding the Bullet, Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption, The Girl Who Loved Tom Gordon, The Stand, The Mist, Under the Dome, A Good Marriage, Mr. Harrigan’s Phone, N., Squad D
Castle Rock serves as a nexus for paranormal events, connecting a staggering number of stories, plotlines, and characters within the Stephen King universe. The town’s bizarre events are explored more deeply in the Hulu original series of the same name.

45 Castle View, Maine Castle View is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Needful Things, The Girl Who Loved Tom Gordon, Lisey’s Story, Elevation, Gwendy’s Final Task, The Body, A Good Marriage, Gwendy’s Button Box References Bag of Bones and Riding the Bullet
Castle View appears in many pivotal events in the Stephen King universe, including Gwendy’s 1970s routine on the Suicide Stairs, Dennis Lachance’s 1960 burial, and Patricia McFarland’s refuge in 1999. Additionally, Amanda Debusher’s residence and The Birches’ anniversary party enrich Castle View with their twisted and macabre humor.

44 Chamberlain, Maine Stephen King’s fictitious version of Chamberlain is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Carrie, Call References ‘Salem’s Lot, The Body, Gerald’s Game
Chamberlain holds significance in the Stephen King universe as the ghost town that witnessed Ewen High’s devastating 1979 Prom Night massacre in the novel Carrie. It’s important to note that Chamberlain is a real town in Maine, but the version in the novel is fiction, featuring topography that’s different compared to its real-world counterpart.

43 Debaria Debaria is located in the In-world region of All-world

Titles Appearances Treachery
Debaria is vital in the Stephen King universe, housing Our Lady Of The Rose for Gabrielle Deschain’s repentance. It’s also the site for Roland’s mission to hunt the shapeshifting skin-man from his youth, forcing him to confront his childhood fears.

42 Derry, Maine Derry is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances 11/22/63, Bag of Bones, Dreamcatcher, Gerald’s Game, Gwendy’s Final Task, Insomnia, It, Lisey’s Story, Pet Sematary, The Dark Tower, The Running Man, The Tommyknockers, Fair Extension; Secret Window, Secret Garden, Autopsy Room Four, Hearts In Atlantis, Mrs. Todd’s Shortcut, Mute, The Night Flier, The Road Virus Heads North, On Slide Inn Road, The Bird and the Album, Castle Rock References Gwendy’s Magic Feather, Rat, Storm of the Century
Derry features in multiple novels in King’s Macroverse. Its dark history includes mysterious disappearances and tragic incidents, thanks to an ancient evil known as Pennywise, who shares a deep connection with the Macroverse’s expansive mythology. It’s even theorized that Pennywise is Randall Flagg.

41 Desperation, Nevada Desperation is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Desperation, The Regulators (published under Stephen King’s pseudonym, Richard Bachman)
Desperation is a mining town near the Desatoya Mountains, notable for doubling as Tak’s gateway to Keystone Earth. Inspired by Ruth, Nevada, it became the setting for Desperation, which centers on a town murdered by its sheriff.

40 Eluria Eluria is located in All-world, near a portal to Keystone Earth’s Desperation

Titles Appearances The Little Sisters of Eluria
Eluria is a small town situated in the Desatoya Mountains. Roland discovers it is inhabited by Slow Mutants and Little Sisters while pursuing the Man in Black, leading to an eerie secret involving the town’s vampiric inhabitants.

39 Eustace, Oklahoma Eustace is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances The Stand
Eustace is connected to the character Edward M. Norris, a NYPD detective in The Stand. Harry Trent travels from Eustace before passing the plague to Detective Norris, who then spreads it to Sarah Bradford and several patients in Polliston, Kansas.

38 Gatlin, Nebraska Gatlin is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Children of the Corn References The Last Rung on the Ladder; Cell; Who, What, Where, Wendigo?; It
Gatlin is the primary setting for Children of the Corn. The town undergoes a religious upheaval, marked by children killing all adults and adopting a disciplined, Bible-centric lifestyle under the influence of “He Who Walks Behind the Rows”.

37 Gilead Gilead is located in the In-world region of All-world

Titles Appearances The Gunslinger, Wizard and Glass
Gilead is the capital of the Barony of New Canaan, and has symbolized hope and recovery in The Dark Tower series ever since the Great Cataclysm. Its fall to John Farson’s forces when Roland Deschain was 16 marks a defining moment in Roland’s quest.

36 Hammer Crossing, Kansas Hammer Crossing is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances The Stand
Hammer Crossing is mentioned briefly in The Stand. Ed Norris considers traveling to the town to find a doctor when his family begins showing symptoms of the plague.

35 Hambry, Mejis Hambry, Mejis is located in the In-world region of All-world

Titles Appearances Wizard and Glass, The Gunslinger Born
Hambry is the capital of the barony of Mejis along the Clean Sea coast in The Dark Tower series. It serves as the main setting in Wizard and Glass, and is important in Roland’s quest after he is sent to Hambry by his father to investigate the town’s resources.

34 Harrison, Ohio Harrison is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Firestarter
Harrison is the site of the Lot Six experiment. Conducted at Harrison State College in 1969 by The Shop, this chemical drug experiment aimed to grant psychokinetic powers to 12 students. However, the experiment’s unpredictable and dangerous nature led to its termination.

33 Harwich, Connecticut Harwich is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Low Men in Yellow Coats
Harwich is the setting for Low Men in Yellow Coats, which is set in 1960 and tells the story of Bobby Garfield’s friendship with Ted Brautigan, who possesses psychic abilities. The narrative explores connections to The Dark Tower series, introducing the Low Men as servants of the Crimson King.

32 Hastings Glen Hastings Glen is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Firestarter
While being pursued by government agents from The Shop, Charlie and Andy McGee chose to lie low in The Slumberland Motel, situated in Hastings Glen. The two Firestarter protagonists note the town’s quiescent and secluded atmosphere, but little else happens here before they hitch a ride to Vermont.

31 Heaven’s Bay, North Carolina Heaven’s Bay is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances Joyland
Serving as the setting for Joyland, the novel centers on college student, Devin Jones, after he takes a summer job at a carnival in 1973. The story takes a dark turn as he uncovers the carnival’s grim secrets and tragic history, including a murder mystery.

30 Hemingford Home, Nebraska Hemingford Home is located on Keystone Earth

Titles Appearances The Last Run on the Ladder, Children of the Corn, The Stand, It, 1922, Billy Summers References Mister Yummy
Hemingford Home is where Wilfred confesses to murdering his wife in Stephen King’s novella, 1922. In addition to its proximity to Gatlin, it is also connected to The Shining due to the novel, Billy Summers, wherein the characters reference the Overlook Hotel.
