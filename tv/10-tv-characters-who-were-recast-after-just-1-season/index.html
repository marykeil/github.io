10 TV Characters Who Were Recast After Just 1 Season

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Recasting a character in a TV show can have significant effects on the trajectory of the series. Creative differences or a lack of audience connection can prompt the need for a recast. Recasting can inject new life into a series and allow showrunners to align characters with their creative vision.

Literature Review

Casting is important when making TV shows. However, creative differences or a lack of audience connection may prompt recasting after the show begins.

Casting is a crucial aspect in creating a TV show, as an actor’s ability to bring a character to life can significantly impact the success of the series. Occasionally, creative differences or a lack of audience connection may prompt the need to recast a character after the show has begun. Introducing a new actor to portray a character audiences have already started to know can have intriguing effects on the show’s trajectory, for better or worse. When a series first begins, the cast and crew are still finding their footing. During this initial period of adjustment in the first season, anything can happen, from scheduling conflicts to a desire to take the show in a different creative direction. As a result, there are times when an actor may get cast for the pilot episode, but it becomes evident that they are not the right fit for the role or the overall show dynamic. There are many TV shows known for major cast changes, and this can significantly impact the path of the show. While jarring, this type of casting change can occasionally inject new life into a series.

10 Jason DiLaurentis From Pretty Little Liars Parker Bagley was replaced by Drew Van Acker

Pretty Little Liars
Drama Mystery

Release Date June 8, 2010 Cast Troian Bellisario , Ashley Benson , Holly Marie Combs , Lucy Hale , Bianca Lawson , Laura Leighton , Chad Lowe , Shay Mitchell , Nia Peeples , Ian Harding Seasons 7

Parker Bagley was originally cast as Jason DiLaurentis, the older brother of the missing Alison, in season 1 of Pretty Little Liars. However, in season 2, he was recast with Drew Van Acker in the role. The reason Jason was recast in Pretty Little Liars stemmed from a desire to take the character in a different direction, which required an actor with more experience. Van Acker continued to portray Jason until the finale, while Bagley’s time as the character was limited. The change allowed the showrunners to develop Jason’s arc to align with their creative vision.

9 Robin Hood From Once Upon A Time Tom Ellis was replaced by Sean Maguire

Once Upon A Time
Romance Fantasy Adventure

Release Date October 3, 2011 Cast Ginnifer Goodwin , josh dallas , Lana Parrilla , Robert Carlyle , Jennifer Morrison , Jared Gilmore Seasons 7

The role of Robin Hood was played by Tom Ellis in one episode during season 2 of Once Upon a Time. Once the character returned the following season as a more prominent figure, Sean Maguire took over the part. While Ellis briefly introduced the character, Maguire stepped into the role for the remainder of the series going forward. The recast enabled the showrunners to realize their vision for Robin Hood as a more integral player in the story after his initial minor appearance. Maguire brought the characterization they wanted to the forefront through the end of the show’s run.

8 James Kent From True Blood Luke Grimes was replaced by Nathan Parson

True Blood
Drama Supernatural Horror Romance Thriller Fantasy

Release Date September 7, 2008 Cast Ryan Kwanten , Kristin Bauer van Straten , Sam Trammell , Alexander Skarsgard , Stephen Moyer , Rutina Wesley , Anna Paquin , Chris Bauer Seasons 7

James was originally played by Luke Grimes when the character was introduced in True Blood season 6. When the show’s final season went into production, the role was recast with Nathan Parsons taking over as James instead. The reason Luke Grimes was recast in True Blood is the actor chose to exit the role due to creative differences over the new direction envisioned for James in season 7. The character wound up in a same-sex relationship story arc with Lafayette that Grimes was allegedly not comfortable depicting. The recast enabled the creative team to stay true to their vision.

7 Kate Kane From Batwoman Ruby Rose was replaced by Wallis Day

Batwoman
Adventure Crime Action

Release Date October 6, 2019 Cast Nicole Kang , Javicia Leslie , Meagan Tandy , Ruby Rose , Dougray Scott , Camrus Johnson , Rachel Skarsten Seasons 2

Ruby Rose was the first to portray the title character in CW’s Batwoman series. However, after starring for one season, Rose departed the show following abuse allegations on the Batwoman set. When the character of Kate Kane returned in season 2, she was recast with Wallis Day in the role. The showrunners then took things in a different direction by creating a new character, Ryan Wilder, as Batwoman instead of Kate. Javicia Leslie stepped into the part and played Ryan until Batwoman concluded after three seasons in 2022. Ryan proved popular enough that she was later incorporated into The Flash.

6 Reggie Mantle From Riverdale Ross Butler was replaced by Charles Melton

Riverdale
Drama

Release Date January 26, 2017 Cast Camila Mendes , Marisol Nichols , Ashleigh Murray , Cole Sprouse , Ross Butler , Madelaine Petsch , K.J. Apa , Luke Perry , Lili Reinhart , casey cott Seasons 7

Ross Butler originated the role of Reggie Mantle in season 1 of Riverdale. Filming commitments to 13 Reasons Why, in which he played Zach, led to a scheduling conflict. The showrunners offered Butler a choice to either split time between the two shows with reduced roles or focus on just one character. He opted to exit Riverdale in order to dedicate himself to the 13 Reasons Why role that he felt a stronger connection to portraying. With Butler’s departure, Reggie was then recast with Charles Melton stepping into the role in season 2.

5 Mandy From Shameless Jane Levy was replaced by Emma Greenwell

Shameless
Comedy Drama

Release Date January 9, 2011 Cast William H. Macy , Jeremy Allen White , Justin Chatwin , Ethan Cutkosky , Joan Cusack , Emmy Rossum , Emma Kenney , Cameron Monaghan , Noel Fisher , Steve Howey , Shanola Hampton Seasons 11

Jane Levy first played the role of Mandy Milkovich in a recurring capacity during season 1 of Shameless. Levy was recast in Shameless in order to take on the lead role in the new comedy series Suburgatory, and Emma Greenwell took over as Mandy from season 2 onwards. Levy’s departure was necessitated by her casting in another show, prompting producers to bring in Greenwell. The recast allowed Shameless to retain the character despite losing the original actress to a new opportunity that conflicted with continuing in the role.

4 Meg Griffin From Family Guy Lacey Chabert was replaced by Mila Kunis

Family Guy
Animation Comedy

Release Date January 31, 1999 Cast Seth MacFarlane , Alex Borstein , Seth Green , Lacey Chabert , Mila Kunis , Mike Henry , Patrick Warburton Seasons 21

Meg Griffin was originally voiced by Lacey Chabert for Family Guy’s first season. Her busy schedule prevented her from continuing. Consequently, Chabert amicably departed from the role after season 1. She was then replaced by Mila Kunis who took over as the voice of Meg in season 2. Kunis stepped into the part starting with the episode “Da Boom” and has remained in the role ever since. Even though everyone in Family Guy hates Meg, the character voiced by Kunis has maintained popularity among fans for the majority of the show’s run.

3 Daario Naharis From Game Of Thrones Ed Skrein was replaced by Michiel Huisman

Game Of Thrones
Drama Action Adventure

Release Date April 11, 2011 Cast Emilia Clarke , Lena Headey , Richard Madden , Michelle Fairley , Kit Harington , Maisie Williams , Alfie Allen , Peter Dinklage Seasons 8

Ed Skrein originated the role of Daario Naharis in Season 3 of Game of Thrones, appearing in three episodes. However, Skrein exited the role prior to season 4 over reported “political differences” with the studio, the exact nature of which was not specified (via Entertainment Weekly). With Skrein’s departure, Michiel Huisman was brought in to take over the character starting in season 4. While Game of Thrones has had many recasts over the years, this particular one allowed the show to move forward with the role of a new actor, despite the unclear circumstances surrounding Skrein’s exit.

2 Ann Veal From Arrested Development Alessandra Torresani was replaced by Mae Whitman

Arrested Development
Comedy

Release Date March 15, 2019 Cast Jeffrey Tambor , Ron Howard , Michael Cera , Alia Shawkat , David Cross , Jason Bateman , Portia de Rossi , Tony Hale , Will Arnett , Jessica Walter Seasons 5

Ann Veal’s portrayal in Arrested Development started unremarkably, with Alessandra Torresani taking on the role for a single first-season episode. Plans to rotate actresses for the character as a running gag were abandoned due to Mae Whitman’s breakout performance over a multi-episode arc. Whitman’s portrayal resonated so strongly that further recasts were discarded. Despite Ann’s brief embodiment by another actress, Whitman monopolized the role from her debut and for all future appearances as George Michael’s personality-void girlfriend. Whitman transformed Ann from a potentially forgettable character into a memorably odd presence.

1 Helene Beesly From The Office Shannon Cochran was replaced with Linda Purl

The Office
Comedy Drama Sitcom

Release Date March 24, 2005 Cast Mindy Kaling , Jenna Fischer , Kate Flannery , Ed Helms , Craig Robinson , Paul Lieberstein , Ellie Kemper , B.J. Novak , Angela Kinsey , Oscar Nunez , Rainn Wilson , Brian Baumgartner , Phyllis Smith , Leslie David Baker , Creed Bratton , Steve Carell , John Krasinski Seasons 9

In The Office, Helene Beesly, Pam’s mom, initially appeared in season 2, portrayed by Shannon Cochran for her debut episode. The character didn’t return until a surprise recasting in season 6 when Linda Purl took over the role and continued through season 7. While Cochran briefly introduced Helene years prior, Purl became a more regularly recurring parental figure, particularly when the show revisited exploring Pam’s relationship with her mother. The recast allowed The Office to further develop Helene’s storyline with a new actress who stayed consistent through multiple appearances.
