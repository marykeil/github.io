Literature Review

Bird Box is a hugely popular Netflix movie, but it’s most famous for the memes it spawned, particularly the Tom Hollander “Holding Eyes Open” meme.

Bird Box, a 2018 gripping post-apocalyptic thriller, captivated audiences worldwide with its unique premise, but it’s best known for the “Holding Eyes Open” meme. The film, starring Sandra Bullock, unfolds in a world where an unseen presence drives people to kill themselves upon sight. The survivors, including Bullock’s Malorie, navigate this new world blindfolded to avoid the fatal consequences of laying eyes on the mysterious force. This high-stakes narrative, blending intense drama with survivalist intrigue, has made the movie a memorable entry in the genre, and Bird Box was Netflix’s most-watched movie ever upon release. Surprisingly, Sandra Bullock’s best horror movie became the source of an internet meme sensation. Among the most popular were the “holding eyes open” memes. These memes typically feature an image from the film where characters are forcibly holding their eyes open to confront the unseen terror, but with humorous, everyday scenarios as captions. This juxtaposition of a high-tension scene with mundane or relatable situations struck a chord with audiences, turning a moment of suspense into the “Holding Eyes Open” meme, a canvas for comedy.

10 Moms Know Best The Meme Encapsulates A Familiar Family Moment

This “Holding Eyes Open” meme perfectly encapsulates a universally relatable family moment, turning the tense scene from Bird Box into a comedic representation of a common household scenario. As a child, everyone has searched high and low for something, only to have their mothers effortlessly find it in seconds. The image of someone forcibly holding their eyes open, taken from the film’s intense context, is juxtaposed with the exasperated, all-knowing gaze of a mother. 9 The Social Coercion To Watch Bird Box Being Forced To Watch Trending Movies & Shows

This meme taps into a common social experience: the peer pressure to conform to popular trends, particularly in entertainment. The image from Bird Box of a character having their eyes forcibly held open becomes a metaphor for the social coercion to watch a trending show or movie. It humorously depicts the internal struggle of an individual who, despite widespread enthusiasm and recommendations, remains uninterested in jumping on the bandwagon. It captures that universal moment of struggling to stay focused while being temporarily blinded.
