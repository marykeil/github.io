Carl Weathers 10 Best Moments As Greef Karga In The Mandalorian

SUMMARY

Carl Weathers’ Greef Karga is a major highlight of The Mandalorian, and his character development from a disgraced Guild magistrate to the High Magistrate of Nevarro is wonderful. Carl Weathers’ portrayal of Greef Karga’s strength, loyalty, and humor is brilliantly subtle and simultaneously impactful. Weathers’ performance as Greef Karga will be forever memorialized in Star Wars thanks to the incredible legacy he led in The Mandalorian.

Literature Review

Carl Weathers’ Greef Karga has been a staple of The Mandalorian since the beginning. Here are the actor’s 10 best moments as Din Djarin’s ally.

Carl Weathers’ Greef Karga has always been one of the biggest highlights of The Mandalorian, from his introduction as the disgraced magistrate of Din Djarin’s Bounty Hunter’s Guild to the High Magistrate of the planet Nevarro itself. Though acting as an adversary to the titular Mandalorian early on, Greef became one of Din Djarin’s closest friends over time, with him being the one who gives Din and Grogu their home on Nevarro in The Mandalorian season 3 ending. He’s gone from a disgraced magistrate to the person responsible for Nevarro’s prosperity, all while being the TV show’s most dependable supporting character. The Mandalorian season 3 is now sadly the end of Greef Karga’s Star Wars legacy, as Weathers tragically passed away on February 1st. Weathers’ impact on The Mandalorian and the Star Wars galaxy, however, is now forever immortalized by the brilliant performance he gave as the High Magistrate of Nevarro. From the way he made audiences laugh to the profound scenes that showed his character’s true heart, here are Weathers’ 10 best moments as Din Djarin’s and Grogu’s beloved ally, Greef Karga.

The Mandalorian Cast & Characters Guide The Mandalorian has created a long list of new Star Wars characters and brought back some familiar ones, with an incredible cast to match.

10 Greef Karga Wins A Shootout With Pirates Season 3, Episode 1 “Chapter 17: The Apostate”

The Mandalorian season 3 premiere may have been met with mixed reviews, but Weathers’ performance as Greef is easily one of its biggest highlights. When Pirate King Gorian Shard’s men, including the Nikto Vane, demand a drink in the town’s school, Greef stands his ground. Despite having a Mandalorian by his shoulder, Karga takes Vane head-on by himself, easily beating the pirate in a shootout. It’s Din who finishes off the pirates, but Greef’s warning shot at Vane proves his own skill - and Weathers’ portrayal of his strength and loyalty to his people is beautifully subtle, beat out only by nine others.

9 Greef Karga Takes Down A TIE Fighter Season 2, Episode 4 “Chapter 12: The Siege”

In one of the episode’s most thrilling action sequences, Greef’s the one who steps up to the gun of the Trexler Marauder to take aim at Imperial TIE fighters while Cara Dune steers them through the lava ravine of Nevarro. Greef is successful in blasting down one of the Imperial aircraft, and even though it does end up taking out the Marauder’s gun, it’s still an impressive display of Karga’s skill and fearless leadership. Weathers fully commits to this moment of high action and balances it with great humor, though some of his other highlights manage to outshine it.

8 Greef Karga Lies To Keep Din Djarin Safe Season 2, Episode 4 “Chapter 12: The Siege”

The only thing better than Greef’s skill in an action sequence is his ability to talk his way out of a situation. When Carson Teva begins to question Karga about the Razor Crest appearing on a manifest, Greef is quick to shut down the possibility, solely for the sake of keeping Din Djarin safe after his earlier run-in with the New Republic. This moment is a true testament to Greef’s and Din’s friendship, and Weathers perfectly shows the nonchalance that would be truly characteristic of a former Guild magistrate. His heart, however, shines even brighter in other moments.

7 Greef Karga Welcomes The Mandalorians Back To Nevarro Season 3, Episode 5 “Chapter 21: The Pirate”

The Mandalorian makes a point of showing how Din Djarin needed to convince his allies to come to Greef’s aid following their early season 1 conflict, and it pays off when Karga gives his speech welcoming the Mandalorians to call Nevarro their home. Weathers’ delivery of Greef’s heartfelt speech is immensely powerful, from his pauses that emphasize his sincerity to his call for the Mandalorians to be considered heroic liberators. This moment serves as a beautiful stepping stone for the story of the Mandalorians, and it only falls short of a few other heartfelt moments from Weathers’ Karga. You may no longer have a home planet, but you do now have a home.

6 Greef Karga Gives Din Djarin & Grogu A Home Season 3, Episode 8 “Chapter 24: The Return”

The only thing more touching than Greef giving a home to the Mandalorians is him giving a home to the Mandalorian. Weathers’ pure joy pours out in this scene, from him being the first to call Grogu by his new name of “Din Grogu” to him being surprised by Din’s gift of IG-11. This scene now closes out Weathers’ journey as Greef Karga in The Mandalorian, and it’s a perfect summary of Greef’s dedication to both his close allies and those he serves as the High Magistrate. Only Weathers’ keen sense of humor and heart manage to beat this one out.

All 24 Episodes Of The Mandalorian, Ranked From Worst To Best As Star Wars’ first live-action TV show, The Mandalorian is both historic and beloved. Here are all 24 of its episodes, ranked from worst to best.

5 Greef Karga Stays With The People Of Nevarro Season 3, Episode 5 “Chapter 21: The Pirate”

When Pirate King Gorian Shard informs Greef Karga that he’s taking over the town, Greef is adamant about staying with his people, despite how easy it would be for him to slip away safely. After contacting Carson Teva for help, Karga personally guides his people away from the town to an area of safety until their backup arrives. At the end of the battle, Karga even leads his people to help take a final charge, though it ends up not being necessary. Weathers is brilliant at portraying this strength as a leader, and it’s certainly his biggest highlight in the role of High Magistrate.

4 Greef Karga Convinces Din Djarin To Let Grogu Use IG-12 Season 3, Episode 7 “Chapter 23: The Spies”

In what’s easily one of the most comedic moments of The Mandalorian season 3, Greef Karga not only has the IG-12 mech suit commissioned for Grogu, but he also convinces Din Djarin to let the little one use it. Greef offers up his office as a practice space, despite all the fragile items Grogu could - and almost did - break, and he supports Grogu when the little one communicates to Din that he’d like to use the suit. Weathers’ comedic edge in this moment is absolute perfection, as is his ability to act truly convincing in swaying Din to his and Grogu’s side.

3 Greef Karga’s Heart Is Changed By Grogu Season 1, Episode 7 “Chapter 7: The Reckoning”

One quick brush with death at the hands of Din Djarin isn’t quite enough to change Greef’s heart in The Mandalorian season 1, but Grogu saving him from certain death later on becomes the key. Karga not only shoots down two of his own bounty hunters to prove his change of heart to Din, Cara Dune, and Kuiil, but he also assures Din that he can kill Greef without violating the Guild Code. Weathers is perfect in this moment, showing Karga’s guilt as he reckons with his old intentions. Only his humor manages to beat this impactful moment out.

2 Greef Karga Thinks Grogu’s Trying To Eat Him Season 1, Episode 7 “Chapter 7: The Reckoning”

In the aforementioned moment of Grogu rescuing Greef from death comes one of not only Karga’s most hilarious lines, but also the show’s. While Grogu is Force healing Greef’s poison-infused wound, Karga lets his adrenaline-led panic manifest into a fearful insistence that Grogu is, in fact, trying to eat him rather than heal him. It’s only Kuiil’s gentle nudge that gets Greef to relax enough to let the little one work his Force magic. Weathers’ delivery is the key to making this moment work, balancing Greef’s fear with his own sense of humor in a way that’s only been outmatched by one other moment.

1 Greef Karga Asks Grogu To Do The “Magic Hand Thing” Season 1, Episode 8 “Chapter 8: Redemption”

Weathers truly shines as Greef Karga in The Mandalorian season 1 finale, but one moment stands above the rest. While Moff Gideon’s TIE fighter pursues the Nevarro allies, all ideas are on the table, and Greef’s eager to go for the one everyone’s thinking. He lifts his hand to mimic Grogu’s own as he urges the little one to “do the magic hand thing!” Grogu, in true toddler fashion, simply waves back at Karga, mistaking the gesture for a moment of greeting. Everything about Weathers’ delivery, from his physical acting to his exasperated “I’m out of ideas,” is sheer perfection, and truly iconic.

While this moment and those that have preceded it are certainly Greef Karga’s best, it’s hard to narrow down such a list of Weathers’ incredible moments as Nevarro’s High Magistrate. Weathers truly gave all of his pure, powerful soul to his Star Wars role, and the idea of what else could have been will always linger as the story of Din Djarin and Grogu goes on. What Weathers already gave, however, will always be more than enough, as The Mandalorian will forever memorialize his legacy and remember his impact on the galaxy far, far away.
