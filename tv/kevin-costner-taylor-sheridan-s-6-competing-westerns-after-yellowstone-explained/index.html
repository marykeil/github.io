Kevin Costner & Taylor Sheridan s 6 Competing Westerns After Yellowstone Explained

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Kevin Costner’s passion project, Horizon, will be a pivotal release for his late-stage career and his return to directing after over twenty years. The release of Horizon: An American Saga - Chapter 2 shortly after the first film could either build up anticipation or backfire for Costner’s ambitious Western movie series. Taylor Sheridan’s spinoff projects, 6666 and 2024, will expand the Yellowstone franchise and carry high stakes in maintaining the prestige and popularity of the original show.

Literature Review

With Kevin Costner and Taylor Sheridan seemingly parting ways as Yellowstone comes to a close, their upcoming Western projects will be in competition.

Kevin Costner and Taylor Sheridan have partnered for five seasons of Yellowstone, but the next few years will see them competing with various Western projects. Sheridan, who previously wrote films like Hell or High Water and Sicario, developed Yellowstone for its 2018 release, and it’s since become one of the biggest TV shows on television. The series has since become one of the most viewed on television. Kevin Costner has led the show as patriarch John Dutton, pushing him to a new career peak late in the game, decades after his initial rise to stardom. Unfortunately, Yellowstone season 5, part 2, has had several controversies, most notably issues surrounding Kevin Costner and Taylor Sheridan. At the moment, it’s tentative whether Costner will appear in the show’s final season, as his priorities have shifted to his self-funded passion project, the Horizon films. His Western movie series will be in competition with Sheridan’s continuous expanse of the Yellowstone franchise. Both Costner and Sheridan will be prominent names in the Western genre going forward, and both have several projects already announced in the genre.

6 Horizon: An American Saga Kevin Costner’s Horizon is a long-term passion project he’s writing, directing, and starring in.

The first installment of Costner’s passion project, which he’s starring in, directing, and co-written, is Horizon: An American Saga. The film will be released on June 28, 2024, starring famous actors like Sam Worthington, Jana Malone, Luke Wilson, and Yellowstone co-stars like Danny Huston and Will Patton. Western movies haven’t been box office draws in the 21st century, but Costner’s Yellowstone platform may have established an audience, creating precedence for a Western as a massive cinematic event. Horizon is a story Costner has pursued for decades and will be his return to directing after over twenty years. His directorial debut, Dances with Wolves, is considered one of the best Westerns ever made, even winning the Oscar for Best Picture. Despite his success with that film and other starring roles in the early ’90s, he couldn’t get Horizon made at the time, though the project lingered with him for decades, as its production was finally announced in early 2022. With the amount Costner has invested in the project, Horizon should be a pivotal release for his late-stage career.

5 Horizon: An American Saga - Chapter 2 Kevin Costner’s Horizon sequel could be a summer blockbuster or a pivotal flop.

Possibly even more impactful for Costner is Horizon: An American Saga - Chapter 2. The sequel will be released shortly after the first installment on August 16, 2024. It’s an interesting experiment with release dates, as even if the first film doesn’t find immediate financial success, it could build up traction for the sequel. However, a month and a half may not be enough time to establish anticipation for a follow-up. Costner reportedly wants four films in his Horizon saga, and receiving the financing for more sequels will ride heavily on Chapter 2. With the cultural phenomenon of Barbie and Oppenheimer as major summer blockbuster films in 2023, it’ll be fascinating to see whether Horizon can find its footing as a similar cultural event. If the first film is well-received critically, the event of a looming sequel releasing just months later could get audiences rushing to the box office. The limited window wouldn’t allow time for the first film to be released on streaming, so audiences wanting to participate in said event would have to make it to theaters to see both. It’s a fascinating strategy that could totally backfire for Costner.

4 6666 Taylor Sheridan’s 6666 will expand the Yellowstone world, centered around Jimmy and the 6666 Ranch.

The Yellowstone spinoff 6666 could completely break the mold of Taylor Sheridan’s Western franchise, expanding it for the first time beyond the Dutton Ranch. While previous spinoffs, 1883 and 1923, explored earlier generations of the Dutton family, 6666 is growing on the franchise by moving to a different ranch and state. The 6666 Ranch has been seen in Yellowstone before, with Jimmy’s season 4 arc taking him there to train as a cowboy. The sequel would continue to focus on Jimmy’s character, offering a familiar face to Yellowstone fans tuning in. 3 2024 2024 is the most direct Yellowstone spinoff from Taylor Sheridan.

The other Yellowstone spinoff would offer a more direct follow-up to the original show, as Taylor Sheridan’s currently titled series, 2024, seems like it could potentially integrate known Dutton family characters, including Kayce, Beth, and Rip. It’s also the show that’s rumored to have Matthew McConaughey starring, and Michelle Pfieffer has recently entered discussions for the show’s cast. Neither star is confirmed, and McConaughey reportedly won’t sign on until he’s read a script (via Puck). The future of 2024 is up in the air, but more news should be available soon. 2024 is as pivotal to Taylor Sheridan as Horizon is to Kevin Costner, as the spinoff has the most pressure in moving the Yellowstone franchise forward. The prequel shows have been excellent so far, but 2024 will be the most direct continuation of the saga, proving whether it can maintain the same prestige and popularity in Costner’s exit. Sheridan has proven he can write compelling television at a rapid rate, but the centerpiece of his Western franchise needs to be an enormous hit to continue growing the series.

2 Landman Taylor Sheridan’s next standalone Western series will star Billy Bob Thornton.

Landman
Drama

Cast Billy Bob Thornton , Ali Larter , Michelle Randolph , Jacob Lofland , Alejandro Akara , James Jordan , Kayla Wallace , Paulina Chávez , Mark Collie Seasons 1 Creator(s) Taylor Sheridan , Christian Wallace Where To Watch Paramount+

Taylor Sheridan has produced various series for Paramount+, from Westerns like Lawmen: Bass Reeves to crime dramas like Mayor of Kingstown. Not all of his work has been connected to the Yellowstone franchise, and Landman seems to be another standalone Western series. Landman looks like a particularly promising series, with Billy Bob Thornton set to lead and Ali Larter and Demi Moore signed on to co-star. Landman may not be the most impactful series for Sheridan coming up, as it has no broader franchise implications, though it’ll be another expansion of Paramount’s growing original television library.

1 Empire Of The Summer Moon Taylor Sheridan has acquired the rights to another Western story, which he’ll adapt and direct.

Not to be confused with the recent Martin Scorsese movie, Empire of the Summer Moon is a book that Taylor Sheridan recently won the rights to (via Deadline). The book examines the history of the Comanche tribe and their struggles with American frontiersmen, and could be one of the most action-packed and unique Western experiences put on screen. It’s unknown right now whether Sheridan is planning to adapt Empire of the Summer Moon into a TV series or film, but it could be a significant project for him. If he directs it as a film, it could compete with Horizon. Sources: Puck, Deadline
