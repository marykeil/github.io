Ariel s 6 Sisters In Disney s Little Mermaid Remake Explained (& What The Movie Changed)

Contents

SUMMARY

Ariel has six older sisters who are responsible for different parts of the sea and assist King Triton in his duties. Each sister has unique characteristics and roles, such as Mala being fearless and committed to protecting the reefs, and Caspia being selfless and putting her people first. The live-action remake provides more details about Ariel’s sisters through merchandise, showcasing their individual personalities and storylines.

Literature Review

Ariel’s sisters were included in the live-action version of The Little Mermaid, but they are very different from their animated versions.

The live-action remake of Disney’s The Little Mermaid made a couple of changes to the animated classic, among them Ariel’s sisters, who, though they appear in the movie, are very different from their animated versions. The list of Disney’s live-action remakes continues to grow, and now part of it is The Little Mermaid. Director Rob Marshall and writer David Magee took the 1989 animated classic (based on the 1837 fairy tale of the same name by Hans Christian Andersen) and brought it to life with Halle Bailey as Princess Ariel and Jonah Hauer-King as Prince Eric. Watch on Disney+ The Little Mermaid’s live-action remake follows the same story as the animated version, with mermaid Ariel being fascinated by the human world and making a dangerous deal with sea witch Ursula (Melissa McCarthy) to become human. The Little Mermaid brought the most notable characters from the animated movie to life, along with some minor characters that are an essential part of Ariel’s life. Falling into the latter category are Ariel’s sisters, who briefly appear in the 1989 adaptation and have even less screen time in the 2023 live-action version.

7 Ariel Has Six Older Sisters The Seven Daughters Represent The Seven Seas

Though Ariel’s mother is absent from both the original Disney animated movie and the modern live-action remake, she still has a large family under the sea. Ariel is the youngest of Triton’s seven daughters. In the original animated movie, her sisters are Attina, Alana, Aquata, Arista, Adella, and Andrina. There is clearly a theme with all of their names beginning with “A,” but that changes in the live-action movie. It’s also not the only change for The Little Mermaid. Ariel’s sisters might have a brief role in the live-action movie, but the roles they play in the sea are significant. Each of Ariel’s sisters is responsible for a specific part of the sea, reporting back to their father on the state of their corner of the ocean. They help to clean up shipwrecks and pollution caused by humans as well. Their brief time on screen shows that they all share in King Triton’s responsibilities for the sea. Because the roles of Ariel’s sisters are limited in the live-action The Little Mermaid, much of what the audience learns about them individually is from Disney’s merchandise. The sisters feature in lines of dolls and books, and there is a lot of information about them in that merchandise.

6 Mala Played By Karolina Conchet

Princess Mala represents Princess Attina from the animated movie, Triton’s firstborn daughter. Mala is the ruler of the Chaine Sea, and her color palette is hot pink, orange, and yellow. Mala is described as the most fearless out of all of Triton’s daughters (though that might be debatable after everything Ariel went through), and she’s committed to protecting the reefs at any cost. As such, she hates shipwrecks, as they pollute the ocean. Mala is a natural and strong leader, and she’s also known for her sense of humor. Mala used to send messages on seaweed scrolls to humans before King Triton forbade any interactions with humans, and following her mother’s death, she started hating humans and the damage they do to the sea. As Triton’s eldest daughter, Attina is the first in line to take over Atlantica, so King Triton has prepared her well for that. As a result, Attina is the less impulsive of the sisters, and she constantly worries about not letting her father down.

5 Indira Played By Simone Ashley

Princess Indira is inspired by Princess Alana from the animated version, and she serves as the ruler of the Brinedive Sea. Indira is a yellow-orange mermaid, with these colors representing the sunny and warm waters of the Brinedive Sea. Indira is described as the most generous of all the daughters of the sea, and she’s also empathetic, generous, and virtuous – however, she despised humans just as much as her father did, until Ariel’s marriage to Eric changed the ideas they had of humans. Princess Alana, on the other hand, isn’t exactly a leader, and she’s more focused on looking beautiful than on the politics of the Seven Seas. Although she might seem shallow and self-obsessed, Alana is kind, soft-spoken, and intelligent.

4 Caspia Played By Nathalie Sorrell

Princess Caspia is based on Princess Adella from the original animated movie. Princess Caspia is the ruler of the Apneic Sea, and she has blonde-pink hair, with her color palette being orange, yellow, and different shades of blue. Princess Caspia is described as the most selfless of all of Ariel’s sisters, always putting her people first, which makes her a kind and compassionate leader. However, Princess Caspia is said to be a bit of a recluse and only travels for meetings. Just like Mala, she’s very protective of the corals and comes up with various laws to ensure that they can be safe in her kingdom. The animated Princess Adella isn’t as selfless as the live-action Princess Caspia, and she’s all about beauty, making her quite vain. Princess Adella cares more about boys than her family, and is always desperate to talk to them.

3 Tamika Played By Sienna King

Princess Tamika is based on Princess Aquata from the animated movie. Princess Tamika is the ruler of the Fracus Sea, and she has one of the most detailed and vibrant designs, wearing blue, orange, red, and yellow, with details on her arms, neck, and forehead, representing the vibrant fish and coral found in her sea. Princess Tamika is described as the toughest of the daughters of the sea, as well as the most tactical one said to “rule with an iron fin”. However, Princess Tamika is said to rule with grace and an admiral’s strength, and she will fight sharks to keep her people safe. The animated Princess Aquata isn’t as tough as Princess Tamika, and she especially hates when her sisters borrow her things without her permission, and she’s willing to do whatever she needs to get them back.

2 Karina Played By Kajsa Mohammar

Princess Karina is based on Princess Arista from the animated movie. Princess Karina is the ruler of the Saithe Sea (the Dark Sea), which is mostly coated with ice, which is why she has white hair and wears white, black, and different shades of blue. Princess Karina is described as the most decisive, intuitive, and reclusive of Ariel’s sisters, and developed a fear of humans after her mother died (though she got over it after Ariel’s marriage to Princess Eric). She doesn’t have much in common with her animated counterpart. Princess Arista tends to “borrow” things from her oldest sisters, especially Aquata, and she has a zest for adventure just like Ariel.

1 Perla Played By Lorena Andrea

Princess Perla is based on Princess Andrina from the 1989 animated version of The Little Mermaid, and she’s the ruler of the Piton Sea. Perla is described as the most charismatic of all of King Triton’s daughters (yes, even more than Ariel), and she’s all about warmth and kindness, so much so that it’s said that she once charmed and stopped a group of orcas from attacking a colony of penguins. Princess Perla is a red mermaid with some white and blue details on her tail, most likely a representation of the temperate and deep waters she rules over. As a ruler, Perla is said to do so with a good temperament and is very careful with the choices she makes, putting decisions to a vote to keep the peace. Just like King Triton and most of her sisters, she started hating humans after the death of her mother. In the animated movie and its expanded media, her counterpart, Andrina is the least developed of Ariel’s sisters, so not much is known about her other than she’s portrayed as a sort of “class clown”, bringing a lot of sass and sarcasm to the original The Little Mermaid.
