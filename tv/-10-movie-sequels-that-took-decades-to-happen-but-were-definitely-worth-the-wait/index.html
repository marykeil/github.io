10 Movie Sequels That Took Decades To Happen But Were Definitely Worth The Wait

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Sequels to beloved franchises often face skepticism and resistance due to the risk of disappointing newer and older audiences alike. Strong material, inspired performances, and fresh perspectives can make long-awaited sequels worth the wait and improve their overall quality. Movies like “The Incredibles 2,” “Bad Boys for Life,” and “Rocky Balboa” prove that well-executed sequels can enhance the laughs, action, and drama of their predecessors.

Literature Review

More often than not, long-awaited movie sequels don’t work out for many reasons. However, there are a few notable examples of some being successful.

Although Hollywood takes a major risk whenever it releases a sequel to a movie that first came out decades prior, a few notable movie sequels have proven the wait well worth the risk. Hollywood’s decision to release a sequel to a movie that was released so many years ago is a big risk because newer audiences may not be familiar with the newest installment’s source material, thus feeling less compelled to go through the trouble of seeing it. Meanwhile, older audiences might not see it in protest as they’ll feel the sequels will be underwhelming and ruin the original. Furthermore, a huge issue that sequels to beloved franchises also face is the undeniable feeling that they’re made purely as cash grabs, a major contributing factor to why audiences may not engage with the material. There exist many valid reasons to skip out on sequels to movies that are decades old. However, cinema history has proven time and again that if the material is strong, the performances of the significantly older leads are inspired, and the sequels offer something fresh and interesting to say about their predecessors, Hollywood can produce movie sequels that turn out to be worth the wait.

17 Iconic Movie Franchises Where The Best Film Is A Sequel While most movie franchises lose something in the churning out of sequels, some series see their best installments come after the original film.

10 The Incredibles 2 The laughs and visuals from 2004’s The Incredibles are improved significantly in the 2018 sequel.

While The Incredibles 2’s villain didn’t live up to the high expectations set by its predecessor’s Syndrome, The Incredibles 2 still tells a compelling story that centers around family and independence, while boasting some of Pixar’s best visuals ever seen in one of their movies. Furthermore, the voice-over performances from everyone involved prove that each actor was invested in the source material as they offer plenty of joy and laughs, thus supporting the argument that inspired performances in years-long sequels can strongly improve its overall quality. While The Incredibles is a classic, The Incredibles 2 is a visually impressive follow-up. The Incredibles 2

Release Date June 15, 2018 Director Brad Bird Cast Sarah Vowell , Samuel L. Jackson , Holly Hunter , Craig T. Nelson , John Ratzenberger , Catherine Keener , Huck Milner , Bob Odenkirk Runtime 2h 6m

9 Bad Boys For Life Bad Boys for Life is the 2020 sequel to 2003’s Bad Boys II.

Bad Boys for Life sees A-listers Will Smith and Martin Lawrence team up once again as their iconic buddy cop duo, Mike Lowry and Marcus Burnett respectively, to investigate a notorious pair of mother and son drug lords in 2020s Miami, Florida. The fun chemistry shared between leads Smith and Lawrence, tongue-in-cheek jokes about growing old, and its fresh-faced cast of characters comprising of May December’s Charles Melton and High School Musical 3: Senior Year’s Vanessa Hudgens, Bad Boys for Life offers enough laughs and action to make it one of the best sequels of the 2020s so far. Bad Boys for Life

Release Date January 15, 2020 Director Adil El Arbi , Bilall Fallah Cast Will Smith , Martin Lawrence , Kate del Castillo , Joe Pantoliano , Vanessa Hudgens , Alexander Ludwig , Charles Melton Runtime 124 Minutes

8 Rocky Balboa 2006’s Rocky Balboa sees Sylvester Stallone lace up the gloves again since 1990s Rocky V.

One of the longest gaps between sequels in a well-established movie franchise, Rocky Balboa sees the legendary titular boxer come out of retirement at sixty years old to take on the reigning, undisputed heavyweight champion of the world, Mason “The Line” Dixon, in a movie that’s rife with just as much heartwarming drama regarding family and one’s legacy as it is with testosterone-ladened energy and action-packed boxing scenes. With real-life heavyweight boxer Anotonio Tarver delivering a solid performance that makes Mason one of Rocky’s best opponents, Rocky Balboa delivers a solid sequel despite its absurd premise. Rocky Balboa

Release Date December 20, 2006 Director Sylvester Stallone Cast Sylvester Stallone Runtime 102minutes

7 Clerks II Clerks II contains Clerks’ signaure brand of humor twelve years after the original’s release.

Written and directed by Kevin Smith, Clerks II sees beloved characters Dante and Randal return in Smith’s View Askewniverse - that is, Smith’s cinematic universe in which many classic characters, places, themes, and even plot points coexist, in a raunchy comedy rife with brilliant performances from some of the industry’s best actors and actresses. While Clerks II is hilarious, its careful handling of themes about acceptance and moving on from one juncture of life to the next while infusing his special brand of humor are what make Clerks II a good sequel, and a strong entry in the franchise. Clerks 2

Release Date July 21, 2006 Director Kevin Smith Cast Kevin Smith , Jeff Anderson , Rosario Dawson Runtime 97minutes

6 The Color of Money The Color of Money presents its protagonist from 1961’s The Hustler in a new and compelling light.

The Color of Money is the engaging sequel to 1961’s The Hustler, and it sees Tom Cruise’s Vincent Lauria go toe-to-toe against Paul Newman’s Eddie “Fast Eddie” Felson in a tense sports drama about loyalty and greed, all while infusing director Martin Scorsese’s artistic visionary storytelling that reinforces why he’s one of the industries best filmmakers. Although The Color of Money is stylistically different than The Hustler, featuring more quick cuts and sweeping cinematography than its predecessor, it thrives on its ability to redefine Fast Eddie as its villain, thus heightening the father-son messaging.

Disney’s Star Wars Sequel Trilogy Only Has ONE Bad Movie Audiences look at the Sequel Trilogy of Star Wars as a disgrace to the franchise, but the truth is that only one of the three films was a bad movie.

5 Star Wars: The Force Awakens Star Wars: The Force Awakens sets the stage for more Star Wars stories.

2015’s Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens became the first new entry in the Star Wars saga ten years after the release of Star Wars: Episode III - Revenge of the Sith, creating an immense amount of hype and excitement shortly after its announcement. Featuring brand-new characters as well as beloved established characters, Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens perfectly demonstrated what Disney had planned for the main entries in the franchise while also setting up possibilities in other forms of media and entertainment. Furthermore, Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens proved to audiences and critics that the beloved space opera was perfectly capable of continuing in the modern era by brilliantly connecting the newer characters to the beloved characters of the previous trilogies. While subsequent entries in the sequel trilogy didn’t quite maintain the same level of excitement as Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens, its existence left an indelible mark on the franchise, and the entertainment industry at large. Star Wars: Episode VII - The Force Awakens

Release Date December 17, 2015 Director J.J. Abrams Cast Daisy Ridley , John Boyega , Oscar Isaac , Harrison Ford , Mark Hamill , Carrie Fisher , Adam Driver , Andy Serkis , Lupita Nyong’o , Max Von Sydow , Peter Mayhew , Simon Pegg Runtime 136 Minutes

4 Bladerunner 2049 Bladerunner 2049 successfully explores the world and themes 35 years later.

Bladerunner 2049 features many of the same themes and philosophies that were explored in 1982’s Bladerunner while including impressive visuals and complex characters that help further enrich the unique world created by author Philip K. Dick and filmmaker Ridley Scott. Additionally, Bladerunner 2049’s blend of action and drama allows it to explore the concepts of Artificial Intelligence, something very timely now more than during the time of its release, in a way that compels audiences to engage with the movie beyond a simple sci-fi action flick. Although not a commercial success, Bladreunner 2049 is an excellent thought-provoking affair.

3 Toy Story 3 Toy Story 3 dramatizes the lives of its beloved characters more than ten years later.

Released in 2010, Toy Story 3 sees its iconic characters, Woody, Buzz, and Jessie, embark on a new mission to thwart the plans of the treacherous Lotso, while also attempting to find a way to keep the now college-bound Andy in their lives. Rife with excellent voice-over performances, Toy Story 3 is a visually impressive movie that highlights themes of love, friendship, and acceptance in a way atypical of movies catered toward child audiences. Although hilarious, Toy Story 3 truly excels in delivering its drama in an impactful and meaningful way. Toy Story 3

Release Date June 18, 2010 Director Lee Unkrich Cast Jodi Benson , Ned Beatty , Tim Allen , Tom Hanks , Joan Cusack Runtime 103 minutes

2 Top Gun: Maverick Top Gun: Maverick successfully reignites interest in the Top Gun franchise 30+ years later.

Top Gun: Maverick follows renegade test pilot turned flight instructor, Captain Pete “Maverick” Mitchell as he continues to push the envelope and rub his superiors the wrong way, in a story that’s just as much about regret and family as it is about Tom Cruise performing incredible stunts once again. Top Gun: Maverick surpasses its predecessor by emphasizing the emotional aspects of the story and highlighting the damage that Maverick’s rebellious ways on other people. With likable characters, great action, and Top Gun 3 in development, Top Gun: Maverick successfully revived a nearly forgotten series. Top Gun: Maverick

Release Date May 27, 2022 Director Joseph Kosinski Cast Jennifer Connelly , Jake Picking , Raymond Lee , Tom Cruise , Lewis Pullman , Monica Barbaro , Miles Teller , Danny Ramirez , Val Kilmer , Manny Jacinto , Ed Harris , Glen Powell , Jon Hamm Runtime 130 minutes

1 Mad Max: Fury Road Mad Max: Fury Road blends its high-octane action with its drama more effectively than previous entries.

Mad Max: Fury Road not only sees the return of the titular anti-hero, Max Rockatansky, but it introduces viewers to new characters like Immortan Joe and Furiosa, in an explosive post-apocalyptic action movie that highlights the franchise’s signature aesthetic. 2015’s Mad Max: Fury Road’s themes of survival improved since 1985’s Mad Max Beyond Thunderdome; its strong feminist themes and protagonist Furiosa offer a new perspective in the otherwise testosterone-dominated affair. Combined with excellent performances from leads Charlize Theron and Tom Hardy and engaging action, Mad Max: Fury Road is perhaps the best long-awaited movie sequel ever made.
