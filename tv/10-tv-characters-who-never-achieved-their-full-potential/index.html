10 TV Characters Who Never Achieved Their Full Potential

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

TV characters can’t all get equal attention, resulting in untapped potential for some. Supporting characters with brilliant performances can be more compelling than the main characters. Some TV characters could have had bigger roles and added depth to their respective shows.

Literature Review

TV shows with ensemble casts can’t give everyone equal attention, and this often leads to some great characters having their potential wasted.

TV shows which juggle a lot of characters can’t give them all equal attention, and this means that sometimes a great character fails to live up to their potential. Difficult decisions often have to be made with long-running TV shows, so some characters are cut down in their prime before their story fully develops. Sometimes this is due to practical concerns with the actors, but most frequently it’s a conscious decision to sideline supporting characters in favor of a show’s stars. Unfortunately, this leaves a lot of characters with untapped potential. TV characters who deserve more than what they get can be found in plenty of shows. Brilliant performances or inspired writing can elevate side characters to being among the most compelling in the entire show, so it can feel as if they have a much longer story to tell even when their arc reaches its conclusion. Audiences grow attached to characters who they spend a lot of time watching, and when fan-favorite characters are killed off or fade into the background it can be very disappointing. Even if their shows were successful, some TV characters could have had much bigger roles.

10 TV Shows That Should Have Killed Main Characters (& When) Usually, the death of a main character in a TV series is devastating, but there are cases when the show would’ve been better off with a death.

10 Sal (Mad Men) Played by Bryan Batt

Release Date July 19, 2007 Cast Jon Hamm , Elisabeth Moss , Vincent Kartheiser , January Jones , Christina Hendricks , Bryan Batt , Aaron Staton , Rich Sommer Streaming Service(s) AMC Plus

Just as Sal was starting to grapple with his identity, he was fired from Sterling Cooper.

Salvatore Romano immediately stands out from the chauvinists at Sterling Cooper Advertising Agency. He still engages in the usual misogynistic banter, but there’s a barely perceptible note of performance to his comments. As it turns out, Sal’s whole life is a performance, and he conceals his homosexuality from everyone, even himself. Just as Sal was starting to grapple with his identity, he was fired from Sterling Cooper after rejecting the advances of an executive from Lucky Strike. If Sal had stayed as one of Mad Men’s main characters, he could have brought something different to the show.

9 Ruth Powers (The Simpsons) Voiced by Pamela Reed

Release Date December 17, 1989 Cast Tress MacNeille , Julie Kavner , Harry Shearer , Pamela Hayden , Nancy Cartwright , Hank Azaria , Dan Castellaneta , Yeardley Smith Network FOX

The Simpsons has far more interesting male characters than female ones, and building up Ruth Powers’ role could have been one way to redress this imbalance. In a town full of caricatures - the bumbling police chief, the jaded children’s entertainer, the greedy industrialist - Ruth feels more like a real person. She’s a single mother who feels trapped in the everyday drudgery of life, but to Marge, she represents a romantic sense of individualistic freedom. Ruth could have been one of The Simpsons’ best female characters, but she fades away after the season 5 episode “Marge on the Lam.”

8 Creed Bratton (The Office) Played by Creed Bratton

Release Date March 24, 2005 Cast Mindy Kaling , Jenna Fischer , Kate Flannery , Ed Helms , Craig Robinson , Paul Lieberstein , Ellie Kemper , B.J. Novak , Angela Kinsey , Oscar Nunez , Rainn Wilson , Brian Baumgartner , Phyllis Smith , Leslie David Baker , Creed Bratton , Steve Carell , John Krasinski Network NBC

The documentary crew at Dunder Mifflin don’t have time to focus on every employee, so Creed mostly takes up space in the background. When he does get on camera, Creed shows time and time again that he is an unpredictable wildcard. Creed wasn’t meant to be on The Office in any meaningful way, and his infrequent appearances have added to his mysterious allure. There are plenty of hints to his sordid extracurricular activities, such as when he shows up to the Halloween party covered in blood, but the show could have played up these mysteries a little more.

7 Lonny Flash (Hunters) Played by Josh Radnor

Release Date February 21, 2020 Cast logan lerman , Al Pacino , Josh Radnor , Kate Mulvany Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

Lonny is often the forgotten man in Hunters. The self-important actor acts as the comic relief at time, but he has a surprising amount of depth which makes him much more than just a stereotype. The relationship between Jonah and Meyer drove the first season, but Hunters season 2 could have benefited from ditching the flashback sequences and giving some of the secondary characters more to do. While Joe and Sister Harriet become more important, Lonny is only important due to his relationship with Roxy. This is a shame, because Josh Radnor’s charming comedic performance deserves more attention.

6 Jenna (What We Do In The Shadows) Played by Beanie Feldstein

Release Date March 27, 2019 Cast Doug Jones , Natasia Demetriou , Matt Berry , Mark Proksch , Kayvan Novak , Harvey Guillen , Kristen Schaal Streaming Service(s) Hulu

Jenna is brought to the vampires’ household as a potential meal, but Nadja eventually takes an interest in her, and decides to turn her into a vampire. Nadja takes on an unlikely maternal role, but Jenna brings out the best in her. Giving Jenna a bigger role would also have changed Guillermo’s place in the household, since he is always so envious of the people who his housemates decide to turn instead of him. Jenna is still alive, so there’s some hope she could return for What We Do in the Shadows season 6.

5 Kino Loy (Andor) Played by Andy Serkis

Release Date September 21, 2022 Cast Genevieve O’Reilly , Adria Arjona , Diego Luna , Kyle Soller , Alan Tudyk , Stellan Skarsgård , Denise Gough , Forrest Whittaker Streaming Service(s) Disney Plus

Andy Serkis stole the show in Andor as Kino Loy, a prisoner alongside Cassian on Narkina 5. Kino is a vital leader of the facility-wide riot and uprising which leads to Cassian’s escape, although he himself can’t swim, so he appears to stay behind. Andy Serkis has hinted at his Star Wars return, so hopefully Kino could become a more central character. He’s too compelling to waste on just a few episodes, and Serkis would be a great addition to the main cast. Kino has a lot of the qualities that the rebellion needs. Diego Luna recently revealed that Andor season 2 is close to wrapping up filming.

4 Barry Zuckerkorn (Arrested Development) Played by Henry Winkler

Release Date March 15, 2019 Cast Jeffrey Tambor , Ron Howard , Michael Cera , Alia Shawkat , David Cross , Jason Bateman , Portia de Rossi , Tony Hale , Will Arnett , Jessica Walter Streaming Service(s) Netflix

Henry Winkler’s incompetent lawyer is one of the show’s best recurring characters, but he still could have had a more important role.

Arrested Development’s Bluth family require a lot of legal help, and they have to rely on the dim-witted and easily flustered Barry Zuckerkorn. According to his own business card, Barry is “very good,” but in reality he doesn’t even understand the law. Henry Winkler’s incompetent lawyer is one of the show’s best recurring characters, but he still could have had a more important role. Barry is so useless that Michael often takes it upon himself to construct his father’s case. He does this for “Mock Trial for J. Reinhold,” but his dynamic with Barry is just as fun.

3 Moscow (Money Heist) Played by Paco Tous

Release Date May 2, 2017 Cast Álvaro Morte , Darko Perić , Fernando Cayo , Maria Pedraza , Paco Tous , Rodrigo De la Serna , Enrique Arce , Belén Cuesta , Pedro Alonso , Hovik Keuchkerian , Jaime Lorente , Esther Acebo Streaming Service(s) Netflix

Moscow is one of the key members of The Professor’s team in Money Heist. Moscow is Denver’s father, meaning that he is more personally invested than most of the other robbers. His decisions have led him into an extremely risky heist where he and his son are both on the brink of being caught. Moscow also acts as a paternal figure to other members of the gang, such as Rio and Tokyo. His death is a tragic scene so early on, but killing him off so soon means that his full potential is cut short.

2 Dr. Spaceman (30 Rock) Played by Chris Parnell

Release Date October 11, 2006 Cast Tina Fey , Alec Baldwin , Tracy Morgan , Jane Krakowski , Jack McBrayer , Scott Adsit , Judah Friedlander , Katrina Bowden , Keith Powell , Lonny Ross Streaming Service(s) Peacock

Dr. Spaceman is Tracy’s comically useless doctor in 30 Rock. He is originally introduced as a punchline, a snippet of Tracy’s incoherent rambling which turns out to make perfect sense, but he became a popular recurring character. Chris Parnell’s performance as Dr. Spaceman is a huge reason why the character works so well, equipped with plenty of nonsensical one-liners which underline his lack of medical knowledge. He’s everything that a recurring character in a sitcom should be, but he could have been more directly involved, even for just an episode or two.

1 Ra’Nell (Top Boy) Played by Malcolm Kamulete

Release Date 2011-00-00 Cast Ashley Walters Seasons 5

Top Boy could have shown his evolution from childhood innocence to a life much closer to Dushane and Sully’s, or maybe he would have chosen a different path.

Ra’Nell is one of the most important characters in the first two seasons of Top Boy. Alongside his friend Gem, Ra’Nell shows how the younger generation at Summerhouse turn to dealing drugs when they are left with no other viable options. Ra’Nell wants to provide for his mother as gentrification creeps toward the neighborhood, but his character disappears after season 2. If he had stayed, Top Boy could have shown his evolution from childhood innocence to a life much closer to Dushane and Sully’s, or maybe he would have chosen a different path.
