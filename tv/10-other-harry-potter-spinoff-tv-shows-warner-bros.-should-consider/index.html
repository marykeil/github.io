10 Other Harry Potter Spinoff TV Shows Warner Bros. Should Consider

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Warner Bros and HBO are open to developing multiple TV series based on the Harry Potter universe, including potential spinoffs such as a Cursed Child adaptation and a prequel series on the Marauders and Snape. The Fantastic Beasts movies may not continue as planned, but a TV series focusing on Newt Scamander and Albus Dumbledore could be a better format for exploring their stories. There is potential for other TV series set in the Wizarding World, such as a Voldemort prequel, a Quidditch-based drama, or a series centered around the Ministry of Magic or other wizarding schools. A prequel series on the Hogwarts founders in the Middle Ages could also be intriguing.

Literature Review

Warner Bros is considering other TV show ideas to go along with Max’s Harry Potter remake, and we have a few suggestions about what they should be.

The Harry Potter remake may not be the only TV series Warner Bros and HBO create, so a variety of other Wizarding World stories should be considered. Though a 10-year-long show following Harry’s story has been confirmed, it was recently reported (via Screen Rant) that Warner Bros Discovery is open to “developing more than one idea based on Harry Potter.” A group of writers has gotten together to discuss their pitches, so other TV series may soon be in the works as well. The news that HBO would be creating a full Harry Potter remake, with every season dedicated to each of J.K. Rowling’s books, was a surprise since there are various Wizarding World stories that fans expected to happen first. So far, the only spinoffs to follow the successful Warner Bros movies have been the Fantastic Beasts movies and the Harry Potter and the Cursed Child stageplay, and neither of these has been explored as far as their potential. However, the news that Warner Bros and Max are open to multiple Harry Potter shows means these stories and more could make it to a TV format.

10 Harry Potter Book Details That Can Massively Improve The Show Compared To The Movies The Harry Potter movies are hard to beat, but there is a way for HBO’s TV reboot to improve its chances — by treating J.K. Rowling’s books as gospel.

10 A Harry Potter & The Cursed Child TV Series Harry Potter’s sequel may be better adapted for TV.

Since Harry Potter and the Cursed Child hit the stage (with the screenplay published in book form) in 2016, fans have been looking out for a movie announcement. Of course, this would be easier said than done. The story of Harry’s son, Albus Potter, was written for theater and has a very different tone and structure than the other Harry Potter movies. However, the story itself holds great potential. The announcement that Warner Bros would be remaking Harry Potter into a TV series seems to kill all Cursed Child movie hopes, but a TV show might be an even better medium.

9 A Marauders Generation Prequel Series A TV series could expand on the Marauders, Snape, & Lily’s stories.

One of the more highly requested Harry Potter spinoffs is a story following the Maruader generation. Harry’s parents and their friends got up to a lot of mischief during their day at Hogwarts, and the consequences of their choices heavily impacted Harry’s later story. Therefore, a TV series exploring James Potter, Sirius Black, Remus Lupin, Peter Pettigrew, and Lily Evans and Severus Snape could create the perfect foundation for the Harry Potter remake. They could even play out simultaneously, allowing for some crossover episodes as Harry learns about his parents.

8 A Fantastic Beasts & Where To Find Them Series Fantastic Beasts Can Continue in a TV format.

The Fantastic Beasts movies had a good start, but things quickly fell apart. There were initially supposed to be five installments, but the franchise’s future is unclear after the third movie, Fantastic Beasts: The Secrets of Dumbledore, underperformed at the box office. Still, fans haven’t forgotten about Newt Scamander, and Albus Dumbledore’s backstory has always been interesting. Though Fantastic Beasts didn’t work as a movie series, a TV show might prove to be a better format. Every episode could explore a new titular beast while Dumbledore’s tale continues to play out.

7 A Voldemort Prequel Series There’s a lot more to learn about Lord Voldemort’s story.

The Harry Potter movies left out a great deal about Voldemort’s past and family history, and this was a significant disappointment. It’s assumed that, as a book-faithful adaptation, HBO’s Harry Potter remake will remedy this, but there may be an even better way to add a stronger foundation to the Dark Lord’s story. A prequel centering on Voldemort’s life before Harry Potter, including the details surrounding his conception, upbringing, and rise to power, could begin around the same time as HBO’s main series. This would allow it to catch up with Harry’s story by Half-Blood Prince.

6 A Quidditch-Based TV Series A Harry Potter sports drama could be the perfect companion for the remake.

As exciting as spinoff series following characters like Voldemort or the Marauders would be, it’s possible that these stories would be thoroughly covered by the Harry Potter remake itself. For this reason, it may be better if Warner Bros’ other series followed a brand new story set in the wizarding world. A possible example would be a Quidditch series, loosely based on the structure of Rowling’s Quidditch Through the Ages book. The series could center on a professional Quidditch team and feature all the drama and wizarding world fun with brand-new characters (with a few Harry Potter character cameos, of course).

5 An Order Of The Phoenix Series A dark prequel series following the First Wizarding War would make a great TV show.

The First Wizarding War happened before Harry Potter, so audiences never saw the birth of Dumbledore’s Order of the Phoenix. However, the way it’s described to Harry, Voldemort’s first war was even worse than the second, with far more than just Lily and James Potter meeting tragic ends. A TV spinoff following these events would most certainly be dark, but it would mean a better understanding of what Voldemort’s initial downfall meant to the wizarding world. Moreover, it would allow characters like Frank and Alice Longbottom to finally shine.

4 A Ministry Of Magic Series A Ministry of Magic Series could be an intriguing political drama.

The Ministry of Magic is often seen as the root of all evil in the Harry Potter series, which makes it an exciting setting for a potential second TV series. There’s no question that a lot of shady things have happened at the British wizarding government, so a political drama seems especially fitting. The show could potentially center around one of the more notable Ministers for Magic and follow a Game of Thrones-like format that would see characters vying to get their adversaries out of their way to take power. Perhaps Dolores Umbridge could even make an appearance.

3 A TV Series Following Other Wizarding Schools A Harry Potter TV spinoff could bounce from school to school.

The Harry Potter books and movies introduced other European wizarding schools, but it was later revealed that there are several worldwide. Rowling has detailed on the Wizarding World website the general locations of these schools and how they differ from Hogwarts, and this has opened the door to many intriguing stories. A possible TV spinoff series could jump between each of these, tying the stories of various characters together in unique ways. It would mean an exciting and diverse tale that takes the wizarding far beyond what anyone has seen yet.

All 7 Wizarding Schools In Harry Potter (& Where They’re Located)  The wizarding world of Harry Potter is full of skillfully concealed magical schools, but there are seven that have gained worldwide renown.

2 A Herpo The Foul Prequel Series This Harry Potter spinoff would be set in Ancient Greece.

Though it’s not a name mentioned in the Harry Potter books or movies, Herpo the Foul is surprisingly important to the central plot of Harry’s story. He was a Dark wizard from ancient Greece and is remembered as the first-ever breeder of Basilisks and the inventor of Horcruxes. As a Parselmouth, it’s often assumed that he was an ancestor to Salazar Slytherin and, therefore, Voldemort himself. A TV series following this character would be fascinating since there are so many unanswered questions regarding his character—like what his Horcrux was and what happened to it. Herpo the foul’s Horcrux was never found, which means he could still be alive during the events of Harry Potter (either as a formless soul or within a new body).

1 A Hogwarts Founders Prequel Series A Middle Ages Harry Potter TV prequel would expand the Wizarding World

Most Harry Potter fans can quickly answer which Hogwarts House they belong to, but relatively little is known about the Founders themselves. Helga Hufflepuff, Salazar Slytherin, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Godric Gryffindor are the four powerful witches and wizards responsible for creating Hogwarts, and their legacies (and falling outs) have a significant impact on the story of Harry Potter. Not only would their individual stories be worth a TV remake, but the Middle Ages setting would bring a whole new flavor to the Wizarding World.
