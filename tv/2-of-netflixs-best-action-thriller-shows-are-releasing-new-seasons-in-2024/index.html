2 Of Netflixs Best Action Thriller Shows Are Releasing New Seasons In 2024

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Exciting news for fans of action thrillers on Netflix as both “The Night Agent” and “The Recruit” are returning for second seasons in 2024. These shows share similarities, including young agents caught in dangerous political webs, but also have key differences, such as cast and storyline. Viewers can expect answers and intense action in the upcoming seasons, but the success of these shows will depend on audience reception and viewership numbers.

Literature Review

2024 will be a big year for Netflix as the streaming service introduces new seasons of two of its most popular action series of the previous year.

A new year means new content on streaming services, and it seems that Netflix already has a very exciting line-up as two of its most popular action thriller shows, The Night Agent and The Recruit, are returning for second seasons in 2024. Last year, both The Night Agent and The Recruit rose to the top of Netflix’s ranks, garnering incredible amounts of views and fans. This was especially notable considering that both series were debuting their first seasons in 2023. Now, this popularity is paying off as the series gear up for their highly-anticipated next seasons.

It isn’t a big surprise that both The Night Agent and The Recruit did well in the same year. The series share a lot of commonalities, with the most prevalent being their genre. Both shows are action thrillers that follow young agents who, beyond their control, get sucked into complex webs of local and international politics which could easily turn out to be deadly. While The Night Agent centers on Gabriel Basso’s FBI agent, Peter Sutherland, The Recruit follows Noah Centineo’s CIA lawyer, Owen Hendricks. Either way, both series have something in them that make audiences want more and more.

The Night Agent & The Recruit Are Both Returning In 2024

Considering the successes of The Night Agent and The Recruit, it’s very exciting that both shows will return with new seasons in 2024. Back in March 2023, during its very first week of release, The Night Agent was renewed for a season 2, but it was only recently confirmed by Netflix that The Night Agent season 2 is currently filming. The new season does not have a release date, but is potentially slated for 2024. The Night Agent season 2 will see the return of Gabriel Basso and Luciane Buchanan, along with the introduction of Teddy Sears and Brittany Snow.

Meanwhile, The Recruit is in a similar boat. The show was released at the tail end of 2022, and in January 2023, it was quickly renewed for a second season. Only a week or two before The Night Agent season 2 was announced, The Recruit season 2 got its announcement. According to Netflix, the second season started filming in January 2024, and most are expecting it to be released in December 2024, similar to the first season. Noah Centineo will certainly return as Owen Hendricks, alongside many newcomers.

One new addition to The Recruit season 2 is Teo Yoo, who recently starred in 2023’s Past Lives.

How The Recruit & The Night Agent Are Similar (& How They’re Different)

As previously mentioned, The Night Agent and The Recruit are cut from the same cloth. This explains why both series were so well-received when they first started streaming on Netflix, and why they are both receiving their second seasons at the same time. Their similarities have everything to do with their stories.

Both The Night Agent and The Recruit revolve around young male protagonists who work for the U.S. government and are given tasks that are outside their depth, yet they both rise to the occasion, protecting important young women despite the danger.

However, The Night Agent and The Recruit are different shows. One obvious difference is their lead actors. While Gabriel Basso is returning to the screen after a break in his career, Noah Centineo has remained relevant in recent years due to the To All The Boys I’ve Loved Before series. Furthermore, The Night Agent has a more contained story than The Recruit does. While The Recruit has a complex international angle, The Night Agent remains firmly rooted in Washington. Lastly, The Night Agent is based on a novel, while The Recruit is not, which could affect their storylines.

What To Expect From The Night Agent Season 2 & The Recruit Season 2

The main thing to expect from The Night Agent season 2 and The Recruit season 2 is answers. The Night Agent ended on a positive note, with Peter flying away toward his next mission, but season 2 will have to uncover how this affects his relationship with Rose, and also, whether the villains of last season will make a return after surviving their defeat. On the flip side, The Recruit season 2 has a massive cliffhanger to unravel, as Max’s life hangs in the balance and Owen is on the edge of a major mission in South Korea.

In this way, both The Night Agent and The Recruit have big stories coming their way, and hopefully season 2 will be able to handle all the action and intensity. While anticipation is high for these series, there is no telling how the numbers will fare once they finally release. Although both The Night Agent and The Recruit garnered outstanding numbers during their first seasons, their second seasons still have to prove themselves. If they do, then audiences can prepare themselves for another year of great television.
