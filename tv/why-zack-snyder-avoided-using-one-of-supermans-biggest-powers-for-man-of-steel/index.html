Why Zack Snyder Avoided Using One Of Supermans Biggest Powers For Man of Steel

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Superman’s Super Breath was intentionally left out of Man of Steel due to a conscious decision by Zack Snyder and producer Christopher Nolan. The absence of Super Breath in the film was because it didn’t fit the specific situations and battles depicted in the storyline. Superman eventually uses his Super Breath in Zack Snyder’s Justice League, suggesting that it was included in response to fan demand and to showcase additional powers of the character.

Literature Review

One of Superman’s biggest powers was conspicuously omitted from his debut in Man of Steel, but Zack Snyder explained why he avoided it.

Of Superman’s many powers, one of his biggest did not appear in his DCEU debut, Man of Steel, in a conscious decision by Zack Snyder. With the benefit of hindsight, Man of Steel is much less divisive today than in 2013, with many now lauding the fresh take on the titular hero, and Henry Cavill’s portrayal of Superman in particular. At the time, it drew criticism for being too dark and gritty, with some Superman super-fans lamenting the lack of some of his most iconic features.

Snyder touched on this in an interview with Empire in 2013. In it, he spoke of how the movie’s producer, Christopher Nolan, was the person to curtail the inclusion of some of Superman’s more silly features at this point in the DCEU timeline, despite the comic book “dorks,” Zack Snyder and David S. Goyer, assuming that Man of Steel would feature everything down to the “red underwear.” Not including Super Breath (AKA Frost Breath), however, was Snyder’s decision. Snyder Explained Why He Didn’t Use Super Breath In Man Of Steel

Snyder did not include Superman’s Super Breath because no situation arose that called for its use, but that didn’t mean it wasn’t a part of his repertoire. Snyder revealed that much of the Superman “mythology” was always present. He notes that Man of Steel did not include any kryptonite, but that didn’t mean it didn’t exist. He also noted how Lex Luthor was absent from the movie, even though his buildings populated Metropolis. He also confirms that the Lana who appears briefly early on is, in fact, Lana Lang.

The brawl between Superman and the Kryptonians saw some consequential uses of laser vision and Kal-El and Zod’s godlike, destructive strength. With the Kryptonians being equally matched, it’s not hard to see why Superman’s Super Breath didn’t make the cut in that particular battle. Snyder’s words were then vindicated later as Superman’s Super Breath made an appearance in Zack Snyder’s Justice League.

Why Superman Used Super Breath Later In The DCEU

Superman’s Super Breath was first seen in Zack Snyder’s Justice League after he shrugged off a blow from Steppenwolf’s Electro Axe which he proceeded to freeze and destroy. This, while cool, is a pretty unusual situation to have deployed the ability - particularly when the burning oil rig in Man of Steel would have been a distinctly more applicable use of the power. That’s not to mention the plethora of other situations where breath that can essentially disintegrate something could come in handy.

One reason that Superman may not have deployed his Super Breath until this moment could be that he found himself even more empowered post-resurrection, with his Super Breath being awakened along with him. With the oil rig occurring so early on in Man of Steel, it may also be that he hadn’t gotten to grips with the perfect counter until then. The likely truth, however, is that the Super Breath came after fans began calling for it, and that every other situation preceding this one could have been solved with his other powers.
