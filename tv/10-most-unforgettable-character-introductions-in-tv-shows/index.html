10 Most Unforgettable Character Introductions In TV Shows

Literature Review

A great character introduction can set the tone for an entire TV show or change its course. Here are some of the best examples in television history.

A great character introduction can completely change the course of a TV show. If handled properly, a new TV character can transform the tone, direction, and even the legacy of any series, creating unforgettable moments that live long with the audience. Irrespective of whether a show is already established or just starting, introducing characters is a key tool in getting viewers onboard quickly and adding to the drama. There are many different ways for a TV show to introduce characters. In some cases, this can involve ratcheting up the suspense – keeping the character’s identity secret for as long as possible in order to emphasize their importance or power. Conversely, some shows take a completely different approach, introducing major players seemingly at random with an iconic quip or catchphrase. Many of the best TV show character introductions utilize one or both of these methods to great effect. While there’s no set formula for a great character introduction, getting it right can make or break a series.

10 TV Characters Who Never Achieved Their Full Potential
TV shows with ensemble casts can’t give everyone equal attention, and this often leads to some great characters having their potential wasted.

10 Bender Futurama – Season 1, Episode 1

Futurama
TV-14 Animation Adventure Comedy

Release Date March 28, 1999 Cast Katey Sagal , Billy West , Lauren Tom , Maurice LaMarche , David Herman , Tress MacNeille , John DiMaggio , Phil LaMarr Seasons 11 Showrunner Matt Groening

Futurama’s first episode set the tone for the show’s boundary-pushing, savagely satirical take on the future. The series’ approach was obvious from the get-go, and neatly encapsulated by Fry’s introduction to his future best friend, Bender Bending Rodriguez. The foul-mouthed robot instantly established his iconic status by greeting Fry with his catchphrase, “Bite my shiny metal a**”. However, the scene went up a notch with a blackly funny skit set in a “suicide booth” in which Bender both tries to kill himself and still insists on reusing a quarter. The moment not only captures the essence of Futurama, but expertly establishes one of its most beloved characters.

9 Rachel Friends – Season 1, Episode 1

Friends
Comedy Drama Sitcom

Release Date September 22, 1994 Cast Jennifer Aniston , Courteney Cox , Lisa Kudrow , Matt LeBlanc , Matthew Perry , David Schwimmer Seasons 10 Showrunner Marta Kauffman

Sitcoms can sometimes struggle to create iconic character introduction moments, as it isn’t always obvious which new arrivals will resonate with audiences. With Jennifer Aniston’s first moment on Friends, however, there was no doubt that a star had been born. Walking into Central Perk in a full wedding gown after Ross expresses his desire to “just…be married again”, Rachel became a classic sitcom character from the outset. Even if Friends hadn’t gone from strength to strength, Aniston’s arrival would still remain an ingenious character introduction.

8 President Bartlet The West Wing – Season 1, Episode 1

The West Wing
Drama

In Aaron Sorkin’s classic political procedural, the senior staff of President Josiah Bartlet (Martin Sheen) face the daily challenge of helping to run the country from the West Wing of the White House. The series features an ensemble cast that also includes Rob Lowe, Dulé Hill, Bradley Whitford, Allison Janney, Richard Schiff, and John Spencer. 

Release Date September 22, 1999 Cast Martin Sheen , Bradley Whitford , Allison Janney Seasons 7 Showrunner Aaron Sorkin

While an explosive character introduction can work well, sometimes keeping them in the background can be even more effective. For much of The West Wing episode 1, Martin Sheen’s President Bartlet is a background presence – unseen, and yet controlling the lives and actions of everyone else. It is only during a staff debate about the 10 commandments that he finally speaks, entering the fray with the immortal words “I am the Lord, your god.” In a single line, and having built up the suspense, Bartlet establishes himself as the show’s defining driving force, setting the stage for all the drama to come.

7 Tywin Lannister Game of Thrones – Season 1, Episode 7

Game Of Thrones
Drama Action Adventure

Release Date April 11, 2011 Cast Emilia Clarke , Lena Headey , Richard Madden , Michelle Fairley , Kit Harington , Maisie Williams , Alfie Allen , Peter Dinklage Seasons 8 Showrunner David Benioff , D.B. Weiss

In any adaptation, handling the arrival of an important and powerful book character on screen can be a delicate operation. With Tywin Lannister’s first scene, Game of Thrones not only did the source material proud, but defined the patriarch as a force to be reckoned with. Seen butchering a stag (foreshadowing his role in bringing down the House of Baratheon), he instantly establishes his intelligence, ruthless ambition, and authority over his son Jaime. His challenge to “Say something clever” is one of the first moments where Jaime seems visibly cowed – a testament to Charles Dance’s acting and the success of the scene.

6 Desmond Lost – Season 2, Episode 1

Lost
Adventure Drama Mystery Sci-Fi Supernatural

Release Date September 22, 2004 Cast Evangeline Lilly , Naveen Andrews , Henry Ian Cusick , Daniel Dae Kim , Harold Perrineau , Dominic Monaghan , Emilie de Ravin , Jorge Garcia , Josh Holloway , Michael Emerson , Terry O’Quinn , Matthew Fox , Ken Leung , Elizabeth Mitchell , Yunjin Kim Seasons 6 Showrunner Damon Lindelof , Carlton Cuse

Lost became a smash TV hit because of its uncanny ability to set up intriguing mysteries. In season 1, a key concern for viewers was what exactly was down the ominous hatch that the islanders were desperately trying to open. This was answered from the outset in season 2 in one of the most unexpected and subversive character introductions ever. It is revealed that Desmond – who is not clearly seen throughout the introduction – has established an underground bunker beneath the hatch, complete with impressive modern amenities. It is a genuinely shocking twist that had many viewers second-guessing whether they were even watching the right show.

5 Gus Fring Breaking Bad – Season 2, Episode 11

Breaking Bad
Crime Drama Thriller

Release Date January 20, 2008 Cast Dean Norris , Bob Odenkirk , Aaron Paul , RJ Mitte , Anna Gunn , Giancarlo Esposito , Betsy Brandt , Bryan Cranston , Jonathan Banks Seasons 5 Showrunner Vince Gilligan

Part of what makes Gus Fring’s Breaking Bad introduction so unique is that it’s split into two distinct parts. Initially, he presents as a mild-mannered restaurant manager, keen to assist Walt and Jesse with whatever they require for service. However, when Walt returns to the restaurant having deduced his true identity, he presents as a malevolent, Machiavellian presence, capable of hiding in plain sight. This approach not only highlights how easy it would be to underestimate Fring, but also that Jesse and Walt are about to cross swords with a different class of criminal.

4 Omar Little The Wire – Season 1, Episode 3

The Wire
Drama

Release Date June 2, 2002 Cast Lance Reddick , Michael B. Jordan , Idris Elba , Dominic West , Wood Harris Seasons 5 Showrunner David Simon

The Wire is famous for its litany of powerhouse performances – from Idris Elba to Dominic West – yet none have arguably aged as well or created as iconic a legacy as Michael K. Williams’ portrayal of Omar. Simultaneously terrifying and strangely sympathetic, the character was a complex and compelling blend of contradiction, and his towering screen presence was instantly established by his introduction. With his sinister whistling and nonchalant wielding of a shotgun, terrified residents were sent scattering with cries of “Omar’s coming!”, heralding one of The Wire’s best characters. Although he had been seen previously, this moment represents his true introduction.

3 Oberyn Martell Game of Thrones – Season 4, Episode 1

With such an extensive ensemble cast, it’s inevitable that Game of Thrones would nail a few character introductions. With Oberyn Martell, however, the show pulled off the seemingly impossible by both satisfying the growing TV audience and preexisting book lovers. “The Red Viper” had long been a fan favorite, and anticipation ahead of his arrival was extremely high. In a single three-minute scene, the show showcased his quick wit, carnality, and penchant for violence at a stroke. It’s a testament to the success of this character introduction that audiences still mourn his all-too-soon death.

2 Walter White Breaking Bad – Season 1, Episode 1

Not just a hair-raising character introduction, but one of the most exhilarating opening moments in any TV pilot, Breaking Bad’s Walter White introduction only becomes more impressive in hindsight. Featuring the show’s iconic RV, White is seen bursting out of the vehicle in his underwear as sirens begin to wail in the background. It’s fast, frenetic chaos that not only provides a great contrast with Walter’s character by the series’ end, but also stands up on its own merit.

10 TV Shows That Went Downhill After A Beloved Supporting Character Died
Killing off main characters is always risky, but the death of supporting characters can also play against a TV show if not handled correctly.

1 Tony Soprano The Sopranos – Season 1, Episode 1

The Sopranos
Crime Drama

Release Date January 10, 1999 Cast James Gandolfini , Lorraine Bracco , Edie Falco , Michael Imperioli , Dominic Chianese , Steven Van Zandt , Tony Sirico , Robert Iler , Jamie-Lynn Sigler Seasons 6 Showrunner David Chase

Most great TV character introductions, by necessity, take place within the narrative of the show itself. However, The Sopranos broke the mold by using its opening credits as both a functional way of introducing the personnel involved and as a means of establishing the show’s legendary patriarch. Driving through New Jersey with his signature cigar in hand, Tony Soprano is instantly identified as the lord of all he surveys. As The Lion King’s opening song puts Mufasa center stage, Tony Soprano achieved immediate icon status thanks to the strains of “Woke Up This Morning” before he’d even said a word.
