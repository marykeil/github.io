Gyeongseong Creature Season 2s Story Is Good News For Fans Of Park Seo-joon

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Park Seo-joon will play a new character named Ho-jae in Gyeongseong Creature season 2, although this character could still be Tae-sang. The new season will get to explore a different version of the main couple’s relationship. Gyeongseong Creature season 2 will be a new beginning for the K-drama, now set in the present day.

Literature Review

Park Seo-joon will star in Gyeongseong Creature season 2 after playing Tae-sang in season 1, and his “new” character couldn’t be more exciting.

Gyeongseong Creature season 2’s story means Park Seo-joon will get to showcase his acting chops in what could be one of the best

sequels to a historical K-drama. The Netflix 2024 K-drama slate update included a brand new look at Gyeongseong Creature 2, which will be set in 2024 rather than during World War II. Despite the new setting, the two lead Gyeogseong Creature actors will return in the new season, although it is still difficult to say whether they are both reprising their original roles.

The list of Park Seo-Joon K-dramas and movies includes highly successful projects, which set the bar high for what Gyeongseong Creature was going to deliver. The same can be said about Han So-hee, who plays Chae-ok on the show. Gyeongseong Creature2 is reuniting the two stars, and while this is exciting by itself, the K-drama’s new setting makes their return even more promising. Although Chae-ok’s return can be explained by her transformation into a monster at the end of season 1, how exactly Park’s character will fit into season 2 has yet to be confirmed.

Park Seo-joon Could Be Playing A Brand New Character In Gyeongseong Creature 2 The actor will play “Ho-jae” in Gyeongseong Creature season 2

The Gyeongseong Creature season 2 synopsis reveals that Park Seo-joon is playing “Ho-jae,” a character that looks just like Jang Tae-sang. Ho-jae first appeared in Gyeongseong Creature season 1’s ending credits scene, during which the show jumped 79 years into the future to reveal a 2024 Seoul. Considering the historical K-drama was mostly set in 1945, seeing Park Seo-joon in a scene set in 2024 raised a lot of questions. Although this potentially new character was never referred to as Tae-sang, the scene played out as if it was a major twist that will be built on in season 2.

There are a couple of theories to explain why Gyeongseong Creature season 2 will be set in the present day, as well as why the show’s lead actors are returning even though their characters should be dead by now. Regardless of which theory turns out to be true, the fact is that Park Seo-joon will get to play a very different version of his Gyeongseong Creature character in season 2, which is great news for fans of the actor. For example, if Ho-jae is not Tae-sang after all, Park will be playing an entirely different person.

Even if Ho-jae is Tae-sang under a different name, the actor will still get to portray a significantly different version of the show’s protagonist. Assuming Tae-sang became immortal thanks to the Najin parasite, which he may have come in contact with after drinking the contaminated glass of water from season 1, he will not be the same person he was 80 years ago. Tae-sang’s story was all about surviving and staying alive no matter what, yet not aging a single day for 80 years while everyone around him eventually died must have taken a toll on the character.

Gyeongseong Creature 2 Will Have To Rebuilt Tae-sang & Chae-ok’s Relationship Han So-hee & Park Seo-joon will get to play a new version of the couple

Another promising aspect of Gyeongseong Creature 2 is the fact that Han So-hee and Park Seo-joon will get to play a new iteration of the couple they portrayed in the first season.Gyeongseong Creature season 2’s synopsis indicates that Han So-hee is still playing Chae-ok, who received the Najin parasite at the end of season 1 and is likely immortal now. Assuming the same thing happened to Tae-sang, the couple will have to rebuild things now that almost 80 years have passed since their complicated love story began in 1945.

The first Gyeongseong Creature season 2 clips suggest that Chae-ok and “Ho-jae” do not fully trust each other, which is curious considering Tae-sang and Chae-ok were in love. The question of who is Park Seo-joon playing in Gyeongseong Creature 2 has yet to be answered, but having Chae-ok become romantically involved with a new character that happens to look like Tae-sang would be a disappointing storyline. A more interesting outcome would be to have Tae-sang pretend he is a different person to protect her, or even establish that Tae-sang has lost his memory.

If both Han So-hee and Park Seo-joon are still playing their original characters, Gyeongseong Creature season 2 could be about how their love for each other withstood the passage of time.

Gyeongseong Creature 2’s Story Means It Will Be A New Beginning For The Show Gyeongseong Creature is no longer a period piece

There are quite a few directions in which Gyeongseong Creature season 2 could go, from Tae-sang being a 110-year-old man who looks like he is in his 20s to reincarnation. Regardless of which storyline the Netflix K-drama follows, it is safe to say Gyeongseong Creature will be a very different series once it returns, which is exciting. Turning a period show into a story set in the present day is a bold, risky decision that could lead to great moments if done right. Gyeongseong Creature season 1 had problems, but season 2 can be a new beginning for the series.

If both Han So-hee and Park Seo-joon are still playing their original characters, Gyeongseong Creature season 2 could be about how their love for each other withstood the passage of time and reignited 79 years later. The new season could also be about these two characters falling for each other again, especially if they do not have memories from their 20th-century lives. Lastly, Gyeongseong Creature can create several parallels between the previous season and the new one, allowing both stars to deliver alternate versions of what they did in season 1.
