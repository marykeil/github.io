SUMMARY

Tokyo Vice season 2 features several of its greatest returning characters and introduces exciting new actors and actresses. The highly-anticipated series premiered on February 8, 2024, exclusively on Max, with new episodes releasing on Thursdays. The show is based on the memoir Tokyo Vice: An American Reporter on the Police Beat in Japan and follows Jake Adelstein, an American reporter in Tokyo’s crime beat.

Literature Review

Tokyo Vice keeps most of its outstanding cast intact in its highly-anticipated second season while also adding a handful of exciting newcomers.

The cast of Tokyo Vice season 2 features many returning characters from season 1 and also adds a wide variety of new actors and actresses. The first two episodes of the highly-anticipated series premiered on February 8, 2024, exclusively on Max. Created by J.T. Rogers, Tokyo Vice is based on the 2010 memoir Tokyo Vice: An American Reporter on the Police Beatin Japan written by the real-life Jake Adelstein. New episodes of Tokyo Vice season 2 will be released on upcoming Thursdays exclusively on the Max streaming service.

Tokyo Vice’s 8-part season 1 debuted back in April 2022 to widespread critical acclaim. The modern neo-noir set is set in Tokyo and features extensive use of both Japanese and English languages. Following the cliffhanger ending of Tokyo Vice season 1, Tokyo Vice season 2 is set to pick up right where season 1 left off after several key characters’ fates were called into question. The series follows Jake Adelstein, an American reporter for a premier Tokyo newspaper working on the crime beat, who finds himself in the middle of a budding war between rival gangs.

Ansel Elgort as Jake Adelstein Previous works include: Baby Driver, The Fault in Our Stars, West Side Story

Actor: Ansel Elgort, 29, is a well-known American actor who is one of the main characters in Tokyo Vice. Elgort was born in New York City and started acting professionally in 2013. He rose to prominence from his performance in the young adult coming-of-age movie The Fault in Our Stars. He is also known for his supporting role in the Divergent films and has earned a Golden Globe nomination for his leading role in Edgar Wright’s Baby Driver.

Notable Movies & TV Shows:The Fault in Our Stars (2014), Divergent (2014), Baby Driver (2017), Billionaire Boys Club (2018), West Side Story (2021).

Character: Ansel Elgort plays ambitious news reporter Jake Adelstein in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Ken Watanabe as Hiroto Katagiri Previous works include: Inception, Godzilla, The Last Samurai

Actor: Ken Watanabe, 64, is a captivating Japanese actor who has appeared in many celebrated Hollywood and Japanese films and television series. Watanabe is best known for playing tragic onscreen heroes such as General Tadamichi Kuribayashi in Letters from Iwo Jima and Lord Katsumoto Moritsugu in The Last Samurai. Watanabe earned an Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actor in 2004 for The Last Samurai. He is also highly recognized for his role in Christopher Nolan’s Inception.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: The Last Samurai (2003), Memoirs of a Geisha (2005), Letters from Iwo Jima (2006), Inception (2010), Godzilla (2014), Isle of Dogs (2018).

Character: Ken Watanabe plays lone wolf Tokyo police detective Hiroto Katagiri in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Rachel Keller as Samantha Previous works include: The Society, Fargo, Legion

Actor: Rachel Keller, 31, is an American actress who was born in Los Angeles and grew up in Saint Paul, Minnesota. Keller’s acting career started with supporting roles in acclaimed network series such as The Mentalist and Supernatural. She is most known for her role as Syd in the FX series Legion as well as Cassandra Pressman in the Netflix series The Society. She also appeared in the 2022 film A Man Called Otto alongside Tom Hanks.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Fargo (2015), Legion (2017–2019), The Society (2019), A Man Called Otto (2022).

Character: Rachel Keller plays independent nightclub owner Samantha in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Shô Kasamatsu as Akira Sato Previous works include: Gannibal, Remember to Breathe, Love You as the World Ends

Actor: Shô Kasamatsu, 31, is a Japanese actor born in Aichi, Japan who moved to Tokyo in 2011 to pursue a career in professional acting. Kasamatsu is known for his previous dramatic works such as Demekin (2017), Love and Murder of Sheep and Wolf (2019), and Kamen byôtô (2020). He appears in more than 50 dramas over the course of a 4 year span between 2018 and 2021.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Demekin (2017), Love and Murder of Sheep and Wolf (2019), Kamen byôtô (2020), Gannibal (2022 - ), Tokyo Vice (2022 - ).

Character: Shô Kasamatsu plays a polished Yakuza gangster with a soft side in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Rinko Kikuchi as Eimi Previous works include: Pacific Rim, Westworld, Babel

Actor: Rinko Kikuchi, 43, is a renowned Japanese actress who became the first Japanese woman to be nominated for an Academy Award in 50 years for her performance in Babel (2006). Kikuchi has a celebrated Hollywood career, having appeared in notable works such as the Japanese adaptation of Sideways (2009), Pacific Rim (2013), 47 Ronin (2013), and the acclaimed HBO series Westworld (2018). She portrays Yoko Ono in the 2023 Japanese film Yoko.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Babel (2006), Sideways (2009), Pacific Rim (2013), 47 Ronin (2013), Westworld (2018), Yoko (2023).

Character: Rinko Kikuchi plays a seasoned news editor and Jake’s superior at Meicho in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Shun Sugata as Ishida Previous works include: Kill Bill, Vol. 1, The Last Samurai, Bunraku

Actor: Shun Sugata, 68, is a celebrated Japanese actor with an extensive filmography that spans more than 40 years. He has appeared in some of the best films ever made, such as Quentin Tarantino’s Kill Bill: Volume 1 and Kill Bill: Volume 2. He also was featured in Martin Scorsese’s 2016 contemplative religious epic Silence. He even appeared in one episode of Fuller House in 2022.

Notable Movies & TV Shows:The Last Samurai (2003), Kill Bill: Volume 1 (2003), Kill Bill: Volume 2 (2004), Bunraku (2010), Silence (2016).

Character: Shun Sugaya plays Oyabun Ishida, leader of the Chihara-Kia gang in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Ayumi Tanida as Tozawa Previous works include: Downtown Rocket, Alice in Borderland, Angel Flight

Actor: Aymi Tanida, 48, is a well-known Japanese actor with a major villain role in Tokyo Vice seasons 1 and 2. Tanida was born in Shizuoka, Japan, and has appeared in more than 30 films and television series since making his acting debut in 2011 on the series Princess Go. Outside of Tokyo Vice, Tanida has mostly acted in Japanese films and television shows.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Downtown Rocket (2015 –2018), 24 Japan (2020), Jam: The Drama (2021), Alice in Borderland (2022), Vivant (2023).

​​Character: Ayumi Tanida plays a ruthless, revolutionary leader of the Tozawa clan in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Tokyo Vice Season 2 Supporting Cast & Characters

Miki Maya as Shoko Nagata – Mika Maya, 60, is a new addition to the Tokyo Vice cast, playing Superintendent of the National Police Agency, Shoko Nagata. Maya’s previous credits include Downtown Rocket (2015–2018).

Yôsuke Kubozuka as Noaki Hayama – Yôsuke Kubozuka, 44, is another new cast member in Tokyo Vice season 2, playing a Yakura member. Kubozuka’s previous works include The Kindaichi Case Files (1995).

Ayumi Ito as Misaki – Ayumi Ito, 43, is a Japanese actress from Tokyo. She will reprise her role as Misaki, Tozawa’s love interest in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Kosuke Tanaka as Tin Tin – Kosuke Tanaka, 27, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as Tin Tin, a close friend and colleague of Jake Adelstein at Meicho in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Takaki Uda as Trendy – Takaki Uda, 45, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as Trendy, a close friend and colleague of Jake Adelstein at Meicho in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Hyunri as Erika – Hyunri, 37, is a Japanese actor of Korean descent. She will play a newcomer named Erika in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Takayuki Suzuki as Masa – Takayuki Suzuki, 33, is a Japanese actor who will play a newcomer named Masa in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Nobushige Suematsu as Gen – Nobushige Suematsu, 41, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as a Yakuza named Gen in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Kazuya Tanabe as Yabuki – Kazuya Tanabe, 38, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as a high-ranking associate of Tozawa in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Kôsuke Toyohara as Baku – Kôsuke Toyohara, 58, is a Japanese actor and singer who will reprise his role as Baku, the Editor-In-Chief at Meicho in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Eugene Nomura as Kobayashi – Eugene Nomura, 51, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as Oyabun Ishida’s right-hand Yakuza in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Jessica Hecht as Jake’s Mother – Jessica Hecht, 58, is an American actress who will reprise her role as Jake’s mother in Tokyo Vice season 2. Her previous credits include Breaking Bad, Friends, and The Sinner.

Atom Mizuishi as Katio Sato – Atom Mizuishi, 28, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as Sato’s younger biological brother in Tokyo Vice season 2.

Hajime Inoue as Vice Minister Shigematsu – Hajime Inoue, 62, is a Japanese actor who will reprise his role as Vice Minister Shigematsu in Tokyo Vice season 2.
