10 Deleted Scenes From The Office Superfan Episodes We Can t Believe They Cut

Literature Review

The Office Superfan Episodes offer plenty of new hilarious scenes, including 10 that it’s hard to believe weren’t included in the original cut.

The Office Superfan Episodes are now streaming on Peacock, and the hilarious extended cuts include 10 scenes that it’s hard to believe weren’t included in the original episodes. The Office is one of the most beloved comedy series ever, so it makes sense that Peacock opted to give fans an extended version of the show. Its popularity has also led to early development on a reboot of The Office, proving that the original show’s success is enough to overcome any concerns about the show’s humor not fitting into the modern cultural climate. The Office’s writing room was full of brilliant comedic minds, and the wealth of hilarious scenes that had to be cut proves it. It’s agonizing to consider how difficult it must have been for the writers to make the final decisions on which scenes would be included, considering how many great ones they had. It’s telling that by the end of The Office, there were so many great leftover scenes that Peacock was able to release seven extended seasons of the show, giving fans 10 incredible scenes that arguably should’ve been in the original cuts.

10 Jan Spray Paints The Neighbor’s Dog — “Dinner Party” The Office Superfan Episodes Season 4, episode 13

“Dinner Party” is one of The Office’s most iconic episodes, and the Superfan edition does not disappoint. This particular episode has a multitude of hilarious deleted scenes, but the best one is when Michael reveals to their guests that Jan spray-painted a swear word on their neighbor’s dog. It’s shocking this scene was left out not only because of how funny it is, but also because it shows how unhinged Jan is at this point. To be fair, Jan’s instability is displayed plenty, but it still would’ve been fun to see this scene included in the original cut.

9 Jan Has Both Michael And Pam Log Michael’s Day — “Initiation” The Office Superfan Episodes season 3, episode 5

One of the times audiences rooted for Michael Scott comes in the hilarious season 3 episode, “Initiation”. With pretzel day ongoing and Ryan’s tutelage under Dwight beginning, a lot is going on in this episode, and there are plenty of great deleted scenes in the Superfan edition. One clever and fitting scene that should have been included in the original episode is when Jan asks both Michael and Pam to keep a log of how Michael spends his time. In the original episode, Jan only asks Pam to do it, but showing that she wants Michael to make one as well, so she can compare the two, would have made the scene even funnier. As Pam is writing things like “11 AM, Cosby Impression” on her log, it would have been funny to see how Michael’s version compared. This scene also shows that Jan is clever enough to catch Michael out, and it hints at the insecurity of Michael’s job, which could’ve added extra tension to the episode.

8 Michael Stands Up To Jo For Andy — “Whistleblower” The Office Superfan Episodes season 6, episode 26

There is a great moment in one of the deleted scenes from The Office Superfan Episodes season 6, episode 26, when Michael proves that he is a great boss. During this episode, Sabre is trying to figure out who leaked the news about their faulty printers, and when Jo finds out that it was Andy, Michael stands up to her and fights for Andy’s job, suggesting that she take responsibility for selling cheap printers rather than taking it out on her employees. This deleted scene should have been included originally to show how dedicated Michael is to his staff.

7 Michael Makes His Own Diversity Day Acronym — “Diversity Day” The Office Superfan Episodes season 1, episode 2

One of the earliest episodes of The Office sees Michael make a total mockery of the company’s diversity training while earnestly trying to contribute. Michael’s sincere obliviousness to his offensive jokes and attitude are on full display in the deleted scenes from this episode, as he attempts to make an inclusivity acronym of his own, which ends up spelling “incest”. This scene should’ve been included not only for the slapstick humor, but also because it further demonstrates the genuine good-hearted obliviousness of Michael Scott.

6 Phyllis Claims Bob Vance Thinks She’s 31 — “The Convict” The Office Superfan Episodes season 3, episode 9

One of the more outrageous jokes from The Office Superfan Episodes’ wealth of deleted scenes comes when Phyllis holds a co-worker’s baby and says, “My fiancé, Bob Vance, wants me to have a dozen babies,” and then continues in a hushed tone, “But he thinks I’m 31.” This is one of the funniest lines of the entire episode, which makes it perplexing that it wasn’t included in the original cut. This could have added another hilarious layer to Bob and Phyllis’ relationship, which is already lovably unique and full of surprises.

5 Michael Offers Oscar $4,000 Worth Of Coupons To Fix His Financial Issues — “Money Part 2” The Office Superfan Episodes season 4, episode 8

When Michael’s precarious financial situation comes to the fore in The Office season 4, episode 8, one of the best scenes is cut from the original episode. Desperate to find a quick fix for his growing credit card debt, Michael turns to Oscar, offering the accountant $4,000 worth of coupons to help with his financial issues. Firstly, it’s absurd that Michael had $4,000 worth of coupons, and if he really did have that many, why would he not use them instead of continuing to rack up debt? This hilarious deleted scene shows how desperately inept Michael is at personal finance.

4 Michael Invests In CDs — “Money Part 2” The Office Superfan Episodes season 4, episode 8

Another hilarious deleted scene from “Money Part 2” that cannot be overlooked is when Michael claims he has invested in a sizable portfolio of CDs. However, Oscar quickly realizes that Michael had been unaware of the financial term CD, which means Certificate of Deposit, and instead spent years buying CDs, the compact discs used to listen to music. This hysterical realization shows how completely ignorant of the world Michael is, and it’s one of the funniest jokes in the episode, which makes it strange that it was cut from the original episode.

3 Michael Gets Left With A $400 Bill After The Dundies — “The Dundies” The Office Superfan Episodes season 2, episode 1

“The Dundies” is another classic episode of The Office, so it’s no surprise that it has one of the best deleted scenes from the Superfan Episodes. When Michael neglects to tell his employees that the company won’t be paying for their dinner, several people leave their bills behind, forcing Michael to make up the $400 difference. This scene is fitting considering he told them they wouldn’t have to pay, but the really great part about it is that Michael doesn’t even care. He is so filled with joy from the successful Dundies that the bill doesn’t concern him one bit. Michael is just so happy to have been able to entertain people and bring some laughter into their lives, that money never crosses his mind. With that in mind, this is one of the most endearing Michael Scott moments, showing the selfless side of him that’s often hidden beneath his need for gratification. This could have been a great moment to deepen fans’ connection to the character, but luckily, The Office manages that just fine anyway.

2 Pam Asks Goldenface Why His Face Is Gold — “Threat Level Midnight” The Office Superfan Episodes season 7, episode 16

“Threat Level Midnight”, another classic episode of The Office, also has loads of great deleted scenes in the Superfan edition. One of the best comes when Pam asks Jim’s movie character, Goldenface, why his face is gold. He responds that he worked in a gold factory where the boss never fed them, so they were forced to eat gold, which turned his face gold. This interaction is easily funny enough to have made the original cut, and it shows a moment of Jim and Pam bonding in front of Roy, which could have been a nostalgic addition to the episode.

1 Dwight Tries Even More Tactics To Break Jan’s Stroller — “Baby Shower” The Office Superfan Episodes season 5, episode 4

In The Office season 5, episode 4, “Baby Shower”, Jan brings her newborn daughter into the office to meet Michael and the others at Dunder Mifflin. Upon seeing the expensive stroller Jan bought, Dwight takes it upon himself to rigorously test its durability. The Superfan cut of this episode includes several more ridiculous attempts by Dwight to prove the stroller’s inadequacy, some of which could have been hilarious additions to the original cut. The best of these scenes is when Dwight simply pushes the stroller into the middle of the road, careless of any cars that might pass by.
