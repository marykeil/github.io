10 Supporting Characters Who Made Their TV Shows So Much Better

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Great supporting characters can make a TV show better and more popular than the stars. Secondary characters help contribute to the immersive worlds of their shows. Supporting characters can steal the show and make TV shows their own.

Literature Review

Supporting characters can be just as important to a TV show’s success as the protagonists, and in rare cases they are even more compelling.

Great supporting characters can make a TV show much better, and they can even be more compelling and popular than the stars of the show. While most TV shows primarily focus on just a handful of characters, it’s important for the supporting cast to feel just as real. If only a few characters are worth watching, then an entire show could fall flat. Additionally, interesting secondary characters help contribute to the immersive worlds of their shows. In some extreme cases, supporting characters make TV shows their own. It can be hard to draw focus from the periphery of a show, but great actors tend to find a way to bring something unique to their characters that make them vital to the show’s success. Secondary characters also give writers the chance to experiment a little more. If a new idea doesn’t work out, the character can simply fade into the background, but if it strikes gold, the character helps make the show much better. No matter how much a show focuses on one character, the supporting cast can always steal the show.

The 30 Best TV Characters Of All-Time
Though there have been countless characters who have made their mark in television history, Ranker has highlighted those voted as the all-time best.

10 Victoria Neuman Played by Claudia Doumit in The Boys

Release Date July 26, 2019 Cast Elisabeth Shue , Jensen Ackles , Goran Visnjic , Jessie T. Usher , Chace Crawford , Dominique McElligott , Laz Alonso , Nathan Mitchell , Aya Cash , Colby Minifie , Karl Urban , Erin Moriarty , Karen Fukuhara , Jack Quaid , Antony Starr , claudia doumit , Tomer Capon Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

Since season 1, The Boys has gradually revealed more and more supes created by Vought, not all of whom are presented as shiny superheroes worthy of being in The Seven. As well as plenty of failed experiments, there are a few supes who live incognito, masquerading as regular people. Victoria Neuman is a member of congress who campaigns for more oversight of Vought’s activities, but the true nature of her relationship with the corporation is unclear. Revealing Victoria as the “Head-Popper” sets up a tantalizing season 4, as her political take on a more sinister tone.

9 Richard Splett Played by Sam Richardson in Veep

Release Date April 22, 2012 Cast Anna Chlumsky , Timothy Simons , Julia Louis-Dreyfus , Reid Scott , Tony Hale , Matt Walsh , Kevin Dunn , Sufe Bradshaw Streaming Service(s) Hulu , HBO Max

Selina Meyer’s ruthless hunger for power draws a collection of sycophants and schemers into her orbit, but Richard Splett is one character of unshakable moral fortitude. Richard’s greatest quality is that he is just as charming and sincer whether he’s on camera or behind closed doors. Everyone else tries to either manipulate or ignore Richard, but he ends up outshining all of them on his rise to the top. In this way, Richard is a ray of hope for America’s political future. All of Veep’s best seasons happen after Richard’s introduction.

8 Dasha Played by Harriet Walter in Killing Eve

Release Date April 8, 2018 Cast Adrian Scarborough , Sandra Oh , Nina Sosanya , Edward Bluemel , Kirby Howell-Baptiste , Danny Sapani , Fiona Shaw , Kim Bodnia , Harriet Walter , Steve Pemberton , Sean Delaney , Henry Lloyd-Hughes , David Haig , jodie comer , turlough convery , Raj Bajaj , Gemma Whelan , Camille Cottin , Darren Boyd Owen McDonnell Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Netflix

Dasha represents the life that Villanelle desperately wants to escape.

Villanelle can thank Dasha for shaping her into the world-class assassin she is, but she resents her for her harsh methods. Dasha represents the life that Villanelle desperately wants to escape. She is manipulative and cynical, with a repulsive sense of superiority. Dasha’s actions indirectly bring Villanelle and Eve much closer together. She may be cruel and emotionally detached, but she’s the best visual idea of The Twelve that either Eve or Villanelle can muster. She clings to the idea of the Soviet Union, showing that even she has romantic pipe dreams.

7 NoHo Hank Played by Anthony Carrigan in Barry

Release Date March 25, 2018 Cast Sarah Goldberg , Anthony Carrigan , Henry Winkler , Glenn Fleshler , Stephen Root , Bill Hader Streaming Service(s) Hulu , HBO Max

NoHo Hank is oddly personable and happy-go-lucky for a high-ranking member of the Chechen Mafia. His life of crime doesn’t fit with his outgoing personality, and he often talks about contract killings in a casual and chatty way. His one-sided relationship with Barry is consistently one of the funniest parts of the show. Despite the fact that Barry openly despises him, NoHo Hank believes that they’re just two good buds teasing each other. Hank flips between being a villain and an unusual ally, but Barry’s final season gives him the show’s most emotional send-off.

6 Leon Black Played by JB Smoove in Curb Your Enthusiasm

Release Date October 15, 2000 Cast Larry David , Jeff Garlin , Cheryl Hines , Susie Essman , J.B. Smoove Streaming Service(s) Max

After the Black family are invited to stay with Larry following Hurricane Katrina, Leon worms his way into Larry’s life and stays put. Leon and Larry appear to have nothing in common, coming from extremely different backgrounds, but they both share a brutally honest philosophy, and the belief that the world is against them. Larry’s dynamic with Leon is key to plenty of the show’s best moments, especially when JB Smoove improvises one-liners which force Larry to break character. With Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12 coming out, the duo are closer than ever, although Leon has still never seen Seinfeld.

5 Janet Played by D’Arcy Carden in The Good Place

Release Date September 19, 2016 Cast Kristen Bell , William Jackson Harper , Jameela Jamil , D’Arcy Carden , Manny Jacinto , Ted Danson Streaming Service(s) Hulu

Despite starting the show as a glorified digital assistant, Janet develops into one of the show’s most fascinating characters.

Of all the inventive touches in The Good Place, the character of Janet is the most original. She’s an omniscient, powerful being who exists as a kind of personal assistant in the Good Place. Despite starting the show as a glorified digital assistant, Janet develops into one of the show’s most fascinating characters. Janet is not a human, but she’s uniquely placed to comment on the nature of humanity and its place within the tangled cosmic mess of the universe. She begins to develop more feelings, opinions, and overall humanity as the show progresses.

4 Tom Wambsgans Played by Matthew MacFadyen in Succession

Release Date June 3, 2018 Cast Hiam Abbass , Alan Ruck , Sarah Snook , Kieran Culkin , Natalie Gold , Nicholas Braun , J. Smith-Cameron , Matthew Macfadyen , Peter Friedman , Brian Cox , Arian Moayed , Jeremy Strong , Rob Yang , Dagmara Dominczyk Streaming Service(s) HBO Max

Tom is an outsider looking in at the Roy family, but he’s just as greedy and dysfunctional as any of them. Succession shows Tom and Shiv’s relationship as chaotic and volatile. Although they share feelings for one another, they are more drawn to money and power, and they constantly betray each other. Tom often takes his frustrations out on Greg, the one person he knows he can completely control. He treats him as a protégé, a pet, and a disappointing son, all to soothe his massive ego. Tom helps bring the worst out of everyone, and this makes Succession tick.

3 Sophie Chapman Played by Olivia Colman in Peep Show

Peep Show focuses on the friendship between two diametrically opposed roommates, Mark and Jez. Everyone can see themselves as one or the other. Mark is uptight and controlling, and Jez is irresponsible and frivolous. Sophie shows what can happen when normal, functioning people spend too much time with either one of them. She starts out as a smart and driven loan manager working alongside Mark, but she is gradually worn down and broken emotionally, sliding into depression and addiction. Mark idealizes Sophie, but he doesn’t care for the reality when he actually gets what he wants.

2 Mr. Burns Voiced by Harry Shearer in The Simpsons

Release Date December 17, 1989 Cast Tress MacNeille , Julie Kavner , Harry Shearer , Pamela Hayden , Nancy Cartwright , Hank Azaria , Dan Castellaneta , Yeardley Smith Network FOX

Excluding the Simpson family, Mr. Burns gets more focus than anyone else in Springfield. The owner of Springfield’s nuclear power plant is the embodiment of corporate greed at any cost, and he revels in being the villain. With his toadying sidekick Smithers by his side, Mr. Burns is responsible for plenty of evil schemes, even though he doesn’t need any more money. Mr. Burns tries to murder Bart in his pursuit of the Hellfish treasure, he plans to skin the Simpson family’s greyhounds to make a coat, and he even blocks the sun from shining on the town.

1 Peggy Olson Played by Elisabeth Moss in Mad Men

Release Date July 19, 2007 Cast Jon Hamm , Elisabeth Moss , Vincent Kartheiser , January Jones , Christina Hendricks , Bryan Batt , Aaron Staton , Rich Sommer Streaming Service(s) AMC Plus

Peggy is always forced to consider how others perceive her. She needs Don’s approval, but she has to balance this with the notion that Joan and some of the other women in the office may look down upon her career ambitions. Ultimately, Peggy is headstrong enough not to conform to other people’s expectations of her, including her mother. Peggy’s journey is arguably more interesting than Don’s, as she has to fight relentlessly to succeed in the male-centric world of advertising. She’s one of Mad Men’s most compelling characters, and a rebuttal against the phony promise of the American dream.
