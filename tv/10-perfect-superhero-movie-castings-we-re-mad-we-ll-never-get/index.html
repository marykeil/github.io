10 Perfect Superhero Movie Castings We re Mad We ll Never Get

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Fan artists envision Keanu Reeves as a gritty Wolverine, but at 59 years old, he may be too old to commit to the role. Jensen Ackles’ perfect look and experience voicing Batman make him an ideal choice, but his age may conflict with the desired younger iteration of the character. Daniel Radcliffe’s violent roles and stature make him an interesting option for Wolverine, but Hugh Jackman’s potential return makes it unlikely for anyone new to take on the role.

Literature Review

Despite being perfect fits for certain superhero roles, some actors never got their chance to make the most popular fan casts a reality.

Comic book franchises like the Marvel Cinematic Universe, Fox’s X-Men, and the DCEU are rife with missed opportunities for casting that fan artists have had a hard time letting go of. Sometimes, it seems as though certain comic book roles are made for a given actor, and artist renderings of what they might look like in costume only make it harder to let go of fan castings that would’ve been better than what panned out. To this day, it’s hard not to look back on the roles that never panned out without wondering what could have been. There are multiple factors at play that can explain why some of these no-brainer castings fell through, and why they’ll likely never come to fruition. Scheduling conflicts, lack of interest, or the cruel passage of time can rule out an actor, especially for the busy production of a superhero film, time and time again. Whether the window of opportunity has simply passed or the existing casting for the same character is too iconic to be replaced, it’s a shame these sought-after fan castings will likely never manifest on the big screen.

10 Keanu Reeves as Wolverine

Between The Matrix series and the John Wick franchise, Keanu Reeves has had plenty of experience tearing his way through groups of bad guys that would translate excellently to a gritty re-imagining of Wolverine. Fanart depicting the Canadian actor as his home country’s most famous mutant hero made the prospect all the more exciting, and Reeves himself expressed interest in Wolverine, adding further fuel to the fire. Sadly, at 59 years old, Reeves may simply be too long in the tooth to realistically commit to a new X-Men movie series.

9 Jensen Ackles as Batman

Voicing the Caped Crusader in DC’s Tomorrowverse animated continuity, Jensen Ackles has already honed his Batman performance to at least some degree. Casting the Supernatural veteran as another famous detective seems like an obvious good idea, considering Ackles’ perfect look and clear enthusiasm. The only pain point to the concept of Ackles as a live-action Batman is his age. At 45 years old, it’s unclear whether the new DCU would be interested in re-treading Ben Affleck’s ground as an older, wearier Dark Knight.

8 Daniel Radcliffe as Young Wolverine

Having already captained the wildly popular Harry Potter franchise from a young age, Daniel Radcliffe is no stranger to heading a popular franchise. Daniel Radcliffe as Wolverine may seem like a strange choice, but the actor proved he can handle violent roles in 2019’s Guns Akimbo, and at 5'5" tall, he could be the first live-action Wolverine to accurately portray the character’s short stature from the comics. Unfortunately, the return of Hugh Jackman’s Wolverine in Deadpool 3, and possibly beyond, makes it unlikely that anyone new will be taking up the claws and cowl anytime soon.

7 Alan Ritchson as Batman

Another famous TV gumshoe interested in translating his sleuthing skills to the World’s Greatest Detective, Reacher’s Alan Ritchson has expressed interest in Batman. The musclebound actor certainly has the look of a superhero, and has dipped his toes into live-action DC roles before as Hawk in Titans and, less recently, Aquaman in Smallville. However, Reacher’s popularity may present a significant hurdle for Ritchson’s chances, the Amazon series sure to present conflicts with a nascent superhero franchise likely to have a busy shooting schedule.

6 Winona Ryder as Mystique

Winona Ryder has an incredible range as an actress, making her a perfect candidate for the supervillainess requiring the most flexibility of them all, the shape-shifter Mystique. Fan art postulating on what Ryder would look like in the character’s trademark blue skin presents a surprisingly compelling image, even if the window for Ryder playing a younger version of the character, a la Jennifer Lawrence, may have passed. However, even if the MCU does opt for a more mature Mystique, lingering interest from Rebecca Romajin, the first actress to portray the mutant, may win out over a new casting.

5 John Boyega as Blade

In the Star Wars sequels, John Boyega’s infectious energy and clear enthusiasm shone through as a leading character in a reboot of a beloved franchise. Compared to Wesley Snipes’ classic, if decidedly low-energy performance, Boyega could bring the vampire hunter a much-needed infusion of eager acting ability. Unfortunately, with Mahershala Ali slated to star as Blade in his eponymous MCU production, a re-cast at this stage in the game is incredibly unlikely.

4 Emily Blunt as The Invisible Woman

After being one of the most popular fan-castings for years, John Krasinski’s Mr. Fantastic finally got to see the light of day in Doctor Strange and the Multiverse of Madness. Krasinski’s real-life spouse, Emily Blunt, has acted alongside him before as husband and wife in A Quiet Place, and would’ve been perfect to do the same as The Invisible Woman. Despite having a perfect look for Sue Storm, Krasinski’s lack of involvement in the MCU’s upcoming Fantastic Four would make her appearance now less likely.

3 Nikolaj Coster-Waldau as Constantine

Best known for playing a different wise-cracking jerk with a British accent as Jamie Lannister in Game of Thrones, Nikolaj Coster-Waldau would lend himself excellently to the role of John Constantine. Coster-Waldau’s dashing good looks, dirty blonde hair, and utter expertise in portraying Jamie Lannister’s tired incredulity in the later seasons of Game of Thrones would make him no less than perfect for the chain-smoking magician. Tragically, the abysmal reputation of the 2005 Constantine movie with Keanu Reeves makes the character unlikely to see the light of day in a new DC film any time soon.

2 Donald Glover as Spider-Man

One of the most famously well-received fan casting campaigns ever, the demands for Donald Glover to play a live-action Miles Morales Spider-Man were once deafening. The comedian and rapper expressed clear enthusiasm as well, even incorporating the idea into one of his stand-up comedy specials. While Glover got to play Miles’ uncle, Aaron Davis, in Spider-Man: Homecoming, and even got to wear The Prowler’s cloak in Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse, his window for playing Miles Morales has likely closed, as any live-action version of the character will likely want to go with the teenage hero audiences are more familiar with.

1 Bryan Cranston as Lex Luthor

Despite his humble beginnings as a bumbling comedic dad in Malcolm in the Middle, Bryan Cranston went on to represent the face of TV villainy as the despicable Walter White in Breaking Bad. It was inevitable for a comparison to be drawn between his Walter White performance and DC’s most famous bald mastermind, Lex Luthor. Passed up in favor of Jesse Eisenberg’s neurotic Lex in the DCEU, it’s a crying shame that Cranston has likely since aged out of realistically playing Lex Luthor, despite his proven success as a despicable manipulator audiences can’t get enough of.
