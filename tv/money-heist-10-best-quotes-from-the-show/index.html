Money Heist 10 Best Quotes From The Show

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

“Death Can Be The Greatest Opportunity Of Your Life.” - Berlin “To Love, You Need Courage.” - Nairobi “There Are No Right Sides In Hell, Major.” - Palermo

Literature Review

From Berlin’s bold promises to Tokyo’s last words, there’s no shortage of unforgettable quotes on Netflix’s Money Heist, but which are the best ones?

Money Heist quotes match up well with other Netflix shows as the series thrust fans into a thrilling world of unbelievable robberies, all boldly pulled off by a crew of lovable thieves. The show starts with a criminal mastermind called The Professor who puts together a team that plans to rob the Royal Mint of Spain. Over the next few seasons, the crew changes with some new members and attempts to pull off more heists in brilliant ways. However, while part of the fun is watching the heists, the best part of the show is the relationship between the thieves.

The best Money Heist quotes allow them to show their personality as they attempt to achieve the Professor’s dreams. From the Royal Mint to the Bank of Spain, the members of the gang have done and said some unforgettable things along the way. From Nairobi’s brave declarations to Tokyo’s last words, there’s no shortage of memorable quotes from the massively popular series. With the conclusion of Money Heist Part 5, there are great quotes from all the main characters that remain quotable to this day.

              Money Heist Character Guide: Every Robber's Real Name & Backstory   Netflix crime series Money Heist follows a group of robbers who pull off daring heists. Here are the real names of the gang, and their backstories.

“Death Can Be The Greatest Opportunity Of Your Life.” Berlin

Berlin’s story arc is one of the more fascinating ones in Money Heist. It’s so compelling that he appears several times long after his demise in season 2. Fans learn more about Berlin’s seemingly nonchalant attitude towards his terminal illness throughout the show. He never feels sorry for himself and is unapologetic about his grand goals for the last few months of his life. His hedonistic personality ensures that he always strives to get what he wants, even if it is a dignified death for the sake of his crew’s lives.

“To Love, You Need Courage.” Nairobi

Among the Money Heist quotes that perfectly capture Nairobi’s personality, her line about love and courage that comes after an impassioned monologue is at the top. Her response to Palermo’s needless taunting puts him in his place. She unashamedly tells him she is bold enough to tell Helsinki that she loves him. Although the unrequited feelings weigh on her, she prefers having them out in the open instead of being left unsaid. This is the right decision, as her tragic fate not long after that same speech shows.

“There Are No Right Sides In Hell, Major.” Palermo

Palermo faces off against Sagasta in a tense scene in the final season. Both sides were hurting as Sagasta lost the majority of his men while Palermo and the crew suffered the loss of Tokyo. As they sit across each other while the rain dramatically pours from the broken roof of the Bank of Spain, Palermo points out they’re not so different. Sagasta corrects him, telling him he’s fighting for the right side. Palermo’s response is a smart comment not just about their situation in the bank, but about the message of the show as a whole.

              Money Heist: One Quote From Each Main Character That Goes Against Their Personality   From's Tokyo's optimism to Helsinki's outburst, these are the best quotes from Money Heist that were surprisingly out of character for each robber.

“In The End, Love Is A Good Reason For Everything To Fall Apart.” Tokyo

Tokyo is the most polarizing character on the show and one of Tokyo’s best quotes in Money Heist highlights exactly why. Her chaotic relationship with Rio put the rest of the gang in trouble more than once. An argument between them in the first season leads to the identification of the robbers thanks to a hacked phone. Tokyo’s impulsivity and need to party also indirectly push the group to pursue another heist. It’s all worth it for her, though, as nothing matters more than her love for Rio and these Money Heist quotes prove that.

“Every Day, I’d Wake Up Wondering If It Was My Last.” Rio

Rio’s actions created more frustration than sympathy after his unexpected run-in with the CNI. He did give up paradise for a poorly timed phone call, after all. That sentiment changed once it was revealed what he had to go through in Alicia’s hands, though – every waking moment was hell for him. His video admission of how they tortured him and locked him in a tiny box is painful. It puts things into perspective for him and gives him the courage and maturity to call out Tokyo’s toxic behavior.

“Either We Both Win Or We Both Lose. Colonel" The Professor

“It’s Time For You To Face It, This Is Checkmate.”

The way The Professor corners Tamayo in the finale is his most brilliant “checkmate” yet. Even Lisbon is shocked to hear about the secret plan the Professor had been brewing long before the heist began. Tamayo had two choices: he could refuse the gold-covered brass and kill the gang, then watch the nation fall into bankruptcy, or accept The Professor’s demands and fake gold, which turns him into a hero in the country’s eyes. It’s a risky but ingenious solution no one saw coming and caps off the exhilarating final heist.

“A Lot Of People Believe We Only Find One True Love In Our Lives. But What They Don’t Realize Is That We Can Have Several Lives.” Tokyo

Although Tokyo has transformed over the Money Heist’s seasons, one thing that never changes is that she’s a romantic. She lost her first and true love in a tragic way, which helps her understand there can be more than one love and one life within a lifetime. She tries to help Rio grasp this concept during their last moments together, tearfully telling him not to be sad, as it’s the “first day of” his “next life.” It’s a tear-jerking concept perfectly portrayed in the heartbreaking scene, which redeems her character.

“I’m A Thief. Son Of A Thief. Brother Of A Thief. I Hope Someday To Be The Father Of A Thief.” The Professor

There’s an uncharacteristically tense moment between Lisbon and The Professor when they find themselves up against the wall in the Bank of Spain. Lisbon confronts him about how he keeps saying heists are his way of talking to his deceased father and brother. She says he does it for himself, not for anyone else. The Professor has his biggest transformation on Money Heist when he echoes this conversation in his standoff with Tamayo a few minutes later. He looks back towards Lisbon for a moment before saying this quote.

“I Thought If I Ran Fast Enough, The Sadness Wouldn’t Catch Up With Me.” Alicia Sierra

Alicia’s role in the final season was a mystery. She tortures Rio and holds The Professor hostage and any fear about her loyalty is removed after a moving scene between her and The Professor. As they hide in an apartment, she asks him to pretend to be her friend, just visiting the baby. She starts to cry when she recounts her husband’s devastating death, admitting her involvement in the heists is an attempt at avoiding her feelings. It’s at that moment the Professor decides he’ll never let her down, as she’s officially part of the gang.

“It Didn’t All Work Out The Way We Wanted. We’re Not All Here, But We Did Achieve Something.” The Professor

During the gang’s tearful final reunion after the tough heist, they gather in the hangar, hugging each other and crying. They know that they have all gone through something extraordinary. The Professor gives a heartfelt final speech and as the camera pans, fans notice missing faces in the crowd. As Rio, Monica, Stockholm, Denver, and the rest of the group share smiles, as they have achieved something throughout the series. This was a perfect final Money Heist quote that wrapped up the Netflix series.
