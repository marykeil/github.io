Literature Review

Homicide: Life on the Street was a procedural crime drama that starred Andre Braugher. It’s missing from streaming services, but NBC is working on it.

Homicide: Life on the Street has been missing on streaming services, but according to David Simon, NBC is working on it. The trailblazing Homicide rivaled The Wire, capturing the essence of Baltimore’s homicide unit with gritty realism and complex character studies. Airing from 1993 to 1999, the critically acclaimed series set new standards in the genre, and was renowned for its raw, unfiltered depiction of city life and the moral ambiguities faced by law enforcement. Its unique cinematography, compelling storylines, and strong performances, particularly by Andre Braugher as Detective Frank Pembleton, solidified its place in television history.

The show’s significance has been reinvigorated following the sad passing of Andre Braugher on December 11, 2023, from lung cancer. Diagnosed just a few months prior at age 61, Braugher had been a smoker but had quit in 2010. His death has evoked a wave of nostalgia and renewed interest in Homicide: Life on the Street. Co-stars and fans alike have paid homage to Braugher, fondly remembering him for the indelible mark he left on the show and its audience. Now, following the demand for Homicide: Life on the Street to hit streaming, there has been some major headway.

NBC/Universal Is Trying To “Clear Music Rights” For Homicide: Life On The Street, According To David Simon

In a recent development, David Simon, the writer and producer of Homicide: Life on the Street and the author of Homicide: A Year on the Killing Streets, the book on which the show is based, shared exciting news for fans on Twitter. He revealed that NBC/Universal, in collaboration with Fremantle for overseas rights, is diligently working to clear music rights for Homicide, aiming to bring it to streaming platforms.

The producer noted that the efforts to revive the series on streaming services were partly inspired by Andre Braugher’s legacy, hinting at the depth of Braugher’s impact on the show. “Andre alone ought to rate such,” Simon poignantly added, recognizing the late actor’s significant contribution. Given that the serious was produced by NBC, it’s more than likely that when the show does become available on streaming, it’ll be on Peacock.

Simon Shares Appreciation For Homicide: Life On The Streets’ Andre Braugher After His Passing

Reflecting on Braugher’s passing, Simon shared his thoughts, emphasizing the privilege he felt in writing for such a “magnificent actor.” The writer tweeted:

“I’ve had some days to reflect on Andre Braugher and his passing, and with time and a bit more effort, I’ve been able to summon a variety of recollections and some real pride at for a time having the chance to write lines — some of them good — for a magnificent actor…”

Simon narrated an instance where Braugher questioned a line Simon wrote for Detective Frank Pembleton, in which Pembleton refers to a female character as “mama,” showcasing Braugher’s commitment to his character’s authenticity. In a long chain of tweets, Simon revealed how embarrassed he was at the time.

However, despite the initial hesitation, Braugher delivered the line with a unique interpretation, staying true to Pembleton’s character in Homicide: Life on the Street, much to Simon’s admiration. “At least, when the shot concluded, he was charitable enough to look over to the monitors and give me a tight, quick smile,” Simon reminisced, reflecting the respect and mutual appreciation that existed between the actor and the writer. These insights not only highlight Braugher’s exceptional talent but also underscore the profound impact he had on the series and those who worked with him.
