Rick & Morty Season 8 Has Already Teased Undoing One Of Season 7 s Best Stories

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Morty’s recent character development in season 7 may be undone in season 8, as hinted by Mr. Poopybutthole’s actions in the stinger. The show’s formula has changed in season 7, with less reliance on Rick’s amoral antics and Morty’s naivety, but season 8 could potentially go back to the old ways. Season 8 should continue with Rick and Morty’s character growth for more compelling and unpredictable storytelling, especially considering the new lead character introduced in season 7.

Literature Review

Rick and Morty season 8 seems set to give the heroes more independence, but the season 7 finale’s stinger secretly hinted that this might not happen.

While Rick and Morty season 8 may finally see its heroes hold onto their character growth, one supporting star’s role in the season 7 stinger doesn’t bode well. Rick and Morty can be somewhat frustrating when it comes to character growth. When the Adult Swim hit began airing back in 2013, it was a silly, anarchic sci-fi comedy where little mattered, and the status quo was usually reinforced by the end of each episode. However, as Rick and Morty progressed, the show began to take its main characters and their internal lives more seriously. The following seasons yielded mixed results.

In seasons 6 and 7, Rick and Morty both tried to learn from their mistakes and adapt their behavior. However, many episodes of the series trapped them in the same sort of storylines they experienced years earlier. Rick and Morty’s season 7 finale seemingly promised to change this as Morty went through a nightmarish hallucinatory trip that made him realize just how much he relied on Rick’s approval. Although the episode itself made it look like Morty would grow more self-sufficient in season 8, the show’s stinger hinted that all of Morty’s recent character development could soon be undone.

Mr. Poopybutthole’s Fate Usually Reflects Rick & Morty’s Own Stories The recurring character’s season 7 stinger doesn’t bode well for Rick and Morty

Broadly speaking, Mr. Poopybutthole’s role in the season finale stingers reflects Rick and Morty’s current roles and where they will go in the following season. Season 5 ended with the duo in dire straits, and then cut straight to a stinger that featured Mr. Poopybutthole at his lowest point. Similarly, season 6 ended with Poopybutthole deluding himself into thinking he had moved on just after viewers saw Rick rabidly swear to Morty that his Rick Prime revenge quest wasn’t driving him to madness. In both cases, Mr. Poopybutthole’s bleak circumstances were mirrored by Rick and Morty’s own dispiriting situations.

As such, it was somewhat surprising when Rick and Morty set up season 8 with a stinger wherein Mr. Poopybutthole used Rick’s portal gun to enter another dimension, knock out that dimension’s version of himself, and take his place. This reunited Poopybutthole with his love interest, but her suspicious glance made it clear that she didn’t trust him and knew something was wrong. Moreover, the fact that Poopybutthole attempted this scheme at all proves just how much he betrayed his apparent character growth from the season premiere. In that outing, Poopybutthole promised his friends he would try to move on.

Rick & Morty Hinted That Season 8 Will Undo The Main Duo’s Development Rick and Morty’s character growth could soon come undone

Mr. Poopybutthole replacing himself in an alternate dimension proved that Rick and Morty’s characters could return to their status quo yet again in season 8. Season 7 mostly avoided Rick and Morty’s tired formula, with fewer episodes revolving around Rick’s amoral antics and Morty’s well-meaning naivety. Rick realized that killing Rick Prime wouldn’t bring him happiness and Morty learned that relying on Rick would always limit his potential, two big developments that implied the pair might be less codependent and more self-assured in season 8. However, Poopybutthole’s tendency to echo their circumstances makes this season 8 change less likely.

If Mr. Poopybutthole ignored his epiphany from the season 7 premiere, then there is no reason the heroes wouldn’t do the same. Rick and Morty could go back to their old ways in season 8, just like Poopybutthole ignored his character development in the season 7 finale. This could prove a disappointment, as it would make more solo episodes featuring just Rick or just Morty less likely and result in more outings that followed the show’s most well-worn story formula. This would be particularly unfortunate given one of Rick and Morty season 7’s most welcome surprises.

Why Season 8 Shouldn’t Undo Rick & Morty’s Growth Rick and Morty season 8 needs to give the duo more independence

Since Rick and Morty set up a new lead character in season 7 by highlighting Summer’s role in the show, it would be doubly disappointing if season 8 saw the show take a step back. Both Rick and Morty had interesting, original solo adventures in season 7, and they would lose the freedom to star in these episodes if they fell back into their old routine in season 8. While Poopybutthole’s desperate attempts to regain his old life were blackly comedic, this was only because the show rarely focuses on his antics, and he is a minor character.

In contrast, every episode relies on Rick and Morty, and their stories could easily become predictable in season 8 if their season 7 character development is undone. Rick and Morty’s many movie parodies may provide a lot of the show’s laughs, but it is the sheer invention and subversion of the series that keeps viewers coming back. The longer Rick and Morty maintains the same dynamic without any change or growth, the less compelling the show will feel. As such, Rick and Morty season 8 can’t undo the pair’s character development, even if Poopybutthole seemingly predicted this.
