Star Wars Dark Side TV Show Risks Making A Major Sith Mistake

2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments

SUMMARY

The new Star Wars show risks stripping away the mystery surrounding the Sith and the character of Palpatine by revealing too much about their backstory. Literature Review

One upcoming Star Wars TV show, The Acolyte, will finally reveal the Sith perspective. But as exciting as that may be, it risks a major mistake…

Star Wars’ new TV show focusing on the dark side of the Force might make a big Sith mistake. Set during the High Republic Era, Star Wars: The Acolyte will be the first Star Wars TV show to really explore the perspective of the Sith. The upcoming Star Wars show will reveal how the Sith infiltrated the Jedi and the Republic, setting the stage for Palpatine’s rise to power.

Little is known about The Acolyte’s story, beyond the fact a group of Jedi - including Amandla Stenberg’s Padawan as the main character - will deal with the encroaching darkness of the Sith. The Acolyte is bound to reveal more about the Sith Order, given it will partly be told from their perspective, and will partly show Jedi actually investigating the possibility of the Sith’s return. And yet, as exciting as this may be, Star Wars risks making a big mistake.

Star Wars Canon Has Restored A Sense Of Mystery To The Sith

In 2014, Disney chose to conduct the largest canon change in Star Wars history. They wiped the old Star Wars Expanded Universe from canon, and in doing so they also erased the history of the Sith. The old EU - now known as “Legends” - had explored the Sith in countless stories set during the Old Republic Era, with the tale of Darth Bane and the Rule of Two coming under particular scrutiny.

It’s striking to note that the Disney era has been careful to avoid telling any Sith origin stories. What little has been confirmed about the history of the Sith comes from throwaway, often contradictory references. Since the new canon has been around for a decade, this can only be deliberate - at least until now.

Star Wars Canon Has Restored Mystery To Palpatine & The Sith

Star Wars canon has avoided answering questions about the Sith for a decade now. Given the sheer amount of Star Wars content that has been released - ranging from TV shows to comics, from novels to games - that can only be deliberate. It suggests Lucasfilm has been quietly working to restore a sense of mystery and intrigue to the Sith.

Canon has even avoided giving Star Wars’ most powerful and prominent villain, Sheev Palpatine, a proper backstory. Not much about his history in canon has been revealed beyond that he killed his master, Darth Plagueis. This withholding of information has been used as a powerful narrative device. It has kept an air of mystery around Palpatine and his motivations. It has made him even more interesting. Audiences can’t help but wonder what made him the person he is, and how he came to be a manipulative genius. This refusal to explore his backstory makes Palpatine feel like the ultimate Bogeyman.

Star Wars: The Acolyte Risks Stripping Away The Mystery

The Acolyte will finally dive into the history of the Sith, revealing something about their past. It may also potentially shine a light on where Palpatine, the arch-manipulator, fits into their story. It is unlikely that Palpatine himself will appear in the show since it’s set roughly 100 years before the events of Star Wars: Episode I - The Phantom Menace. However, it’s possible that his master, Darth Plagueis could appear. Given that Muuns live for over 100 years and Plagueis could manipulate Midi-chlorians in canon to create, destroy, and extend life, it’s possible that he was alive during The Acolyte.

As exciting as this is, it risks removing some of the mystery. What makes the Sith so terrifying is that little is known about them. They are a malevolent force that lurks in the shadows - a cryptic occult order that guards its secrets jealously. Giving them a detailed backstory and explaining how the dark side of the Force works could take away from that.

Further, this mysteriousness suits the nature of the Sith - especially the Sith of the High Republic Era. There is a reason they were believed to be extinct for centuries despite influencing governments. Their ability to operate in secret and manipulate events using insidious means is an inherent part of their collective character. Diving too deep into the mechanisms of the dark side and the origins of the Sith risks getting away from this character.

There’s A Crucial Reason The Sith Are Better As A Mystery

The audience’s lack of knowledge of the Sith reflects the Jedi’s lack of knowledge. One of the biggest plot points throughout Star Wars is the elusiveness of the Sith. The Jedi are often unable to sense them. Just as they are able to hide themselves from the Jedi - even when in the main Jedi Temple - they are able to hide themselves from the viewers. This puts the audience in the Jedi’s shoes and makes the Sith threat feel real. Depending on how much The Acolyte reveals about the Sith and how it does so, it could remove that parallel. Star Wars will need to tread with great care on this point - and it will be very easy to stumble.

The Acolyte will be released on Disney+ sometime in 2024.
