15 Funniest Scenes From The Bear, Ranked

SUMMARY

The humor in the show “The Bear” is more like real-world conversations between friends, where contrasting personalities make anything hilarious. The fantastic ensemble cast of “The Bear” is crucial to its comedic success, with each character bringing their own distinct sense of humor. The show excels at both witty dialogue and physical comedy, creating humorous moments that range from subtle exchanges to chaotic and ridiculous situations.

Literature Review

The Bear recently won the Golden Globe for Best Comedy TV Series. While it’s undoubtedly a dramedy, these moments prove its comedic merits.

FX’s The Bear isn’t purely a comedy show, but moments of tension and drama are complemented by levity and well-timed humor. For season 2, The Bear won the Golden Globe for Musical or Comedy TV Series, and it’s fair to say it fits neatly between the comedy and drama genres, both of which are relatively loose parameters. While traditional sitcoms like Seinfeld construct their episodes around jokes, the humor of The Bear is more akin to real-world conversations between friends, where a blend of contrasting personalities can make anything hilarious. The Bear is exceptional at creating distinct characters and, thus, different senses of humor. Carmy isn’t a notably funny character, but his dry, deadpan expressions and quick wit make for hilarious moments. Richie’s loudmouth antics clash with Sydney’s intelligence and wit, making for great contrasting banter. Fak is primarily a comedic relief character whose wholesome energy provides consistent and joyous humor. Like the dramatic success of The Bear, the show’s comedy relies on its fantastic ensemble.

15 Fak Brothers Baseball Card Investment Season 2, Episode 6

“Fishes” is the best episode of The Bear so far, mainly due to its intensity. The episode is filled with cameos, including actors Jamie Lee Curtis, Bob Odenkirk, and comedian John Mulaney. In the episode, the Fak brothers make a sales pitch to Mulaney’s Stevie, trying to get him to invest $500 in their plan to flip the value of baseball cards. With perfect delivery, Mulaney states, “Do I have access to 500 bucks? I absolutely do. I’m a 43-year-old man,” playing the straight man to the goofy brothers.

14 “Snyder Cut Motherf***ers” Season 1, Episode 1

The Bear pulls no punches in the show’s premiere episode, “The System.” At the end of the episode, Richie calms a crowd by firing a gun into the air and then talking into a loudspeaker. He delivers a hilarious line, calling the mob of people “Incel, QAnon, 4chan, Snyder Cut motherf***ers,” embarrassing the pack dressed in ridiculous costumes. That specific, witty dialogue is what the series excels at.

13 Carmy Gets A Call From The Fridge Guy Season 2, Episode 2

An underlining plot in The Bear season 2 is Carmy’s relationship with Claire and the possibility of him finding happiness while opening a stellar restaurant. Throughout the season, he’s meant to call the fridge guy and get the door handle fixed, ultimately backfiring in The Bear’s season 2 ending. One of the first times it’s mentioned, Marcus enters the room and tosses the phone to Carmy, who, not expecting it, ducks and watches as the phone shatters behind them. Carmy dryly replies, “I’ll call him back,” showing how humor interweaves with fast-paced dialogue.

12 The Fak Brothers Dance On Christmas Season 2, Episode 6

Another great moment from “Fishes” is when Richie films the Fak brothers dancing to music. It’s simple physical comedy that works so effectively amidst the chaos of the episode. Richie constantly has excellent chemistry with Fak, and this subtle moment shows that it’s not only with the one brother. The pair of brothers have some magnificent moves between them.

11 Fak Threatens To Call Mom On Richie Season 2, Episode 2

In another comedic restaurant moment from “Pasta,” Richie and Fak get into one of their juvenile arguments over mold in the ceiling. Fak discovers the mold, and Richie insists that Fak not freak out and “call Mom,” referring to Sugar. Marcus hilariously watches from the sideline as the two bicker like children, showing the wholesome familial side of the restaurant.

10 The Ceiling Falls On Richie Season 2, Episode 2

Shortly after Richie downplays the mold problem, he insists again that the situation is under control. In a perfect moment of karmic justice, Richie gives a speech about the status of the ceilings, saying, “Were we to have mold, it would collapse when I go like this,” before tapping the ceiling, which comes crashing down on him. Richie is immediately covered in mold and dust. If that wasn’t enough, a ball shortly follows, bouncing off Richie’s head.

9 “No. Sir, That Was My Brother.” Season 1, Episode 4

The season 1 episode “Dogs” is filled with funny moments and one of the series’ darker, more deadpan comedic exchanges. Carmy and Richie are catering for a child’s birthday party, and Carmy is slicing oranges at their table when an old man comes over and says, “Carmen, is that you? I thought you killed yourself,” referring to Michael. Carmy dryly replies, “No. Sir, that was my brother,” and the man walks away, ending the exchange. It’s dark humor, which is often what The Bear exceeds at.

8 The Caulk Scene Season 1, Episode 2

In a scene filled with innuendo, Carmy and Richie argue over an issue at the restaurant that leads to a poor health inspection. Carmy insists that Richie goes to the hardware store to purchase some caulk, a material used to seal cracks in walls. Carmy says, “You’re gonna get some caulk, and you’re gonna caulk that sh**,” to which Richie replies, “FYI, I’m not your f***ing gopher.” Carmy’s hilariously witty response is, “FYI, you caulked it up; you’re gonna caulk it out!” The overplayed innuendo adds to the humor, comedically demonstrating how the restaurant staff communicates.

7 “I Wear Suits Now” Season 2, Episode 8

Richie goes through a significant arc in The Bear, season 2. The episode “Forks” is pivotal to him, realizing his potential and value to the restaurant as a people person after training at another restaurant. In “Bolognese,” the effects of that stint are visible, as Richie wears a suit throughout the episode. In a subtle yet funny moment with Sydney, she acknowledges his attire, and Richie replies, “I wear suits now.” As Richie became a fan-favorite and one of the most likable characters in The Bear, the quote became a meme.

6 Hotdog Argument Season 1, Episode 4

In another humorous moment from “Dogs,” Carmy notices the two didn’t bring ketchup to the birthday party. He asks Richie why he didn’t pack ketchup, who replies, “What kind of ahole is going to put ketchup on a hotdog?” Carmy simply replies, “A child, Richie,” to which Richie rebuttals with, “A child ahole.” Carmy is notably frustrated by Richie’s ignorance but has nothing to say aside from “You’re a child a**hole.” It’s a great exchange that showcases the difference in maturity between the two.

5 Sugar Works Her Magic On Uncle Jimmy Season 2, Episode 5

In The Bear, Sugar gets more involved in season 2, adding charm and humor to the restaurant dynamic. It’s clear that Uncle Jimmy has a soft spot for his niece, who utilizes this to get him to help out. Nat butters up Uncle Jimmy, which works like a charm, and the funniest part of the scene is Carmy and Richie spectating. As Sugar exits, she whispers, “Go f*** yourself” to them, and Carmy hilariously comments, “That was f***ing gross, Sugar.”

4 Fak Wheels Out The BallBreaker Machine Season 2, Episode 1

In season 1, episode 1, Fak tries to pitch Carmy on a “Norwegian knockoff of Mortal Combat” called “Ballbreaker.” In the season 2 premiere, the game makes a comedic return as Fak wheels it out of the restaurant. He sings a song to the machine and kisses it, promising to provide it with a new home. It’s a wholesome moment of humor and a fun call-back to season 1.

3 Sydney Stabs Richie Season 1, Episode 7

In one of the many tense outrages in The Bear, Sydney accidentally stabs Richie. During their argument, she threatens him with a knife, and forgetting it’s there, he turns around and backs up into it. 2 Richie And Fak Fight Season 1, Episode 5

In a mock interview, Richie and Fak begin to yell at each other, and a funny verbal confrontation turns into an even funnier fight. The argument gets tense, and the camera cuts away to a different area of the restaurant momentarily before screams can be heard from the other room. Soon, the pair run into the room with the others, fighting, resulting in some fantastic reactions. Carmy observing them and saying, “Oh, that’s cool,” in a dry tone adds to the humor.

1 The Birthday Party Xanax Incident Season 1, Episode 4

The funniest moment and one of the most ridiculous sequences in The Bear happens at the end of Carmy and Richie’s catering stint. In a conversation between Carmy and Pete, the latter points out that all the kids at the party have fallen asleep. Carmy notices shortly after that a bottle of Xanax had been mixed in with the drink, knocking out all the kids. When he explains the situation to Uncle Jimmy, the pair hilariously examine the scene, to which Jimmy quips, “What are they? F***ing dead?”
