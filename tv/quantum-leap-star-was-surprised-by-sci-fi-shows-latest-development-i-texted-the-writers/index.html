Quantum Leap Star Was Surprised By Sci-Fi Shows Latest Development I Texted The Writers”

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Ben’s potential romances take an unexpected turn in Quantum Leap season 2, with scientist Hannah now married with a child. Addison’s engagement to Tom surprises viewers who have seen her unconditional love for Ben in season 1. Addison’s decision to move forward with Tom shows her taking ownership of her life’s direction, adding a new dynamic to the show.

Literature Review

Exclusive: Quantum Leap’s Caitlin Bassett admits that she didn’t expect Addison’s life-changing choice in season 2, episode 9, “Off the Cuff.”

Season 2, episode 9 of NBC’s Quantum Leap takes viewers by surprise by throwing a wrench in both of Ben’s potential romances. After a three-year time jump forced Addison to move on with Tom, Ben finds love with scientist Hannah Carson (Eliza Taylor, The 100), whom he continuously reunites with during his leaps. “Off the Cuff” sees Ben run into Hannah once more, only to discover that nine years have passed since their time in Giza, and she is now married with a child. To further complicate matters, Addison confronts Tom about finding a ring and accepts his spontaneous marriage proposal.

Addison’s engagement to Tom comes as a shock due to the unconditional love she displays for Ben in Quantum Leap Season 1. It’s easy to conceive that Addison would move on after years of believing Ben is dead, but his reappearance and possible homecoming have left her at a loss. In an exclusive interview with Screen Rant, Caitlin Bassett, who plays Addison, admits that she was also surprised by her character’s life-changing decision. Read Bassett’s full quote below:

Caitlin Bassett: I was surprised. I texted the writers. [Laughs] I was like, “Um, guys?” and then they told me where it was all going, and I was like, “Oh, okay. I see.” My favorite part about it was, in the moment when she brings it up, not in a romantic area, or like, “Hey, babe, we need to talk about a thing,” she had her decision before she brought it up. She knew what was probably going to happen, whether in that moment or not. And so, all of a sudden, we start seeing Addison drive her own version of the ship. Instead of reacting to Ben, and what he’s doing, and what’s happening, she’s like, “Okay, that might happen. That might not happen. I don’t know. But it’s time for me to make a decision about my life.” And all of a sudden, we start seeing her take a lot more ownership of her life’s direction, which is really cool.

Quantum Leap Should Follow Through With Addison & Tom’s Engagement

Addison and Tom may be preparing to tie the knot, but if viewers have learned anything from Ben and Addison’s failed engagement, it’s that nothing in Quantum Leap is ever certain. If Tom’s theory is correct and the team does manage to bring Ben home, reuniting with her former fiancé could cause Addison to second-guess her decision. However, because the premise of the show revolves around Ben’s leaps, it’s unlikely that any reunion will be long-lasting.

Ben admits in “Off the Cuff” that Addison should have someone who can be there for her in all the ways she deserves. As long as he continues to leap, the two will never be able to have a fulfilling relationship. Still, a reconciliation between them feels imminent due to the amount of time Quantum Leap continues to invest in Ben and Addison’s current dynamic.

To give the pairing a satisfying story, viewers must truly fear for the couple’s fate. Addison’s marriage to Tom would accomplish just that.

Tom’s likability also causes fans to question their allegiance, as it’s difficult to root against a character who has everyone’s best interests at heart. Rather than being threatened by Ben, Tom has retroactively searched for a way to bring Addison’s ex home, despite the threat it could pose to his relationship. There’s bound to be heartbreak no matter which direction the series takes, but following through with Tom and Addison’s wedding would serve Quantum Leap by adding to its unpredictability and further emphasizing that nothing is set in stone.
