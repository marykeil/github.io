One 2024 Oscars Acting Race Already Looks Over 2 Months Before The Ceremony

SUMMARY

Da’Vine Joy Randolph is dominating the Best Supporting Actress race at the 2024 Oscars. Randolph has already won multiple awards and is listed as the favorite to win in Oscars bets. “The Holdovers” may only have one potential Oscar win, and that could be for Da’Vine Joy Randolph as Best Supporting Actress.

Literature Review

While the competition at the 2024 Oscars is tight, there’s one category that already has a clear winner, two months before the ceremony.

While the competition at the 2024 Oscars is tight in various categories, there’s one that seems to be over two months before the ceremony. After a couple of rough years due to the COVID-19 pandemic, 2023 was a great year for cinema. 2023 not only saw huge box-office releases, like Barbie and The Super Mario Bros. Movie, but also highly-anticipated movies that were delayed due to the pandemic and movies that were critically acclaimed, earning nominations for various awards in different categories.

Among the best movies of 2023, and which have gotten various nominations for different awards, is The Holdovers. Directed by Alexander Payne and written by David Hemingson, The Holdovers is set in 1970 and 1971 and follows Paul Hunham (Paul Giamatti), a strict classics teacher at a New England boarding school who is forced to stay at school over the Christmas break to chaperon the students who have nowhere to go. Among the nominations for the 2024 Academy Awards that The Holdovers got is Best Supporting Actress for Da’Vine Joy Randolph, who has become the clear favorite in the Oscar race.

Da’Vine Joy Randolph Is Dominating The Best Supporting Actress Race Before The Oscars

The nominees for Best Supporting Actress at the 96th Academy Awards are Emily Blunt for Oppenheimer, Danielle Brooks for The Color Purple, America Ferrera for Barbie, Jodie Foster for Nyad, and Da’Vine Joy Randolph for The Holdovers. This category has a variety of performances as these are all very different movies, but there’s already one nominee dominating the race in this category with different awards, and that’s Da’Vine Joy Randolph.

Da’Vine Joy Randolph played Mary Lamb in The Holdovers, the head cook at Barton Academy who is grieving the loss of her son, who was killed serving in the Vietnam War. With her performance as Mary, Randolph has earned nominations for various awards, including the Golden Globes, BAFTAs, Critics’ Choice Movie Awards, and Screen Actors Guild Awards (SAG Awards). At the time of writing, Randolph has already taken home the Critics Choice award, Golden Globe, Los Angeles Film Critics Association award, New York Film Critics Circle award, and National Board of Review award, all in the category of supporting actress.

The Best Supporting Actress Oscars Odds Are Significantly In Da’Vine Joy Randolph’s Favor

While surprise wins at the Academy Awards are not uncommon (and are usually controversial), the actors who win in their category at other big awards are usually the ones who end up taking the Oscar. Randolph is certainly in great company in the Best Supporting Actress category at the Oscars, but she’s already dominating the race, becoming the clear favorite. Not only that, but Randolph is also listed as the favorite to win in places with Oscars bets, so she’s not just the critics’ and industry’s favorite, but also the audience’s.

The Holdovers is also nominated at the Academy Awards for Best Picture, Best Actor (Giamatti), Best Original Screenplay, and Best Film Editing (Kevin Tent). Despite the undeniable great work of the whole cast and crew of The Holdovers, looking at the movie’s track record during the award season, Randolph could very well be the film’s only Oscar win.

The Holdovers has won a couple of awards in the category of Best Picture, but it’s not the favorite to win the Oscar, same with Giamatti as Best Actor. It’s to be seen which surprises the 96th Academy Awards will bring, but it’s unlikely Da’Vine Joy Randolph won’t win Best Supporting Actress.
