10 Biggest Problems With The Harry Potter TV Remake Idea

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Remaking the Harry Potter series for television faces significant challenges due to oversaturation in the market and skepticism from fans. The show must address social issues, plot holes, and narrative missteps in order to distinguish itself and attract a new generation of fans. In order to stand out, the series needs to bring a fresh perspective to the well-known tales and make meaningful efforts to improve diversity and LGBTQ+ representation.

Literature Review

No matter how beloved the Harry Potter universe once was, it may not have enough magic left to warrant yet another iteration of the franchise.

Harry Potter is one of the biggest modern fantasy franchises to come out in the past few decades, but after so many spin-offs and adaptations, there are significant issues to overcome if a television remake is going to get off the ground. Reboots and remakes have gained so much popularity in recent years that the appetite for that kind of media is slowly abating. With the stage play, Harry Potter and the Cursed Child, and the Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them film series both coming out so recently, the market is saturated with Harry Potter. When HBO acquired the rights to make a Harry Potter series, many fans were skeptical, and for good reason. If the show is going to distinguish itself and become popular with a new generation of fans, it has to carefully balance dedication to the source material, and a serious overhaul of the social issues, plot holes, and narrative missteps that have come to the forefront of the cultural conversation. While the show could present the opportunity to right the wrongs of the films, it has its work cut out for it.

12 Book Changes The Harry Potter Remake Should Make The Max Harry Potter TV show promised to be a more faithful adaptation of the Harry Potter books, but it should make some changes to the books.

10 J. K. Rowling’s Public Controversies Many fans no longer align themselves with the author of the books

The author of the series, J.K. Rowling, has broadcast many unpopular opinions, especially about the LGBTQ+ community, in the years since her novels were published. As Rowling will be involved in the series, and garner a portion of the royalties, watching the series would be seen by some as support for, and a condoning of her sentiments. As so many LGBT+ people initially felt seen by the themes of accepting outsiders in the books, her criticism of trans activism has made it difficult, or impossible, for many fans to support any of her endeavors moving forward.

9 Nostalgia For The Original Films Adults grew up watching the film series in their childhood

Harry Potter was a large part of many people’s childhoods, especially the generation that’s coming into adulthood in the 2020s. Regardless of what the movies may have left out, or how they could be improved upon, films that are seen in childhood become representative of that period and hold a lot of nostalgia. Anyone who has watched the films will be comparing the show to them throughout their viewing, and their perception will be skewed.

8 Finding A Fresh Take On The Story After growing into such a large franchise there aren’t many new angles to explore

If the show is going to stand out among the many iterations of the franchise, it must bring a new spin to the well-known tales. If the Cursed Child movie happens, the show could get lost in the deluge of content that’s being produced based on the wizarding world. A new perspective can mean many things, and since there is such a broad range of fans of the novels, the TV series must walk a fine line in trying to please everyone. Making interesting narrative choices that aren’t pandering isn’t easy for any series, especially one so high profile.

7 Lack Of Diversity In Source Material The few characters of color are poorly represented, and no effort is made at intersectionality

Though the representation of characters of color is severely lacking, and what representation there is relies heavily on stereotyping, that is not the only way that marginalized groups are excluded in the novels and films. Including actors of different ethnicities, races, gender expressions, as well as neurodivergent individuals are only a few of the ways that the series could improve upon the original content. However, these changes must be made with authenticity and sensitivity to do justice to the people marginalized by the novels. Inclusion just for the sake of optics would be a major downfall for the upcoming show.

6 Audiences Tiring Of The Franchise Too many different spin-offs and reboots have made the stories stale

There are only so many ways that the world of a story can be explored, and there’s the possibility that fans will stop being interested if the storylines are too repetitive. The Marvel Cinematic Universe has been facing these issues since the conclusion of the Avengers series, seeing a significant decrease in viewership of their recent films. If viewers simply aren’t engaged by revisiting a story they already know, there’s not much that can change their minds.

5 Lack Of LGBTQ+ Representation Little to none at all, or additions made post-publishing

Though LGBTQ+ characters were part of retroactive additions to the Harry Potter canon, not all of them were justified or well-thought-out. In the books, practically no characters are discussed or described as being part of the LGBTQ+ community, and the most prominent character that Rowling has claimed to be gay is Dumbledore. If the series intends for this to become part of the story, then it can’t be added as an afterthought. There must be purposeful and nuanced plot points that expand beyond merely announcing the identity of a character.

4 Making Audiences Connect With New Actors Most fans are attached to the child actors they watched grow up in the films

Recasting the characters for Harry Potter’s remake will be one of the most difficult undertakings for the series. Not only do current fans have deep connections and attachments to the original actors, but the films got extremely lucky with the abilities of the child actors they cast. Not every child actor grows up to be a skilled performer, and the commitment of dedicating one’s entire childhood to one franchise is a big ask. Additionally, iconic U.K. actors like Alan Rickman, Maggie Smith, and Michael Gambon have become synonymous with their roles and the performances they gave. 3 Including The Fantastic Beasts Lore A spin-off franchise that was not as popular as the original series

Though the Fantastic Beasts films were not as popular as the Harry Potter films, they were watched widely enough to mean that the new background information introduced must be included. Much of this pertains to Dumbledore and his backstory. The movies were told from Harry’s perspective, so his lack of knowledge about the past of the wizarding world is acceptable. However, at this point, too much history has been added to the franchise to be ignored. How this is taken into consideration when making the show will set the tone for the series.

2 Getting Fans Who Have Disavowed The Series To Watch For many reasons, audiences have moral scruples with supporting the new show

There are plenty of people who are excited about the possibility of a new series, but a not insignificant portion of fans have decidedly turned their back on the franchise forever. This begs the question of whether the show is going to rely on appealing to a younger audience who are being introduced to Harry Potter for the first time. Due to the internet and the fast spread of information, nothing stays under wraps for long, and new fans will quickly become acquainted with the political issues of the series, and decide if they want to support it.

1 There Is No Real Reason To Remake It The story has already been properly told, and any updates will feel forced

The only motivating factor that makes sense is that producers and filmmakers are being driven by the inevitable paycheck they’ll receive if the Harry Potter series becomes popular. While this may be a cynical sentiment, there aren’t other clear reasons besides correcting some of the mistakes that the films make. However, the films told a fully realized story and included the work of some of the greatest living filmmakers, actors, and creators of the modern era.
