The Watcher Season 2 Has A True-Crime Show Problem (But It Can Be Fixed)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The Watcher Season 2 may shift away from real-life events, potentially affecting its appeal to true crime fans. The unresolved cliffhanger from Season 1 could lead to a fresh direction for the show’s narrative in Season 2. The possibility of transitioning to a true crime anthology series for The Watcher Season 2 opens up new storytelling avenues.

Literature Review

The Watcher season 2 presents an interesting issue that doesn’t usually concern true-crime shows, but it can be solved with some creative thinking.

The Watcher season 2 may need to overcome an intriguing true-crime obstacle when it’s being produced, but there is a simple way to solve the issue too. Stories from the true-crime genre, like The Watcher, often appeal to their fans because they’re inspired by events that happened in reality. While thrillers written from scratch still have appeal, the fact that a version of the scenario on-screen actually unfolded adds an extra layer of unease. The Watcher season 1 was able to cash in on the true-crime hype, but The Watcher season 2 could redefine the genre’s boundaries.

The Watcher season 1 first hit Netflix in October 2022 and took its narrative from real-life events. The Watcher cast was filled with stars. As well as being led by Naomi Watts and Bobby Cannavale, the show also had other notable names, such as Richard Kind and American Pie’s Jennifer Coolidge. Netflix originally billed The Watcher as a miniseries but quickly commissioned The Watcher season 2 the month after its release. While there is plenty of cause to be excited about the show’s follow-up season, there are some difficult decisions that will have to be made before it can happen.

The Watcher Season 2 Has No Remaining Real-Life Events On Which To Base Its Story Further installments of The Watcher could be pure fiction

Despite The Watcher season 2 being confirmed, the show’s renewal is a little more unconventional than most. The Watcher season 1 was based on real events, and it also included a great many of the case’s findings and ended with a line of text that makes the show difficult to continue with any level of authenticity. The Watcher season 1 confirms that the real culprit was never identified in reality.

Although it makes for great entertainment, The Watcher season 1 isn’t completely faithful to what really happened. The true story behind The Watcher was mainly concerning the family in question being harassed and stalked by a mysterious and unknown figure. For instance, the house’s underground passageway didn’t exist in the real building and was added to benefit the narrative. So, it goes to show that The Watcher’s writers are more than comfortable taking creative liberties if it makes the story more engaging.

The Watcher Season 1 Ended On A Cliffhanger There is no resolution for The Watcher season 1 (Just like in the real case)

The Watcher’s season 1 finale ended with the dreaded words, “The Watcher case remains unsolved.” As such, it becomes difficult to imagine how The Watcher season 2 can pick up where it left off, having already exhausted its source material. So, given that The Watcher has already reached a natural finishing point, season 2 could explore an avenue that isn’t usually open to other true-time stories.

The Watcher case remains unsolved.

Despite the hasty renewal of The Watcher, very little is known about season 2’s cast or story. However, it’s possible that The Watcher season 2 could use the season 1 cliffhanger to its advantage. All the relevant facts surrounding the original cast may have already been used up, but that doesn’t necessarily stop Netflix from continuing the narrative themselves. This would allow for further theorizing and speculation before finally giving the audience a certain level of closure by writing a satisfying ending to the tale rather than leaving it unresolved.

So, although The Watcher’s season 1 cliffhanger ending can be viewed as frustrating, it’s also ripe for continuation. The Watcher isn’t the first true-crime show to end with a tantalizing loose end, and it definitely won’t be the last. However, it could start a new trend of shows picking up where reality ends and adding a conclusion that the real story wasn’t fortunate to receive. Speculation about unsolved cases is one of the biggest appeals of true-crime media, and so The Watcher season 2 taking this route could just be an extension of the craze.

Adding To The “Real” Events Of The Watcher Season 1 Would Be Risky The show’s true-crime genre label could be called into question

The Watcher season 1 draws on its real-world origins to create a level of nail-biting tension that isn’t always achievable with fully fictional narratives. The audience’s knowledge of what they’re watching being based in reality is part of what gives it its appeal. So, continuing The Watcher with scripts that aren’t based on genuine events could detract from its quality and make it less appealing to true-crime fans.

Adding to the events of The Watcher with speculative theorizing would also arguably move the show out of the genre of true crime and into a realm more akin to historical fiction. While The Watcher season 1 took creative liberties with its source material, it was still grounded in reality and only augmented itself with fictional elements. The creation of The Watcher season 2 by extending the events of the previous installment risks the show losing its identity and makes the decision to remove the “limited series” label difficult to justify if it doesn’t turn out well.

However, for all the risks involved in making The Watcher season 2 a sequel to the previous installment, it could still be an innovative step forward for storytelling in the crime-fiction genre. In addition, it would also allow Bobby Cannavale, Naomi Watts, and the rest of the cast from The Watcher season 1 to return and shoot more episodes. The casting for the show’s first season was brilliant, so it would be a shame not to see them again.

No major characters died in The Watcher season 1, so they could all technically return.

The Watcher Season 2 Could Focus On A Different Story Altogether An anthology series may be the right step forward for The Watcher

The lack of information about The Watcher season 2’s story means there’s no guarantee that the show will continue to follow the Brannock family. This gives the option to alter course and for The Watcher to become a true-crime anthology show rather than a serial drama. Comments from The Watcher star Naomi Watts give the impression that this could be a very possible course of action for the coming installment. When speaking with Entertainment Weekly, Watts said, “I don’t know if we’ll be coming back.”

While Watts’ statement could be in relation to her being as out-of-the-loop as the show’s fans, it could also be a sign that the main cast will be swapped out so The Watcher season 2 can tell an entirely new story. The title of the show doesn’t explicitly reference the events of season 1, but rather the shadowy individual who sent the ominous letters to the Brannock family. Therefore, although The Watcher season 2 would likely be limited to similar cases about stalkers or voyeurs, it could be the start of the show’s transition into a true-crime anthology series.

The Watcher season 1 is available to stream on Netflix. There is currently no release date for The Watcher season 2.
