10 TV Actors That Netflix Can Thank For Its Success

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Netflix owes its success to talented actors who have made its shows popular, both fresh talent and established actors. Working with Netflix provides actors access to a huge global audience, making it a badge of honor for some. Casting the right actors is essential for successful Netflix shows, and the platform has a track record of choosing the right talent.

Literature Review

Netflix is the biggest streaming service in the world, and it owes some of this success to a group of TV actors who helped make its best shows.

Netflix can stake a claim to being the most influential streaming platform in the world, and it owes a huge amount of that success to the actors who have made its shows so popular. While Netflix has developed a reputation for nurturing fresh talent, it also benefits from established actors. Some actors have appeared in multiple Netflix shows, becoming synonymous with the streaming service. These actors have helped Netflix stand out from the crowd and become the most popular source for original TV shows on streaming. Working with Netflix has become a badge of honor for some actors, since Netflix provides access to an enormous global audience. Successful Netflix shows will be seen by hundreds of millions of people all over the world, so casting the right actors is essential. Fortunately for them, Netflix has an impressive track record of casting the right actors in the right roles. Without the work of a few great actors, Netflix wouldn’t have become what it is today, and its best shows are consistently those with outstanding casts.

15 Best Netflix TV Shows Of 2023 Despite the competition, Netflix is still one of the biggest and most prestigious streaming platforms. Here are their best TV releases of 2023.

10 Millie Bobby Brown The breakout star of Stranger Things has become one of Netflix’s leading lights

The first season of Stranger Things introduced a small town full of perplexing mysteries, but none were as captivating as the origins of Eleven, a child with strange paranormal abilities who seemingly had no experience of the world. Millie Bobby Brown was essential to the show’s success in its first season, outshining the other child actors in the show handily. She has also appeared in the two Enola Holmes movies for Netflix, and her upcoming fantasy adventure movie Damsel looks likely to continue her beneficial relationship with the streaming service.

Stranger Things Season 5: Renewal, Cast, Story Conclusion & Everything We Know Stranger Things season 5 will bring the epic Netflix sci-fi horror saga to a conclusion, and here’s everything we know about Eleven’s return.

9 Julia Garner Garner made Ozark what it was, then got her own role in the spotlight

Julia Garner plays Ozark’s gloriously profane Ruth Langmore, a volatile supporting character who frequently steals the show. Garner won three Emmy awards for Best Supporting Actress In A Drama Series during Ozark’s run, underlining just how important she was to the show’s popularity. After Ozark, she played another villainous maverick, the cosmopolitan con artist Anna Delvey in Inventing Anna, a biopic true crime miniseries from Shonda Rhimes. Again, Garner delivered a brilliant performance. She also played a minor role in the Netflix series Maniac, starring Emma Stone and Jonah Hill.

8 Jason Bateman A comedic actor who took a powerful dramatic turn

Although the Netflix seasons of Arrested Development failed to recapture the show’s original brilliance, it was still successful for the streaming service. Jason Bateman plays Michael Bluth, the son of a real estate mogul who is arrested for embezzlement, and it falls on him to save the family business. After Arrested Development ended, Bateman took an unexpected move by leading Ozark. The comedy actor made the transition to drama look simple. He contributed to the show’s success as both an actor and director, proving that he can’t be easily categorized.

7 Laverne Cox The actor and activist has pioneered trans representation on mainstream TV

Laverne Cox also appeared in Inventing Anna alongside Julia Garner. She plays Kacy Duke, a fitness trainer to the stars who becomes sucked into Anna Delvey’s orbit. She appeared in all nine episodes of the mini series. Cox also plays an important role in Orange Is the New Black, one of Netflix’s first huge hits. Cox’s performance as Litchfield inmate Sophia Burset earned her an Emmy nomination, making her the first trans actor to ever achieve the feat. Sophia is an ally and a friend to Piper, although more of her troubled past is gradually revealed as the series unfolds.

6 John Mulaney Mulaney has been huge for Netflix as both an actor and a comedian

John Mulaney has created several comedy specials for Netflix, boosting the platform’s credentials as a leader for stand-up, but he’s also had an important role as an actor. His musical special John Mulaney & the Sack Lunch Bunch combines both acting and improvisational comedy, taking inspiration from beloved children’s shows like Sesame Street. But Mulaney’s biggest contribution to Netflix’s TV catalog is Big Mouth, the raunchy animated coming-of-age sitcom he stars in with Nick Kroll. Season 8 of Big Mouth will be the show’s last, with Mulaney’s Andrew Glouberman in high school.

5 Will Arnett The gravelly voice behind Netflix’s biggest animated hit

Will Arnett’s Gob Bluth was one of the zaniest, most unpredictable characters in Arrested Development. The brilliant ensemble cast would not have been the same without Arnett, but his next Netflix project gave him more attention as the undisputed star of the show. Arnett plays the eponymous equine in BoJack Horseman, an experimental sitcom about a washed-up actor desperately clinging to his waning influence. Arnett also plays grizzled detective Terry Seattle in the improvised murder mystery show Murderville, which features a new celebrity guest trying to solve a crime with each episode.

15 Shows To Watch If You Like BoJack Horseman BoJack Horseman became a hit for Netflix, with its intelligent combination of humor and pathos. Here are ten shows to watch that hit the same notes.

4 Pedro Alonso Alonso stood out from the crowd in Money Heist and got his own spinoff

In 2017, Netflix acquired the global streaming rights to a Spanish crime show which the platform renamed Money Heist. It was an instant phenomenon, and Netflix soon ordered a continuation of the series. Pedro Alonso stood out among Money Heist’s ensemble cast, with his cool and collected persona often at odds with the volatility and recklessness of Berlin’s actions. Berlin is a criminal with a sense of showmanship, and he even makes the members of his own gang feel uneasy. The Money Heist spinoff focusing on Berlin premiered in December 2023.

3 Jenna Ortega Ortega turned a difficult character into a compelling heroine

Wednesday Addams first appeared in comic strips in the 1930s, and she was a key character in The Addams Family TV show in the 1960s. Rather than trying to completely transplant the character into a modern setting, Netflix’s Wednesday incorporates plenty of anachronistic touches which better suit Wednesday’s Gothic tones. Jenna Ortega had the tough task of making a morbid and emotionally closed-off character appear sympathetic and compelling, but her performance in season 1 was an unequivocal success, and season 2 of Wednesday promises more of the same. Ortega was also a standout performer in season 2 of You.

2 Lee Jung-Jae Lee is vital to Netflix’s most successful non-English language show ever

The unprecedented global success of Squid Game can be partly attributed to the show’s stunning production design and universal egalitarian theme, but it wouldn’t work nearly as well without such intriguing performances. Lee Jung-Jae was already a well-known actor in South Korea, but his role as Seong Gi-hun catapulted him to international stardom. Lee is essential to Squid Game’s dystopian tone, and he was nominated for a slew of awards after the first season was released. Lee is set to appear in the second season of Squid Game and the mysterious Star Wars show, The Acolyte.

Squid Game Season 2: Renewal, Cast, Story & Everything We Know Squid Game, the Korean Netflix phenomenon that took the world by storm in 2021, is heading back for season 2, and here’s everything we know.

1 Simone Ashley The British actor is adept in both comedy and drama

Simone Ashley stars in two of Netflix’s most popular shows, Bridgerton and Sex Education. In Sex Education, she plays Olivia, one of “The Untouchables” and an intimidatingly popular student at Moordale. Despite having limited screen time, Ashley still provides some memorable moments, making the most of what she’s given. Bridgerton gives her a much juicier role. She plays Kate, who travels to England with her sister and gets thrust into the unfamiliar surroundings of Britain’s aristocratic elite. She is strong-willed and witty, protecting both herself and her sister from unwanted attention.
