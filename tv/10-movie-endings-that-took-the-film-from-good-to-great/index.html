10 Movie Endings That Took The Film From Good To Great

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

A shocking revelation in Primal Fear’s ending showcases Edward Norton’s exceptional acting talent. The Usual Suspects’ shocking end reveal completely turns the film on its head. Rogue One: A Star Wars Story’s Darth Vader fan service ending is ten minutes of pure spectacle.

Literature Review

From memorable visuals to shocking twists, a movie’s final moments can take it from good to great, and these ten films demonstrate how.

Sometimes, a movie’s ending can take it from good to great and there are several examples of this throughout Hollywood history. Whether that’s due to a shocking twist, a final visual, or a thrilling climax, a film’s ending is often what audiences remember most. It’s the final impression and the last part of the film they see. A great ending resonates, lingering in the viewer’s mind long after the credits roll. It can redefine the entire narrative, casting earlier scenes in a new light or bringing a satisfying resolution to the story’s arc. This pivotal moment can elevate the entire film, staying with the audience long after the film has ended. Crafting the perfect ending is crucial; a misstep can lose viewers, while a masterful one can make a film unforgettable. An exceptional ending can become the defining feature of a movie. This underscores an important notion: the power of an ending can distinguish a good movie from a great one. Often, it’s the ending that people remember most about a movie. The most complete and satisfying movie endings do more than just finish the story - they can offer viewers a fresh perspective on the entire film, surprise or horrify, or offer spectacular visuals that end the film on a high note.

10 Primal Fear (1996) A shocking revelation in Primal Fear’s ending showcases Edward Norton’s exceptional acting talent.

Primal Fear (1996)

Release Date April 1, 1996 Director Gregory Hoblit Cast Richard Gere , Laura Linney , John Mahoney , Alfre Woodard , Frances McDormand , Andre Braugher , Maura Tierney , Edward Norton Runtime 130 Minutes

The 1996 thriller Primal Fear, has one of the most surprising endings of all time. Starring Richard Gere as defense attorney Martin Vail and Edward Norton as altar boy Aaron Stampler, the movie’s climax delivers a stunning revelation. Initially, Aaron appears as a timid, innocent figure, but the final scene shockingly unveils that his violent alter-ego “Roy” is, in fact, his true self, and the meek Aaron never existed. This twist in Primal Fear’s ending showcases Norton’s exceptional performance and leaves both the audience and Vail in a chilling state of disbelief. However, the film has since received negative criticism from modern audiences for inaccuracies surrounding mental illness.

9 The Usual Suspects (1995) A shocking end reveal completely turns the film on its head.

The Usual Suspects
R Crime Mystery Thriller

Release Date August 16, 1995 Director Bryan Singer Cast Kevin Spacey , Kevin Pollak , Benicio Del Toro , Gabriel Byrne , stephen baldwin , Chazz Palminteri

The Usual Suspects, directed by Bryan Singer, revolves around a boat massacre and the interrogation of a small-time con man (Kevin Spacey). The film follows his intricate recounting of what happened. However, the reveal that the con man is actually the elusive criminal mastermind Keyser Söze adds a shocking end twist. This twist, highlighting the concept of an unreliable narrator, turns the entire narrative upside down and cements the film’s status as a classic. Despite the controversies surrounding Spacey, the ending remains a standout in cinematic history for its sheer unpredictability.

8 Rogue One: A Star Wars Story (2016) Ten minutes of Darth Vader fan service done right.

Rogue One: A Star Wars Story
12+ Action Sci-Fi Adventure

Release Date December 13, 2016 Director Gareth Edwards Cast Felicity Jones , Diego Luna , Ben Mendelsohn , Donnie Yen , Mads Mikkelsen , Alan Tudyk , Jiang Wen , Forest Whitaker

In Rogue One: A Star Wars Story, the iconic hallway massacre by Darth Vader is a fan favorite moment and significantly enhances the film’s ending. This prequel to A New Hope follows Jyn Erso (Felicity Jones) and her team, who embark on a perilous mission to steal the Death Star plans. The film’s climax, featuring Vader’s formidable display of power, is a visual spectacle of well-crafted fan service and it does exactly what it sets out to do - it makes Vader scary again. The scene effortlessly blends into the moments preceding A New Hope, serving as a bridge into the Original Trilogy.

7 Annihilation (2018) A visually spectacular and ambiguous ending leaves viewers with unanswered questions.

Annihilation
R Sci-Fi Documentary Mystery Thriller

Release Date February 22, 2018 Director Alex Garland Cast Sonoya Mizuno , Kola Bokinni , Jennifer Jason Leigh , Gina Rodriguez , Cosmo Jarvis , Oscar Isaac , Tessa Thompson , Tuva Novotny , Natalie Portman , David Gyasi

Annihilation by Alex Garland culminates in a profound, visually spectacular ending that raises questions about the characters’ fates. The story follows Lena, a scientist on a mission to save her husband, Kane, who is affected by a mysterious force called the Shimmer. In the climax, Lena confronts a clone created by the Shimmer. The clone then appears to self-destruct, and Lena and Kane are later reunited. The ending of the movie leaves the audience in a state of uncertainty about the extent of the Shimmer’s impact on the couple. The ending’s ambiguity, marked by their unsettling, glowing eyes, suggests a deeper, possibly sinister transformation.

6 The Secret Life Of Walter Mitty (2013) Walter finds happiness in who he is.

The Secret Life of Walter Mitty
PG Adventure Comedy Drama

Release Date December 25, 2013 Director Ben Stiller Cast Ben Stiller , Kristen Wiig , Shirley MacLaine , Adam Scott , Kathryn Hahn , Sean Penn

The Secret Life of Walter Mitty, directed by Ben Stiller, finishes with an inspiring ending that transforms Walter (Ben Stiller), a daydreamer, into a man of action. Throughout the film, Walter embarks on a real-world adventure to find a crucial photo, moving away from his mundane routine. The ending includes him finding the photo in an unexpected place, confronting his boss, and connecting with his crush, Cheryl (Kristen Wiig). This upbeat ending hammers down the theme of breaking free from life’s constraints and embracing real adventures. It’s an inspiring end to a film full of optimism.

5 The Mist (2007) The Mist has one of the most controversial and disturbing endings in film.

The Mist
R Sci-Fi Horror

Release Date November 21, 2007 Director Frank Darabont Cast Laurie Holden , Thomas Jane , Andre Braugher , Toby Jones , Marcia Gay Harden

The Mist has one of the most decisive movie endings of all time. The 2007 film adaptation of Stephen King’s short story is dark, disturbing, and famous for its hard-to-watch ending. The Mist is a horror film following a group of townspeople trapped in a supermarket by a mysterious, deadly mist containing monstrous creatures. The film ends when David, after finding his wife dead and running out of gas, tragically shoots his friends and son before realizing he has no bullet left for himself. It’s harrowing, extremely dark, and exactly the kind of shocking ending that takes this horror movie from good to great.

4 Se7en (1995) Evil wins in the end in Se7en’s shocking twist ending.

Se7en
R Thriller Drama Crime Mystery

Release Date September 22, 1995 Director David Fincher Cast Brad Pitt , gwyneth paltrow , John C. McGinley , Morgan Freeman , Kevin Spacey

Nearly three decades later, the ending of David Fincher’s Se7en still stands out as a defining moment in cinema. The story follows detectives Somerset (Morgan Freeman) and Mills (Brad Pitt) as they track a serial killer basing his murders on the seven deadly sins. The climax reveals the killer’s sinister plan: the final two victims are himself and Mills’ wife, Tracy. Mills, in a fit of wrath, completes the cycle by killing the killer. This shocking twist ending demonstrates just why Se7en is labeled a masterpiece. Evil wins out in the end, as the film’s final moments demonstrate how even the righteous can be driven to sin.

3 Don’t Look Now (1973) Based on the short story by Daphne du Maurier.

Don’t Look Now
R Horror Thriller Drama

Release Date November 18, 1973 Director Nicolas Roeg Cast Clelia Matania , Hilary Mason , Julie Christie , Donald Sutherland , Adelina Poerio

The film adaptation of Daphne du Maurier’s short story is famous for having one of the most shocking and surprising twist endings in cinema history. Set in Venice, it follows a grieving couple, John Baxter (Donald Sutherland) and Laura Baxter (Julie Christie), coping with their child’s accidental drowning. In this climax, John, haunted by the image of a small figure in a red coat similar to his deceased daughter’s, encounters not his child, but a disfigured dwarf serial killer who violently ends his life. This startling twist transforms Don’t Look Now from an eerie horror movie into a gut-wrenching classic.

2 The Conversation (1974) The film’s protagonist is perfectly summarized by a strong final visual.

The movie follows surveillance expert Harry Caul (Gene Hackman), who becomes obsessed with a recorded conversation, mistakenly believing it signals a murder plot. The gripping finale reveals Caul’s error: the supposed victims were actually the plotters. This twist leads to a poignant final scene where a paranoid Caul, warned to stay uninvolved, frantically searches his apartment for bugs, ending up alone and playing his saxophone amidst the ruins of his life. The powerful visual of Caul playing the saxophone, the only thing left undamaged in his apartment, perfectly captures Caul’s character - a lonely, paranoid man.

1 The Graduate (1967) An ambiguous ending that perfectly captures the uncertainty of the future.

The Graduate
PG Romance Comedy Drama

Release Date December 21, 1967 Director Mike Nichols Cast Dustin Hoffman , Katharine Ross , Murray Hamilton , Anne Bancroft , William Daniels

The Graduate is famous for having one of the most famous endings of all time. After Benjamin Braddock (Dustin Hoffman) disrupts Elaine’s (Katharine Ross) wedding, the two impulsively escape on a bus. However, their initial excitement fades into confusion and apprehension about their future. Director Mike Nichols captures this pivotal moment in a lingering shot of the couple’s silent, blank expressions. Filled with uncertainty and complexity, The Graduate’s ending transforms the film from good to a timeless masterpiece thanks to superb acting and exceptional directing.
