10 TV Scenes That Made Us Realize A Character Was About To Die

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Subtle clues like a particular song or a character’s demeanor often foreshadow TV character deaths, setting up viewer anticipation. Iconic TV character deaths, like in Game of Thrones or Breaking Bad, linger with audiences due to their shocking and unique execution. Television shows often use narrative cues and character interactions to hint at impending deaths, leaving viewers emotionally invested.

Literature Review

From scenes in How I Met Your Mother to Game of Thrones to The Sopranos, these indicators made audiences realize a character was about to die.

While some TV deaths are completely shocking, some shows prepared audiences with scenes that indicated a death was coming. There are several ways a TV series can give away an impending character death. Shows like Game of Thrones integrated subtle foreshadowing even in their most shocking death scenes. Other shows have used narrative clichés like a character having a particularly happy or redeeming moment, which perceptive television audiences might receive as a hint that their time is up. Given the intense attachment viewers have to television characters, it’s no surprise that TV deaths linger with audiences for years after the show’s release. There are many TV character deaths fans still talk about despite having happened years ago, and shock value is often a huge reason why. Great shows like The Wire and Succession found unique, clever ways to subvert audience expectations with iconic deaths, setting them up in ways no one could see coming.

10 The Rains Of Castamere Play At The Red Wedding Game of Thrones Season 3, Episode 9

Game Of Thrones

Release Date April 11, 2011 Cast Emilia Clarke , Lena Headey , Richard Madden , Michelle Fairley , Kit Harington , Maisie Williams , Alfie Allen , Peter Dinklage Seasons 8 Streaming Service(s) HBO Max

Famously, the Red Wedding is one of the most unexpected things to happen in Game of Thrones, devastating audiences who’d fallen for Robb Stark and the sense of hope he offered the series. While it was an extraordinarily shocking moment for most viewers, it’s fair to say that there’s a clear indicator preparing audiences for what’s about to happen. As the doors to the dining hall in the Twins are shut, “The Rains of Castamere” is played by violinists attending the wedding. Catelyn Stark is notably skeptical of the music choice, as “The Rains of Castamere” is known as a Lannister song. The lyrics tell the story of Tywin Lannister’s crushing defeat of House Reyne, a rebel vassal of the Lannisters, years before Game of Thrones. At the Red Wedding, the song is played, indicating that another group of rebels opposing Tywin Lannister’s might is about to be crushed. The song is played at earlier instances in the show, such as the episode “Blackwater,” to introduce TV audiences to the song and its meaning in preparation.

9 Eddie And Dustin’s Conversation Stranger Things Season 4, Episode 9

Stranger Things
Fantasy Horror Drama

Release Date July 15, 2016 Cast Finn Wolfhard , joe keery , Jamie Campbell Bower , Brett Gelman , Caleb McLaughlin , Maya Hawke , David Harbour , Matthew Modine , Priah Ferguson , Gaten Matarazzo , Winona Ryder , Charlie Heaton , Sadie Sink , Millie Bobby Brown , Joseph Quinn , Dacre Montgomery , Natalia Dyer , Noah Schnapp Seasons 4 Franchise Stranger Things Streaming Service(s) Netflix

Stranger Things season 4 is undoubtedly an excellent piece of television, with Eddie’s death being one of the most tragic moments in the series, given how quickly he’d risen to become a fan favorite. Given the show’s tendencies to introduce new characters and subsequently kill them off, that could’ve been a hint of its own that Eddie was doomed from the moment he was introduced in season 4. However, Eddie and Dustin’s final conversation made it obvious he wasn’t going to last.

8 Arzt Messes With Dynamite Lost Season 1, Episode 24

Lost
TV-14 Adventure Drama Mystery Sci-Fi Supernatural

Release Date September 22, 2004 Cast Evangeline Lilly , Naveen Andrews , Henry Ian Cusick , Daniel Dae Kim , Harold Perrineau , Dominic Monaghan , Emilie de Ravin , Jorge Garcia , Josh Holloway , Michael Emerson , Terry O’Quinn , Matthew Fox , Ken Leung , Elizabeth Mitchell , Yunjin Kim Seasons 6

Word to the wise: playing with dynamite typically isn’t a great idea. In the Lost season 1 finale, the minor character Leslie Arzt is established as an arrogant high school science teacher who fancies himself far more intelligent than he actually is. His hubris leads to his demise as he condescendingly explains to the other survivors how to handle dynamite. At this point, what’s about to happen is self-evident, yet the sudden explosion is still quite a jumpscare.

7 Oberyn Martell Gets A Bit Too Arrogant Game of Thrones Season 4, Episode 8

Game of Thrones is known for being a show where no character is safe, and nothing really ever seems to end well for the good guys. When Oberyn Martell faces off with The Mountain in Tyrion’s trial by combat, things appear to be going well, with the Dornish prince seemingly the perfect foil for the hulking knight. But nothing good ever lasts in George R.R. Martin’s world, as the downed Mountain uses his last bit of strength to topple Oberyn over and crush his skull with his bare hands. Oberyn’s demise is one of the most brutal and horrific deaths in Game of Thrones, and that’s saying something. The indication that Oberyn is about to be killed is his arrogance, as he looms over The Mountain, demanding that his enemy admit to his past atrocities before dying. Anticipation builds as Oberyn plays with his food, and after a certain point, it becomes clear that his victory is too good to be true.

6 Gus Fring Exits His Car To Epic Music Breaking Bad Season 4, Episode 13

Breaking Bad
Crime Drama Thriller

Release Date January 20, 2008 Cast Dean Norris , Bob Odenkirk , Aaron Paul , RJ Mitte , Anna Gunn , Giancarlo Esposito , Betsy Brandt , Bryan Cranston , Jonathan Banks Seasons 5 Streaming Service(s) Netflix

Another fantastic TV death hinted with epic music is that of Gus Fring in Breaking Bad’s penultimate season. In one of the most shocking Breaking Bad moments, Gus Fring enters a nursing home to face off with his long-time enemy, Hector Salamanca. Little does he know Walter White has prepared a bomb to end his life. The build-up to the scene is the most significant indicator that a climactic death is underway, as Breaking Bad sparingly uses a musical score throughout the series. The song “Goodbye” by Apparat, playing to Gus Fring’s final walk, is as straightforward a sign as any.

5 Bobby Bacala In The Model Train Store The Sopranos Season 6, Episode 20

The Sopranos
TV-MA Crime Drama

Release Date January 10, 1999 Cast James Gandolfini , Lorraine Bracco , Edie Falco , Michael Imperioli , Dominic Chianese , Steven Van Zandt , Tony Sirico , Robert Iler , Jamie-Lynn Sigler Seasons 6 Streaming Service(s) Max

It wouldn’t be entirely accurate to state that Bobby Bacala’s death was easy to see coming. It’s genuinely one of the most heartbreaking TV deaths ever and one of the most devastating scenes in The Sopranos. However, by season 6, most viewers will have noticed that the series is very precise with its time. With that being said, spending precious minutes in a model train store in the penultimate episode of a show about the mafia has to have a purpose, and it serves as the calm before the storm as Bobby is tragically gunned down.

4 Tracy’s Wedding Comment How I Met Your Mother Season 9, Episode 19

How I Met Your Mother
Comedy Drama

Release Date September 19, 2005 Cast Josh Radnor , Jason Segel , Cobie Smulders , Neil Patrick Harris , Alyson Hannigan , Bob Saget Seasons 9 Writers Carter Bays , Craig Thomas Streaming Service(s) Amazon Prime Video

The reveal that Tracy had passed away was shocking, though How I Met Your Mother left many clues in preparation. One specific scene in the episode “Vesuvius” sees Ted and Tracy having dinner, and Ted refers back to Robin’s wedding and her mother almost not showing up. Tracy responds, “What mother is going to miss her daughter’s wedding?” Ted begins to cry, though it’s unclear why. Ted being overcome with emotion could only be explained by knowing Tracy was ill, and it wouldn’t be a stretch to have already assumed her death, given her absence in most of the show.

3 Dan Jenkins Home Invasion Yellowstone Season 2, Episode 10

Yellowstone
TV-MA Western Drama

Release Date June 20, 2018 Cast Finn Little , Brecken Merrill , Gil Birmingham , Denim Richards , Ian Bohen , Danny Huston , Kelly Reilly , Forrie J. Smith , Cole Hauser , Kelsey Asbille , Wes Bentley , Jefferson White , Kevin Costner , Luke Grimes , Ryan Bingham Seasons 5

Dan Jenkins never really seems cut out for the world of Montana cowboys and ranches. By the end of Yellowstone season 1, he’s already hung from a tree and seems down and out, yet he returns as a loose ally to the Dutton ranch for season 2. When he hires his bodyguard and begins to prepare himself for combat, it becomes clear that he’s going to be killed in a shootout that he’s not competent enough to participate in. The moment his home is invaded in the season 2 finale, it’s clear that his time is up.

2 Moira Queen Knows Oliver Is The Arrow Arrow Season 2, Episode 20

Arrow
Superhero Drama Action Mystery Crime

Release Date January 10, 2013 Cast colin donnell , Paul Blackthorne , Willa Holland , Susanna Thompson , Katie Cassidy , David Ramsey , Stephen Amell Seasons 8 Writers Greg Berlanti , Marc Guggenheim , Andrew Kreisberg

Superheroes tend to have trouble maintaining parental relations, which is heavily apparent in the Arrowverse, as anytime a character seems to reconcile with their parent, they’re killed off shortly after. Moira Queen suffered her fate at the end of Arrow season 2, just after admitting to Oliver that she knew he was the Arrow. The scene is too good to be true, as Oliver’s vigilante alter ego was central to their drama in the first couple of seasons, and perceptive audiences could’ve predicted her death was imminent.
