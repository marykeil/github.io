10 Fargo Characters We d Love To See Return In Future Seasons

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Fargo has had characters from past seasons crossover into new ones, suggesting that old characters could return in minor roles. Season 6 of Fargo is likely to happen, with possibilities of exploring different time periods and bringing back old characters. Each season of Fargo introduces new casts, but the addition of returning characters would be welcomed by viewers.

Literature Review

Though each season of Fargo tells a different story, there is enough overlap to suggest that characters from old seasons could return in the future.

Each season of Fargo tells a completely new story with new characters, but there is just enough overlap to suggest that some characters could still make their return in the future. There have been a few instances of characters from one season crossing over into another, like how Mr. Wrench shows up alongside Nikki Swango on a prison bus in season 3, although he first appears in season 1. This suggests that showrunner Noah Hawley is not averse to bringing back old characters for minor roles, and there are plenty of intriguing criminals and cops to choose from. Season 6 of Fargo looks set to go ahead, after Hawley initially claimed that season 5 would bring the show to an end. At this early stage, there’s no telling what the season might include, but Fargo has jumped around in time from the 1950s all the way up to 2020, so the possibilities are endless. Even characters who died in Fargo could be brought back if the next season is set earlier in time. Each season of Fargo brings in an exciting new cast of actors, but some returning characters would also be welcome additions. All 5 seasons of Fargo can be streamed on Hulu.

10 Ole Munch Played by Sam Spruell in Season 5

Ole Munch, or “Oola Moonk” when spelled phonetically, is the most mysterious character in season 5 of Fargo. His initial attempt to kidnap Dot makes him seem like a typical goon-for-hire, much like his inept partner, but Ole is much stranger. He speaks in the third-person and frequently uses puzzling metaphors which sound like fables. His presence at the family dinner table in the season finale emphasizes how out of place he is, and there are still plenty of unanswered questions about Ole Munch’s backstory in Fargo which could be explored in more depth.

9 Hanzee Dent Played by Zahn McClarnon in Season 2

Hanzee became the catalyst for one of Fargo’s greatest episodes when he turned on the Gerhardt family and made a play to establish his own criminal empire. He’s completely unrecognizable in season 1, but he eventually changes his identity and becomes the crime kingpin Moses Tripoli. How Hanzee transformed from an adept tracker into a lazy and ineffectual mob boss doesn’t get much coverage in season 1 of Fargo, suggesting that there’s a lot of key information still missing from his story. A Fargo season set in the 1980s or 1990s would be a good opportunity to explore Hanzee’s evolution.

8 Oraetta Mayflower Played by Jessie Buckley in Season 4

Season 4 of Fargo was probably the show’s least popular, but there were a few standout characters. Jessie Buckley excelled as the angel of death-style killer, Oraetta Mayflower, a psychopathic nurse who gets involved with the Fadda crime family. Oraetta is executed for her role in Donatello Fadda’s death, so she could only be involved if a future season of Fargo were to be set before 1950. She could still be a haunting presence, especially since Ethelrida discovered her hidden stash of trophies and newspaper clippings, suggesting that she had dozens of victims.

7 Bill Oswalt Played by Bob Odenkirk in Season 1

Bill Oswalt is the incompetent police officer who quickly finds himself way out of his depth when he is promoted to Chief. He always sees the best in people, which is an endearing quality, but not a very useful one for someone in charge of solving a grisly murder. Bill recognizes that he isn’t cut out for the job, and he decides to retire. Bill Oswalt doesn’t really belong in Fargo, a show teeming with selfish villains and opportunists, but it’s this incongruous juxtaposition which makes him such a compelling audience surrogate.

6 Mike Milligan Played by Bokeem Woodbine in Season 2

Mike Milligan makes it out of season 2 with his life, but he finds himself trapped in an office job. Mike is a villain who seems to enjoy the violent nature of his work. His work gives him purpose in a brutal and uncaring society. He is still ambitious, but he finds that the reality of advancing within organized crime is much less exciting than being a soldier in an eternal war. Seeing Mike later in life could reveal if he sticks to his uninspiring new job, or if he rebels and seeks the comfort of chaos.

5 Pepper & Budge Played by Jordan Peele and Keegan Michael-Key in Season 1

Season 1 of Fargo separated itself from the movie in many ways, and Pepper and Budge were key to establishing a different tone. The incompetent FBI agents are stuck in a basement sorting files rather than seeing real action in the field. Their bickering is classic Key and Peele comedy, a much different approach to the movie’s dark humor. Fargo presents a wide variety of law enforcement officers, with Pepper and Budge somewhere towards the bottom of the pile, but their comic relief is a constant highlight which keeps things moving at a good pace.

4 V. M. Varga Played by David Thewlis in Season 3

Season 3 of Fargo was a great acting showcase for Ewan McGregor, who played both Stussy brothers, but David Thewlis’ slimy business executive Varga was a consistent scene-stealer. Varga’s fate is left a mystery at the end of season 3. He is captured, but he is confident that he will be released. Varga is one of the most powerful villains in all of Fargo, transcending the usual standard of gang warfare. He’s a ruthless capitalist with no attachments to anything other than money, and a toxic manifestation of human greed.

3 Indira Olmstead Played by Richa Moorjani in Season 5

One of the main characters in Fargo season 5, Indira Olmstead starts out working for the Scandia Police Department, but she later accepts a job offer from Lorraine Lyons. This decision seems as though it was heavily influenced by her money troubles, but she and Lorraine have wildly different ethical standards. The time jump in the Fargo season 5 finale shows that Indira is still working for Lorraine a year later. However, it’s not hard to imagine that the two will butt heads at some point. Any Fargo season set later than 2020 should check in on their relationship.

2 Lorne Malvo Played by Billy Bob Thornton in Season 1

Lorne Malvo is the catalyst who sets in motion the dark chain of events in season 1. After a seemingly innocuous conversation with Lester, Lorne murders his old bully as an act of retribution. In this way, Lorne represents the dark nature at the center of Lester’s soul. He carries out every vile impulse without restraint or consideration for the legal and moral ramifications of his actions. Sometimes, he commits sadistic crimes for his entertainment, or he tries to get others to accept their true identity as primal beasts trapped by society’s contrived ideals.

1 Marge Gunderson Played by Frances McDormand in Fargo (1996)

The Fargo TV series is tenuously connected to the movie, with a few Easter Eggs for Coen brothers fans and some thematic ties to the plot. However, none of the characters from the movie have made their way into the TV show. Marge is the moral soul of Fargo, and a compelling protagonist in a world filled with unscrupulous characters. She’s a huge reason why the Fargo movie is the gold standard which the TV series has to strive towards, and seeing her even in a minor role could reveal how the events affected her positive outlook.
