10 Funniest Scenes In Loudermilk, Ranked

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Loudermilk is a hilarious TV series with gut-bustingly funny scenes, thanks to Ron Livingston’s cynical performance and a colorful cast of characters. Memorable moments include outrageous courtroom drama, dark comedy encounters, and hilarious coffee shop squabbles that reveal the character’s persona. The show balances comedic chops with genuine sincerity and drama, making it one of the funniest TV shows of the streaming era.

Literature Review

Loudermilk has managed to keep the laughs coming over 3 seasons, with irreverent humor that isn’t afraid to tackle all kinds of subjects.

Loudermilk is quickly becoming one of the most irreverent comedy TV series of the streaming era, with plenty of gut-bustingly funny scenes that stand out. The series follows the struggles of Sam Loudermilk, a washed up former music critic and recovering alcoholic Seattleite who finds himself acting as a career counselor, despite his sardonic outlook on life. While it has moments of genuine sincerity and drama, Loudermilk’s comedic chops are what help it stand out as one of the funniest TV shows to come out of the 2010s. All 3 Seasons of Loudermilk are available to stream on Netflix and Amazon Prime.

There are several aspects to Loudermilk that make it so rife with comedy. The star of the show is Ron Livingston’s cynical performance as Loudermilk himself, channeling comedy greats that made a living off of complaining like Larry David to tremendous effect as he bitingly dishes out unwanted observations on the frustrating world around him. Of course, the extended cast of colorful regulars at Loudermilk’s support group also each lend their own unique sense of humor to the show, keeping the series’ focus shifting and preventing things from getting too stale or repetitive.

10 New Guy Exposes Allison’s Infidelity Season 2, Episode 5

When Loudermilk is sued by a man he allegedly saved from choking to death by giving the heimlich maneuver, he’s forced to reluctantly accept the legal representation of New Guy in court. New Guy is a decidedly unorthodox lawyer, and the proceedings go off the rails when he exposes Allison’s unfaithfulness to her boyfriend, Carl, who was testifying against Loudermilk. From Ricky Blitt’s astonishing delivery, crying that his client “gave it to her every which way”, to Loudermilk’s clarification that it was only “a couple of ways”, this out-of-pocket courtroom drama is a memorable moment.

9 Loudermilk Loses An Argument To The Suicide Guy Season 2, Episode 6

After saving his life, Loudermilk and Ben make good on their promise to go see a movie with the aptly-named “Suicide Guy”, who they had literally talked off a ledge earlier. Their discussion about the movie ends with the suicidal man feeling pity for Loudermilk’s dour outlook on reality and depressing life, leaving him with the barb “I’m surprised you beat me to the ledge”. Even if it’s not the most laugh-out-loud funny scene, there’s a certain dark comedy poetry to the suggestion that Loudermilk’s life is sadder than someone who nearly ended their own.

8 “Ladies First” Season 1, Episode 1

The cold open of Loudermilk’s very first episode, this scene set an important precedent for Loudermilk’s exhausting squabbles with strangers over various minutiae. After holding the door open at a coffee shop for a young woman with the line “Ladies’ first”, Loudermilk is instantly outraged when she expects to put in a lengthy work order ahead of his simple black coffee. She hilariously points out his hypocrisy in this pointed interaction, which immediately tells the audience everything they need to know about the character in just a few tight jokes, even if it’s mostly setup for what’s to come.

7 Loudermilk Tries To Order Hot Water Season 3, Episode 9

Season 3, even if not as dense with jokes, proved it can be just as funny as earlier seasons. When Loudermilk gets up to his usual exasperated antics, berating another hapless barista for refusing to sell him simple hot water to add his own tea bags. What really sets the scene over the top is a surprise assist from Lizzie, sarcastically thanking the barista for “looking the other way, Schindler”. The cherry on top is the barista’s fear that Loudermilk will assault him with his beverage, bracing for a literal burn to add to the verbal one.

6 “Aladdin’s Genie” Season 2, Episode 7

As Loudermilk continues, Ron Livingston has proven he can keep up with the best of Larry David’s bits in Curb Your Enthusiasm in terms of bitter observational humor. While berating a young man at a bus stop for his vaping habit, Loudermilk fires a stray at an innocent old man, using his appearance as a punchline to one of his quips about the nasty habit. When the unfazed nicotine addict disappears in a literal puff of smoke, Loudermilk compares him to a genie, only for the old man to retaliate – His wish didn’t come true because Loudermilk was still there.

5 The Valley Girl Accent Scene Season 2, Episode 1

One of Loudermilk’s unmistakably funniest scenes has also become its most famous, doing the rounds on Tiktok and introducing many new viewers to the show. Another coffee shop encounter, Loudermilk harasses a young barista for her performative valley girl accent, mockingly imitating her vocal fry back at her. Even though he’s once again justifiably identified as an “a**hole”, Loudermilk wins in the end, getting the girl to drop the facade and speak in her true voice.

4 Ben And Loudermilk Talk Down The Suicide Guy Season 2, Episode 6

Ben and Loudermilk’s second encounter with Suicide Guy was funny in its own way, but their initial encounter with him is even better. Both Ben and Loudermilk trip over their words in hysterical ways in a desperate attempt to talk the young man down from a literal cliff, assuring him he “has balls” enough to jump before realizing that they might be encouraging him to do so. Mirculously, Loudermilk somehow manages to convince Suicide Guy not to jump if he promises to see a movie with him – So long as it’s “none of that comic book bull****”.

3 The Chicken Story Season 1, Episode 7

Loudermilk’s oddball cast of support group members keeps the show’s comedy or genuine emotional beats from feeling too one-note, balancing out Loudermilk’s cynicism with a healthy dose of absurdity. One of the best moments of the group’s interaction came with one member recalling a story in which he had intimate relations with a farm animal. Beyond the shock value of such a bizarre statement, the humor keep piling up as the entire group becomes engrossed in the mechanics of the foul act. Crass, shocking, but unstakably funny, this scene is the series at its comedic best.

2 New Guy Tells His Story At A Coffee Shop Season 1, Episode 1

When Loudermilk loses access to the reliable church for hosting group sessions, he has to make an attempt at convening in public. Things go hilariously wrong when New Guy very loudly recalls engaging in some eyebrow-raising activities at the depths of his addiction, scarring a hapless bystander and her child. The resulting line of questioning from the other group members adds on to the joke until the laughs are relentless, much to the dismay of Loudermilk, quickly becoming accustomed to public embarrassment.

1 The Group Accuses Each Other Of Killing Hanky Season 2, Episode 3

Towards the beginning of season 2, Loudermilk’s group has to grapple with the death of Hanky Klocko, a man whose tragic passing is unmistakably a blessing to many of the group’s members, who had owed him money. It isn’t long before accusations of murder begin flying around the room. Each of the group’s eclectic members is given a chance to make a joke here, making the most of the show’s cast and they all land so fast and hard that New Guy’s admission to taking a bath in Loudermilk’s apartment after soiling himself.
