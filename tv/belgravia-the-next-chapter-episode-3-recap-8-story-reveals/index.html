Belgravia The Next Chapter Episode 3 Recap 8 Story Reveals

2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments

    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 created chaos for the Trenchard household, worsening Frederick and Clara’s relationship. More information about Dr. Ellerby’s life was revealed, potentially convincing Clara that he might actually become her ally and friend instead of someone who could jeopardize her and Frederick’s marriage. Secrets and conflicts between the characters are escalating, promising even more drama to come in Belgravia: The Next Chapter.

Literature Review

Belgravia: The Next Chapter ep 3 included some bonds mended and others wholly riddled with turmoil, ensuring the Trenchards’ future to be turbulent.

Warning! SPOILERS about Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 ahead. Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 included resounding successes as well as precarious developments, worsening the already shaky understanding Frederick and Clara had reached in episode 1. With some storylines like the Dunns employing Nell taking a step back and others like Frederick’s business deals and his family problems being more prominent, Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 managed to create much chaos for the Trenchard household, for the lord and lady, and the servants alike. With James and Frederick’s unplanned reunion happening in the episode’s ending, Belgravia: The Next Chapter will have a lot of damage to undo. Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 also introduced more information about Dr. Stephen Ellerby’s life when he isn’t treating those from the upper classes, highlighting both his general approach to patients and his dedication to treating the injured at protests. Clara’s newfound admiration for him changed their dynamic and revealed a common approach, one that could hopefully convince the Duke of Rochester not to go through with his plan for Peter, if the Duchess of Rochester weren’t to convince him alone. With the multiple situations brewing, Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 surely guaranteed drama to continue for the Trenchards.

8 Outbursts Won’t Be Lacking With All The Secrets At The Trenchard Residence Enright’s letters, Clara’s subterfuge, and Davison & Fletcher’s deal will cause drama

Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 ending with Frederick storming out of the house ensured that the rift between Clara and him wouldn’t be mended anytime soon, especially as he viewed James very much as the villain of his story. Not only was the meeting completely unplanned, even for Clara who eventually wanted the two brothers to reconcile, but everybody was also shocked by the ordeal, no one more so than Enright, who had warned Clara to stay away from James. Frederick and Clara’s disagreement undid all the good progress that made Frederick trust her help with his business deal. Secrets are potentially catastrophic also for the Trenchard servants, with Davison knowing a bit about the reason why Fletcher forgot valuables at the Duke of Rochester’s Kent residence. Enright relentlessly continuing with his secret letters that Mawd is tasked to deliver also encountered a complication with Mrs. Enright seeing the letter exchange, and the lie he told her will surely come back to trouble him, especially if he were to go on lying with her witnessing another exchange. All their secrets will likely come to light in the most displeasing way possible, just like it happened between Clara and Frederick.

7 Clara’s Problems With Frederick May Push Her To Pursue A Friendship With Ellerby She grew closer to him in Kent because she agreed with his perspective on nature

Among Belgravia: The Next Chapter’s characters, Ellerby was probably the least liked by Clara because of his insinuations about Clara and Frederick’s relationship. However, their progress in Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3 showed Ellerby in a new light to Clara, letting her find out about how much they had in common, like their devotion to nature. His invitation to an art exhibition might even deepen their potential friendship after Frederick’s outburst, as Clara will try to fix the situation and might want to rely on Ellerby’s advice, just like she did before deciding to write to James.

6 Fletcher’s Meeting In Kent Hints There May Be More To His Talk With Davison Davison felt sorry for him but his first reaction was to wonder if she would report him

When Davison apologized to Fletcher for not having intervened in the horrific event she witnessed, it looked like she believed Fletcher to be a victim of a violent crime. However, her reaction was visibly shocked when she saw Fletcher flirting with one of the Rochesters’ servants before hiding in a secret room in Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3. Moreover, when Fletcher confronted her in the Belgravia: The Next Chapter premiere, he asked her how long he had before she shared his secret with the others, hinting that Davison could have misconstrued what she saw when Fletcher was a teenager.

5 The Marquise’s Fondness For Clara Might Make Business Arduous For Frederick His aggressive reaction & anger at Clara won’t endear him to the Marquise

The Marquise d’Etagnac only stopped stalling and decided she wanted to pursue a business deal with Frederick once Clara suggested she should have invested in his project, proving that she held Clara in high regard and wanted the best for her. However, Belgravia: The Next Chapter episode 3’s ending might make things difficult even for Frederick’s business, as he couldn’t trust Clara anymore after her going against his back to contact James. Refusing to involve Clara and skirting the Marquise’s questions about her can endanger Frederick’s business endeavor, were the Marquise to pick up on their problems.

4 Rochester’s Comments In Kent Might Turn Frederick To Feel For Peter Rochester’s resentment towards Peter is eerily similar to Oliver’s towards Frederick

Securing the Marquise’s investment in Kent guaranteed Frederick to miss the rest of the party, and also meet a disgruntled Duke of Rochester because of their problem with Peter. No matter how different Peter and Frederick’s situations were, Frederick couldn’t seem to stop empathizing with him, especially once Percy admitted to resenting Peter for his epilepsy, and because it was his firstborn not to be healthy. This could not only pose problems for Frederick and Percy’s friendship, but it could also cause Frederick to remove himself from the situation when he and Clara could actually help the Duchess convince Percy.

3 Enright’s Surprise At The Incident Betrays His Usual Readiness He will see his ignorance as yet another failure to protect Frederick

Enright was already shocked when James wouldn’t budge, insisting that he should tell Clara that he was there. However, when Frederick discovered James in the drawing room and that Clara had been writing to him, Enright was completely overwhelmed by the matter, not being able to do anything except repeat that he didn’t know. Having witnessed Oliver’s abuse of Frederick, Enright was always protective of him, doing anything to make Frederick’s life easier and being even against Frederick’s marriage because of Clara’s inventiveness. He could view being unable to stop his suffering as a bigger lapse than dropping a spoon.

2 Frederick’s Anger At Clara’s Meddling Hints At Problems Between Them He was starting to trust her but he viewed her calling James a betrayal

Frederick’s outburst in front of the entire household mirrored plenty of similar events from The Gilded Age and Downton Abbey, and Belgravia: The Next Chapter will have to show Frederick and Clara doing a lot of work to meet again halfway before things can return to normal. After all, not only was he blindsided, but he had also opened himself up to Clara by telling her it was James’s fault that his father didn’t love him, and she had willingly gone behind his back. While his reaction could be seen as unwarranted because Clara was only trying to foster a reconciliation between them, she did betray his trust, putting them both at fault – him for his explosive fit against Clara, and her because she went against his back. Belgravia: The Next Chapter has shown countless times their obvious love for each other, but Frederick and Clara will have to double their work to be able to reach a middle ground where they can trust each other again, or their love for one another won’t survive this tribulation.

1 Frederick’s Explosive Reaction Against James Shows He Sees Him At Fault Frederick doesn’t have clarity of mind when thinking back on his childhood

Frederick was able to endure his memories of Oliver abusing him, and even Percy’s cruel words about his son Peter. However, his aggressive, immediate reaction against James proved he wasn’t able to see a different side apart from his own, as he saw James as the bad guy and could never believe Oliver to be at fault. While Frederick could be clearheaded in matters of business and of remembering terrible moments only to share them with Clara, he still couldn’t withstand facing James, making his acceptance of Oliver’s villainy his main Belgravia: The Next Chapter storyline.
