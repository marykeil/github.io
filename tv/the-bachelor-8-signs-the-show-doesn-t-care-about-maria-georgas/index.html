The Bachelor 8 Signs The Show Doesn t Care About Maria Georgas

SUMMARY

The Bachelor season 28 producers don’t seem to care about Maria Georgas. Maria faced bullying from her fellow contestants Sydney Gordon, Lea Cayanan, and Jess Edwards. The Bachelor season 28 producers allowed bullying to continue, pushing Maria to nearly self-eliminate, proving that they prioritize drama over the well-being of the contestants.

Literature Review

The Bachelor’s Maria Georgas has had a difficult time on the show so far. There are many signs that the producers care more about the drama than her.

The Bachelor season 28 contestant Maria Georgas has been put through the wringer on the show, and it’s clear that the producers don’t really care about her, but would rather focus on the drama instead. Maria, a 29-year-old executive assistant from Kleinburg, Ontario, Canada, captivated Joey on premiere night. She and Joey kissed that night, and she continued to enthrall him as the weeks went by. However, throughout The Bachelor season 28, Maria has been the target of bullying. The focus of her story has been her conflicts with Sydney Gordon, Lea Cayanan, and Jess Edwards, all of whom seem to have exaggerated or flat-out lied about their issues with her. During week 6, Maria finally received a one-on-one date with Joey in Montreal, Canada, but she had to endure so much bullying before that. Here’s why it seems as though The Bachelor producers don’t have Maria’s best interests at heart.

1 Maria’s Bullies Might Be Bachelor Producer Plants Sydney Gordon & Lea Cayanan Have Created So Much Drama

At times, Sydney and Lea’s reactions to Maria were so extreme that it appeared that they might be producer plants. It was very suspicious that they were the only two contestants who didn’t receive dates with Joey during week 1. This could indicate that the producers were manipulating the situation because they were never there for Joey in the first place. In addition, Lea met Joey when he was announced as the Bachelor lead during Charity Lawson’s “After the Final Rose” episode. She was given an envelope that she wasn’t allowed to open until premiere night. She ended up receiving a card that allowed her to steal a one-on-one date from any of the women before hometowns. However, Lea burned the card in the fire. This could all have been staged by the producers to create drama. Sydney’s issue with Maria began when Madina Alam expressed concern about being one of Joey’s oldest contestants at age 31. Maria spoke to another contestant about how Madina shouldn’t worry about her age because she was such a beautiful woman. However, Sydney overheard her and misconstrued it as Maria talking badly about Madina. She then called Maria a “mean girl” and a bully, which was false, and made it her mission to tell everyone, including Joey, the so-called truth about her. This continued even after Madina and Maria smoothed things over between them. After Sydney was eliminated, Lea picked up where she left off and began to attack Maria. However, it didn’t seem that Maria did anything wrong. If Sydney and Lea truly were planted by the Bachelor producers to create conflict, then this shows that they didn’t care about Maria, who was a genuine contestant. All they wanted was to intensify the drama, but it came at Maria’s expense.

2 Maria Has Been Pushed To Her Limit Maria Has Cried

It’s very difficult to watch Maria crying in front of the other women and in her confessionals. She’s been at a loss as to why some of the other women don’t like her. A few of the women, including Madina, Allison Hollinger, and Lexi Young had been good friends to her, but, a lot of the time, Maria felt completely alone in her journey. If the producers truly cared about Maria, they wouldn’t further embarrass her by showing her crying on-screen in her weakest moments. Her vulnerability is being exploited for the sake of a dramatic storyline.

3 Maria Was Forced To Go On A Two-On-One Date With Her Bully, Sydney Gordon Sydney Was Finally Eliminated

It’s been a Bachelor tradition for many seasons for rivals on the show to go on two-on-one dates with the lead. It’s always uncomfortable for everyone involved, and usually results in one of the women being eliminated. However, it was especially cruel to force Maria to spend a whole day with her bully, Sydney. Although the two-on-one date gave Maria the chance to confront Sydney, and even remind her that everything was being filmed, and her lies would eventually be exposed, it still created a situation that made Maria feel terrible. Maria did get some satisfaction when Joey finally eliminated Sydney on the date. However, she should never have been put in that situation in the first place by the Bachelor producers.

4 Maria Had To Wait For A One-On-One Date With Joey Graziadei Maria Didn’t Have A Date With Joey Alone Until Episode 6

Maria and Joey had a strong connection since the Bachelor premiere night, but she didn’t have a one-on-one date with him until episode 6 in Montreal, Canada. While some might argue that Joey didn’t choose her for one, the two-on-one date definitely was part of the delay. It’s hard to believe that Joey wouldn’t have given Maria a one-on-one date earlier in the season if he were in complete control of the situation. Although this is just speculation, it would make sense that the producers would rather keep Maria on group dates with her bullies in order to continue the drama. This is inexcusable.

5 Maria Has Implied That There’s More To The Story Maria Hinted That The Truth Would Come Out

In an Instagram Story shared after The Bachelor season 28 episode 5 aired, Maria asked fans to keep things positive, and not respond with hate to anyone, as they watched the show. She shared a picture of herself posing with her fellow contestants, including Sydney and Jess, who’d attacked her for stealing Joey during the rose ceremony cocktail party even though she already had a rose from a group date. This was hypocritical of Jess because she’d spoken to Joey for a second time on premiere night, stealing him away from Taylor Wiens. Maria wrote, “We are all watching together at the same time. Things I’m seeing are new to me as well and are hard to watch but please during this time, don’t send any hate to anyone.” Maria then implied that there was more to the story as she added, “Things will come to light as soon as they can I promise you that so just remember PEACE LOVE AND POSITIVITY.” Maria will probably not be able to speak out against the Bachelor producers any time soon, but she might be able to reveal more information about what actually happened during the show during “The Women Tell All” episode.

6 Maria Has Been Exploited To Create Drama Maria’s Feelings Have Been Hurt

If it’s true that the Bachelor producers have been creating a dramatic situation with plants in the cast, then they’ve also been exploiting Maria. It’s completely unfair that Maria’s journey has been so challenging so far. If the producers had any hand in that, it’s really a shame. No one should have to endure the relentless bullying that Maria has. In addition, the drama has taken away from her time with Joey. When Maria finally had the chance to open up to Joey when she had one-on-one time with him during a group date, she told him about her relationship with her mother, which has been difficult at times. She explained that they’d been in a serious car accident when she was one year old, and it was a miracle that she was still alive. Her mother broke almost every bone in her body, which led to her having depression. She said that this caused her mom not to be the mother that she really wanted to be. Maria’s dad was a rock for them both, and she and her mother are still working on their relationship. Although it was nice to hear some of Maria’s backstory, after everything she’s been through on the show, it felt as though it were just another moment in which the producers were exploiting her emotions.

7 The Bachelor Producers Don’t Stop The Drama The Bullies Are Allowed To Bully

The Bachelor producers will step in if anyone gets physical with others, but they don’t do anything to stop emotional abuse on the show. For years, villains have terrorized their fellow contestants, with no consequences on the show except for if the lead decides to eliminate them. Perhaps there should be more stringent rules about bullying other contestants on The Bachelor. There’s a difference between having an argument with someone, and having to endure constant bullying like Maria has. In addition, allowing the lead to review some footage of the women could help as well, so that the truth could come out sooner, and avoid a lot of heartache. The fact that the producers would rather allow the drama to escalate shows that they don’t care about Maria or anyone else who’s been mistreated on the show. The Bachelor is supposed to be about people falling in love, not about women tearing each other down.

8 Maria Almost Self-Eliminated From The Bachelor Maria Eventually Decided To Stay For Joey

Maria was so upset at the end of The Bachelor season 28 episode 4, that she was ready to self-eliminate. This was because Lea was angry that Madina was nice to Maria after Sydney left. Lea expected that, if Madina was Sydney’s friend, then she wouldn’t be a friend to Maria. When Madina told Maria this, Maria was very hurt. Maria eventually told the producers that they could give her two-on-one date rose to another woman because she wanted to leave. She said, “they win,” in reference to the other women. However, Maria eventually decided to stay. The fact that Maria liked Joey so much and had such a deep connection with him, but was ready to leave to put an end to her bullying shows that she’d been pushed to her limit. It would’ve been very unfortunate if the producers let the bullies push Maria out of the show for their own selfish reasons. The Bachelor producers have proved over and over again that they don’t have Maria’s best interests at heart. Her emotional well-being is being exploited to create drama, as her bullies are allowed to continue to hurt her. If the producers really cared about Maria and the other women, they’d put a stop to the drama, or they’d at least choose not to focus so much of it on the show. It’s clear that they really don’t care about Maria, but she’s risen above the bullying and become the shining star of the show. They should realize what a beautiful and strong woman they have in Maria. The Bachelor producers must do better because Maria deserves better.
