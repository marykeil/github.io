10 Marvel Villains Not Adapted To Live-Action That d Be Perfect For Spider-Man 4

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Spider-Man 4 may introduce a new Marvel villain that has never been seen in live-action before. Potential new villains include Scorpion, Silver Sable, Black Cat, Tombstone, Spot, Mister Negative, Jackal and Morlun. It’s also possible that Marvel Studios may make Ned Leeds a new Spider-Man villain, or introduce a new heavily-rumored adversary in Spider-Man 4 .

Literature Review

Spider-Man 4 has been rumored to be featuring a villain that has never been adapted into live-action, and there are several powerful candidates.

Marvel Studios may be adapting a new villain into live-action for the upcoming Spider-Man 4 in the MCU, and there are several threatening and powerful candidates for the next foe to face Tom Holland’s Peter Parker. After completing his first MCU trilogy, comprising Spider-Man: Homecoming, Spider-Man: Far From Home and Spider-Man: No Way Home, Tom Holland is set to return as Peter Parker’s Spider-Man in Marvel Studios’ Spider-Man 4. News on the project has been scarce, but recent speculation suggests that Peter Parker may be battling a brand-new adversary in Spider-Man 4, one that hasn’t yet been seen in live-action. Since his debut in 2016’s Captain America: Civil War, Tom Holland’s Spider-Man has fought a variety of iconic Marvel Comics villains, including the Vulture, the Shocker, Thanos, Mysterio, and several foes from Sony’s previous Spider-Man projects. Recent rumors suggest that Spider-Man 4 will introduce a Marvel Comics villain that has never been adapted into live-action before, which creates several opportunities for Marvel Studios. With the deal between Marvel Studios and Sony still standing strong, any number of Spider-Man-related villains from the comics could make an appearance in Spider-Man 4, potentially helping to create Tom Holland’s most intense MCU adventure yet.

6 Perfect Spider-Man 4 Villains The MCU Has Already Set Up Tom Holland’s Spider-Man 4 is in the works, and following his first trilogy, the MCU has already set up great villain choices for his next film.

10 Mac Gargan’s Scorpion Needs A Payoff In The MCU Michael Mando Played Mac Gargan In Spider-Man: Homecoming

Although Michael Mando debuted as Mac Gargan in 2017’s Spider-Man: Homecoming, his transformation into the Scorpion never occured on-screen, but Spider-Man 4 has the potential to reprise the villain. While Mac Gargan made his first Marvel Comics appearance in 1964 as a private investigator hired by J. Jonah Jameson to investigate Peter Parker, 1965’s Amazing Spider-Man #20 saw him become the Scorpion after an experimental procedure left him with an irremovable Scorpion-like armor. Spider-Man 4 paying off Homecoming’s tease would be a great full-circle moment, as Mando’s Mac Gargan was a minor antagonist for Tom Holland’s first solo MCU project.

Marvel Confirmed MCU Spider-Man’s Scorpion Setup Never Mattered Mac Gargan is teased to become Scorpion in Spider-Man: Homecoming, but the end-credits scene of Morbius completely upheaves that plan.

9 Silvija Sablinova’s Silver Sable Would Be A Great New MCU Antihero Silver Sable Also Has A Connection To Another Rumored MCU Villain

Silver Sable debuted in Marvel Comics in 1985’s The Amazing Spider-Man #265, and has since been depicted as an ally and sometimes-opponent to Peter Parker’s Spider-Man. Introduced as a mercenary, Silver Sable would be the perfect antihero to appear in Spider-Man 4, particularly because of her connections to Doctor Doom, a heavily-rumored MCU villain for the Fantastic Four in Phase 6. Sablinova’s state of Symkaria borders Doctor Doom’s home, Latveria, so not only could Spider-Man 4 debut Silver Sable, and potentially her team, the Wild Pack, but the project could also tease the introduction of Marvel’s iconic Fantastic Four villain.

8 Felicia Hardy’s Black Cat Can Be A New Love Interest For Spider-Man Zendaya’s MJ Hasn’t Been Confirmed For Spider-Man 4

In the wake of Doctor Strange’s memory wipe spell in 2021’s Spider-Man: No Way Home, Spider-Man 4 presents the opportunity to introduce a new love-interest for Tom Holland’s Peter Parker. Felicia Hardy’s Black Cat, who first appeared in 1979’s The Amazing Spider-Man #194 as an adversary to Spider-Man, eventually formed an on-again, off-again romance with the wall-crawler, so could fill the void left by Zendaya’s MJ in Spider-Man 4. Able to bring bad luck to her enemies, Black Cat would be a strong addition to the MCU, particularly after Sony canceled its planned Black Cat and Silver Sable live-action project. By March 2017, Sony Pictures had been developing a female-led Spider-Man spinoff, Silver & Black, with Gina Prince-Bythewood set to direct. Pre-production work had begun, but Prince-Bythewood’s unhappiness with the script caused delays, and she instead set to work on The Old Guard, with Sony canceling Silver & Black a month later. The last update hinted at Silver & Black being adapted into a TV series, though it’s unclear whether this is still happening.

7 Alonzo Lincoln’s Tombstone Connects Spider-Man To Another MCU Hero Tombstone Is An Enemy Of Spider-Man & Daredevil In Marvel Comics

Popular theories have suggested that Peter Parker could team up with Charlie Cox’s Matt Murdock, a.k.a. Daredevil, in Spider-Man 4, creating connections between the MCU’s street-level heroes, which creates the chance for Tombstone to be introduced to live-action. After debuting in 1988’s Web of Spider-Man #36, Alonzo “Lonnie” Lincoln went on to become a persistent adversary of both Spider-Man and Daredevil, so could be the perfect connective tissue between the two MCU heroes. Marvin “Krondon” Jones III voiced Tombstone in Sony’s Spider-Verse franchise, but the villain hasn’t branched into live-action yet, though Spider-Man 4 could finally change this.

6 Johnathan Ohnn’s Spot Could Cross Over From Sony’s Spider-Verse The Spot Battled Miles Morales In Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse

Similarly to Tombstone, Johnathan Ohnn’s Spot has also appeared in Sony’s Spider-Verse franchise, presenting a major threat to Miles Morales in 2023’s Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse. However, Spider-Man 4 presents the opportunity for the multiversal supervillain to cross-over into the live-action MCU. Since Ohnn worked for Kingpin, who has been billed as the Thanos of the street-level MCU, the foundations have already been laid for his live-action debut. In the Spider-Verse films, the Spot poses a huge threat to the entire multiverse, which includes Marvel Studios’ Earth-616, so it would make sense for him to threaten Tom Holland’s Spider-Man, too. Jason Schwartzman is set to return to voice Johnathan Ohnn’s Spot in Sony’s animated sequel Spider-Man: Beyond the Spider-Verse, though it’s unclear when this project will release, as it was recently removed from Sony’s upcoming release schedule.

5 Mister Negative Fits Perfectly In The MCU’s Upcoming Street-Level Storylines Mister Negative Has A Strong Connection To Wilson Fisk’s Kingpin

Much like the Spot, Mister Negative has major connections to Wilson Fisk’s Kingpin in Marvel Comics, particularly during Kingpin’s time as Mayor of New York City, as Kingpin gifts Mister Negative control over Chinatown and the Lower East Side. This evolution for Kingpin has been teased for the MCU’s upcoming street-level stories, including Spider-Man 4, so it would make sense for Mister Negative to make his live-action debut soon. With his command over Darkforce and Lightforce, Mister Negative is a powerful adversary to Spider-Man, the Punisher and Shang-Chi in Marvel Comics, so could bring these heroes together in the MCU.

4 Miles Warren’s Jackal Could Introduce A New Spider-Man To The MCU Jackal Creates A Spider-Man Clone In Marvel Comics

Originally Miles Warren, a professor at Empire State University, this obsessive supervillain became the Jackal in 1975’s The Amazing Spider-Man #129. Although it’s his obsession with Gwen Stacy that causes him to transform himself into the Jackal, Miles Warren became more well-known for perfecting cloning technology, which allowed him to develop clones of Spider-Man, including Ben Reilly and Kaine Parker. The Jackal’s live-action debut in Spider-Man 4 could mark the introduction of a new Spider-Man in the MCU, potentially adapting 1994’s controversial Clone Saga storyline for the MCU’s Spider-Man, and setting up a strong replacement for Tom Holland’s Peter Parker.

3 Morlun Poses A Threat To The Entire Multiverse Morlun Would Fit In The MCU’s Multiverse Saga Perfectly

One of Spider-Man’s most dangerous adversaries is Morlun, a multiversal supervillain who poses a threat to every version of Spider-Man across every reality. Introduced in 2001’s The Amazing Spider-Man (Vol. 2) #30, Morlun hails from Earth-001, and travels the multiverse to hunt down and kill Spider-Totems. Morlun is the perfect villain to introduce in the MCU’s Multiverse Saga, particularly with Sony’s Spider-Verse franchise exploring a similarly-scaled threat. Morlun even temporarily kills Peter Parker in the Spider-Man: The Other storyline, but the hero transforms into a human-spider mash-up to defeat the villain, which could be depicted in the live-action Spider-Man 4.

2 Ned Leeds Has Been Teased As The MCU’s Hobgoblin Jacob Batalon Has Played The MCU’s Ned Leeds Since Spider-Man: Homecoming

Jacob Batalon was introduced as Ned Leeds, Peter Parker’s best friend, in Spider-Man: Homecoming, but has been teased to become a major supervillain. Spider-Man: No Way Home saw Ned acquire magical abilities, but also hinted at his transformation into a villain, potentially an adaptation of Marvel Comics’ Hobgoblin. Inspired by the Green Goblin, Ned Leeds could become the Hobgoblin in Spider-Man 4, especially since he doesn’t have the positive influence of Peter Parker anymore. This would be great to showcase a new dynamic between the former allies, and bring this iconic Marvel Comics villain into live-action for the first time.

MCU Theory: Spider-Man 4 Does Introduce Hobgoblin (But It’s Not Ned Leeds) Spider-Man 4 is rumored to see Ned Leeds become the Hobgoblin, but it can introduce the real Hobgoblin thanks to the events of No Way Home.

1 Mephisto Could Create Many New Spider-Man Villains Mephisto Has Been Rumored To Be Joining The MCU Soon

The demonic Mephisto has been rumored to appear in several MCU projects, including WandaVision, Agatha: Darkhold Diaries and Ironheart, but Spider-Man 4 could be the best place for his live-action debut. Although he has battled a huge number of Marvel Comics heroes, Mephisto has persisted as one of Spider-Man’s most dangerous adversaries. The demon was responsible for the transformations of Norman Osborn and Harry Osborn into the Green Goblin and Kindred, and even caused the loss of Peter Parker’s marriage to MJ, so would make for the perfect live-action villain for Tom Holland’s next web-slinging MCU adventure in Spider-Man 4​​​​​​​.
