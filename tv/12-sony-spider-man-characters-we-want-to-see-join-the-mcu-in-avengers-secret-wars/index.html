12 Sony Spider-Man Characters We Want To See Join The MCU In Avengers Secret Wars

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Characters from Sony’s Spider-Man projects could potentially appear in Avengers: Secret Wars , adding to the diverse cast of superheroes and villains from the Marvel multiverse. Tom Hardy’s Venom and Jared Leto’s Morbius are likely candidates to make appearances in the crossover event, building on their previous connections to the MCU. There is potential for other characters like Kraven the Hunter, Madame Web, Spider-Man 2099, and Miles Morales to join the MCU in Avengers: Secret Wars , while Tobey Maguire and Andrew Garfield will also likely return as their Spider-Men.

Literature Review

Several characters from Sony’s Spider-Man projects have the potential to join the MCU proper in Marvel Studios’ upcoming Avengers: Secret Wars.

Many characters introduced in Sony’s Spider-Man projects could cross over into the MCU proper in Marvel Studios’ Multiverse Saga-ending Avengers: Secret Wars. Marvel Studios chief Kevin Feige announced the development of Avengers: Secret Wars for the MCU’s Phase 6 in July 2022, with the project presumably being adapted from Marvel Comics’ Secret Wars storylines from 1984 and 2015. Both of these stories involved most of the heroes and villains from across Marvel Comics’ vast multiverse, meaning that 2027’s Avengers: Secret Wars will likely be full-to-bursting with superpowered characters not only from the MCU, but also from other live-action Marvel franchises. MCU characters will take center stage in Avengers: Secret Wars, but the project has also been rumored to include characters from Fox’s X-Men and Fantastic Four projects, previous Daredevil, Blade and Ghost Rider movies, and also those from Sony’s various Spider-Man franchises. Sony and Marvel Studios have had a strong relationship since Tom Holland debuted as Peter Parker’s Spider-Man in the MCU’s Captain America: Civil War, so characters from Sony’s projects are very likely to appear in Secret Wars. There are many Marvel characters who could appear in Avengers: Secret Wars, but some would have a greater impact than others.

12 Eddie Brock’s Venom Could Interact With The MCU’s Spider-Man Venom (2018), Venom: Let There Be Carnage (2021), Venom 3 (2024)

Tom Hardy kicked off Sony’s Spider-Man Universe in 2018’s Venom as investigative journalist Eddie Brock, who became host to the alien symbiote Venom. Hardy is very likely to make a reappearance as Venom in Avengers: Secret Wars, particularly since he’s already shown up in the MCU, being dragged into the post-credits scene of Spider-Man: No Way Home by Doctor Strange’s spell. There is speculation that Spider-Man 4 or Secret Wars could see Tom Holland’s Spider-Man bond with the Venom symbiote, so it would be great to see him perform opposite Tom Hardy’s Eddie Brock in the Phase 6 crossover event. Tom Hardy is set to return as Eddie Brock’s Venom in 2024’s Venom 3, his trilogy-capper in Sony’s Spider-Man Universe that is scheduled for release on November 8, 2024.

11 Kraven The Hunter Would Bring Aaron Taylor-Johnson Back To The MCU Kraven The Hunter (2024)

After appearing as Pietro Maximoff, a.k.a. Quicksilver, in Avengers: Age of Ultron, Aaron Taylor-Johnson is making the move to Sony’s Spider-Man Universe to portray Sergei Kravinoff, the titular villain-come-antihero in 2024’s Kraven the Hunter. Kraven the Hunter’s first trailer proves this may be the perfect role for Taylor-Johnson, but since Kraven and Spider-Man are prominent enemies in Marvel Comics, it would be great to see him return to the MCU to face Holland’s Spider-Man in Secret Wars. Kraven is one of the SSU’s most highly-anticipated additions, so it would be great to see him shared by Sony and Marvel Studios.

10 Jared Leto’s Morbius Could Get Redemption In Secret Wars Morbius (2022)

2022’s Morbius was a major disappointment for Sony, as Jared Leto’s debut as the vampiric Michael Morbius became a critical and commercial failure. Despite Morbius receiving criticism, the opportunity is there for the character to seek redemption in the MCU proper, as Avengers: Secret Wars could breathe new life into Morbius and bring him into contact with some of his Marvel Comics allies. Morbius is a very popular character in Marvel Comics, so deserves another shot at a live-action career, though it may also be the case that Marvel Studios would want to distance Secret Wars from Morbius’ previous calamity.​​​​​​​

9 Secret Wars Can Bring Adrian Toomes’ Vulture Back To The MCU Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017), Morbius (2022)

One point of contention in Morbius was that Michael Keaton’s Adrian Toomes, a.k.a. the Vulture, was somehow pulled into Sony’s Spider-Man Universe in the film’s post-credits scene. This was seemingly due to Doctor Strange’s spell in Spider-Man: No Way Home, but made no sense in either projects’ narrative. Adrian Toomes may have started his career in the MCU, but he is now in Sony’s live-action franchise, so Avengers: Secret Wars may provide the perfect opportunity to return him home. Not only could this solve Morbius’ apparent Sinister Six set-up, but could also tease future Vulture storylines in the MCU proper. Michael Keaton’s Adrian Toomes was the first villain for Tom Holland’s Peter Parker in his solo debut, 2017’s Spider-Man: Homecoming. Toomes became the Vulture after losing work to Tony Stark and the Department of Damage Control, but was taken down by Spider-Man while trying to steal Avengers technology.

8 Madame Web Can Become A Powerful New Hero In The MCU Madame Web (2024)

Dakota Johnson’s Cassandra Webb is set to be the next spider-based hero introduced into Sony’s Spider-Man Universe. 2024’s Madame Web will see her gain psychic abilities that allow her to see the future within the “spider world”, and will bring her into contact with a range of new Spider-Women. In Marvel Comics, Madame Web is a precognitive mutant, so she could be a very exciting addition to Avengers: Secret Wars’ line-up. The SSU’s depiction of Madame Web will evidently be vastly different from her Marvel Comics roots, but her clairvoyant abilities would be a huge asset in Secret Wars nonetheless.

7 Secret Wars Can Explore The Spider-Verse With Madame Web’s Spider-Women Madame Web (2024)

As well as focusing on Dakota Johnson’s Cassandra Webb, Sony’s Madame Web will also introduce Sydney Sweeney’s Julia Cornwall, Celeste O’Connor’s Mattie Franklin and Isabela Merced’s Anya Corazon as three new live-action Spider-Women. If Johnson returns as Madame Web herself in Avengers: Secret Wars, these three new spider-based heroes shouldn’t be too far behind. This may allow Secret Wars to explore something of a Spider-Verse storyline, amid the greater adventure featuring other MCU heroes. This would potentially one-up the grandeur of seeing three Spider-Men in No Way Home​​​​​​​, even if only for a brief moment in the larger MCU narrative.

6 Miguel O’Hara’s Spider-Man 2099 Could Create A Double Role For Oscar Isaac Spider-Man: Into The Spider-Verse (2018), Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse (2023)

While it’s perhaps more likely for live-action characters from Sony’s Spider-Man Universe to crossover into the MCU for Avengers: Secret Wars, it’s also possible that any number of heroes and villains could make their way from Sony’s animated Spider-Verse franchise. This could include Miguel O’Hara’s Spider-Man 2099, a hardened version of Spider-Man from a future reality who acts as an antagonist to Miles Morales in Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse. Oscar Isaac voices Spider-Man 2099, so he could get a double role in Avengers: Secret Wars if his villainous Spider-Man and Moon Knight are both featured in the Phase 6 event. Oscar Isaac has played a number of characters in various Marvel franchises, including En Sabah Nur, a.k.a. Apocalypse, in Fox’s X-Men: Apocalypse, Miguel O’Hara, a.k.a. Spider-Man 2099, in Sony’s Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse and Across the Spider-Verse, and Marc Spector/Steven Grant/Jake Lockley, a.k.a. Moon Knight, in Marvel Studios’ Moon Knight series on Disney+.

5 The Spot Could Pose A Major Problem To The MCU’s Multiverse Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse (2023)

While several Spider-Man variants could certainly make their way into the MCU in Avengers: Secret Wars, the opportunity is also there for a great number of villains to cross universes. This could include the multiversal villain the Spot, the twisted alter ego of Johnathan Ohnn who targeted Miles Morales in Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse. The Spot has already been shown to appear in a live-action reality, as he previously opened a portal to Sony’s Spider-Man Universe, and since he has the potential to travel the entire multiverse, it would make sense for him to threaten the MCU.

4 Sony Never Used Black Cat & Silver Sable, But Secret Wars Could Introduce Them Sony’s Canceled Black Cat & Silver Sable Project

Indeed, Black Cat and Silver Sable have never actually appeared in any project developed by Sony, but a project featuring the pair of antiheroes had been planned for several years, initially as a spinoff from The Amazing Spider-Man franchise, and later as part of the SSU. Gina Prince-Bythewood was set to direct before moving on to The Old Guard, leaving Silver & Black in development limbo. Spider-Man 4 or Avengers: Secret Wars may be the perfect opportunity to finally bring these Marvel Comics characters into live-action,​​​​​​ potentially setting them up to have permanent futures in the MCU opposite Holland’s Spider-Man.

Sony’s Marvel Universe: 10 Projects Reported To Be In Development Now that Sony has Spider-Man back from the MCU, they have several future projects centered around the Marvel franchise in various development stages.

3 Gwen Stacy’s Spider-Woman Deserves A Live-Action Adaptation Spider-Man: Into The Spider-Verse (2018), Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse (2023)

Gwen Stacy has appeared several times in previous live-action projects, portrayed by Bryce Dallas Howard in 2007’s Spider-Man 3 and Emma Stone in The Amazing Spider-Man franchise. However, the character has never suited up as her version of Spider-Woman in live-action, but Sony’s Spider-Verse franchise has proven how brilliant this could be. Similarly to Oscar Isaac, Hailee Steinfeld would get a double role in Avengers: Secret Wars if she appears as Gwen Stacy’s Spider-Woman, as she has voiced the hero in Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse and Across the Spider-Verse​​​​​​​, but could give Gwen Stacy her MCU debut in Phase 6.

2 Live-Action Miles Morales’ Spider-Man Has Been Teased In The MCU Spider-Man: Into The Spider-Verse (2018), Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse (2023)

Since his existence in the MCU was teased back in 2017’s Spider-Man: Homecoming, there has been speculation that Miles Morales will one day make his MCU debut, introducing a new Spider-Man to Marvel Studios’ franchise. In Marvel Comics, Miles Morales’ Spider-Man joins the main continuity during 2015’s Secret Wars event, so Marvel Studios’ Avengers: Secret Wars may be the perfect place for Sony’s Miles Morales to make his way to the MCU. While Shameik Moore may be too old to portray the live-action Spider-Man, there are many rumored actors who could be the MCU’s Miles Morales.

Live-Action Miles Morales Is Great News For The MCU’s Secret Wars Sony are developing a live-action film focused on Miles Morales’ Spider-Man, which may be perfect for Marvel Studios’ upcoming Avengers: Secret Wars.

1 Tobey Maguire & Andrew Garfield’s Spider-Men Can Return To The MCU In Secret Wars Sam Raimi’s Spider-Man Franchise (2002-2007), Marc Webb’s The Amazing Spider-Man Franchise (2012-2014)

While any number of Spider-Man-related characters could cross over from Sony’s franchises for Avengers: Secret Wars, the most likely inclusions are Tobey Maguire’s Spider-Man and Andrew Garfield’s Spider-Man. Both of these actors returned in No Way Home, so it would make sense for them to once again return to team up with Tom Holland’s Peter Parker once again. This could introduce Maguire and Garfield’s Spider-Men to a whole host of other Spider-People and their villains, though with Avengers: Secret Wars promising to be a jam-packed spectacle anyway, there simply may not be space for all of these characters to appear.
