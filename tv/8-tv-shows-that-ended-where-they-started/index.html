8 TV Shows That Ended Where They Started

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Some TV shows choose to wrap up the series by returning to where it all began, providing a satisfying circular narrative. Shows like Breaking Bad, Succession, and Modern Family use this “returning” to tie up the narrative in a conclusive and meaningful way. Whether it’s Walt securing his family’s future in Breaking Bad or the characters gathering at Central Perk in Friends, going back to the start can be a great way to close a series.

Literature Review

Most TV shows follow an arc that takes their characters from point A to point B, but some shows opt for a more circular narrative instead.

Some TV shows choose to wrap up the series in a way that is very reminiscent of the first episode, with characters ending up where it all began. For a TV show to reach its maximum potential, the characters must experience growth and development. As a result, this mostly means a show will see characters moving beyond humble beginnings, but plenty of shows opt to go back for the ending. Unlike traditional TV montage episodes that celebrate a series as it goes on, having the entire show end where it began can provide a satisfying circular narrative. Whether the characters establish their biggest goals in episode 1, only to have them realized in the finale, or the physical location or group of friends return, revisiting the first outing can be a great way to close a series. Some shows manage to use this “returning,” to neatly tie up the narrative in a conclusive and meaningful way.

8 Breaking Bad Walt Is Dying

From the very beginning of Breaking Bad, Walt’s life is spiraling out of his control. With a cancer diagnosis and him becoming distant from his family, it’s hard to watch as his life hits rock bottom. Breaking Bad also features one of the best endings of any TV show, which appears to echo many of the same elements from the show’s earliest outings. Walt spent the series’ duration building an empire, accumulating wealth, and trying to ensure his family would be provided for. The journey is wild, and it takes him away from his family, but ultimately, when Walt lies dying in Breaking Bad, he is secure in the knowledge that his family will be taken care of. It’s the perfect resolution to where the show began. His death is more explosive than the diagnosis could have predicted, but the finality is just the same.

10 Best Breaking Bad Episodes, Ranked Breaking Bad is easily one of the best shows of all time with every element coming together, but there are still some episodes that outshone the rest.

7 Succession The Roy’s Fight For Power

Succession focuses on the complex dynamics between the Roy family, as their father’s health and the company’s future appear to hang in balance. What follows in the show’s four seasons is a power struggle between the Roy kids and their father, who continues to be an imposing and oppressive force in their lives despite his health issues. The finale may have seen Logan Roy finally in his grave, but it didn’t stop his influence from turning the bickering siblings against one another until they all fell from grace, and Tom, the outsider, ended up taking the CEO’s chair.

6 Loki Burdened With Glorious Purpose

The Loki series makes several references to the show’s cyclical nature, from the first and last episode sharing the same title to the addition of Ouroboros as a character, whose name literally refers to an eternal cycle of destruction and rebirth. As the series opens, Loki appears to revel in his destiny, a god who is “burdened with glorious purpose.” While Loki initially takes pleasure from this line and sees himself as significant, the series twists the same line of dialogue, shifting the focus to the “burden” when Loki ultimately realizes his destiny and claims his glorious purpose for eternity.

5 Modern Family Life Goes On As Always

Both the kids and the parents in Modern Family have grown up a lot throughout the series, but the ending also echoes back to where it all began. Despite 11 seasons, the final episode shows how much and how little some things change over time. The start of Modern Family shows the families introducing themselves, and Jay and Gloria watching Manny playing sports. In the final episode, this motif is repeated with them watching their younger son playing sports, and them cheering from the sideline once again.

4 How I Met Your Mother Chasing The Dream Girl

How I Met Your Mother follows the story of Ted Mosby as he recounts the events that led up to meeting his wife. Ted dates several girls and narrowly misses out on meeting his future wife dozens of times. However, the show starts with him pursuing his dream girl, Robin. The series shifts the relationship so that they are just friends, but when Ted’s wife is unexpectedly revealed to be dead in the finale, Ted’s kids encourage him to go back to Robin, ending the show where it all began, with the Blue French Horn in Ted’s hand.

10 TV Shows That Undid Seasons Of Character Growth In Their Final Episodes It’s not often that a series finale is so awful that it destroys the entire show, but How I Met Your Mother’s ending managed to do just that.

3 Friends At The Usual Hang Out

A favorite spot for a group of young friends living in New York City, Central Perk is a quirky café, perfect for winding down and connecting at any time. The final episode sees everyone about to set off on different paths, but before they go, Rachel suggests that they grab a coffee together, which is the perfect way to wrap up the show. Considering the show starts in the coffee shop, it also gives the show a circular feeling that makes it perfect for rewatching again and again.

2 The Office A Documentary About A Typical American Office

The Office is a series that has a unique narrative framing in that the whole show is set up as a documentary about an average American workplace. The show opens with the employees being interviewed in the office and talking about their experiences being there. The rest of the series follows their interactions and their misadventures over the years of their lives. Despite changes in the company, the office being at risk of shutting down, and new employees coming and going, the final episode concludes with the central characters being interviewed just as it began.

1 Manifest The Journey Continues

Manifest is a supernatural drama about a commercial airplane with 191 passengers that mysteriously disappeared for five and a half years. When the passengers and crew return, the world has moved on, including their friends, families, and loved ones. The series ran for four seasons as the passengers attempted to reintegrate and follow their newfound purposes in life. However, when the series came to an end in 2023, the passengers who were deemed to have been good were transported back on the plane having never disappeared in the first place.
