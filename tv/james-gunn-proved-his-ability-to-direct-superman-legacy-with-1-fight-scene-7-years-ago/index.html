James Gunn Proved His Ability To Direct Superman Legacy With 1 Fight Scene 7 Years Ago

SUMMARY

James Gunn’s focus on upcoming DCU releases shows that his MCU days are behind him, making him the perfect director for Superman: Legacy. Gunn’s depiction of a fight involving Superman in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 proves he can handle a flying brawl with grace and power. Gunn’s success with lesser-known heroes in Guardians of the Galaxy suggests he can turn the characters in Superman: Legacy into megastars.

Literature Review

One choice scene from Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 shows exactly how James Gunn could handle a brawl between two flying superpowered beings.

With more than a year to go before the DCU commences with Superman: Legacy, prospective fans are providing evidence as to why James Gunn is the perfect director for the movie. Gunn’s focus on the upcoming DCU releases means that his MCU days are behind him, with Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 being the last Guardians film that he was involved with. It was during his time as the visionary behind this franchise that fans were won over by Gunn’s superhero movie talents, something which he would later amplify with the release of the critically acclaimed Suicide Squad.

It was in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2, however, that Gunn showed how well he could depict a fight involving Superman - something that @The_Hammer_Fan pointed out via Twitter/X. The post features a GIF that depicts part of the climactic fight between Peter Quill and his father, Ego. The scene shows Quill before he relinquishes his Celestial powers, and, as such, is capable of going toe-to-toe with one of the MCU’s most powerful beings. The high-octane action and choreography in the scene are one of Guardians of the Galaxy’s most memorable spectacles - and it’s easy to picture Superman in a similar fight.

How Star-Lord’s Fight With Ego Proves James Gunn Is Perfect For Superman: Legacy

Superman: Legacy is the movie that will kick off the new cinematic franchise, meaning James Gunn is personally setting the trajectory for his vision for the DCU. As the co-CEO of DC Studios alongside Peter Safran, the pressure is on Gunn to do the beloved character justice for his first outing in the rebooted franchise. Superman’s mid-flight tussles with similarly powered enemies are among the most important scenes to get right, and with this scene, it looks like Gunn can handle a flying brawl with no problems.

Man of Steel was a divisive depiction of Superman in combat. While his brawl with Zod practically leveled Metropolis, it was suitable in conveying just how godlike Superman and his adversary were in their sheer power. The speed and power in the Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 fight, meanwhile, depicts the power of two godlike beings with an enthralling grace. While it remains to be seen whether Superman will exercise more caution when fighting in Metropolis in Superman: Legacy, it’s safe to say that Gunn will bring plenty of positives in other areas.

How Else Guardians Of The Galaxy Proves James Gunn Is Right For Superman: Legacy

One of the standout announcements for Superman: Legacy is the characters set to be involved. While the eponymous hero will be taking center stage, the movie will still platform a plethora of DC heroes who have yet to make their way to live-action movies. These include Metamorpho, Mister Terrific, and Guy Gardner, to name a few. Guardians of the Galaxy is proof of how Gunn can handle a group of lesser-known heroes, and turn them into megastars.

Not only that, but an about-turn on the tone of the movie could be exactly what’s needed for Superman: Legacy. One of the criticisms leveled at Man of Steel, and the DCEU as a whole, was the dark and gritty tone it adopted (before Aquaman and The Flash attempted a change of pace). Gunn’s comedic repertoire, especially Guardians of the Galaxy, signifies that Superman: Legacy might depict a Superman story like never before with a lighthearted spin - which is a fresh and exciting new direction for the Man of Steel.
