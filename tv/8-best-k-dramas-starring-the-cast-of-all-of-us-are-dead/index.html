8 Best K-Dramas Starring The Cast Of All Of Us Are Dead

2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The All of Us Are Dead cast includes both established and up-and-coming actors from other popular K-dramas. All of Us Are Dead season 2 is expected to bring back the main characters, perhaps even Cheong-san, who seemingly died in the first season. Fans of the All of Us Are Dead actors can enjoy other K-dramas such as Little Women, Hope or Dope, Delivery Man, Hospital Playlist, The Matchmakers, Sweet Revenge, Revenge of Others, and Squid Game.

Literature Review

The cast of All of Us Are Dead has starred in some other great Korean shows, both before and after the success of Netflix’s zombie K-drama.

The All of Us Are Dead cast includes both established and up-and-coming actors, and they have been in some other incredible K-dramas released before and after the Netflix zombie series. Just a few months after the success of Squid Game, another Netflix Korean show became a global hit – All of Us Are Dead. Based on the Now In Our School webtoon, the series followed a group of students as they tried to make it through the first few hours of a zombie apocalypse that started in their school. Park Ji-hu, Yoon Chan-young, Cho Yi-hyun, and Lomon played season 1’s main characters. Most All of Us Are Dead characters are very compelling, but the series primarily focuses on On-jo, Cheong-san, Nam-ra, and Su-hyeok. All of these characters are expected to return in season 2, even though Cheong-san seemingly died at the end of the first season. What All of Us Are Dead season 2’s story will be now that most of the city was destroyed has yet to be revealed. Fortunately, even though the release date for the season hasn’t been confirmed yet, those who want to see more of the All of Us Are Dead actors have plenty of great K-drama options. Watch All of Us Are Dead on Netflix

8 Little Women (2022) Starring Park Ji-hu as Oh In-hye

A modern adaptation of Louisa May Alcott’s novel of the same name, Little Women (2022) tells the intertwined stories of three sisters as they try to navigate life together. Between issues with both of their parents and problems at work, the three sisters find themselves in the most delicate yet important year of their lives, especially after an immense amount of money comes into play. Little Women may have slow pacing, but the series delivers great moments that will generate both laughs and tears. Park Ji-hu plays On In-hye, the younger sister, whose dream is to be an artist. Little Women (2022) is streaming on Netflix

7 Hope or Dope (2022) Starring Yoon Chan-young as Gong Yoon-tak

Around the same time, All of Us Are Dead took the world by storm, Yoon Chan-young also starred in a less popular yet equally interesting K-drama – Hope or Dope. It tells the story of Kyung Da-jung, a teenage girl who is used as a drug mule by her mother. Da-jung never managed to escape, but after a failed negotiation involving dangerous criminals, she was forced to flee home and hide in the rural area. Yoon Chan-young plays Gong Yoon-tak, a boy Da-jung meets in her new home. Hope or Dope is an action-packed drama that ran for two seasons. Hope or Dope is streaming on Viki.

6 Delivery Man (2023) Starring Yoon Chan-young as Seo Young-min

Coming off of his success in All of Us Are Dead season 1, Yoon Chan-young starred as the lead character in 2023’s Delivery Man. This supernatural K-drama with a lot of comedy follows Seo Young-min, Yoon Chan-young’s character, who works as a taxi driver to make some extra money but encounters some strange occurrences. More specifically, one night Young-min realized there was a ghost riding his taxi. From then on, his car became the go-to transport option for spirits who still had unfinished business in the world of the living. It’s a creative premise that makes for some hilarious scenes. Delivery Man is streaming on Viki.

5 Hospital Playlist (2020) Featuring Cho Yi-hyun as Jang Yoon-bok

Although Cho Yi-hyun only plays a supporting character in Hospital Playlist, this medical K-drama is too good not to be recommended. Airing from 2020 to 2021, Hospital Playlist is a “Grey’s Anatomy meets Glee” type of show where the daily work life of a group of doctors becomes more special thanks to music. It follows five doctors who have known each other since medical school and who, in addition to working together, also share a love for music. Hospital Playlist ran for two seasons with a total of 24 episodes. Cho Yi-hyun played Yoon-bok, the sister of one of the protagonists. Hospital Playlist is streaming on Netflix.

4 The Matchmakers (2023) Starring Cho Yi-hyun as Jeong Soon-deok

For fans of both historical K-dramas and All of Us Are Dead, there is no better option than The Matchmakers. The series, which stars Cho Yi-hyun as Jeong Soon-deok, takes place in the early years of the Joseon era and sees the lead character living a secret life as a matchmaker. Soon-deok has found comfort in her secret job following the death of her husband, an experience that Shim Jung-woo, who lost his wife shortly after their marriage, can relate to. When they start to work together, The Matchmakers becomes a hilarious, wholesome series that leads into a love story. The Matchmakers is streaming on Viki.

3 Sweet Revenge (2017) Starring Lomon as Shin Ji-hoon and featuring Cho Yi-hyun as Ye-ri

Several years before playing Su-hyeok in All of Us Are Dead, Lomon starred as Ji-hoon in Sweet Revenge. The web series premiered in 2017 and told the story of Ho Goo-hee, who found a way to take revenge on those who did her wrong thanks to a mysterious app that shows up on her phone. Sweet Revenge also features Cho Yi-hyun, who plays one of the students and would go on to star alongside Lomon in All of Us Are Dead. It must be noted that, while Sweet Revenge 2 happened, the sequel series followed a different set of characters. Sweet Revenge is streaming on Prime Video.

2 Revenge of Others (2022) Starring Lomon as Ji Soo-heon

Following the death of her brother, a high school student begins to investigate those around him, especially the school’s bullies. This leads her to Jo Soo-heon, played by Lomon, who found a rather unusual job by beating up bullies in exchange for money from the people they were harassing. Despite having elements of comedy and romance, Revenge of Others deals with heavy themes as Chan-mi plots to bring down everyone responsible for what happened to her twin brother. Korean dramas about revenge are usually great, as recently seen with The Glory, and it is no different for Revenge of Others. Revenge of Others is streaming on Hulu.

1 Squid Game (2021) Featuring Lee Yoo-mi as Ji-yeong

Lee Yoo-mi, who plays Na-yeon in All of Us Are Dead, also shined in another massively successful Netflix Korean drama released a few months earlier – Squid Game. Netflix’s most-watched show of all time, Squid Game became more than just a series, earning the status of a pop culture phenomenon thanks to its simple yet engaging premise of a deadly battle royale designed for people struggling with financial difficulties. Although Lee Yoo-mi’s Ji-yeong is not one of the protagonists, she is arguably one of the most important Squid Game characters when it comes to highlighting how emotional the series can be. Squid Game is streaming on Netflix.
