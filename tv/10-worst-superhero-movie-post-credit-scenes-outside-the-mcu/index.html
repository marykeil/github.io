10 Worst Superhero Movie Post-Credit Scenes Outside the MCU

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Non-MCU superhero movies often have post-credit scenes that fall flat, lacking execution or follow-up from studios. Shazam’s post-credit scene with the evil caterpillar Mister Mind was bizarre and didn’t go anywhere in the DCEU timeline. The Flash’s post-credit scene with Aquaman was confusing and didn’t build hype for his impending sequel or answer audience questions about the multiverse.

Literature Review

Many superhero movies have tried to copy the same post-credit formula that the MCU popularized, but several have fallen flat on their faces.

The MCU popularized the post-credit scenes trend, and many superhero movies have embarrassed themselves by trying to follow the same formula. Audiences were stunned during the post-credits of Iron Man when Nick Fury appeared, telling Tony Stark about the Avengers Initiative. Since then, MCU films almost always have mid-credit and post-credit scenes that tease future films, and typically cause viewers to rave about them online. This trend found its way to other superhero franchises, as they wanted to generate the same level of excitement that the MCU did. DC films and non-MCU Marvel films usually have post-credit scenes that try to get viewers more invested in their universes. Unfortunately, many of these fell flat as they were either poorly executed or the scenes received no follow-up from the studio. Here are the 10 worst superhero movie post-credit scenes outside of the MCU timeline.

10 MCU Post-Credits Scenes That Didn’t Go The Way We Expected
The MCU likes to tease upcoming projects in post-credits scenes, but the movies that follow don’t always take their queues from these teasers.

10 Dr. Thaddeus Sivana Speaks to A Caterpillar Shazam

Shazam!
PG-13

Release Date April 5, 2019 Director David F. Sandberg Cast Faithe Herman , Jack Dylan Grazer , Ian Chen , Asher Angel , Marta Milans , Mark Strong , Cooper Andrews , Andi Osho , grace fulton , Zachary Levi , Ron Cephas Jones , Ava Preston Runtime 138 minutes

Shazam ends with Mark Strong’s Thaddeus Sivana being locked up in prison, writing cryptic symbols on a well. However, he isn’t alone, as he is visited by an evil caterpillar hell-bent on world domination. Most viewers were confused as to who this was, but comic book readers recognized this as Mister Mind, a highly intelligent alien caterpillar with telepathic powers. While Mister Mind does return in another post-credit scene in Fury of the Gods, this moment lands flat, as it’s very bizarre, and it didn’t go anywhere within the DCEU timeline before the franchise concluded.

9 The Man In The Shadows Talks To Dr. Connors The Amazing Spider-Man 2

The Amazing Spider-Man 2
PG-13

Release Date May 2, 2014 Director Marc Webb Cast Andrew Garfield , Felicity Jones , Chris Cooper , Emma Stone , Martin Sheen , Paul Giamatti , Sally Field , Jamie Foxx , Dane DeHaan Runtime 142 minutes

Andrew Garfield never got the third solo Spider-Man film he deserved, and that’s mainly because Sony focused too hard on trying to build a Spider-Man universe, rather than creating a great film. This post-credit scene, where The Man in the Shadows visits Dr. Curt Connors in prison, is indicative of what went wrong. The Man in the Shadows, a.k.a Gustav Fiers, is teased earlier, setting up the Sinister Six. Here, he asks Connors about Peter Parker’s father, trying to establish more mystery around him. Unfortunately, since no third film came out, this scene is just awkward and meaningless.

8 Deadpool’s Severed Head Talks X-Men Origins: Wolverine

X-Men Origins: Wolverine
pg-13

Release Date April 30, 2009 Director Gavin Hood Cast Hugh Jackman , Ryan Reynolds , Liev Schreiber , Taylor Kitsch Runtime 107minutes

X-Men Origins: Wolverine completely botched Deadpool. For some reason, the iterations of Wade Wilson the movie brings to the table is drastically different to the comics, with laser vision, his lips sewn shut, and blades attached to his arms. After Wolverine cuts his head off, it’s presumed that he’s dead. However, a post-credits scene sees his head opening his eyes and telling the audience, “Shhhh,” teasing his possible return. Fortunately, this Deadpool never returned, and Ryan Reynolds got the chance to shine as the Deadpool audiences know and love.

7 The Flash and Superman Race Justice League

Justice League
PG-13

Release Date November 17, 2017 Director Zack Snyder Cast Jeremy Irons , Gal Gadot , Amy Adams , Jesse Eisenberg , Ciarán Hinds , Ben Affleck , Henry Cavill , Ezra Miller , J. K. Simmons , Jason Momoa , Ray Fisher

One controversial choice that Joss Whedon’s Justice League made was suggesting that Superman is as fast as The Flash. While Superman does have super speed, he’s not conventionally depicted with the same time-defying speed that The Flash has - although the comics have brought this into question more than once historically. As such, the DCEU pair decide to race in a post-credits scene, mirroring DC’s own comic race between the iconic heroes. However, the winner of the race is never revealed, and it ends in a freeze-frame right when the race begins. It’s an underwhelming way to deliver on this running gag, and it also leaves viewers with an anti-climactic finish, as the final moment of the movie experience is essentially a fade-to-black right in the middle of some action.

6 Shazam Is Recruited To The Justice Society Shazam: Fury of the Gods

Shazam! The Fury of the Gods
PG-13

Release Date March 17, 2023 Director David F. Sandberg Cast Helen Mirren , Adam Brody​ , Michelle Borth , Faithe Herman , Jack Dylan Grazer , Ian Chen , Jovan Armand , Asher Angel , Lucy Liu , Ross Butler , Marta Milans , Meagan Good , Cooper Andrews , Rachel Zegler , Zachary Levi , D.J. Cotrona , grace fulton Runtime 130 minutes

DCEU movies in 2023 were placed into a complicated position after the announcement of the DCU, as it seemed more than likely any events that took place in the franchise were going to be rewritten shortly. The Shazam! Fury Of the Gods post-credits scene where Emilia Harcourt and John Economos recruit Shazam to join the Justice Society fares from this feeling perhaps worst of all, since many viewers saw it already assuming that it wouldn’t really be going anywhere. After seeing the Justice Society in Black Adam, the wasted potential of this scene is arguably all the worse, as it underlines how much the sequel’s post-credits didn’t need to be teasing a relatively small superhero group. It also ties Shazam! Fury Of the Gods closer to Black Adam, which swiftly became more awkward when the latter movie saw a divided reception upon release.

5 Woody Harrelson Wears Hideous Wig To Tease Carnage Venom

Venom
PG-13

Release Date October 5, 2018 Director Ruben Fleischer Runtime 112 Minutes

Carnage is one of Spider-Man’s most intimidating villains, but he couldn’t have been introduced in a more goofy manner. Woody Harrelson’s Cletus Kasady debuts in the Venom post-credits scene where Eddie Brock visits him in prison. The character is wearing a hideous curly wig that was thankfully changed in Venom: Let There Be Carnage, but he also spouts some hilariously corny dialogue. The scene ends with him saying, “There’s gonna be carnage,” in a not-so-subtle tease for what’s to come. However, instead of being exciting, it comes across as somewhat more silly, being perhaps too on-the-nose to really land.

4 Barry Allen Takes Care of A Drunk Aquaman The Flash

The Flash
PG-13

Release Date June 16, 2023 Director Andres Muschietti Cast Ezra Miller , Michael Keaton , Ben Affleck , Sasha Calle , Michael Shannon Runtime 2 hours 24 minutes

After sitting through the credits of The Flash, audiences are treated to a cameo from Jason Momoa’s Aquaman. He is being carried out of a bar by Barry Allen, who is trying to explain the concept of the multiverse. It’s not made totally clear why Arthur Curry is here, though this may have possibly been intended as a teaser for Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom - though the sequel did attempt to distance itself from the DCEU in order for audiences to not be alienated by it being the last movie in the DCEU timeline, making this scene even more complicated with that concept in mind. Ultimately, the moment serves to reduce Aquaman to lying drunkenly in a puddle when it ideally should have been building hype for his impending sequel. It also explains nothing of the myriad of questions audiences had about the multiverse, or what happened to Ben Affleck’s Batman because of the multiversal shifts that take place during The Flash.

3 Charles Xavier Is Somehow Still Alive The Wolverine

The Wolverine
PG-13

Release Date July 26, 2013 Director James Mangold Cast Famke Janssen , Will Yun Lee , Brian Tee , Hugh Jackman Runtime 2h 6m

The most confusing part of the post-credits scene from The Wolverine is that it takes place after the events of X-Men: The Last Stand. In that film, Professor X was killed by Jean Grey and Magneto lost his powers. However, Magneto has his powers back and Charles is alive and well. Wolverine is just as confused as the audience, and Professor X does not explain how he’s alive. While it’s cool to see Patrick Stewart and Ian McKellen in their iconic roles, this scene created a lot of unanswered questions.

2 Orm Eats A Cockroach Burger Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom

Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom
PG-13

Release Date December 22, 2023 Director James Wan Cast Jason Momoa , Amber Heard , Patrick Wilson , Dolph Lundgren , Yahya Abdul-Mateen II , Temuera Morrison Runtime 124 Minutes

Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom’s post-credits scene drew especial ire from many viewers because of the note it ended the DCEU on. What began with the death of Krypton in Man of Steel now ended with Patrick Wilson’s Orm eating a cockroach in a burger in Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom. This doesn’t offer anything meaningful, and it’s an awkward way of finishing an already lackluster finale to the DCEU.

1 Vulture Meets Morbius To Discuss Spider-Man Morbius

Morbius
PG-13

Release Date April 1, 2022 Director Daniel Espinosa Cast Adria Arjona , Al Madrigal , Jared Harris , Matt Smith , Jared Leto , Tyrese Gibson Runtime 108 minutes

Sony has teased the Sinister Six multiple times, and Morbius attempts to do so again in its post-credits, but with disastrous results. How Michael Keaton’s Vulture ended up in the Sony Spider-Man Universe is already a convoluted and hard-to-parse concept, but this is made worse by his meeting with Jared Leto’s Morbius, which is executed horribly. Keaton and Leto seemingly aren’t in the same room as each other, as their acting is somewhat off, and the editing is strange. It’s also a confusing conversation in its own right, as it raises the question of whether Morbius should even know who Spider-Man is - something that could have felt like foreshadowing or teasing the as-of-yet unseen Sony hero had it not come off as so uncanny.
