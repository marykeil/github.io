10 The Batman 2 Villain Candidates Who Would Hurt Bruce Wayne On A Personal Level

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Batman’s villains who were once friends or allies can hurt him on a personal level due to their knowledge and history with him. Villains like Scarecrow and Clayface have unique abilities that can traumatize Bruce Wayne by exploiting his fears. The Batman 2 could introduce new villains like Hush or Anarky to challenge Batman both emotionally and ideologically.

Literature Review

The Batman was a personal battle for the Dark Knight. To continue this trend The Batman 2 should cast a villain who knows how to hurt Bruce Wayne.

The Batman’s villain forced Bruce Wayne to examine his behavior, so if The Batman 2 wants to continue that trend, the DC movie will choose a villain who can hurt the hero on a personal level. Luckily, Batman has no shortage of excellent rogues who know how to get under his skin. Famously, the Joker has always been a Batman movie villain who knows how to push the Dark Knight’s buttons and get a reaction out of him, but he isn’t the only one. Several of Batman’s villains are people he knew first as Bruce Wayne, who later became criminals. These characters are expertly positioned to hurt Bruce in a personal way in The Batman 2 because they could get around the separation of Bruce and his Batman persona. These villains may also know that Bruce is Batman, giving them an extra edge in messing with his feelings. They may also threaten the people he loves, or at least use them against Bruce to get to him in The Batman 2.

10 Hush Was One Of Bruce’s Oldest Friends Hush Has Appeared In Gotham And Batwoman

Thomas Elliot is one of the few Batman villains who was friends with Bruce before he was Batman. This childhood friend of Bruce’s proves to be the perfect foil as his story basically makes him the Anti-Batman. Thomas tries to kill his wealthy parents for their money, but Thomas Wayne saves their lives. Thomas Elliot takes his revenge out on Bruce when they grow up, and he even learns Bruce is Batman. His friend’s betrayal is especially painful for Bruce, who idealizes the time of his life when his parents were still alive.

9 The Joker Hints At History With Batman In A Deleted Scene Barry Keoghan Played The Joker In The Batman

The Batman universe already has a version of the Joker, though only his voice appeared in the movie; Barry Keoghan’s Joker appeared more fully in a deleted scene. In that scene, Joker hints at a history with Batman, referring to their upcoming “anniversary.” Joker proves to be insightful in the scene, instantly clocking Riddler’s motives after a brief glance at some crime scene photos. He then begins needling Batman, trying to get a rise out of him. It is clear that this Joker wants to find out what makes Bruce tick, and if he is The Batman 2’s villain he will probably figure it out.

8 Scarecrow’s Fear Toxin Can Traumatize Bruce Again Scarecrow Has Appeared In Titans, Gotham, and The Dark Knight Trilogy

Few things get to Bruce Wayne like memories of his parents. Scarecrow might not know Bruce on a personal level, but he doesn’t have to. His fear toxin does the work for him and can force Bruce to relive the worst parts of his life. A healthy dose of the stuff and Bruce will go from the intimidating Batman back to a scared child in Crime Alley. Forcing Bruce to relive his trauma is a unique skill that only Scarecrow can pull off.

7 Hugo Strange Can Use Bruce’s Secret Identity Against Him Hugo Strange Appeared in Gotham

Hugo Strange is a psychiatrist who is best known for figuring out Batman’s secret identity. The Batman sets Bruce on a path to bettering himself, so it is possible he could be going to therapy in The Batman 2. Making Strange Bruce’s doctor could give the villain insight into Bruce’s personal life that few villains ever get. He could use these details to mess with Batman in ways other villains would never be able to.

6 Catwoman Could Break Bad And Bruce’s Heart Zoë Kravitz Played Selina Kyle in The Batman

Though Selina Kyle AKA Catwoman has largely been depicted as an anti-hero lately, she is always straddling the line between hero and criminal. If The Batman 2 wanted to take things in a different direction, they could make Selina go full villain and work against Bruce. This wouldn’t only be a fresh take on the character, it would hurt Bruce Wayne in a very personal way, since the first movie established his romantic interest in Catwoman.

5 Clayface Could Impersonate Bruce’s Loved Ones Clayface Has Appeared In Birds Of Prey (2002), Gotham, and Pennyworth

One of Clayface’s powers is to shape-shift into other people. This means he could pose as someone close to Bruce Wayne for his own personal gain. This could lead to Clayface learning he’s Batman, or the villain could just be trying to manipulate Bruce in hopes of getting his hands on the Wayne fortune. Alternatively, he could pose as one of Batman’s allies, like Catwoman, and trick Bruce into confiding in him. Either way, this ability can give Clayface unparalleled access to Bruce and the ability to hit him where it hurts.

4 Ra’s al Ghul Was Bruce’s Mentor And Trainer Ra’s al Ghul Appeared In Gotham, Arrow, And The Dark Knight Trilogy

Ra’s al Ghul is often depicted as being the one who initially trained Bruce Wayne. This means that the villain knows Bruce is Batman, and also knows personal details about him. To make things even messier, Bruce is sometimes romantically linked with Ra’s al Ghul’s daughter, Talia. This means Ra’s could leverage either Bruce’s feelings for his daughter or Bruce’s secret identity against him if Batman tries to stand in his way.

3 Two-Face Was Once Batman’s Ally And Friend Harvey Dent And Two-Face Have Appeared In Gotham, Batman, Batman Forever, And The Dark Knight

As a district attorney, Harvey Dent was a strong ally for Batman because he helped ensure the criminals Bruce caught would be put away. In some iterations, Harvey is also personal friends with Bruce Wayne. This makes his transformation into Two-Face a serious blow for Bruce because he isn’t just losing an important ally in his mission, but also a personal friend. Two-Face doesn’t even have to try to hurt Bruce in a personal way, the simple fact that he becomes a villain will torture Bruce enough.

2 The Court Of Owls Might Have Planned The Waynes’ Killing The Court Of Owls Appeared In Gotham

In the comics, the Court of Owls is a shadowy cabal that controls Gotham. They and their immortal Talon assassins have shaped the city over the years, killing those who get in their way. If Thomas and Martha Wayne posed a threat to the Court, it wouldn’t be a big surprise if they had orchestrated their killing and made it look like a simple mugging. This would give Bruce a very personal reason to go after them and continue the intrigue about the Waynes’ murder that was set up in The Batman.

1 Anarky Can Cast Doubt On Bruce’s Mission Anarky Has Appeared In Arrow

Though recent iterations of Anarky have typically been uncomplicated villains, the original version of the character posed much more of a quandary for Bruce Wayne. Anarky has a similar mission to Batman: He wants to help people. However, Anarky wants to do so by tearing down institutions he sees as unjust, while Batman largely wants to maintain the status quo. Anarky could continue the trend of villains in The Batman universe, challenging Bruce both physically and on an ideological level, forcing him to reconsider his mission and the way he goes about it in The Batman 2.
