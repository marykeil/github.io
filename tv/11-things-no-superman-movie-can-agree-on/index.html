11 Things No Superman Movie Can Agree On

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Superman movies can’t agree on key aspects like Krypton’s appearance, Metropolis’ location, and Superman’s costume. The motivations of Superman’s villains and the way Jonathan Kent dies also differ in different movies. The moral code of Superman, the character of Lex Luthor, and how the world perceives Superman are also inconsistent across movies.

Literature Review

Superman may well be the quintessential superhero, but there are several points upon which the Man of Steel’s own movies can never seem to agree on.

Though the hero is undeniably one of the most iconic figures in pop culture, there are certain ideas that no Superman movie can appear to agree on. Superman’s status as perhaps the world’s foremost superhero is undeniable, and this honor extends to the world of film. Just as his stories helped pioneer the medium of comic books, the best Superman movies have helped elevate the superhero genre and widen its mainstream appeal. As Superman has been featured in many films over his long history, there are a number of ideas about the character that aren’t always consistent. With so many different stories from so many different eras, it’s no wonder that ideas about how best to bring Superman to life on the big screen have changed over the years. For each new actor to play Superman or each new director handling his story, there are new notions about elements of his character or history. These discrepancies might well be an important part of creative storytelling, but they also make it seem that different movies simply can’t agree on the character. With that in mind, here are 11 things that Superman movies can’t seem to agree on.

Every Superman Movie Ranked From Worst To Best Superman films have shown both the best and the worst the superhero genre has to offer. Let’s rank all the Man of Steel’s theatrical releases.

11 What Krypton Looks Like Superman’s Planet Has Had Many Looks

One of the most important elements of Superman’s story lies in his origin, as the destruction of his birth planet, Krypton, has a huge impact on the role he plays on Earth. Though most Superman movies agree on that point, they can’t always agree on what exactly Krypton was like. Movies such as Richard Donner’s 1978 film Superman imagined it as a world made up of large crystals, whereas Superman Returns implied it was much rockier. Man of Steel depicted it as more verdant and geographically diverse, highlighting that no Superman movie can agree on what Krypton actually looked like.

10 Where Exactly Metropolis Is Located The Location Of Superman’s Fictional City Isn’t Consistent

Over the years, there have been many versions of Metropolis, Superman’s city of residence, introduced in different movies. It’s always as large and vibrant a city as its name implies, but no Superman movie seems to agree on where exactly it is. Many movies imply it’s a coastal city, as boats and docks often feature heavily, but others heavily insinuate that it’s the nearest city to Smallville, which is in the landlocked state of Kansas. Exactly where Metropolis exists within the world isn’t entirely clear, and different Superman movies have very different ideas about it, too.

9 The Motivations Of Superman Villains Why Superman’s Villains Do What They Do Keeps Changing

Superman has faced a wealth of villains over his tenure as a hero, and several of them have also made it to the big screen. However, Superman movies always seem to disagree on the nature of their villains’ motivations, especially when adapting the same villain for a new story. For example, Lex Luthor is sometimes fueled by greed and other times by hatred for Superman or by some baser instinct to exert control over others. General Zod sometimes hopes to conquer and others to simply destroy, highlighting that Superman movies aren’t always sure why their villains act as they do.

8 How Jonathan Kent Died The Varied Deaths Of Clark Kent’s Father Are Crucial Parts Of His Story

In most stories, another defining moment of Superman’s origin is the death of his adoptive father, Jonathan Kent. Unfortunately, different Superman movies have different ideas on how exactly this happened, with it sometimes being the result of a heart attack and Man of Steel asserting that he sacrificed himself to a tornado to protect Clark’s secret. Considering the vast tonal chasm between the two deaths and their implications for Superman’s own story, it’s clear that the movies’ differing ideas about Jonathan Kent’s death are the result of distinct visions for the hero’s story.

7 Superman’s Costume Is Often Missing Key Features No Superman Movie Agrees On A Specific Costume

Superman’s overall aesthetic is well-established, and most movies do very little to change the core traits of the hero’s costume. However, experimentation across different movies has seen key elements of his outfit go through some changes, indicating that different filmmakers have their own ideas of what his costume should look like. For example, Henry Cavill’s Superman didn’t don the traditional red briefs on his costume, and later movies also saw him wear a black suit. Seemingly, in the name of innovation, it appears that Superman movies don’t always agree on the character’s classic costume.

6 Superman’s Moral Code The Man Of Steel’s Rules Are Always Changing

As the foremost hero in the DC Universe, Superman is a leader and a shining example of a being of steadfast morals. Sadly, different movies don’t always agree on the exact nature of those morals, leading to a variety of ideas on Superman’s stance on various topics. Though the hero is usually shown doing all he can to prevent loss of life, Man of Steel showed him killing Zod to protect the Earth. Whether or not Superman should kill his villains ultimately comes down to morals, and different movies have conflicting opinions on how appropriate this is.

5 Lex Luthor Changes Drastically From One Continuity To The Next The Iconic Superman Villain Is Often Rewritten To Fit A New Narrative

No Superman story would be complete without an iteration of the hero’s arch-nemesis, meaning that there have been as many live-action versions of Lex Luthor as there have of Superman. However, no two Superman movies can seem to agree on a specific style of character for Lex, resulting in huge changes to his personality in different continuities. Gene Hackman’s Luthor was a brilliant but violent criminal, where Kevin Spacey’s was more of a conniving con-man, and Jesse Eisenberg’s was a manic xenophobe. This simply makes it seem that Superman movies can’t agree on the best way to approach Luthor’s character.

4 Whether The World Actually Loves Superman Earth Can’t Make Its Mind Up About The Kryptonian

Another hugely inconsistent point in Superman movies is the way that the citizens of Earth react to the Kryptonian hero. In many stories, they revere him in light of his unflinching heroism, but in others, they are suspicious or altogether hostile towards him. The different narrative elements these ideas bring to a story make for interesting developments, especially when it comes to exploring the hero’s own character. Even though the result of these conflicting ideas is largely good, Superman movies still can’t agree on whether Earth loves him or fears him.

3 Whether Lois Lane Should Know Clark’s Secret Clark Doesn’t Always Trust Lois Completely

Other than Superman himself, perhaps the most important character in any given Superman movie is Lois Lane. There have been multiple versions of Lois Lane over the hero’s cinematic past, but there’s a clear divide between those who know Clark Kent’s super secret and those who don’t. Whether or not Lois knows that Clark Kent is Superman changes from movie to movie, with her knowledge of his dual identity not an essential part of the mythos but rather an interesting addition. It may not matter in the grand scheme of things, but different Superman movies can’t agree either way.

2 How Badly Kryptonite Affects Superman Varies Wildly Superman’s Weakness Isn’t Always Insurmountable

Superman’s most famous weakness, Kryptonite, features in most modern Superman movies in one form or another. Despite the substance being a mainstay of his stories, Superman movies can never seem to agree on a consistent way in which it affects the hero. In some stories, it incapacitates him completely, while in others, it causes him physical pain. In some, he begins to feel nauseous, and in others, the Kryptonite threatens to kill him outright. Exactly how badly the radioactive pieces of his homeworld can hurt him is anyone’s guess, as even the movies don’t seem to know.

1 The Varied Tones Of Different Movies Are Jarring Different Directors Have Different Ideas About The Best Tone For A Superman Movie

Since the movies of the DCEU introduced a very different Superman, it’s become clear that different movies featuring the hero boast incredibly different tones. The earlier movies made by Richard Donner in the late 1970s and early ‘80s were much more light-hearted in tone, with only a little more serious subject matter. Zack Snyder’s Man of Steel was far bleaker, examining a darker side of the hero and his story. The varied tones of different movies are especially jarring when considered alongside one another, making it the biggest issue upon which no Superman movie can agree.
