Curb Your Enthusiasm Season 12 Episode 1 Recap Larry s Atlanta Twist & 9 Other Best Moments

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Maria Sofia becomes a breakout star, causing Larry’s worst nightmare to come true. Larry is stuck dating Irma for six months at the insistence of her sponsor. Larry’s hilarious mishaps include yelling at Siri and getting arrested for providing water at a polling station.

Literature Review

Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere has gotten the final season off to a great start with some hilarious gags and a surprise twist ending.

Warning: This article contains spoilers for Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 1. Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 1, “Atlanta,” has gotten the show’s final season off to a great start with some hilarious gags and a surprise twist ending. The season premiere sees Larry, Leon, and Maria Sofia traveling to Atlanta for a paid appearance at a rich businessman’s birthday party. Naturally, calamity ensues as Larry runs afoul of the birthday boy, most of his party guests, the staff at his hotel, and Maria Sofia’s emotional support dog, Pechuca. The Atlanta trip gives the show a change of scenery and gives Larry a whole new city in which to cause mayhem. Since Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12 has been marketed as the series’ last season, this premiere episode could very well be the beginning of the end – and it’s set the stage for a great final chapter. “Atlanta” brings back a fan-favorite character who hasn’t been seen since season 7 and follows up on some of the best storylines from season 11. From the grieving waiter to the Siri outburst, there are a ton of memorable moments in Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere.

10 Maria Sofia Is The Breakout Star Of Young Larry Larry’s worst nightmare has come true

In the last season of Curb Your Enthusiasm, Larry was blackmailed into casting the supremely untalented Maria Sofia Estrada in his new show, Young Larry. The opening montage of Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere reveals that Maria Sofia became the breakout star of the show, and one of the biggest celebrities in the world. She appears on the covers of magazines like Vogue and People, she wins an Emmy for her role as Marsha Lipshitz, and she’s a guest on Jimmy Kimmel Live!. Larry’s worst nightmare has come true.

9 Larry Is Stuck With Irma For At Least Six Months Irma’s sponsor is forcing Larry to stay with Irma for a minimum of six months

Last season, Larry begrudgingly dated city councilor Irma Kostroski – who he described as “the most unappealing person I have ever met” – in a bid to get a local law repealed. In season 12, despite the five-foot fence storyline having blown over, Larry is still dating Irma. A flashback reveals that Larry is still dating Irma at the behest of her sponsor, who thinks it would jeopardize her recovery if Larry doesn’t stay with her for a minimum of six months. So, he’s counting down those months religiously, marking off the days on a secret calendar hidden away in a drawer.

8 The J.G. Wentworth Jingle Irma can’t get the J.G. Wentworth jingle out of her head

When Irma is reintroduced in Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere, she’s just as annoying as ever. She waltzes into Larry’s kitchen and disturbs his peaceful breakfast with her millionth rendition of the operatic J.G. Wentworth advertising jingle. Larry begs her to stop, but she says she can’t, because the song is stuck in her head. Not only does this scene highlight how much Irma gets on Larry’s nerves; it makes a larger point about the irritation of a catchy corporate jingle – and they couldn’t have chosen a more grating jingle to make that point.

7 Larry Yells At Siri “One option I see…”

In a classic Curb Your Enthusiasm moment, Larry gets in his car and asks Siri for directions to a restaurant. Siri mishears Larry and presents the wrong information over and over until Larry becomes infuriated and starts yelling at Siri for being a “f***ing moron!!” This is a typical case of Larry getting irrationally furious over a mundane irritation of everyday life. Not everybody would scream at Siri and slam their phone down like Larry does, but everybody can relate to the frustration of an A.I. assistant that mishears every single request.

6 The Grieving Waiter A waiter mourning the loss of his mother puts Larry in a “no-win situation”

When Larry, Jeff, and Susie are waiting for their food at a restaurant, one of the other servers tells them that their table’s waiter recently lost his mother, so he’s been distracted. According to Larry, this is a “no-win situation,” because the waiter is too grief-stricken to bring them their food on time, but they can’t complain to the management because then they’ll look insensitive. Eventually, Larry decides to collect his own food. He compassionately tells the waiter, “Sorry for your loss,” before callously taking the tray from his hands, creating a hilarious juxtaposition.

5 “D*** Dialing” Leon has discovered an alternative to butt-dialing

A running gag throughout Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere is that Larry keeps accidentally butt-dialing people. This gag is paid off brilliantly at the end of the episode when a butt-dial ends up costing Larry a lot of money. Leon says he’s never butt-dialed anyone, because he keeps his phone in his front pocket, but he does sometimes “d***-dial” people. This is a hysterical J.B. Smoove riff that keeps escalating the absurdity. Before long, Leon is impersonating his own penis dialing a phone number.

4 Auntie Rae Returns Leon’s aunt hasn’t been seen since season 7

The season 12 premiere of Curb Your Enthusiasm brings back Auntie Rae, Leon’s aunt who lived with Larry when her family was displaced by a hurricane. Auntie Rae hasn’t been seen since season 7, episode 2, “Vehicular Fellatio,” when she stormed out of Larry’s house due to a scandalous misunderstanding. 15 years later, she seems to have forgiven him, because she greets Larry with a warm welcome when he comes to visit her in Atlanta. Auntie Rae unwittingly gets Larry arrested at the end of the episode, so she may return in subsequent season 12 installments.

3 Larry Wears Auntie Rae’s Glasses Larry has to wear Auntie Rae’s glasses after she breaks his

While Larry is at Auntie Rae’s house, she tries on his glasses and stretches them out, so they won’t stay on his face anymore. Since Auntie Rae has a similar prescription, she lends him a spare pair of hers, which has a ridiculously extravagant design. This becomes a hilarious sight gag, so to speak, that persists for the rest of the episode. When Larry gets into an elevator, everyone else in the elevator immediately stares at his new frames. When he meets eyes with a woman wearing similar glasses, they share an unspoken moment that gets a huge laugh.

2 The “Brookie” Conundrum Larry is barred from using Brooke’s nickname

One of the aspects of mundane human interaction tackled by Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere is the question of who can or cannot refer to somebody by their affectionate nickname. Larry meets a woman named Brooke whose friends call her “Brookie,” but he’s told he can’t call her “Brookie,” because they’re not close enough. The plot thickens when Larry meets a man named Carl who has “Brookie” privilege despite having only met her once, the same as Larry.

1 Larry Gets Arrested His mugshot is hilariously familiar

At the end of Curb Your Enthusiasm’s season 12 premiere, Larry stops by a polling station where Auntie Rae is waiting to vote, so he can return her glasses. He offers her bottled water to combat the blistering Atlanta heat and he’s promptly arrested for violating a recently passed Georgia law that forbids people from supplying voters with food or water while they’re waiting. This arrest leads to the episode’s hilarious final image: Larry’s mugshot, a direct recreation of Donald Trump’s own mugshot from when he was arrested in Atlanta on August 24, 2023.
