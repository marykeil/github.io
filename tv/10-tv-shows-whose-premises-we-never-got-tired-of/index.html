10 TV Shows Whose Premises We Never Got Tired Of

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2000 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Most television shows follow a formulaic structure to maintain consistency and engage viewers. Compelling character dynamics and fresh storytelling are essential to avoid monotony in plotlines. The success of a show lies in its ability to maintain audience interest through character-driven stories and relatable experiences.

Literature Review

Having a formulaic plot isn’t necessarily a bad thing. Some TV shows use this repetition to the show’s advantage, creating something unique.

Most television shows begin with a basic premise that the series follows to build consistency and develop character, and when the show is strong enough, the repetition elevates the story instead of making it boring. Though procedurals are the most frequently referenced when it comes to formulaic structure, every show follows a formula to a certain extent. There has to be consistency across characters, plotting, and storylines, or else the show would be considered an anthology. There are many premises that a show can rely on to frame the story. Often, formulas regarding a profession, like police or doctors, can become boring the quickest, but that’s not always the case. Important aspects that every show must include to avoid monotonous plotting are compelling character dynamics and writing that introduces familiar topics and tropes in a new way. Character-driven stories with plenty of room for mishaps make for engaging television.

15 Best Procedural TV Shows, Ranked Within the realm of procedural television shows, a select few redefine the genre, setting themselves apart with compelling narratives and innovation.

10 The Nanny (1993 - 1999) A fashionable young woman from Queens becomes the nanny to children on the Upper East Side

The Nanny
Sitcom

Release Date June 23, 1999 Cast Fran Drescher , Lauren Lane Seasons 6 Story By Fran Drescher Writers Fran Drescher Network CBS Streaming Service(s) HBO Max , Prime Video Directors Fran Drescher

Fran Fine (Fran Drescher) is a fish out of water among the rich and successful Sheffield family, but it soon becomes clear that she’s exactly what they need. Though the comedic beats of the show often rely upon how different Fran is from the rest of them, the plots are inventive enough that these situations remain interesting. Part of this is due to the will-they-won’t-they relationship between Fran and Mr. Sheffield (Charles Shaughnessy). Though their constant romantic missteps can get repetitive, their chemistry makes it worth the wait.

9 The Golden Girls (1985 - 1992) The joys of growing older and sticking with friends

The Golden Girls
Sitcom

Release Date September 14, 1985 Cast Betty White Seasons 7 Story By Susan Harris Writers Susan Harris Network NBC Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Disney+ , Prime Video Directors Susan Harris

The Golden Girls was revolutionary for many reasons, one being its focus on women in the latter half of life, as well as how strong the storytelling was until the end. Each character was unique and brought her own brand of comedy to the show. This provided ample opportunities for clashing between the main characters, and for them to demonstrate how differently they handle tough situations. Though the series never branched too far out for the daily tasks of the women, it was easy to spend time with such fabulous characters.

8 Friends (1994 - 2004) A beautiful New York fantasy with real heart in the writing

Friends
Comedy Drama Sitcom

Release Date September 22, 1994 Cast Jennifer Aniston , Courteney Cox , Lisa Kudrow , Matt LeBlanc , Matthew Perry , David Schwimmer Seasons 10 Story By Marta Kauffman Writers David Crane , Marta Kauffman Network NBC Franchise(s) Friends Directors David Crane , Marta Kauffman Where To Watch Max

Friends is what everyone wants living in New York to be like. The apartments are huge, there’s plenty of downtime, and there’s a friend group of gorgeous people who can’t stop dating each other. Though the on-screen pairings between the main cast of Friends can grow tiresome, watching the characters get themselves in and out of wild situations never does. When a series has the longevity of Friends there’s a reason, and it’s because of the simple premise that no one can get enough of the show. Living in an amazing city with hilarious, if sometimes ridiculous, people is enough.

7 New Girl (2011 - 2018) The show by and for millennials

New Girl
TV-14 Comedy

Release Date September 20, 2011 Cast Zooey Deschanel , Max Greenfield , Hannah Simone , Damon Wayans Jr. , Lamorne Morris , Jake Johnson Seasons 7 Writers Elizabeth Meriwether Network FOX Showrunner Elizabeth Meriwether

The chemistry between Jess (Zooey Deschanel) and Nick (Jake Johnson) on New Girl is one of the long-lasting hallmarks of the series, but the show was only as successful as it was because of the platonic relationships. It starts by throwing Jess into an apartment with three men she doesn’t know and unfolding how the group gets to know each other and brings the others out of their shells. Though few sitcom tropes are subverted, the show is a tribute to the millennial style of humor and experience of feeling lost in the world, which is relatable to every generation.

6 Parks And Recreation (2009 - 2015) Chronicling the trials and tribulations of local government

Parks and Recreation
Sitcom Comedy Mockumentary

Release Date April 9, 2009 Cast Amy Poehler , Nick Offerman , ​Chris Pratt2 , Aubrey Plaza , Adam Scott , Rashida Jones , Retta , Jim O’Heir , Aziz Ansari , Rob Lowe Seasons 7 Story By Michael Schur, Michael offerman Writers Michael Schur , Nick Offerman Network NBC Streaming Service(s) fuboTV , Peacock Directors Michael Schur

Starring Amy Poehler as Leslie Knope, the government employee who faces every setback and bureaucratic nightmare with a smile and a positive attitude, Parks and Recreation is one of the best feel-good sitcoms out there. Though Leslie and her team constantly come up against ungrateful citizens in their town and are rarely recognized for their hard work, the show prides itself on being genuinely funny without making mean jokes. The love that’s at the heart of the series is what made it so enjoyable when it was on air, and makes it a treat to watch today.

5 Law & Order: SVU (1999 - ) The most successful iteration of the franchise

Law and Order: SVU
TV-14 Crime Drama Mystery Thriller

Release Date September 20, 1999 Cast Christopher Meloni , Mariska Hargitay , Richard Belzer , Dann Florek , Michelle Hurd , Stephanie March , Ice-T , BD Wong , Diane Neal , Tamara Tunie Seasons 24 Story By Dick Wolf Writers Dick Wolf Network NBC Franchise(s) Law and Order

While Law & Order may have started the crime procedural craze, SVU perfected it. Since its premiere in 1999, there have been plenty of episodes that are hard to watch and the saddest SVU episodes have required a tissue box at the ready. Despite being a procedural that revolves around the detectives solving a new case every episode, the show has pushed itself to include season-long arcs and plenty of character development for the mainstays of the cast. Of all the crime dramas on TV, Law & Order: SVU will be rewatched for years.

4 Gossip Girl (2007 - 2012) Scandal, betrayal, and plenty of money

It would have been easy for Gossip Girl to grow tiresome, as there are only so many ways that fabulously wealthy people can stab each other in the back, but that wasn’t the case. Fortunately for the show, and viewers, the creators and writers didn’t take the series too seriously. They were aware that it wasn’t a prestige drama, and so they weren’t afraid to have fun with the premise and make plenty of tongue-in-cheek jokes at their own expense. Additionally, staying up to date with the complex web of relationships and amazing fashion is half the fun of watching.

3 The Simpsons (1989 - ) The pinnacle of adult animation

The Simpsons
Animation Comedy

Release Date December 17, 1989 Cast Tress MacNeille , Julie Kavner , Harry Shearer , Pamela Hayden , Nancy Cartwright , Hank Azaria , Dan Castellaneta , Yeardley Smith Seasons 35 Story By Matt Groening and James L. Brooks Writers Matt Groening , James L. Brooks , Sam Simon Network FOX Franchise(s) The Simpsons Directors David Silverman , Jim Reardon , Mark Kirkland

Though The Simpsons has been accused of using tired sitcom tropes, the show is also credited with inventing those same tropes and evolving as a series enough to subvert them. After running for so many years, it would be reasonable if the show got repetitive, or started reusing old material, but The Simpsons’ success can be attributed to how fresh every episode feels. Since its inception, adult animation has taken off, but the series remains one of the hallmarks of the genre because it has stayed on the cutting edge of popular culture. While topical jokes can sometimes date a series, The Simpsons has its finger on the pulse to avoid that problem. Additionally, the series takes its ongoing premise of spoofs and satirical references to other films and TV shows to the next level with the creators’ dedication. Proving that an episodic format can have plenty of longevity, the show uses that concept to its advantage with one-off episodes that take place in totally different worlds.

The Simpsons Just Adapted Death Note Better Than Netflix The Simpsons’ Treehouse of Horror episode featured an adaptation of Death Note. The successful segment has cast a darker shadow on Netflix’s version.

2 Seinfeld (1989 - 1998) A show about nothing for nine seasons

Seinfeld
Comedy

Release Date July 5, 1989 Cast Jerry Seinfeld , Julia Louis-Dreyfus , Jason Alexander , Michael Richards Seasons 9 Story By Jerry Seinfeld Writers Jerry Seinfeld , Larry David Network NBC Streaming Service(s) Netflix Showrunner Larry David

Seinfeld is the definition of a character-driven show. The core four cast members and their relationships are what propels the show forward, along with the clever writing that can find comedy in any situation. Despite these comedic moments, and the show’s general premise being a sitcom, there were plenty of dark moments on Seinfeld that prove how far the show was willing to push the boundaries of TV. Before Seinfeld, the concept that the doldrums of everyday life are interesting enough to carry a series would have been unthinkable, but the show’s innovation made it interesting to the very end.

1 Gilmore Girls (2000 - 2007) A mother and daughter balance life, love, and work in Connecticut

Gilmore Girls
Comedy Drama

Release Date October 5, 2000 Cast Lauren Graham , Scott Patterson , Sean Gunn , Keiko Agena , Matt Czuchry , Alexis Bledel , Yanic Truesdale , Kelly Bishop , Melissa McCarthy , Edward Herrmann , Liza Weil , Jared Padalecki , Milo Ventimiglia Seasons 7 Story By amy sherman-palladino Writers Amy Sherman-Palladino Network The WB Streaming Service(s) Netflix

The dynamic between Lorelai (Lauren Graham) and Rory (Alexis Bledel) as a mother-daughter pair closer in age and maturity than one might expect, and their revolving door of romantic troubles might rely on the same essential conflicts, but it always keeps the audience guessing. Gilmore Girls has come to be one of the most beloved family dramedies of all time due to how easily it can be rewatched. What keeps it interesting is that even though certain parts of the world remain the same, how the characters interact with that world develops along with their characterization.
