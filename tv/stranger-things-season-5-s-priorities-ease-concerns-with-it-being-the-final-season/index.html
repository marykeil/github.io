Stranger Things Season 5 s Priorities Ease Concerns With It Being The Final Season

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Stranger Things season 5 will prioritize giving proper closure to the main characters. The creators are avoiding the mistake of introducing too many new characters in the final season. The show must strike a balance between spectacle and narrative to properly wrap up the story.

Literature Review

Stranger Things season 5 has a lot to cover, but the producers seem to have their priorities in order, which is great news for the main characters.

Stranger Things season 5 has a lot to cover and solve, and luckily, the producers’ priorities are easing some big concerns. After four successful seasons full of drama, action, and shocking reveals, Stranger Things is coming to an end with its fifth season. Stranger Things season 4 has been the series’ biggest season so far, and even though it solved a couple of mysteries about the Upside Down and more, it left many more that will have to be addressed in the final season – but that’s not the only thing Stranger Things season 5 has to do.

The ending of Stranger Things season 4 left the audience with a major cliffhanger as Vecna (Jamie Campbell Bower) wasn’t defeated, and after attacking Max (Sadie Sink), his big plan was set into motion. The final scene of the season saw the Upside Down bleeding into the human world through massive cracks across Hawkins, painting a very somber future for Hawkins and, possibly, beyond. This, along with mysteries like Vecna and the origins of the Upside Down, other loose ends, and the introduction of new characters raised concerns about the main characters and their respective endings, but the producers seem to have their priorities in order.

    Gaten Matarazzo's Stranger Things Season 5 Pitch Is Exactly What The Show Needs  Although plot details about Stranger Things season 5 remain unknown, Gaten Matarazzo has an idea that is what the show's final season needs.

Stranger Things Season 5 Will Give Its Main Characters The Time They Deserve Stranger Things season 5’s producers know the main characters are the priority.

Stranger Things has its young cast that the audience has seen grow over the course of four seasons, a teenage cast, and an adult cast, all of them considered main characters as they have all had a key role in the biggest plots. Because of this, one of the biggest concerns about Stranger Things season 5 and the magnitude of it is that it won’t be able to give proper closure to every one of these characters. This concern grew when Linda Hamilton’s casting was announced and Amybeth McNulty was confirmed to return as Vickie, Robin’s crush.

The producers seem to have their priorities in order and know that the main cast should be given the time and attention they need.

While having Linda Hamilton on board and Vickie coming back is not bad, Stranger Things season 5 is at risk of either underdeveloping these characters or focusing too much on them and not giving the main characters the time they deserve. Luckily, the producers seem to have their priorities in order and know that the main cast should be given the time and attention they need. Speaking to People in February 2024, director and producer Shawn Levy said that the heart and soul of Stranger Things are its characters, and in this final season, they are “intact and are as central as spectacle”.

Given how big Stranger Things season 4 was and its massive cliffhanger ending, season 5 is expected to be as big or more, which is a risky move as the team behind the series could end up focusing more on making an event out of the season rather than on its quality. Levy’s words should ease these concerns as they show the producers are aware that the most important thing in Stranger Things season 5 is to wrap up the characters’ stories.

Stranger Things Season 5 Learned From Past Seasons’ Mistakes Stranger Things season 5 is already avoiding some past mistakes.

Shawn Levy isn’t the only one aware of what’s truly important in Stranger Things season 5. Speaking to IndieWire in 2022, series’ creators Matt and Ross Duffer spoke about introducing new characters in every season of Stranger Things and how they planned to break this pattern in the final season. Ross Duffer explained that they always want to make sure that new characters are an integral part of the narrative, and that any moment spent with a new character takes time away from those who have already been there. Matt Duffer added they were trying to resist adding new characters in season 5 so they can focus “on the OG characters”.

The Duffers seem to have learned from these past mistakes and are consciously looking to avoid them in Stranger Things ’ final season.

Over the course of four seasons, Stranger Things has introduced some very interesting characters, though most of them end up dead, as happened with Bob Newby and Eddie Muson. However, there have been complaints about there being too many new characters that, just like the Duffers said, only take the attention away from the main characters who have been around since season 1 (or season 2, as is Max’s case). The Duffers seem to have learned from these past mistakes and are consciously looking to avoid them in Stranger Things’ final season.

Stranger Things Must Find Balance Between Spectacle & Narrative Stranger Things has to be careful not to neglect certain elements.

Season 5 also has a lot of things to address and mysteries to solve in order to give a satisfying ending to the show.

As mentioned above, Stranger Things season 5 is expected to be a huge spectacle and event given how big season 4 was, the shocking reveals it made, and its cliffhanger ending – if season 5 went smaller, being the final season, it would most likely be seen as a disappointment, which would hurt the show’s legacy. Season 5 also has a lot of things to address and mysteries to solve in order to give a satisfying ending to the show, but in addition to all this, it also has to go back to its main characters to properly wrap up their stories.

With all these expectations and needs, the writers of Stranger Things season 5 have to find a balance between making a big spectacle and giving the main characters the time they deserve and proper closure, which even though is a big challenge, it shouldn’t be an impossible one.
