Joey Graziadeis Biggest Bachelor Season 28 Fear Revealed As His Search For Love Continues

SUMMARY

Joey Graziadei opens up about his fear of not finding the right person or ending in a way that doesn’t lead to his “forever person” on The Bachelor season 28. Joey reflects on the hectic and wild first night as The Bachelor, but is confident in the women and excited to get to know them more. Leading The Bachelor has been a different experience for Joey, as he grapples with the power to affect other people’s emotions and struggles to connect with some of the cast members.

Literature Review

The Bachelor season 28 star Joey Graziadei had one major fear heading into his time as the lead on the series, and revealed it in a recent episode.

The Bachelor season 28 star Joey Graziadei has been open and vulnerable in the past, and recently opened up about his biggest fear in taking on the role of a lifetime. As Joey’s journey on The Bachelor is just getting started, many have been trying to figure out what his time as the lead of the series will be like. For Joey, who finished as the runner-up on The Bachelorette season 20 with Charity Lawson, it has been difficult for him to open up about his feelings with the cameras present. After showing vulnerability throughout his time on The Bachelorette, seeing him on The Bachelor season 28 has been refreshing.

In an interview with People, Joey shared what he’s most fearful of in becoming The Bachelor. Joey explained that though his hope for his time as The Bachelor is to end with the love of his life, he fears that won’t be the case. “My biggest worry is just, truthfully, not making the right decision or finding the right person, or possibly just ending in a way that doesn’t end with my forever person,” Joey shared. “Truthfully, that’s a real fear of mine.” While it remains to be seen whether Joey will find his person, he’s on the right track.

Joey Graziadei’s “Glad” First Group Date Is Over

Joey explained that while he’s excited to be on his journey as The Bachelor, the early stages weren’t the easiest for him to get through. “Night one was hectic and wild, but I feel really confident about the women that are here and I’m just excited to get to know more about them,” Joey shared, adding that he was “glad to get [the first group date] out of the way.” Joey was inspired by the way he saw Charity hold herself during their season, explaining she “was very intentional, made everyone feel comfortable and open, so if I have even an ounce of that, I’m going to be grateful.”

For Joey, leading The Bachelor season 28 has been a completely different process for him than appearing on The Bachelorette season 20. He shared that though he’s happy to be in the driver’s seat, having the power to make or break other people’s emotions is a big deal for him. With Joey’s first impressions made and first roses having been handed out, he’s already starting to find himself more attuned to some of The Bachelor season 28 cast, while he’s struggling to get to know others. Joey’s time as the lead will move quickly, with just weeks until viewers know who, if anyone receives his final rose.

As Joey’s time as The Bachelor continues, things will get increasingly more dramatic for the group of women on the series. Joey’s choices will navigate which relationships continue and which crumble, but the women’s reactions could lead to bigger and brighter things within Bachelor Nation. While Joey can only wind up with one woman at the end of the season, how he navigates getting there will be the most fascinating ride viewers of The Bachelor can imagine.
