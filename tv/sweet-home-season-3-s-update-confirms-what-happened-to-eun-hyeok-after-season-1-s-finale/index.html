SUMMARY

Eun-hyeok, played by Lee Do-hyun, survived the destruction of the Green Home and is a major character in Sweet Home season 3. Eun-hyeok transformed into a monster and was in a cocoon during his absence, indicating a different transformation than what Hyun-su went through. There are hints that Eun-hyeok may become a villain in season 3, setting up a potential conflict between him and Hyun-su.

The most recent Sweet Home season 3 updates confirm what happened to Lee Do-hyun’s Eun-hyeok after season 1’s finale and tease what his role in the show will now be. The first season of Sweet Home, which was a massive hit and got the Netflix K-drama show renewed for two more seasons, ended with the destruction of the Green Home building. While Eun-hyeok did not die on screen, he stayed behind to make sure his friends could escape, resulting in his ultimate sacrifice. However, Sweet Home season 2’s ending revealed that Eun-hyeok was not dead after all.

The final seconds of Sweet Home 2 saw Eun-hyeok rising from the wreckages of the Green Home, a triumphant return that proved a lot of fan theories right. Lee Do-hyun’s character is back, and he is set to play a major role in season 3. Eun-hyeok’s return was shocking, and it raised some questions regarding what exactly happened to him during the events of the season 1 finale. The entire building was destroyed, to the point it would be impossible for any human to survive. With Eun-hyeok among the confirmed Sweet Home3 characters, some answers will finally be provided.

Sweet Home Season 3 Confirms Eun-hyeok Became A Monster In Season 1 Eun-hyeok was inside a cocoon

Netflix’s latest Sweet Home season 3 update, which was released alongside the platform’s 2024 K-content slate, confirms that Eun-hyeok had just come out of a cocoon at the end of season 2. Taking many by surprise, the Sweet Home season 2 finale revealed that Lee Do-hyun’s character was alive, with a twist. Eun-hyeok had no clothes on and was walking as if he had just woken up, suggesting he was no longer human. Earlier in the season, Eun-yu and the others encountered a woman from the Green Home in similar conditions, and she had monster powers.

Therefore, it is safe to say Eun-hyeok turned into a monster right before the destruction of the Green Home, which is how he survived the collapse of the building. Not only that, but Eun-hyeok also entered the cocoon stage of the transformation. This explains why he took so long to return – Eun-hyeok was inside a cocoon while his monster transformation progressed. Eun-hyeok becoming a monster had been a popular theory since the season 1 finale, during which blood came out of his nose a few minutes before the building was attacked.

Eun-hyeok Went Through A Different Transformation From Hyun-su Eun-hyeok could be even more powerful than Hyun-su

Toward the end of Sweet Home season 2, Dr. Lim encounters multiple monster cocoons and states that the creatures are evolving.

Hyun-su is no longer the only main Sweet Home character who has become a neo-human – Eun-hyeok is also a monster. However, there is one big difference between the two characters. Whereas Hyun-su once woke up and realized he had a monster alter ego, Eun-hyeok went through a cocoon transformation. Hyun-su never entered such a stage, and his transformation was a more “traditional” one. It is still unclear how and why the cocoon comes to be, but it appears to be the next stage of the monster’s evolution.

Toward the end of Sweet Home season 2, Dr. Lim encounters multiple monster cocoons and states that the creatures are evolving. As such, it can be assumed that these cocoons have only recently started to appear, and that they represent a much more advanced stage of the curse. If so, then Eun-hyeok could be even more powerful than Hyun-su, who is already an extremely skillful neo-human. Sweet Home could be setting up a fight between Eun-hyeok and Hyun-su, which would be tragic considering their friendship but would certainly lead to cool moments.

Will Lee Do-hyun’s Eun-hyeok Be A Villain In Sweet Home Season 3? Eun-hyeok’s return could be bittersweet

Hyun-su managed to control his monster powers and never became a villain, although he did have some scary moments. However, the same may not be the case for Eun-hyeok, whose return looked quite sinister. Lee Do-hyun’s character was smiling to the camera at the end of season 2 as if the show was teasing Eun-hyeok’s return as a villain. Additionally, the first official Sweet Home season 3 footage sees Eun-hyeok wearing a fancy, all-black suit while appearing in front of a soldier, teasing a possible antagonist role for the character.

As great as it is to have Eun-hyeok back, it would make a lot of sense for Sweet Home to subvert expectations and bring him back as a villain. The show may be setting up a war between humans and neo-humans, which could put characters like Eun-hyeok and Hyun-su on different sides of a confrontation.
