10 Invincible Scenes That Won t Look As Good In The Live-Action Movie

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Live-action Invincible movie faces challenges with choreographing fight scenes, restrictions in style and effects, and budget limitations. Invincible’s graphic depictions of violence and complex narrative make it compelling, with Mark being a non-typical superhero. Animation allows for realistic aerial and underwater scenes, as well as detailed transformations and battle sequences that would be difficult to replicate in live-action.

Literature Review

Though the prospect of a live-action adaptation of Invincible is exciting, many of the scenes lend themselves better the the animation medium.

Warning! Spoilers for Invincible season 1 and season 2 part 1, as well as descriptions of violence. The Invincible series has been lauded by comic-lovers and those new to the franchise alike, and part of this is due to how well the show translates scenes from the comics into animation, a feat that will be more difficult in live-action. A live-action film will face many challenges, including choreographing fight scenes, restrictions in style and effects, and the limitations that budget can have on the outcome of a film so reliant on otherworldly elements. Proving itself worthy of continuing the legacy of the story could be a difficult task for the movie. Following the story of Mark Grayson AKA Invincible (Steven Yeun), the son of Omni-Man (J.K. Simmons), a half-human-half-alien superhero grappling with the legacy of his father’s actions, Invincible is well-known for its graphic depictions of violence. In both the world of comic and adult animation, Invincible toes the line of what can be shown, and how many times Mark can be beaten down before he stops getting up. However, the complex nature of the narrative is what makes it so compelling. Mark isn’t a typical superhero, and the world he inhabits isn’t one where the good guys always win.

10 Invincible Vs Omni-Man Season 1, Episode 8, “Where I Really Come From”

The finale of season 1 doesn’t just level up every fight scene throughout the season but also packs an emotional punch that changes the course of the show forever. Omni-Man admits to Mark that his purpose on Earth is to subjugate the citizens for his people, the Viltrumites, so they can take over the planet. In a valiant attempt to stop him, Mark battles with his father and is beaten to a pulp. Having two powerful characters go toe to toe meant that the fight spans continents in the air and on the ground. In a valiant attempt to stop him, Mark battles with his father and is beaten to a pulp.

9 The Flaxan Invasion Season 1, Episode 2, “Here Goes Nothing”

Early on in Invincible, Mark goes through initial tests to see what kind of hero he’s going to be, and his first major battle with the Flaxans almost takes him out. No matter how many times he and his friends defeat them, they come back a few seconds later, smarter and stronger than ever. The battle decimates a city and requires an enormous number of Flaxans to descend on the city en masse. It’s a case where animation suits the storytelling well because of how much damage can be depicted without causing it in reality.

8 Flying / Aerial Action Which occurs in almost every episode

Several of the main characters in Invincible have the power to fly, and every Viltrumite does. Mark and Omni-Man spend some of their best fights and most emotional conversations in the sky, as do Atom Eve (Gillian Jacobs) and other prominent heroes. Though the aerodynamics of the characters’ flights aren’t always accurate to physics, they do hold a consistent logic to them, and make aerial scenes just as realistic as those on the ground. Making the use of a green screen as unobtrusive as possible is the only way the scene will carry the same weight.

7 Robot’s Transformation Season 1, Episode 7, “We Need To Talk”

Robot (Zachary Quinto) is a mysterious figure, until his true form is shown, and the audience realizes that the robot he inhabits is a tool for his physically weak body. However, when he meets Monster Girl (Grey DeLisle), he wants to have a physical body, so he can interact with her. To do this, he creates a machine that places his consciousness in a new body, and the visuals of when the transformation happens are fantastic. It’s the details of this moment that make it stand out so well, and make animation so effective in its portrayal.

6 Angstrom Levy’s Machine Season 2, Episode 1, “A Lesson for Your Next Life”

Angstrom Levy (Sterling K. Brown) is one of the major villains introduced in Invincible season 2, and even though few could compare to Omni-Man, his opening episode is memorable. He has the power to open portals to other dimensions and uses this to find every version of himself and put their knowledge into his brain. He does this by enlisting the Mauler Twins (Kevin Michael Richardson) to create a complex machine that will do the work for him. Of course, Mark comes along to stop the Twins, leading to an explosion and the plan going wrong. This leaves Angstrom with too much information for his brain to process and turns him against Mark forever. What makes the scene so much better in animation is how many versions there are of Angstrom that all have their own unique design and are seen by the audience. Each one was carefully constructed and placed in the frame, and doing this in live-action will take even more time and money, and not do justice to the original scene.

5 Underwater Atlantis Fight Season 2, Episode 2, “Episode 2”

Much like the aerial scenes, and those in space, shooting underwater can pose just as much of a challenge for live-action. Especially if the battle is as big as the one Mark had with the giant sea monster in Atlantis. Mark defies the laws of gravity daily, but even he has some limits underwater. It’s not only the fight that’s visually stimulating in this episode but also the grandeur of Atlantis. Invincible is well-known for using its animation to make even less significant set pieces just as detailed and beautiful as the rest of the show.

4 The Guardian’s Death Season 1, Episode 1, “It’s About Time”

Though Omni-Man killed the Guardians in the pilot, The Immortal (Ross Marquand) returns and there’s one intriguing story tease Invincible season 2 has because of him. When Omni-Man kills the Guardians, it’s a gruesome and drawn-out scene, and though The Immortal comes back, the others aren’t so lucky. It’s an excellent introduction to Omni-Man and his incredible power, as well as a great setup for questioning his motives throughout season 1. Without an effective portrayal of this scene, Omni-Man’s abilities and his characterization will take a hit.

3 The Battle Beast Season 1, Episode 5, “That Actually Hurt”

Though it’s short, the Battle Beast sequence is significant for Mark’s character development and is a brutal awakening to the limits of his powers. Mark considers himself practically invincible, but season 1 is all about him learning how vulnerable he is. The emotional impact of Battle Beast’s beating, as well as the fact that he must be saved by his father, are represented in the fight’s brutality. Incorporating plenty of gore without crossing a line will be a challenge for the film, as the difference between violence in animation and violence in real life can be visceral for audiences.

2 Monster Girl Transformations, Fighting, Practical Effects vs. Digital

Both Robot and Monster Girl are supposed to be children in Invincible season 2 because of Robot’s transformation, and the fact that Monster Girl ages backwards every time she transforms. It’s her transformation that poses one of the bigger problems for the series. Though the live-action film will have to deal with many creatures and monsters, Monster Girl is a bigger player. Keeping her monster form consistent, and looking real enough to interact with the other characters in scenes that are fast-paced and full of action will be important. Other superhero films have fallen flat in this area because of the challenge that’s posed by making a hero look like a realistic but emotionally expressive monster. Overall, the Monster Girl problem is representative of the bigger issue of how the creatures will look in general. The balance between practical effects and the use of CGI needs to be a conscious choice, as too much of either will spoil the look of the film.

1 Viltrumites vs. Allen Season 2, Episode 3, “This Missive, This Machination!”

Allen the Alien (Seth Rogen) met Mark in season 1 when he mistakenly came to test Earth’s defenses against an alien invasion, but Allen comes up against a much bigger threat in season 2. A trio of Viltrumites comes to interrogate him about what happened between Mark and Omni-Man, but Allen’s duty in life is to stop the Viltrumites, so he refuses to tell them anything. While Allen hasn’t been the biggest part of Invincible so far, his actions in this episode are not only valiant but defy gravity as the fight occurs in space. Having brief forays into space is one thing, but recreating this fantastic battle while considering the effects of zero gravity and the difference between Allen and the Viltrumites’ fighting style will be interesting to see in live-action. There are other moments, before this, when Mark or his father are in space, and there are sure to be more as the series progresses. Outer-space effects can be hit or miss for many films, especially those in the superhero genre, so making the setting as stylized as possible will be the best route for the movie. invincible
