House Of The Dragon 9 Genius Moments Of Foreshadowing That Will Be Important In Season 2 & Beyond

SUMMARY

Literature Review

House of the Dragon’s overarching narrative is mapped out in the book Fire & Blood, and several season 1 moments foreshadow key upcoming events.

SPOILERS AHEAD for Fire & Blood, the book House of the Dragon is based on!HBO’s House of the Dragon is set to be a sprawling television epic, and season 1 prepared audiences for the long haul with several genius moments of foreshadowing. Based on George R.R. Martin’s novel Fire & Blood, the series is set hundreds of years before the events of Game of Thrones. The first season established the show’s characters and premise, building up toward a bloody civil war between rival factions of the Targaryen dynasty, the blacks and the greens, represented by Rhaenyra Targaryen and Aegon II Targaryen, respectively. Unlike Game of Thrones, which was only partially complete throughout its duration, the overarching narrative of House of the Dragon is already mapped out, and book readers know what will happen. With that in mind, season 1 offered many moments of on-the-nose foreshadowing, leaving hints for those who know what to expect. While the TV series may not be an exact adaptation of its source material, several clever moments hint at key events to come. House of the Dragon season 2 will be released in Summer of 2024.

9 Aemond Says He Should Be King Season 1, Episode 9

Aemond and Aegon, the sons of Alicent Hightower and Viserys Targaryen, are two of the most fascinating characters introduced late in House of the Dragon season 1. Aegon, the heir to the Iron Throne, is notably ignorant, bratty, and immature, while Aemond is ambitious and determined to defend his house. In a scene with Criston Cole, Aemond suggests that he’s the one who deserves to be king. This foreshadows an important arc where Aemond will rule the Seven Kingdoms in his brother’s stead after a devastating injury.

8 “The Last Ring Has No Legs At All” Season 1, Episode 6

Helaena Targaryen’s dialogue is filled with important hints at future events, as she spews prophetic comments in almost every scene. In season 1, episode 6, she plays with a bug, saying, “The last ring has no legs at all.” This can be interpreted in different ways. It can be tied to Game of Thrones, where Bran Stark, the last known king of Westeros, lost the use of his legs in season 1. Or, in the House of the Dragon era, it could refer to her brother and husband, Aegon II, whose legs will be shattered, falling off his dragon in the Battle of Rook’s Rest.

7 Blood & Cheese Season 1, Episode 5

There are several setups and clues to Blood & Cheese in House of the Dragon season 1. Blood and Cheese are characters that audiences will quickly become acquainted with in season 2, in what should be one of the most gruesome, violent scenes in the Game of Thrones TV universe. This refers to a pair of assassins who Daemon Targaryen hires to kill one of Aegon II’s children in response to the murder of Lucerys Velaryon at the end of season 1. In both episode 4 and episode 9, characters are shown using secret tunnels under the Red Keep that lead into the city. Blood and Cheese will use these tunnels to enter the palace in season 2. In season 1, episode 5, Criston Cole murders Joffrey Lonmouth, and shortly after, a rat can be seen by a pool of blood. This is a direct reference to the names Blood and Cheese. Lastly, Mysaria is a recurring character in season 1, and her return late in the season reveals that she now operates a spy network in King’s Landing, which Daemon will use to hire the assassins.

6 A Fortune-Teller Predicts Rhaenyra’s Death Season 1, Episode 4

During her walk around King’s Landing with Daemon in episode 4, Rhaenyra is briefly asked by a fortune teller if she wants to know her death. To someone who hasn’t read Fire & Blood, the moment is rather easy to miss. For book readers, it’s incredibly on-the-nose foreshadowing. Immediately after the question is asked, the scene cuts to an ornamental dragon breathing fire. This refers to Rhaenyra’s death by execution from Aegon II’s dragon, Sunfyre. Joffrey Baratheon also mentions this death in Game of Thrones, so TV fans who paid close attention have already had it spoiled.

5 Helaena Cries In Front Of A Window Season 1, Episode 4

There’s a clever moment of foreshadowing the first time audiences are introduced to Helaena Targaryen. Shortly after Alicent’s marriage to Viserys, she gives birth to Helaena, who can be seen as a baby in episode 4. In the scene, Alicent holds her next to a window as she cries, hinting at Helaena’s tragic fate. In Fire & Blood, Helaena dies by suicide, jumping out her window in the Red Keep. Her death is met by an uproar from King’s Landing, as the people demand justice for the beloved queen.

4 Daemon And Aemond’s Rivalry Season 1, Episode 8

Daemon is the most powerful House of the Dragon character heading into season 2, but he’s neck and neck with his nephew Aemond. Aemond grows prominent late in season 1, developing into a perfect foil for Daemon as a skilled swordsman and dragonrider. After the dinner scene in episode 8 goes ary, it almost seems Daemon will have a word with Aemond, but the series leaves it be. Their rivalry will continue to grow throughout the war until, eventually, the pair have a fatal duel on dragonback, claiming the lives of both Targaryens and their dragons.

3 Rhaenyra Promises To Return On Dragonback Season 1, Episode 8

In the calm before the storm during episode 8, Rhaenyra and Alicent seem to be finally rekindling their long-lost friendship, bonding over their love for Viserys and each other. Rhaenyra has to return to Dragonstone, but she promises to return to King’s Landing on dragonback after giving birth. This is cleverly worded, as the next time Rhaenyra visits King’s Landing, it will be on dragonback, though it won’t be on friendly terms as the scene implies. Rhaenyra will take the capital and the Iron Throne back, though only for half a year.

2 Alicent Confuses Arryk And Erryk Season 1, Episode 8

Late in House of the Dragon season 1, audiences are introduced to twin Kingsguard members Arryk and Erryk Cargyll. In episode 8, Alicent mistakes one for the other, and while this summarizes the audience’s confusion about the identical knights, it’s also a clever moment of foreshadowing. Finding themselves on opposite sides in the war, Arryk will be sent to impersonate his brother as an assassin to kill Rhaenyra but will instead stumble upon Erryk. As seen in the House of the Dragon season 2 trailer, Arryk and Erryk will have a duel that claims both of their lives.

1 Rhaenys Escapes The Dragonpit Season 1, Episode 9

To escape King’s Landing and support the Blacks in House of the Dragon season 1, Rhaenys Targaryen takes her dragon, Meleys, and escapes from the Dragonpit. This scene mirrors a later event from the Dance of the Dragons called the “Storming of the Dragonpit,” where a rioting mob will assault the pit and kill five dragons.
