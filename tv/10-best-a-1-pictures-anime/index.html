10 Best A-1 Pictures Anime

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2400 words 11 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

A-1 Pictures has created numerous beloved and iconic anime titles since its inception in 2005, making it a favorite among fans. While there may be some forgettable titles, the studio’s iconic anime generally outweigh the less interesting ones, Anime like Solo Leveling , Silver Spoon , and ERASED showcase the studio’s ability to adapt varied source material and create captivating experiences.

Literature Review

A-1 Pictures is a beloved studio with a rich history that has delivered dozens of influential anime, but these are truly the cream of the crop.

Thanks to the studio’s rich history and passionate creatives, most anime fans would count at least one of the best A-1 Pictures anime among their favorites. Even in a medium where so many anime studios are vying for the top, it is easy to understand why A-1 Pictures holds a special place in many fans’ hearts, having created countless beloved titles since its inception in 2005. Naturally, the more titles a studio produces, the more likely there is to be a variance in quality and originality. After all, even the medium’s most talented and creative people are unable to deliver numerous successful projects continuously. As such, it would be reasonable to say that A-1 Pictures has had its fair share of forgettable titles, but they are undoubtedly outweighed by the numerous iconic anime that the studio is known for, which cover all genres and themes, providing something for everyone.

10 Solo Leveling (2024) Based on the Manhwa by Hyeon-Gun & Seong-Rak Jang

When the enigmatic Gate suddenly appeared one day, connecting the human world with magical dungeons inhabited by dangerous monsters, the world quickly realized that it was not only a threat, but also an opportunity. To fight against the otherworldly beasts, ordinary people received extraordinary abilities, allowing them to become hunters. However, not all hunters are equal, with Jin-Woo Sung being known as the world’s weakest hunter. Yet, after facing his demise in a dungeon overrun with monsters, he is selected by the System and gains unlimited potential to grow. Although this series is currently airing, meaning that its quality cannot yet be concluded upon, it would seem that, based on preliminary reviews, Solo Leveling has the potential to rival some of the medium’s most popular titles. Its world and premise may be somewhat generic, but fans of power fantasy stories are bound to enjoy the exhilarating character development and beautifully animated, vibrant action scenes. Moreover, it seems to not only faithfully adapt the beloved source material, but also elevate the experience, thanks to the anime medium’s unique strengths. Watch on Crunchyroll

9 Silver Spoon (2013) Based on the Manga by Hiromu Arakawa

Stressed by his parents’ expectations and tired of having to accommodate their preferences, Yugo Hachiken decides to get away from his family by enrolling in Ooezo Agricultural High School, a boarding school in the Hokkaido countryside. Initially convinced that he would perform well just about anywhere, as he is confident in his learning abilities and hard-working nature, Yugo soon finds himself far behind his colleagues, who are more used to farming life. Despite being a fish out of water, Yugo is determined to keep up with his friends and find his own goals in life. Following a flawed yet realistic and relatable protagonist, Silver Spoon tells a compelling tale of self-discovery. Thanks to its focus on the agricultural industry and the depth it goes into exploring the farming world, it could have easily risked exhausting viewers with the details. Instead, its well-researched setting does nothing but to further bring the interesting characters to life, providing an engaging, heartwarming experience that shows how people can learn from overcoming difficulties and facing new situations.

Hiromu Arakawa, who created Silver Spoon, is perhaps best known as the author of Fullmetal Alchemist, which is heralded as one of the most influential shōnen titles.

Watch on Crunchyroll

8 ERASED (2016) Based on the Manga by Kei Sanbe

Satoru Fujinuma is a struggling 29-year-old manga artist, working hard to leave his mark on the industry. While he may appear ordinary on the surface, he secretly possesses a supernatural ability; whenever tragic disasters take place, he is thrust back in time by a few minutes. Using the ability he calls “Revival”, he has prevented countless catastrophes throughout his life, saving strangers from lethal accidents. But one day, after he is wrongfully accused of murder, he is sent back into the distant past, where he seeks to save his grade-school classmate Kayo Hinazuki and solve the mystery behind her disappearance. ERASED quickly became one of the most popular titles in the mystery genre, thanks to its supernatural twist and masterful storytelling. The series keeps viewers hooked from the beginning to the end, always revealing further details about the underlying mystery at the perfect time. Furthermore, it is also able to tackle serious themes, such as abuse, without making the overall experience overwhelmingly depressing. All in all, ERASED is easily one of the best thriller anime, having rightfully earned its reputation as a captivating, one-of-a-kind drama. Watch on Hulu

7 Lycoris Recoil (2022) Original Series Written by Asaura & Directed by Shingo Adachi

Direct Attack is a secret organization, working behind the scenes to maintain peace and public safety in Japan. As part of the “Lycoris” program, orphaned girls are raised to become formidable assassins, taking out anyone that could disrupt national safety, and dealing with cases that range from arms trafficking to terrorism. After Takina Inoue, an exceptional Lycoris who values efficiency above all, disobeys orders, she is forced to transfer to LycoReco, an unusual café. Here, she meets Chisato Nishikigi, a prodigy with a unique outlook on life. As a winner of Crunchyroll’s 2023 anime awards, Lycoris Recoil provides an incredibly original and entertaining experience. Although action fans are likely to be drawn in by the numerous high-octane fighting scenes, there is a lot more to this title than meets the eye. Another important appeal consists of the surprisingly well-developed characters, whose personalities and interactions contribute significantly to the enjoyment of the series. Last but not least, it also doesn’t hesitate to tackle thought-provoking moral dilemmas in a tactful and intriguing manner. Watch on Crunchyroll

6 Kaguya-sama: Love is War (2019) Based on the Manga by Aka Akasaka

Shuchiin Academy is a prestigious private school, attended only by the cream of the crop, the rich and the nobles. At the top of the student body lie its president, Miyuki Shirogane, and vice president, Kaguya Shinomiya, two highly respected students who excel in all academic fields. However, despite having developed romantic feelings for each other, neither of them is willing to be the first to admit it. In a battle of pride and love, Miyuki and Kaguya constantly try to deceive the other into confessing, resulting in a captivating psychological war. Innovating upon a lighthearted and often predictable type of story that rarely stays with viewers past the closing scenes, Kaguya-sama: Love is War is a modern rom-com classic that leverages the best attributes of its genre. The charming ongoing conflict between Miyuki and Kaguya may define the title, but the supporting cast is no less memorable, featuring a variety of colorful and chaotic characters that fans can’t help but love. Naturally, the vibrant animation perfectly matches the atmosphere, and the music provides some of the medium’s catchiest tunes. Aka Akasaka, who created Kaguya-sama: Love is War, is also known as the author behind the story of [Oshi No Ko], a brilliant series with an equally fantastic anime adaptation.

Watch on Crunchyroll

5 Your Lie in April (2014) Based on the Manga by Naoshi Arakawa

Once considered a prodigy that all child musicians looked up to, Kousei Arima exited the scene after his mother passed away, which resulted in him no longer being able to hear the sound of his own piano. Even though his world has lost much of its color, Kousei lives a comfortable, yet uneventful life with his beloved friends. But everything changes when he encounters Kaori Miyazono, a free-spirited violinist whose inspiring and unorthodox performance reignites his passion for music, and reminds him of the beauty of life. Widely recognized as one of the best tragic romances in anime, Your Lie in April follows an incredibly emotional journey of love and growth. Even though some of its developments are easily predictable, the sincerity and innocence of each scene allow the series to remain charming and compelling from its first episode to the final one. The well-developed characters bring this moving story to life, and the stunning visuals and awe-inspiring soundtrack complete the experience splendidly. Watch on Crunchyroll

4 86 Eighty-Six (2021) Based on the Light Novel by Asato Asato & Shirabi

To fight the “Legion”, autonomous drones of the Empire of Giad, the Republic of San Magnolia developed their own unmanned army, known as the “Juggernaut”. However, unbeknownst to the public at large, these drones are piloted by the Eighty-sixers, humans who were forced to renounce their citizenship and fight for the country, while the ignorant silver-haired Alba live peaceful and luxurious lives behind protective walls. When Vladilena Milizé becomes the new Handler of Spearhead Squadron, she seeks to connect with them in ways that others never did. Not too dissimilar from industry giants like Attack on Titan, 86 Eighty-Six explores a unique world where survival is not guaranteed, and the stakes are always high. From the intriguing, well-developed world, to the complex story, and fantastic characters, everything about this title is simply outstanding. Featuring production values that are likewise impressive, resulting in breathtaking visuals and a remarkable soundtrack, 86 Eighty-Six is not only a brilliant mecha anime, but also a beautiful title that any anime fan is likely to appreciate. Watch on Crunchyroll

3 Anohana: The Flower We Saw That Day (2011) Original Series Written by Mari Okada & Directed by Tatsuyuki Nagai

Jinta Yadomi used to be inseparable from his group of childhood friends, always hanging out and playing around together. But after Meiko Honma’s tragic death, the rest of the group began to distance themselves from each other, unable to accept the idea of being without their dear friend. When Menma suddenly shows up before Jinta, asking him about a forgotten wish, he dismisses her as a mere illusion. Later on, however, he begins to think that it might be her ghost following him, and finds himself forced to face the past that he had chosen to forget. Widely recognized as one of the saddest anime, Anohana: The Flower We Saw That Day tells a tale emotional enough to move even the most stoic anime fans. Exploring profound and delicate themes such as overcoming grief and dealing with loss, with a depth that is rarely encountered in the medium, this remarkable title has reached many hearts and inspired viewers to ponder the meaning of life. The relatable characters and grounded setting only further bring the story to life, and the memorable soundtrack alone is often enough to invoke sadness and bring fans to tears. The creative trio behind Anohana: The Flower We Saw That Day, formed of screenwriter Mari Okada, director Tatsuyuki Nagai, and artist Masayoshi Tanaka, who go by the name of Super Peace Busters, are also behind The Anthem of the Heart, Her Blue Sky, and the upcoming Fureru.

Watch on Crunchyroll

2 Space Brothers (2012) Based on the Manga by Chuuya Koyama

Impressed and inspired by what they believe to be a UFO, brothers Hibito and Mutta Nanba find their shared aspiration, of becoming astronauts and exploring outer space. Years later, only Hibito managed to be where they pictured themselves being at that point, with Mutta working for an automotive company instead. But after a work incident causes him to lose his job, Mutta tries once again to take a step towards his childhood dream. The acceptance of his application marks only the beginning of an ambitious journey full of hurdles and obstacles. Space Brothers is one of the most inspiring and grounded titles of all time, seeking to prove that it’s never too late to follow one’s dreams, even after facing numerous setbacks that may make it seem impossible. Yet, while the protagonist’s journey represents the focus of the series, the supporting cast isn’t neglected either, with even minor characters proving equally interesting and well-developed, thus adding to the enjoyability of the story. Moreover, by adding a good amount of clever comedy into the mix, the series becomes that much more memorable.

1 From the New World (2012) Based on the Novel by Yuusuke Kishi

In a world redefined by the sudden awakening of psychic powers, countless wars and fights have caused great destruction. Seeking to end the conflicts and maintain a certain level of peace, psychic users have decided to isolate themselves from the rest, living in towns such as Kamisu 66, which serves as the primary location for the story. Here, a group of kids, who are just starting to unlock their powers, are about to learn that their supposed utopia may be no more than an illusion, with the dark secrets of society being intentionally obscured by their elders. From the New World tells a riveting tale, set in a world unlike any other, the atmosphere of which has yet to be replicated in the medium. Using unique supernatural elements to great effect, and leveraging interesting and creative twists, the series tactfully explores a variety of heavy themes that range from inherent human greed to discrimination. Thanks to its complex story, intricate world, and realistic characters, this original and thought-provoking title deserves to be remembered as one of the best A-1 Pictures anime. Watch on Crunchyroll
