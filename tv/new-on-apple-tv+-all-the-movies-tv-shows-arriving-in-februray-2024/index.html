SUMMARY

Apple TV+ offers exclusive original content, positioning itself as an alternative to Netflix with star-studded shows like Ted Lasso and The Morning Show. New releases in February 2024 include the historical series “The New Look,” the sports documentary “The Dynasty: New England Patriots,” and the soccer documentary “Messi’s World Cup: The Rise of a Legend.” Apple TV+ continues its lineup with the sci-fi movie “Constellation” and ongoing shows like “Masters of the Air” and “Criminal Record.”

Literature Review

Known for its award-winning original content, the Apple TV+ catalog grows from month to month. Here’s everything new on Apple TV+ in January 2024.

Apple TV+, is known for its award-winning original movies and shows, and there are plenty of new movies and TV shows dropping on the platform in February 2024. Since its launch, Apple TV+ has positioned itself as an alternative to streamers like Netflix by offering exclusive original content not found anywhere else. Unlike other streaming services, the Apple TV+ catalog remains mostly constant with very little departing every month. The streamer frequently adds new high-profile movies and shows that have helped make Apple TV+ one of the more prestigious entertainment platforms.

Though not every original series or movie is a hit, Apple TV+ has struck gold on several occasions with such shows as Ted Lasso, The Morning Show, and Severance. The streaming service successfully diversified into the worlds of children’s programming and sports as well. While it might not be a massive source of content like Netflix and Hulu, the best TV shows on Apple TV+ are star-studded affairs that rival the biggest budget network programs. Each month sees the arrival of exciting new content for Apple TV+, and February 2024 is no exception.

For more on the movies and TV shows coming to other streaming platforms, check out what’s new on Hulu and new on Max.

The New Look February 14, 2024

The New Look BiographyHistoryDrama Release Date February 14, 2024 Cast Glenn , Ben Mendelsohn , Juliette Binoche , Maisie Williams , John Malkovich , Emily Mortimer , Claes Bang Seasons 1

Premiering on Apple TV+ on February 14, 2024, the new historical series The New Look takes viewers back to World War II. The series takes place in Nazi-occupied Paris and focuses on the rise of Christian Dior and his groundbreaking influence in the world of fashion design. However, on top of the horrors of war during those years, a more personal story is told about Dior’s intense rivalry with another prominent fashion designer in Paris - Coco Chanel. While it might seem superficial, it is the rise of fashion that helped Paris regain its identity.

Ben Mendelsohn (Talos from the MCU’s Captain Marvel) stars as Christian Dior while Academy Award winner Juliette Binoche (The English Patient) stars as Coco Chanel. The series also stars Maisie Williams (Game of Thrones) as Catherine Dior, John Malkovich (Space Force) as Lucien Lelong, Emily Mortimer (Mary Poppins Returns) as Elsa Lombardi, and Glenn (Damages) as Carmel Snow. The New Look will feature 10 episodes, with the first three dropping on February 14 and the finale arriving on April 3.

The Dynasty: New England Patriots February 16, 2024

Apple TV+ has a new sports-themed documentary coming in February 2024 with The Dynasty: New England Patriots. Helmed by Tiger director Matthew Hamachek, the documentary shows the rise of the Patriots in the NFL, from Tom Brady’s arrival to the team to its 20-year run with multiple Super Bowl appearances and several championship wins. The documentary is a 10-part series, and it appears that it will look at everything about the Patriots dynasty, from the good to the very bad.

This includes controversies like “Deflate-Gate” as well as the murder trial and conviction of star tight end Aaron Hernandez. It also looks like it will delve into whether this was Bill Belichick’s team or if it was Tom Brady who truly led them to multiple championships. Based on the bestselling book by Jeff Benedict, the documentary talks to everyone from Belichick and Brady to owner Robert Kraft and several players from throughout the years.

Messi’s World Cup: The Rise Of A Legend February 21, 2024

A second Apple TV+ sports documentary is also arriving in February 2024 with Messi’s World Cup: The Rise of a Legend. This is shorter than the Patriots’ documentary, checking in at just four parts, but it focuses more on the positive moments that saw Lionel Messi rise to the top of international soccer. This offers a behind-the-scenes look into the life and career of Messi, one of the best soccer players in history, and it shows him achieving the greatest success in his sport.

The feature will look at his five FIFA World Cup appearances and his FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 win. That win is looked at in soccer as one of the best finals in history. The documentary will also have Messi describing his journey and greatest moments in his own words, with the feature filmed in Paris, Qatar, and Argentina.

Constellation February 21, 2024

Constellation Sci-FiThriller Release Date February 21, 2024 Cast Noomi Rapace , Jonathan Banks , James D’Arcy , Lenn Kudrjawizki , William Catlett , barbara sukowa , Carole Weyers Seasons 1

Constellation is a sci-fi movie starring Noomi Rapace (Prometheus) as Jo, an astronaut who returns to Earth after a disaster in space — only to discover that key pieces of her life seem to be missing. The movie is a drama about how this woman has to piece together what she lost, and figure out the truth and hidden history of space travel so she can regain what she lost in her life away from Earth.

Created by Peter Harness (Wallander), Constellation seems like it deals with a myriad of themes, from the trauma faced by astronauts after a near-death experience to possible interstellar travel to alternate worlds. On top of Rapace, the series also stars James D’Arcy (Oppenheimer) and Jonathan Banks (Breaking Bad). It also features twins Rosie and Davina Coleman as Alice, Jo’s daughter. The series has eight episodes and will air its finale on March 27.

Continuing Shows In February 2024 On Apple TV+ Masters Of The Air Ends On March 15

Masters of the Air Release Date January 26, 2024 Seasons 1

A new WW2 series premiered on Apple TV+ in January 2024. Masters of the Air has some incredibly prestigious names attached to it, both in the cast and behind the scenes. Steven Spielberg and Tom Hanks, who previously teamed up for Saving Private Ryan, Band of Brothers, and The Pacific, are bringing the miniseries to Apple TV+. Also, Cary Joji Fukunaga (True Detective) directed the first four episodes. It also has some star power on the screen, with Golden Globes winner Austin Butler (Elvis) and Oscar-nominee Barry Keoghan (The Banshees of Inisherin) in the lead roles.

The new Apple TV+ WW2 drama is about the 100th Bomb Group of the United States Air Force (aka, The Bloody Hundredth) as they battle the Nazis in World War II. Masters of the Air premieres its first episodes on January 26, 2024, and the finale will air on March 15. There will be a total of nine episodes in the first season of the war series on Apple TV+.

Criminal Record Ends On February 23

Criminal Record Not Yet RatedCrimeDramaThriller Release Date January 12, 2024 Cast Peter Capaldi , Cush Jumbo , Stephen Campbell Moore , Charlie Creed-Miles , Shaun Dooley , Dionne Brown Seasons 1

Doctor Who star Peter Capaldi stars in the crime thriller Criminal Record, a movie that paints a picture of a former police investigation gone bad. Capaldi is Detective Chief Inspector Daniel Hegarty, a veteran police detective who earned a confession in an old murder case. However, when a young detective named Detective Sergeant June Lenker (Cush Jumbo) learns that the man who confessed might be innocent, she sets her sights on Cush, as she tries to discover the truth behind the original murder case.

Taking place in Britain, the two battle it out while dealing with race and the police failing to do their jobs right in the country. Criminal Record is made by Paul Rutman, who previously made Vera and Indian Summer. There are eight episodes in the first season, which premiered on January 12 and wraps up on February 23 with its finale.
