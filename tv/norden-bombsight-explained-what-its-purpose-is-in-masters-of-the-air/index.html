Norden Bombsight Explained What Its Purpose Is In Masters Of The Air

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1000 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

“Masters of the Air” brings attention to the lesser-known Norden Bombsight, a highly accurate weapon used during World War II. The Norden Bombsight revolutionized bombing accuracy by directly measuring a bomb’s ground speed and direction, allowing for quicker and more precise attacks. Despite its limitations at high altitudes, the Norden Bombsight played a significant role in the United States’ day bombing strategy during the war and continued to be used in subsequent conflicts.

Literature Review

Apple TV+’s Masters of the Air portrays many real aspects and details from World War II, from B-17 bombers to technology like the Norden Bombsight.

As a war drama, it is no surprise that Apple TV+’s Masters of the Air provides sneak peeks of various real-life details from World War II, including a lesser-known weapon called the Norden Bombsight. Masters of the Air is a war drama created by famous duo Tom Hanks and Steven Spielberg, who previously worked on projects such as 2000’s Band of Brothers and 2010’s The Pacific. The series, based on the book “Masters of the Air: America’s Bomber Boys Who Fought the Air War Against Nazi Germany” by Donald L. Miller, tells the true story of the 100th Bombardment Group.

When watching Masters of the Air, it is easy to get sucked into the story and Masters of the Air’s characters, but the war setting is just as enthralling. This is because the series successfully adds important backstory and context through Major Harry Crosby’s narration so that audiences not only understand the stakes of the story, but also the real-life consequences of the war. For example, Masters of the Air offers specific insight into the 100th’s war strategy, their B-17 bombers, and the technology the show refers to as “one of the greatest secret weapons in the war”: the Norden Bombsight.

What The Norden Bombsight Actually Is

Though it makes an appearance in Masters of the Air, the Norden Bombsight is a real piece of equipment used by the United States Air Force during World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam War. First and foremost, a bombsight is a device that allows the military to drop bombs with accuracy. This type of technology has been around as far back as World War I. However, the Norden Bombsight was a new model that provided much more precision than any other bombsight had ever given before, which explains why it was so important during World War II.

Unlike bombsights of the past, the Norden Bombsight had the ability to almost directly measure a bomb’s ground speed and direction. Previously, a bombsight user would need to go through various manual steps to get this type of information, wasting precious time. Therefore, using the Norden Bombsight meant that the U.S. Army Air Forces were able to make quicker and more direct attacks on targets. Because of the weapon’s accuracy, the Norden Bombsight was partially kept a secret well into the war, and though it often performed less well than intended, its legacy lived on.

How The U.S. Used The Norden Bombsight In World War 2

What made the Norden Bombsight so important for the United States was that the technology was especially good for day bombing, and that was one of America’s main strategies during the war. While the United Kingdom completed nearly all of their air assaults in the dark of the night, the United States participated in the much riskier day bombing. Day bombing allowed the airmen to have better visibility of their targets, yet made them vulnerable to counterattacks. With the light of day and the Norden Bombsight on their side, the U.S. was better equipped to carry out successful missions.

In Masters of the Air episode 2, members of the 100th are teased by British RAF soldiers for their use of daylight bombing.

However, as previously mentioned, the Norden Bombsight did not always function as well as hoped. Although the technology was supposed to be known for its accuracy, the device rarely succeeded at being exact due to high altitudes during missions. Therefore, the U.S. Army Air Forces implemented various changes to help the Norden Bombsight be more helpful, such as flying in new positions such as the “combat box” formation. Additionally, attempts at precision bombing were halted in place of area bombing, which ended up being more precise.

The Norden Bombsight After Masters Of The Air

The Norden Bombsight was used in two wars after World War II, however, this was not really out of a desire to use them, but its necessity. Following World War II, precise bombsights like the Norden Bombsight were no longer a hot commodity because nuclear power quickly took center stage, and atomic bombs needed far less accuracy to be effective. However, the Norden Bombsight couldn’t retire fully. During the Korean and Vietnam wars, old bomber planes were put into use with their Norden Bombsights still in them. Therefore, they were in use all the way until the 1970s approximately.

In the end, the Norden Bombsight had a fantastic reputation, as noted by Masters of the Air, but was not nearly so successful in real life. Though the technology was designed for precision and accuracy, altitude and cloud cover were factors that made it difficult for the Norden Bombsight to meet expectations. Despite this, the Norden Bombsight is still a vital piece of technology from World War II, and its legacy appearing in Masters of the Air makes complete sense.
