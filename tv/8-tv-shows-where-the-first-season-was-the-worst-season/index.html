8 TV Shows Where The First Season Was The Worst Season

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The first season of a TV show may not reach the same heights as later seasons, but it can still improve and return for season 2 better than ever. Shifting the dynamics and focus of a show’s characters can greatly improve its quality and garner more viewer support. The production value of a TV show can increase as its ratings improve, leading to a better overall product.

Literature Review

When a TV series has a disappointing first season, it’s up to the rest of the show to increase the quality and ensure every other season is amazing.

Creating a successful first season of a television show is a delicate balance and many series that go on to achieve high acclaim start at a low point in season 1. The first season might not necessarily be bad, but when compared to the rest of the series, it doesn’t reach the same heights. If season 1 is completely different from the remaining seasons, it’s not uncommon that viewers will skip over it entirely and start with season 2. While the pilot of a TV show must capture the viewer’s attention and introduce the world of the story, the rest of season 1 has the harder job of establishing the pace of the show and keeping the audience watching. If a show has garnered enough attention to be picked up for a second season, the writers and creators have much more room to play with the premise and experiment with tone. Additionally, if the ratings increase in the later seasons, that means the overall production value will go up and lend itself to the creation of a better product.

15 Disappointing TV Shows That Turned Things Around In Season 2 A lot rides on the first season of a TV show, but even if it doesn’t live up to the hype, it can return for season 2 better than ever.

8 Parks And Recreation (2009 - 2015) Downtrodden public servants try to hold out hope for the future

Parks and Recreation
Sitcom Comedy Mockumentary

Release Date April 9, 2009 Cast Amy Poehler , Nick Offerman , ​Chris Pratt2 , Aubrey Plaza , Adam Scott , Rashida Jones , Retta , Jim O’Heir , Aziz Ansari , Rob Lowe Seasons 7 Story By Michael Schur, Michael offerman Streaming Service(s) fuboTV , Peacock Directors Michael Schur

Today, Parks and Recreation is defined by the unflappable optimism of protagonist Leslie Knope (Amy Poehler), but in the first season, her can-do attitude was treated with derision rather than admiration. Season 1 of the show was modeled closely after The Office, which famously poked fun at Michael Scott (Steve Carrel), and made jokes at the expense of its characters. This was the wrong approach for a show like Parks and Recreation and the creators soon realized this. If the other characters weren’t rooting for Leslie, then the audience wasn’t going to either, so in season 2 the group dynamic shifted. The butt of the joke became the town and the ridiculous antics the residents got up to, as well as the outlandish lengths Leslie would go to for the people she represented. This change was a vast improvement on the flat, and often depressing, format of season 1, and ensured that the rest of the series was imbued with plenty of empathy.

7 It’s Always Sunny In Philadelphia (2005 - ) A gang of unlikable bar owners who grow into hilarious people

It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia
Comedy

Release Date August 4, 2005 Cast Charlie Day , Rob McElhenney , Kaitlin Olson , Glenn Howerton , Danny DeVito Seasons 16 Writers Rob McElhenney , Glenn Howerton , Charlie Day , David Hornsby Streaming Service(s) Hulu Directors Matt Shakman , Fred Savage , Daniel Attias , Randall Einhorn , Richie Keen , Todd Biermann

The gang in It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia has changed a lot since season 1, and for good reason. One of the biggest changes after season 1 was the introduction of Frank (Danny DeVito), who provided an essential element to the group dynamic and formed an excellent comedic duo with Charlie (Charlie Day). When watching season 1, it can be difficult to believe that the show has been running for sixteen seasons, as there are jokes in the pilot that would have the series instantly canceled today. Fortunately, the writers and actors quickly found the right way to satirize their characters, and have turned a group of totally unlikable people into a beloved team that audiences root for. Not only was the content of season 1 questionable, but also the quality. As the series progresses, it’s interesting to see how the budget increases alongside the growing popularity, bringing nicer cameras and higher production value, as well as larger set pieces that make the world feel more real.

6 Buffy The Vampire Slayer (1997 - 2003) From cheerleader to savior of the universe

Buffy The Vampire Slayer
Action Comedy Drama Horror Supernatural

Release Date March 10, 1997 Cast Sarah Michelle Gellar , Nicholas Brendon , Alyson Hannigan , Charisma Carpenter , Dan Rubin , David Boreanaz , Seth Green , James Marsters Seasons 7 Story By joss whedon Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Disney+ Directors Joss Whedon

There’s a certain campy charm to season 1 of Buffy The Vampire Slayer, but the complex character and plot arcs of the following seasons put the relatively straightforward season to shame. The Master (Mark Metcalf) is an interesting villain, but he doesn’t subvert the vampire story tropes that the rest of the series does. Additionally, Buffy (Sarah Michelle Gellar) is just starting to learn who she is and coming into her own as a slayer. This is an important part of her origin story, but her best moments come much later when she knows who she is.

5 The Office (2005 - 2013) An overly emotional and inappropriate boss and his disgruntled employees

The Office
Comedy Drama Sitcom

Release Date March 24, 2005 Cast Mindy Kaling , Jenna Fischer , Kate Flannery , Ed Helms , Craig Robinson , Paul Lieberstein , Ellie Kemper , B.J. Novak , Angela Kinsey , Oscar Nunez , Rainn Wilson , Brian Baumgartner , Phyllis Smith , Leslie David Baker , Creed Bratton , Steve Carell , John Krasinski Seasons 9 Story By Ricky Gervais, Stephen Merchant Writers Mindy Kaling , Paul Lieberstein , Michael Schur , Ricky Gervais , Greg Daniels , B.J. Novak Directors Greg Daniels , Paul Lieberstein , Paul Feig , Randall Einhorn , Ken Kwapis

The Office was known for its cringe-inducing humor and pushing the boundaries of what was politically correct to say and do on television. Though every season has its moments, season 1 of The Office includes many things that wouldn’t happen now. Michael’s character sees big changes throughout the series’ run, and by the time he leaves, he’s not the annoying, inappropriate boss but a dear friend to the other people on the show. As the show progressed, less time was dedicated to Michael’s faux pas’ and more to the interpersonal relationships of the characters.

4 The Simpsons (1989 - ) A new kind of animation with notable longevity

The Simpsons
Animation Comedy

Release Date December 17, 1989 Cast Tress MacNeille , Julie Kavner , Harry Shearer , Pamela Hayden , Nancy Cartwright , Hank Azaria , Dan Castellaneta , Yeardley Smith Seasons 35 Story By Matt Groening and James L. Brooks Franchise(s) The Simpsons Directors David Silverman , Jim Reardon , Mark Kirkland

Adult animation was a new genre when The Simpsons premiered in 1989, and churning out high-quality episodes in a short time frame proved difficult for the first season of the show. Many of the characters were still only loose archetypes of who they would become, and the world of Springfield was in development. Unfortunately, the biggest note for season 1 of The Simpsons is the standards of the animation. While the kinks did get worked out, and the animation today is stunning, it caused some problems in the early run of the show.

3 The X-Files (1993 - 2018) Two FBI agents with different perspectives on life, but compatible investigative skills

The X-Files

Release Date September 10, 1993 Cast Gillian Flynn , David Duchovny , Robert Patrick , Annabeth Gish , Mitch Pileggi Seasons 11 Story By Chris Carter Writers Chris Carter Streaming Service(s) Hulu , Disney+ Franchise(s) The X-Files Directors Chris Carter

Mulder (David Duchovny) and Scully (Gillian Anderson) milked their amazing chemistry for many seasons on The X-Files before the show’s creators allowed the couple to finally admit their feelings for each other. However, this tension was one of the best parts of the show and is much more on display in season 1. The professional distance between them is lessened, as are the questions about whether aliens are real. In the following seasons, the writers would try to keep their relationship and the mysteries as ambiguous as possible, which lent itself to a better product overall.

2 Seinfeld (1989 - 1998) Larry David and Jerry Seinfeld’s long-running show about nothing

Seinfeld
Comedy

Release Date July 5, 1989 Cast Jerry Seinfeld , Julia Louis-Dreyfus , Jason Alexander , Michael Richards Story By Jerry Seinfeld Writers Jerry Seinfeld , Larry David Streaming Service(s) Netflix Directors Jason Alexander

Though Seinfeld season 1 only has five episodes, it was enough to turn heads, despite it being unrecognizable from the rest of the series. The series was an experiment, as there was no clear premise besides Jerry and his friends sitting around, commenting on the world around them, and going about their daily lives. No one was sure if this would be successful, so only a few episodes were ordered for season 1. Fortunately, season 2 came along, and the series began to hit its stride and provide the hilarious observational comedy it’s so well-known for.

1 Agents Of S.H.I.E.L.D. (2013 - 2020) A look into the lives of the unsung heroes of the MCU

Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.
Sci-Fi Action Adventure Superhero

Release Date September 24, 2013 Cast Ming-Na Wen , Brett Dalton , Chloe Bennet , Clark Gregg , Elizabeth Henstridge , Iain De Caestecker , Henry Simmons Seasons 7 Story By Jack Kirby Writers Jed Whedon , Maurissa Tancharoen , Joss Whedon Franchise(s) Marvel , Marvel Cinematic Universe Directors Kevin Tancharoen , Jesse Bochco , Billy Gierhart , Vincent Misiano , Garry A. Brown

Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. was developed as a network show based on stories from the Marvel Cinematic Universe, but it became its own entity and distinguished itself in the canon. However, all of this could have been lost if the show had not improved so much from season 1 to season 2. Though season 1 was still engaging, it was too predictable and leaned too heavily on a procedural format. By taking risks and delving into the magic of the Marvel world, Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. cultivated a loyal audience.
