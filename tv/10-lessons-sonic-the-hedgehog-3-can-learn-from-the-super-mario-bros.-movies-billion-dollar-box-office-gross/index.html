10 Lessons Sonic The Hedgehog 3 Can Learn From The Super Mario Bros. Movies Billion-Dollar Box Office Gross

Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Sonic the Hedgehog 3 should embrace the wackiness of the franchise, just like The Super Mario Bros. Movie did with the Mario series. The movie should include more character cameos from the Sonic franchise, giving fans a chance to see their favorite characters on screen. Sonic the Hedgehog 3 should rely more on the iconic music from the games, like The Super Mario Bros. Movie did with its recognizable soundtrack.

Literature Review

Sonic the Hedgehog 3 is the first Sonic movie in the post-Mario movie era, and here are 10 lessons that the sequel can learn from Mario’s success.

Sonic the Hedgehog 3 is the first Sonic the Hedgehog movie to have been released since The Super Mario Bros. Movie’s $1 billion box office gorss, and here are 10 things that the upcoming movie can learn from it. The rivalry between Mario and Sonic is an age-old battle, with the two video game titans being competitors for decades now. Both mascots have now entered the realm of feature films, and if Sonic the Hedgehog 3 wants to stand up against the successes of The Super Mario Bros. Movie, it needs to learn these 10 lessons. Sonic the Hedgehog 3 is the highly-anticipated continuation of the beloved movie series, with it continuing to adapt the iconic video game series of the same name. Sonic the Hedgehog 3 will see the return of Sonic, Tails, and Knuckles, but it will also debut one of the greatest antiheroes from the Sonic franchise: Shadow the Hedgehog. A teaser for Sonic the Hedgehog 3 has started building up hype for the sequel, and while its already on the right track, it really needs to learn from The Super Mario Bros. Movie.

10 Sonic The Hedgehog 3 Shouldn’t Be Scared To Lean Into The Wackiness Of The Games

One of the biggest lessons that Sonic the Hedgehog 3 needs to learn from The Super Mario Bros. Movie is that the film shouldn’t be scared to lean into the wackiness of the franchise. Nintendo’s Mario series contains some pretty wild aspects, and The Super Mario Bros. Movie didn’t shy away from this, fully embracing the video game lore. The Sonic the Hedgehog movies, on the other hand, attempt to ground themselves in the real world, something that should be less emphasized in Sonic the Hedgehog 3.

9 Mario Was Full Of Video Game Character Cameos (& Sonic 3 Should Be Too) Custom Image by Grant Hermanns

The Super Mario Bros. Movie features character cameos throughout the entire film, with all kinds of popular Mario characters appearing on screen for mere moments. The Sonic the Hedgehog movies, however, have barely had any cameos, with only a few members of the video games franchise’s cast making their way into the film series. The Sonic the Hedgehog franchise has a massive roster of characters, and many of them could easily make their way into the movie series, even if they’re only background cameos.

8 Mario Proves That Sonic The Hedgehog Doesn’t Need Its Human Characters

One of the biggest complaints about the Sonic the Hedgehog movies are the human characters, with Sonic being adopted by a human family in the first film. Throughout the two movies, several storylines focus on the lives of the human characters, with this not having much to do with the Sonic lore. The Super Mario Bros. Movie, however, didn’t feel the need to squeeze in original human characters, with the film trusting Mario, Peach, and the rest to solely carry the story. While Sonic the Hedgehog 3 can’t completely cut out the humans, they should be sidelined.

7 Sonic Shouldn’t Be Afraid To Get Even Bigger Stars For Its Voice Roles

Sonic the Hedgehog has taken an interesting approach to its casting, getting stars like Ben Schwartz and Idris Elba to voice Sonic and Knuckles, while Tails’ video game voice actor Colleen O’Shaughnessey returned for the movie. However, Sonic’s biggest star is undoubtedly Jim Carrey, who plays the live-action Dr. Robotnik. The cast of The Super Mario Bros. Movie, however, is full of A-list stars like Chris Pratt, Jack Black, and Seth Rogen, something that Sonic the Hedgehog 3 should mimic with its new characters. Shadow the Hedgehog will have to be cast, but there is the potential for much more.

6 Mario Didn’t Shy Away From Its Iconic Music (& Neither Should Sonic)

The Sonic the Hedgehog games famously have some of the best soundtracks in video game history, but they aren’t being utilized to their fullest extent in the movie series. While the soundtrack of The Super Mario Bros. Movie does contain some popular songs,it heavily uses tracks from the video games, with the games’ composer even working on the film. Sonic the Hedgehog has had remixes of a few of its more iconic tracks, but it should rely on the music from the games even more.

5 Sonic The Hedgehog 3 Should Lean Into The Franchise’s Spinoffs

The Sonic the Hedgehog movies have been pretty faithful to the source material, but they have mostly pulled from the mainline games. There are all kinds of Sonic the Hedgehog video game spinoffs that expand the world of the franchise even more, but they have been mostly ignored by the movies. The Super Mario Bros. Movie heavily used Mario Kart for inspiration when making one of its most prominent action scenes, something that was praised by fans. Sonic the Hedgehog 3 could implement elements from Sonic Riders and other spinoffs, following this Mario movie trend.

4 Sonic The Hedgehog Shouldn’t Be Afraid To Reinvent Characters

Sonic the Hedgehog is full of iconic characters with big personalities, and many of them will probably appear in the movie series eventually. While these characters have all been developed in the games, that doesn’t mean that they can’t experiment with these characters. The Super Mario Bros. Movie completely reinvented Princess Peach, giving her an entirely new personality that worked for the story of the film. Sonic the Hedgehog 3 could do this as well, reinventing characters that are speculated to appear, like Amy Rose or Rogue the Bat.

What Is Knuckles, Paramount+’s New Sonic Show?
Before the next Sonic the Hedgehog movie, the franchise is putting out a highly anticipated animated series following the iconic echidna, Knuckles.

3 Sonic The Hedgehog 3 Needs To Feature Even More Easter Eggs

The Super Mario Bros. Movie’s references and Easter eggs are scattered throughout the entirety of the film, with fans of the Mario franchise having secrets to pick up on in nearly every scene. While Sonic the Hedgehog and Sonic the Hedgehog 2 have featured references to the franchise, the number of Easter eggs isn’t nearly as dense as those seen in The Super Mario Bros. Movie. Sonic the Hedgehog 3 should incorporate even more elements from the video games, scattering them throughout the film.

2 Sonic Has Its Own Mario & Luigi Dynamic (& Sonic 3 Should Use It Better)

The relationship between Mario and Luigi is at the heart of The Super Mario Bros. Movie, giving the film a layer of emotion that carries the story. The Sonic the Hedgehog does attempt to tackle some similar themes, but it hasn’t done it nearly as well, mostly because it is ignoring its own version of the Mario and Luigi dynamic. Sonic and Tails are best friends in the video games, but they have just met in Sonic the Hedgehog 2, meaning that they lack this relationship, but Sonic the Hedgehog 3 should lean further into this.

1 Sonic Shouldn’t Be SEGA’s Only Video Game Movie Franchise

The Super Mario Bros. Movie was just the beginning of Nintendo’s film ambitions, as a movie based on The Legend of Zelda has already been confirmed to be in development. While SEGA’s other franchises may not be as big as some of Nintendo’s, there are several that could be easily translated into film, with the company expanding beyond Sonic the Hedgehog. Franchises like Super Monkey Ball, Golden Axe, and Shenmue are all popular enough to warrant film adaptations, following up on Sonic the Hedgehog 3.
