I Am Legend s Alternate Ending Explained What Happens & Why It Was Cut

SUMMARY

The alternate ending of I Am Legend is truer to the original novel and provides a deeper understanding of the infected. The alternate ending reveals that the infected creatures are self-aware, intelligent, and have feelings, causing Neville to question his own morality.

I Am Legend 2 will follow the alternate ending, allowing Will Smith’s character to survive and continue the story in a new direction.

Literature Review

The alternate ending to I Am Legend is considered better than the divisive original - so much so that it’s the version I Am Legend 2 will follow.

The alternate ending of I Am Legend explained a lot more about the infected than the theatrical version, was much truer to Richard Matheson’s 1954 novel, and made the rest of the movie much deeper. The alternate I Am Legend ending was first revealed on the Will Smith movie’s DVD release, and quickly became viewed as the superior version. So popular was the alternate ending that the upcoming I Am Legend 2 won’t follow the original, while Warner Bros. has made the I Am Legend alternate cut available to stream.

However, this means many now want to know what happens in the I Am Legend alternate ending and why it wasn’t used. Despite being adapted several times before, one aspect of the I Am Legend book that’s never been adapted faithfully is the conclusion. The end of the I Am Legend novel sees Robert Neville die by his own hand, and reveals the infected are intelligent. What’s more, they fear and loathe Neville due to his killing of their species. While the theatrical cut betrayed this impactful conclusion, the I Am Legend alternate ending stays much truer to it.

I Am Legend’s Alternate Ending Explained The Alternate Ending Exposes The Infected Aren’t What They Seem

The violence shown by the infected towards Robert is, from their perspective, out of necessity.

One of the reasons the I Am Legend alternate ending has been made canon is because, unlike the theatrical cut, Will Smith survives. In I Am Legend’s alternate ending, Robert Neville learns that the infected are self-aware, possess a degree of intelligence, and — most importantly — have feelings. The revelation that shocks Neville to his core is that the infected (who in the book are named Darkseekers) are terrified of him. The violence shown by the infected towards Robert is, from their perspective, out of necessity.

The vampire-like infected are attacking his home not to kill him, but to save one of their own. Their assault is a rescue mission to free the infected female that Neville abducted as a test subject. The moral weight of Neville’s relentless experiments to find a cure for the virus is the focal point of the I Am Legend alternate ending. Realizing that the creatures are still intelligent, have a form of communication, and have developed their own society, Neville lets his test subject rejoin her kin.

He never says so out loud, but he seemingly realizes that he has become a monster to the Darkseekers too (just as in the book). However, the realization is what causes Will Smith’s character to survive in the alternate I Am Legend ending. The original ending has Neville sacrifice himself to save the newly discovered survivors Anna and Ethan. However, with the Darkseekers reunited with their captured kin in the alternate ending, they simply leave. Neville then takes the cure and heads to Vermont a changed man, with Anna and Ethan in tow.

Why The I Am Legend Alternate Ending Wasn’t Used Audiences Had A Hard Time Accepting Neville As The Villain

The I Am Legend alternate ending wasn’t used in the final cut for multiple reasons. The theatrical cut made several changes from early drafts, including a version in which Neville’s dog survives. Ultimately, the final ending for I Am Legend was chosen based on audience reception, with the “Will Smith dies” scenario being responded to most favorably. The theatrical ending to I Am Legend was controversial among fans of Richard Matheson’s novel, however, as they felt it failed to thematically capture what made the ending to the book so poignant.

The narrative of the theatrical cut may be closer to the I Am Legend novel in terms of Robert Neville dying, but it arguably misses the philosophical point of a story that’s about morality more than it’s about zombies. While the alternate I Am Legend ending is still far from the book, it’s much closer in spirit, as Neville learns that he isn’t quite as righteous as he thought.

Unfortunately, as revealed by director Francis Lawrence, the reason the I Am Legend twist ending was changed to the more heroic one seen in theaters after test audiences had intensely negative reactions to an ending that paints Robert Neville as the villain.

I Am Legend 2 Will Follow The I Am Legend Alternate Ending The Sequel Will Ignore The Theatrical Cut’s Controversial Conclusion

Ever since I Am Legend 2 was announced with Will Smith returning alongside new cast member Michael B. Jordan, there have been a lot of questions. Since Robert Neville died at the end of I Am Legend, it would be difficult to envisage Will Smith’s return without it simply being flashback scenes (perhaps via Neville’s video logs).

However, by making the I Am Legend alternate ending the canon finale, there are no narrative complexities to Robert Neville being in the sequel. I Am Legend 2 will continue the story where the alternate ending left off rather than the theatrical cut, showing what Robert did after he survived Manhattan and fled with the cure.

While the alternate I Am Legend ending explains how Will Smith returns for the sequel, it still leaves a lot of questions I Am Legend 2 must answer. If Neville had the cure, did he give it to the rest of the survivors or infected mutants, or did he realize they should be left alone in the lives they built?

There is also the question of where the tension in I Am Legend 2 comes from, since the infected are revealed to not be a mindless threat in the new I Am Legend ending. Perhaps Neville becomes their protector against other humans that don’t understand them. However, it could be interesting if the movie leans into Will Smith as the villain with Michael B. Jordan as the hero stopping him from killing the infected.
