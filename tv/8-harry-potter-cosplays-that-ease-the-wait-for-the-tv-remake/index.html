8 Harry Potter Cosplays That Ease The Wait For The TV Remake

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Harry Potter cosplayers showcase the diversity and creativity in the fandom, eagerly awaiting the upcoming TV series. Cosplayers recreate iconic moments from the Harry Potter series, such as the Yule Ball, with great attention to detail. Cosplayers embody beloved characters like Severus Snape, Fleur Delacour, Bellatrix Lestrange, and Hogwarts professors, bringing them to life.

Literature Review

While awaiting HBO’s TV remake of the Harry Potter film series, there’s plenty of brilliant, creative, and inventive cosplay designs to be enjoyed.

With HBO’s Harry Potter TV series still a way off, there are plenty of great Harry Potter cosplays to enjoy. While there are still few confirmed details on the Harry Potter TV show, that hasn’t stopped fans from being prepared for it. Over the years, the sheer popularity of the Potterverse has allowed it to expand way past the original source, allowing audiences to meld and adapt the premise to their own preferences. With many excited to fall in love with Hogwarts all over again, Harry Potter cosplayers have shown a great amount of diversity and creativity in the fandom. Cosplaying has become incredibly popular in recent years, especially with the success of events such as the San Diego Comic-Con, with thousands gathering to dress up as their favorite fictional characters. The Potterverse has a range of various magical characters to choose from, differing in age, gender, and even species. While it’s expected that the TV remake of the Harry Potter movies won’t appear on screens for a while, there are loads of brilliant cosplays of Hogwarts characters to help build the excitement.

8 Harry Potter And Draco Malfoy Bitter Frenemies

The two grew up on opposite sides of an army that neither asked to be a part of, at least not initially.

Easily the greatest example of raging frenemies in fictional history, there’s a lot of hope that the Harry Potter TV show will spend more time on the complicated rivalry between Harry Potter and Draco Malfoy. The two grew up on opposite sides of an army that neither asked to be a part of, at least not initially, and their constant clashing was a huge plot point in Harry Potter. Despite their differences, Harry and Draco did have an odd, if not traumatic, connection together. The tension between the two resulted in a lot of fans pairing them together as a couple, and it is one of the most popular Harry Potter setups for cosplayers to replicate. Despite there never being anything canonical to support this, that’s the beauty of cosplaying, allowing those to put themselves into the shoes of these characters. Although it’s unlikely that the Harry Potter TV show will see Harry and Draco have anything between them, that doesn’t stop cosplayers from adapting the story themselves.

7 Harry, Hermione, And Ron The Beauty Of The Yule Ball

One of Harry Potter’s most beautiful and picturesque scenes, in both the Wizarding World films and the books, is the Yule Ball. The film of Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire famously saw Hermione’s (Emma Watson) dress in a different color than in the books, which is something that HBO’s TV remake can hopefully rectify. Regardless of this small detail, this scene is a huge moment for all three members of the trio. There’s a lot of excitement to see the Yule Ball on-screen once again.

While this post sees the recreation of the film version of the dress, it’s not important, given the beautifully detailed attire of all three cosplayers. Not only are the outfits incredibly accurate to the film, but also smaller elements, such as hair and makeup. There’s a lot of excitement to see the Yule Ball on-screen once again, not just because of Hermione’s famous rant, but also because it’ll be a great opportunity for HBO’s costuming team to get creative.

6 Severus Snape The Darkest Mind In Potions

Alan Rickman’s portrayal of Severus Snape is so iconic, that it’s hard to believe that someone other than him will have to play the Potions Master. The legendary actor passed away in 2016, but his performance is well-remembered worldwide, with many cosplayers enjoying changing themselves into the angst-filled, brooding Professor. It’s fascinating to see how cosplayers can change their entire appearance to fit a character, regardless of who they are. Cosplayer itscreamsjessie1 is a great example of diversity in the fandom, showing that portrayals of certain fictional characters don’t have to be linked to a particular ethnicity.

5 Fleur Delacour Beauxbatons’ Triwizard Champion

While there are plenty of great supporting characters in Harry Potter, Fleur Delacour has been a distinct favorite with cosplayers, with the young, French witch first being introduced in Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire. The Beauxbatons’ silken uniform, in particular, is a look that has been replicated often, which is no surprise given how beautiful it is. With the world of special effects in TV and films being bigger than ever, it’ll be great to see how HBO executes Fleur’s introduction scene, which saw the students of Beauxbatons releasing a group of magical butterflies in the Great Hall.

4 Bellatrix Lestrange The Cruelest Witch In Azkaban

While Voldemort is the clear villain in Harry Potter, there were plenty of other antagonists, including Bellatrix Lestrange. The psychotic witch was played by Helena Bonham Carter in the Harry Potter films, but as of yet, there’s been no confirmation as to who will portray the role in the new TV series. Bellatrix is a great character for cosplayers to take on, especially with her large, struggled hair and recognizable, erratic body language. She’s also a brilliant option because of how simple her appearance is, so cosplayers don’t have to break the bank to have fun as Bellatrix.

3 Slytherin Student Ambitious And Cunning

One brilliant thing about cosplayers is that their imaginations are limitless. With Hogwarts being such a large school, and a lot of focus being on a small percentage of the entire body, a lot of cosplayers create their own characters and assign them to one of four houses. Creating original characters allows for the Potterverse to be expanded by its audiences and with such a magical premise, it is no surprise that cosplayers enjoy delving into their own Hogwarts alter egos. Slytherin is a great house to join too, allowing cosplayers to delve into Hogwarts’ darker sides.

2 Hufflepuff Student Dedicated, Patient, And Loyal

With each house having its own values, it’s a great chance for cosplayers to develop their characters’ personalities and preferences. During the events of Harry Potter, Hufflepuff’s house is headed up by Herbology Professor Sprout, who was portrayed by Miriam Margolyes in the movies. A lot of students had a connection to the subject, but Hufflepuff students, in particular, were often drawn to the study of magical plants, and this is often seen in cosplay designs. Because of the sheer amount of flora and creatures in the Potterverse, it allows for cosplayers to accessorize, like fantazio.ir did with this Mandrake.

1 Minerva McGonagall And Albus Dumbledore Hogwarts’ Fiercest Leaders

Harry Potter simply wouldn’t be the same without two of Hogwarts’ greatest teachers, Professors McGonagall and Dumbledore. In the film series, the two were portrayed by Dame Maggie Smith and Michael Gambon, the latter having passed away in 2023. Both characters are remembered for their distinct personalities and emotional attachments to Harry, as well as their individual tenures as the head teacher of Hogwarts. While there are plenty of great students for cosplayers to take on, there are also lots of teachers to portray too, showing just why Harry Potter characters have been such a popular choice.
