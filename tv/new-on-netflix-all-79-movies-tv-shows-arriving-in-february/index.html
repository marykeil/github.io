New On Netflix All 79 Movies & TV Shows Arriving In February

SUMMARY

February 2024 will bring a diverse range of new TV shows and movies to Netflix, including romance, animation, comedy, and thrillers. Subscribers can look forward to the live-action adaptation of Avatar: The Last Airbender and the romantic miniseries One Day, among other exciting releases. There will be something for everyone, from binge-worthy multi-season shows to new Netflix originals, ensuring subscribers won’t be disappointed.

Literature Review

November on Netflix is packed with new TV shows and movies including Everything Everywhere All At Once and seasons of Brooklyn Nine-Nine.

The new TV shows and movies arriving on Netflix in February 2024 will certainly get subscribers excited, as once again the streaming platform is offering a plethora of titles across an incredibly diverse range of genres. Netflix refreshes its catalog monthly with new content, so there’s always something fresh to look forward to — both in terms of well-known classics and new Netflix originals. February 2024 is no different, with a wide array of new options for subscribers to choose from including binge-worthy multi-season shows, new Netflix originals, and classic movies debuting on the platform for the first time.

February 2024 will see some big new releases and Netflix Originals like the adaptation of the best-selling romance novel One Day. When it comes to new Netflix TV shows, February will also see the return of some exciting new series like the live-action Avatar: The Last Airbender series. It will certainly be a busy month in February with all the new movies and TV shows on Netflix, and subscribers certainly won’t be left wanting.

February 1 20 New Additions On Netflix

¡Sálvese quien pueda! American Assassin Anaconda Enough Fury The Great Gatsby (2013) How to Train Your Dragon 2 It (2017) Magic Mike’s Last Dance Moneyball The Other Boleyn Girl Pacific Rim Resident Evil Resident Evil: Retribution S.W.A.T.: Under Siege Shot Caller Something’s Gotta Give Tom and Jerry (2021) X Young Sheldon: Season 6

The first of the month is usually when Netflix adds the majority of its titles. While that is the case with February as well, it is a small number of new additions to kick off the month than is typical for the streaming service. There are still a lot of great options to choose from for the month with something for Netflix users of all kinds.

There are great family-friendly options like the animated adventure How to Train Your Dragon 2 and the live-action Tom and Jerry. There are also gritty thrillers like the war movie Fury starring Brad Pitt and the underrated crime movie Shot Caller. Horror fans can enjoy the first of the two-part adaptation of Stephen King’s It as well as the excellent throwback horror movie X from Ti West. To coincide with the premiere of the final season of Young Sheldon, Netflix is also adding season 6 of the popular sitcom spinoff to ensure fans are up-to-date before the end.

February 2 Three Arrivals Including A New Animated Movie

Let’s Talk About CHU Orion and the Dark Plus One

Three titles arrive on Netflix on February 2 including an intriguing new animated Netflix Original movie. Orion and the Dark follows a young boy who is afraid of everything until he meets the embodiment of Dark who teaches him that he cannot let fear of the unknown hold him back. The movie brings audiences into a strange world of the beings who control the night, but is especially intriguing given that it was written by Oscar-winner Charlie Kaufman. It will be joined by the rom-com Plus One as well as the comedy series Let’s Talk About CHU from Taiwan.

February 3 Spielberg’s Sci-Fi Blockbuster

Ready Player One

February 3 will see the addition of a Steven Spielberg blockbuster on Netflix. Based on the novel by Ernest Cline, Ready Player One is a grand sci-fi story set in a future in which society escapes into a virtual reality video game. When the game creator dies, he sets up a challenge for players to seek out a hidden Easter egg within the game. Though it is not regarded as Spielberg’s best movie as it lacks some of his magic, it is fun to see him operating in a big CGI world of nostalgia and fun references.

February 5 Two Popular Comedy Shows Come To Netflix In Their Entirety

30 for 30: Four Falls of Buffalo 30 for 30: Nature Boy Dee & Friends in Oz Monk: Seasons 1-8 My Wife and Kids: Seasons 1-5 The Re-Education of Molly Singer

February 5 will see six new titles being added to Netflix, including the complete series of two popular shows. With the rise in popularity in the murder mystery genre, now is the perfect time for Monk to make a comeback. Following the well-received Monk movie, all eight seasons of the show will be added to Netflix starring Tony Shaloub as a freelance detective with OCD who helps police investigations. Also arriving are all five seasons of Damon Wayan’s domestic sitcom My Wife and Kids.

There will also be sports documentary specials being added to Netflix on this day from the excellent 30 for 30 series. Nature Boy is a look at iconic wrestler Ric Flair, but just in time for the Super Bowl, the really interesting one is Four Falls of Buffalo which follows the four consecutive years the Bills made it to the Super Bowl only to lose.

February 7 A New Documentary About A Strange Cult

Love Never Lies Poland: Season 2 Part 2 Luz: The Light of the Heart Raël: The Alien Prophet

Netflix’s latest cult documentary will be arriving on February 7 along with two international shows. Raël: The Alien Prophet follows the bizarre and incredible story of a religion that grew out of a belief and fascination in alien life only to grow into a controversial cult with an eccentric leader. The Polish version of the reality dating show Love Never Lies also returns to Netflix along with the Brazilian family adventure show Luz: The Light of the Heart.

February 8 An Adaptation Of A Best-Selling Romance Novel

One Day

A new romantic miniseries is arriving on February 8 on Netflix. Based on the novel by David Nicholls, One Day follows Dexter and Emma, two young people who meet for the first time just as they are about to graduate and go about their separate lives. However, over the years, these two are drawn back together by that initial connection as the series explores the different paths they have gone down which continues to pull them away from each other. It could make for the ideal Valentine’s Day watching experience this month.

February 9 A True-Crime Documentary And Four International Titles

A Killer Paradox Alpha Males: Season 2 Ashes Bhakshak Lover, Stalker, Killer

February 9 will feature a new true-crime documentary coming on Netflix as well as four new international titles. Lover, Stalker, Killer is a documentary that tells the story of a man who tried online dating for the first time only to meet someone who proved to be a dangerous connection.

This will be joined by the Korean comedy thriller series A Killer Paradox about a man who accidentally kills a serial killer and goes on the run. Also arriving is the Indian drama Bhakshak based on true events, the romance movie Ashes from Turkey, and the second season of the Spanish comedy series Alpha Males.

February 10 A Star-Studded Comedy Sequel

Horrible Bosses 2

While Horrible Bosses 2 did not receive the same box office and critical success as the original, it’s an underrated comedy with a great cast. Jason Bateman, Jason Sudeikis, and Charlie Day return as three friends who find themselves on the cusp of success with a new business idea only for it to be stolen from them by a new partner (Christoph Waltz). As a way to get what they want, they decide to kidnap his dimwitted son (Chris Pine). Along with the exciting new cast members, Jennifer Aniston, Jamie Foxx, and Kevin Spacey return for the sequel.

February 11 The Final Season Of A Hit Thriller Series

The Blacklist: Season 10

February 11 adds one more season of a very bingeworthy thriller series. James Spader stars in the series as a world-renowned criminal who turns himself into the authorities and provides them with a list of the most dangerous international crime figures who he will help them track down and stop. The 10th season of the show which brings The Blacklist to an end will be added to Netflix, allowing users to binge it in its entirety.

February 13 A Sports Docuseries, A Comedy Special And A Turkish Comedy

Kill Me If You Dare Sunderland ‘Til I Die: Season 3 Taylor Tomlinson: Have It All

Three very different titles arrive on Netflix on February 13. The third season of Sunderland ‘Til I Die sees the return of the charming sports docuseries that explores the unwavering loyalty and passion of fans of the Sutherland football club during the highs and lows of their seasons. This will also be joined by a new comedy special from the hilarious Taylor Tomlinson entitled Have It All. Finally, the Turkish rom-com Kill Me If You Dare will also be added.

February 14 A New Rom-Com And Reality Dating Show

A Soweto Love Story Good Morning, Verônica: Season 3 The Heartbreak Agency Love Is Blind: Season 6 Players

A new Netflix Original rom-com will be arriving on February 14 along with the return of a popular reality dating series, providing some great Valentine’s Day plans. Players stars Gine Rodriguez as a woman who is an expert at devising successful hookup approaches for her male friends but finds that she needs their help when she falls for a new love interest. Love is Blind also returns for season 6 in which people participate in a matchmaking scenario all about the connection rather than the looks.

These two titles will also be joined by three new international additions to Netflix with the German rom-com The Heartbreak Agency, the Turkish rom-com A Soweto Love Story, and the third season of the Brazilian mystery series Good Morning, Verônica.

February 15 A New Comedy Series

AlRawabi School for Girls: Season 2 The Catcher Was a Spy Crossroads House of Ninjas Little Nicholas: Life of a Scoundrel Ready, Set, Love The Vince Staples Show

February 15 marks the debut of an intriguing new series on Netflix. The Vince Staples Show is a scripted series starring rapper and actor Vince Staples as a fictionalized version of himself dealing with the many misadventures of life in his hometown. It looks to combine humor and an elevated sense of satire set in the music world which could feel reminiscent of Atlanta.

Also arriving on this date is Britney Spears’ movie debut with the road trip story Crossroads as well as the period spy thriller The Catcher Was a Spy starring Paul Rudd. Another new series is the Japanese action series House of Ninjas which will be joined by other international titles like the rom-com Ready, Set, Love from Thailand, the documentary Little Nicholas: Life of a Scoundrel, and the second season of AlRawabi School for Girls from Jordan.

February 16 A Cult Series And A James Cameron Movie

The Abyss Comedy Chaos Einstein and the Bomb Warrior: Seasons 1-3

While Avatar: The Way of Water and Titanic showcase James Cameron’s epic water-based filmmaking, they were both preceded by The Abyss. One of Cameron’s most underrated movies, The Abyss is a sci-fi drama that follows a group of deep-sea divers who discover an alien lifeform in the depths of the ocean. It is a gripping movie with the kind of ground-breaking special effects Cameron has become known for.

The Abyss will also be joined by the arrival of all three seasons of the cult series Warrior. Though Warrior was canceled before season 4, it remains a popular series with many fans calling for its revival. That is likely going to continue with more people discovering it on Netflix. Also on February 16 is the Indonesian comedy series Comedy Chaos and the documentary Einstein and the Bomb.

February 19 A Reality Series And Children’s Show

Little Angel: Volume 4 Rhythm + Flow Italy

Two titles joined Netflix on February 19 with the music competition show Rhythm + Flow Italy which follows the search for the next rap superstar in Italy and the children’s animated series Little Angel.

February 20 A Family-Friendly Animated Movie And Comedy Special Voice of Lana Condor in Ruby Gillman, Teenage Kraken

Mike Epps: Ready to Sell Out Ruby Gillman, Teenage Kraken

Netflix can sometimes offer a second opportunity for shows that failed to find new life. Such could be the case with DreamWorks Animations box office bomb Ruby Gillman, Teenage Kraken. The movie follows a young and shy descendant of a race of sea creatures who finds that she holds a great purpose in the ocean kingdom. The film could become a new family-friendly favorite for those who missed it in theaters. Also arriving is a new comedy special from Mike Epps entitled Ready to Sell Out.

February 22 A Live-Action Adaptation Of A Beloved Anime

Avatar: The Last Airbender Southpaw

Netflix’s live-action adaptations of beloved anime have been a bit of a mixed back with the highs, like One Piece, and the lows, like Cowboy Bebop. However, the streaming service now attempts to find success with its biggest franchise yet in the genre. The live-action take on Avatar: The Last Airbender promises to be an epic blockbuster series that will tell the story of Aang and his friends who battle the villainous Fire-Nation in an attempt to save the world. The movie will also be joined by the intense boxing drama Southpaw starring Jake Gyllenhaal and Rachel McAdams.

February 23 Four New Additions Including Best Picture Winner And Returning Sports Documentary

Everything Everywhere All at Once Formula 1: Drive to Survive: Season 6 Mea Culpa Through My Window: Looking at You

February 23 will be debuting some very exciting titles on Netflix. Ahead of the Oscars in March, Netflix is adding the most recent Best Picture winner with the wild, beautiful, and inventive Everything Everywhere All At Once. Michelle Yeoh won her Oscar for playing a woman who, while dealing with her taxes, is pulled into a multiversal war in which she is the key to saving everything. The movie features irreverent humor, exciting action, bold ideas, and a lot of heart that makes it a truly unique movie experience.

Also on his date is the return of the very popular Netflix sports docuseries Formula 1: Drive to Survive. Season 6 of the show will once again take audiences behind the scenes of the high-stakes racing world as it follows the drivers and various personalities who compete in the intense sport. Tyler Perry’s latest movie Mea Culpa also arrives on Netflix starring Kelly Rowland and Trevante Rhodes as well as the Spanish romance movie Through My Window: Looking at You.

February 24 An Animated Comedy, Reality Show Classic, And Awards Show Airing

The 30th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards Marcel the Shell with Shoes On The Real World: Season 9

A unique animated movie will be added to Netflix on February 24. Marcel the Shell with Shoes On features the voice of Jenny Slate, who also co-wrote the movie, as the titular character, a mollusk shell with eyes who becomes the unexpected focus of a new documentary. The movie has a strange and hilarious sense of humor and is elevated further by its lovable protagonist. It’ll be joined by season 9 of the iconic reality series The Real World as well as the first Netflix airing of the Screen Actors Guild Awards with the 30th anniversary of the awards.

February 26 Four Seasons Of A Beloved Sitcom

Blippi Wonders: Season 3 Brooklyn Nine-Nine: Seasons 1-4

One of the most binge-worthy sitcoms in recent years will be adding several seasons to Netflix on February 26. Brooklyn Nine-Nine is a charming, funny, and endearing comedy series that follows the colorful and lovable characters of the titular NYPD precinct. The first four seasons of the show will be added to Netflix with the rest still available on Peacock. However, four seasons of the show still provide plenty of laughs and heartwarming moments from the series.

February 28 A Sci-Fi Sequel To A Netflix Original

American Conspiracy: The Octopus Murders The Mire: Millennium Code 8 Part II

2019 saw the arrival of the Netflix sci-fi action movie Code 8 starring real-life brothers Stephen Amell and Robbie Amell. Now the brothers return for the sequel with Code 9 Part II dropping on February 28. Set in a future in which people with superpowers are outlawed, the sequel picks up with Robbie Amell’s electricity-powered Connor on the run and in need of help from his former criminal associate Garrett (Stephen Amell). Also debuting is the documentary American Conspiracy: The Octopus Murders and the Polish thriller The Mire: Millennium.

February 29

The Tourist: Season 2

Ending the month of February is the highly-anticipated return of a mystery season. The Tourist stars Jamie Dornan as a man who wakes up in the Australian outback with no memory with the truth of his past threatening to catch up with him as he tries to put the pieces together. The second season of the show continues to explore the riveting mystery of this man with plenty more action and intrigue promised for the story.
