All Air Force Ranks In Masters Of The Air Explained

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Understanding the characters’ ranks in Masters of the Air is important to grasp the true story being told. The lowest rank in the series is Sergeant, which holds a position of power within the Air Force. The highest ranked characters in Masters of the Air are Colonels, leading vast numbers and holding important leadership roles.

Literature Review

Due to Masters of the Air’s big cast, it is no surprise that there are various, and sometimes confusing, military ranks included in the series.

Although Masters of the Air is a thrilling watch, for many its expansive cast and military history can be confusing, especially when it comes to the characters’ jobs and ranks in the Air Force, which fortunately, can be simplified and explained. Masters of the Air is a war drama series that is a companion to Tom Hanks and Steven Spielberg’s World War II shows, 2000’s Band of Brothers and 2010’s The Pacific. Masters of the Air tells the true story of the 100th Bomb Group, often called the “Bloody Hundredth,” during World War II. Sometimes Masters of the Air makes it easy to figure out which characters have higher positions than others, but at other times, this is far from the truth. Due to the size of the cast, it can become very difficult to pull apart which characters are officers and which are NCOs. While it isn’t entirely vital to the plot, it is important to understand the characters’ ranks because of the true story they are telling. Similarly, it is important to remember that Masters of the Air depicts the U.S. Army Air Forces rather than the United States Air Force.

7 Sergeant Midlevel Enlisted Rank

The lowest rank in Masters of the Air is the role of sergeant. A sergeant is under the rank of NCO, or noncomissioned officer, however, it is also a position of power within the rank. In this way, sergeants are typically leaders among larger groups of NCOs. In the case of the U.S. Army Air Forces, a sergeant would preside over a force of airmen, who are ranked the lowest in their branch. Some examples of sergeants in Masters of the Air include Sgt. Ken Lemmons (Raff Law), Sgt. William Quinn (Kai Alexander), and Sgt. William Hinton (Oaklee Pendergast). Although Masters of the Air’s cast list only reads “Sgt.” before its sergeant characters, the truth of the matter is that there are various different kinds of sergeants. In the U.S. Army Air Forces, there are 10 types of sergeants, ranging from Staff Sergeant to Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force. When it comes to the characters listed above their specific ranks are as follows: Sgt. Ken Lemmons was a Master Sergeant, Sgt. William Quinn was a Technical Sergeant, and Sgt. William Hinton was a Staff Sergeant. Additionally, their positions in the Air Force differ from their rank.

6 Second Lieutenant Company Grade Officer

Following the various types of sergeants, the next highest rank in Masters of the Air is the second lieutenant. A second lieutenant is the lowest rank within the commissioned ranks, which are the highest ranks in the U.S. military. In general, these are the most esteemed and highest-paying roles in the U.S. military. Therefore, the second lieutenant is an important and impressive rank. In Masters of the Air, there are three second lieutenant characters: 2nd Lt. Richard D. Macon (Josiah Cross), 2nd Lt. Alexander Jefferson (Branden Cook), and 2nd Lt. Robert Daniels (Ncuti Gatwa).

5 First Lieutenant Company Grade Officer

Immediately above the second lieutenant is the first lieutenant. Once again, like the second lieutenant, the first lieutenant holds one of the lower rankings among the very high-ranking commissioned ranks. This is ultimately a very vital role within the U.S. military. Masters of the Air is chock full of first lieutenants, and in fact, the majority of the characters hold this rank. Some of the most important first lieutenant characters in Masters of the Air include Lt. Curtis Biddick (Barry Keoghan), Lt. Roy Frank Claytor (Sawyer Spielberg), Lt. David Friedkin (Freddy Carter), and Lt. Howard Hamilton (Jordan Coulson).

4 Captain Company Grade Officer

The rank following first lieutenant and second lieutenant is captain. A captain is the final rank within the company grade officer subsection. Typically, a captain takes a leadership role among a larger group of soldiers or a company, similar to a sergeant among NCOs. Captains are also able to become instructors at flight schools or combat training centers. Within the Masters of the Air cast, there are four captains in total. These include Capt. Joseph Payne (Louis Greatorex), Capt. Bernard DeMarco (Adam Long), Capt. John D. Brady (Ben Radcliffe), and Capt. Oran Petrich (Jojo Macari).

3 Major Field Grade Officer

Above a captain, a first lieutenant, and a second lieutenant is a major. A major is the lowest rank within the field grade officer subsection. In general, majors hold positions of great power within the U.S. Army Air Forces. For example, a major could take the role of a flight commander or could have a high-ranking position on the operations side of things. This range of job options actually makes itself relevant in the first episodes of Masters of the Air, as Major John “Bucky” Egan wishes he held a more hands-on position in the Air Force. Notably, Masters of the Air’s three leading characters hold the rank of major. Gale “Buck” Cleven (Austin Butler), John “Bucky” Egan (Callum Turner), and Harry Crosby (Anthony Boyle) are all majors within the U.S. Army Air Forces. Overall, their rank being so high from the very start of the series shows the importance of their characters, but also, the importance of their actions within the war. It is also interesting to see the different positions they hold. While Major Gale Cleven and Major John Egan held positions of Command Pilot, Major Harry Crosby was a navigator.

2 Lieutenant Colonel Field Grade Officer

The rank directly above a major is a lieutenant colonel. This is a mid-level role within the commissioned ranks, and is right in the middle of the field grade officer subsection. In some ways, a lieutenant colonel is like a second-in-command. They can hold junior positions in high-ranking staffs or act as squadron commanders in various different subsects of the Air Force. In total, there are two lieutenant colonels in Masters of the Air. They are Lt. Col. John B. Kidd (Edward Ashley) and Lt. Col. James W. Lann (John Schwab).

1 Colonel Field Grade Officer

Finally, the highest ranking character in Masters of the Air holds the rank of colonel. A colonel is the highest rank in the field grade officer subsection. There are only five ranks that stand above a colonel. In the Air Force, a colonel typically leads a vast number of men, usually in the thousands, and has a role as commander, vice commander, group commander, etc. In Masters of the Air, there are three characters that hold the rank of colonel: Colonel Harold Huglin (Nikolai Kinski), Col. Albert Clark (Sam Hazeldine), and Col. Thomas Jeffery (Christopher Lakewood).
