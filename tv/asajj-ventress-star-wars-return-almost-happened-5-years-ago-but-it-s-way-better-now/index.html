Asajj Ventress Star Wars Return Almost Happened 5 Years Ago (But It s Way Better Now)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Asajj Ventress’s return in The Bad Batch season 3 was originally planned for a different Star Wars property which would have been a decidedly bad decision. Dave Filoni has always wanted to bring back Ventress and initially proposed her return in Star Wars Resistance, but her inclusion was voted against. The Bad Batch is the right place to bring back Ventress because the show’s established characters can hold their own meaning her appearance enhances the story without overshadowing it. Additionally, the show’s timeline makes it easier to fill in two years of Ventress’s story compared to the five decades required in Resistance.

Literature Review

The return of Asajj Ventress teased for Star Wars: The Bad Batch season 3 was actually planned 5 years ago for a different Star Wars TV show.

The return of Asajj Ventress teased for Star Wars: The Bad Batch season 3 was initially planned five years ago for a different Star Wars property which would have proven to be a decidedly poor decision. As the wait for upcoming Star Wars TV shows grew nearly unbearable, Lucasfilm released the first trailer for The Bad Batch season 3 in January 2024. This trailer teased what was to come in the show’s final season from a reunion of the titular Clone Force 99 to the rescue of Omega from the clutches of the Empire.

However, undoubtedly the most surprising aspect of The Bad Batch season 3’s story teased by the trailer is the return of Asajj Ventress. Asajj Ventress was a prequel-era Sith introduced in Star Wars: The Clone Wars who supposedly died in the canon novel Dark Disciple. That said, The Bad Batch season 3 is seemingly retconning this by having Asajj be introduced during the Dark Times of the Empire’s reign. Interestingly, The Bad Batch is not the first time Lucasfilm has attempted bringing Ventress back with the character’s return initially being planned for another Star Wars property five years ago.

Dave Filoni Never Intended Asajj Ventress’ Death To Stick

The return of Ventress has been planned for a while, with Star Wars creatives including Dave Filoni wanting to resurrect the character since the release of Dark Disciple. Shortly after finishing work on Star Wars: The Clone Wars and Star Wars Rebels, Dave Filoni was responsible for creating another TV show set in a galaxy far, far away. The TV show in question was Star Wars Resistance, an animated show that centered on the titular Resistance fighting back against the First Order during the time of the Star Wars sequel trilogy.

Initially, one of Star Wars Resistance’s plots involved Asajj Ventress. During the production of Star Wars Resistance season 2, Dave Filoni pitched bringing Ventress back as an archaeologist who hunts Sith relics to oppose the First Order. This character eventually became Mika Grey, with Ventress’ inclusion being voted against. This proves that Dave Filoni has never intended to allow Asajj Ventress’ Dark Disciple death to stick with the Star Wars filmmaker constantly searching for a way to see her return to the franchise’s various stories.

Asajj Ventress’ planned Resistance role would have seen her engage in a tantalizing duel with the Star Wars sequel character of Kylo Ren.

Why The Bad Batch Is The Right Place To Bring Ventress Back

Five years later, it seems as though Dave Filoni finally has his wish to bring Ventress back. While Filoni has not been as actively involved in The Bad Batch’s production as he was with The Clone Wars or Rebels, he still serves as the show’s creator and one of its many producers. This means he likely had a say in bringing back Asajj Ventress for Star Wars: The Bad Batch alongside the showrunners Jeniffer Corbett and Brad Rau. As it turns out, Ventress’ return in The Bad Batch is a much wiser choice than the initial plan for Resistance.

The reason as to why Star Wars Resistance did not include Ventress was the worry that her character would overshadow the rest of the show. Star Wars Resistance has never been Lucasfilm’s most popular TV show and including Ventress likely would have rectified this, but there is no denying it would have been the only talking point of what was an already-underwhelming series. That said, Ventress’ return in Star Wars: The Bad Batch season 3 proves that Lucasfilm views the characters in the show to be well-established on their own merits by this point in the story.

The Bad Batch’s central characters already have established stories, connections, relationships, and character arcs meaning a character like Asajj Ventress would not be the focal point of the show. Regardless of whether Ventress appears or not, the characters of Star Wars: The Bad Batch are strong enough to carry a series on their own. As such, Ventress’ appearance in The Bad Batch would only bolster an already great story rather than completely eclipse an underwhelming one like Star Wars Resistance.

If Asajj Ventress had appeared in Star Wars Resistance, it would mean that the show would have had to fill out almost five decades of the character’s life after she last appeared in the Clone Wars.

Also, Ventress’ role in Star Wars: The Bad Batch has another benefit related to the Star Wars timeline. If Asajj Ventress had appeared in Star Wars Resistance, it would mean that the show would have had to fill out almost five decades of the character’s life after she last appeared in the Clone Wars. This would have been an incredibly difficult task, not only because of Asajj’s somewhat unbelievable older age by the time of Resistance but also the amount of time that would need to be dedicated to adequately explain her story in between both projects.

When concerning The Bad Batch season 3, however, the show only takes place two years after Dark Disciple. The last canon Ventress appearance was set in 20BBY while The Bad Batch is set in 18BBY. Therefore, it is much easier for the writers of The Bad Batch to fill in two years of Ventress’ story than the 50 that would have been required should she have appeared in Star Wars Resistance. All of this only proves how smart a decision it was to bring back Ventress for Star Wars: The Bad Batch rather than Star Wars Resistance five years prior.
