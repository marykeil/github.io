8 Lessons One Piece Learned From Netflix s Worst Anime Adaptations

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Netflix learned from past failures and included the original anime creators in the live-action adaptation of One Piece, resulting in a successful and faithful adaptation. TV formats are better suited for live-action anime adaptations, as it allows for a more thorough and faithful portrayal of the original story without significant cuts. Casting actors that resemble the animated characters is crucial for a successful adaptation, as it helps maintain the familiarity and essence of the beloved anime.

Literature Review

Netflix struck out with several ill-received live-action anime adaptations, and the lessons learned from each made all the difference for One Piece.

Of all Netflix’s live-action anime adaptations, One Piece is easily the best, and this all comes down to the lessons the streaming platform learned from its past failures. The idea of taking a beloved anime and turning it into a movie or series is nothing new, and several have been released over the years to varying success. Even Netflix has managed a few relative gems, but the vast majority of announcements that old favorites were being adapted to live-action have ended in disappointment. Thankfully, Netflix seems to have paid attention to what has or has not worked. Netflix’s live-action anime adaptations, from movie to TV series, include titles like Death Note, the Fullmetal Alchemist movies, Cowboy Bebop, and, of course, One Piece. Every couple of years, the streaming service announces that yet another beloved anime will be reimagined in live-action, which often invites skepticism from audiences. There are dozens of ways to ruin a story, but the success of One Piece proves that it can be done right. Of course, this meant that other live-action anime adaptations had to crawl so One Piece could run, with each teaching Netflix an important lesson.

10 Best Live-Action Anime TV Shows, Ranked There are plenty of fantastic live-action adaptations of anime TV shows to enjoy, from Future Diary: Another World to Netflix’s One Piece.

8 Original Anime Creators Need To Be Included In Netflix’s Remakes Netflix rarely lets the original creators of anime get involved with the live-action remake.

Many of Netflix’s anime adaptations lost the flavor of the original series, and this has a lot to do with the creators of said anime having no involvement in the project. Since the directors and writers of the remakes often have no understanding of why fans have loved series like Cowboy Bebop or Death Note for so long, they cannot show the property the appropriate respect, which translates to a sloppy or superficial adaptation. Thankfully, Netflix learned this lesson and brought Eiichiro Oda on board for One Piece, which made a significant difference.

7 TV Formats Are Better For Most Live-Action Anime Live-action anime movies like Fullmetal Alchemist were forced to make significant cuts.

It’s certainly possible to create a good movie based on an anime series, but it’s much harder to pull off. Even the simplest anime that follow standard story formulas don’t typically have the kind of rise and fall required for film, but that hasn’t stopped Netflix from attempting to force complex stories into a single installment. The Fullmetal Alchemist series was even broken into several movies, but even this meant significant cuts had to be made to the plot. One Piece is several times longer than Fullmetal Alchemist, so a TV format was an absolute necessity.

6 Netflix Learned That Casting Is Key For Live-Action Anime Adaptations Casting actors that look like the animated characters is key for a successful adaptation.

Anime characters are larger than life—it’s a staple of the genre—and this makes bringing them to live-action all the more complicated. Character appearances are especially difficult to duplicate on screen. Netflix has seemed averse to letting its anime adaptation cast members look like groups of cosplayers, which means movies like Death Note are nearly unrecognizable. One Piece sees a crew of wildly colorful characters, and it was clear from the live-action series that Netflix took a lot of time with casting and costume design. The characters felt new but familiar. Death Note is Netflix’s lowest rated live-action anime adaptation, only managing 36% on Rotten Tomatoes.

5 Reinventing Successful Anime Stories Is Asking For Trouble Live-action anime adaptations like Death Note made too many changes to the story.

Perhaps the greatest lesson Netflix could learn from its past anime failures is that completely changing major aspects of the plot is sure to result in angry fans.

Perhaps the greatest lesson Netflix could learn from its past anime failures is that completely changing major aspects of the plot is sure to result in angry fans. It seems that past attempts at this have either added far too many dramatic elements that did nothing to enhance the story (Death Note) or drastically reduced the fun to the point that audiences were bored to tears (Cowboy Bebop). One Piece made some tasteful changes, which enhanced the story in a way that justified the live-action adaptation’s existence. Still, in the end, it was entirely recognizable as the anime everyone initially fell in love with.

4 Live-Action Anime Adaptations Can Still Feel Like Anime Too many of Netflix’s live-action adaptations tried to strip away the “anime” feel.

Of course, the entire point of live-action anime adaptations is that they bring a story from one medium to another. There are aspects of anime—especially the over-the-top dialogue—that wouldn’t go over as well with live actors, but that isn’t to say that the flavor of this art form can’t be mixed in. This is again where Netflix’s Death Note and Cowboy Bebop went wrong. They were approached like mainstream media, but since anime fans are still likely to be the primary audiences, this doesn’t work. One Piece felt like a real-life anime, not an attempt at something more commercial.

3 Netflix Learned A Good Live-Action Anime Adaptation Is Worth The Budget Netflix didn’t hold back on One Piece’s special effects and it made all the difference.

The vast majority of Netflix’s live-action adaptations suffered from low budgets. It was easy enough for The Ingenuity Of The Househusband to look good in this format, but movies and series like Fullmetal Alchemist and even Death Note suffered due to poorly done visual effects. Skimping out on the budget is a fast way to cheapen an adaptation and make it difficult to take seriously. One Piece was a different story. The visual effects were excellent, and that was only possible because Netflix was willing to invest the money.

2 One Piece Respected Beloved Characters (Unlike Other Netflix Adaptations) Changing character personalities completely changes an anime.

Like any piece of fiction, the colorful characters are the heart and soul of anime. However, some of Netflix’s adaptations, most notably Death Note, changed these characters entirely. Light was hardly the same character in the live-action version since he lost the cool, collected genius (and madness) that made him so unique in the original anime. This huge factor (among the other mistakes mentioned above) resulted in Death Note being considered one of the worst live-action anime movies of all time. Thankfully, Netflix learned from this, and One Piece’s characters were left intact.

Netflix’s One Piece Fixed The Most Common Complaint About The Anime 24 Years Later Netflix’s live-action One Piece season 1 changed many elements from the manga, but it improved upon the most common One Piece anime criticism.

1 Live-Action Anime Has To Be Approached Like An Equal Art Form Live-action anime adaptations must be just as artistic as their original counterparts.

Art is a significant part of what makes anime so unique. By adapting these stories to live-action, the drawn-out characters and sceneries are obviously lost, and after all this color and visual magic are stripped away, there is very little left over. This problem was at the center of each of Netflix’s anime adaptation failures and was used as an argument against live-action remakes of popular anime happening in the future. Without the art, why even bother? However, watching Netflix’s live-action One Piece feels just as much like watching an artistic masterpiece as watching the anime.

However, watching Netflix’s live-action One Piece feels just as much like watching an artistic masterpiece as watching the anime. The creativity hadn’t been stifled, the character design was just as loud and fun, and the sceneries were absolutely breathtaking. Though the world was different from the anime or manga, the Netflix series looked like Eiichiro Oda’s own pen had created it. This is the only way that a live-action anime adaptation is even worth it, and hopefully, Netflix has learned from One Piece’s success, as well as its past failures, that art is everything.

One Piece (Live-Action)
Based on the popular manga/anime series, One Piece is a live-action Netflix adaptation of the story from Eiichiro Oda. The show follows the exploits of a band of pirates, the Straw Hats, led by the energetic and adventure-loving Monkey D. Luffy. Luffy is a young man cursed with strange powers after accidentally eating a mysterious fruit. With his friends Zoro, Nami, Usopp, and Sanji, Luffy will head across the vast ocean to find the legendary treasure, the One Piece.
