SUMMARY

Steven’s past was filled with parties and arrests, but he wanted to be a responsible dad for his kids. Steven’s sister revealed that his dad may not be his biological father, leading him to search for the truth. A DNA test revealed Steven’s ancestry and connections to Ireland, but he still doesn’t know who his real father is.

Literature Review

Former 90 Day Fiancé cast member Steven Frend has revealed a shocking fact about his family during his latest 90 Day Diaries appearance with Olga.

Steven Frend is talking about his painful past involving his dad in the latest episode of 90 Day Diaries. Steven used to spend most of his summers in Ocean City. Steven was a player, but everything changed when he met Olga. Steven was intrigued by Olga’s accent and liked how smart, funny, and good-looking she was. Steven knew Olga was only in the U.S. for the summer and was not expecting Olga to get pregnant. Steven had only spent a month and a half with Olga at that point.

Olga had to go back to Russia within a week. Steven proposed to Olga and applied for a K-1 visa to get her to the U.S. Once her visa was approved, Olga arrived with baby Alex. Steven and Olga got married in a courthouse ceremony on August 30, 2019. They lived with Steven’s grandmother for a few years in Maryland but later moved to New York. Steven and Olga separated in October 2020 but got back together when Olga found out she was pregnant again with a baby girl. They decided to work on their relationship following the big news.

Steven Lived With His Grandmother On 90 Day Fiancé 6

Steven was a 20-year-old from Bowie, Maryland during 90 Day Fiancé season 6. Steven was working as a waiter at a restaurant. Steven said it wasn’t what he wanted to do; however, Steven didn’t graduate from high school, and it was the best he could have done at the time. Steven joked that since he was young, his life was “party after party after party.” Steven had been arrested a few times for doing graffiti and was even kicked out of the house at 16. Since then, Steven had been bouncing around between his friends and grandmother’s house.

Steven Was Told His Dad Wasn’t His Biological Father

Steven wanted to be a part of his baby’s life because he wanted to be able to do all the things he didn’t do with his dad. In season 6, Steven revealed his dad had died when Steven was seven from Type 2 diabetes. It was a hard time for Steven, and he didn’t want his kid to grow up without a dad. Since having two kids, Steven started thinking about his past, such as not knowing the truth about his family. Steven revealed when he was 16 years old and doing something in the kitchen, his sister walked in.

Steven’s sister was crying because their uncle let it slip that Steven wasn’t his father’s biological child, but he “wanted to take him in and raise him.” Steven revealed how heartbreaking it was for him to find out the person who was everything to him “may not be his biological father.” Steven’s mom couldn’t remember much apart from a last name that sounded Irish. Steven’s relationship with his mother was “very complicated.” He thought now that he was older, he needed to have the answers himself and did a DNA test.

Steven’s DNA Test Reveals His Dad Is “Somewhere In Ireland”

Olga started seeing a change in Steven since he started doing self-work. Steven’s test results came two months later and revealed who his ancestors were and where he came from. The report revealed Steven was “Northwestern European,” 80% and 44.2% British and Irish. Steven had looked up the last name his mom had told him online and saw the name on the list of relatives. He found someone who was his second cousin.

Steven’s dad’s name was not on the list, but there were some other relatives he could reach out to. Steven thought the results would give him a clearer picture, but he needed more time to sit with it. Steven said he would keep trying to find out who his real father was. As the 90 Day Diaries episode ended, little Alex asked Steven “Where’s your dad?” and Steven replied, “I don’t know, I guess somewhere in Ireland.”
