7 Monsterverse Mysteries Solved By Monarch s Show

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Monarch: Legacy of Monsters reveals the truth about the military’s failed attempt to kill Godzilla in 1954, and how they mistakenly believed it was successful. The show explains where Godzilla went after defeating the MUTOs, revealing that he spent much of his recovery in the Sahara Desert. APEX and Monarch have a confirmed partnership, with Monarch making a deal with APEX in 2015, explaining their connection and the futuristic technology Monarch possesses.

Literature Review

Seven lingering mysteries from Godzilla and Kong’s four Monsterverse movies finally received answers in Apple TV+’s Monarch: Legacy of Monsters.

Warning: Spoilers for Monarch: Legacy Of Monsters season 1

Multiple Monsterverse mysteries finally received answers in Monarch: Legacy of Monsters season 1. With Legendary’s Monsterverse being a vast world where the existence of Titans touches countless lives, Monarch: Legacy of Monsters was able to tell a contained story separate from Godzilla and Kong’s four movies. However, that’s not to say it didn’t overlap with events in the films in any way. In fact, Apple TV+’s show drew quite a lot from the movies and in doing so, included a few unexpected connections to the Monsterverse’s past.

Over the course of its ten-episode first season, Monarch: Legacy of Monsters found time to tie into a number of established Monsterverse details and developments. A storyline set in 1954 culminated in a close examination of the U.S. military’s failed attempt to kill Godzilla, which offered a much better understanding of this pivotal moment in Monsterverse history. Flashbacks to 2012 in Monarch: Legacy of Monsters episode 7 revealed crucial parts of APEX’s origin story, and a massive Titan reveal in 1952 tied up a six-year-old loose end.

What Happened After The Military Tried To Kill Godzilla

2014’s Godzilla explained that in 1954, the military used nuclear bombs, such as the Castle Bravo “test,” to kill Godzilla. Clearly, it didn’t work, but exactly what happened that day was curiously left ambiguous. It raised a lot of questions, especially since the military’s actions risked their own destruction at Godzilla’s hands. According to Monarch: Legacy of Monsters, the military (and Monarch) wrongly believed their plan was a success. Whether it’s because he was knocked unconscious temporarily or he simply submerged underwater and swam away, Godzilla let their attempt on his life go unpunished.

A year passed before Monarch found out that Godzilla survived the bomb. And it was a year more before the military knew the truth; afraid that they would just attack him again, Monarch agreed to keep Godzilla’s continued existence a secret. They changed their minds, though, when they realized that in the absence of massive Titan news, the government would shut down Monarch.

Where Godzilla Went After Beating The MUTOs

Godzilla: King of the Monsters jumped forward by five years, leaving a massive gap in Godzilla’s Monsterverse timeline. Monarch: Legacy of Monsters episode 6 helped rectify that by showing where Godzilla was in 2015. Hiroshi Randa, Bill Randa’s son, traveled to the Sahara Desert and activated a Monarch device powered by gamma radiation that essentially acts as a “Titan phone.” Using the device triggered the emergence of Godzilla, who had already been asleep beneath the desert floor. From the looks of things, this is where Godzilla spent much of his recovery after taking so many hits fighting the MUTOs.

APEX’s Relationship With Monarch

APEX and Monarch are the two most powerful organizations in the Monsterverse, yet Godzilla vs. Kong offered no indication of what their relationship was. Regardless, it was a major point of speculation, as it was difficult to imagine they could co-exist without any interaction or conflict. There was even talk of a possible alliance between them, given the futuristic technology Monarch was armed with in King of the Monsters. Theoretically, it could have been provided by APEX. Suspicions of an APEX-Monarch partnership were confirmed in the Apple TV+ series when it was revealed that Monarch made an unspecified “deal” with APEX in 2015.

How APEX Had A Skullcrawler

One of the products of Monarch’s deal with APEX explained another mystery from Godzilla vs. Kong. Right before the Mechagodzilla reveal, the human characters saw a Skullcrawler in APEX’s underground facility. Somehow, APEX had gotten their hands on the eggs of Kong’s ancestral enemies, who live on Skull Island, a place far-removed from human civilization. In the Monarch: Legacy of Monsters season 1 finale, it was confirmed that by 2017, APEX had built a base on Skull Island. Presumably, Monarch gave them access to the region, which would have put them in a position to acquire their own Skullcrawler.

Why Monarch Was On The Verge Of Collapse In 1973

The beginning of Kong: Skull Island explained that Monarch was close to being shut down, despite the government having full aware of the Titans’ existence thanks to its encounter with Godzilla. How Monarch wound up in such a bad place was cleared up by Monarch: Legacy of Monsters episode 9, which revealed that with Monarch’s support, the military launched Operation Hourglass, a plan designed to get a team into the Hollow Earth. When the project ended in disaster in 1962, the Department of Defense decided to cut Monarch’s funding.

How APEX Know About The Hollow Earth

The twist that revealed APEX’s presence on Skull Island was coupled with the twist that made APEX the people responsible for Monarch: Legacy of Monsters’ human characters getting out of the Monsterverse’s Axis Mundi world. Their involvement in this incident in 2017 sheds light on how Walter Simmons and APEX were so confident that there was a subterranean world underneath the Earth’s surface in Godzilla vs. Kong. As it turns out, the expedition to the Hollow Earth in the film wasn’t APEX’s first foray into this corner of the Monsterverse.

What Sunk The U.S.S. Lawton In 1944

In Kong: Skull Island, it was noted that during World War II, a monster destroyed a nuclear submarine called the U.S.S. Lawton. The mysterious sinking of the vessel is what prompted President Harry Truman to start Monarch in the first place. As for what Titan was responsible, Godzilla was the original suspect among fans for years on account of his reputation for lurking in the ocean. The Monsterverse finally debunked this theory in Monarch: Legacy of Monsters episode 1, which confirmed that the culprit was a totally different monster.

A giant winged Titan, since dubbed the Ion Dragon, wrecked the U.S.S. Lawton and took its wreckage - presumably through a Hollow Earth tunnel - and brought it to its home in the Philippines. Visiting the area is how the eventual leaders of Monarch first came together. Since it was what gave them undisputed proof that Titans exist, it can be considered the starting point that led to several more Titan discoveries.

As for what ultimately happened to it after Monarch: Legacy of Monsters, the Ion Dragon was either severely wounded or killed by Godzilla during a battle in Axis Mundi in 2017. For now, it remains to be seen if he’s still alive in the Monsterverse. Either way, the Ion Dragon’s role in the destruction of the U.S.S. Lawton makes it a fundamental part of Monsterverse. After all, Monarch may not have been formed without it.
