Anime Adaptation of Shonen Jump Samurai Series Gets Exciting New Teaser

SUMMARY

Exciting news dropped for fans of Elusive Samurai, with a new promotional video giving viewers a sneak peek at the upcoming anime adaptation. The video introduces a new character, Nezu Kojiro, who is described as a loyal ally of the protagonist, Hojo Tokiyuki, in his quest to reclaim his rightful position as the leader of the Hojo Clan. Elusive Samurai blends historical fiction with creative twists, appealing to anime fans interested in feudal Japanese military history. It comes from the creator of Assassination Classroom and promises a unique take on shonen tropes.

Literature Review

As Yusei Matsui’s Elusive Samurai gears up for its premiere, fans are treated to another look in an all-new video of the upcoming anime series.

Fans of Shōnen Jump’s Elusive Samurai just got exciting news about the upcoming anime adaptation with a new promotional video, giving viewers a sneak peek at the upcoming anime as work on it progresses smoothly.

Posted on X by the Elusive Samurai anime official account, the promotional video reveals a new character visual of the Elusive Warrior member Nezu Kojiro (voiced by Mari Hino). In addition, the video also provides a brief introduction of Kojiro’s background, describing him as a loyal ally of the protagonist, the heir to the Kamakura shogunate, Hojo Tokiyuki, who will employ his bravery and expertise in swordsmanship to assist Tokiyuki in reclaiming his rightful position as the leader of the Hojo Clan.

This is the most recent in a series of promotional videos that Cloverworks, the anime company producing the series, has been putting out since news dropped in March 2023 that the three-year Weekly Shōnen Jump veteran manga would be adapted for television. However, it is only the second video to give fans a more detailed look at the characters. Elusive Samurai is set to premiere later in 2024.

Elusive Samurai Puts A Creative Spin On Historical Fiction Produced by Cloverworks, based on the manga by Yusei Matsui

Created by Yusei Matsui of Assassination Classroom fame, Elusive Samurai centers on Tokiyuki’s quest to avenge his father’s death and return his clan to prominence after the elder Hojo’s trusted counselor Takaoji Ashikaga betrayed the family and ended their reign as rulers. Due to his uncanny ability to escape dangerous situations unscathed, Tokiyuki narrowly escaped Ashikaga’s betrayal and has been on the run ever since. Despite being the heir to a clan known for its sword-wielding abilities, Tokiyuki has never been very good with blades, which sets him apart as a pretty unusual protagonist for a samurai story. Fortunately, while in exile, he finds a group of followers willing to risk their lives to protect him, who will form Elusive Samurai’s unique cast of heroes.

Elusive Samurai attracted a fair amount of attention for its mix of fact and fiction. There was a real Hojo Tokiyuki in Japanese history. Just as in the story, his father Takatoki was the last leader of the Kamakura Shogunate that ruled over Japan from 1192 to 1333, and was overthrown by his most trusted counselor, Takaoji. With its mix of historical facts and original spins on classic shonen tropes, Elusive Samurai is sure to attract anime fans interested in feudal Japanese military history.

Elusive Samurai Has Roots In Assassination Classroom

Prior to Elusive Samurai, Matsui worked on Assassination Classroom, the hit manga about an octopus-like superhuman creature known as Koro Sensei, who decides to teach a junior high school class for a year to give them a chance to kill him before he destroys the world. With a mix of action, thrills, and humor, Assassination Classroom appealed to a wide variety of fans. Elusive Samurai continues and expands on Matsui’s ability to depict serious subjects with a smile and to evolve shonen tropes with his unique touch, giving Shōnen Jump another potential blockbuster anime.
