Summary

The shocking reveal of a German spy in episode 4 of Masters of the Air parallels Inglourious Basterds In Quentin Tarantino’s Inglourious Basterds, Hicox’s small “three-finger” gesture gives away his true identity. The unexpected death and revelation of the German spy in Masters of the Air showcases the dangers of subtle aspects of war.

Sometimes it is inevitable that films and television that center on World War II are going to feel similar to each other, but when it comes to Masters of the Air episode 4 and Quentin Tarantino’s Inglourious Basterds, there is an undeniable similarity. In episode 4 of Masters of the Air, the 100th takes on another mission that ends in a major and mysterious tragedy, while Sgt. Charles K. Bailey and Sgt. William Quinn attempt to escape Belgium. While the former storyline is upsetting, the latter leads to some truly shocking and thought-provoking moments.

One highlight from Masters of the Air episode 4 is when a so-called American soldier named Bob is revealed to be a German spy. In one moment, the men are walking towards freedom, and in the next, Bob is being shot down. Though the Belgians don’t explain how they knew Bob was a German spy, it is revealed by a test performed on the men prior to leaving. Bob wrote the European date rather than the American one. Additionally, his lighter looked to be German, not American. This revelation shows just how dangerous the war was on all fronts.

Masters Of The Air’s Spy Discovery Is Similar To What Happens In Inglourious Basterds

Masters of the Air is not the only World War II project that has included spies being revealed. In 2009, famed director Quentin Tarantino made a war movie called Inglourious Basterds, and in it, there is a similar sequence wherein characters’ true identities are given away through small details. In Inglourious Basterds, Michael Fassbender’s character, Archie Hicox, helps Stiglitz and Wicki on their mission by going undercover at a Nazi-occupied pub. Ultimately, Hicox’s bad German accent grabs the attention of the German officers, but it is a small gesture he makes that tips them off to his real identity.

Hicox’s big mistake was the “three-finger gesture,” that he made. Though it seems like an arbitrary detail, this movement was notably British, not German, and this let the real German officers know that Hicox was not who he said he was. Hicox’s gesture ended up being fatal, as the only survivors of the ensuing shootout were Wicki and German film star Bridget von Hammersmark. Ultimately, this situation aligns fairly perfectly with Masters of the Air episode 4, except with the reverse situation. The German was revealed to the Americans rather than the British to the Germans.

Inglourious Basterds mixes real history and fiction by depicting an alternate history story of two plots to assassinate Nazi Germany leadership. Though it shares its name with a 1978 war film, the movie is not based on a true story, nor is it a remake.

Masters Of The Air’s German Infiltrator Is One Of Its Most Shocking Stories Yet

Though Masters of the Air already deals heavily in intense and shocking content, the German spy of episode 4 is definitely one of the series most surprising moments thus far. When the 100th embarks on a flight, the audience already has a sense of what is going to happen. However, when it came to Bob, no one was expecting his identity to be fake. Like Quinn and Bailey, the audience was likely shocked at Bob’s sudden death and revelation. It is scenes like these that show the subtle aspects of the war that were just as dangerous as all-out combat.
