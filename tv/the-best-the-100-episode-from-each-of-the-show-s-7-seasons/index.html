The Best The 100 Episode From Each Of The Show s 7 Seasons

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Strong character dynamics and moral dilemmas make The 100 a compelling watch, even through its ups and downs. “We Are Grounders: Part 2” from season 1 showcases intense action and sacrifices, setting the stage for the rest of the series. Season 5’s “Damocles: Part Two” could have served as a satisfying series finale, wrapping up character arcs effectively.

Literature Review

The 100 ran for seven seasons on The CW, and the show’s dipped at points — but the best episode of each season is a reminder of what made it great.

The 100 started out as a teen series about survival, but the CW show became much more as time went on — and the best episodes from each season showcase the series’ growth. Like any television series, The 100 has its ups and downs, with some seasons offering more consistent quality than others. The show’s early outings are often lauded as its best, but even the later seasons of The 100 have their strong points. The CW show is at its strongest when it’s juggling themes of survival and morality — and when its character dynamics are compelling. The latter is why the final seasons of The 100 got so much flak, as the character changes and relationship inconsistencies hurt the ending of the series. Even with The 100’s conclusion becoming one of the least satisfying TV finales out there, there are a few reminders of what made the show so great. The best episodes from each season showcase the cast’s talent and highlight the series’ themes. The 100 seasons 1-7 are currently available to stream on Netflix.

8 The 100 Season 1 - “We Are Grounders: Part 2” (Episode 13)

The 100 has a knack for delivering great finales, and season 1 starts this trend with episode 13, “We Are Grounders: Part 2.” After 12 episodes of conflict between the “sky people” and the Grounders, tensions come to a head in the first season’s final episode. The Grounders make good on their promise to attack the Hundred’s camp, and Clarke and her friends do everything they can to stop their advances. What ensues is an action-packed battle that requires some harrowing sacrifices to be made. “We Are Grounders: Part 2” also ends on a huge cliffhanger, generating excitement for everything to come. While there are many strong contenders for The 100 season 1’s best episode, “We Are Grounders: Part 2” takes the cake for its ability to blend all the strengths of the previous chapters in a single installment. The episode highlights the relationships formed over the course of the first 12 episodes — showcasing fantastic acting from the likes of Eliza Taylor and Bob Morley — and it drives home how far people will go to ensure their own survival. The stakes are higher than ever, and Clarke and co.’s response sets up the rest of the series perfectly.

7 The 100 Season 2 - “Blood Must Have Blood: Part 2” (Episode 16)

6
The 100 season 2’s best episode is also its finale, as “Blood Must Have Blood: Part 2” is one of the most heart-wrenching and memorable installments of the entire series. After being abandoned by their Grounder allies, Bellamy and Clarke are left to save their people from Mount Weather by themselves. Their desperation pushes them to do the unthinkable: kill everyone inside the mountain, choosing the lives of their people over both their enemies and allies. This choice is a harrowing one that haunts them for the remainder of the show. Emotions are high, and The 100’s cast conveys them well. “Blood Must Have Blood: Part 2” also has several of the best lines of the entire show, from “maybe there are no good guys” to “I bear it so they don’t have to.” In general, this installment drives home the impossible choices that come with being a leader, something season 2 consistently brings up through Clarke and Lexa’s interactions. While there are some close contenders from earlier in the outing, this one hits all the necessary beats.

5 The 100 Season 3 - “Watch The Thrones” (Episode 4)

Season 3’s “Watch the Thrones” breaks from the trend of The 100’s finales being its best episodes, but that’s for a good reason. The latter half of season 3 is bogged down by plot issues, and the killing of Lexa and Lincoln has tarnished this outing’s legacy. However, the earlier installments that take place in Polis are some of the CW series’ best chapters. “Watch the Thrones” is the greatest among them, ramping up the intensity of the show’s politics, showing Commander Lexa at her finest, and delivering plenty of satisfying moments between Lexa and Clarke. Lexa’s fight with Roan is one of the most gripping moments of The 100 season 3, leaving viewers to wonder if the fan-favorite character will survive the encounter. That fact that she rids herself of the Ice Queen’s plotting and declares Roan king further showcases her power, and it provides an unexpected twist in an episode that easily could have taken a more predictable route. The acting from Alycia Debnam-Carey is great, and Lexa’s comments about leaders doing what’s best for their people brings together the show’s themes. All in all, it’s a great chapter — and just slightly stronger than “Ye Who Enter Here.”

4 The 100 Season 4 - “Praimfaya” (Episode 13)

The 100 season 4 sees the Grounders and Skaikru preparing for another apocalypse, and the “Death Wave” finally arrives in the last episode. While the installments leading up to “Praimfaya” are packed with action and emotion, the finale itself proves just a bit stronger. “Praimfaya” sees characters like Octavia and Bellamy bidding farewell to one another — potentially for good — highlighting the relationships that define the series. As if that weren’t emotional enough, it also sees Clarke sacrificing herself to save her friends — one of the most powerful moments in the entirety of The 100. Bellamy and Clarke’s conversation about the head and the heart will leave viewers misty-eyed, and both Eliza Taylor and Bob Morley knock their scenes out of the park. It’s hard to watch as Bellamy makes the choice to leave Clarke behind, and his and Raven’s final moments could have made a stunning ending to the show. Of course, Clarke survives the Death Wave, resulting in three more seasons. Still, The 100 could have ended on a strong note with “Praimfaya,” one of its most poignant episodes.

3 The 100 Season 5 - “Damocles: Part Two” (Episode 13)

Perhaps the best episode of The 100 overall, season 5’s “Damocles: Part Two” probably should have been the series finale. Between Bellamy’s heartfelt speech about how Maddie “can be better” than they are to Monty’s unexpected video message, “Damocles: Part Two” offers a hopeful takeaway despite the tragedies of earlier seasons. It sees the characters finally learning from their mistakes and choosing humanity over survival. This is what the series builds towards all along, and the season 5 finale brings the show’s themes together brilliantly. While certain characters’ endings get fleshed out further in later seasons, “Damocles: Part Two” also offers a satisfying enough conclusion for each of their arcs. From Octavia’s first steps towards redemption to Clarke and Bellamy’s moves to reconcile, the character moments in The 100 season 5, episode 13 are powerful. Ending with Maddie as a burgeoning leader is also a great move.

2 The 100 Season 6 - “Nevermind” (Episode 7)

The 100 season 6 is where the series starts to truly go downhill, but there are a few strong episodes over the course of its run. The best is “Nevermind,” which sees Clarke and Josephine facing off inside Clarke’s mind. The episode is somewhat trippy, as Clarke ventures through her memories, bringing back some surprising faces and reminding viewers what makes The 100 so great. Perhaps “Nevermind” is the best episode for the nostalgia factor, but it also sees Clarke returning to form after some major changes in season 5. It also showcases Eliza Taylor’s talent, as well as Josephine actor Sara Thompson.

1 The 100 Season 7 - “Anaconda” (Episode 8)

The 100 season 7 doesn’t dig deeply enough into the early episodes’ lore, and its ending is one that leaves a bad taste in many viewers’ mouths even today. Perhaps it’s no surprise, then, that “Anaconda” is the strongest episode of the final outing. The 100 season 7, episode 8 takes viewers back in time, showing how the first apocalypse unfolded. Intended as a backdoor pilot for the scrapped The 100 prequel series, “Anaconda” introduces strong characters and expands upon the show’s backstory and lore. For this reason, it’s season 7’s best episode — and evidence that The 100 prequel should have gone forward.

The 100
TV-14 Sci-Fi Mystery Drama

The 100 is a sci-fi post-apocalyptic TV series set ninety-seven years after a nuclear war has destroyed civilization. When a spaceship housing humanity’s lone survivors send one hundred juvenile delinquents back to Earth in hopes of possibly re-populating the planet, they discover that Earth is still inhabited. Now constantly in a state of power flux with warring clans, cannibals, and mountain-dwellers, the juveniles must attempt to survive while re-establishing contact with the Ark.
