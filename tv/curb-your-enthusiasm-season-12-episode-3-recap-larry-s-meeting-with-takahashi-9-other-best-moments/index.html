SUMMARY

Warning: This article contains spoilers for Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 3. Curb Your Enthusiasm’s final season continues with another great episode – episode 3, “Vertical Drop, Horizontal Tug” – full of classic Larry David moments. In Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 3, “Vertical Drop, Horizontal Tug,” Larry returns from Atlanta and resumes his usual antics in Los Angeles. Larry accidentally hits an Academy Award-winning actor with a golf ball (and later a bagel), argues with Richard Lewis about who’s going to die first, and brokers an insemination agreement between Leon and a married woman who’s trying to conceive. There aren’t many developments in season 12’s overarching storyline in episode 3. There’s hardly a mention of Larry’s arrest in Georgia and the lengthy trial that is sure to ensue. Rather than furthering the serialized plot, “Vertical Drop, Horizontal Tug” is more of a typical standalone episode of Curb Your Enthusiasm – and a classic one at that. From Irma’s “sex musk” to Larry being “railroaded” by a Tubman to more trouble with the country club’s manager, Mr. Takahashi, Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 3 has some hilarious moments.

Curb Your Enthusiasm Season 12 Episode 2 Recap: Larry’s Hair Dye Parody & 9 Other Best Moments
From Jeff’s runny hair dye to Larry’s mysterious familiar lawyer, there are plenty of hilarious moments in Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 2.

10 Larry, Jeff, Susie & Leon Return To L.A. The Atlanta adventure is over

After spending the first two episodes of the season in Atlanta, Curb Your Enthusiasm is back in Los Angeles. “Vertical Drop, Horizontal Tug” opens with Larry, Jeff, Susie, and Leon stepping off the plane in L.A. and complaining about their flight. There’s no specific mention of what’s going on with Larry’s trial after he pleaded “not guilty” at the end of episode 2, “The Lawn Jockey,” but the case will presumably continue in the L.A. courts as the season goes on.

9 Sienna Miller Is Larry’s New Love Interest Larry bumps into Sienna Miller at the airport

While he’s leaving the airport, Larry bumps into Sienna Miller, guest-starring as herself. Miller has heard about Larry’s heroic efforts to quench the thirst of a voter in Georgia and praises him for taking action. As they part ways, Miller tells Larry to keep in touch, which he takes as flirtation. He’s annoyed by this, because he’s stuck with Irma for at least six months on the orders of her A.A. sponsor, so he can’t date anyone else for the time being.

8 Wordle Spoilers Apparently, spoiling Wordle is the lowest a person can get

“Vertical Drop, Horizontal Tug” reveals that just about every character in the Curb Your Enthusiasm universe is an avid player of Wordle. Each day, Wordle gives players six chances to guess a five-letter word. According to Curb, giving away that five-letter word is the worst thing a person can do. Larry and Leon get mad at Irma for spoiling Wordle, Larry gets mad at Richard Lewis for spoiling it, and Ted, Cheryl, and Susie all get mad at Larry for spoiling it. Of all the terrible things Curb characters have done, spoiling Wordle doesn’t seem that bad.

7 Larry Gets “Railroaded” By A Tubman Larry decries the “un-Tubman-like behavior” of Harriet Tubman’s great-nephew

Larry’s new next-door neighbor is Duane Tubman, the great-nephew of Harriet Tubman, the abolitionist who pioneered the Underground Railroad. When Duane expects Larry to chip in for the removal of his lemon tree, Larry chastises him for “very un-Tubman-like behavior.” Larry believes Harriet Tubman would’ve brought him a basket full of lemons. Freddy Funkhouser says that Duane is “a Tubman in name only” and points out the irony of Larry getting “railroaded” by a Tubman.

6 Irma’s “Sex Musk” According to Irma, she releases a lot of musk during sex

When Freddy is telling everyone at the dinner table about having sex with his neighbor to help her get pregnant, Irma tells him that releasing “sex musk” is very good for conception. She turns to a dismayed Larry and says, “I release a lot of sex musk, don’t I, Larry?” Tracey Ullman continues to wring every possible laugh out of the character’s incessant unpleasantness and Larry David continues to nail Larry’s horrified reactions to Irma’s sex talk. They’re a classic on-screen pairing – no wonder David brought Ullman back for another season.

5 Larry Steals A Golf Lesson Larry eavesdrops on Troy Kotsur’s golf lesson

While sitting on a bench at the golf course, Larry overhears Troy Kotsur’s teacher giving him a lesson and picks up the titular trick that drastically improves his game. Later on, while he’s sitting in a bathroom stall, Larry eavesdrops on another golf lesson and picks up another useful tip. Larry’s ongoing rivalry with the golf teacher who keeps catching him eavesdropping on his lessons is one of the funniest running jokes in the episode.

4 Larry Has A Typically Complicated Relationship With Susie’s Puppy Frankie Larry has never had a great relationship with dogs

One of Curb Your Enthusiasm’s greatest running gags is Larry’s tumultuous relationship with dogs like Oscar and Angel Muffin. In “Vertical Drop, Horizontal Tug,” Ted and Cheryl get Susie a puppy for her birthday (which is a very Ted and Cheryl thing to do). Susie names the puppy Frankie after Frank Sinatra, and as usual, Larry shares a complicated relationship with the dog. Depending on his mood, he either wants to pet the puppy or tell it to “shut the f*** up!”

3 Larry Hits Troy Kotsur With A Golf Ball Larry didn’t bother yelling “Fore!” because the Oscar-winning actor is deaf

While Larry is waiting for Troy Kotsur, the Academy Award-winning star of CODA, to finish his turn on the golf course, Jeff and Richard tell him to go ahead and hit the ball, because he’ll never be able to hit it so far that it hits Kotsur. However, after picking up the “vertical drop, horizontal tug” tip from Kotsur’s teacher, Larry sends the ball soaring right into Kotsur’s back. Larry doesn’t bother yelling out, “Fore!,” because the deaf Oscar winner won’t hear him, so he tries waving instead, but it’s too little too late.

2 Larry Argues With Richard Lewis About Who Will Outlive The Other Larry wants to live 20 years longer than Richard out of spite

Larry and Richard’s arguments often escalate to objectionable places, but their latest argument takes the cake. After bickering about being included in each other’s will, Larry and Richard start arguing about which one of them is going to outlive the other. Larry is determined to stay alive for another 20 years just to outlive Richard out of spite. Jeff is Larry’s friend, but Richard is his brother – they can say anything to each other.

1 Larry Uses Leon’s “Trick” To Get Out Of Trouble With Mr. Takahashi “Long-Ball Larry” makes a comeback

All throughout Curb Your Enthusiasm season 12, episode 3, Leon’s testicles keep popping out of his shorts. At first, Larry is repulsed by the sight of them. But when the sight of Leon’s testicles causes Duane to cut his meeting with Larry short and not charge him for the lemon tree removal, Larry realizes the power of exposed balls. When he’s called in for another meeting with the country club’s manager, Mr. Takahashi, Larry borrows Leon’s “trick” and wears a pair of short shorts to the meeting. Suffice to say, Mr. Takahashi doesn’t keep him there long.
