5 Times Anakin Skywalker Proved He Really Was A Great Teacher

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Anakin & Ahsoka’s unique mentorship in The Clone Wars show the power of family & bonds in the Star Wars universe. Anakin’s tough love challenges boosted Ahsoka to survive even after their apprenticeship ended. Anakin’s holograms for Ahsoka reveal his dedication as a teacher, influencing future generations of Jedi.

Literature Review

As Ahsoka Tano’s master during the Clone Wars, Anakin Skywalker had many teachable moments that greatly impacted her, and here are some of his best.

At its heart, Star Wars has always been about family and the relationships that shape someone, and Anakin Skywalker had an amazing master-apprentice relationship with his Padawan, Ahsoka Tano. Anakin originally had no interest in taking on a Padawan, but after a mission on Christophsis seen in the Star Wars: The Clone Wars movie, Anakin agrees to train the young Togruta Jedi. Over the course of The Clone Wars, audiences get to see both Anakin and Ahsoka grow, mainly because of the multiple lessons they learn from one another. During The Clone Wars season 5, Ahsoka is falsely accused of bombing the Jedi Temple and, despite Anakin knowing and eventually proving Ahsoka’s innocence, Ahsoka chooses to leave the Jedi Order. Even after Ahsoka’s apprenticeship ended, she was able to use the knowledge she gained while training under Anakin in whatever struggles she faced. Especially after the declaration of Order 66 and Ahsoka’s time as part of the Rebel Alliance, she used skills learned from Anakin to ensure she remained safe, but still able to help others. Here are 5 of his best moments as Ahsoka’s teacher.

How To Watch Star Wars Movies In Order
Here is how to watch all Star Wars movies and TV shows chronologically and in order of release, and how each fits into the Star Wars timeline.

5 Ahsoka Is Able To Survive Trandoshan Hunters Star Wars: The Clone Wars Season 3, Episode 21 “Padawan Lost” & Episode 22 “Wookiee Hunt”

The final arc of The Clone Wars season 3 features Ahsoka Tano getting kidnapped by Trandoshans and hunted for sport on a remote island. Immediately, Ahsoka is looking for ways to take down her captors and escape the island. With the help of other Jedi younglings fighting to stay alive, Ahsoka and her allies hatch a plan to save themselves. While Ahsoka is missing, Anakin is constantly worried and feels it’s his fault she was taken. Plo Koon provides a voice of reason, saying that Anakin had taught Ahsoka everything he knew, and that meant she’d find her way back. The pair’s reunion at the end of “Wookiee Hunt” provides a sweet moment where Ahsoka credits her ability to survive to her master. It’s a wonderful moment where Anakin acknowledges that he has taught Ahsoka well, and he’s visibly proud of her. Ahsoka showed great strength and leadership when the odds were not in her favor, and that is a testament to what she had learned from Anakin, who never seemed to be phased by the odds, and proudly led his men into battle.

4 Anakin Takes Ahsoka’s Training To The Next Level Tales of the Jedi Season 1, Episode 5 “Practice Makes Perfect”

Tales of the Jedi provided audiences with six shorts that helped enrich many stories from the prequel and Clone Wars era of Star Wars. Tales of the Jedi mainly focused on Count Dooku and Ahsoka Tano, and episode 5 brought audiences back to key points in Ahsoka’s journey as a Jedi. As a challenge, Anakin enlists the help of Captain Rex and some of the 501st to help train Ahsoka. With their blasters set to stun, Ahsoka is tasked with fending off her soldiers. Ahsoka passes out constantly from the hits she takes, but Anakin insists she keeps going. While it may seem like tough love, Anakin did this harsh kind of training to make her a better Jedi. He tells Ahsoka, “As your master, I’m responsible for you. The best way I can protect you is to teach you how to protect yourself.”This kind of training ends up being exactly what saves Ahsoka when Order 66 is declared, as she had trained how to fend off Rex and the rest of the 501st for most of her apprenticeship. Anakin’s training was harsh, but also prepared Ahsoka for when the Republic fell and things became more dangerous.

3 Anakin Finishes Ahsoka’s Training Ahsoka Season 1, Episode 5 “Part Five: Shadow Warrior”

Hayden Christensen made a triumphant return to Star Wars for Ahsoka season 1. In Ahsoka episode 5, Anakin appeared to his former apprentice, saying that it was time he finished her training. The two had last seen each other over ten years prior, when Ahsoka took on Anakin’s alter ego, Darth Vader in Star Wars Rebels. Anakin’s main point to Ahsoka was to make her realize she had to choose to live, giving her the ultimatum to live or die. Ahsoka had to learn to not fear the past, but instead embrace it and move forward and be better. Anakin took Ahsoka through a journey in the World Between Worlds, bringing The Clone Wars to live-action for the first time. Two different battles were featured, including an early mission Anakin and Ahsoka were a part of on Ryloth, as well as Ahsoka’s battle during the Siege of Mandalore. Anakin resorted to harsh methods and dueled his former apprentice to make her embrace all that she was—the good and the bad. This lesson was pivotal for Ahsoka and helped her become Ahsoka the White. Hayden Christensen is not yet confirmed to be returning as Anakin Skywalker for Ahsoka season 2.

2 Anakin Teaches Ahsoka How To Persevere The Clone Wars Season 1, Episode 19 “Storm Over Ryloth”

One of Anakin Skywalker’s biggest flaws was his confidence in his own abilities, something that had rubbed off on Ahsoka early on in her apprenticeship. In “Storm Over Ryloth,” Ahsoka is tasked with leading a squadron of clones in a space battle, but defies Anakin’s direct order—leading to a majority of her men being killed. It was up to Anakin to explain to Ahsoka that while she could handle what the Separatists were throwing her way, she still had to lead her men, even if that meant losing the battle to save them. It was a costly lesson to learn, but certainly one Ahsoka never made again. Anakin expertly navigated Ahsoka’s guilt and feelings of failure to make the tragedy a learning experience. He never lost faith in his Padawan, but instead expected her to step up and rise to the occasion when she ultimately had to lead the 501st again. This was one of Anakin’s and Ahsoka’s first missions together, so it was pivotal to see Anakin mentoring his Padawan so early on in The Clone Wars to further establish that relationship.

1 Anakin Leaves Holograms For Ahsoka Star War Rebels Season 2, Episode 18 “Shroud of Darkness” & Ahsoka Season 1, Episode 7 “Part Seven: Dreams and Madness”

While never seen during the Clone Wars era of Star Wars, it’s been revealed that Anakin Skywalker made training holograms for Ahsoka to practice. Anakin made more than twenty of these recordings, meaning there are at least 18 that audiences have yet to see. Taking extra time to make these for his Padawan was intentional and in the recording seen in Ahsoka, Anakin explains that he won’t always be there to help her, so she needs to learn how to fight for herself. It’s known that the recording seen in Rebels was also used by other Jedi, namely Ezra Bridger, to help with his lightsaber training. While the recordings weren’t made for anyone other than Ahsoka, Anakin inadvertently also helped train a whole new generation of Jedi. If Ahsoka was willing to let Ezra view Anakin’s recordings, Ahsoka would likely allow her own apprentice, Sabine Wren, to do the same. It’s unclear if Anakin Skywalker will return for Ahsoka season 2, but hopefully, audiences will get to see more of Anakin as one of the best teachers in Star Wars.
