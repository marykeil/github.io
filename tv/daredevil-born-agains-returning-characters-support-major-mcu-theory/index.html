Daredevil Born Agains Returning Characters Support Major MCU Theory

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Several actors from Netflix’s Daredevil series will be reprising their roles in the MCU’s Daredevil: Born Again, but it may not end well for all of them. There is speculation that returning characters, including Karen Page, will only appear in three episodes of Born Again, possibly indicating their demises. Daredevil: Born Again might explore the impact of the Blip on the MCU’s everyday inhabitants, with recent rumors perhaps supporting this idea.

Literature Review

More Marvel Television characters are returning in the MCU’s Daredevil: Born Again, but details about their return could support a shocking theory.

Details concerning returning characters from Marvel Television’s Defenders Saga in Marvel Studios’ Daredevil: Born Again could support a major and world-changing MCU theory. Since being announced in 2022, Daredevil: Born Again has been through some massive changes, and these changes have expanded to encompass every Disney+ series currently being developed by Marvel Studios. These changes have brought about some good news, however, as several actors from Netflix’s Daredevil series have now been confirmed to be reprising their roles in the MCU proper, though a popular theory suggests that Phase 5’s Daredevil: Born Again won’t end well for all of them.

Daredevil: Born Again’s cast features the returns of Charlie Cox as Matt Murdock’s Daredevil, Vincent D’Onofrio as Wilson Fisk’s Kingpin and Jon Bernthal as Frank Castle’s Punisher, all of whom debuted in Netflix’s Daredevil. More recently, Wilson Bethel was confirmed to be returning as Benjamin Poindexter, and Deborah Ann Woll and Elden Henson have also been confirmed to be reprising their beloved roles of Karen Page and Foggy Nelson, respectively. These inclusions are incredibly exciting for the upcoming series, suggesting that Born Again may be a continuation of the popular Marvel Television series, but their returns could also spell tragedy.

              The MCU's Daredevil: Born Again Is Looking Even More Like A Proper Daredevil Season 4   Following another exciting character return, the MCU's Daredevil: Born Again is looking more and more like a proper fourth season of the Netflix show.

Karen Page & Foggy Nelson May Only Return For 3 Episodes Of Daredevil: Born Again

While nothing has yet been confirmed by Marvel Studios, recent speculation suggests that both Wilson Bethel and Deborah Ann Woll will only be appearing in three episodes of Daredevil: Born Again (via X). Elden Henson’s fate as Foggy Nelson is less clear, but it’s possible he may not be a prominent supporting character either. This may cause confusion, as Foggy and Karen Page are crucial to Matt Murdock’s career as a lawyer, and to his activities as the vigilante Daredevil, but the idea of them appearing in only a small portion of Born Again could support a heart-breaking MCU theory.

An old theory concerning Daredevil: Born Again suggests that the series might be the perfect place to finally explore Avengers: Endgame’s five-year-long Blip. Most projects after Endgame have all but ignored the events of the Blip, but Born Again’s grounded and gritty nature means it can examine the impact of the Blip on the MCU’s everyday inhabitants. Rumors of Karen Page and Foggy Nelson only being included in Born Again for a short time perhaps hint at them falling victim to Thanos’ Infinity War snap, leaving Matt Murdock alone, and giving Born Again a brilliant opportunity to explore the Blip.

Born Again Is Retaining Some Of Its Pre-Overhaul Content

This theory is again supported by the fact that some of the material filmed prior to Daredevil: Born Again’s creative overhaul is still being kept in the series. Before 2023’s WGA and SAG-AFTRA strikes, Marvel Studios had started production on Born Again, but the series didn’t include Karen and Foggy, and saw Matt operating a new law firm without his friends. The creative overhaul of the series has created the opportunity for Deborah Ann Woll and Elden Henson to return, but it seems Matt still eventually end up starting his own firm, perhaps dissolving the new Nelson, Murdock & Page.

              Daredevil: Born Again Gets  r To The Original Netflix Show With New Series Update   As Daredevil: Born Again's release draws nearer, a new update about the Marvel Cinematic Universe series makes it even closer to the Netflix show.

If Daredevil: Born Again shows Karen Page and Foggy Nelson being killed by Thanos, it makes sense for Matt Murdock to spend the next five years moving on and starting a new direction. This would explain why he leaves Nelson, Murdock & Page to start his own business. It’s possible that Karen Page may leave New York City following her return after the Blip, perhaps deciding to return to Vermont to reunite with her estranged father following her ordeal, which would leave Matt to run his new law firm, and Foggy perhaps returning to work at Hogarth, Chao & Benowitz.

Born Again Rumor Supports Major Blip Exploration Theory

Assuming these rumors are true, it stands to reason that the first three episodes of Daredevil: Born Again would continue on from the finale of Marvel Television’s Daredevil season 3. Set photos for the Phase 5 series have shown Charlie Cox, Elden Henson and Deborah Ann Woll walking out of an office with Nelson, Murdock & Page signage, suggesting this may be the case. However, if Woll is only set to appear in three episodes, it’s possible that the Blip may occur early-on, creating the perfect opportunity for a large portion of Born Again to explore life during the Blip.

While Karen Page and Foggy Nelson would likely return later on in Born Again, it would be great for the series to investigate life during the Blip. Only a few projects have gifted brief glimpses into the Blip, despite fans calling for Marvel Studios to show what went on during this tumultuous period. Most recently, Daredevil was shown fighting Maya Lopez’s Echo during the Blip, suggesting his feud with Wilson Fisk’s Kingpin was exacerbated by the loss of his friends. Confirmed details surrounding Daredevil: Born Again are scarce, but the series could alter the landscape of the street-level MCU significantly.
