Real-Life Hall Of Fame Baseball Player John Smoltz s Cameo In The Hill Explained

SUMMARY

MLB Hall of Famer John Smoltz plays a baseball commentator in the final scene of The Hill, showcasing his success as a player and media personality. The Hill tells the true story of Rickey Hill, a young man with physical disabilities who overcomes great odds to become a talented baseball player in Texas. John Smoltz’s own career parallels Rickey Hill’s journey, as he was signed by the Detroit Tigers out of high school and went on to win multiple awards, including induction into the Baseball Hall of Fame.

Literature Review

Hall of Fame baseball player John Smoltz has an extended cameo in the Netflix film The Hill that mirrors a role he now has in real life.

Major League Baseball Hall of Famer John Smoltz has a cameo in The Hill, and his role is explained by his successful career following his tenure as a professional baseball player. Smoltz’s appearance in the movie appears in the climactic final scene where Rickey Hill is given a shot to prove himself as one of the best baseball players in his state.

Starring Colin Ford as Rickey Hill and Dennis Quaid as Rickey’s father, James, The Hill is a true story about a young man overcoming great odds against him for the game he loved. Despite many physical disabilities, Hill was devoted to baseball and was able to become one of the most talented young players in the state of Texas. The Hill shows that his dedication did allow him to be successful at some of the highest levels of competition for as long as his body still let him play.

The Hill is currently streaming on Netflix.

John Smoltz Plays The Baseball Commentator In The Hill’s Climactic Game Scene

In the final climactic scene of The Hill, Rickey Hill plays in a scrimmage arranged for some of the best young baseball players in Texas. That game is arranged by legendary baseball scout Red Murff (Scott Glenn) and is meant to be a showcase for Wood to see if he is ready to face big-league talent. The game, played at a stadium in Brenham, Texas, features a game commentator played by Hall of Fame pitcher and commentator John Smoltz.

John Smoltz, while a long-time professional baseball player and current commentator, is not from Texas and has no true connection to the Rickey Hill story. His appearance, however, reflects both the success Smoltz had as a player and in the media following his playing career.

John Smoltz’s Real-Life Career As A Major League Baseball Player & Post-Retirement Color Commentator Explained

In one important way, John Smoltz’s real life story mirrors that of Rickey Hill. Smoltz was a highly-regarded baseball prospect in Lansing, Michigan and was planning to play baseball at Michigan State University. However, because of his extreme talent, he was signed straight out of high school by the Detroit Tigers, similar to how Rickey Hill was signed by the Montreal Expos at age 19.

Smoltz would be traded to the Atlanta Braves at age 20, where he would become a cornerstone of their pitching staff and help them win multiple Division titles in his 20 years with the team. From 1988 to 2008, Smoltz would make eight All Star teams, win the 1996 Cy Young Award, and league the lead in wins and strikeouts twice. Six years after his playing career ended in 2009, Smoltz was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame by 83% of the voters.

John Smoltz is the only pitcher in MLB history to have at least 200 wins and 150 saves.

In 2014, similar to his role in The Hill, John Smoltz would go to work for Fox Sports as a color commentator for Major League Baseball games. He would also do work for the MLB Network and would go on to win a Sports Emmy Award for the first time in 2021. Because of his experience and popularity calling professional games, he was asked to cameo as a radio announcer for the final scene where Rickey Hill goes 11-for-11 in the scrimmage.
