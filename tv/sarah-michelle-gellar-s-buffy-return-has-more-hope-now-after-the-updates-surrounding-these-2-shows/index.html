Sarah Michelle Gellar s Buffy Return Has More Hope Now After The Updates Surrounding These 2 Shows

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Sarah Michelle Gellar’s schedule is now open due to the cancelation of “Wolf Pack,” potentially allowing her to return to a “Buffy the Vampire Slayer” revival. Dolly Parton reveals that a revival of “Buffy” is being worked on, hinting at the return of the original characters. The success of a “Buffy” revival would heavily depend on Sarah Michelle Gellar’s involvement, as she was integral to the original show’s success.

Literature Review

Reports about a Buffy the Vampire Slayer revival have been circulating for years, and two recent updates indicate Sarah Michelle Gellar might return.

In an age where revivals and reboots of popular television shows are quickly becoming the norm, rumors about a possible Buffy the Vampire Slayer revival rage on, and new reports prove how a new Buffy project could easily include Sarah Michelle Gellar. As many know, Gellar starred as the titular character — Buffy Summers — in the 1997 supernatural drama series. The show aired on The WB for five seasons, from 1997 to 2001, before moving to UPN for its final two seasons. The series finale premiered on May 20, 2003, and since then, many have wanted more Buffy, and they have gotten it.

Following the Buffy the Vampire Slayer finale, Dark Horse Comics published a series of comic books written by Joss Whedon that continue Buffy and the Scooby Gang’s stories. Additionally, Audible released Slayers: A Buffyverse Story, a podcast featuring the return of James Marsters as Spike, Charisma Carpenter as Cordelia, Anthony Head as Giles, Juliet Landau as Drusilla, Amber Benson as Tara, and more in October 2023. The nine-episode audio series took place ten years after the events of the show’s finale and revolved around Spike traveling to an alternate reality. Now, a Buffy revival is reportedly in the works.

Wolf Pack’s Cancelation Frees Up Sarah Michelle Gellar’s Schedule

Sarah Michelle Gellar’s schedule has seemingly been cleared following the cancelation of Wolf Pack on Paramount+. The supernatural teen drama television series was created by Jeff Davis, who based the show on Edo van Belkom’s 2004 novel of the same name. Wolf Pack revolves around a group of teenagers bitten by a werewolf while trying to escape a raging fire in the forest. Gellar played Kristin Ramsey, an arson investigator tasked with discovering how the fire started. The series premiered in January 2023, and one year later, Paramount+ canceled it.

Wolf Pack’s ending means that Gellar won’t be under the contract of another series and could theoretically reprise her iconic role as Buffy Summers.

Despite positive fan reaction, Wolf Pack was not received well by critics, and the streaming platform seemingly wasn’t happy with the show’s ratings. However, according to Deadline, the biggest probable reason behind the cancelation was budget cuts, as Paramount+ has been axing shows left and right, and the company has undergone mass layoffs. Nevertheless, Wolf Pack’s ending means that Gellar won’t be under the contract of another series and could theoretically reprise her iconic role as Buffy Summers in a Buffy the Vampire Slayer revival.

Dolly Parton’s Buffy The Vampire Slayer Reboot Update Is A Good Sign For Sarah Michelle Gellar’s Return

Another recent update is a good sign regarding the probability of Sarah Michelle Gellar signing on to play Buffy again in a Buffy the Vampire Slayer revival. Dolly Parton, an uncredited producer on the 1997 series and its spinoff, Angel, through her production company, Sandollar Productions, revealed that a continuation of Buffy the Vampire Slayer is being worked on. Parton told Business Insider:

“They’re still working on [a Buffy revival]. They’re thinking about bringing it back and revamping it.”

Although Parton’s comments are mostly vague, the quote includes a few keywords that suggest those developing a Buffy the Vampire Slayer revival want it to include original characters from the 1997 supernatural drama series. The country singer said, “They’re thinking about bringing it back and revamping it.” Perhaps this means that the revival will bring back some of the original actors from the show, and the future project will revolve around them. If the producers of the reported Buffy continuation are trying to bring back the former series regulars, they would be remiss to exclude Gellar.

Buffy the Vampire Slayer is available to stream on Hulu.

Why A Buffy The Vampire Slayer Reboot Needs Sarah Michelle Gellar

Given the hype around Buffy the Vampire Slayer and reboots and revivals in recent years, a Buffy revival is inevitable. Although nothing has been made official, Dolly Parton’s comments and previous reports indicate that a continuation of the popular 1990s supernatural series is in development. Much is unknown about the revival, including its story and cast. Parton’s quote suggests that the original cast may be involved in the future project, but nothing is certain. However, if a Buffy revival does come to fruition and centers around the original Scooby Gang, Sarah Michelle Gellar has to be involved.

There would be no Buffy without Gellar’s Buffy Summers. She was integral to the show’s success, and one cannot think of the series without picturing Gellar’s face. It wouldn’t feel right if the revival didn’t feature Gellar in some capacity, even if she is limited to a smaller role. Hopefully, the cancelation of Wolf Pack and Parton’s update on a Buffy the Vampire Slayer revival will lead to Gellar reprising her role as Buffy in the upcoming project.
