10 TV Shows That Were Saved By One Perfect Casting Change

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Casting changes in TV shows can be risky, especially when it comes to child actors. Aubrey Anderson-Emmons successfully replaced the original actresses and portrayed Lily consistently in Modern Family. Recasting a character can work well if the new actor brings their own strengths and stays true to the character’s traits. Skylar Astin’s portrayal of Greg in Crazy Ex-Girlfriend added a fresh dynamic to the show while staying true to the themes of magical realism. Sometimes, recasting leads to groundbreaking representation. Eartha Kitt’s performance as Catwoman in Batman was a landmark moment for Black women in leading superhero roles, bringing new life to the character and raising the overall quality of the show.

Literature Review

Recasting an actor on a TV series doesn’t mean that a character is gone forever, and sometimes, the new actor brings a fresh take on the role.

The actor cast in a pivotal role in a television show can make or break the series, so when a TV series recasts a character, the new actor must benefit the show. These casting changes are almost always obvious to the audience. So, the reasoning the writers and creators give for the character looking different or being replaced by a new character must hold up within the world of the show. When these casting changes happen early on, it’s less significant, but the later they come in the series run, the more pressure there is to be perfect. A lot of the reception comes down to the ability of the actor and how they handle being a newcomer on the show. Fitting in among the existing characters and elevating their dynamic is vital. Additionally, there is a careful balance between standing out with a different performance style than the previous actor or mimicking the traits that predated them. Often, the change works best when the actors play to their strengths and have a strongly written character to bring to life. When these casting changes happen early on, it’s less significant, but the later they come in the series run, the more pressure there is to be perfect.

10 TV Characters Who Were Recast After Just 1 Season
Casting is important when making TV shows. However, creative differences or a lack of audience connection may prompt recasting after the show begins.

10 Modern Family (2009 - 2020) Lily; Original Actresses: Ella & Jaden Hiller; New Actress: Aubrey Anderson-Emmons

Modern Family
TV-PG

Release Date September 23, 2009 Seasons 11 Writers Christopher Lloyd , Steven Levitan Network ABC Streaming Service(s) Hulu Showrunner Christopher Lloyd , Steven Levitan

Casting a child actor is always a gamble, especially when the show sees the character age from infancy to young adulthood. Lily is introduced in the first season as a baby when Cam (Eric Stonestreet) and Mitchell (Jesse Tyler Ferguson) adopt her. For legal reasons, as a baby, she was played by the twins Ella and Jaden Hiller, but when the children left acting, Aubrey Anderson-Emmons became the full-time Lily. This was a successful decision as Anderson-Emmons stuck with the series until the end, which kept her character as consistent as a child actor can be.

9 Arrested Development (2003 - 2019) Ann; Original Actress; Alessandra Torresani; New Actress: Mae Whitman

Arrested Development

Release Date March 15, 2019 Seasons 5 Writers Mitchell Hurwitz Network CBS Streaming Service(s) Netflix Showrunner Mitchell Hurwitz

Arrested Development was a comedy with surrealist elements, so recasting Ann after a brief appearance in season 1 made sense for the tone of the show, as well as Ann’s character. Alessandra Torresani was the first Ann and was later replaced by Mae Whitman, but Arrested Development’s original plans for Ann were to recast her every time the character was seen, to drive home how forgettable the character was. Whitman ended up sticking around because she gave such a hilarious performance and lessened the need for a revolving door of Ann’s.

8 The Fosters (2013 - 2018) Jesus; Original Actor: Jake T. Austin; New Actor: Noah Centineo

the fosters

Release Date June 3, 2013 Seasons 5 Network ABC Streaming Service(s) Hulu

Noah Centineo has worked across both rom-com and action genres, but one of his first major roles came as the unexpected replacement for Jesus in the family drama The Fosters. When Jake T. Austin felt that his time on the series was no longer fulfilling, Centineo became the unlikely scene-stealer of the show starting in season 3. He remained on the show through the final season and has even made guest appearances on the spin-off, Good Trouble. Centineo may be more committed to his movie career at this point, but he has The Fosters to thank for his early exposure.

7 Crazy Ex-Girlfriend (2015 - 2019) Greg; Original Actor: Santino Fontana; New Actor: Skylar Astin

Crazy Ex-Girlfriend

Release Date October 12, 2015 Seasons 4 Writers Aline Brosh McKenna , Rachel Bloom Network The CW Streaming Service(s) Netflix , Apple TV+ Showrunner Aline Brosh McKenna

Crazy Ex-Girlfriend leading lady Rebecca (Rachel Bloom) was frequently entangled in dramatic romantic relationships, and her time with Greg was no exception. Santino Fontana played one of Rebecca’s less-than-healthy love interests, Greg, in season 1 and early season 2, but the actor decided to leave the series, resulting in a melodramatic breakup. However, the show brilliantly pivoted to recast Greg as Sklar Astin in season 4. Playing into the themes of magical realism consistently present in the show’s format, the version of Greg that returned in season 4 was a new man, and so Rebecca saw him differently.

6 Once Upon A Time (2011 - 2018) Robin Hood; Original Actor: Tom Ellis; New Actor: Sean Maguire

Once Upon A Time

Release Date October 3, 2011 Seasons 7 Writers Adam Horowitz Network ABC Streaming Service(s) Netflix Showrunner Adam Horowitz

As fairy tales were constantly being rewritten in Once Upon A Time, making Robin Hood the love of The Evil Queen’s (Lana Parrilla) life was par for the course in the series. Tom Ellis, now well-known for his role as Lucifer in Lucifer, was the first actor to take on the part, but as he left for other opportunities, Sean Maguire stepped up to be Regina’s leading man. After Ellis left, Robin became a major player in the series, so it only made sense for someone who was able to commit to several seasons to sign on for the role.

5 Shameless (2011 - 2021) Mandy; Original Actor: Jane Levy; New Actress: Emma Greenwell

Shameless

Release Date January 9, 2011 Seasons 11 Writers Paul Abbott Streaming Service(s) Netflix Showrunner John Wells

Mandy might not have been a member of the Gallagher family on Shameless, but she was frequently intertwined with their misadventures throughout the series. In season 1, Jane Levy portrayed Mandy, giving her a more youthful and mischievous feel that didn’t lend much depth to the character. When Emma Greenwell was brought on, it eased the transition to a more serious, tortured Mandy, who acutely felt the confines of her life on the South Side of Chicago. Like many characters on the show, her arc is a tragic one, but Greenwell makes it easy to empathize with her.

South Side hellraiser Mandy Milkovich was recast after Shameless season 1, with Emma Greenwell taking over the role from original actress, Jane Levy.

4 Batman (1966 - 1968) Catwoman; Original Actress: Julie Newmar; New Actress: Eartha Kitt

Batman 1960s
Adventure

Release Date March 18, 1968 Seasons 3 Writers Lorenzo Semple Jr. Streaming Service(s) Apple TV+

Eartha Kitt wasn’t just a talented singer but also a fantastic actress who brought new life to the role of Catwoman after Julie Newmar left Batman in season 3. Kitt becoming Catwoman was a landmark moment of representation for Black women in leading roles within the superhero genre. There was no better choice than Kitt, a charismatic performer who had great chemistry with Adam West. Though her time on the series only lasted a season, it was an unforgettable addition to the show that raised the overall quality of the program tremendously. There was no better choice than Kitt, a charismatic performer who had great chemistry with Adam West.

3 New Girl (2011 - 2018) Coach: Damon Waynes Jr.; Winston: Lamorne Morris

New Girl
TV-14

Release Date September 20, 2011 Seasons 7 Network FOX Showrunner Elizabeth Meriwether

Though Lamorne Morris’ character, Winston, wasn’t a direct replacement for Damon Waynes Jr.’s character, Coach, he essentially fulfilled the same role on the show. However, Winston became a fan-favorite character that evolved beyond what his character started as. Waynes Jr. briefly returned in season 4, but his character didn’t fit into the group dynamic the way that Winston did. Coach is one of the roommates in the pilot, but by episode 2, Winston had arrived, and with him, a more developed character that played off the other roommates more effectively.

2 Riverdale (2017 - 2023) Reggie; Original Actor: Ross Butler; New Actor: Charles Melton

Riverdale
Not Rated Drama

Release Date January 26, 2017 Seasons 7

Recently, Charles Melton received buzz for his role in May December, a film that showcased his range as an actor after his tenure on the TV series Riverdale. Melton stepped in as Reggie, a student at Riverdale High, in season 2, replacing Ross Butler, who had scheduling conflicts. Riverdale wasn’t taken seriously by critics, but its popularity was a testament to the actors. They were consistently reinventing their characters since the story arc of each season drastically changed the setting, period, and character dynamics. Melton seamlessly slipped into the role of Reggie as if he had always been there.

1 Game Of Thrones (2011 - 2019) Daenerys; Original Actress: Tamzin Merchant; New Actress: Emilia Clarke

Game Of Thrones

Release Date April 11, 2011 Seasons 8 Writers D.B. Weiss , George R.R. Martin , David Benioff Streaming Service(s) HBO Max Franchise(s) Game of Thrones Showrunner David Benioff , D.B. Weiss

After the first failed pilot, Game of Thrones recast the actress for Daenerys, taking a chance on Emilia Clarke despite her relatively unknown status. Of course, this was the best-case scenario for the series, which got picked up after massive reshoots and became one of HBO’s most popular series while it was airing. Despite the changes to Daenerys’ character that came under fire in the later seasons, Clarke never shied away from the complex dramatic scenes required of her. Watching her evolve from a frightened young woman to a true queen proved Clarke was the right choice for the show.
