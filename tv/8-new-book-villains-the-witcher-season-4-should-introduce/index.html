8 New Book Villains The Witcher Season 4 Should Introduce

SUMMARY

Literature Review

The Witcher season 4 is in danger of having a shortage of villains, but luckily, there are 8 book villains it can introduce to allay any fears.

The Witcher season 4 will need to add some new villains after some key character deaths in season 3, and eight book characters are excellent candidates for that role. The Witcher is based on the novel series of the same name by Polish author Andrzej Sapkowski, and though it takes no shortage of creative liberties, the show includes plenty of characters and stories from the book series. However, The Witcher season 3’s ending killed off several important villains, leaving a void in the show that will need to be filled. The Witcher season 4 will need to address its sudden shortage of villains, especially where Vilgefortz is concerned. The mage’s two main henchmen, Lydia and Rience, are both killed in season 3, leaving him working on his ambitious schemes alone. Further, The Witcher is in dire need of new characters in the Nilfgaardian faction, which is shrunken by Cahir’s defection and Fringilla’s scheming in season 3. Luckily, there are eight book villains who can easily be introduced and refresh The Witcher’s villain roster going into season 4.

Netflix’s The Witcher Cast & Character Guide Netflix’s fantasy series The Witcher stars Henry Cavill as a skilled monster slayer. Here’s our guide to the show’s cast and characters.

8 Leo Bonhart — Bounty Hunter Hired by Stefan Skellen to hunt Ciri

Leo Bonhart is one of the most intimidating villains in The Witcher book series, and he is an essential character for The Witcher season 4. Bonhart is a deadly swordsman known for killing witchers, which is incredibly impressive considering the mutations that make witchers stronger and faster than normal people. In the books, Bonhart is hired to track down Ciri, capturing and tormenting her for a time before she can escape. Bonhart is the perfect candidate for fixing The Witcher’s villain problem by adding a harrowing new antagonist to the mix.

7 Stefan Skellen — Imperial Coroner Member of the Nilfgaardian Secret Service

One of the most important book characters still missing from The Witcher is the Nilfgaardian coroner, Stefan Skellen. As a high-ranking member of the Nilfgaardian Secret Service, Skellen has the backing of Emhyr var Emreis, but he proves to be less loyal than the Imperator believes. Skellen is a complex character with shocking motives, and it would be a huge missed opportunity if he were not included in The Witcher. With the show’s current shortage of villains, season 4 is the perfect time to introduce this fascinating antagonist.

6 Vattier De Rideaux — Head Of Nilfgaardian Military Intelligence Viscount of Eiddon

Vattier de Rideaux is the head of Nilfgaardian military intelligence, and he is one of Emperor Emhyr’s closest advisors. Including Rideaux would be a great way to deepen The Witcher’s Nilfgaard plot by adding a complex new character with a fresh perspective within the Nilfgaardian faction. In the books, he is a highly intelligent character, but his vices ultimately lead to disaster for Nilfgaard, which could make for an incredibly engaging storyline in the show. Further, with Fringilla presumably leaving Nilfgaard to govern Cintra, The Witcher will need another character in Nilfgaard to stand beside Emhyr.

5 Schirrú — Half-Elf Assassin One of Vilgefortz’s henchmen

As for providing a new henchman for Vilgefortz, one of the best candidates from the book series for that role is the half-elf assassin, Schirrú. In the books, Schirrú works for Vilgefortz, carrying out various assassinations and other tasks that the mage deems unworthy of his personal attention. He is an intimidating figure and an adequate fighter, but his real skill is being a treacherous schemer. Someone will have to support Vilgefortz after Lydia and Rience’s deaths in The Witcher season 3, and Schirrú is probably the most logical option for that role.

4 Morteisen — Commander Of The Morteisen Battle Group Daerlanian officer in the Nilfgaarian Army

Morteisen is another character from the Nilfgaardian faction who has a good chance of showing up in The Witcher season 4. Morteisen is the commander of the Nilfgaardian force that Geralt and his friends fight at the Battle for the Bridge on the Yaruga, which earns Geralt the official right to his long-used moniker, Geralt of Rivia. If the show follows the books, then Morteisen should be a new villain in The Witcher season 4, though he may not last long. Even so, he represents another valuable opportunity for the show to grow its catalog of strong antagonists.

3 Isengrim Faoiltiarna — Scoia’tael Commonly known as the Iron Wolf

Isengrim Faoiltiarna is an influential member of the Scoia’tael who becomes a ranking member of the Nilfgaardian Army after earning the trust of Emperor Emhyr. In the books, Isengrim is an integral part of the Scoia’tael’s involvement in the coup on Thanned, and he later fights for Nilfgaard at the momentous Battle of Brenna. Isengrim could be used to tie the elven plot back into the Nilfgaardian one after Francesca vowed revenge against Emhyr in The Witcher season 3. He could also be used as a dissenting voice within the Scoia’tael faction, advocating for an alliance with Nilfgaard.

2 Auberon Muircetach — King Of The Alders King of the Aen Elle elves

In The Witcher books, Ciri travels to another world where she meets Auberon, King of the Aen Elle elves. The phrase Aen Elle refers to the elves of this world that Ciri visits, which is also where Eredin and the Wild Hunt originally came from. The Aen Elle take Ciri in because they believe in a prophecy that says her child will have untold power, and they want to use that power to take control across multiple dimensions. To reach that end, the Aen Elle try to convince Ciri to have a child with Auberon, so they can control the prophesied child of destiny. The Witcher show seems to be changing this story slightly, however, probably seeking to avoid the strange incest plot between Emhyr and Ciri. The show’s solution seems to be making Ciri the child of prohpecy rather than her child, which could change the way Auberon fits into the story. Though his role is likely to be different because of the story changes, it’s still important for The Witcher to include Auberon, because if Ciri is going to interact with the Wild Hunt as the show has suggested, she will have to travel to the land of the Aen Elle first.

1 Menno Coehoorn — Nilfgaardian Field Marshal Governor of Cintra

Menno Coehoorn is one of Emperor Emhyr’s most trusted generals in the books, as shown by his appointment as Governor of Cintra. The Witcher is nearing a period of all-out war between Nilfgaard and the North, so it’s important for the show to introduce some Nilfgaardian military figures to focus on during the war. Coehoorn is the perfect option for that role because he is a high-ranking member of the army and a well-known figure from The Witcher franchise. He would also work as part of the Cintran plotline, perhaps taking over as Cintran Governor if Fringilla declines the role.
