8 Percy Jackson Storylines The Disney Show Must Cover In Season 2

Literature Review

Percy Jackson and the Olympians season 2 needs to introduce several new storylines and characters which are vital to the overall plot of the series.

The introduction of supporting characters like Tyson, Circe, and Blackjack will add depth to the story and enhance character development. The Great Prophecy, which foretells Percy’s role in Olympus’ future, is a crucial plot point that needs to be explored in the next season.

Disney’s Percy Jackson and the Olympians has several storylines from the books to cover in season 2. Percy Jackson and the Olympians stars Walker Scobell as Percy, Leah Sava Jeffries as Annabeth Chase, and Aryan Simhadri as Grover Underwood. Season 1 follows the plot of The Lighting Thief, the first novel in Rick Riordan’s Percy Jackson book series. In the show’s first season, the main trio embarks upon a quest to prevent a war among the Greek gods, but discovers a bigger gathering threat along the way. Book fans know that there are countless more adventures, monsters, and conflicts among the gods in Percy, Annabeth, and Grover’s future. At this time, there is no official confirmation that the show is being renewed, but there have recently been some promising updates about Percy Jackson and the Olympians season 2. In Riordan’s second book, The Sea of Monsters, a bigger overall storyline starts to come together, with more supporting characters and subplots appearing. If Disney hopes to adapt the whole Percy Jackson story, they will need to include some of these storylines next season.

8 Biggest Differences Between Walker Scobell & Logan Lerman’s Percy Jacksons Although Logan Lerman and Walker Scobell both get key elements of Percy Jackson’s personality right, their iterations of the character differ.

8 Luke’s Past With Annabeth And Thalia Flashbacks of Luke, Annabeth, and Thalia would tell the audience more about Luke’s betrayal.

The fact that Luke Castellan (Charlie Bushnell) was like family to Annabeth should not be downplayed from how it is depicted in the books. Annabeth has a crush on Luke and never completely gives up hope that he will see the error of his ways. The divide between them could be fleshed out on the show with a few flashbacks of their time on the run with Thalia Grace, the daughter of Zeus (Lance Reddick). The show also includes the detail of Luke having a scar on the right side of his face. A flashback showing how he got the scar could be used to further contextualize his decision to side with Kronos. In the books, Luke got the scar when he was sent on a quest by his father Hermes (Lin-Manuel Miranda) and battled the dragon Ladon. This experience contributed to his growing resentment towards the Olympians.

7 Blackjack And Percy Talking To Horses Percy makes a Pegasus friend and learns he can communicate with horses.

Blackjack is a black Pegasus whom Percy frees from Kronos’ ship in Sea of Monsters. Blackjack then pledges his loyalty to Percy and is involved in many future adventures. Additionally, Percy can telepathically communicate with Blackjack because his father Poseidon (Toby Stephens) created horses. This amusing detail appears in the Lightning Thief novel, when Percy discovers his ability to talk to a zebra. Luckily, the show has not shied away from including the books’ goofiest details. Fans would be pleased to see Percy use this ability in the next season, along with Blackjack’s introduction.

6 Annabeth Faces The Sirens Annabeth decides she wants to hear the Sirens’ song – with disastrous results.

Percy and Annabeth also come across the Sirens during their voyage. Annabeth believes that she will become wiser by hearing the Sirens’ song, which will show her what she desires most. While Percy blocks out his hearing, he ties Annabeth to the mast of their ship. When she hears the Sirens, Annabeth sees her parents together and a version of Luke who isn’t working for Kronos. Annabeth frees herself from the mast and Percy has to stop her from swimming to the Sirens’ island. Annabeth then tells Percy that her fatal flaw is hubris, or pride. It is a moment that should be covered in the show because it further develops Annabeth’s character while incorporating more famous mythological beings.

68 Biggest Changes Disney’s Percy Jackson Show Makes To The Books Percy Jackson and the Olympians is being praised for its fidelity to the source material, though that does not mean there are no changes to the books.

5 Percy’s Feelings About Poseidon Claiming Tyson Percy finds out that he has a Cyclops half-brother.

The second book also introduces Tyson, a kind-hearted Cyclops whom Poseidon officially claims as his son when Percy gets back to Camp Half-Blood. The gods do not typically claim their monster children like this and Percy is frustrated with his father because of the judgment he gets at camp for being around Tyson. Poseidon is also not speaking to Percy, which doesn’t help. However, Percy grows to love Tyson as a brother and the two have a heartfelt relationship that should be included in the show.

4 Clarisse And Percy Become Friends (Kind Of) Clarisse and Percy are forced to help each other out, leading to mutual respect.

Clarisse La Rue (Dior Goodjohn), daughter of Ares (Adam Copeland), is presented as a bully archetype when Percy meets her at camp. However, they come to at least respect each other. The quest to find the Golden Fleece to heal Thalia’s tree is given to Clarisse, but Percy and Annabeth follow her anyway. However, Percy correctly interprets Clarisse’s prophecy and tells her that she needs to take the Fleece back to camp alone. As a result, Clarisse gets most of the glory for the quest succeeding. Percy and Clarisse still don’t necessarily like each other, but she appreciates that he let her be the hero, and they recognize that they are fighting for the same cause. Goodjohn only appears briefly as Clarisse in season 1, and it will be great to see her character development next season.

3 Circe’s Spa Percy and Annabeth run across Circe, who appears in the Odyssey.

Sea of Monsters also features the famous witch Circe. Percy and Annabeth run across “C.C.’s” spa in the Sea of Monsters, where Annabeth gets a spa treatment and Percy is temporarily turned into a guinea pig. Season 1 allows some time for Medusa (Jessica Parker Kennedy) to discuss her story, revealing harsh realities about how women are treated in Greek mythology. It would be fitting if Circe was able to do the same, since she is known for turning the men who came to her island into pigs. At Circe’s spa, Percy also meets the pirate Blackbeard and Reyna, the latter of whom is a major character in Riordan’s Heroes of Olympus series. Reyna and her sister are Circe’s assistants but are taken captive by Blackbeard’s crew when they leave the spa. It will be interesting to see if the showrunners try to include Reyna in this episode. This would indicate their intentions to eventually expand the story into a Rick Riordan universe, beginning with a Heroes of Olympus sequel TV show.

2 Athena And Annabeth Athena continues to play an important role in Annabeth’s story.

Athena does not appear until the third book, but it seems unlikely that the showrunners will want to wait another season to introduce Annabeth’s mother. They have already moved up the first appearances of Hephaestus (Timothy Omundson) and Hermes. Athena, the goddess of wisdom, is an intimidating and calculating character. There is a lot to unpack with her, and the writers need to get started soon if they want to cover everything. Athena expects a lot from Annabeth and doesn’t like Percy. Athena and Poseidon are rivals in Greek mythology because the people of Athens picked Athena as their patron over Poseidon.

All 11 Greek Gods In Disney’s Percy Jackson TV Show Explained Disney’s Percy Jackson and the Olympians TV show, which covers Rick Riordan’s The Lightning Thief and its sequels, features multiple Greek gods.

1 The Great Prophecy Percy has a bigger role to play in Olympus’ future than he knows.

Season 1 tells the audience that Zeus, Poseidon, and Hades (Jay Duplass) agreed not to have any more demigod children because their children were too powerful. However, there is a more specific reason. In the books, the Great Prophecy foretells that a child of one of the “Big Three” gods will either destroy or save Olympus. This prophecy was first heard around the time of World War II, when the three brothers made their pact. This is hinted at in the Percy Jackson season 1 finale. Kronos (Nick Boraine) tells Percy in a dream that “[Percy’s] survival is key to [Kronos’] return.” Both Zeus and Poseidon can be accused of endangering Olympus by having a demigod child: Zeus with Thalia, and Poseidon with Percy. They are also unaware of Hades’ two surviving children, who were born before the pact was made. The prophecy is a vital plot point that needs to be explained in the next season of Percy Jackson and the Olympians because it is the backbone of the whole story.
