15 Star Wars Quotes That Prove Why Baylan Skoll Is Ahsoka s Best Character

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Baylan Skoll is the standout character in Ahsoka , with memorable quotes that showcase his wisdom and moral complexity. Baylan’s dialogue raises questions and challenges other characters, making his interactions enjoyable to watch. Baylan’s ability to manipulate others and his long-term perspective make him a compelling and unique Star Wars villain.

Literature Review

While Ahsoka brought back several fan-favorite Star Wars characters, newcomer Baylan Skoll was the show’s best, highlighted by many memorable quotes.

Ahsoka Tano may be the titular Star Wars character of Ahsoka season 1, but Baylan Skoll’s catalog of quotes proves that he is the show’s best character. Baylan earned instant acclaim when the series premiered, with each episode revealing more about his motivations and how far he would go to achieve his goals. The late Ray Stevenson’s brilliant portrayal elevated Baylan’s already well-written dialogue to make him Ahsoka’s standout character. Baylan’s most memorable Ahsoka quotes collectively demonstrate his wisdom, ruthlessness, and moral complexity, while also raising questions that keep the viewer guessing. His interactions with the other characters are always a treat, either by offering a unique perspective or by challenging them in unexpected ways. 15 Ahsoka quotes prove why Baylan Skoll is a perfect Star Wars villain and one of the best characters of the Disney era.

15 “You’re right about one thing, Captain. We are no Jedi.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 1 “Part One: Master and Apprentice”

Baylan’s introduction proved he would be a perfect foil for Ahsoka Tano, using the same line she said to Darth Vader in the season 2 finale of Star Wars Rebels. Whereas Ahsoka continued to defend the light and protect people at all costs, Baylan chose a darker path, and this scene demonstrates the brutality hidden beneath his calm exterior. It also raises several questions about Baylan’s past that made him able to transition so easily between formal negotiation and deadly execution.

14 “For some, war. For others… a new beginning.” / “Power. Such as you’ve never dreamed.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 2 “Part Two: Toil and Trouble”

Recognizing things as they are is often a sign of wisdom, and like Ahsoka, Baylan can always view situations objectively while still pursuing his goals. His poetic delivery perfectly summarizes what is to come and assures Shin Hati that their actions will lead to victory. This is one of Baylan’s most memorable quotes in Ahsoka and highlights the dichotomy of light and dark in the Star Wars galaxy.

13 “Her presence in the Force is elusive. Yet her determination is vivid. She is coming.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 2 “Part Two: Toil and Trouble”

Stevenson’s performance always elevates even the most simple dialogue in Ahsoka, making Baylan’s reading of his opponent highly captivating. Baylan always sees things as they are, and this carries over to his Force perception, effectively explaining Ahsoka’s presence in the Force and quickly determining that she will find them. It’s a small but powerful moment that develops Baylan’s connection with Ahsoka two episodes before the characters meet face to face.

12 “To kill her will be a shame. There are so few Jedi left.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 2 “Part Two: Toil and Trouble”

Baylan is far more compelling and interesting than most Star Wars villains due to moments when his noble side shines through, and his regret over killing Ahsoka is one of the most vivid. He’s not afraid to take her life if it means achieving his goals, but he takes no pleasure in it, offering a quick glimpse into the man he was before the fall of the Jedi Order. It also makes the viewer sad when the two eventually lock blades, knowing that they could have been allies in another life.

11 “Faith? I lost that a long time ago.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 4 “Part Four: Fallen Jedi”

Even so, Ahsoka makes sure the audience understands that, despite his noble qualities, Baylan is not the Jedi he once was. His blunt reply to Morgan about his lack of faith is understandable, yet it’s also telling, highlighting a major difference that led him and Ahsoka down separate paths. His viewpoint is understandable after everything he lost, but it’s also a heartbreaking reminder of how far Baylan has fallen.

10 “Everyone in the Order knew Anakin Skywalker. Few would live to see what he became. Surely that must leave a mark.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 4 “Part Four: Fallen Jedi”

Baylan may not be a Sith Lord, but he is a master of manipulation, and his second line to Ahsoka cuts straight to what he knows will hurt her most. It’s also a fascinating account of Anakin’s turn to the dark side, acknowledging the burden of the Jedi who survived the purge. Baylan notes how the Jedi who died never had to learn that he betrayed them, unlike survivors who learned the truth. It reveals that Baylan has accepted this reality so it can no longer harm him, but he still hopes to use it against Ahsoka.

9 “It is an unfortunate evil, but speaks to a greater truth. One must destroy in order to create.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 4 “Part Four: Fallen Jedi”

Villains see themselves as the hero of their own story, so their actions are what separate them from the hero. This quote illustrates that Baylan’s intentions are good, but unlike Ahsoka, he is willing to sacrifice others and cause suffering to achieve what he feels is a better future. Despite this, there is truth to what Baylan says, demonstrating Ahsoka’s strength when she stands against him despite his words.

8 “It didn’t need to come to this. But you know no other way.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 4 “Part Four: Fallen Jedi”

Baylan’s final line to Ahsoka during their first duel cuts just as deep as his first, attacking her character due to her history of violence. Ahsoka admitted in Star Wars: The Clone Wars season 7 that being a soldier was all she knew since becoming a Padawan, and her experience in the war helped shape her destiny. Baylan’s words are effective because he believes what he says, forcing the viewer to pause and wonder if he’s right that their duel could have been avoided.

7 “Your family died on Mandalore because your Master didn’t trust you.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 4 “Part Four: Fallen Jedi”

Baylan shows off his ability to manipulate others again with Sabine, reading her thoughts and using her past against her. While he could easily kill Sabine and take the map before she destroys it, Baylan chooses a sympathetic approach and encourages her to join him, knowing she could be useful. His line finally reveals the fate of Sabine’s family and twists the narrative against Ahsoka, convincing Sabine that going with him is her best option to not lose any more family.

6 “I gave her my word. And unlike her former Master… I shall keep mine.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 4 “Part Four: Fallen Jedi”

Baylan using Ahsoka’s actions against Sabine doesn’t stop there, as he orders Shin to spare her life. While he could have let Sabine die after getting the map, Baylan decides to play the long game and keep her as an ally. It also creates a greater rift between Sabine and Ahsoka, something Baylan can use no matter how events play out.

5 “The fall of the Jedi, rise of the Empire. It repeats again and again and again.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 6 “Part Six: Far, Far Away.”

This quote is Baylan’s most insightful, calling out the never-ending struggle between light and dark that constantly plunges the galaxy into war. It’s also consistent with Baylan’s character, as he prefers to take a long-term view, rather than paying too much attention to a single conflict. The scene could also be viewed as meta-commentary on how the Star Wars franchise tends to repeat itself at different points in the timeline, hinting that Ahsoka season 2 could tackle something new.

4 “What I seek is the beginning, so I may finally bring this cycle to an end.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 6 “Part Six: Far, Far Away.”

Despite his flaws, viewers can respect Baylan for not falling into the same trap as other Star Wars villains, who only care about gaining power for themselves. He recognizes that the kind of power the Empire seeks is only temporary, so he seeks a greater power that can help end the cycle altogether. It’s incredibly compelling writing that leaves the audience dying to know exactly what Baylan plans to do.

3 “I miss… the idea of it. But not the truth, the weakness. There was no future there.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 6 “Part Six: Far, Far Away.”

Star Wars has continued to focus on the problems with the Jedi Order that led to its destruction, and Baylan summarizes it perfectly. He acknowledges that the Order did mean something to him, almost wishing he could still believe it, but he is unable to ignore the mistakes of the Jedi. Baylan’s mentality expands on Luke Skywalker’s debate with Rey about whether the Jedi Order should end or evolve.

2 “Something calls to me. Can’t you hear it? Something stirs here. Can’t you see it?” Ahsoka season 1, episode 6 “Part Six: Far, Far Away.”

Some of the most memorable Star Wars dialogue feels poetic, a fitting description for Baylan’s delivery to Shin. He sounds like a prophet trying to convert a pupil, challenging her to sense Peridea the way he does and see the universe in a new light. It harkens back to how Yoda challenged Luke to disregard what he thought he knew about life and embrace what the Force made possible.

1 “One parting lesson, Shin. Impatience for victory will guarantee defeat.” Ahsoka season 1, episode 7 “Part Seven: Dreams and Madness”

Baylan’s final line to Shin was his most fitting, foreshadowing the flaw that would lead to her defeat. They may be the villains, but Baylan and Shin had a wonderful master and apprentice relationship in Ahsoka, so it was strangely heartwarming to see him let her go to pursue her own goals. From beginning to end, Baylan Skoll’s dialogue and Ray Stevenson’s delivery were top-notch, making him the best Star Wars character in Ahsoka. Ahsoka season 1 is available to stream on Disney+
