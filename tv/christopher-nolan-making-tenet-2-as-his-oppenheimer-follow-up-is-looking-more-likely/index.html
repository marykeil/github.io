Christopher Nolan Making Tenet 2 As His Oppenheimer Follow-Up Is Looking More Likely

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The chances of Christopher Nolan making “Tenet 2” have grown recently due to the re-release of the original film. Warner Bros.’ attempt to mend its relationship with Nolan is a positive sign for the potential sequel. “Tenet 2” has the opportunity to surpass the original’s success, especially with a post-COVID release and a potentially star-studded cast.

Literature Review

Tenet 2 has yet to be officially announced, but recent developments make it being Christopher Nolan’s Oppenheimer follow-up look more likely.

The chances of Christopher Nolan making Tenet 2 after Oppenheimer continue to grow thanks to recent developments with the original movie. Nolan’s ambitious sci-fi movie Tenet debuted in 2020 to a mixed reception, as the director fully embraced his obsession with time with a bold, original story. Starring John David Washington and Robert Pattinson, Tenet was thought to be a movie that could launch an original franchise for Nolan if everything went well. However, the odds of it happening seemingly continued to decrease over the years before fortunes started to potentially turn.

The speculation that Tenet 2 could be Christopher Nolan’s next movie after Oppenheimer has largely been unfounded to this point. There has been no evidence that a Tenet sequel is in development, with Nolan and Warner Bros.’s fractured relationship viewed as a problem that would prevent the franchise from returning. Oppenheimer’s box office success and Oscars favor opened the door for Nolan to take his career in any number of directions from here. While his immediate plans are still unknown, the path for Tenet 2 is starting to clear up at the perfect time for Nolan.

              Will There Be A Tenet 2? What Could It Be About?   Could Tenet buck the trend and lead to Christopher Nolan making a mind-bending, time-distorting sci-fi sequel with the original cast?

Tenet’s Theatrical Re-Release Is A Great Sign For Tenet 2’s Chances

The chances of Tenet 2 have taken a step forward with news that Warner Bros. will re-release Tenet in theaters. The studio confirmed reports that Nolan’s sci-fi movie will return to theaters on a limited basis over three years after its original debut. Tenet’s re-release will only last for one week and is envisioned as a way to help promote Dune: Part Two, another Warner Bros. production, by being shown in 70mm IMAX. This comes after Tenet had an initial box office gross of $365 million worldwide, which was both impressive during COVID-19 and disappointing with its $200 million budget.

Even without the debate about its box office, Tenet’s original theatrical run was mired in controversy. The film was delayed repeatedly during the pandemic due to Christopher Nolan’s instance that it received an exclusive theatrical release. He wanted Tenet to be part of Hollywood’s efforts to bring people back to theaters and ensure cinemas did not shut down permanently. While Tenet avoided a same-day streaming release, the movie was still released in theaters when COVID-19 was a significant danger, leading to more controversies for WB and Nolan about the safety of audiences, and brought complains about the sound mixing.

With Tenet now returning to theaters, there is a chance that more interest in Nolan’s movie will come. The majority of audiences who go to theaters to watch Tenet could be doing so with a repeat viewing, but there are also sure to be others who will finally experience the movie for the first time. Any increase in popularity that the film receives would be a point in Christopher Nolan’s favor when it came to making Tenet 2 a reality. This is especially true as audiences experience (or re-experience) Tenet’s ending and how it sets up a sequel.

Warner Bros. Fixing Its Relationship With Christopher Nolan Was Needed For Tenet 2

The other key to Tenet’s re-release in theaters is that it shows a way that Warner Bros. is attempting to fix its relationship with Christopher Nolan. The studio and director had a long-term partnership dating back to Insomnia in 2002, which resulted in most of Christopher Nolan’s movies coming from WB. However, the way that Tenet was handled led to Nolan growing unhappy with Warner Bros. and ultimately deciding to leave. This is how he wound up making Oppenheimer for Universal, leaving Warner Bros. on the outside of his potential first Best Picture-winning feature.

Christopher Nolan was reportedly also upset that Warner Bros. released Barbie on the same day as Oppenheimer

Warner Bros. and Christopher Nolan’s disagreements meant that Tenet 2 would never happen, as the studio controls the rights to the franchise through its involvement in the first movie. It appears as though all parties involved are attempting to move past whatever happened before and reforge a great bond. This is surely part of the reason why WB wants to re-release Tenet in theaters; it will make Nolan happy. This gives the director a lot of power when it comes to discussions about him possibly making another movie at Warner Bros., whether that would be Tenet 2 or something else.

Christopher Nolan Has Already Teased A Return To Blockbusters After Oppenheimer

One of the biggest questions surrounding the fate of Tenet 2 is what direction Christopher Nolan wants to take his career after Oppenheimer. The movie’s 13 Oscar nominations position it as a frontrunner to win multiple categories at the 2024 Oscars, so he will be left to decide if he wants to pursue more Academy Awards or return to making action/sci-fi blockbusters. The director has refrained from confirming what is next after Oppenheimer, but he did tease (via Yahoo) that whatever it is will be “not as bleak.” That’s a good sign for Nolan’s next movie being a blockbuster.

Oppenheimer is undoubtedly the darkest movie that Nolan has made to date, so his desire to move on from this subject matter and go back to something more in his wheelhouse is promising for Tenet 2. The original 2020 action/sci-fi movie is the ultimate Christopher Nolan movie, for better or worse. Now that he is likely to have the pressure off of winning a Best Director or Best Picture Oscar after Oppenheimer, he should feel the freedom to go back to blockbusters. That could mean returning to the world of Tenet and creating his own original franchise with Tenet 2.

Why Tenet 2 Still Needs To Happen 4 Years Later

Tenet might be one of Christopher Nolan’s most divisive movies to date, but that should not immediately rule out making a sequel. After spending so much time with the Dark Knight trilogy and telling standalone sci-fi stories with Inception and Interstellar, Tenet was the first movie to combine Nolan’s sci-fi love with franchise experience. It is a completely unique blockbuster with action sequences and a type of non-linear storyline that no one else is attempting, much less at this scale. It would be incredibly exciting to see what Nolan would do to take Tenet 2 to new heights.

There is also a strong chance that Tenet 2 would be a much more successful film than its predecessor. Not releasing in the middle of a pandemic would already be a huge benefit for the sequel, as the post-COVID box office has shown there are opportunities for massive performances. Oppenheimer was a shining example of this and the perfect reminder of just how much of a draw Christopher Nolan’s name is for audiences. Add in what would likely be an even more star-studded cast, and Tenet 2 would have the chance to live up to the expectations Tenet originally had.

Of course, the question is whether Christopher Nolan wants to make Tenet 2. The director has never fully commented on the possibility of making another installment. However, star John David Washington has expressed his eagerness to return as The Protagonist (via Esquire). It would not come as a surprise if Nolan has ideas about where the story could go in a potential sequel. Now that Tenet is going back to theaters and his relationship with Warner Bros. is on the mend, perhaps he will begin to consider this possibility a bit more and make it a reality at last.
