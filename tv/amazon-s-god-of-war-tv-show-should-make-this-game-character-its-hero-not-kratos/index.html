Amazon s God Of War TV Show Should Make This Game Character Its Hero (Not Kratos)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The God of War TV show has the option to focus on Kratos’ son, Atreus, as the main protagonist, offering a unique perspective and adding depth to the story. While Atreus could be the main character, it would be important to still include Kratos in a prominent role, but not overshadow Atreus’ journey. Aligning the TV show with the game canon would allow for a dynamic storytelling device, where events in the show and games can reference and impact each other, creating an exciting and rare approach to video game adaptations.

Literature Review

Kratos has led every God of War game in the series so far, but Amazon’s TV adaptation would benefit by anointing a different protagonist.

Warning: This article contains information on the story of the God of War game series, which could spoil the TV adaptation.The main character of Amazon’s upcoming God of War TV show may seem obvious, but there’s actually an alternative to Kratos who could add more to the story. Kratos has led every installment of the God of War video game series to date. As one of the most recognizable modern video game characters, the adaptation will also likely follow the exploits of the titular god. However, the TV show could set itself apart from other game adaptations by replacing Kratos with a different main protagonist.

There is currently very little information on Amazon’s live-action God of War TV show, so it’s unclear how much the story will diverge from the source material. What’s clear is that the adaption will have no shortage of games to delve into when looking for compelling pieces of lore on which to base its narrative. With the game franchise dating back to 2005, every God of War game adds to the various eras in Kratos’ timeline. However, the potential key to making a truly unique show could lie in the more recent additions to the property.

Atreus Would Be A Great Main Hero For Amazon’s God Of War TV Show Kratos could still appear in the God of War TV show as a side character

Atreus, who was first introduced in 2018’s God of War soft reboot game, has a very interesting lineage.

Rather than retelling certain portions of Kratos’ story from the games, Amazon’s TV show has the option of focusing instead on Kratos’ son. Atreus, who was first introduced in 2018’s God of War soft reboot game, has a very interesting lineage. Not only is he part god due to being Kratos’ offspring, but Atreus is also part giant. His mother, Faye, is only shown in flashback sequences but has a backstory that rivals that of Kratos.

Faye is portrayed by Deborah Ann Woll in God of War Ragnarök , who is perhaps best known for her role as Karen Page in Daredevil .

Atreus’ presence so far in the games has been filled with interesting pieces of lore regarding his abilities and strong links to Norse mythology. His temperament is different from his father’s, with the latter having a history of turning to violence to solve his problems. Atreus is portrayed as a much more sensitive soul who only engages in combat when necessary. However, he has also been shown to be a capable warrior when required. As a much younger character than his father, he has much more capacity for growth and would therefore make a more layered TV protagonist than Kratos.

Although focusing on Atreus could be a fascinating approach for the God of War TV show, it would be foolish to omit Kratos entirely. As such, Kratos should still definitely have a prominent role in the show but be kept at a distance so as not to overshadow Atreus. While this feels like a difficult balance to find, the show’s writers can look to the games for how to achieve this.

The God Of War TV Show Could Follow Atreus While The Games Stay With Kratos The God of War franchise could split its story between two mediums

While Kratos struggles with the emotion of setting his young son free, he acquiesces to Atreus’ desire to forge his own destiny.

The ending of 2022’s God of War Ragnarök game provides the perfect opportunity for Atreus to take over as the story’s main character. Ragnarök ends with Atreus parting ways with Kratos. Atreus leaves his father on good terms, stating he is leaving Kratos to go on a mission to save the Giants. While Kratos struggles with the emotion of setting his young son free, he acquiesces to Atreus’ desire to forge his own destiny. Amazon’s God of War TV should pick up where the game ends and follow Atreus on said quest.

The process of making the more recent God of War games involves the facial and motion capture of real actors. As a result, the characters’ physical appearances are generally very similar to their flesh-and-blood counterparts. This would make the casting of Atreus for the God of War show a simple affair, as Sunny Suljic could reprise his role from the games on which the show is based. All the while, Kratos could remain the main character of the game series and continue his story via that medium.

Amazon’s God Of War Aligning With Game Canon Would Be A Dynamic Storytelling Device A TV show sharing the same canon as a video game isn’t a common occurrence

In order for the focus on Atreus to be especially effective, the show could easily align itself with the canon of the games. Due to the fact that Atreus is embarking on a new adventure at the end of Ragnarök, the show would only need to provide limited context regarding the events of the games. However, it would allow Kratos’ cameos in the show to directly reference what has happened or is currently happening in the original arm of the God of War franchise.

Similarly, Atreus’ story in the show would be easy to reference in the games if he were ever to return. In short, both father and son could follow their own respective callings while both the Amazon show and the game series remain part of the same continuity. TV is the future of video game adaptations, and maintaining the same canon between two mediums only adds to the exciting possibilities going forward. This is an incredibly rare approach to video game adaptions and so the originality would be both rewarding for existing fans and still accessible to God of War newcomers.

There is currently no release date for the God of War TV show.
