He-Man s Shocking New Power Change Sets Up A Whole New Future For Masters Of The Universe

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

He-Man’s new transformation in Masters of the Universe: Revolution shakes up the status quo and raises questions about the future of the characters, including Battle Cat. He-Man shares his power with Teela, granting her a unique new transformation and revolutionizing her role in the franchise. The possibility of He-Man sharing his power with multiple allies opens up new possibilities for power-ups and battles in the future of Masters of the Universe.

Literature Review

A massive change to how He-Man’s powers work in Masters of the Universe: Revolution seemingly changes the status quo for multiple characters.

Warning: Spoilers for Masters of the Universe: Revolution

Masters of the Universe: Revolution just gave He-Man a new transformation, one that threatens to shake the status quo for multiple protagonists in the classic cartoon franchise. Ever since the original Masters of the Universe animated series, He-Man has always been supported by a team of capable heroes, but one in particular has always stood out as his primary ally. Across every series in the Masters of the Universe franchise, He-Man has been one half of a powerful duo, with the other half being Battle Cat, the transformed version of He-Man’s cat, Cringer.

Kevin Smith’s Masters of the Universe threatened this status quo with He-Man’s controversial “death” in Masters of the Universe: Revelation part 1. Of course, He-Man’s return restored some semblance of normalcy, with He-Man and Battle-Cat fighting together again in the second half of the ten-episode series as well as in Masters of the Universe: Revolution. The Masters of the Universe: Revolution finale shook things up again, this time with a new transformation that was perceived as permanent for the hero. But how it works raises several questions about for the future of the characters, Battle Cat included.

He-Man’s New Transformation In Masters Of The Universe Explained

Over the course of the five-episode series, Masters of the Universe: Revolution rolled out two new designs for He-Man, the first being Battle Armor He-Man and the second making its debut in the finale. The new transformation, which appears to be original to the show, was born from Orko, Man-At-Arms, and Gwildor’s subplot in Masters of the Universe: Revolution. He-Man entrusted the sword to Orko and Man-At-Arms in the hopes of getting the Sword of Power dramatically enhanced so that he could use its power to cure all of Eternia of a techno-virus created by Motherboard.

Naturally, this would call for the sword to be able to share energy with Eternia’s entire population. Understanding that He-Man can only share the sword’s power with one entity (which is typically Cringer) made Gwildor’s “modifications” necessary. Thanks to the expertise of the live-action Masters of the Universe movie’s returning character, they succeeded in upgrading the sword so that when He-Man activated his sword again in episode 5, it gave him a whole new look. As Skeletor acknowledged in the episode, both He-Man’s new transformation and Skeletor’s new “Skeletek” form represent the “nexus between magic and technology.”

Masters Of The Universe Hints He-Man & Teela Have The Same Power Level Now

He-Man’s transformation scene confirms he can do exactly as he hoped, as he was shown sharing his power with Teela, granting her a unique new transformation of her own. This is a surprising development for a number of reasons, not the least of which being what it means for Teela. This move allowed He-Man and Teela to fight as equals, which is in stark contrast to her usual role in the cartoons. Traditionally, Teela is one of the heroes who provides backup to He-Man as he rides on Battle Cat against Skeletor’s forces. Courtesy of her form, the new series has revolutionized Teela’s place in the franchise.

How He-Man and Teela fought together in the Masters of the Universe: Revolution finale was a spectacular demonstration of both characters’ capabilities. Teela received one significant upgrade already when she became the replacement for Sorcereress, and now she’s arguably stronger than ever. Teela’s new strength, combined with her wings, makes her a force to be reckoned with, and based on how she seemed to keep up with He-Man almost effortlessly, there’s a sense that gap that existed between them is gone now. In addition to forming a romantic relationship for the first time, He-Man and Teela have formed what may be one of the franchise’s strongest partnerships yet.

What He-Man & Teela’s New Partnership Means For Battle Cat

What happens between He-Man and Teela raises concerns about where Cringer fits into the equation. However, it’s important to note that shortly after powering up Teela, He-Man shared his energy with Cringer off-screen and was seen riding Battle Cat later. This confirmed that He-Man’s sword can do exactly what he had hoped, which is to transfer energy to multiple targets. This goes to show that in spite of the focus on He-Man and Teela, the latter hasn’t necessarily replaced Battle Cat as He-Man’s go-to partner in battle.

That said, it does create the possibility of a shift in their dynamic going forward. If what happened with Teela is a sign of things to come rather than a one-off team-up, they may be powering up like this regularly after Revolution. If that happens, Masters of the Universe’s most iconic duo may suddenly transform into a trio. This would heighten Teela’s role, but could come at the cost of Battle Cat’s importance to He-Man’s story. In any case, though, it seems He-Man is far from done riding Battle Cat into battle, and may instead lean on both characters going forward in Masters of the Universe’s future after Revolution.

Can He-Man Share His Power With His Other Allies In Masters Of The Universe?

He-Man’s new ability to give more than one power the Power of Grayskull carries interesting implications for other characters. In Masters of the Universe: Revolution, there are no limits set on the Sword of Power’s new capabilities. Gwildor designed it that way for a singular purpose, which was fulfilled as soon as He-Man saved Eternia from Motherboard’s techno-virus. But the show doesn’t confirm if the sword still retains its power. He-Man and Teela remained in their new transformations, thus implying that the change to the sword is permanent.

If He-Man can technically share his power with multiple characters, it’s worth wondering why he would stop with Teela and Cringer when new threats emerge. It could be that the need arose, he could also power up the other warriors that fight at his side, including Andra, the original Mat-At-Arms, and what’s left of He-Man’s team of heroes. Theoretically, he could empower each character with powerful transformations, complete with new abilities and designs comparable to the changes made to Teela.

Using a technique of this magnitude would allow the next installment in Kevin Smith’s He-Man revival to deliver a battle of even greater proportions than Masters of the Universe: Revolution’s ending. Heading in this direction would grant the franchise an opportunity to pay off the changes to the landscape of Masters of the Universe’s world. It would also put more pressure on villains like Skeletor, who would need an even stronger army to stand a chance against multiple characters fueled by the Power of Grayskull.
