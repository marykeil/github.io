SUMMARY

Global cast enhances the authenticity of the docuseries, with talent from various backgrounds contributing to engrossing reenactments. Interviews with modern-day historians provide impactful information, but the cast adds to the engaging nature of the series. This docuseries combines historical facts with reenactments to chronicle the rise of Alexander the Great as one of history’s greatest leaders.

Literature Review

Alexander: The Making of a God is a recently premiered Netflix docuseries with a huge cast of unknown and known actors from all over the world.

Netflix docuseries Alexander: The Making of a God is enhanced by a cast that is pulled from all over the globe, adding to the historical documentary series’ authenticity and engrossing reenactments. From video game voiceover credits to starring in Game of Thrones, there is no shortage of talent in the cast of the series to complement the real experts who also provide commentary throughout the show. As a historical docuseries, facts are at the forefront alongside reenactments of events that occurred during Alexander’s lifetime.

As a docuseries, it follows the life and times of Alexander the Great as he rises to become one of the greatest leaders of all time. With plenty of battle action and characters to support Alexander in his quest to conquer countries, audiences also learn about the leader from various modern-day historians. Despite the most impactful information coming through these interviews, the cast of Alexander: The Making of a God helps make the informational aspect all the more engaging.

Buck Braithwaite as Alexander the Great

Actor: Alexander: The Making of a God is Buck Braithwaite’s first real starring role. Other than having over 3,300 followers on Instagram, little is known about Braithwaite’s personal life, except that he likes to travel.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Masters of the Air (2024), Nefarious (2019), Fair Play (2023).

Character: As the title character, Braithwaite plays the famous leader and king as audiences watch him grow into his leadership abilities to become the mythical great commander.

Mido Hamada as King Darius

Actor: Having been raised in Germany and born in Egypt, Hamada is best known for his television roles and as a video game voiceover actor, landing his first big break in The Path to 9/11 in 2006. Fluent in German, English, and Arabic, he studied acting at the Oxford School of Drama and currently lives in London.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: American Sniper (2014), Emerald City (2017), Unknown (2011).

Character: King Darius is one of Alexander’s enemies and the leader of the Persian army who loses control of Persia after being defeated by Alexander.

Dino Kelly as Ptolemy

Actor: Born in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Kelly grew up in Norway and has starred in many television roles and video games during his life, including Peaky Blinders and The Good Liar. Fluent in both Norwegian and Bosnian, he attended the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art and currently has over 2,546 followers on Instagram.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Peaky Blinders (2017), The Good Liar (2019), Invasion (2021).

Character: As Alexander’s right-hand man and one of the most trusted generals, Ptolemy took over for Alexander after the leader’s death. According to the series, he may have been in love with Alexander.

Nada El Belkasmi as Barsine

Actor: A mechanical engineer as well as an actor, Nada El Belkasmi is currently based in Paris. She is no stranger to portraying historical figures, having acted in Ancient Empires as Roxana and in Queen Cleopatra in an uncredited role as Octavia. She also has over 319,000 followers on Instagram.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Queen Cleopatra (2023), Ransomed (2023), and Ancient Empires (2023).

Character: Barsine was rumored to be one of Alexander’s many mistresses during his lifetime and the mother of Heracles.

Alain Ali Washnevsky as Mazaeus

Actor: An Iranian-Austrian actor, Washnevksy has an extensive history in American television, having had an extensive plethora of roles from Tehran as Larijani to in the comedy series The Conners as Samir. He was originally a real estate agent and is fluent in German, English, Farsi, and French.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Lucifer (2019), The Conners (2018), Tehran (2023).

Character: In Alexander: The Making of a God and in reality, Mazaeus was one of the few Persian nobles who supports Alexander’s rise to the throne and a Hellenistic satrap.

Will Stevens as Hephaestion

Actor: Stevens is mostly famous for his roles onstage, having starred in many Shakespeare classics like King Lear to even directing a short film called 12 Parts. He studied psychology and philosophy at Oxford in 2017 and studied drama at the Central School of Speech and Drama in London.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Silent Witness (2020)

Character: Hephaestion is one of Alexander the Great’s rumored romances and is a close friend of the leader while he rules as king.

Agni Scott as Stateira

Actor: Agni Scott is most famous for her roles in 2016’s Bridget Jones’s Baby and 2022’s Persuasion, having starred alongside both Renée Zellweger and Dakota Johnson. She has an extensive television career and has also done multiple voiceovers for video games, most notably Assassin’s Creed.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Bridget Jones’s Baby (2016), MI-5 (2007), Persuasion (2022).

Character: As the daughter of Darius III, Scott’s character, Stateira, eventually becomes the wife of Alexander the Great after he takes over Persia.

Jadran Malkovich as General Parmenion

Actor: Jadran Malkovich, 47, has an extensive acting and directing career, with credits including the role of Disco in Ted Lasso (2023) and Trotsky in the video game Assassin’s Creed Chronicles. Malkovich is fluent in several languages including German, and has a massive following on Instagram, with over 24,000 followers.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Ted Lasso (2023), Alex Rider (2020), Assassin’s Creed Chronicles (2015).

Character: The character General Parmenion serves as a high-ranking general under both Alexander and Alexander’s father, King Philip.

James Oliver Wheatley as Attalus

Actor: British actor James Oliver Wheatley is known for appearing as Blackbeard in The Lost Pirate Kingdom and the role of Sicky in 5lbs of Pressure. He is no stranger to period dramas and his social media posts range from headshots to inspirational quotes.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Burn Country (2016), 5lbs of Pressure (2024, The Lost Pirate Kingdom (2021).

​​​​​​​Character: Serving under Alexander’s father, King Philip II, Wheatley plays military commander Attalus, the uncle of Philips’ wife Eurydice.

Waleed Elgadi as Bessus

Actor: Waleed Elgadi, 45, is known for his television and video game voiceover roles. He is best known for his role as Sayed in 2016’s A Hologram for the King as well as his role as Khalid in 2010’s Four Lions. He is originally from Sudan and raises awareness about the genocide in Darfur on his social media platforms including Instagram, X (formerly Twitter), and Facebook.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: The 355, Four Lions, A Hologram for the King.

​​​​​​​Character: Elgadi plays the Macedonian king who kills Darius III and eventually has his short reign ended by Alexander, who has Darius III killed after he conquers Macedonia.

Soad Faress as Oracle

Actor: Originally from Ghana, Faress has appeared in numerous television series ranging from Dosh khaleen High Priestess in Game of Thrones to several recurring roles in ITV’s Casualty as well as her portrayal of Usha Gupta in The Archers. Born to Irish and Syrian parents, Faress studied drama at the Guildhall School and currently lives in London.

Notable Movies & TV Shows: Casualty (2003-2023), Game of Thrones (2012), The Sandman (2022), The Spy (2019), Christopher Robin (2018).

​​​​​​​Character: As the Oracle, Faress plays an Egyptian seer that Alexander meets during his conquering of Eygpt.

Alexander: The Making of a God Supporting Cast & Characters

Mansour Badri as Satrap Mazaces - Known for his TV appearances in a variety of Moroccan TV dramas, movies, and short films, including (2019), The Physician (2013), and War Dogs (2016).

Kosha Engler as Olympias - Kosha Engler is an American-British actress who made her debut in HBO’s The Wire in 2002 as well as Disney’s Tuck Everlasting. She is also known for her voice acting roles in several video games, including LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga (2022) and Terminator: Resistance (2019).

Steven Hartley as General Memnon: Steven Hartley, 63, is an English actor who has starred in many theatre and television roles, including All Creatures Great and Small and Call the Midwife as well as a an extensive past in video game voiceovers.

Marie-Batoul Prenant as Euridyce: A French-Moroccan actress, Prenant has not had an extensive acting career but is well known for her role as Sarah in The Moderator (2022) and her roles in two short films, The Color of Mandarines (2022) and Chrysalis (2020).

Christopher Sciueref as King Philip: Originally from London, Sciueref is primarily a TV and video game actor, having starred in 2012’s Skyfall and in the English version of Final Fantasy XVI prior to being in Alexander: The Making of a God.
