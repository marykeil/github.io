Invincible Confirms Omni-Man s True Replacement Before Season 2, Part 2

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Omni-Man’s capture leaves a void in Thraxa, requiring someone to step up and replace him as their defender. Mark has taken on the role of protecting Thraxa, embracing his father’s position. The final battle of Season 2 will test Mark’s similarities to Omni-Man and determine whether he is truly his father’s replacement.

Literature Review

Omni-Man has been one of the most significant characters throughout Invincible, but his true replacement has been revealed ahead of season 2, part 2.

Invincible looks to have confirmed Omni-Man’s true replacement ahead of season 2’s return. Omni-Man is one of the most important characters in the entire franchise, acting as season 1’s biggest antagonist before showing signs of change in season 2’s first part. While there is a chance Omni-Man is still a villain in Invincible’s TV show, his role in the opening half of season 2 as well as his comic book story would suggest the Viltrumite has reformed. However, part 1 of Invincible season 2 ended with Omni-Man being captured, suggesting someone has to step up and replace him.

Omni-Man being captured by the Viltrumites leaves a lot of responsibilities to be filled. Not only has his role as Earth’s greatest protector not truly been filled yet, but Thraxa is also without someone to lead and defend it. In season 2, part 1, Omni-Man found himself without purpose after failing to conquer Earth. As he aimlessly flew across space, Omni-Man saved some Thraxans from certain death, eventually settling on Thraxa and having a child. Thraxa’s vulnerability leaves the planet in need of a defender after Omni-Man’s capture, and Invincible season 2, part 2’s trailer seems to confirm his replacement.

Invincible season 2, part 2 is scheduled to return to Amazon Prime Video on March 14, 2024.

Mark Has Taken Omni-Man’s Place On Thraxa In Invincible Season 2 Mark Is Filling His Father’s Role On Planet Thraxa

Season 2, part 2’s trailer reveals that Mark has taken Omni-Man’s place on Thraxa after the fight that ensued on the planet. Although he was reluctant to stay and help when he first arrived, Mark eventually joined his father in fighting off the Viltrumites. While they ultimately lost the fight, they did prevent Thraxa from being completely destroyed, despite the high number of casualties. With Invincible season 2 introducing Omni-Man’s other son, it provided Mark with an extra reason to protect the planet, and his involvement in defending it seems to be continuing in part 2.

It may seem like defending the planet is just part of Mark’s good nature, but he doesn’t seem in a hurry to leave. In the trailer, Mark claims “I’m sure they’ve got it covered” in response to a Thraxan claiming Earth will need Mark’s strength. He is also wearing the same clothing that Omni-Man wore on the planet, suggesting that he has embraced the role. Mark may not want to be the king of Thraxa, but he does seem to be pivotal in rebuilding the planet in season 2, part 2, indicating he has followed in his father’s footsteps.

Mark Becoming Like Omni-Man Was A Major Theme In Invincible Season 2, Part 1 Season 2, Part 1 Heavily Focused On Mark’s Fear Of Becoming Like His Dad

The first part of Invincible’s second season explored the theme of Mark becoming like Omni-Man. After the events of season 1, Mark understandably starts resenting his dad, hating him for the pain and destruction he caused on Earth. However, with Mark having Viltrumite blood and the same powers as his father, he had concerns about ending up just like Omni-Man. This resulted in Mark desperately trying to prove to everyone that he was different and was a true hero. Other characters also compare the two or try to carefully avoid mentioning Omni-Man to Mark, knowing how sensitive the subject is.

Although Omni-Man may be on a path toward redemption, killing the Guardians of the Globe and countless civilians means Earth still hates him. As a result, some people on Earth are worried Mark may become just like Omni-Man, which has weighed on Mark’s mind. Season 1’s ending showed how different they are, with Omni-Man seeing humans as inferior, while Mark would jump at the chance to save just a single person. Despite this, Invincible season 2 proved an Evil Mark Grayson would be nearly unstoppable and that if Mark were to embrace Omni-man’s original mission, they would be incredibly similar.

Invincible Season 2’s Final Battle Will Prove Whether Mark Is Omni-Man’s Replacement Season 2’s Major Villain Will Test Mark And Omni-Man’s Similarities

Invincible’s season 2’s final battle will be the perfect chance to prove whether Mark is Omni-man’s true replacement. Season 2 seems destined to end with Mark fighting Angstrom Levy, with part 2’s trailer pointing heavily towards this. Levy hates Mark for the very fact he is like his father in so many universes. With Omni-Man and Mark conquering several versions of Earth, Levy wants to kill every version of the protagonist. This battle will be the ultimate test for Mark, as fighting someone so powerful with a personal vendetta will help determine what kind of hero he truly is.

Considering Mark is still growing as a hero, there is the chance he fails to hold back, permanently injuring Levy or even killing him. This would make him just like his father, proving Levy right about Mark being another Omni-Man. However, showing restraint and defeating Levy without resorting to extremes would emphasize that Mark is his own hero and that he possesses some of his dad’s positive traits. Omni-Man and Mark have a complex relationship, especially given Omni-Man’s current redemption arc, but Invincible season 2 will prove whether Mark is his father’s replacement or if he’ll forge his own path.
