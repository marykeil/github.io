10 TV Characters It s Basically Impossible To Hate

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Beloved TV characters are often those who prioritize others, making them relatable and endearing. Moral fortitude and bravery can earn a character respect and adoration from the entire fan base. Characters who combine likability with intriguing traits can captivate audiences and enhance the drama of a show.

Literature Review

Although most TV characters divide opinion, some courageous and kindhearted souls are almost universally beloved within their show’s fan bases.

Most TV characters have their fans and detractors, but there are some good-natured souls who are universally beloved. Since TV shows can be on for many years, most characters need to have some kind of flaw which keeps them interesting to viewers and makes them a constant source of conflict. This usually stops them from being loved by everyone. Even if most fans like a character, there tend to be just as many who point out their shortcomings, especially after watching them for so many episodes across so many years. Compelling characters and lovable characters rarely overlap, but there are a few exceptions where a fan-favorite is both a good person and a fascinating character. The most universally beloved characters are often those who put other people’s needs first, whether they’re a loyal friend or a loving family member. People can grow attached to characters they watch very often, so it can be a huge bonus when a show has a character who is easy to identify with. Moral fortitude and bravery are also admirable traits, and characters with both will usually be well-respected at the least, and possibly even beloved by a show’s entire fan base. Beloved TV show characters can make fans much more invested in the drama of a show.

10 Movie Characters It’s Basically Impossible To Hate People have their own favorite movie characters, but some characters are universally loved because of their moral courage and good humor.

10 Uncle Iroh Played by Mako and Greg Baldwin in Avatar: The Last Airbender (2005-2008)

Uncle Iroh is a spiritual and wise mentor to his nephew Zuko and Aang. When backed into a fight, his firebending skills make him a powerful adversary, but his philosophy preaches peace and harmony above all else. Iroh is the most quotable character in Avatar: The Last Airbender, since he has the intelligence to take a step back and see things from an improved perspective, even if his respect of the other elements makes some firebenders brand him as a traitor. Iroh will be played by Paul Sun-Hyung Lee in Netflix’s upcoming live-action version of ATLA.

9 Grogu The Mandalorian (2019-)

Known initially as “The Child”, or “Baby Yoda” to some fans, Grogu is the lovable companion to Din Djarin in The Mandalorian. Din Djarin was initially hired to kidnap Grogu and deliver him to Gideon. He’s told that the target is 50 years old, so he doesn’t expect an adorable child. Din Djarin’s relationship with Grogu changes him and gives him a deep emotional connection worth fighting for. As a bounty hunter, it helps to have as few attachments as possible, but Grogu is special. The Mandalorian reveals part of Grogu’s tragic backstory, making him even more sympathetic. Grogu also appeared in The Book of Boba Fett between seasons 2 and 3 of The Mandalorian.

8 Richard Splett Played by Sam Richardson in Veep (2012-2019)

In the toxic political world of Veep, Richard Splett is the sole beacon of hope. He isn’t as manipulative, ruthless, or cruel as any of his colleagues. This is partly because he simply doesn’t have the aptitude for any of that, but it’s also because he’s a kind soul who likes to believe in the good in everyone. Veep’s best seasons all come after Richard is introduced as an assistant at Selina Meyer’s book signing. He improves the show by acting as a moral counterpoint to the corrupt and underhanded environment of Washington D.C.

7 Arya Stark Played by Maisie Williams in Game of Thrones (2011-2019)

Arya Stark is one of a handful of characters who survive Game of Thrones from beginning to end, and she remained popular throughout the entirety of the show’s run. Arya is guided by her own sense of what is right and wrong, refusing to bow down to anyone who she doesn’t believe deserves it, regardless of their status. Despite her own high-born pedigree, she strives to treat others equally, and she never hesitates to back up her ideals with action if needed. She’s a capable fighter and a loyal ally to have by one’s side.

6 Jason Mendoza Played by Manny Jacinto in The Good Place (2016-2020)

Jason is a reckless dolt who is assigned to The Good Place’s equivalent of hell, but he’s the most inoffensive and lovable character on the show. He’s a simpleton, emphasized by the bizarre idiocy of his death, but he is also a source of love and positivity, which is a perfect antidote to Tahani’s ego and Chidi’s indecision. Most of Jason’s contributions to the team are morale-based, rather than useful on a practical level. He’s the ineffectual member of a group project who still holds everyone together with his uplifting attitude.

5 Sam Obisanya Played by Toheeb Jimoh in Ted Lasso (2020-2023)

Sam doesn’t share Ted Lasso’s love of cheesy slogans and quirky southern aphorisms, but he buys into his message of positivity and togetherness all the same. Everything Sam does is guided by his loyalty to his friends and his strong moral compass. He turns down a big-money move from Edwin Akufo to stay with his team when they need him, and he risks his job by publicly denouncing AFC Richmond’s sponsor when he discovers their legacy of pollution in his home country, Nigeria. Naturally, Sam receives the full support of the team, showing how much they care about him.

4 Rose Nylund Played by Betty White in The Golden Girls (1985-1992)

Betty White’s charming portrayal of Rose on The Golden Girls made her one of the most beloved TV actors of her generation. Rose’s naivety often sets up some zingers from her roommates, but she always responds with good humor. Rose is probably the most loving character on the show. She is always trying to help her friends, and she works as a grief counselor and a volunteer at various organizations in her spare time. Despite a relatively sheltered existence, Rose approaches the new chapter of her life after her husband’s death with open arms.

3 Captain Raymond Holt Played by Andre Braugher in Brooklyn Nine-Nine (2013-2021)

When Captain Holt first enters the precinct, he seems like a strict authoritarian who is going to put an end to Jake’s childish games. However, he soon discovers a more empathetic and nuanced approach to leadership, and the squad support him because they love and trust him. Captain Holt’s no-nonsense persona puts him at the center of many of Brooklyn Nine-Nine’s best episodes, like when he tries to act heterosexual in witness protection or when he takes the Halloween heist to Machiavellian extremes. He’s a great boss, but he’s also a father figure to Jake, Amy, and the others.

2 Phil Dunphy Played by Ty Burrell in Modern Family (2009-2020)

In Modern Family’s dysfunctional Dunphy-Tucker-Pritchett-Delgado family, Phil is the overenthusiastic puppy-dog whose optimism can sometimes rub people the wrong way. He loves his wife and children even when they are at their lowest, and this is probably because he is so in touch with his inner child. Phil has maintained his childlike enthusiasm and curiosity, but he has still matured where it matters, understanding his responsibilities as a father and a husband. It helps that Ty Burrell is capable of selling the show’s goofiest physical comedy and funniest phrases with ease.

1 Miranda Bailey Played by Chandra Wilson in Grey’s Anatomy (2005-)

At the beginning of Grey’s Anatomy, the interns hate working under Miranda Bailey, but it’s her tough love that helps shape them into the doctors they need to be. It’s true that she has a lot of attitude, but it’s an admirable quality to be able to speak one’s mind so freely. Bailey has developed a lot over the course of the show, and Chandra Wilson has confirmed that she is returning for season 20 of Grey’s Anatomy. She’s one of very few characters who have been there since the beginning, so fans have seen her endure a lot.
