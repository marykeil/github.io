Dixon & Reacher Have Already Outdone S1 s Roscoe Romance For A Surprisingly Spicy Reason

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Reacher’s relationships with Karla Dixon and Roscoe Conklin differ in their approach to love. Reacher and Roscoe’s physical relationship in season 1 is hesitant and professional, while Reacher and Dixon get physical immediately in season 2. The romance between Reacher and Dixon is unlikely to last beyond season 2 due to the format of the source material and the limited appearances of Dixon’s character.

Literature Review

Reacher has had two love interests in as many seasons, but his relationship with Dixon is very different when compared with his Roscoe romance.

Warning: This article contains spoilers for Reacher season 2.Reacher has shown its title character in a romantic entanglement with a different woman in each of its first two seasons, but there’s a difference between his relationships with Karla Dixon and Roscoe Conklin. Although Reacher has had genuine feelings for both women, there’s one factor that separates the dynamics that arose in each scenario. Dixon and Roscoe have their similarities, but their approaches to love diverge from one another.

Reacher season 1 shows Reacher assisting Roscoe in Margrave as the pair try to solve his brother’s murder. Their run-in is purely coincidental, and their relationship starts from scratch. Season 2 has meant a reunion between Reacher and the rest of his old unit, the 110th Special Investigations Unit. Dixon is a key member of the 110th and the Reacher season 2 cast, making her someone with whom Reacher has a history - one that they’re exploring together.

Dixon & Reacher Are Sleeping Together Throughout Reacher Season 2 Reacher and Roscoe’s physical relationship was very stop-start

Season 1 shows the relationship between Reacher and Roscoe developing over time, and there’s sexual tension from day one. Although it’s obvious there’s an attraction between them, the pair keep one another at arm’s length for much of Reacher season 1 in the name of professionalism. The pull becomes too much in season 1, episode 4, “In a Tree,” when Roscoe joins Reacher in the shower. The next day, the two agree that their sexual encounter can’t change anything, or the investigation will become compromised.

Reacher and Dixon get physical almost immediately in Reacher season 2, first sleeping together as early as the second episode. When it comes to light that Reacher returned her implied feelings even while serving together, they make up for lost time and have sex more than once alongside their ongoing murder investigation. As a result, Reacher and Dixon’s romantic dynamic feels much more like a traditional relationship than Reacher and Roscoe’s. However, part of this may come from Dixon having a shared history with Reacher, whereas Roscoe did not.

Dixon & Reacher’s Relationship Is Still Unlikely To Last Beyond Season 2 The format of Reacher’s source material makes it difficult

Reacher is based on a series of books by Lee Child. The books, despite having a generally linear continuity, are designed to be read in any order. This is, in part, due to Reacher’s preference to always keep moving. Therefore, all the stories remain isolated as he drifts around the country. Amazon’s Reacher TV show also adheres to this basic framework, making each season a largely standalone adventure. This makes it difficult for characters to carry over from one season to the next, Karla Dixon included. This is also evidenced by Willa Fitzgerald’s Roscoe Conklin remaining a member of the Reacher season 1 cast only.

Karla Dixon is also used incredibly sparingly in the books, meaning her character is likely to only appear in Reacher season 2. As a result, the romance between Reacher and Dixon will probably end the same way as it did between Reacher and Roscoe at the end of Reacher season 1. However, the show has already broken Child’s format a few times, the most prominent example being Oscar Finlay’s Reacher season 2 cameo.

“Bad Luck and Trouble” is the book used by Amazon to create Reacher season 2, a story in which Finlay does not originally appear. Therefore, it’s not outside the realm of possibility that Dixon could come back more often than she does in the books. If Serinda Swan’s Karla Dixon were to make an additional appearance in the future, it may just be a brief guest spot like Malcolm Goodwin’s Oscar Finlay. This would make it tricky for Reacher and Dixon’s romance to continue beyond Reacher season 2.

              Reacher Finally Acknowledges Roscoe In Season 2 (But You Probably Missed It)   Although Reacher season 2 avoids making direct references to Roscoe, it features a subtle nod to her character that may be easily missed by viewers.

A Reacher Season 1 Line Hints Roscoe Might Still Be Reacher’s True Love Reacher seemed reluctant to leave Roscoe behind

Even if Reacher and Dixon were to remain together, it may not be the right pairing. The ending of Reacher season 1 features a sincere heart-to-heart between Reacher and Roscoe, where the pair share their true feelings for one another. With his signature sincerity, Reacher states his regret at not being able to stay in Margrave but that Roscoe would be the only person capable of making him do so, revealing, “If there was anyone who could make me stay close to the flame, it would be you.” Seeing as Reacher knew Dixon already at this point in the show’s timeline, the confession implies his feelings for Roscoe surpass those for Dixon.

Like many of Lee Child’s characters, Roscoe never returns in the Reacher books, so her relationship with the show’s title character may be limited to season 1. Despite this, Amazon’s decision to bring back Finlay for season 2 shows that anything is possible in the on-screen adaptations of Child’s work. Given the nomadic nature of Alan Ritchson’s character, it’s also possible that there’ll be even more Reacher romances before a comeback is considered for either Roscoe or Dixon.
