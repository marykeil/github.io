Giancarlo Esposito Now Has Two Upcoming TV Roles That Could Become His Gus Fring Replacement

SUMMARY

Giancarlo Esposito has two upcoming TV roles that could rival his iconic character in Breaking Bad . One of his upcoming roles is in the AMC series Parish , where he plays the lead character thrown into the criminal world. Esposito will also star in The Residence , a murder-mystery series set at the White House, where he plays one of the leads.

Literature Review

Giancarlo Esposito is done with the Breaking Bad universe now that Better Call Saul has ended, but he has two exciting replacement TV shows coming up.

Giancarlo Esposito is best known for playing the intimidating Gus Fring in Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul, but the actor has two upcoming TV roles that could become his replacement for the AMC franchise. Esposito has appeared in a number of projects since his departure from Breaking Bad, and his schedule has likely been freed up even further by Better Call Saul ending. While he’s appeared in some major TV shows, including The Boys and The Mandalorian, he hasn’t done anything comparable to Breaking Bad.

Netflix’s Kaleidoscope is perhaps the closest Esposito has come to playing another criminal mastermind, and the series and role never quite reached the acclaim of Breaking Bad. Still, Esposito gets to flex his acting talent and play a character as complex as Gus Fring — though one that’s far more sympathetic. The true villains Esposito has played since Gus have either been in starkly different types of series or just less memorable than AMC’s drug lord. Fortunately, Esposito has two exciting TV roles coming up, and they could become his “new Breaking Bad.”

Giancarlo Esposito Has 2 Exciting TV Roles Now That His Time In The Breaking Bad Universe Has Ended

Esposito has two exciting TV roles coming up now that his time in the Breaking Bad universe has ended, and both dramas will give him the opportunity to step into characters with as much depth as Gus Fring. The actor’s upcoming AMC series, Parish, will see him playing the lead role. Esposito’s title character will find himself thrown into the criminal world after picking up a gangster in his taxi. In a sense, the series puts the Breaking Bad star in a role more similar to Walter White than Gus. Still, he’ll need to navigate the criminal underworld and do the unthinkable, making this a compelling follow-up.

Parish premieres on AMC on March 31, 2024.

Deadline also announced that Esposito will be taking on Andre Braugher’s role in the show The Residence following the Brooklyn Nine-Nine star’s tragic death in December 2023. The Residence is a murder-mystery series that’s set at the White House, and it’s produced by Shonda Rhimes. The Breaking Bad alum will play one of the series’ leads: White House Chief Usher A.B. Wynter. This character is a far cry from Esposito’s usual roles, but his prominence will give Esposito a chance to dominate the screen and leave a memorable impression.

Giancarlo Esposito Will Star In Another Upcoming Show (Aside From Parish & The Residence)

Parish and The Residence are far from the only projects Esposito has lined up in the near future. In addition to starring in the upcoming horror movies MaXXXine and Abigail, Esposito will take on another TV show in 2024: Guy Ritchie’s The Gentleman. It’s unclear how large the actor’s part in the upcoming series is, but it’s another exciting project for him on the heels of Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul. Plus, it’s a gangster show based on Ritchie’s 2019 movie — meaning it’s in the crime genre, where Esposito often shines.

Guy Ritchie’s The Gentlemen TV show is set to premiere in March 2024.
