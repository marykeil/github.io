Wheel Of Time s Massive Book Changes Will Be Nothing Compared To Season 3

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Season 3 of The Wheel of Time will make even bigger changes to the source material than the previous seasons, which have already faced criticism for alterations. The decision to skip “The Dragon Reborn” and adapt “The Shadow Rising” instead risks losing important story developments and character arcs. Combining two books, “The Dragon Reborn” and “The Shadow Rising,” in one season would require significant changes and alterations to fit both narratives, impacting major characters’ storylines.

Literature Review

The changes made in The Wheel of Time’s first two seasons will hardly compare to season 3, which is on track to be even more different from the books.

Warning: Spoilers for The Wheel of Time season 2

The changes made to the source material in The Wheel of Time’s first two seasons are guaranteed to pale in comparison to season 3. In its efforts to bring Robert Jordan’s award-winning fantasy series from the page to the small screen, Amazon Prime Video made some noteworthy changes to the original Wheel of Time books. While some have been welcomed, it’s not surprising that many have been met with criticism.

Due to the size of the cast in the novels and the sheer length of each book, it’s understandable that at least some adjustments had to be made for the story to work in a television format that only allows for eight episodes per season. That explains why certain characters haven’t appeared or have had their roles reduced. However, that’s not the extent of The Wheel of Time’s changes. It also elevated background characters like Anvaere Damodred, redefined key relationships, and included new events that never happened in the books. But while seasons 1 and 2 certainly took numerous liberties, they’ll surely be outdone by season 3.

Skipping The Dragon Reborn Would Completely Change Wheel Of Time’s Story

Though the season hasn’t even released yet, it’s already apparent that season 3 will have to differ from the course set by the books, simply because of the novel it’s choosing to adapt. It’s been confirmed that The Wheel of Time season 3 will cover The Shadow Rising, the fourth installment in the series. What stands out about that decision is that book 3, The Dragon Reborn, should be next in line now that Rand has defeated Ishamael in Falme. Jumping straight into The Shadow Rising would change things significantly, especially considering that multiple story developments took place in The Dragon Reborn.

The Dragon Reborn features a handful of arcs The Wheel of Time would risk losing if its events don’t happen. The biggest, of course, would be Rand al’Thor taking the Stone of Tear and claiming Callandor, the weapon he’s destined to use to fight the Dark One at the Last Battle. There’s also the progression that Elayne and Egwene experience at the White Tower, as both still need to complete their Accepted tests and meet Galad and Gawyn (Egwene’s main love interest). Other stories worth mentioning are Mat’s team-up with Thom Merrilin and Perrin’s long search for Rand, which introduces Faile.

Many of these stories hold great importance to the wider narrative. Mat’s arc helps give a backstory to Thom, one of the books’ biggest characters, while also drawing Elayne’s family into the bigger picture. Getting Callandor is a massive step that Rand needs to take to fulfill the Dragon Reborn prophecy, and time needs to be made for Elayne and Egwene to progress in their training, particularly since the latter is fated to become the Amyrlin Seat in Wheel of Time’s future. As for Perrin, Faile becomes his wife, so teaming up with her on the road is similarly essential to the show’s future.

Combining Two Wheel Of Time Books Would Force Several More Changes

Due to the significance of the aforementioned storylines, The Wheel of Time skipping everything in The Dragon Reborn is extremely unlikely. A more feasible scenario is that the show finds ways to combine parts of The Dragon Reborn with The Shadow Rising. However, this route would also facilitate multiple changes. To fit The Eye of the World and The Great Hunt’s narratives into single seasons, the show had to adjust when characters were introduced, where stories occurred, and which characters were involved. Even more of these changes would be necessary if The Wheel of Time tried to adapt two books at once.

For instance, Wheel of Time may have to write out Tear entirely if it tries to incorporate The Dragon Reborn’s ending in season 3’s story. Tear is a long way away from the Aiel Waste, and the battle that took place there is far removed from his journey through the Aiel Waste.As for Egwene, there’s no reason why she’d meet Gawyn in Tanchico (the place where she goes in The Shadow Rising) so Wheel of Time would have to alter the trajectory of one of the characters’ stories to make that meeting happen. Similar allowances would have to be made to work in Juilin Sandar, Faile, and Thom Merrilin.

In addition to the characters mentioned above, Mat and Perrin’s arcs would also be heavily affected. If Mat goes with Rand to the Aiel Waste in season 3 as he did in The Shadow Rising, he can’t go with Thom and meet Queen Morgase in Caemlyn since these stories happen in different corners of the world and are thus incompatible. Whichever arc it gives to Mat will rob him of the other. All things considered, no major character’s story would go untouched by the changes that will follow the move from The Great Hunt to The Shadow Rising.

Wheel Of Time’s Fires Of Heaven Plan Could Make Season 3’s Changes Even Worse

Further complicating matters is The Wheel of Time’s plans for The Fires of Heaven. The fifth book in the series, The Fires of Heaven continues Rand’s experiences in the Aiel Waste, pits him against Rahvin of the 13 Forsaken, and sees him grow into his role as a leader. According to showrunner Rafe Judkins, The Wheel of Time season 3 will draw on “elements” of The Fires of Heaven as it explores the events of The Shadow Rising. In other words, Amazon’s show may find itself juggling as many as three different books in the series.

Since too much happens in The Dragon Reborn for it to be ignored entirely and The Fires of Heaven factors into the story in some shape or form, The Wheel of Time would have to cut some of the books’ stories in order for them to fit into a single season. After all, it’s important to note that each book is more than enough material for an eight-episode season; adding anything else to the mix could make the story unrecognizable from what was in the source material. When it comes to The Fires of Heaven, the show’s best option may be to keep its place in season 3 to a minimum, allowing it to take center stage in The Wheel of Time season 4.

The first two seasons of The Wheel of Time are available to stream on Amazon Prime Video.
