Albert Han s Complete 9-1-1 Timeline

SUMMARY

Albert Han evolves from a minor character to a main character throughout the six seasons of 9-1-1, showcasing his own special moments. Albert’s relationship with his older brother Chimney is complex and evolves throughout the series, with Chimney eventually realizing his jealousy towards Albert and their reconnection healing old wounds. Albert becomes a firefighter like his brother but later questions if he truly wants to pursue this career path, exploring other possibilities for his future. However, he ultimately plays a crucial role in bridging the gap between his family’s past and present.

Literature Review

Albert Han is a recurring character in 9-1-1 who at one point even becomes a main character, here’s Albert’s story through every season of the show.

Albert Han is a recurring character on 9-1-1 who ends up becoming a main character in the cast over the course of the series. 9-1-1 is a police procedural drama about the various emergency service workers in Los Angeles. The show was co-created by Ryan Murphy though has few of the hallmarks that his better-known series have, making it unique among his filmography. 9-1-1 is an episodic series that premiered in 2018 and has lasted for six seasons. Despite being canceled by Fox, 9-1-1 season 7 is in production thanks to ABC.

There are plenty of likable characters in 9-1-1 who come from all walks of life and have various duties in the LAPD, LA Fire Department, dispatchers’ offices, and emergency medical services. One of the members of the LAFD and a main character in the series, Howard “Chimney” Han (Kenneth Choi), has a younger half-brother who lives in Korea. Albert Han (John Harlan Kim) first appears in 9-1-1 as a minor character in Chimney’s arc, but his screen time slowly increases throughout the six seasons of the show, earning his own special moments.

S3 - Albert Han Moves From Korea To Los Angeles

Albert first appears in 9-1-1 in season 3, episode 9 “Seize the Day”. In this episode, he unexpectedly shows up at Chimney’s doorstep, having come all the way from Korea to LA, with the expectation he would live with his distant half-brother. Albert explains he wanted to escape their overbearing father and Chimney is glad to have him stay. However, resentful feelings regarding his father create tension between the brothers, though Chimney says that he’s over it.

Things come to a head when Albert meets the rest of Chimney’s friends and coworkers, and they take to Albert better than they ever have to Chimney. Chimney lashes out at Albert and nearly drives him away, apologizing just in time before he leaves for good. Albert represents Chimney’s old life, and Chimney realizes he’s actually jealous of Albert. Albert may have found his father overbearing, but Chimney would have given anything for his father to pay him attention, essentially having been abandoned by him as a child.

Now, Albert is once again getting more attention than Chimney, and the older brother can’t help but compare this situation to his feelings regarding his father and family. Chimney may be one of the best characters in 9-1-1, but that doesn’t mean he’s free from mistakes. Albert, for his part, readily forgives Chimney and shows himself to be a mature, honest young man. Chimney and Albert spend the rest of season 3 together, with Albert only appearing in two more episodes.

9-1-1 also has a spinoff series, 9-1-1 Lonestar, starring Rob Lowe, which is still airing on Fox. This series follows a similar format to 9-1-1 but takes place in Austin, Texas.

S4 - Albert Officially Becomes A Main Character & A Firefighter

In season 4 of 9-1-1, Albert not only becomes a main character of the series but a firefighter as well, following in his brother’s footsteps. Once Chimney moves back in with Maddie (Jennifer Love Hewitt), Albert has to find new accommodations and ends up living with Buck (Oliver Stark), Maddie’s younger brother and a fellow firefighter. Friends for the most part, Buck and Albert nearly fall out when Albert briefly dates Buck’s ex, Veronica, making up afterward.

Later in the season, Albert is involved in a major car crash when a drunk driver sends his car flipping end-over-end into a ditch in a dramatic and chaotic scene. Emergency service workers barely reach him in time but manage to resuscitate him with CPR, though he remains in a coma for several days. When he awakes, he sees his older brother weeping. His coma coincides with the birth of his niece, Maddie and Chimney’s daughter, and the news of her birth raises both their spirits.

Albert recovers in Chimney and Maddie’s home but begins to feel like a burden considering they also have a baby to attend to. Fortunately, he has the LAFD certification course to keep himself occupied with. In the season 4 finale, Albert announces that he’s passed the LAFD course and will become a full-time firefighter, one of his goals since arriving in America.

S5 - Albert Reconsiders If He Really Wants To Be A Firefighter

In season 5, Albert Han returns as a recurring character rather than a member of the main cast. Actor John Harlan Kim even missed the season 5 premiere. Showrunner Tim Minear told TV Insider,

“Albert is on Nancy Drew. [Laughs] Albert passed his LAFD training and he’s in a different house and we will be checking in with him very shortly.”

Minear is referring to the 2019 CW TV show Nancy Drew, based on the classic children’s mystery book series of the same name. Kim appears as a recurring character in that series as Agent Park, a character who briefly dates Nancy.

Despite working hard to become a firefighter with the Los Angeles Fire Department, Albert begins to reconsider his decision to pursue this career in 9-1-1 season 5. The first time Albert has the chance to firefight alongside his brother is at a call center fire. Albert and Chimney crawl onto the roof only for it to collapse, nearly sending Albert into the flames below were it not for Chimney, who catches him at the last second. Events like this one and the approaching end of his probationary period have Albert questioning if he even wants to be a firefighter.

Albert had been questioning this path for a while, vocalizing his fears and worries to Chimney, but his older brother dismissed them as the usual uncertainties new recruits always have. However, after the events at the call center, Chimney tells Albert that if leaving is what he wants, then that’s okay too. It’s a sweet brother-to-brother moment that shows how far the pair have grown since he arrived from Korea in season 3. The bad history they’ve shared regarding their father has grown into a strong and loving fraternal relationship.

As he explores what he wants to do next, Albert takes an aptitude test that says he would be well suited for a career as an archaeologist, librarian, sociologist, trial lawyer, or cosmetologist. While 9-1-1 never shows which, if any, of these paths Albert decided to take, it’s good to know he has a life filled with opportunities ahead of him after season 5.

S6 - Having Returned To Korea, Albert Visits Chimney

Sometime between season 5 and season 6, Albert returned to Korea, and is now presumably living happily with his parents, but he does return to America in two episodes of 9-1-1. Chimney is happy to see his half-brother again but recoils when he sees Albert has secretly brought his mother and their father. After an awkward reunion, Chimney invites everyone in for dinner and to meet Maddie’s parents and Chimney’s baby Jee.

Chimney confronts Albert, asking him what he was thinking, but Albert counters that even if Chimney had a bad relationship with his father, that doesn’t mean Jee should have to have a poor one with her grandfather. Watching the families meeting and greeting, Chimney realizes that his younger brother is right and that bringing their father to America has helped mend some wounds.

This is a critical scene for Albert. He came to Los Angeles hoping to escape the problems he was having in Korea, but after enough time spent there, he realized where his true home was. But what he also realized was that his brother was missing a piece of home as well. Chimney needed some sense of closure with his father and Korea, and Albert is the only one in a position to make that happen. Thankfully, he does, and Albert bridges the gap between a family with the experience he gained during his time in LA.

What’s Next For Albert In 9-1-1 Season 7

It does not appear that John Harlan Kim will be returning to 9-1-1 in anything larger than a guest capacity in 9-1-1 season 7. But there is one notable opportunity that would make sense for Albert to return to the show: Maddie and Chimney should be getting married in season 7. This would be the perfect opportunity to bring Albert back.
