Niat Ruc” - What Crowleys Bentley License Plate Means In Good Omens

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1000 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Crowley’s Bentley and its personalized license plate “Niat Ruc” have a deeper meaning, referencing the suicidal leaves section in Monty Python’s The Meaning of Life. The license plate is also a nod to Terry Gilliam, who worked on the animation of The Meaning of Life and was previously involved in a failed film adaptation of Good Omens. The license plate’s meaning becomes more significant in Good Omens season 2, as Crowley and Aziraphale part ways and Crowley’s character takes on a darker outlook on life in season 3.

Literature Review

Good Omens is packed with small details with deep meanings and fun connections to other works, and among them is Crowley’s “Niat Ruc” license plate.

Good Omens’ Crowley (David Tennant) only has one best friend outside of angel Aziraphale (Michael Sheen) — his Bentley, and Crowley’s car has a distinctive personalized license plate “Niat Ruc” with a symbolic deeper meaning (as expected from a story by Neil Gaiman). After saving the world in season 1, Good Omens season 2 saw Aziraphale and Crowley in a new story, as the first season covered the complete source novel. They didn’t have to deal with the destructive plans of Heaven and Hell. Instead, they had to keep archangel Gabriel (Jon Hamm) safe.

As Good Omens season 2 tells a brand-new story that goes beyond the source material, many characters didn’t return. There were some overlaps and returning faces from Good Omens season 1 though, along with some well-known settings and objects such as Crowley’s car. Good Omens season 2 took viewers back to Aziraphale’s bookshop and saw the return of Crowley’s always faithful black Bentley, which accompanied him on many adventures. Crowley’s car is one-of-a-kind, but what has caught the attention of viewers the most is the license plate, which reads “Niat Ruc.” It has a deeper meaning than it seems.

Crowley’s License Plate Is “Curtain” Backwards, Indicating Death Crowley’s Car In Good Omen Both Hints At His Demonic Purpose & References Monty Python

Crowley’s license plate might sound like a sort of spell or something that only a demon would understand, but it’s the word “curtain” spelled backward – however, this doesn’t mean it doesn’t have a deeper meaning, and it’s also a reference to another piece of pop culture. Speaking to Syfy in 2019, Neil Gaiman and producer and director Douglas Mackinnon confirmed that Crowley’s “Niat Ruc” license plate is a reference to the suicidal leaves section in Monty Python’s The Meaning of Life, where the writing on the mausoleum reads “Niat Ruc.”

“The license plate of Crowley’s Bentley is ‘Curtain’ backwards… Curtain backwards, like it’s the final curtain… There are so many more easter eggs that I don’t think we could just casually list them all. But the lovely thing about all of these things is that you can miss them, and you won’t mind.”

Crowley’s “Niat Ruc” license plate is also a nod to actor and director Terry Gilliam, who wrote and worked on the animation of The Meaning of Life. In the late 1990s and early 2000s, Gilliam was set to direct a film adaptation of Good Omens, but the project lacked enough funds, and Gilliam moved on to other projects. Although Gilliam was still up for directing a Good Omens movie in 2006, the project still couldn’t get the funds it needed, and it eventually evolved into a TV series with a third season in the making.

What Crowley’s License Plate Means For His Character’s Future The “Curtains Closing” Imagery Continues To Gain Significance

Good Omens had a couple of twists and big reveals up its sleeve and ended on a heartbreaking note with Crowley and Aziraphale parting ways. Crowley finally accepted his feelings for Aziraphale and opened up to the angel about it, but Aziraphale had just been offered the spot of supreme archangel in Heaven. Aziraphale told Crowley he could join him and become an angel again, but Crowley wanted Aziraphale to stay on Earth with him.

Crowley, then, chose the “final curtain” over a life with Aziraphale in Heaven, marking the end of their partnership and destroying all hope of them being together and living a happy life on Earth, without Heaven and Hell bothering them.

Through flashbacks since season 1, Good Omens showed how Aziraphale and Crowley complemented each other over time, with the angel bringing out the best of the demon. Without Aziraphale and with a broken heart, Crowley might have a more disastrous and dark outlook on life in Good Omens season 3. The “Niat Ruc” license plate will now be more fitting for Crowley after the events of Good Omens season 2, and he surely won’t be the same Crowley viewers have come to know so well in season 3.
