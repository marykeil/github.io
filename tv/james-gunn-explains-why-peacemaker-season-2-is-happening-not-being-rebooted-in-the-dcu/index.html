James Gunn Explains Why Peacemaker Season 2 Is Happening & Not Being Rebooted In The DCU

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 600 words 3 minutes 
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

James Gunn teases why he chose to continue Peacemaker with a second season instead of starting over. Gunn says that Peacemaker’s jump from the DCEU to the new DCU will not be confusing. Gunn “has a way in” to explain the DC series changing universes.

Literature Review

James Gunn is currently writing Peacemaker season 2, and he has now commented on how the show will jump from the DCEU to the new DC universe.

James Gunn has talked about the decision not to reboot Peacemaker for the new DC Universe. Gunn is currently halfway done writing Peacemaker season 2’s story, which will take the series to a new setting, as the old DCEU has been replaced with the new DC Universe, which starts this year with the animated Creature Commandos. While details on how Peacemaker season 2 will fit in with DC’s upcoming releases are not clear, Gunn has commented on why the series will not be rebooted.

On Threads, Gunn teased why he chose to move ahead with Peacemaker season 2 instead of rebooting the series following the DCEU’s reboot in favor of the new DC Universe.

While Gunn said fans will have to wait to see how exactly Peacemaker season 2 will be different due to the new DCU, the director teased that “It won’t be confusing.” According to Gunn, he has “a way in” to explain how the Peacemaker characters are canon to the DC Universe with season 2.

How Peacemaker Season 2 Could Fit In The New DC Universe The New Season Is Part Of The DCU’s Chapter One

While Gunn has yet to reveal any details from the story of Peacemaker season 2, some of the director’s updates on the series have teased what fans can expect from the DCU show. Recently, Gunn confirmed Peacemaker season 2’s place in the DCU. According to the new universe’s creative chief, Peacemaker season 2 will be part of the DCU’s Chapter One.

Gunn has already confirmed that several members from Peacemaker season 1 will continue as their characters in the new DC Universe, which includes John Cena as Peacemaker, Freddie Stroma as Vigilant, Viola Davis as Amanda Waller, and more. Following Peacemaker season 1’s ending, the series could have some interesting ties to another DCU TV show. That show would be none other than Waller.

Davis will finally get to lead her own project as Amanda Waller and Peacemaker season 1’s ending teased what the DCU’s Waller series could be about. Leota Adebayo revealed to the world in Peacemaker season 1 that her mother, Waller, was in charge of Project Butterfly and the Suicide Squad. DC’s upcoming Waller series, which is confirmed to feature characters from Peacemaker, could deal with the fallout of that decision, setting up the story of Peacemaker season 2.
