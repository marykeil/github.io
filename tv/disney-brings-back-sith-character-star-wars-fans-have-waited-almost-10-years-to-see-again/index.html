Disney Brings Back Sith Character Star Wars Fans Have Waited Almost 10 Years To See Again

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Asajj Ventress, a notable Sith character from Star Wars: The Clone Wars, has returned to Star Wars after 10 years. Ventress will make her return in Star Wars: The Bad Batch season 3, as the new trailer for the animated series’ final season has revealed. Ventress’ return comes with notable character differences already, most notably her yellow-bladed lightsaber that’s a stark contrast from her old red lightsabers.

Literature Review

10 years after her last appearance in Star Wars: The Clone Wars, one Sith has officially made her return in The Bad Batch season 3 trailer.

Star Wars just announced that Star Wars: The Bad Batch season 3 will premiere on Disney+ on February 21, and the trailer signaled that one Sith character from Star Wars: The Clone Wars is finally making an appearance after her fate was sealed in the canon novel Star Wars: Dark Disciple. The Bad Batch season 2 ended with several cliffhangers, from the presumed death of one of the Bad Batch’s own, Tech, to the kidnapping of Omega by the Empire as a motivator to make Nala Se help with his clone project. The new trailer added even more questions.

Star Wars has at last shared the first trailer for The Bad Batch season 3, and it features the return of the previously Sith character Asajj Ventress. She was last seen helping Ahsoka in The Clone Wars series before her journey in Dark Disciple. Although Ventress is confirmed to have died to save Quinlan Vos’ life in Dark Disciple, her status in the wake of the rise of the Empire is less clear, and the trailer has some interesting hints.

Asajj Ventress Returns In The Bad Batch Season 3 Trailer

Before showing her on screen, The Bad Batch season 3 trailer includes a voice-over from Asajj Ventress, saying “I wasn’t planning on killing you, but you’re making it very tempting” over a black screen. It then cuts to a shocking image of Ventress, showing her not only with a head of hair rather than her iconic shaven head look but also, and more notably, holding a yellow lightsaber. She then goes on to effortlessly block multiple blaster bolts, presumably from Clone Force 99, before running at whomever she is speaking to.

Ventress’ last appearance was during The Clone Wars season 5, when Ahsoka Tano was falsely accused of murder and on the run. During that time, Ventress helped Ahsoka, although both Ahsoka and Anakin Skywalker were suspicious of her, believing it was Ventress who had framed Ahsoka before it was revealed to be Barriss Offee. While that indicated that she may be less committed to the dark side, this is still a major shift for the character, who was previously not only aligned with the dark side but also trained under none other than Count Dooku until he discarded her.

After assisting Ahsoka, however, Asajj disappeared from the show, much to the disappointment of the fans. Asajj was always an interesting character, having come from the Nightsisters of Dathomir originally, training under Jedi Master Ky Narec until his death, becoming an apprentice to Count Dooku, and then being essentially in exile and left in ambiguity. It seems that finally The Bad Batch will give audiences answers about her fate after The Clone Wars and in the wake of Order 66.

The Bad Batch Season 3 Trailer Hints Ventress Has Returned To The Jedi

Ventress has made her return with a yellow-bladed lightsaber, hinting that the previous Sith may have returned to the Jedi way since viewers last saw her in The Clone Wars series. While she may not be fully a Jedi, her previous training under a Jedi Master and the color change of her lightsaber signal that she may have returned to the light side of the Force, suggesting she may be well on her way to becoming one. This would be a thrilling turn for Star Wars and innumerable ways the franchise could continue her story.

With such a brief appearance in the trailer, it’s difficult to say what shape her arc will take in this final installment of The Bad Batch, but all signs point to Asajj taking the path of a Jedi. This is somewhat supported by her Star Wars history thus far, too, as she maintained a certain level of affection, if not for the Jedi, then for her former master at least, even keeping his lightsabers in her castle. This would also give a compelling redemption arc to a character who has experienced significant loss and rejection throughout her life.

Why Is Asajj Ventress Confronting Clone Force 99?

Given that the trailer hints that Asajj has rejected the Sith path, as she’s replaced her red lightsabers with yellow—another fascinating choice by Star Wars, as that color was made famous by Rey at the end of Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker—it raises the question of why she was fighting Clone Force 99. In the trailer, it seems the Bad Batch is shooting at Ventress, and she is dodging their blasts and poised to attack. It isn’t clear what would have spurred this confrontation on and what would have been ‘very tempting’ about killing them.

Theoretically, had Asajj returned to the light side, she and the Bad Batch would be on the same side. This could mean that, although donning a yellow lightsaber, Asajj hasn’t actually rejoined the Jedi path. It could also be that the edits of the trailer were intentionally misleading; perhaps it isn’t the Bad Batch and Asajj Ventress fighting one another after all. Of course, there are numerous possibilities, but the answers remain to be seen. What is confirmed is that Asajj Ventress will return in The Bad Batch season 3, which will begin with a three-episode premiere on February 21.
