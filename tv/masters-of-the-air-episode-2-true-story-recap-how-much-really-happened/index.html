Summary Bucky’s demotion to COO of the 418 squad was true in real life, as documented by the American Air Museum. Ken Lemmons likely had a relationship with local children, as his role as Ground Personnel would have involved them. While there is no record of a 349th plane crashing during a practice flight, such accidents were common during World War II.

In Masters of the Air episode 2, the 100th Bomb Group enjoy some much-needed fun after suffering losses during their first mission, but are any of these amusing moments true to real life? Masters of the Air is a World War II drama that is a companion to Bands of Brothers and The Pacific. In particular, it tells the true story of the 100th Bomb Group, nicknamed the “Bloody Hundredth” after suffering immense losses during the summer of 1943. It’s based on Donald Miller’s book, “Masters of the Air: America’s Bomber Boys Who Fought the Air War Against Nazi Germany.” While Masters of the Air episode 1 set the scene for the 100th Bomb Group’s important players, episode 2 expands more on the world of Thorpe Abbotts. New characters are introduced, demotions and promotions are handed out, and hints are dropped about certain mysterious characters. Meanwhile, the soldiers await their next dangerous mission which, based on their decreasing numbers, could be absolutely lethal. All in all, Masters of the Air episode 2 digs deeper into the characters and their more human sides.

8 Did Bucky Get Demoted From Air Exec? Bucky becomes COO of the 418 squad

In episode 1, Bucky is told by Colonel Harold Huglin that his lax behavior isn’t suitable for an Air Exec. In return, Bucky says he would prefer to be flying planes rather than doing paperwork. This conversation has repercussions in episode 2 when Bucky announces that he has been demoted to COO of the 418 squad, which, in reality, is exactly what he wanted. Ultimately, this demotion did happen in real life. The American Air Museum writes that Egan began his time with the 100th as Operations Officer, but in June 1943 he became the leader of the 418th.

7 Did Ken Lemmons Let Local Children Help Him With The Planes? Ken Lemmons allows local children to burn off engine oil

Episode 2 introduces Sgt. Ken Lemmons to the Masters of the Air cast, a talented 19-year-old member of the Ground Personnel. Soon enough, it is revealed that Lemmons has a sweet relationship with the children living around Thorpe Abbotts. He tells them they can help him clean up by setting fire to the airstrip, which excites them. Although Lemmons’ relationship with the children is not documented online, this detail could certainly be true. Lemmons was the subject of a book, “The Forgotten Man,” which explored his pivotal role as Ground Personnel, which likely involved the locals as well.

6 Did A 349th Plane Crash During A Practice? New members of the 349th perished during a practice flight

While much of Masters of the Air episode 2 has a happier tone, there are still moments of darkness, including a plane accident which kills new members of the 349th. In the previous episode, most of the 349th were shot down by German soldiers. Therefore, the unit must be recouped, however it seems to be suffering from bad luck, as proven by this accidental crash during a test flight. In real life, there is no account of an accidental crash of a 349th plane. However, plane crashes during test flights such as these were quite common during World War II.

5 Did Buck, Bucky, & Biddick Get Into A Fight With British Soldiers? Buck challenged British soldiers to a fist fight

During Masters of the Air episode 2, Gale “Buck” Cleven, Bucky, Curtis Biddick, and other 100th soldiers drink alongside British soldiers at a local pub. However, rather than making friends with their fellow militarymen, the British soldiers judge the 100th, criticizing their nicknames and their day bombing technique. As a surprising result, Buck challenges the British to a fist fight, which Biddick wins. Whether this fist fight truly happened is unclear. Like many personal moments such as these, there aren’t many records beyond primary sources, which are hard to come by. If anything, this scene shows a different side to Buck’s character.

4 Did Harry Crosby Take Bubbles’ Place As Lead Navigator? Bubbles’ sudden illness leads Crosby to become the permanent Lead Navigator

One significant part of Masters of the Air episode 2 aside from the fun and games is Harry Crosby’s newfound promotion. When Crosby’s friend Bubbles becomes sick, Crosby takes his place as Lead Navigator for a mission. Although Crosby is afraid, he successfully brings the 100th home, and furthermore, leads Biddick to safely to Scotland. While Crosby may or may not have unexpectedly taken the place of a soldier named Bubbles, it is true that he quickly became the Lead Navigator of the 100th. He went on to lead them through various important missions.

3 Did Biddick’s Plane Crash In Scotland? Plane trouble forced Biddick to crash land in Scotland

The 100th Bomb Group’s main mission in episode 2 was to take a twelve-hour flight to Trondheim, Norway, and while this trip was mostly successful, one hitch was that Curtis Biddick had to land in Scotland. After his plane began showing signs of mechanical troubles, Biddick was ordered to land in Scotland, led by Harry Crosby. Though the landing looked quite risky, Biddick ultimately landed him and his crew safely in a vegetable patch. As it happens, this crash landing did happen in real life. The vegetable patch belonged to an RAF commanding officer in Aberdeen.

2 Was There Really A Bike Race Among The 100th? Buck and Bucky participate in a 100th Bomb Group bike race

After the successful mission to Norway, it seems that the men of the 100th Bomb Group get to enjoy a fun evening of dancing and music. Bucky sings along with the band, and he and Buck call Biddick in Scotland. But the cherry on top is an impromptu bike race around a building in Thorpe Abbotts that ends with Buck and Bucky in a pile on the floor. It seems that the real 100th were not unfamiliar with bike races. According to the diary of Maj. Marvin Bowman, the 100th often held bike races around the perimeter of the base.

1 Was Buck’s Father Really A Gambler? Buck reveals a bit of his surprising backstory

At the end of Masters of the Air episode 2, while Buck and Bucky are watching bombs fly down near Thorpe Abbotts, Buck reveals why he does not participate in drinking, dancing, or gambling of any kind. He reveals that his father was a serious gambler, and that he often spent his childhood sleeping in gambling parlors, which kept him on the straight and narrow as an adult. It is unclear whether this surprising backstory is true to the real life Buck Cleven, however, it definitely offers more insight into his character moving forward in Masters of the Air.
