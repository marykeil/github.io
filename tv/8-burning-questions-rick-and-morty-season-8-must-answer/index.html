8 Burning Questions Rick And Morty Season 8 Must Answer

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Morty’s growth leads to more independent adventures in season 8. Mr. Poopybutthole’s mystery deepens as he settles into a new universe. Rick faces the reality of his loss and may seek reconciliation with Birdperson.

Literature Review

Rick and Morty is a show that raises just as many questions as it answers, and with season 8, the show continues to have a lot to unpack.

Rick and Morty has been going strong since 2013, but it has raised several questions that need to be answered in Rick and Morty season 8. Rick and Morty is a beloved animated series aimed at a more mature audience, starring the genius inventor, Rick Sanchez, and his grandson from another dimension, Morty Smith. The series is complex and generally indulges in episodic adventures, but there’s also a larger interconnected story throughout. Rick and Morty season 7 returned in 2023 and will feature a lengthy gap until season 8, which is set to be released in 2025. With changes behind the scenes, the series may need some extra time to prepare for the future of the show, but hopefully, that means the series will revisit some elements that still need to be answered. Season 7 saw the demise of Rick’s ultimate nemesis, Rick Prime, but there is still a lot left to explore in the multiverse of Rick and Morty.

8 Will Morty Go On More Solo Adventures? Now That He Has Overcome His Greatest Fears

For most of the series, Rick and Morty have had a symbiotic relationship, with Rick needing Morty to accompany him on his wild adventures and keep him grounded, or sometimes simply to serve as a shield. Morty relied on the connection with his grandfather at first out of a deep sense of love and obligation, but over time, the relationship experienced tremendous challenges that divided the adventurous duo, and in turn caused Morty to only see himself as a companion to his grandfather. In the season 7 finale, that all changed. Rick and Morty introduced the Fear Hole, a powerful portal that shows people their deepest, darkest fears and forces them to confront them. Morty was trapped in the hole for what felt like decades to him, but outside, it was a very brief time. The hole helped Morty to recognize his codependent relationship with his unhinged grandfather and forced him to confront a reality where he needed to rely on himself and make a life without his grandpa Rick. With this revelation, season 8 could see Morty’s growth continue forward to become even more independent and have his own independent adventures.

7 Is Mr. Poopybutthole Going To Be Discovered? Now That He’s Hiding In A New Reality

For years, the quirky character, Mr. Poopybutthole, has continued to appear in the show in a reduced capacity after his introduction. Often framing the seasons when he appears in the short cold opens or tags at the beginning and end of each series. Over these short clips, Poopybutthole’s life has fallen into disrepair and seen him separate from his wife, but season 7’s end tag saw him use Rick’s portal gun to settle into a universe where he is still with his wife. Despite just arriving, his wife appears immediately suspicious.

6 How Is Rick After Killing Rick Prime? He Got His Vengeance

Season 7 saw Rick finally locate and face his greatest foe, Rick Prime. Rick Prime was the person who made Rick C-137 choose to explore the multiverse after he killed his wife and child in the C-137 universe. Rick then spends his life in pursuit of Rick Prime, even going so far as to move into his universe and adopt his grandson. For the entire series run, Rick has been chasing Rick Prime, and now that he’s finally dead, Rick needs to face the reality of his loss instead of focusing on his desire for revenge.

5 Will Birdperson And Rick Reconcile? Rick Wasn’t Always A Good Friend

Rick and Birdperson were best friends for a long time before the events of the show took place. They fought together, had adventures, and partied, as seen in episodes like “Ricksy Business” in season 1, but things soured between them when Birdperson got into a serious committed relationship. Rick is an incredibly selfish character at the best of times, but seeing his friend happy, Rick struggles to accept the change in their dynamics. Things were then made even worse when it turned out that Birdperson’s beloved, Tammy, was an undercover agent trying to capture Rick and him. Rick wasn’t there to support his friend, and when Birdperson was brutally killed, Rick failed to save him. Following this, Rick gathered his friend’s body parts and set to work on bringing him back to life, which he managed to do in season 5, but once again, Rick proved how selfish he was when he admitted that he wasn’t planning to reveal Birdperson had a child with Tammy. He appeared once again in season 7, but the two have yet to mend their relationship.

4 Will Rick And Morty Go Beyond The Curve? Since The Purpose Of The Curve Is Now Fulfilled

Rick and Morty has discussed the Central Finite Curve a couple of times, revealing that it contains every universe where Rick is the smartest person alive. At points in the series, it was broken, restored, and most recently shaken up by Rick, which helped him find and defeat his ultimate rival, Rick Prime. The only person to go beyond this Curve so far is Evil Morty, but with the purpose of the Curve having been served, namely, finding Rick Prime, it could be time for Rick and his family to venture beyond the Curve and explore other realities.

3 What’s Happening With Evil Morty? And Where Has He Disappeared To?

Evil Morty traveled beyond the Curve in season 5, and he briefly returned in season 7 to aid Rick C-137 in taking down his nemesis. Evil Morty is now living outside the Curve, but what is he doing in the wider multiverse, and will Rick and Morty ever need to fight him? Evil Morty is the most formidable opponent that the team has ever come across, and he’s managed to win against them and complete his missions on multiple occasions, sparing Rick and Morty more out of disregard than anything else.

2 Will Rick Find A New Purpose? He Finally Seems To Be Finding Happiness

Rick is slowly learning to let go of his past without the hold of his nemesis still running free. However, Rick is not the healthiest person, and his obsessive and addictive tendencies could see him chasing a new goal soon. Rick may decide to look for his best friend Birdperson and settle their differences, or explore the universe beyond the Curve. There are plenty of ways for his story to continue, but with his track record, it’s unlikely to be straightforward and could continue to cause stress for his family.

1 Will Summer Get To Play An Even Bigger Part? Summer Is More Like Her Grandpa Than Morty

Since the second season, Summer has seen her role expand. At first, she was just the older sister left at home while Rick and Morty went on adventures, but Summer has proven herself a capable traveler like her grandpa and space mom. Summer is driven to adventure and exploration, and she makes the episodes where she has a bigger role better, so seeing her take on more adventures makes sense for Rick and Morty going forward.
