The 10 Most Dramatic Bachelor Nation Self Eliminations Ranked

SUMMARY

The upcoming Bachelor season 28 will have the largest number of women competing for the bachelor’s heart in the show’s history.

Literature Review

Bachelor Nation shows have a robust history of contestants quitting mid-season. Here are the 10 most dramatic self-eliminations of all time, ranked.

Since The Bachelor first aired on ABC in 2002, Bachelor Nation shows have had a robust history of contestants quitting midstream. The shows normally revolve around a lead handing out roses to contestants that he, or she, wants to get to know better. But sometimes, for a myriad of reasons, contestants decide to reject the rose. The most recent Bachelor Nation installment was The Golden Bachelor season 1, which was historic in many ways. It followed the franchise’s first senior lead, Gerry Turner, as he dated 22 senior women. The pilot season was a hit among fans. The Bachelor season 28 is set to premiere in a few weeks. It will follow tennis pro Joey Graziadei as he searches for love. A total of 33 women will be moving into the villa to try to win Joey’s heart, the biggest number in the show’s history. As the women get ready to compete for the final rose, let’s take a look back at Bachelor Nation’s 10 most dramatic self-eliminations.

10 Cassie Randolph The Bachelor Season 23

Cassie Randolph became the first contestant in Bachelor Nation history to quit and win the final rose, all in the same season. Cassie made it all the way to the final four during The Bachelor season 23, but she chose to send herself home right before the fantasy suite. The sudden shift in Cassie’s feelings came after her father expressed his disapproval of the couple’s relationship. Her leaving the show made Colton Underwood realize that she was the woman for him. Colton convinced her to come back and accept his final rose. The couple dated for a while but split up in 2020.

9 Madison Prewett The Bachelor Season 24

Madison Prewett made it to the final two during The Bachelor season 24. After their fantasy suite date, Madison decided that she and airline pilot Peter Weber were too different to be together and sent herself home. During the second night of the two-part finale, Peter proposed to model Hannah Ann Sluss, but during After The Final Rose, it was revealed that Peter and Hannah had broken up. Though both Peter and Madison both admitted that they still had feelings for each other, they ultimately decided not to try dating again. In 2021, Madison married Grant Troutt after three months of dating.

8 Michael Allio The Bachelorette Season 17

Single dad Michael Allio lost his wife to cancer a few years before becoming a contestant on The Bachelorette season 17. The widower connected with Katie Thurston and made it to the final four. Things were going well between him and Katie, until his 4-year-old son, James, FaceTimed him, asking his dad why he didn’t want to see him anymore. Though Michael had developed strong feelings for Katie and would have liked to have continued getting to know her, he felt that his responsibility as a parent to his son had to come first, so he quit the show (via Bachelor Nation).

7 Greg Grippo The Bachelorette Season 17

During The Bachelorette season 17, Katie Thurston gave her first impression rose to Gregg Grippo. During their first one-on-one date, Katie revealed she was falling for him. He scored another one-on-one date soon after that. Greg was a front-runner all season long and even made it to the final four. During the hometown dates, Greg told Katie he loved her. Katie, who had made it clear that she wasn’t going to tell anyone she loved them until the end, replied, “I just love looking at you,” which really upset Greg (via Bachelor Nation.) Feeling that Katie had dismissed his feelings, he quit the show on the spot and went home.

6 Sydney Lotuaco The Bachelor Season 23

Professional NBA dancer Sydney Lotuaco eliminated herself from The Bachelor season 23. She eliminated herself because she realized that former athlete Colton Underwood wasn’t really interested in pursuing a relationship with her. “With the choices you’ve been making, you’re taking a lot of easier choices with the people you’re pursuing. If that’s what you’re looking for, great, do that, go after that,” Sydney told him at the time. “There are some very wonderful people in that group. Find them. Figure out who they are. Don’t be distracted by shiny things.”

5 Brendan Morais The Bachelorette Season 16

During The Bachelorette season 16, Tayshia Adams picked Brendan Morais to go on the season’s first one-on-one date. They continued to date throughout most of the remainder of the season. Brendan made it to the final four and to the hometown visits. He also made it to the point in the competition that the couple met with jeweler Neil Lane to select an engagement ring. This ritual spooked Brendan so much that he immediately broke things off with Tayshia and went home before their fantasy suite date. Having been married and divorced once before, Brendan wasn’t ready to take another risk.

4 Serena Pitt The Bachelor Season 25

25-year-old fashion and beauty publicist Serena Pitt made it to the final four during The Bachelor season 25. She had taken Matt James to her hometown in Canada and he even met her mother. Things seemed to be going well for Serena when she told Matt that he wasn’t the guy for her. This turned out to be a premonition because Serena met Joe Amabile on the spin-off Bachelor In Paradise season 7. Joe and Serena got engaged during the finale and managed to maintain a long-distance relationship until they were officially married in 2022.

3 Clay Harbor The Bachelorette Season 14

During The Bachelorette season 14, NFL player Clay Harbor was injured during a group date and had to go to the hospital. Ironically, he was injured during a game of flag football. Becca Kufrin was so impressed by him that she gave him the group date rose. However, at the following rose ceremony, Clay decided to quit the show because he had found out he needed to get surgery on his injured wrist. With his career on the line, he had surgery and was able to recover in a few months thanks to working hard in physical therapy.

2 Ali Fedotowsky The Bachelor Season 14

During The Bachelor season 14, Ali Fedotowsky chose her career over Jake Pavelka. She had made it to the final four and had even shown Jake around her hometown. She broke things off because she learned that she’d lose her job if she didn’t immediately return to work. While driving away from Jake, she wondered aloud if she’d done the right thing. Ironically, Ali would become The Bachelorette and, during her season, Frank Neuschaefer quit because he still had feelings for his ex.

1 Joan Vassos The Golden Bachelor

Joan Vassos of The Golden Bachelor proved that, for her, family came first. Things were going great for the 60-year-old private school administrator. She had surprisingly won the group date talent show, which won her some one-on-one time with Gerry Turner. He liked her so much, that he gave her a rose then and there, securing her spot until the next episode. Sadly, Joan got a call from home and learned that her daughter, who had just given birth, needed her. Though Joan didn’t want to leave, she put her daughter first and left The Bachelor Nation show. The Bachelor season 28 airs on Monday, January 22, 2024, at 8/7c on ABC.
