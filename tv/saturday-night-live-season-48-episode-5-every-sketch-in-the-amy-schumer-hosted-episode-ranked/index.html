SUMMARY

“WKTVN News” sketch relies too heavily on hillbilly stereotypes, falling short with an awkward conclusion. Literature Review

With Amy Schumer acting as host, Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5 is one of the highlights of that year, and here’s every sketch, ranked.

Saturday Night Live, a cornerstone of American late-night television, has delivered a unique blend of comedy, satire, and performance for almost 50 years, and Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5 is a perfect example. Over the years, SNL has not only been a launchpad for numerous comedians and actors, but it has also provided incisive and often hilarious commentary on politics, society, and pop culture. The show’s format typically includes a guest host and a musical guest, with the host participating in various sketches throughout the evening. Amy Schumer, the host of season 48, episode 5, is no stranger to sketch comedy. Rising to prominence through stand-up, her career skyrocketed with the success of her sketch comedy series Inside Amy Schumer, which aired on Comedy Central and won a Peabody Award in 2015. Schumer’s comedy often explores themes of identity, feminism, and social norms, pushing boundaries and provoking thought. Her 2023 appearance on Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5 continued her willingness to tackle tough topics with a mix of humor and candor, making her one of the more memorable hosts of season 48.

12 WKTVN News The Sketch Relies Too Heavily On Hillbilly Stereotypes

In Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5’s “WKTVN News,” Chance Armstrong, portrayed by Michael Longfellow, reports on a local fire and gets caught in a bizarre hillbilly family feud. The sketch, featuring a confused reporter amidst a chaotic scene with characters played by Schumer, Cecily Strong, Molly Kearney, and Bowen Yang, tries to draw humor from absurdity. However, the sketch’s reliance on over-the-top antics and a disjointed storyline makes it fall short. The sketch relies too heavily on the one joke that all hillbillies are cousins, and the attempt to conclude with a group kiss feels more awkward than funny.

11 Cold Open James Austin Johnson Plays Biden In The Bland Cold Open

The cold open features James Austin Johnson as President Joe Biden, delivering a satirical speech that pokes fun at the dullness of Democrats and proposes outlandish replacements like Marianne Williamson (Chloe Fineman) and Guy Fieri (Kearney). While Cecily Strong’s appearance as Stormy Daniels adds a familiar face, the sketch relies heavily on impersonations without a solid premise or narrative arc. It cycles through impressions, ending weakly, and reflects a certain laziness in its construction, lacking the sharp satirical edge SNL is known for.

10 Twitter Moderation Council The Twitter Sketch Is A Missed Opportunity

Drawing on current events, Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5’s “Twitter Moderation Council” sketch dives into a post-Musk Twitter where the content moderation council, played by Fineman and Thompson, are the last employees standing after Musk has fired everyone at the company. Amy Schumer as a “sexy bot” and Punkie Johnson representing Black Twitter add some humor, but the sketch ultimately feels like a missed opportunity. It touches on the controversial Elon Musk takeover and the chaos of firings but fails to deliver incisive satire, instead presenting a series of disjointed impressions that ends abruptly.

9 Jets Fans “Suck My Sleeve” Failed To Become A Catchphrase

“Jets Fans” explores the world of passionate sports fandom with Schumer, Strong, Kenan Thompson, and Andrew Dismukes in Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5. The sketch intersperses casual conversation with sudden, outrageous taunts towards Bills fans. While the concept of sports fans’ extreme behaviors has potential, the execution here feels a little uneven. The phrase “suck my sleeve” as an insult is particularly baffling, leaving audiences unsure if it’s an established saying or a failed attempt at creating a new catchphrase.

8 COVID Commercial The SNL Sketch Hilariously Depicts The Positives Of Catching COVID

The “COVID Commercial” sketch humorously addresses the changing perceptions around COVID-19, suggesting that a positive test could be a convenient excuse for avoiding obligations. The idea of ordering an always-positive COVID test, which already has two red lines, as a means to escape life’s responsibilities is a clever twist. While it may tread a fine line given the ongoing impact of the pandemic, the sketch manages to find humor in the absurdity of using illness as an excuse, reflecting a shift in societal attitudes towards COVID-19 in 2023.

7 The Three Jurors The Courtroom Sketch Feels Like Classic SNL

Featuring Schumer, Yang, and Sarah Sherman as overly enthusiastic jurors, the sketch pokes fun at disruptive courtroom behavior. Their exaggerated reactions and misunderstanding of a Taylor Swift song add a quirky twist. While, the sketch may feel too over-the-top for some, lacking subtlety in its humor, the idea of a juror using a murder weapon to cut her chicken is classic Saturday Night Live. The portrayal of these jurors as modern archetypes recalls classic SNL characters, but with a contemporary twist.

6 Pinx Period Underwear The SNL Sketch Is Reminiscent Of The Best Inside Amy Schumer Sketches

The “Pinx Period Underwear” sketch in Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5 cleverly addresses the modern woman’s desire for more eco-friendly and empowering menstrual products through a fake underwear commercial. This fake commercial parody, with its sharp writing and relatable humor, could easily fit into Schumer’s own comedy show. The sketch successfully balances humor with commentary on women’s health issues, showcasing SNL’s ability to create impactful satire through everyday scenarios.

5 Big Penis Therapy Schumer And Andrew Dismukes’ Are A Hilarious On-Screen Couple

The “Big Penis Therapy” sketch stands out for its comedic exploration of male ego and vulnerability. The sketch cleverly addresses the often stigmatized and sensitive topic of men’s mental health under the guise of a therapy group for men with an unusual condition. It humorously portrays how societal expectations of masculinity can lead to absurd lengths to avoid the stigma associated with seeking help. Andrew Dismukes and Amy Schumer lead this sketch with a balance of humor and insight, subtly satirizing the reluctance many men feel towards therapy and emotional openness.

4 Weekend Update Tammy The Trucker Is One Of Cecily Strong’s Funniest Characters

Cecily Strong’s appearance as Tammy the Trucker in SNL’s “Weekend Update” stands out for its blend of humor and poignant commentary. Strong’s performance brings depth and relevance, touching on issues like healthcare and women’s rights to abortion from the perspective of a stereotypical trucker with a mix of seriousness and levity. The segment also includes clever Donald Trump Jr. jokes and tackles the sensitive topic of antisemitism in football with a delicate balance of humor and awareness. The update ends on a somber note, acknowledging the tragic murder of Takeoff, and offering a moment of respectful remembrance amidst the comedy.

3 Matzo Ball Soup The Soup Sketch Is Absurd But Totally Relatable

The “Soup” sketch in Saturday Night Live season 48, episode 5 clearly tackles the awkwardness of social etiquette, specifically the dilemma of eating while a friend shares emotional troubles. It takes this relatable, everyday scenario and elevates it with a series of increasingly humorous and absurd situations. The brilliance of the sketch lies in its ability to escalate the central dilemma of a friend describing her breakup when all Schumer’s character wants to do is eat her matzo ball soup. This sketch is a testament to SNL’s creative strength in turning simple, mundane moments into memorable and hilarious scenes.

2 The Looker A Pitch-Perfect Parody Of Netflix’s The Watcher

A parody of Netflix’s The Watcher, the sketch sees Schumer delivering an impressive display of physical comedy in the made-up series The Looker. The sketch humorously delves into the private embarrassments of a family, as a husband reads a letter from someone who has been stalking the family. Schumer’s character’s peculiar habits, such as eating a second dinner like a raccoon and being aroused by Property Brothers, are humorously exposed. While the sketch effectively parodies the thriller genre, it’s Schumer’s comedic performance that truly stands out, adding a layer of hilarity to the already amusing premise.

1 Big Dumb Hat The Closing SNL Sketch Is The Episode’s Best

“Big Dumb Hat” stands out as a sharp critique of shallow social media influencers and people whose whole personality is Fall. Schumer’s portrayal of a woman more focused on her online image than developing a real personality is both humorous and incisive. The Saturday Night Live sketch is packed with memorable quotes and hits the mark with its biting satire, reflecting the quality of comedy seen in Schumer’s own sketch series.
