7 TV Shows Canceled Before They Could Conclude Their Original Plan

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Brilliant shows like 1899 and I Am Not Okay With This were canceled after only one season, cutting short their planned storylines. Westworld was planned for five seasons but ended after four, leaving the story in limbo. Shows like Dollhouse and Daredevil had multi-season plans that were scrapped.

Literature Review

Some TV shows are canceled ahead of their time, but this can be made even worse when the creatives have a solid plan for the future of the series.

TV show cancelations can be devastating when a brilliant show with lots of potential is cut too soon, but it’s even worse when the creatives have a plan that never comes to fruition. With so many streaming platforms, networks, and studios constantly bringing out new shows, the studios have to be brutal when it comes to choosing what shows remain, and which get canceled. Just because a show gets good reviews, or has a decent following, sometimes the budget simply can’t be justified, or the creative team and the studios disagree on the long-term vision. While some shows are pitched to networks and studios with a central idea and thorough plans for a first season, other creators have a more fully fleshed-out vision for their projects. Programs like 1899, and I Am Not Okay With This had a plan for where the series would go, but for whatever reason, they were canceled after just one season. Other shows had the chance to enjoy multiple seasons, but the creatives had a larger plan that was scrapped too soon.

7 1899 1 Season Out Of 3 Planned

1899 gained a great deal of attention on Netflix when it was initially released in 2022. However, the series was canceled in early 2023, just months after the show’s first season was released to stream. The show’s creators, Jantje Friese, and Baran bo Odar, also created another successful show for Netflix, Dark, before the creation of 1899. Like Dark, 1899 was intended to run for three seasons to tell the full story as the creators envisioned it (via Collider), but unfortunately, the show didn’t survive to even get a second season.

6 Westworld 4 Seasons Out Of 5 Planned

Westworld is an enduring science fiction Western franchise that stems from the novel of the same name by Jurassic Park sci-fi author, Michael Crichton. Earlier shows and films were created to adapt the story after 1973, but the latest iteration began airing in 2016. The series managed to run for four seasons before being shut down by HBO, but the creators had made statements about their plan for the series to have five seasons in total (via Deadline). The ending for season 4 appeared conclusive, but had the creators gotten their way, it would have continued for one final outing.

5 Dollhouse 2 Seasons Out Of A 5-Year Plan

Buffy the Vampire Slayer creator and Avengers director Joss Whedon also had multiple shows launched and then canceled before their vision was fully realized. For one, Dollhouse was a series starring Eliza Dushku, which followed an elaborate premise about people who would opt into being reprogrammed and wiped clean to earn money working as “Dolls.” The programming would then allow the person to be assigned new identities, talents, skills, and personas to suit whatever needs a client may have. Whedon clarified in an interview with Alan Sepinwall that he pitched the network a five-year plan for the show with a clear and distinct vision for what would happen in several seasons. The lifecycle of the “Dolls” in the series is five years, as that is how much time they opt into for these organizations, before they retire and receive a large payment. Had the series continued, it would have been interesting to see what happens at the end of that cycle and if the “Dolls” did break free from their new life.

4 Daredevil 3 Seasons Out Of 4 Planned

The Netflix Marvel shows experienced varying degrees of success, with shows like Iron Fist and The Defenders flopping, but standing out among its peers, Daredevil received universal praise. The casting of Charlie Cox as the titular hero, aka Matt Murdock, was inspired, and this was further enhanced by his playing opposite the outstanding Vincent D’Onofrio as Kingpin. While Daredevil was certainly the most popular of the Marvel Netflix shows, when Disney and Marvel’s plans changed, and Disney+ was set up, the entire slate of shows on Netflix was canceled. This came as a massive blow to fans of the series as Daredevil season 3’s ending had set up some clear threads to be explored in the following seasons, such as Benjamin Poindexter undergoing an experimental treatment to recover after breaking his back, Murdock reconciling with Foggy Nelson and Karen Page, and Kingpin imprisoned. There is a future for most of these characters with many returning for the upcoming Disney+ series, Daredevil: Born Again, but the series is set to move in a new direction from what Netflix’s Daredevil had set up.

3 I Am Not Okay With This 1 Season Out Of 2 Planned

I Am Not Okay With This was initially planned to run for two seasons on Netflix (via Business Insider), and the coming-of-age dark comedy show almost made it before being canceled ahead of season 2. The show had been renewed for a second season, and with the talented cast winning over audiences and the tantalizing mystery about how Sydney Novak (Sophia Lillis) got her powers, the series appeared to be in good shape. However, the series was canceled despite this due to COVID-related reasons, according to Deadline.

2 Hannibal 3 Seasons Out Of 7 Planned

Hannibal, starring Mads Mikkelsen as the titular serial killer and cannibal, was a popular show during its release on HBO. However, after three seasons, the show was canceled. Series creator, Bryan Fuller, had revealed as early as just before season 1 ended, that there was an extensive plan laid out for Hannibal to continue for seven seasons. While the original three seasons followed original stories about the origins of Hannibal and his relationship with Will Graham, later seasons would have drawn on more inspiration from the Thomas Harris novels that originated the characters.

1 Threshold 1 Season Out Of 3 Planned

Threshold was initially created for CBS, and while the show first received modest ratings, the time slot was moved in an effort to boost views but delivered the opposite effect. Ultimately, it was canceled four episodes before the season 1 finale in the US, while British TV channel Sky1 aired the first season in its entirety. The science fiction series featured an impressive cast, including Carla Gugino, Brent Spiner, Peter Dinklage, and Rob Benedict. The series outlined the first contact with an alien race as coordinated by a secret government agency, and it was promising with a solid execution. In addition, the series had a plan for three seasons, with each season altering the name and exploring a new aspect of an alien invasion. The second season, set to be subtitled Foothold, would have seen the aliens establishing a presence on Earth; before the final season, Stranglehold would have seen the invasion progress with a mass alien presence. However, the show was canceled, and the plan went to waste.
