10 Tragic Sitcom Deaths That Viewers Didn t See Coming

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Sitcom character deaths can be surprisingly moving and unexpected, adding a dramatic element to an otherwise light-hearted show. Even sitcoms that primarily focus on comedy can tackle difficult topics like infertility, heartbreak, and death without straying from their established tone. Unexpected deaths in sitcoms provide an opportunity for the shows to explore themes of loss and grief, resulting in some of the saddest moments in television.

Literature Review

From New Girl to Scrubs to The Big Bang Theory, even the most light-hearted sitcoms can blindside viewers with a devastating character death.

It is unusual for sitcoms to feature character deaths, but it is even more atypical for these deaths to be truly moving and unexpected. Sitcoms are generally viewed as comfort watching, even if they can be as dark as It’s Always Sunny In Philadelphia or South Park. Broadly speaking, even the most satirically sharp sitcoms aren’t intended to be poignant on a regular basis as this is the realm of dramedies. If a sitcom regularly leans into tragic plots, the show becomes a dramedy. Hits like Bojack Horseman and The Good Place made this tonal transition as they grew more serious. However, even bonafide sitcoms can have surprisingly tragic moments. Some of the best sitcoms of all time, from How I Met Your Mother to MASH, featured some truly moving storylines that tugged on the audience’s heartstrings. Vitally, these shows remained sitcoms in terms of their tone, style, and formula even as they took on difficult topics like infertility, heartbreak, and death. Among these themes, it is unexpected deaths that most often offer sitcoms a chance to flex their dramatic muscles, as evidenced by some sitcom deaths that proved as sad as the deaths of any TV drama characters.

10 Walt, New Girl Dennis Farina’s charming grifter Walt died unexpectedly

In season 2, episode 13, “A Father’s Love,” New Girl introduced Nick’s father Walt, a charismatic con man. Nick struggled to trust Walt when he was reunited with him, but the pair eventually formed a bittersweet bond. This made it all the more devastating when Nick’s father Walt died suddenly offscreen only a few episodes later in episode 20, “Chicago.” The proceeding storyline was unusually affecting as Nick came to terms with his father’s complicated legacy. Not everything about New Girl aged well, but this plot stands out as a classic. New Girl

9 Sean, Derry Girls Clare’s father died in the cult hit’s penultimate episode

The ribald coming-of-age sitcom Derry Girls mainly centered on Erin Quinn, but her uptight friend Clare was also a major part of the series. Derry Girls hinted at the possibility that Nicola Coughlan’s Clare was LGBTQ+ before this storyline culminated in her coming out during season 1. However, it wasn’t until season 3 of the cult hit that she gained a love interest and, in a brutally tragic twist, Clare lost her father to an aneurysm the same night she had her first kiss. 8 Marvin, How I Met Your Mother Marshall lost his dad at the worst time possible

While Clare lost her father at a truly agonizing time, How I Met Your Mother’s Marshall was arguably even more unlucky. The likable sitcom hero spent a lengthy stretch struggling with fertility issues before finally revealing this to his parents in season 6, episode 13, “Bad News.” However, Marshall’s father Marvin died suddenly shortly after their last conversation, a brutal twist that made his journey to fatherhood immeasurably harder and provided How I Met Your Mother’s saddest scene ever. 7 Dede Pritchett, Modern Family Modern Family’s Dede death was a rare serious storyline

In Modern Family season 10, episode 5, “Good Grief,” Claire’s mother Dede died offscreen near Halloween. The unexpected timing of this event made the moment all the sadder since Modern Family’s annual Halloween episodes were typically among the show’s zaniest and most high-energy outings. Instead, the manic pacing of the family sitcom slowed down as the Pritchett children reminisced about their late mother. This atypically wistful tone made “Good Grief” a standout episode in season 10. Modern Family

6 Deborah Shoshlefski (The Chair Model), The Office The Office’s darkest episode left Dwight and Michael mourning

It is not really clear whether The Office season 4, episode 14, “Chair Model,” was intended to be sad. However, the outing succeeded admirably as a tragicomic story, with Michael falling in love with a catalog model only to discover that she died years before he even found her photo. While the entire plot sounds silly, it was still a grim shock to discover that the unseen Deborah Shoshlefski died so young when an unfeeling Dwight uncovered her eulogy while searching for her. 5 Professor Proton, The Big Bang Theory Even Sheldon was moved by the death of his childhood icon

Although Young Sheldon made The Big Bang Theory sadder in retrospect by justifying some of Sheldon’s character quirks with moving backstories, the original series wasn’t particularly invested in poignancy. When there were moving moments in The Big Bang Theory, they were typically reserved for the main romantic couples the show centered on. However, an exception was made in season 7, episode 23, “The Proton Transmogrification.” Even the typically cold Sheldon was heartbroken upon learning that his childhood icon Professor Proton died, making this one of the show’s saddest scenes. 4 Mona, The Simpsons Homer’s mother had a crushing exit from The Simpsons

Homer had a fraught relationship with his absent mother Mona throughout The Simpsons. Her first appearance provided the show with one of its most enduringly iconic images as Homer sat on his car’s bonnet and stared at the stars after she left him again. However, it was Mona’s death that cemented her legacy in the show. Although The Simpsons almost never kills off characters permanently, the long-running show made an exception when Mona died while staying with Homer in season 19, episode 19, “Mona Leaves-a.” The Simpsons

3 Paul, 8 Simple Rules John Ritter’s real-life passing resulted in Paul’s sudden death

Paul Hennessy was the lead character of 8 Simple Rules in its early seasons, and John Ritter’s long-suffering suburban dad was a well-liked sitcom hero. When Ritter died suddenly at the age of 54, the actor was eulogized in the series with his character’s unexpected death. Paul’s 8 Simple Rules death was uniquely moving because of both how unexpected the development was and how closely tied the storyline was to Ritter’s untimely real-life passing.

2 Ben, Scrubs Brendan Fraser’s recurring role set up the saddest Scrubs episode

Brendan Fraser first appeared as Jordan’s garrulous brother Ben in the first season of Scrubs and the fan-favorite guest star didn’t return for another two seasons. When he did, Ben only interacted with Dr. Cox who was dismayed to discover that his former brother-in-law never followed up on his leukemia diagnosis. Ben’s final appearance ended with the revolution that he died before the episode began and Dr. Cox had been imagining his interactions with him in a twist ending that was arguably the saddest moment in Scrubs history. 1 Roseanne, The Conners Roseanne’s title character died offscreen before this spinoff began

While the existence of The Conners season 6 proves that the spinoff could continue the story of Roseanne without the show’s former title character, this didn’t make her death any less moving. When Roseanne Barr tweeted racist comments in 2018, her character was killed off and Roseanne’s revival was retooled into The Conners. This resulted in a tragic death as Roseanne overdosed on opioids offscreen, an event that cast a long shadow over the first season of this bittersweet sitcom. The Conners
