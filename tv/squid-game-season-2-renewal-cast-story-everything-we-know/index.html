SUMMARY

Squid Game season 2 is confirmed, with new deadly games and more focus on the Front Man and police involvement. Filming for season 2 started in July 2023, and Squid Game season 2 will premiere some time in 2024. The cast for season 2 includes both returning characters and new additions, with the addition of female competitors in the new installment.

Literature Review

Squid Game, the Korean Netflix phenomenon that took the world by storm in 2021, is heading back for season 2, and here’s everything we know.

After becoming Netflix’s most-watched original show ever, Squid Game season 2 promises even more twisted competitions and deadly games. Squid Game season 1 focused on a group of everyday people invited to take part in the titular event, a secretive competition that carries grave consequences for its losers. Those selected all carry heavy financial burdens to bear, the type of debt that can make life one big ball of stress. Despite the likelihood of death, if winning the game can make their lives less of a living hell, the contestants are on board.

Squid Game burst onto the scene and was instantly beloved by both critics and viewers, then rode massive amounts of positive word of mouth to Netflix viewership records. Following its success, Netflix is looking to make Squid Game into a broader universe, with multiple spin-offs in development. It’s no surprise that Squid Game season 2 is coming, it’s only a matter of how long viewers will have to wait.

Stream On Netflix

The Latest Squid Game Season 2 News The Squid Game Season 2 Release Window Is Revealed

As production for the second season continues, the latest news finds a release window finally revealed for Squid Game season 2. Though a more concrete date has yet to be revealed, it has now been announced that Squid Game will return in 2024, joining a long list of exciting Netflix originals that will air new seasons in the year. A more concrete release date is expected to come sooner rather than later.

Other returning Netflix originals include Bridgerton season 3, debuting May 16th, 2024, and The Diplomat season 2 which will return some time in 2024 as well.

Squid Game Season 2 Is Confirmed Netflix Has Renewed Squid Game

Hwang Dong-hyuk has confirmed Squid Game season 2 is happening. Much of the appeal in season 1 came from the challenges the contestants faced, and Dong-hyuk promises more deadly games in Squid Game season 2. Whether Squid Game season 2 will feature entirely new games or a mix of old and new remains to be seen.

Squid Game Season 2 Production Status When Will Season 2 Be Released?

Though an official release date has yet to be revealed, it is now known that Squid Game season 2 will premiere some time in 2024. Based on the previously announced production timeline, the popular Netflix hit will likely arrive toward the end of the year, and not any time sooner. It was revealed that the new season began filming in July 2023 and was slated to shoot for 10 months. With that in mind, the earliest season 2 could wrap would be May 2024 and a few months of post-production will be needed for the effects-heavy series.

Squid Game Season 2 Cast Who Will Be In The Next Season Of Squid Game?

The Squid Game season 2 cast has already been partially confirmed, including one character whose fate was left uncertain in the first Squid Game season. A casting video debuted at the Netflix Tudum event and shows the surviving cast members from season 1 will be back for season 2. This includes Lee Jung-Jae as Seong Gi-hun, Lee Byeong-Heon as Hwang In-ho, Wi Ha-Joon as Hwang Jun-ho, and Gong Yoo as the person who recruits participants for the game.

There are also 12 new cast members joining the Squid Game season 2 cast, though nothing is known about the character yet. Those new cast members are:

Kang Ha-Neul Yim Si-Wan Park Sung-Hoon Yang Dong-Guen Park Gyu-Young Jo Yu-Ri Kang Ae-Sim Lee David Lee Jin-Uk Choi Seung-Hyun Roh Jae-Won Won Ji-An

Squid Game Season 2 Story What Is The Next Season Of Squid Game About?

Squid Game season 1’s ending definitely left the door open for a sequel, although many wondered if the setup would be vastly different and not focus as much on the actual game. Sole surviving protagonist Gi-hun seemingly opts to try and take down the organization behind Squid Game rather than move on with his daughter, and it’s unlikely he would willingly participate in the game again, even if baited by Squid Game’s resident recruiter.

However, there’s always a way for dystopian organizations like this to force compliance, so perhaps Gi-hun’s daughter will be used to bait him back into the game. Squid Game’s complex in-universe creator Il-nam is also apparently dead, which may lead to an even less fair game than before. Since the Squid Game season 2 plot will also see the return of Jun-Ho to the cast it could be that he survived his brother’s bullet and that the Front Man’s intention was never to kill his brother, but rather, to save him.
