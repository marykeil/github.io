Jake Gyllenhaals 2024 Just Got Even Bigger With A TV Role That Breaks A 30 Year Career Streak

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Jake Gyllenhaal is set to star in a TV series called “Presumed Innocent,” marking his first major TV role in his 30-year career. “Presumed Innocent” is a legal thriller miniseries based on the 1987 novel and centers around Gyllenhaal’s character, a prosecutor who becomes the prime suspect in a murder case. Gyllenhaal will also star in the remake of the movie “Road House” and both projects are slated for a 2024 release.

Literature Review

Jake Gyllenhaal has two projects set to be released in 2024, among them a TV show with which he’s breaking a curious 30-year career streak.

Jake Gyllenhaal has a couple of projects set to be released in 2024, including a TV series with which he’s breaking a 30-year career streak. Jake Gyllenhaal’s acting career began in 1991 in the western comedy film City Slickers, after which he appeared in some of his father’s, Stephen Gyllenhaal’s, projects. Gyllenhaal got his first lead role in a movie in 1999 in the biographical drama October Sky, but his big break arrived two years later when he starred in the sci-fi psychological film Donnie Darko.

Since then, Gyllenhaal has been part of a variety of projects, most notably Brokeback Mountain (for which he was nominated for Best Supporting Actor at the Academy Awards), Jarhead, Zodiac, Nightcrawler, Nocturnal Animals, and Spider-Man: Far From Home. Gyllenhaal has one big project scheduled for a 2024 release with Road House, but he also has a TV series coming up this year on Apple TV+, with which he’s finally breaking a 30-year career streak, as he has been staying away from TV for decades.

Presumed Innocent Is Jake Gyllenhaal’s First Major TV Show Jake Gyllenhaal is leading one of Apple TV+’s upcoming TV shows.

Just a couple of days after Netflix released its trailer of 2024 film and TV releases, Apple TV+ released its trailer of 2024 releases. Despite not having an extensive catalog of movies and TV shows, Apple TV+ has earned its place among other big streaming platforms thanks to the high quality of most of its titles, such as For All Mankind, Masters of the Air, Silo, and Killers of the Flower Moon. Apple TV+ has a list of exciting films and TV shows set for a 2024 release, among them Presumed Innocent, starring Jake Gyllenhaal.

Presumed Innocent is a legal thriller miniseries created by David E. Kelley (Ally McBeal, Big Little Lies, The Lincoln Lawyer) and based on the 1987 novel of the same name by Scott Turow. Presumed Innocent is the story of Rusty Sabich (Gyllenhaal), a prosecutor who ends up becoming the prime suspect in the murder investigation he was involved in – and it was the murder of one of his colleagues. Also starring in Presumed Innocent are Ruth Negga, Bill Camp, Peter Sarsgaard, Lily Rabe, and O-T Fagbenle.

Gyllenhaal is no stranger to TV shows, but Presumed Innocent is the first major TV show in his career. Gyllenhaal’s first TV role was Matthew “Matt” Ellison in one episode of the police drama TV series Homicide: Life on the Street, in 1994. After that, Gyllenhaal appeared as himself in Saturday Night Live (twice as a host, once as a special appearance), Man vs. Wild, Inside Amy Schumer, and he played Mr. Music in the Netflix children’s special John Mulaney & the Sack Lunch Bunch. Rusty Sabich in Presumed Innocent, then, is Gyllenhaal’s first starring role in a TV show.

Presumed Innocent Will Debut After Jake Gyllenhaal’s Road House Remake Jake Gyllenhaal is starring in the Road House remake.

Road House (2024) R Release Date March 21, 2024 Director Doug Liman Cast Jake Gyllenhaal , Daniela Melchior , Billy Magnussen , Jessica Williams , Joaquim De Almeida , Conor McGregor , Lukas Gage , Arturo Castro , B.K. Cannon , Beau Knapp , Darren Barnet , Dominique Columbus , Bob Menery

Presumed Innocent isn’t Jake Gyllenhaal’s only big project to be released in 2024. As mentioned above, Gyllenhaal is starring in Road House, a remake of the 1989 movie of the same name. Directed by Doug Liman, Road House follows James Dalton (Gyllenhaal), a former UFC middleweight fighter who takes a job as a bouncer at a Florida Keys roadhouse. Dalton’s skills are just what the place needs, as it has been attracting the wrong people, and Dalton soon discovers this is no paradise and dangerous people are involved in it, making it a violent and dark place.

Road House will skip a theatrical release and, instead, it will be released on Amazon Prime Video on March 21, 2024. Presumed Innocent will arrive at Apple TV+ on June 14, 2024, giving enough time between Gyllenhaal’s projects but not so much as for Presumed Innocent to miss the wave that Road House will inevitably bring with Gyllenhaal’s performance. With one film project and a TV show coming up in 2024, this year is shaping up to be a big one in Gyllenhaal’s career.

Why It Took Jake Gyllenhaal Over 30 Years To Star As A Lead In A TV Show Jake Gyllenhaal has focused on movies.

Actors usually split their time between movies and TV shows, and it’s not uncommon for them to do more of one than the other – however, it’s curious that Jake Gyllenhaal has mostly stayed away from TV shows, especially those where he doesn’t have to play or appear as himself. Gyllenhaal hasn’t spoken about having a predilection for movies over TV shows or any conscious decision to focus on movies and leave TV projects aside, so it might just be a matter of how things have turned out in his career over the years.

It’s to be seen if Presumed Innocent will lead to more TV roles for Jake Gyllenhaal (especially if it’s a hit with critics and general audiences) or if it will just be a brief pause from his work on the big screen.
