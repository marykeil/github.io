The 10 Best Comedy Central Roasts Ranked

 Ella

Contents

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Comedy Central Roasts are known for their raunchy and hilarious jokes at the expense of beloved and controversial celebrities. While the future of Comedy Central Roasts is uncertain, there are still old episodes full of offensive but satisfyingly funny jokes to enjoy. The subjects of the roasts sometimes don’t enjoy it as much as the audience, but the roasters always bring their A-game to roast them.

Literature Review

Comedy Central has hosted 17 Roasts of beloved and controversial celebrities, and the 10 best are endlessly rewatchable.

Comedy Central Roasts seem to have gone on hiatus, with the last special being Alec Baldwin’s roast in 2019, but the best Comedy Central Roasts still hold up and are endlessly rewatchable. The roasts are known for putting beloved and sometimes controversial celebrities in the spotlight and letting friends, family, comedians, and other entertainers take shots with their best jokes at the subject’s expense. Everyone from movie stars to former presidents has sat on stage and let professional comedians mock them. Sometimes the subjects don’t enjoy it quite as much as the audience. Unfortunately, it doesn’t seem like Comedy Central Roasts will return any time soon much like the network’s sketch shows. The series continued internationally on Comedy Central Africa and Comedy Central Netherlands, but the subjects are almost unknown and the specials relatively tame. However, while Comedy Central Roast remains on indefinite hiatus, there are still several old episodes full of tasteless but hilarious jokes that remain satisfyingly offensive.

The 10 Funniest Characters From Comedy Central Shows, Ranked Ever since South Park, Comedy Central has been home to some of the most hilarious characters on TV, as seen on shows like Broad City and Key & Peele.

10 Pamela Anderson (2005) Roast Master: Jimmy Kimmel

The Roast of Pamela Anderson was expectedly raunchy, hilarious, and garnered plenty of attention given the performers who roasted her. The 2005 special features Courtney Love, Hugh Hefner, and Anderson’s ex-husband, Tommy Lee. Anderson’s history with Tommy Lee, which was depicted in the sometimes inaccurate Pam & Tommy, was unsurprisingly the topic that most of the roasters gravitated toward. While Anderson saw the humor in most of the roasts, the special was marred by controversy due to the highly inappropriate actions of Andy Dick, which were uncomfortable to watch.

9 Rob Lowe (2016) Roast Master: David Spade

The Roast of Rob Lowe featured tons of jokes made at the actor’s expense, and, for the most part, Lowe was a great sport. However, the event was marred thanks to the appearance of Ann Coulter, who was in attendance. Lowe received a verbal beating from the comedians on stage, but it seemed the spotlight was on controversial media pundit Ann Coulter, who was also on stage and tried to roast the actor. Even Lowe threw some blunt jokes at her alongside British comedian Jimmy Carr, who used his pitch-black sense of humor.

8 Donald Trump (2011) Roast Master: Seth MacFarlane

Donald Trump had his very own roast in 2011, which featured Larry King and Snoop Dogg. There were enough jokes that even had Donald’s wife, Melania, hysterically laughing at her husband’s expense. The comedians had Trump’s reality TV series, The Apprentice, and his chain of hotels in their crosshairs. However, if any celebrity who has had the honor of being celebrated with a Comedy Central Roast is ripe for a sequel, it’s Trump. Regardless of where the comedians stand politically, they’d have a laundry list of material following his presidency.

7 Joan Rivers (2009) Roast Master: Kathy Griffin

If anybody can take a joke at their own expense, it’s the late Joan Rivers. She was one of the very few subjects of a Comedy Central Roast who arguably roasted the other comedians and entertainers more than they roasted her. The comedian has never bitten her tongue and has always spoken her true, unfiltered thoughts, and that was no different in the roast. Rivers downed two glasses of wine before tossing the glasses behind her. She then smacked Kathy Griffin across the face, which was followed by a mic-drop speech full of fire jokes.

6 David Hasselhoff (2010) Roast Master: Seth MacFarlane

David Hasselhoff was put in the roast chair and received an exorbitant amount of insults. But one comedian made his presence known by his loud and unique voice. The iconic and memorable comedian Gilbert Gottfried had enough ammunition for everyone. Though other comedians threw everything they had at Hasselhoff, Gottfried’s joke had even Hasselhoff himself nodding and chuckling: “David Hasselhoff is running on the beach in slow motion. What an ***hole! I’ll never forget that sight because I haven’t been able to open my eyes since.”

5 Justin Bieber (2015) Roast Master: Kevin Hart

Justin Bieber was an easy target for any comedian in the 2010s, so he came under heavy fire during his Comedy Central Roast, which he requested himself (via Daily Mail). While the comedians brought their A-game, Bieber’s roast was harder to watch than the other specials. Where other subjects saw humor in the jokes, Bieber, who was still young at the time, looked genuinely hurt. This was clear when Hannibal Buress joked, “I hate your music more than Bill Cosby hates my comedy.” It’s safe to say the pop star got what he wanted and potentially more.

4 William Shatner (2006) Roast Master: Jason Alexander

Known for his most famous role as Star Trek’s Captain James T. Kirk, William Shatner is no stranger to having jokes hurled at him. While there were plenty of good comedians on stage, it was a strange experience when the late Betty White roasted him. Shatner’s friends and former colleagues might have taken the stage to roast the actor, but the insults dropped were just as off-color as usual. Some of the most off-color jokes came from the Golden Girl herself, along with Shatner’s ex-co-star George Takei. Many of Takei’s jokes had Shatner’s mouth hanging open.

3 Bob Saget (2008) Roast Master: John Stamos

The late Bob Saget had tons of great TV roles, most notably Full House. Many friends of Saget and fellow comedians had fun spitting vulgar jokes at Saget’s expense, many of which had him close to tears. The most memorable moment of the Comedy Central Roast was from Saget himself. The actor entered his roast by elevator with two younger women, an old woman fixing her hair, and a goat. As Saget went to take his seat, he stopped and pointed to the goat, and yelled, “Hey, call me!”

2 Charlie Sheen (2011) Roast Master: Seth MacFarlane

Charlie Sheen is no stranger to being roasted. The special was by far the most successful Comedy Central Roast with 6.4 million viewers (via TV by the Numbers). The Roast of Charlie Sheen couldn’t have come at a better time, as Sheen was more famous than ever, but for all the wrong reasons, and the roasts wrote themselves. Many of the jokes revolved around the subject of “winning,” and controversially poked fun at Sheen’s role on Two and a Half Men and his over-the-top personal life, which blended into one persona.

1 James Franco (2013) Roast Master: Seth Rogen

The panel of roasters of Franco’s Comedy Central Roast looked like one of his movies. Jonah Hill, Seth Rogen, Nick Kroll, and several other regular collaborators aired their grievances with vulgar jokes. With jokes calling out Franco for being “the pretty boy” in most of his roles and how his eyes look like they are always closed, some of the roasters played it safe. The gloves came off when they targeted Franco’s pretentiousness. The MVP was Bill Hader, a.k.a. the President of Hollywood, a character that rivals anything he did on Saturday Night Live.
