8 Ways Star Wars Has Been Setting Up Sabine s Jedi Arc For 47 Years

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The idea of anyone being able to access and use the Force has been established in Star Wars since the very beginning, contrary to the backlash against Sabine’s Jedi arc. George Lucas himself stated that anyone can learn to use the Force, and it is not exclusive to the Jedi. It is a matter of effort and training, not just midi-chlorian counts or inherent talent. Other characters like Chirrut Îmwe and Jyn Erso have shown that even without being Jedi, they can still connect with the Force and use it in their own ways. Luke Skywalker also emphasizes that anyone with dedication can become a powerful Force-user.

Literature Review

Sabine Wren’s Jedi arc in the Ahsoka TV show has been controversial, but Star Wars has actually been setting it up for the last 47 years.

Sabine Wren’s Ahsoka Jedi arc has been controversial, but Star Wars has actually been setting it up for all 47 years of its existence. At the end of Star Wars Rebels, Sabine Wren becomes Ahsoka Tano’s apprentice. However, at the beginning of Ahsoka, it was revealed that Ahsoka stopped training Sabine. After some tension in the first couple of episodes, Ahsoka decided to train Sabine once again. Throughout the show, Jedi architect droid Huyang establishes that Sabine has little aptitude for the Force. She fails over and over until she finally accesses the Force in the Ahsoka season 1 finale. Sabine’s Jedi arc received quite a bit of backlash. Audiences protested the idea of someone who was proven to not be Force-sensitive learning to use the Force, saying that it defied Star Wars canon. However, this is simply not the case. The Force was always meant to be accessible to anyone with enough practice, and it has been established in canon at least eight different ways.

How To Watch Star Wars Movies In Order
Here is how to watch all Star Wars movies and TV shows chronologically and in order of release, and how each fits into the Star Wars timeline.

8 Obi-Wan Kenobi’s Original Description Of The Force Sets Up Sabine’s Arc Star Wars (1977)

When explaining the Force to Luke in A New Hope, Obi-Wan describes the Force by saying, “It’s an energy field created by all living things. It surrounds us and penetrates us. It binds the galaxy together.” The idea of the Force surrounding and permeating all things suggests anyone can access it. After all, if it is a part of everyone, then theoretically everyone should be connected to it. There is no suggestion in the original trilogy that the Force is only accessible to an elite group. This, as it turns out, was by George Lucas’ design.

7 George Lucas Insisted Anyone Can Learn To Use The Force The Making of Return of the Jedi by J.W. Rinzler (1981)

In a transcribed 1981 interview, George Lucas explained how the Force works. In that interview, he stated that anyone can access the Force. In J.W. Rinzler’s The Making of Return of the Jedi, Lucas, screenwriter Lawrence Kasdan, and director Richard Marquand discuss Return of the Jedi. In the interview, Lucas explains the rules of the Force: Kasdan: The Force was available to anyone who could hook into it?Lucas: Yes, everybody can do it.Kasdan: Not just the Jedi?Lucas: It’s just the Jedi who take the time to do it.Marquand: They use it as a technique.Lucas: Like yoga. If you want to take the time to do it, you can do it; but the ones that really want to do it are the ones who are into that kind of thing. Also like karate. This proves that Lucas always intended for people with lower midi-chlorian counts to be able to become Jedi with enough effort and training. Learning to use the Force was never only a matter of midi-chlorians and inherent talent. It was always meant to come through hard work and seeking balance.

6 Qui-Gon Jinn’s Midi-Chlorian Revelations Star Wars: Episode I - The Phantom Menace (1999)

When explaining Midi-chlorians to Anakin Skywalker, Qui-Gon Jinn states the following: Midi-chlorians are a microscopic life-form that resides within all living cells…and we are symbionts with them… Without the midi-chlorians, life could not exist, and we would have no knowledge of the Force. They continually speak to us, telling us the will of the Force. When you learn to quiet your mind, you’ll hear them speaking to you. This once again reinforces George Lucas’ original idea of the Force presented in A New Hope. Midi-chlorians, and thus the Force, are the foundation for life in the Star Wars universe. This means that the Force is not only accessible to everybody, but it also sustains everyone’s being. Every single person in the Star Wars universe is deeply connected to the Force, whether they realize it or not. Contrary to popular belief, midi-chlorians do not contain the raw power of the Force themselves. Rather, they are a way of figuring out the strength of one’s connection to the Force, and thus gauging a particular person’s potential. However, taken in the context of Lucas’ earlier comments and the original trilogy, it’s clear that a low midi-chlorian count doesn’t make becoming a Force-user impossible. It simply means that some people have an easier time than others.

5 Kanan Confirmed Sabine Could Connect With A Kyber Crystal Star Wars Rebels (2016)

When training to use the Darksaber with Kanan Jarrus, Sabine states that the lightsaber is getting lighter. Kanan says, “You’re connecting with it; it’s becoming a part of you.” This shows not only that anyone can connect with a kyber crystal no matter the level of midi-chlorians they have in their cells, but also that Sabine specifically had the potential to become a Jedi all along.

Every Star Wars Character That Has Wielded The Darksaber
The Darksaber is a fabled Mandalorian weapon in Star Wars mythology, but the blade has been wielded by a wide variety of different characters.

4 Chirrut Îmwe (Arguably) Uses The Force Rogue One: A Star Wars Story (2016)

While not a Force-user in the traditional sense, Chirrut Îmwe was a Guardian of the Whills who worshiped the Force. As such, he was deeply knowledgeable about it. He reinforced the idea that it resides in all living beings and that anyone can connect with the Force on some level, even if not to the point of becoming a Jedi. Further, while he didn’t have the aptitude of a Jedi and therefore likely had a relatively low midi-chlorian count, there are hints that he was able to use the Force regardless. He is shown to have Jedi-like reflexes and seems to sense where enemies are despite being blind. He also seems to have Force-assisted insight into people. For example, he tells Cassian Andor, “I sense that you carry that prison wherever you go.” The idea of sensing something in Star Wars is almost always tied to the Force.

3 There Are Hints Jyn Erso Uses The Force Too Rogue One: A Star Wars Story (2016)

In Rogue One: A Star Wars Story, Jyn Erso keeps a kyber crystal necklace on her for good luck. When she and the Rogue One squadron are trying to sneak into Scarif, she clutches her kyber crystal necklace, willing that they will be let through. This is exactly what happens, and they are able to steal the Death Star schematics for Princess Leia. This implies that, despite her not being a Jedi, the Force helped her as she was able to connect with a kyber crystal.

2 Luke Skywalker Insists Anyone Can Use The Force The Rise of Kylo Ren #3 by Charles Soule (2020)

Marvel’s The Rise of Kylo Ren #3 comic features a panel where Luke Skywalker states that anyone can use the Force. He explains to Voe that, while some people have more of a natural aptitude for the Force, and thus an easier time learning to master it, anyone can become a powerful Force-user with enough hard work and determination. To use his metaphor, even those whose door is opened an inch can open it wide with practice and dedication.

1 Yoda Knew Being A Jedi Isn’t Just About Force Power What A Jedi Makes by Michael Kogge (2022)

The Jedi Grandmaster Yoda stated explicitly in Michael Kogge’s short story What A Jedi Makes that the Force isn’t what makes a Jedi. He states that it is a devotion to maintaining balance and serving the light side of the Force that truly makes a Jedi. In fact, on rare occasions, people who were not Force-sensitive were allowed into the Order. This is further proof that even the Jedi recognized that anyone could learn to connect with and use the Force with enough training and effort - and Sabine in Ahsoka is no exception.
