Succession Season 4 Episode 4 Recap & Ending Explained

2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Succession season 4, episode 4 reveals who Logan chose as the next CEO, changing the dynamics of the show. The power rankings at Waystar Royco shift in episode 4, with Kendall and Roman gaining significant power. The episode sets the stage for conflicts among the Roy siblings and the dangers Waystar Royco may face.

Literature Review

Succession season 4 episode 4 follows up on the massive episode 3 twist, answering the show’s biggest question and how Waystar will move forward.

Warning: Contains SPOILERS for Succession season 4, episode 4Succession season 4, episode 4 “Honeymoon States” affects the rest of the series going forward. Succession season 4 concludes the beloved HBO comedy-drama series, and by the midpoint of the season, things are starting to heat up in the Roy family. “Honeymoon States” has some massive reveals, with the episode finally revealing who Logan picked as Waystar Royco’s next CEO after years of waiting.

Succession season 4, episode 4, follows up on the previous episode, which featured the bombshell twist of Logan Roy’s death. Logan has been the main antagonist and CEO of Waystar Royco throughout Succession, so this changes everything. While season 4, episode 3 explored the immediate aftermath of Logan’s death, including the emotional reactions of his children, episode 4 expands beyond this, showing the next steps for Waystar Royco. The emotionally charged episode continues to show how Succession’s characters cope with the loss of Logan, giving a complex thematic overtone to the typical cynical business practices of the Roy family.

              Succession Season 5: Why It Isn't Happening   Creator Jesse Armstrong chose to end Succession after four seasons, although the prestige HBO drama series could have continued much further.

Succession Power Rankings: Winners & Losers After Season 4 Episode 4’s Ending

The events of Succession season 4 cause the power rankings to change more rapidly than ever before, with the characters’ standings at Waystar Royco shifting from episode to episode. Several factors in Succession season 4, episode 4, greatly changed the power rankings, including the discovery of Logan Roy’s final will, Waystar Royco’s new vote distribution, and the company board members’ new positions. Also, the interim CEOs have been named, but that doesn’t mean that the race for the heart of Waystar Royco is over. These are the Succession power rankings after season 4, episode 4, from least to most powerful:

  1. Greg - Greg has always been at the bottom of Waystar Royco’s corporate ladder, and while his name does appear in Logan Roy’s final wishes, he still doesn’t hold much power.

  2. Shiv - Shiv has always been at the top of the power ranking until season 4, episode 4. Now that her siblings are co-CEOs and Shiv has been left out in the cold, she no longer holds much sway at Waystar Royco.

  3. Tom - Tom is pretty low out of the possible contenders for the next CEO. However, season 4, episode 3 hints that Tom has a plan going on in the background, and it may be successful enough to bring him back to the top.

  4. Karl - As Karl explains in episode 4, the CFO is often first in line for the CEO position. So while Karl didn’t become the interim CEO, his CFO position and good standing with the board give him a shot at taking over the company in the future.

  5. Gerri - Although Logan planned on firing Gerri in episode 3, his death has boosted Gerri’s confidence, with her back in the big league. Since Gerri has served as interim CEO, she has good standing with the board.

  6. Roman - Roman was named as one of the two co-CEOs of Waystar Royco, filling the interim position until the board decides on an official replacement. This gives Roman a lot of power, but not as much as Kendall.

  7. Kendall - After several seasons of living in his father’s shadow, Kendall has come out on top, with him being one of Waystar Royco’s new co-CEOs. While he shares this responsibility with his brother, Kendall was specifically named in Logan’s final wishes, giving him much more pull at the company.

Why Kendall Works With Hugo To Smear Logan’s Name Kendall Uses His Leverage On Hugo For Help In His Crusade Against Logan

The final scene of Succession season 4, episode 4, sees Kendall approaching Hugo and asking him to smear Logan’s name. Although Hugo seems hesitant to be part of this scheme, Kendall finds out about Hugo’s insider trading earlier in the episode, which the new CEO uses to blackmail Hugo. Kendall has always hated his father, and while he is finally CEO, Kendall is using his newfound power to ruin Logan’s reputation one more time.

Kendall & Roman’s CEO Positions Sets The Roys Against Each Other The Roy Brothers’ Egos Will Likely Rear Their Ugly Heads, While Shiv Is Alienated

Kendall and Roman Roy are appointed interim co-CEOs in Succession season 4, episode 4. While this is a temporary resolution, it also risks tearing the Roys apart. Kendall and Roman may be happy working together now, but the two powerful figures equipped with their massive egos are destined to come to blows eventually. On top of that, it is doubtful that the Waystar Royco board will allow for two CEOs permanently. The implication is that one of them will have to step down — and neither wants to do it.

On top of the inevitable conflict between Kendall and Roman, there is also a third factor: Shiv. Throughout Succession, Shiv seemed more eager to run the company than her siblings, with the decision to leave her out of the co-CEO arrangement being a big blow. She was pretty upset by this, and this decision digs a deeper rift between Shiv and her brothers. Shiv was easily the most qualified to run the company out of the three Roy siblings, making the choice to leave her out even more pointed.

Logan Roy’s Final Wishes Endanger Waystar Is Kendall Really The Best Choice For CEO?

In “Honeymoon States”, Gerri, Frank, and Karl discover a list of final wishes that Logan Roy wrote at an unspecified time in the past, including Kendall taking over the company, with his name underlined – or crossed out. This list leads to Kendall Roy being appointed as CEO, but some other parts of the list have severe implications for Waystar Royco. For example, one penciled-in addendum features “Greg” with a question mark. The suggestions on this list may or may not hold up, but the decision to follow them leads the company down a dangerous path.

Succession Confirms One Pregnancy & Hints At Another In Season 4 Episode 4 Shiv Gets A Call From Her Doctor While Kerry’s Pregnancy Is Just Speculation

Succession season 4, episode 4, also deals with two major pregnancy storylines. The first of these revolves around Shiv. Early Succession season 4 heavily hints that Shiv is pregnant, with one sign being her lack of alcohol consumption. “Honeymoon States” confirms the pregnancy, with Shiv receiving a phone call from her doctor early in the episode. Additionally,, Shiv actress Sarah Snook was pregnant in real life at this time, so it makes sense to work that into the series.

The second pregnancy storyline in Succession season 4, episode 4, revolves around Logan’s assistant, Kerry. There have been rumors of Kerry sleeping with Logan throughout her time in Succession. In Succession season 4, episode 4, she shows up at Logan’s former home to pick up various medications that she had stored in Logan’s room. Kerry then mentions some arrangements Logan had prepared for her, although this conversation with Roman is mostly drowned out, offering plenty of fodder for those rumors.

This is the first credited appearance of Jóhannes Haukur Jóhannesson who plays Mattson’s right-hand man, Oskar Guðjohnsen, though only his voice is heard over the phone. It’s the next episode, “Kill List” where he appears on-screen.

GoJo’s Acquisition Of Waystar Develops In Succession Season 4 Episode 4 Matsson May Be Getting Cold Feet After Logan’s Death

Although GoJo’s acquisition of Waystar Royco has been the show’s main overarching storyline since season 3, it takes a sideline in early episodes of season 4. The acquisition was delayed after Logan Roy’s death, but the Roy siblings immediately try to kick the deal back into high gear in this episode.

The three siblings attempt to call Lukas Matsson to make sure the deal is still on, but they are left hanging. Waystar Royco is interested in the deal once again, but Logan’s death may have shaken GoJo’s trust in the company, setting up what ends up being the major conflict of the final season of Succession.

The Real Meaning Of The Succession Season 4, Episode 4 Ending

Succession season 4, episode 4 “Honeymoon States” is like the wrenching back of a catapult before the tension is released in the remaining episodes. For the first time in the series, Logan is no longer a presence. He may have been dead in season 4, episode 3, but even dying, he is a world-shaking force. It’s this episode that sees everyone grappling with a post-Logan Roy world and no one seems to have a clear picture. It’s so murky that of all people, Kendall and Roman find their way to the CEO position, almost in error.

In Logan’s home, the new order is determined and everyone begins scrambling for their place in Waystar Royco and in the Roy family. The pieces are set in this episode for what will happen in Succession and what will become of each person who has tied themselves so closely to the now departed Logan Roy.
