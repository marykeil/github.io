A Mistborn Movie Could Lead To The Perfect Fantasy & Western Mash-Up (But Would Take 10 Years)

SUMMARY

Brandon Sanderson’s Mistborn movie adaptation is a major step forward for the fantasy genre, with the potential to engage a whole new population of fans. A successful Mistborn movie trilogy could lead to the adaptation of the second book series, bringing new characters and surroundings to the franchise. While a second Mistborn film series is a long way off and not guaranteed, there is still reason to hope for the future of the franchise, and fans can enjoy the other books in the meantime.

Literature Review

The Mistborn movie adaptation is huge for Brandon Sanderson’s high fantasy series, but the first trilogy isn’t the only series in need of a movie.

Brandon Sanderson’s upcoming Mistborn movie adaptation is a major step forward for the fantasy movie genre, however, if the entire trilogy does well with audiences, then an even better fantasy movie mash-up could happen as well. In 2020, fantasy author Brandon Sanderson announced that he would be writing the screenplay for a film adaptation of his 2006 novel, “Mistborn: The Final Empire.” And though the movie has run into development issues as of 2023, it is still happening as of now, which means Mistborn could engage a whole new population of fans.

Although the Mistborn book series includes seven books, the break-up of these novels is a bit more complicated than that. First and foremost, Mistborn’s seven books are two eras within one larger series. The first three books, “The Final Empire,” “The Well of Ascension,” and “The Hero of Ages” make up one trilogy, while “The Alloy of Law,” “Shadows of Self,” “The Bands of Mourning,” and “The Lost Metal,” are a separate series. Though both series take place in the same world, and at times, in the same cities, they take place in completely different times.

A Successful Mistborn Movie Trilogy Could See The Second Book Series Adapted

If the Mistborn movie adaptation succeeds, it could spawn all sorts of sequels. While the initial sequels would be “The Well of Ascension” and “The Hero of Ages,” after that, the second half of Mistborn could be adapted. This would be a great move because it could keep the hype for Brandon Sanderson’s stories alive. Also, it could provide new Mistborn fans with a peek at a different side of the Mistborn world. Meanwhile, those who were already fans of the Mistborn books would not only get to see Vin and Kelsier come to life, but also Wax and Wayne.

If the first Mistborn trilogy does well, it has the potential to flourish into a very popular fantasy franchise with countless avenues to continue going down.

Ultimately, the Mistborn books are just waiting to become a franchise. Brandon Sanderson has written various popular fantasy novels over the years, and Mistborn is definitely a strong entryway into his work, which makes it a good choice for adaptation. If the first Mistborn trilogy does well, it has the potential to flourish into a very popular fantasy franchise with countless avenues to continue going down. The most obvious avenue is adapting the second half of Mistborn, which brings a fresh and exciting ambiance, but there are other books from Brandon Sanderson’s Cosmere that could be adapted as well.

The Alloy Of Law Could Be The Perfect Fantasy & Western Movie Mash-Up

The main reason why the second half of Mistborn should be adapted after the initial Mistborn trilogy is because of its atmosphere. While “The Final Empire” has a setting that is firmly rooted in classic fantasy, with medieval kingdoms and courts, “The Alloy of Law,” is much more modern. It is inspired by 20th century America and has the ambiance of a Western story. This is quite different from the Mistborn trilogy, which may seem risky, but in reality, this switch-up will help the franchise by smashing up genres in a way that has never been seen before.

By adapting “The Alloy of Law” after the first Mistborn trilogy, the franchise will be keeping the magic going without boring audiences. After three movies, audiences will be very familiar with the world Mistborn has introduced, and even if they love the story, they could easily get bored of their surroundings. That is where “The Alloy of Law” comes in. The second Mistborn series can keep the Mistborn story going but with entirely new characters and surroundings. In this way, fans can continue living in this franchise while also continuing to be surprised.

A Second Mistborn Film Series Would Be A Long Way Off

Of course, a “The Alloy of Law” adaptation is very far off. The first Mistborn movie is already stuck in development purgatory, and after it is finally released, two more movies will have to come before it. On top of that, there is a high risk of failure. Mistborn is high fantasy, and that does not always bode well with audiences. The series’ fan base may not be enough. In this way, “The Alloy of Law,” may never happen. And even if Mistborn does succeed, that does not guarantee that “The Alloy of Law” will be made.

Despite this, there is still reason to hope. The Mistborn movie is so far from its release that there is no telling how it will be. Therefore, it doesn’t hurt to be optimistic and hope that the film performs well enough to warrant a Brandon Sanderson franchise. In the meantime, fans who can’t wait for Mistborn should certainly check out “The Alloy of Law,” and the rest of the Cosmere books.
