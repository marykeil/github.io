Rick & Morty Season 7 Subtly Highlighted A Potential New Lead Character

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1000 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Summer proves she is a dynamic lead character in season 7, episode 7. Her storylines deviate from the usual formula, making them more engaging. Rick and Morty’s usual formula limits potential, but looser formula in season 8 gives Summer a bigger role.

Literature Review

Rick and Morty season 7’s most underrated outing proved that the show has an underused supporting star who could be its new hero in season 8.

While Rick and Morty season 7 gave its title characters plenty of time to shine, the show also proved that it has another potential protagonist waiting in the wings. While Rick and Morty is often called anarchic and wild, the show has a surprisingly reliable formula in most of its episodes. Like many sitcoms, most episodes of Rick and Morty typically have an A-story that follows its titular pairing on an adventure and a B-plot that focuses on other members of the Smith family. This formula becomes obvious in its absence when occasional episodes stray from this reliable routine.

The Rick and Morty season 7 finale had no such B-plot, and the episode’s ending revealed that this was because the entire outing took place in Morty’s mind. Similarly, season 3, episode 9, “The ABC’s of Beth,” paired Rick with Beth instead of Morty while episode 5 of the same season, “The Whirly Dirly Conspiracy,” paired up Rick and Jerry. However, the show’s formulaic approach still dominates most of its 71 outings. This can become an issue when it holds back potentially great lead characters. Season 7 proved that this current setup limits one heroine’s potential.

Summer Should Be A Lead Rick and Morty Character In Season 8 Summer’s season 7 episode proved she deserved a bigger role

Rick and Morty season 7, episode 7, “Wet Kuat Amortycan Summer,” proved that Summer is a more dynamic and proactive lead character than Morty. This means she is less reliant on Rick to make her plots engaging and, as a result, her storylines don’t fall back on Rick and Morty’s old formula. “Wet Kuat Amortycan Summer” provided a perfect example of this as Summer reacted to Morty becoming her psychic appendage by partying and accidentally falling victim to an organ theft ring. Her subsequent attempts to retrieve her “Kuato Morty” displayed more initiative than viewers generally see from Morty.

Since Rick and Morty’s entire premise is predicated on Rick being almost all-powerful and Morty being a well-meaning dimwit, their characters never experience much change. While both characters have grown over the years, with Rick gaining some humanity and Morty getting more confident, their dynamic remains largely consistent. As such, Summer’s episodes are a welcome reprieve from Rick and Morty’s routine. Even after a huge canon event like Rick Prime’s season 7 death, Rick and Morty went right back to their regularly scheduled adventures. In contrast, Summer took bigger risks in her season 7 episode, making the outing’s plot less predictable.

Summer’s Stories Have Always Elevated Rick and Morty Summer’s differences from Rick and Morty make her stories stronger

This isn’t the first time the differences between Morty and Summer made her plots more engaging. Season 4, episode 7, “Promortyus” was one of the show’s darkest, funniest deconstructions of its usual formula and this was thanks in large part to Summer’s role. Since Summer ended up stranded on an alien planet that Rick and Morty visited, she offered viewers an idea of what Rick and Morty’s misadventures could look like if the heroes had a different approach. When Rick and Morty returned to the planet, they were disturbed to discover that their violent antics weren’t as funny from the local perspective.

While Rick and Morty season 7’s movie parodies worked well, an extended riff on an obscure character from Total Recall wasn’t the only thing that made “Wet Kuat Amortycan Summer” a standout. It was the fact that most of the episode took place from Summer’s point of view that elevated this outing. From the opening montage, wherein Summer did Rick’s chores and proved that she was just as bored by her sci-fi grandfather’s dimension-hopping adventures as Morty, Summer’s episode showed that she has a story worth telling. Season 5, episode 3, “A Rickconvenient Mort,” hinted at this earlier.

Rick and Morty Season 8’s Looser Formula Gives Summer A Bigger Role The animated comedy is no longer as reliant on its earlier formula

When Summer accompanied Rick on an adventure because Morty was busy with his new romance, she showed her hidden depths while Rick revealed the extent of his loneliness. The limitations of Rick and Morty’s usual formula mean that the show’s central duo rarely talk as equals in most episodes, making this arc a welcome surprise. Rick and Morty season 7 brought back many elements from earlier seasons, but its focus on Summer was one of the most successful.

Luckily, Rick and Morty season 8 can continue this approach by giving Summer another episode of her own. Season 7 gave both Rick and Morty entirely solo episodes this season, as well as the premiere and finale which were mostly solo outings as well. This goes to show that season 8 could successfully focus an episode on Summer and Rick or Summer and Morty without losing the central appeal of the series. For much of “Wet Kuat Amortycan Summer,” Morty only had one line, and the Rick and Morty episode didn’t suffer for it - thanks to Summer’s plentiful main character energy.
