Literature Review

Love Is Blind season 6 premiered on Netflix. Here’s what happened in the first four episodes and how to tune in.

WARNING: SPOILERS FOR LOVE IS BLIND SEASON 6, EPISODES 1-4 BELOW! Love Is Blind season 6 dropped on Netflix, and plenty happened in the first four episodes. As one of the best reality TV shows and one of the most anticipated reality TV shows of 2024, Love Is Blind season 6 was worth waiting for. After a disastrous season 5, the latest installment starring couples from Charlotte, North Carolina, gives the show the renewal it needed. Love triangles, chemistry, and drama are already brewing in four episodes.

Love Is Blind season 1 premiered just before the pandemic and became an instant hit. While couples like Lauren & Cameron Speed and Tiffany & Brett Brown create hope for the experiment to continue working, it’s almost as fun to watch trainwreck couples fall apart. However, the balance is essential, as season 5 featured mostly problematic engagements and only saw one couple head down the aisle. Love Is Blind season 6 certainly will keep viewers entertained.

What Day & Time Are Love Is Blind Season 6 Episodes 1-4 On?

Love Is Blind season 6, episodes 1-4, dropped on Netflix on February 14, 2024, at 3 a.m. ET. The series follows a structure similar to previous seasons, with the first four episodes focusing on relationships in the pods. There is much more focus on political and religious beliefs and digging into deep conversations than in previous seasons. As usual, love triangles have developed, engagements have occurred, and hearts have been broken.

Love Is Blind Season 6, Episodes 1-4 Recap

Love Is Blind season 6 is spicy already. Plenty of drama was alluded to in the preview, and it should be a whirlwind season. During the first episode, relationships begin between Johnny and Amy, who bond over their childhood and mutual connection. Amber Desiree, also known as AD, bonded with Clay, who calls her “AD all day,” and bizarrely grinch-like financial advisor Matthew. Matthew, a walking red flag, seems judgmental and emotionally stunted, alluding to potential drama. Additionally, Chelsea found herself on the top of multiple lists, including mullet-sporting Trevor and small-town Jimmy. Unfortunately for Chelsea, Jimmy also has his eye on Jessica, the first single mother featured on the U.S. version of the show.

Speaking of Jimmy, he’s thrown for a loop two dates in a row, with Jessica sharing she has a ten-year-old daughter and Chelsea admitting she’s divorced. Unlike Trevor, who was non-judgmental and empathetic toward Chelsea, he didn’t take either conversation well. Johnny and Amy seem poised to be the first couple engaged, and another love triangle in the making between Jeramey, Sarah Ann, and Laura emerges. While Matthew was the original red flag, Clay’s desire to know what AD looks like was a giant turnoff. At this point, AD needs to say no to both and leave in peace. Of course, it’s Love Is Blind, so that will unlikely happen.

Unsurprisingly, it’s revealed that Matthew has been saying the same things to AD and Amber, leaving viewers on a cliffhanger into episode 2. While Matthew attempts to talk himself out of the situation, he does a poor job. However, AD falls for it. Goofball Jeramey seems poised to break some hearts, albeit loudly and chaotically. While he and Laura don’t seem compatible, the pod conversations continue despite Jeramey calling Laura the wrong name. On the other hand, the chemistry between Sarah Ann and Jeramey is electric.

AD is having a lousy day choosing between two men with the emotional capacity of a teaspoon, getting gaslit by Matthew and belittled by Clay. While the right thing for AD to do is cut it off with both, she doesn’t. Amber and AD have a complex but essential chat, and it’s revealed that Amber has chosen to leave the experiment. After one last conversation, Matthew insists he’s a good guy and declares he intends to “go get Amber.” Matthew chooses to leave the experiment.

Brittany and Kenneth form a connection that seems aligned in religion and is personally suitable. After reliving childhood trauma, the pair seem poised to enter into an engagement in the coming episodes. Johnny and Amy are the first to tell each other they’re in love and are engaged by the end of the episode. They seem genuine and are the couple to root for so far. After meeting at the beginning of episode 3, their chemistry is palpable.

Clay gets in touch with his emotions and remains shaken from his interaction with AD the previous day. After a difficult conversation, they make amends. Brittany expresses her love for Kenneth over salad, and the pair have a straightforward discussion about race. While she doesn’t care what ethnicity he is, she is concerned he may be 5'4". Despite this, serious conversations about waiting until marriage ensue. Kenneth and Brittany continue to seem aligned in what’s next for them.

Laura is ready for Jeramey to choose her, but he’s still waffling between her and Sarah Ann. While he expresses that Laura is his number one, she seems to dismiss the playfulness in his personality. On the other hand, Sarah Ann is also a little goofy, and she and Jeramey like joking around. Sarah Ann is straightforward and honest about her political beliefs and positions on social media, even addressing her thoughts on abortion, which is sure to be a hot topic after the episodes drop. Jeramey seems less conservative but holds his cards close to his chest.

Brittany and Kenneth get engaged, but Jimmy is still waffling between Jessica and Chelsea. While Jessica wants to be Jimmy’s “first-round draft pick,” it’s clear she’s not. However, Trevor is sure about Chelsea and wants to leave the show with her. Episode 4 opens with the girls talking about peeing in the pool and the shower, so the pods are off to a good start. At this point, if Jeramey chooses Laura, he’s making a huge mistake. They are not compatible. As Clay and AD continue to explore their relationship, he proposes to AD, and the former cheerleader seems to forget she is even interested in another man mere days before.

Brittany and Kenneth’s sweetness is through the roof when they finally meet, and Clay can’t keep his hands off AD. Luckily for her, she meets his physical criteria. Chelsea would be wise to choose Trevor, but Love Is Blind cast members aren’t known for making good choices. Jessica’s letter throws Jimmy for a loop, and he’s unable to reciprocate her feelings. Jessica needs someone who values her 100%, and it’s not Jimmy. After the conversation with Jessica, Jimmy tells Chelsea that he loves her. While Jimmy should go home alone and Chelsea is more compatible with Trevor, everyone makes poor decisions.

Jessica rips Jimmy to shreds while Chelsea claims she is 50/50 between Jimmy and Trevor. Since she’s not set on either of them, it might be wise to leave the pods alone. However, Jimmy reveals he will propose to Chelsea, and Trevor says the same. Audiences are left on a cliffhanger, and it seems like Chelsea will accept a proposal from whoever gets a date with her first. The first four episodes were a whirlwind and will only heat up.

Where to Watch Love Is Blind Season 6 Online

Love Is Blind season 6, episodes 1-4, are currently available for streaming on Netflix. Like previous seasons, episodes 5-8 will air on Wednesday, February 21, with 9-11 dropping on February 28, and the weddings airing on Wednesday, March 6, all at 3 a.m. ET. As fans await weekly episodes, Love Is Blind: Sweden, Love Is Blind: Japan, and Love Is Blind: Brazil are all available for streaming on Netflix.
