Casting 10 DC Superheroes For The DCU That Still Havent Had A Solo Movie (But Totally Should)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

James Gunn’s DC Universe has the opportunity to showcase important and popular heroes that haven’t yet had their own movies. The casting choices for the DCU vary from returning stars to relative unknowns, but what matters most is how well the actors fit their roles. There are a number of talented actors who would be suitable for bringing characters like Green Lantern, Lobo, Vixen, Martian Manhunter, Nightwing, Atom, Green Arrow, Zatanna, Cyborg, and Hawkgirl to life in the DC Universe.

Literature Review

James Gunn has the opportunity to give some love to DC heroes who have yet to get a solo film with the DCU, with plenty of great casting choices.

The DC Universe has the opportunity to put forth the first major movie incarnations of many surprisingly integral characters from the comics, with plenty of untapped Hollywood talent to choose from in terms of representation. Despite a long history of blockbuster films, DC Comics have still never gotten around to granting solo movies to some of their most important and popular heroes. James Gunn’s nascent DC Universe has the opportunity to finally rectify this mistake, clearly being willing to put audiences on to the more obscure stories of the franchise, such as Creature Commandos. Some of DC’s most important characters, such as Batman and Damian Wayne’s Robin, have still yet to be cast in the DCU as of the time of writing. Of the actors that are confirmed for the DCU, there are an eclectic mix of returning stars from holdover properties such as John Cena’s Peacemaker, frequent James Gunn collaborators like Nathan Fillion, and relative unknowns such as David Corenswet. However, James Gunn has proved that he values how well a given actor fits their role most of all, meaning it would be equally likely for him to rope in big names or more underground performers.

10 John Boyega As John Stewart’s Green Lantern

Few core Justice League members have gotten the short end of the cinematic stick like John Stewart’s incarnation of Green Lantern. The architect turned marine turned intergalactic peacekeeper is one of the most compelling characters to have ever donned the Green Lantern ring, but has been eschewed in favor of Hal Jordan time and time again. John Boyega deserves similar redemption for his efforts in the Star Wars prequels and Pacific Rim Uprising. For all the problems those films had, Boyega’s performances were a diamond in the rough, and the British actor deserves another shot at a leading sci-fi role.

9 Jason Momoa As Lobo

Having contributed his Aquaman to the DCEU already, rumors of Jason Momoa joining the DCU as a new character have been swirling for some time now. Of the most famous DC characters that have yet to see a live-action depiction, Momoa would be an absolute shoo-in for the role of Lobo. The actor could easily nail the wild looks, sense of humor, and physicality of the alien bounty-hunting anti-hero, and Momoa himself has already expressed interest in the role, and the chain-wielding outlaw would lend himself perfectly to James Gunn’s dark sense of humor.

8 Zazie Beetz Vixen

A criminally underrated superheroine in the Justice League, Vixen would be a welcome sight to the theatrical DC mythos. Thanks to her connection to the spider God Anansi, Vixen has the ability to channel the primordial force known as “The Red”, connecting all animal life on the planet, through a magical totem, allowing her to mimic the abilities of the animal kingdom. Zazie Beetz is no stranger to superhero action, having already played Domino in Deadpool 2 and Amber in Invincible, and certainly has the charm to convincingly portray Vixen’s secret identity as a model.

7 Chiwetel Ejiofor As Martian Manhunter

Frequently regulated to supporting cast member for the Justice League as a whole, it’s rare that Martian Manhunter gets the spotlight in any medium, let alone an animated movie. The closest the alien hero has come to a solo film is a cameo in Zack Snyder’s Justice League, played by Harry Lennix. However, Chiwetel Ejiofor would be an excellent choice for a true attempt at bringing J’onn J’onzz to life. His distinctive, dignified voice, previous experience as Baron Mordo in the Doctor Strange movies, and impressive acting range would all be crucial to portraying the pensive and often misunderstood Martian Manhunter.

6 Jared Padalecki As Nightwing

The DCU’s premiere Batman movie, Batman: The Brave & The Bold, promises to finally bring the Bat-Family to the big screen, a crucial aspect of The Dark Knight’s mythos that rarely gets good theatrical representation. Considering Damian Wayne will be Robin in the upcoming movie, a Jared Padalecki Nightwing solo movie wouldn’t be out of the realm of possibility. Padalecki’s charisma and detective work in the long-running Supernatural TV series would translate excellently to a solo Nightwing film set in Gotham’s lesser-known sister city, Bludhaven.

5 Simu Liu As Ryan Choi’s Atom

Several different names have taken up the mantle of The Atom in DC Comics over the years, but Ryan Choi is perhaps the single most compelling. Similar to Marvel’s shrinking hero, Ant-Man, an Atom movie could center around the gifted scientist Ryan Choi taking up the mantle of the persona from a retired Ray Palmer. To cast Ryan Choi, the DCU would have to look no further than Simu Liu, who has been woefully underused by the MCU ever since his captivating performance as Shang-Chi. While Liu is currently nursing an injury, the DCU would be wise to welcome him.

4 Austin Butler As Green Arrow

Despite being the face of a long-running CW drama that went on to spawn an entire TV universe named after him, Green Arrow has somehow never made it onto the big screen, let alone as a solo act. James Gunn would be wise to rectify this with a solo Green Arrow movie, and few candidates stand out for Oliver Queen quite like Austin Butler. Having the rare ability to pull off the archer’s trademark blonde facial hair would instantly set him apart from the close-cut Arrowverse version, and Butler’s Oscar-nominated performance in Elvis proves he has charisma to spare.

3 Alexandra Daddario As Zatanna

Much like the original Doctor Strange did for the MCU, a Zatanna solo movie could introduce the arcane into the fold of the DCU early on. Hailing from a long lineage of the magically-gifted race, homo magi, Zatanna’s dual life as a stage performer and superheroine could be excellently portrayed by Alexandra Daddario. Not only is Daddario a dead ringer for Zatanna, but her extensive horror experience in productions like American Horror Story and Texas Chainsaw 3D would lend itself well to the more spooky supernatural forces faced by the character.

2 Trevante Rhodes As Cyborg

Ray Fisher’s Cyborg never quite got the chance to shine in the DCEU, and the cybernetic Justice League staple deserves a solo film that exemplifies what makes him so great. As good as Fisher’s performance was, Trevante Rhodes has the potential to be even better as a fresh incarnation of Cyborg. Not only does Rhodes’ powerful performance in the Academy Award-winning Moonlight make him almost overqualified for the DCU, but his history as a track star and running back is perfect for channeling Victor Stone’s athletic side.

1 Naomi Scott As Hawkgirl

Thanks to the animated series Justice League Unlimited, there was a time when Hawkgirl was the iconic DC team’s second-most iconic female member, just behind Wonder Woman. Few actresses have had a more impressive lineup of female pop-culture icons than Naomi Scott, who has had the pleasure of playing the Pink Ranger in Power Rangers, Princess Jasmine in the live-action Aladdin, and one of Charlie’s Angels, to boot. The DCU has clearly already demonstrated a commitment to the lesser-known heroes of DC’s catalog, and Naomi Scott as Hawkgirl could be a perfect inclusion.
