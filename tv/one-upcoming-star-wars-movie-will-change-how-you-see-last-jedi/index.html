One Upcoming Star Wars Movie Will Change How You See Last Jedi

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The next Star Wars movie, Dawn of the Jedi, will change the way viewers see Star Wars: The Last Jedi due to its exploration of the Jedi’s earliest history. Dawn of the Jedi will focus on the Prime Jedi, the first of their kind, thus shedding light on the origins and history of the Jedi and the Force. The film will challenge Luke Skywalker’s belief that the Jedi are inherently flawed, uncovering thousands of years of Jedi history that predated the events of the prequel trilogy.

Literature Review

Star Wars’ upcoming movie, Dawn of the Jedi, will explore a brand-new era of the timeline that will change the way you see The Last Jedi forever.

Star Wars: Dawn of the Jedi will reveal the history of the Prime Jedi, reflecting a massive update to the understanding of the Jedi’s origins and history and the Force - and thus will reframe the representation of the Jedi depicted in Star Wars: The Last Jedi. The Last Jedi sees a very bitter, pessimistic Luke Skywalker, who has hidden away from the galaxy and believes that the Jedi are inherently flawed and therefore must end. However, his assessment of those failings is limited to his own experiences and his brief exposure to Obi-Wan Kenobi and Master Yoda.

Luke bases his perception of the Jedi’s failure on what he knows of the prequel trilogy’s Jedi Order and his failed attempt to establish a Jedi Temple on Ossus after Return of the Jedi. Although fairly accurate in terms of his Jedi Temple and the Jedi Order’s fall during Palpatine’s rise to power, this doesn’t take into account the thousands of years of history that predated both the prequel era’s Order and his own Temple. Dawn of the Jedi may uncover that history and demonstrate that those flaws, rather than inherent in the Jedi, were never meant to be.

Dawn of the Jedi Will Set Up The Prime Jedi

The first Jedi, also known as the Prime Jedi, will be explored in Dawn of the Jedi, which will be a thrilling new addition. In the Star Wars movies, the earliest depictions of the Jedi Order go only as far back as the prequel era Jedi Order in Star Wars: Episode I - The Phantom Menace. While that was interesting in its own right because it was working backward from the original Star Wars trilogy and filling in the gaps, it was clear that the prequel Jedi Order was flawed.

Now, Dawn of the Jedi has the opportunity to address whether those flaws always existed in the Jedi or if the prequel’s Order got it wrong. Seemingly, Dawn of the Jedi and the upcoming film Star Wars: New Jedi Order will do this in tandem, as Lucasfilm President Kathleen Kennedy has already revealed Rey’s quest to establish a new Order, using the ancient Jedi texts to try to improve the weaknesses of the prequel era Jedi Order. Ideally, both films will address Jedi traditions like the rule against attachments that caused so many issues in the prequel films.

Dawn Of The Jedi Will Reveal What The First Jedi Believed

In addition to addressing the various aspects of the Jedi code reflected in the Skywalker saga, Dawn of the Jedi will seemingly also address the Prime Jedi’s perception of the Force. In particular, the movie will uncover how the first Jedi perceived the light and the dark side of the Force. The imagery on Ahch-To in The Last Jedi, however, seems to signal that the answer may be much more nuanced than the perception of the Force seen in the Star Wars films thus far.

The definition of ‘balance’ in the Force has always been a bit confusing. While many Jedi throughout the Skywalker saga explain the importance of balance, particularly given that the entire Chosen One prophecy’s goal is bringing that balance (certainly seen as a good thing throughout the franchise), and the fact that dark will always rise to meet light and vice versa, the Jedi are constantly trying to snuff out the dark. The objective throughout all three trilogies is to defeat the Sith; that doesn’t exactly suggest ‘balance.’

Excitingly, because the Jedi Temple on Ahch-To shows an image that brings dark and light together, Dawn of the Jedi may reveal that the Prime Jedi didn’t fear the dark side but rather used it alongside the light. This would address another major Star Wars gap; the Jedi are theoretically all about letting go of fear and trusting the Force, yet they clearly fear the Sith and the dark side. This may also shake up the notion that the dark side is explicitly evil and the light side is explicitly good, complicating the Force in a compelling way.

Could Dawn Of The Jedi Introduce Ahch-To?

Ahch-To, first depicted briefly at the end of Star Wars: The Force Awakens and then further explored in Star Wars: The Last Jedi and Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker, is revealed in the sequels to be the home of the first Jedi Temple and the place in which the Jedi Order was first established. This means that it would make considerable sense to introduce the planet in Dawn of the Jedi, which will unpack the history of the Prime Jedi. In addition to having an interesting connection to the sequels, this will answer some important questions about Jedi history.

In The Last Jedi, Luke Skywalker describes Ahch-To as “the most unfindable place in the galaxy.” While surely the galaxy has changed in the thousands of years since, this nevertheless raises questions about the differences between the Prime Jedi and the Jedi Order of the sequels. Rather than an isolated planet, the Jedi Temple in the prequel films is on Coruscant, the populous city which is in many ways the heart of the galaxy. This gives it a very close proximity to the Senate, signaling a connection between the politicians and the Jedi, which was ultimately part of their downfall.

The Jedi Temple on Coruscant is said to have been established about 1,000 years before the prequel Star Wars trilogy, meaning the Jedi have long since been placed in the center of the galaxy.

If Ahch-To was similarly ‘unfindable’ in ancient times, that may mean that the Jedi had a much more remote role in the galaxy. This could prove interesting, as the Jedi depicted throughout the Skywalker saga have a much more service-oriented role, working as peacekeepers for some time, soldiers in the Clone Wars era, and becoming major players in the Rebellion and the Resistance. Particularly given that the Jedi are a ‘religion,’ Dawn of the Jedi may show the Jedi to be a more pious, secluded institution, perhaps more in tune with the Force than interested in the issues of the galaxy.
