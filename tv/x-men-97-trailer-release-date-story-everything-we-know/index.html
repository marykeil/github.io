X-Men 97 Trailer, Release Date, Story & Everything We Know

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

X-Men ‘97 is a revival of the classic ’90s superhero cartoon and the only known continuation of Marvel’s first shared onscreen universe. The show is not part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe, meaning mutants in the MCU won’t be affected by X-Men ‘97. X-Men ‘97 will premiere on March 20, 2024, with multiple seasons planned and a focus on respecting the legacy of the original series.

Literature Review

X-Men ‘97 will be the end of the 26-year wait for X-Men: The Animated Series to continue, and Disney has big plans for the X-Men cartoon reboot.

The X-Men are back at Marvel, and what makes X-Men ‘97 unique is it’s the only revival/continuation of a classic ’90s superhero cartoon on Marvel and Disney’s release slate. The announcement of X-Men ‘97 came as the original show it’s based on, X-Men: The Animated Series, approached its 30th anniversary. X-Men: The Animated Series aired from October 1992 to September 1997, which explains the title of Marvel’s new show. Serving as the head writer of X-Men ‘97 is Beau DeMayo, with Eric and Julia Lewald - former showrunners of X-Men: The Animated Series - serving as consultants on the series.

X-Men ‘97 is also the only known continuation of Marvel’s first shared onscreen universe, which comprises X-Men: The Animated Series and Spider-Man: The Animated Series, a show that ran from 1994 to 1998. This means that, presumably, mutants in the MCU won’t be affected by X-Men ‘97, as the new series is technically not part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe - which may have helped X-Men ‘97 season 2 get approved before the first season has itself released for audiences. Here’s everything else audiences need to know about the X-Men ‘97 trailer, story, and release date.

The Latest X-Men ‘97 News

The most vital recent update regarding X-Men ‘97 is that of its release date and its first trailer reveal, which were brought to audiences in February 2024. The first X-Men ‘97 trailer provides a closer look at the heroes of the animated universe, both in terms of teasing the story of the show, and in terms of showing off the art style that will be employed in the series. Alongside this trailer came the news that X-Men ‘97 season 1 will premiere Wednesday March 20, giving audiences a better understanding of when they’ll be able to actively watch the show.

The other latest notable piece of X-Men ‘97 news was an incredibly positive update about Disney’s plans for the X-Men animated series reboot. As reported by The Direct,X-Men ‘97 will run for multiple seasons on Disney+. While the news that the beloved 90s X-Men cartoon would receive a reboot was received incredibly positively, it was never a guarantee Marvel Studios would invest in the project long-term, especially since many live-action MCU shows have been single-season miniseries.

The decision to have X-Men ‘97 run for more than one season is in line with Bob Iger’s comments about the future of the MCU, wherein he stated the franchise would be focusing more on follow-ups and sequels.

Thankfully Disney and Marvel understand the potential in X-Men ‘97, and they’re taking a thorough and respectful approach. As was also reported by The Direct, lead X-Men ‘97 writer Beau DeMayo was incredibly strict with who he allowed in the writers room. DeMayo would only work with fans of the original 90’s X-Men cartoon on the reboot, and X-Men ‘97 has been crafted by a team of creatives who all understand that (in DeMayo’s words) “You have to respect the work before you’re allowed to add to its legacy.”

X-Men ‘97 Is Confirmed

For those introduced to the X-Men: The Animated Series as a beloved Saturday morning cartoon but don’t follow current MCU trends, X-Men ‘97 was confirmed back in November 2021. The X-Men cartoon reboot was announced alongside other Disney+ Marvel properties, such as Spider-Man: Freshman Year, What If…? season 2, and Marvel Zombies. Hints at an X-Men animated series reboot had been interwoven throughout other MCU properties, foreshadowing the sequel series.

For example, in Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness, Patrick Stewart appeared as Professor Charles Xavier, but this time dressed up as his animated counterpart — with a green suit and a yellow hoverchair. What makes it even more obvious is that the X-Men: The Animated Series theme music plays in the background. In addition, the Disney+ series Ms. Marvel sees a scene in which Ms. Marvel (Iman Vellani) finds out that her powers come from a genetic mutation in her DNA, and the X-Men: The Animated Series theme plays again.

X-Men ‘97 Release Date

X-Men ‘97 is set to premiere on March 20 2024, having been moved back from a prior fall 2023 date. This initial delay was announced alongside a series of other MCU release delays, appearing to be the result of having to push the overall franchise slate back due to the WGA and SAG-AFTRA strikes. While the WGA strike has since been resolved, the SAG-AFTRA strikes remain ongoing, suggesting this date could be subject to further delays depending on when a resolution is found.

For now, audiences who want to catch up on the original show can stream X-Men: The Animated Series on Disney+. At its peak, X-Men: The Animated Series drew 23 million viewers to television screens. Considering the hype surrounding X-Men ‘97, the new show seems ready to surpass the original’s success.

X-Men ‘97 Cast: Every Character Confirmed

Long-time viewers of the original cartoons will be pleased to know that most of the surviving cast members are reprising their characters for X-Men ‘97. This includes Alyson Court who voiced Jubilee, though Court will be voicing a different character in X-Men ‘97 and has reportedly requested for Jubilee to be voiced by an Asian actor instead.

Jubilee, Magneto, and Sunspot are confirmed to be in the X-Men ‘97 cast, but their voice actors are still unknown. Voice actors who are attached to the project, but their characters have not yet been revealed, include Jennifer Hale, Ray Chase, Anniwaa Buachie, Matthew Waterson, JP Karliak, Holly Chou, Jeff Bennett, and A.J LoCascio.

While anticipation is high for the reveal of the MCU’s next mutant character, X-Men ‘97 will be bringing back several of Marvel’s most beloved mutants in one fell swoop. The X-Men ‘97 cast also includes Catherine Disher as Jean Grey, Cathal J. Dodd as James Howlett/Wolverine, Alison Sealy-Smith as Ororo Munroe/Storm, Chris Potter as Remy LeBeau/Gambit, Lenore Zann as Anna-Marie Raven/Rogue, Adrian Hough as Kurt Wagner/Nightcrawler, George Buza as Dr. Henry “Hank” McCoy/Beast, and Christopher Britton as Nathaniel Essex/Mister Sinister.

X-Men ‘97 Story: What We Know

Apart from the fact that Magneto - and not Professor X - will be leading the X-Men, there are a multitude of other reasons why X-Men ‘97 will be different from the movies or any other recent and prominent onscreen adaptation. So far, the only thing that’s known are the new additions to the team (Sunspot, Nightcrawler, Bishop, and Cable), as well as the fact that the X-Men will be taking on Mr. Sinister and his mutant experiments, the Sentinel robot army and the Hellfire club.

The X-Men ‘97 trailer provides a little more solid info on what to expect from the show’s plot, including a summary that stating, “X-Men ’97” revisits the iconic era of the 1990s as The X-Men, a band of mutants who use their uncanny gifts to protect a world that hates and fears them, are challenged like never before, forced to face a dangerous and unexpected new future." The trailer reveals that the team will be working on to try and honor the since-departed Charles Xavier, whose will supposedly provides Magneto with the Charles Xavier School For Gifted Youngsters as well.

Though Marvel hasn’t released much about X-Men ‘97’s plot, the entire premise of the new series has been laid out in X-Men: The Animated Series. This means that X-Men ‘97 won’t be bogged down by world-building at all, and can immediately dive into the main story. Furthermore, just as X-Men: The Animated Series became renowned for masterfully adapting popular ’80s comic book storylines like the Dark Phoenix saga and “Days of Future Past,” X-Men ‘97 will likely adapt X-Men comic book stories from the last decade.

X-Men ‘97 Trailer

The X-Men ‘97 trailer released on February 15 2024, gaining over 700,000 views in one hour. The trailer not only provides a further glimpse at the plot itself - with Magneto vying for control oer the X-Men as the team come to terms with the loss of Charles Xavier - but also with some interesting character developments, including showing a now heavily pregnant Jean Grey. That said, some promotional materials for the show had already come out long before this, providing audiences a look at still-shot preview frames of X-Men ‘97.

Is X-Men ‘97 A Reboot Or A Sequel Series?

X-Men ‘97 won’t be a reboot, but will instead be a direct sequel to the finale of the classic ’90s cartoon. Notably, the X-Men: The Animated Series ending sees Professor X being taken to the Shi’ar Empire for medical treatment, which means that the group’s leader in X-Men ‘97 will be Magneto and/or Cyclops. Though this didn’t sit well with some fans and even Magneto himself, Professor X himself convinced a reluctant Magneto to take on this leadership role. This presents a unique dynamic for X-Men ‘97 to flesh out - one that hasn’t been fully explored in the live-action X-Men movies.
