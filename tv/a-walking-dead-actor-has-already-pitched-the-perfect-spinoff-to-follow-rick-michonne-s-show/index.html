A Walking Dead Actor Has Already Pitched The Perfect Spinoff To Follow Rick & Michonne s Show

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

A potential spinoff focusing on the children of The Walking Dead franchise could provide a fresh perspective and expand the storyline in the future. By using existing characters with substantial foundations in the franchise, a legacy spinoff could maintain familiarity, avoid reused storylines, and be a major crossover with kid characters from each spinoff. The legacy spinoff would be a nod to the comic series’ ending and could highlight how the sacrifices and actions of the children’s parents shaped their post-apocalyptic worlds.

Literature Review

One spinoff idea from a Walking Dead actor would make the perfect show to follow Rick & Michonne’s franchise return, while bridging all the spinoffs.

With The Walking Dead franchise always expanding, one actor has already pitched the ideal spinoff to follow Rick and Michonne’s long-awaited show. The Walking Dead shows extend the franchise to include more granular stories focused on individual characters, such as in The Walking Dead: Dead City and Daryl Dixon. Prior to this, fully fledged apocalypse stories unfolded in Fear The Walking Dead, Tales of the Walking Dead, and World Beyond. Most presently, The Walking Dead: The Ones Who Live suggests another tie to the main show as it resumes Michonne and Rick’s epic story.

Naturally, in a franchise that has been running for 14 years and counting, many characters have joined and left and have captured audiences’ adoration. This isn’t to say that every character or spinoff idea can stand on its own. For instance, The Walking Dead villains spinoff idea can capture a unique perspective on characters’ mindsets in an apocalypse but works best in smaller doses, such as the Dead City Sanctuary flashbacks told from Negan’s perspective. However, a group of characters with substantial foundations in The Walking Dead franchise could be a natural next step, and one actor shares this sentiment.

              All 7 Walking Dead Spinoffs, Ranked Worst To Best   The Walking Dead has had its fair share of spinoffs packed with new characters and intense action, but some were better than others.

A Walking Dead Legacy Spinoff Could Flesh Out The Franchise’s Kid Characters Cailey Fleming suggests a spinoff with her character set in the future.

The Walking Dead children are often short-lived or used more as plot devices in the main show, excluding Carl Grimes, who had a gradual development into a crucial part of the primary cast. Cailey Fleming, who plays Judith Grimes, shared her idea for a Walking Dead spinoff, which could reframe how the kids in the franchise are viewed. “Maybe, 10 years in the future, there’s no zombies left,” says Fleming (via Business Insider). While an entirely zombie-free Walking Dead spinoff is not plausible for longevity reasons, the jump forward in time presents many opportunities.

Interestingly, this idea isn’t fresh to the franchise, as the cut Walking Dead series finale ending included a grown-up version of Judith and the other children helping survivors throughout Atlanta. Combining Fleming’s idea with the cut ending could be an effective way to continue the Grimes legacy, as well as the adopted lineage of major characters like Aaron and Gabriel. Unlike the children of The Walking Dead: World Beyond, which introduced audiences to brand-new characters, existing characters would have more of an appeal, especially since they’re connected to some of the franchise’s most loved characters.

Why A Legacy Spinoff Would Be A Good Way To Rejuvenate TWD’s Franchise A legacy Walking Dead spinoff could expand the franchise without needing to create a new cast of characters.

Granted, a spinoff centered on The Walking Dead’s kid characters wouldn’t work right now as many of the main kids are still linked to existing ongoing spinoffs, like Judith and RJ’s connection to Rick and Michonne and Hershel and Ginny’s connections to Maggie and Negan in Dead City. But introducing a legacy spinoff after Rick and Michonne’s show with Judith as a lead could be a natural next step for the Grimes family. Including other child characters, like Hershel, would work later and could tie into what lies beyond The Walking Dead: Dead City season 2.

              Rick's Walking Dead Show Can Reveal More Grimes Children   New details about Rick Grimes' situation with the CRM mean that he could have had more children after his exit from The Walking Dead in season 9.

To avoid falling into a premature reboot to keep the franchise thriving, a legacy spinoff set in the future with The Walking Dead’s children could be a fresh way to maintain the franchise’s momentum. A change from spinoffs about well-established main adult characters can also help the franchise avoid regurgitating storylines, which is one of Dead City’s biggest problems. Instead, a legacy spinoff would be rooted in familiarity through its characters but expand on their growth, especially if steadfast communities are able to fortify as the New Commonwealth and Alexandria suggest they do at the end of the main series.

Placing the children in a future setting and aging them up will also be a nod to the comic series’ ending and, by extension, Carl’s comic story and legacy. For context, in the comics, the Commonwealth becomes a well-run, high-functioning community with Carl as a major protagonist. A Walking Dead legacy spinoff could highlight how this type of community is achieved, tying into the end of the main show. Plus, it can amplify how the sacrifices and actions of the children’s parents shaped what their post-apocalyptic worlds could be, especially since they were born after the zombie outbreak.

The Walking Dead Has Been Slowly Building A “Next Generation” Cast The Walking Dead’s spinoffs already introduced multiple children who could make up a legacy cast.

As if the cut ending for the series finale wasn’t enough, the franchise has already been building up its “next generation” cast throughout its various spinoffs. Judith and RJ aside, Gracie and Coco from the main show, Mo from Fear The Walking Dead, Ginny and Hershel from The Walking Dead: Dead City, and Laurent from Daryl Dixon all represent the potential for a legacy spinoff. Because all these children are linked to major franchise characters, a show about the kids would be an ultimate bridge between all the franchise’s spinoffs.

While casting could change, current gaps can be filled by the existing actors and future stories can unfold in tandem with older cast counterparts. Untold or under-developed storylines for each child can also be explored through their childhoods’ impacts on their adult lives, such as Judith’s actions after losing her brother and her parents leaving, Coco’s experience after learning more about Rosita, or Mo’s adaption to Alexandria. The Walking Dead legacy spinoff would allow the franchise to creatively continue the apocalyptic story without relying on overused storylines or rebuilding a narrative around entirely new characters.

The next upcoming spinoff, The Walking Dead: The Ones Who Live, premieres February 25, 2024, on AMC and AMC+.
