10 Best TV Shows To Binge Watch Right Now

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2200 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

With an abundance of streaming platforms, there are countless binge-worthy TV shows to choose from, spanning various genres. The best shows to binge-watch are highly reviewed and often designed for viewers who want to watch entire seasons in one sitting. Bingeable praised for their engaging storytelling and seamless connection between episodes, making them feel like long movies.

Literature Review

From Loki and Suits to Sex Education and Black Flag, there are many great TV shows across all genres to binge-watch right now.

With more streaming platforms opening up and competing, incredible amounts of new binge-worthy TV shows are becoming available to audiences. It can be difficult to decide which show to watch with the overwhelming amount of content, with series ranging from dramas to comedies and everything in between. Binge-watching shows became a new outlet for escapism, offering to momentarily disconnect from everyday life and immerse in the unique stories offered. The best shows currently on TV are critically acclaimed and highly reviewed, and many are made to cater to viewers who want to binge-watch entire seasons — often in a single sitting. The most bingeable shows are full of foreshadowing and teases that make rewatching a must, and connect so well that many feel like an incredibly long movie rather than a show. These shows range from comedy and drama to horror and superhero fun. Here are the best series to binge right now, including some of the best TV shows of 2023.

The 50 Best Miniseries To Watch In A Weekend What are the best miniseries to binge over a weekend? Some great short series deliver all the power of a great TV show in a fraction of the time.

10 True Detective (2014- ) An Anthology Series Feels New Every Season

True Detective
Anthology Mystery

Release Date January 12, 2014 Cast Matthew McConaughey , Woody Harrelson , Colin Farrell , Rachel McAdams , Taylor Kitsch , Mahershala Ali , Carmen Ejogo , Michelle Monaghan , Michael Potts , Ray Fisher , Jodie Foster Seasons 3 Writers Nic Pizzolatto

It’s not often that a show that’s been running for a decade makes for a good binge because there would just be too many episodes. True Detective is different, however, because, in that time, there have only been four seasons of the show. It’s also an anthology series, which means a binge-watch could be a very different experience. Each season takes on a new mystery and criminal case being investigated by detectives. Each season also has incredible casts, starting with Woody Harrelson and Matthew McConaughey in the first season. Binge watchers can watch the whole show or simply invest in one season at a time and have equally enjoyable experiences as the story twists and the mystery unfolds. Available to Stream on MAX

9 Gen V (2023–) The Boys’ X-Men-Spoofing Spinoff Is Just As Difficult To Stop Watching

Gen V
TV-MA Action Comedy Drama Superhero

Release Date September 29, 2023 Cast Lizzie Broadway , Jaz Sinclair , Chance Perdomo , Maddie Phillips , London Thor , Derek Luh , Asa Germann , Shelley Conn , Patrick Schwarzenegger Seasons 1 Writers Craig Rosenberg

The Boys’ universe expands with the college-set superhero story Gen V. The spinoff focuses on a group of aspiring supes attending a prestigious college for would-be heroes only to discover a dark secret lurking beneath the surface. Gen V carries on the outrageous tone of the original series with plenty of dark humor and blood in each episode. However, it also stands on its own with a new ensemble of memorable characters and the ability to tackle topical subjects inside of an exciting superhero story. The first season of the show ended with a conclusion that bleeds right into The Boys season 4. Available to stream on Prime Video

8 The Fall Of The House Of Usher (2023) Mike Flanagan’s Take On Edgar Allen Poe Is A Chilling And Unmissable Binge-Watching Experience

The Fall of the House of Usher
TV-MA Drama Horror

Release Date October 12, 2023 Cast Carla Gugino , Bruce Greenwood , Mary McDonnell , Carl Lumbly , Samantha Sloyan , T’Nia Miller , Rahul Kohli , Kate Siegel , Sauriyan Sapkota , Zach Gilford Seasons 1 Writers Mike Flanagan

The collaboration between Mike Flanagan and Netflix is an intense horror story inspired by the writing of Edgar Allan Poe. The Fall of the House of Usher tells the story of a modern dynasty, a family who built an empire in the pharmaceutical world and now must confront a dark family history that threatens to destroy them all. The show has the feel of a horror version of Succession with a dysfunctional and powerful family of flawed people and the nightmares that befall them. The fact the single-season miniseries is self-contained makes it especially easy to binge.

Available to stream on Netflix

7 The Bear (2022–) Carmy’s Struggles In The Restaurant Game Are Easy For Viewers To Lose Themselves In

The Bear
Comedy Drama

Release Date June 23, 2022 Cast Jeremy Allen White , Ebon Moss-Bachrach , Ayo Edebiri , Lionel Boyce , Liza Colón-Zayas , Abby Elliott , Oliver Platt Seasons 2 Writers Christopher Storer

The Bear remains one of the most acclaimed shows on television right now with 13 Emmy nominations for its first season. Season 2 arrived on Hulu this summer and continued the acclaim for the show’s funny, heartbreaking, and intense look behind the scenes of a restaurant in Chicago. With The Bear season 3 confirmed, now is a perfect time to revisit the show or finally catch the latest season. This is especially true as The Bear season 2 includes a memorable Christmas episode that is nerve-wracking, hilarious, sad, and a brilliant episode of television. Available to stream on Hulu

6 Our Flag Means Death (2022–2023) The Best Pirate-Themed Binge-Watch On The Seven Seas

Our Flag Means Death
Adventure Comedy Romance Biography

Release Date March 3, 2022 Cast Rhys Darby , Con O’Neill , Rory Kinnear , Matthew Maher , Samson Kayo , Ewen Bremner , David Fane , Nathan Foad , Taika Waititi , Guz Khan , Vico Ortiz , Joel Fry , Kristian Nairn Seasons 2 Writers Eliza Jiménez Cossio , Zayre Ferrer , David Jenkins

Our Flag Means Death took one of the strangest true stories imaginable and turned it into a hilarious comedy with a surprising amount of heart. The story follows Stede Bonnet, a former English aristocrat who leaves his life of luxury to become a “gentleman pirate,” eventually crossing paths with the infamous Blackbeard and forming an unexpected romance. Season 2 of the show explored the aftermath of that charming romance falling apart and continued to balance the show’s quirky humor with that sweet connection between the characters. Though the series wasn’t renewed for a third season, it’s still a great binge-watch.

Available to stream on Max

5 Sex Education (2019–2023) One Of The Best British Comedies Is Also One Of The Best Binge-Watches

Sex Education
TV-MA Comedy Drama

Release Date January 11, 2019 Cast Gillian Anderson , Ncuti Gatwa , Tanya Reynolds , Aimee Lou Wood , Alistair Petrie , Kedar Williams-Stirling , Connor Swindells , Asa Butterfield , Emma Mackey , Patricia Allison Seasons 3 Writers Laurie Nunn , Sophie Goodhart

Sex education is one of the best representations of British humor, highlighting the thrills and challenges of teenage sexuality. Set in the fictional town Moordale, the show is centered around Otis Milburn (Asa Butterfield), an unpopular socially awkward teenager, who finds that his gateway to becoming “a cool kid” is to open an underground sex therapy clinic for his classmates, using the information he learns from his mother, sex therapist Jean Milburn (Gillian Anderson). The show is known for creating a safe space to explore issues around intimacy, sex, and relationships, and with the neatly-wrapped 4 season story makes Sex Education perfect for binge-watching. Available On Netflix

4 Percy Jackson and the Olympians (2023-) Rick Riordan Brings Greek Myth To The Modern Day

Percy Jackson & the Olympians
TV-PG Action Adventure Fantasy

Release Date December 20, 2023 Cast Walker Scobell , Leah Sava Jeffries , Aryan Simhadri , Jason Mantzoukas , Megan Mullally , Glynn Turman , Adam Copeland , Virginia Kull , Lance Reddick Seasons 1 Writers Rick Riordan , Jonathan E. Steinberg

Inspired by the Rick Riordan series of novels of the same name, Percy Jackson and the Olympians was one of the most highly anticipated series added to Disney+ in 2023. Riordan originally developed the story of Percy, a boy with ADHD and Dyslexia who just happened to be the son of the Greek god Poseidon, for his son, who wanted to feel as though he belonged. The universe grew from there. The universe is a fascinating one and the Disney+ series makes it a treat to watch with impressive set pieces and visuals, and a cast that truly embodies the characters of Riordan’s novels. The first season serves as an introduction to Percy’s world as he finds out the truth about his absent father and adjusts to life as a demigod, complete with a quest to find Zeus’ missing lightning bolt. Available to Stream on Disney+

3 The Power (2023- ) The World’s Power Dynamic Shifts Thanks To Electrocution

The Power
Not Yet Rated Drama Sci-Fi

Release Date March 31, 2023 Cast Toni Collette , Auli’i Cravalho , John Leguizamo , Toheeb Jimoh , Ria Zmitrowicz , Halle Bush , Nico Hiraga , Heather Agyepong , Daniela Vega , Eddie Marsan Seasons 1

Based on the novel by Naomi Alderman, The Power takes an idea present in superhero stories without making a superhero show: what happens when ordinary individuals suddenly develop extraordinary powers? The difference here is that those individuals are all women. Women can suddenly electrocute at will. In a world where the patriarchy is firmly established, this development causes the power structure to begin to shift. While parts of the show itself are slow moving, an incredible cast of Toni Collette and Auli-i Cravalho, among others, combined with this combustible concept makes it a show viewers just don’t want to stop watching. Available to Stream on Prime Video

2 Beef (2023- ) Road Rage Makes For Compelling Character Arcs

Beef
Not Yet Rated Comedy Drama

Cast Steven Yeun , Ali Wong , David Choe , Young Mazino , Joseph Lee , Patti Yasutake Seasons 1

Road rage incidents in fiction often go one of two ways - played for laughs in a quick scene in a comedy or spark a violent path in a thriller. Beef finds a unique way to combine those ideas to craft a compelling story that is funny, dramatic, and highly entertaining. Steven Yeun and Ali Wong star as a pair involved in a road rage incident that just cannot let the circumstances go. Instead of moving on, the two allow their anger to fester as a grudge builds between them. Beef’s first season earned a 98% on Rotten Tomatoes thanks to the performances of the two and the impeccable writing of the series. The first season of Beef (and its cast) picked up a slew of awards at the Emmys, Golden Globes, and Critics’ Choice Television Award ceremonies. Available to Stream on Netflix

1 Loki Tom Hiddleston Leads The Most Bingeable Marvel Disney+ Show

Loki
Adventure Action Fantasy Superhero

Release Date June 11, 2021 Cast Tom Hiddleston , Richard E. Grant , Erika Coleman , Gugu Mbatha-Raw , Sophia Di Martino , Owen Wilson , Wunmi Mosaku , Sasha Lane Seasons 2 Writers Michael Waldron , Eric Martin

After starting out as Thor’s troublemaking brother back in Phase 1, Loki has become one of the most popular MCU characters and the lead of his own acclaimed series. While some Marvel Disney+ show have had mixed reactions, Loki continues to be praised as an exciting adventure through time and space. Loki season 2 dealt with the fallout of the death of He Who Remains and the multiverse madness while also providing big implications for the wider cinematic universe. While it requires a little knowledge of the MCU, the mix of fantasy sci-fi and laugh-out-loud comedy makes Loki easily among the most bingeable shows currently on TV. Available to stream on Disney+
