10 Longest Gaps Between Movie Sequels

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Disney capitalized on its popular movies by releasing sequels several decades after the original films, setting the record for the longest gap between sequels. A sequel to the iconic film Easy Rider was released after 43 years, but was poorly received. The longest gap ever between live-action sequels was 54 years, as seen with Mary Poppins Returns, which successfully maintained the charm of the original.

Literature Review

While successful movies usually have sequels quickly greenlit, there are some examples of decades-long gaps between one movie and the next.

It can appear like every popular movie quickly churned out a follow-up before audiences even had time to take in the first film, however, there were some examples of movies having decades-long gaps between sequels. In the world of animation, this was easier to do as characters didn’t age and sequels could pick up right where they left off without any issues, although live-action movies have also returned after seriously long breaks. Sometimes these sequels recast their long-dormant characters, but other times the original actor returned. The undisputed champion of long-after-the-fact sequels was The Walt Disney Company which, particularly in the late 1990s and early 2000s, capitalized on their most popular movies to release a series of sequels several decades after the films had been released. While there are recent examples of long gaps between sequels such as Blade Runner 2049 and Top Gun: Maverick, there are other films that had even longer breaks before coming back. Shockingly, there have been direct sequels to movies made 40, 50, and even more than 60 years since the last film.

10 The Shining (1980) & Doctor Sleep (2019) 39 years

Doctor Sleep

Release Date October 30, 2019 Director Mike Flanagan Cast Carl Lumbly , Ewan McGregor , Bruce Greenwood , Emily Alyn Lind , Rebecca Ferguson , Alex Essoe , Kyliegh Curran , Zahn McClarnon Runtime 153 minutes

While the novel’s writer Stephen King was not a fan of Stanley Kubrick’s adaptation of The Shining that did not stop the film from becoming one of the most iconic horror movies ever. Considering both the book and the film’s acclaimed legacy, King wrote a sequel called Doctor Sleep that was adapted and released 39 years after The Shining. Doctor Sleep paid homage to the book and movie its story originated from and followed Ewan McGregor as a grown-up Danny Torrance who’s been traumatized by what occurred to himself and his family had the Overlook Hotel so many years before.

9 One Hundred And One Dalmatians (1961) & 101 Dalmatians II: Patch’s London Adventure (2003) 42 years

101 Dalmatians (1961) Animation Adventure

Release Date January 25, 1961 Director Wolfgang Reitherman , Hamilton Luske , Clyde Geronimi Cast Rod Taylor , J. Pat O’Malley , Betty Lou Gerson , Martha Wentworth

Disney’s 1961 movie One Hundred and One Dalmatians acted as the starting point for an entire franchise with an animated series, live-action remake, and even a spin-off villain origin film. With enduring popularity, the story would also receive a direct-to-video sequel 43 years after the original called 101 Dalmatians II: Patch’s London Adventure. Focusing on Patch, the loneliest of all the puppies, left behind as the Radcliffe’s moved to their “Dalmatian Plantation”, who found himself thrust into a wild adventure, 101 Dalmatians II: Patch’s London Adventure was one of the best of Disney’s direct-to-video sequels.

8 Easy Rider (1969) & Easy Rider: The Ride Back (2012) 43 years

The motorbike-riding protagonists of Easy Rider became countercultural icons when the film was first released in 1969. Truly a touchstone for an entire generation, Easy Rider acted as a representation of the rising hippie movement, new attitudes toward drug use, and the free-love lifestyle of the era. A complete product of its time, some may be surprised to learn a sequel called Easy Rider: The Ride Back was released 43 years later. With none of the original cast or crew involved, Easy Rider: The Ride Back was badly received and lacked the edgy bite of the original.

7 Lady And The Tramp (1955) & Lady And The Tramp II: Scamp’s Adventure (2001) 46 years

While aspects of the animated musical romance Lady and the Tramp did not age well, the film was still a Disney classic that provided iconic moments, like the famous spaghetti kiss. With such a strong legacy, Disney released a sequel 46 years after the original in 2001 titled Lady and the Tramp II: Scamp’s Adventure. Mimicking many aspects of the first film, Lady and Tramp’s only son Scamp ran away from home and found love with a rival gang member named Angel. Sadly, Lady and the Tramp II: Scamp’s Adventure felt unnecessary and was a poor addition to the series.

6 Peter Pan (1953) to Return to Never Land (2002) 49 years

Peter Pan

Release Date February 5, 1953 Director Hamilton Luske , Clyde Geronimi , Wilfred Jackson Cast Bobby Driscoll , Kathryn Beaumont , Hans Conried , Bill Thompson , Heather Angel , Paul Collins Runtime 77 minutes

The story of Wendy and her two brothers traveling to the magical island of Never Land with the boy who never grew up in Peter Pan was a Disney classic that received a sequel nearly half a century later. Return to Never Land was released in 2002, 49 years after the original, and featured Wendy’s daughter being kidnapped by Captain Hook and in need of rescuing from Peter Pan. Sadly, Return to Never Land featured forgettable songs and was mostly a retread of the events already explored in a more compelling way in Peter Pan.

5 Cinderella (1950) & Cinderella II: Dreams Come True (2002) 52 years

The timeless tale of the mistreated sister turned princess Cinderella was first released by Disney in 1950 and it would be 52 years before her story was followed up in a direct-to-video sequel called Cinderella II: Dreams Come True. This collection of anthology stories would abandon the classic traditional animation style of Cinderella in favor of an off-putting digital style. Cinderella II: Dreams Come True received a negative reception when it was released and there was a perception it felt more like the remnants of an abandoned television series than a true sequel to the classic film.

4 The Killer Shrews (1959) & Return Of The Killer Shrews (2012) 53 years

The Killer Shrews was a little-known independent science fiction movie about a group of researchers trapped on an island under siege by their giant, venomous, mutant test subjects that received a direct sequel more than 50 years later. The film starred James Best as Captain Thorne Sherman who returned after 53 years for the follow-up Return of the Killer Shrews in 2012. One of the longest gaps ever between live-action sequels, Return of the Killer Shrews maintained the campy B-movie feeling of the original and was a fun celebration of 1950s low-budget horror movies.

3 Mary Poppins (1964) & Mary Poppins Returns (2018) 54 years

Mary Poppins Returns

Release Date December 19, 2018 Director Rob Marshall Cast Lin-Manuel Miranda , Emily Blunt , Angela Lansbury , Meryl Streep , Joel Dawson , Julie Walters , Nathanael Saleh , Colin Firth , Emily Mortimer , Ben Whishaw , Pixie Davies , Dick Van Dyke Runtime 2h 10m

Julie Andrews’ performance as the quintessential English nanny lead of Mary Poppins became an instant classic when it was released in 1964 and Emily Blunt had big shoes to fill when she took over the role for the direct sequel Mary Poppins Returns in 2018. Released 54 years after the original, Mary Poppins Returns represented the longest gap ever between live-action sequels. An enjoyable follow-up, Mary Poppins Returns maintained the charm of the originals and proved the character still had the power to delight children all these years later.

2 Fantasia (1940) & Fantasia 2000 (1999) 59 years

The musical anthology Fantasia acted as a spectacular showcase of Disney magic when it was first released in 1940, a landmark moment in animation it blended classical music and fantastical imagery in work that proved animation could connect with adults and children. The legacy of the film was so great that a sequel called Fantasia 2000 was produced almost 60 years later in 1999. As the first feature-length animated film to be released on IMAX, Fantasia 2000 paid homage to its original with its own groundbreaking achievement and stood as a worthy follow-up to the Disney classic.

1 Bambi (1942) & Bambi 2 (2006) 63 Years

Bambi
G

Release Date August 14, 1942 Director James Algar , Samuel Armstrong , David Hand Cast Hardie Albright , Stan Alexander , Bobette Audrey , Peter Behn , Thelma Boardman , Janet Chapman

The tale of a young deer growing up in the forest, Bambi addressed the topic of death in a way that Disney previously never had when it was released in 1942. The heartbreaking death of Bambi’s mother stood as one of the saddest moments in all of cinema and the sequel Bambi II explored the young deer’s relationship with their father, The Great Prince of the Forest. Bambi II holds the record for the longest gap ever between sequels (via Collider) and featured a narrative where Bambi gained confidence after the untimely passing of their mother.
