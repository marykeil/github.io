Why It s Not The Air Force In Masters Of The Air

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

“Masters of the Air” focuses on the U.S. Army Air Forces during World War II, not the Air Force as we know it today. The U.S. Army Air Forces eventually evolved into the modern Air Force, with its own individual branch due to increasing size and power. Some characters in “Masters of the Air” may have served in the Air Force after World War II, as the U.S. Army Air Forces transitioned into the Air Force.

Literature Review

Masters of the Air dives into the world of the U.S. Army Air Forces, not the Air Force as audiences know it today, but why does this difference exist?

While many may believe that Masters of the Air depicts men who served in the Air Force, the truth of the matter is that the war drama portrays the U.S. Army Air Forces instead, and there is certainly a difference between the two. Apple TV+’s Masters of the Air is a drama series set in the midst of World War II. In particular, it delves into the lives and experiences of the 100th Bombardment Group, or the “Bloody Hundredth.” The show is a companion to Tom Hanks and Steven Spielberg’s Band of Brothers and The Pacific.

Masters of the Air tells several stories at once, both big and small. While the series succeeds at exploring its characters in personal detail, it also sets out to tell a larger story about the 100th Bomb Group, and beyond that, provides context about the American side of World War II. In this way, it is easy to become invested in the story, but on the flip side, it is also very simple to overlook certain specific details. For one thing, Masters of the Air is not about the Air Force as we know it today.

The Air Force Wasn’t Formed Until After World War 2

While today’s audiences are familiar with a branch of the United States military called the Air Force, the story Masters of the Air is telling includes an earlier version of that called the United States Army Air Forces. The modern Air Force was not created until 1947, and Masters of the Air starts in 1943, presumably going until the end of World War II in 1945. Therefore, the Air Force as audiences know it today isn’t present in Masters of the Air, however the series is a strong example of how the Air Force developed over time.

Before the Air Force was its own branch in the military, it was known as the United States Army Air Forces. Essentially, this means that the segment of the military dedicated to air combat was not its own individual branch, but instead, was a part of the Army branch. The U.S. Army Air Forces became its own branch in 1947, but it was first invented and added to the Army branch in 1907. Over the years, what is now known as the Air Force went through many different names and changes, however the Air Force was its final evolution.

How The Air Force Is Different To The Army Air Forces

The most significant difference between the Air Force and the U.S. Army Air Forces is that the Air Force constitutes its own branch, while the U.S. Army Air Forces are a part of the larger Army branch. During the 1940s, the U.S. Army Air Forces was the third segment of the Army branch, which also included the Army Ground Services and the United States Army Service of Supply. However, besides this main change, the Air Force and the U.S. Army Air Forces are not very different at all. In fact, they are essentially the same thing.

The Air Force is simply the modern version of the U.S. Army Air Forces with the only notable difference between them being their name. The Air Force began in 1907 and went through many names, from Aeronautical Division, Signal Corps to U.S. Army Air Corp. Over the years, the Air Force’s name changed because the technology became more advanced and the branch grew bigger via different groups merging to form a singular Air Force. In this way, the Air Force present in Masters of the Air is just like the modern Air Force, but with a different name.

Did Any Masters Of The Air Characters Serve In The Air Force?

Although the men depicted in Masters of the Air are part of the U.S. Army Air Forces, it is possible that they served in the modern Air Force later in life. One individual that definitely did this was the series’ main character, Gale “Buck” Cleven, played by Austin Butler. After World War II, Cleven served in both the Korean War and the Vietnam War. By those times, the U.S. Army Air Forces was decidedly the Air Force. Furthermore, Cleven may not have been the only member of the 100th to see the Air Force in its final form.

Ultimately, the difference between the Air Force and the U.S. Army Air Forces is incredibly subtle. Though audiences may be unsure when they hear the term U.S. Army Air Forces in Masters of the Air, they can be assured that it is not a mistake. The U.S. Army Air Forces is just an earlier version of the Air Force. In the end, Masters of the Air is just a peek into the little-known history of the United States Air Force.
