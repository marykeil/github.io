10 Times TV Actors We re Replaced After Filming Had Already Started

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Actors on TV shows can be replaced for various reasons, such as changes to the show or contractual obligations. Tamzin Merchant was initially cast as Daenerys Targaryen in Game of Thrones but was replaced by Emilia Clarke after the unaired pilot. Lisa Kudrow was originally cast on Frasier but was fired and later found success on Friends.

Literature Review

For reasons like contractual obligations, onset tensions, or just bad luck, plenty of TV actors have been replaced after filming had already started.

Sometimes an actor gets cast on a television series and after a few seasons something comes up and their role gets recast, however, in other instances, they were replaced much quicker and it was only after a very short amount of filming that they were forced to say goodbye. This can happen for several different reasons, often actors get replaced due to changes from an unaired pilot which meant aspects of the series were reshuffled or altered. Other times, contractual obligations actually stop the actor from taking up a role despite both them and the showrunners wanting them for the part. There are some notable examples of replaced actors on major TV series, such as Anita Barone as Carol on Friends who unusually played the part in just one episode and was replaced in all future appearances. Another Friends actress with a similar story was Lisa Kudrow, who had originally landed a major part on a different sitcom, Frasier, but in the end, was not the right fit for the part. There are lots of circumstances that explain TV actors being replaced after filming had already started.

10 Tamzin Merchant As Daenerys Targaryen Game of Thrones (2011 - 2019)

Game Of Thrones
Drama Action Adventure

Release Date April 11, 2011 Cast Emilia Clarke , Lena Headey , Richard Madden , Michelle Fairley , Kit Harington , Maisie Williams , Alfie Allen , Peter Dinklage Seasons 8

It is hard to imagine anybody except Emilia Clarke in the role of Daenerys Targaryen, however, the Mother of Dragons was originally portrayed by Tamzin Merchant in the unaired Game of Thrones pilot. Merchant, known for roles in The Tudors and Salem, was just one of many actors to be recast after the disastrous Game of Thrones pilot, which would have killed the show before it began, was given some much needed changes. In this pilot, Merchant as Daenerys appeared as a much less sympathetic character and the Game of Thrones creators rewrote her backstory for Clarke.

9 Riff Regan As Willow Rosenberg Buffy the Vampire Slayer (1997 - 2003)

Buffy The Vampire Slayer
Action Comedy Drama Horror Supernatural

Release Date March 10, 1997 Cast Sarah Michelle Gellar , Nicholas Brendon , Alyson Hannigan , Charisma Carpenter , Dan Rubin , David Boreanaz , Seth Green , James Marsters Seasons 7

Joss Whedon’s beloved series Buffy the Vampire Slayer underwent several changes from its unaired pilot to its eventual premiere, with the most notable being that Riff Regan played the role of Buffy’s best friend Willow Rosenberg. The role of Willow would later be recast and go to Alyson Hanniger who appeared as a main character in all 144 episodes of Buffy. Previously, Regan appeared in minor roles in television series like Rosanne and Blossom, as well as a recurring role on Sisters as Young Georgie, but none even came close to the cultural impact and popularity of Willow.

8 John Posey As Danny Tanner Full House (1987 - 1995)

Full House (US)
Comedy Family

Release Date September 22, 1987 Cast Bob Saget , John Stamos , Dave Coulier , Candace Cameron Bure , Jodie Sweetin , Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen , Lori Loughlin , Andrea Barber , Scott Weinger Seasons 8

For long-time fans of Full House, it’s difficult to envision anyone except Bob Saget in the role of single dad Danny Tanner. However, when Saget was initially unavailable for the part, actor and comedian John Posey took up the role in the unaired pilot episode. Posey was brought in and filming had even started, but due to Saget becoming available once again he was quickly dropped and recast. In an interview with Yahoo TV, Posey stated that “nobody said there were problems” and it appeared it was just a situation of bad luck, as Saget was always the first choice to play the part. “Nobody said there were problems.” - John Posey

7 Lisa Kudrow As Roz Doyle Fraiser (1993 - 2004)

Frasier
Comedy

Release Date September 16, 1993 Cast Kelsey Grammer , Jane Leeves , David Hyde Pierce , Peri Gilpin , John Mahoney , Jack Cutmore-Scott , Anders Keith , Jess Salgueiro , Toks Olagundoye , Nicholas Lyndhurst Seasons 11

Lisa Kudrow was originally cast as the KACL producer Roz Doyle on Frasier but was fired from the series during the filming of the pilot. According to Frasier director James Burrows, Kudrow got fired from Frasier because Kudrow’s ethereal acting style did not work well against Frasier’s lead actor Kelsey Grammar’s strong personality. However, Kudrow’s firing from the series arguably worked in her favor as it freed up her availability to take up the role of Phoebe Buffay on Friends just a year later, where she was reunited with Burrows who directed episodes of both series.

6 Alex Borstein As Sookie St. James Gilmore Girls (2000 - 2007)

Gilmore Girls
Comedy Drama

Release Date October 5, 2000 Cast Lauren Graham , Scott Patterson , Sean Gunn , Keiko Agena , Matt Czuchry , Alexis Bledel , Yanic Truesdale , Kelly Bishop , Melissa McCarthy , Edward Herrmann , Liza Weil , Jared Padalecki , Milo Ventimiglia Seasons 7

As the best friend of Lorelai Gilmore on Gilmore Girls, Melissa McCarthy first gained recognition as Sookie St. James, but this was almost a much different story as the part initially went to Alex Borstein. Borstein played with part of Sookie, the head chef at the Independence Inn, in just the unaired pilot, and following Gilmore Girls receiving a full-series order Borstein was unable to be released from her contract with MAD TV. This meant Borstein had to be recast as Sookie and her scenes in the pilot were refilmed.

5 Amanda Walsh As Katie The Big Bang Theory (2007 - 2019)

The Big Bang Theory
Romance Comedy

Release Date September 24, 2007 Cast Kaley Cuoco , Johnny Galecki , Jim Parsons , Melissa Rauch , Mayim Bialik , Kunal Nayyar , Simon Helberg Seasons 12

The unaired pilot of The Big Bang Theory actually featured a different female lead than the character audiences would come to know as Penny Hofstadter. Instead, this part was taken up by Amanda Walsh as Kaite, a brash and sarcastic woman who stayed with Leonard after being made homeless by her old boyfriend. According to Digital Spy, audiences did not react well to Katie, who was mean and mocking toward Leonard and the rest of the cast. Instead, for the second The Big Bang Theory pilot, the character was replaced by Penny, who was much more light-hearted, sweet, and likable.

4 Mitchel Musso As Aang Avatar: The Last Airbender (2005 - 2008)

Avatar: The Last Airbender
Animation Adventure Action

Release Date February 21, 2005 Cast Mako , Dee Bradley Baker , Jack De Sena , Michaela Jill Murphy , Zach Tyler , Dante Basco , Mae Whitman Seasons 3

The unaired pilot of Avatar: The Last Airbender was a low-budget test episode made to pitch the series to Nickelodeon and featured Mitchel Musso, best known for Hannah Montanna and Phineas and Ferb, in the lead role of Aang. However, by the time Avatar was commissioned by Nickelodeon the voice actor for Aang had been replaced and he was instead voiced by Zach Tyler Eisen. Elsen would continue to voice the character for all three seasons of Avatar, as well as in video game adaptations.

3 Anita Barone As Carol Willick Friends (1994 - 2004)

Friends
Comedy Drama Sitcom

Release Date September 22, 1994 Cast Jennifer Aniston , Courteney Cox , Lisa Kudrow , Matt LeBlanc , Matthew Perry , David Schwimmer Seasons 10

One example of a recast character whose original actor already appeared on the series was Anita Barone as Carol Willick on Friends. Barone played the ex-wife of Ross Geller on just one episode of Friends before quitting as she wanted to pursue more full-time roles. The role of Carol was recast on Friends, and Jane Sibbett would take up the part for the remainder of the series, appearing in 15 episodes. Interestingly, Sibbett had been previously offered the main role of Rachel Green but was unable to accept it as she was pregnant at the time.

2 Rebecca Gayheart As Inara Firefly (2002)

Firefly
Sci-Fi Drama Adventure

Release Date September 20, 2002 Cast Ron Glass , Morena Baccarin , Alan Tudyk , Adam Baldwin , Summer Glau , Gina Torres , Nathan Fillion , Sean Maher , Jewel Staite Seasons 1

One of the secrets behind Firefly was that the role of Inara was originally played by Rebecca Gayheart. However, according to Showbiz Cheat Sheet, Gayheart did not have strong chemistry with the rest of the cast and was fired after just one day on the set of Firefly. The role was quickly recast and went to Morena Baccarin who would appear in all 14 episodes, as well as the 2005 follow-up movie Serenity. Gayheart also continued acting and took up roles in Nip/Tuck and most recently a minor role in Once Upon a Time in Hollywood.

1 Jordan Reid As Sweet Dee It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia (2005 - Present)

It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia
Comedy

Release Date August 4, 2005 Cast Charlie Day , Rob McElhenney , Kaitlin Olson , Glenn Howerton , Danny DeVito Seasons 16

In the original pilot of the long-running sitcom It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia an actress named Jordan Reid was cast as Sweet Dee. This pilot was shot before the FX series had even been commissioned and was made by the show’s stars Rob McElhenney, Glenn Howerton, and Charlie Day on a budget of less than $200. Reid was the girlfriend of McElhenney, and, by the time the series was commissioned, the two had split up and the part of Dee on Always Sunny was recast. The role eventually went to Kaitlin Olsen who coincidentally began a relationship with McElhenney and the two are now married. Sources: Yahoo TV, Digital Spy, Showbiz Cheat Sheet
