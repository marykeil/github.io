Wednesday Season 2 Is Continuing A TV Trend That s Been Frustrating For Years Now

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Season 2 of Wednesday may follow the disappointing trend of TV shows trying to be like movies, which hasn’t always worked. Jenna Ortega’s comments about the episodes feeling like mini-movies are worrying, as the pacing of season 1 was already great. Treating TV shows like movies has been a common issue with big franchises, and Wednesday could suffer if it adopts this trend.

Literature Review

Despite having a successful first season, Wednesday season 2 appears to be continuing a frustrating TV trend that has been going on for years.

After Wednesday’s successful season 1, a second season has been confirmed, but it looks like it will be continuing a TV trend that has been frustrating for years. Wednesday was one of Netflix’s biggest releases in 2022 as the show achieved mainstream recognition and nailed the more comedic horror style associated with the Addams family. The show received positive reviews and seems like it could be a big series for the streaming platform going forward. Wednesday season 2’s plot will likely pick up right where the first season left off, leaving the potential for the show to last several seasons.

Given how promising the show’s future looks, recent updates leave reason to be concerned that Wednesday may follow the same exhausting format of other TV shows over the past few years. Although the series mastered its pacing and style in its first season, there will be some changes when the show returns, and that may not be a good thing. Jenna Ortega’s comments on Wednesday season 2 suggest the series will improve in various areas and that there is plenty to be excited about. However, there was also one comment that could be an issue for Wednesday’s future.

Wednesday Season 2’s “Movie” Tease Continues A Disappointing TV Trend

Wednesday season 2’s movie tease suggests the series will be continuing a disappointing TV trend. Jenna Ortega’s Wednesday season 2 details indicated that the episodes will feel more like movies. Ortega said “Everything’s bigger [and] more action-packed. I think each episode will probably feel a little bit like a movie.” While the comments about more horror and character development are worth getting excited about, the show’s movie-like episodes aren’t entirely positive. Many TV shows in recent years have gone with this format, which hasn’t always worked, and Wednesday certainly doesn’t need to change its formula to continue succeeding.

Ortega’s comments don’t guarantee that the episodes will be longer, but it isn’t out of the question given Netflix’s history. Wednesday feeling like a series of mini-movies simply isn’t required, as the pacing of season 1 was already great. It may tamper with the show’s approach to storytelling and some episodes could feel detached from the rest of the series. The Wednesday season 2 reveals provided by Jenna Ortega offer a lot of encouragement to the show in terms of story and action, but her movie comments are worrying, as season 1 doesn’t warrant such big changes.

TV Shows Being Treated Like Movies Is A Common Issue With Big Franchises

TV shows getting the movie treatment isn’t a new occurrence and has become a common issue with big franchises. Disney+ has led to Marvel and Star Wars branching out into more TV shows, and while some have been popular and well-received, others have struggled. This is largely down to these IPs being forced into a TV format despite being better suited to movies. Star Wars has done a decent job at making most of their series work, but the likes of The Book of Boba Fett struggled and despite two great seasons, The Mandalorian season 3 dipped in quality.

Marvel has struggled more, often going for six-episode formats, leading to cramped stories and messy conclusions. Some have worked, with Loki and WandaVision being well-suited to TV, but others have notably suffered, like Secret Invasion. Netflix has also been guilty of producing movie-like TV, with each episode of Stranger Things season 4 lasting over an hour. This may differ from how Disney’s shows have adopted movie aspects, but it proves Wednesday could easily follow the same format. Although it has already become a big franchise, Wednesday could suffer by adopting this frustrating trend of treating TV shows like movies.
