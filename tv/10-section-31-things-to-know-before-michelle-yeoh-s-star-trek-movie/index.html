10 Section 31 Things To Know Before Michelle Yeoh s Star Trek Movie

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Star Trek: Section 31 is a secretive organization within Starfleet that operates autonomously and answers to no one, allowing them to commit reprehensible actions. Section 31 has a long history within the Star Trek universe, existing since the early days of Starfleet and playing a role in events such as the Dominion War and the Federation-Klingon War. The upcoming movie will follow Emperor Philippa Georgiou as she joins Section 31 and confronts her past sins, potentially leading to the downfall of the organization or the erasure of its existence.

Literature Review

Michelle Yeoh will return as Philippa Georgiou in Star Trek: Section 31, and here are 10 things to remember about Starfleet’s shadiest department.

As Michelle Yeoh’s Star Trek: Section 31 movie enters production, it’s worth revisiting everything known about Starfleet’s top-secret organization. The Star Trek: Discovery spinoff movie will see Emperor Philippa Georgiou (Michelle Yeoh) join Section 31, presumably after being returned to the 23rd century by the Guardian of Forever (Paul Gilfoyle). Except for Yeoh, Star Trek: Section 31’s cast is all new to the franchise, allowing the movie to reveal more about Starfleet’s most secretive organization. Section 31 originated in Star Trek: Deep Space Nine, introduced in DS9 season 6, episode 18, “Inquisition”. The covert agency attempted to recruit Dr. Julian Bashir (Alexander Siddig) as a member of Section 31, but he refused. Bashir’s commitment to exposing the controversial agency led to Section 31 being deemed a rogue organization in the 24th century. However, it later became clear that Section 31 existed long before the Dominion War. Some say that Section 31 may even have existed since the very foundation of Starfleet itself.

Section 31 Isn’t The Star Trek Movie Fans Want But It Hints At Franchise’s Future Section 31 may not be exactly what fans want, but Michelle Yeoh’s made-for-streaming Paramount+ movie points to what might be next for Star Trek.

10 Section 31’s Title Explained The organization takes its name from the Starfleet Charter.

The original Starfleet Charter is where Star Trek’s mission statement to “boldly go where no man has gone before” originated. However, it’s also where the shadowy intelligence agency got its name. Article 14, section 31 of the Starfleet Charter made allowances for extraordinary measures to be taken during times of extreme threat. Star Trek: DS9’s Dominion War was one such time of extreme threat, while Star Trek: Discovery’s Federation-Klingon War was another. Both wars presumably justified the use of Section 31’s more sinister methods. However, it also seems that Section 31 continues to operate outside of wartime.

9 Section 31 Has Always Been Part Of Starfleet Star Trek: Enterprise’s Malcolm Reed was a member of the organization.

In its final year, Star Trek: Enterprise revealed that an unnamed covert wing of Starfleet was operating in the 22nd century. While they were never explicitly named Section 31 in Enterprise season 4, a representative of the organization referred to the relevant section of the Starfleet Charter. Lt. Malcolm Reed (Dominic Keating) had previously served in Section 31 before he was assigned to the Enterprise NX-01. This established that from the very early days of humanity’s exploration of the universe, a secret group of intelligence operatives was quietly strategizing responses for potential hostile action.

8 Michael Burnham’s Parents Worked For Section 31 The Burnhams’ temporal experiments led to their deaths at the hands of the Klingons.

In the 23rd century, Section 31 experimented with time travel in direct response to the Klingon Empire’s temporal experimentation. Perhaps looking to avoid another Temporal Cold War, Section 31 hired Federation scientists Gabrielle (Sonja Sohn) and Mike Burnham (Kenric Green) to work on the Daedalus Project. They developed a working time suit, which became known as the Red Angel. However, it was believed lost after a Klingon attack which orphaned the young Michael Burnham (Sonequa Martin-Green). Section 31 operative Leland was inadvertently responsible for the fatal attack, due to an illegally acquired Klingon time crystal. The Klingon attack squad tracked the crystal to Doctari Alpha and killed Burnham’s father, Mike, while Gabrielle escaped the devastation with the prototype time suit. Given that the synopsis for Star Trek: Section 31 hints at Georgiou repenting for past sins, she may see avenging the death of her protégé’s father as one way to seek repentance.

7 Section 31 Has No Starfleet Oversight Section 31’s actions are accountable to nobody.

Section 31 is an autonomous agency that doesn’t have to answer to Starfleet for their often reprehensible actions. This allows Starfleet Command to retain plausible deniability for some of Section 31’s worst crimes. Some of these crimes include the attempted genocide of the Changelings and the framing of an innocent Romulan politician in Star Trek: Deep Space Nine. While those crimes are in Section 31’s future, there are hints in Star Trek: Strange New Worlds that the organization sanctioned the use of a biological augmentation during the Klingon War in the form of Dr. Joseph M’Benga’s super soldier serum.

6 Section 31’s NCIA-93 Type Starships Explained Starfleet’s covert agency has its own class of starship.

Section 31’s NCIA-93 starships were smaller than Crossfield class ships like the USS Discovery, making them perfect for use in covert operations. An NCIA-93 starship was capable of altering its appearance to blend into its surroundings. To maintain the disguise, Section 31’s ships were able to mask their warp signature so that they could easily pretend to be an asteroid or a piece of space debris. They had a triangular hull design which was similar to the Delta-type starships seen in multiple episodes of Star Trek: Enterprise. Captain Georgiou will likely be in command of such a vessel in Star Trek: Section 31. Section 31’s ships have a similar camouflage system to the Romulan drone ship from Star Trek: Enterprise, implying that they stole the technology during the Earth-Romulan War.

5 Philippa Georgiou Saved The Lives Of Ash Tyler, L’Rell, and their Son Star Trek: Discovery season 2, episode 3, “Point of Light”

In Star Trek: Discovery season 2, episode 3, “Point of Light”, Georgiou was sent to Qo’noS to tip the scales of power within the Klingon Empire. To help Chancellor L’Rell (Mary Chieffo) regain control of the Empire from a rival, Georgiou convinced L’Rell’s lover, the Klingon-Human hybrid Ash Tyler (Shazad Latif) to leave Qo’noS with their infant son. At a time when xenophobia was rife on the Klingon home world, someone like Ash was a political liability for L’Rell, whom Section 31 needed to remain in control of the Empire. Ash later joined Section 31, and may also return alongside Georgiou in the Star Trek: Section 31 movie.

4 Emperor Philippa Georgiou Doesn’t Officially Exist The Terran Emperor plays the role of her Prime counterpart.

In Star Trek: Discovery season 1, episode 14, “The War Without, the War Within”, Terran Emperor Philippa Georgiou took the role of her Prime Universe counterpart. It was similar to how Jason Isaacs’ Captain Lorca infiltrated Starfleet from the Mirror Universe earlier in the season. Although the crew of the USS Discovery and various high-ranking figures within Starfleet knew the truth, Georgiou continued to play the role of the retired Georgiou during her work with Section 31. The real Georgiou had been killed at the very start of the Federation-Klingon War, but Starfleet covered this up by claiming the captain of the USS Shenzhou had been rescued from a Klingon prison ship.

3 The Control Malfunction Explained Section 31’s A.I. went rogue and almost destroyed the universe.

Following the Federation-Klingon War, Section 31’s threat-assessment system, Control, had a severe malfunction that took down a considerable portion of Section 31 personnel. The artificial intelligence launched a hostile takeover of Starfleet, and deployed an entire armada to destroy the USS Enterprise and USS Discovery. In an alternate future experienced by Gabrielle Burnham, Control used the sphere data aboard Discovery to become fully sentient. The evolved A.I. then went on to destroy all sentient life in the galaxy, forcing Gabrielle to return to the 23rd century to assist her daughter and Starfleet in defeating Control. The whole debacle led to a radical rethink of how Section 31 operated in the 23rd century.

2 Starfleet Took Control Of Section 31 In The 23rd Century Commander Ash Tyler was tasked with reforming the organization.

Captain Christopher Pike (Anson Mount) was furious at the human cost of Section 31’s reckless use of the Control system. Starfleet accepted Pike’s criticisms and committed to overhauling the intelligence organization by putting Ash Tyler in charge. While more transparency over Section 31’s actions was promised, it’s unclear exactly how that has manifested in the 23rd century. Section 31 didn’t appear in Star Trek: TOS, suggesting that they continued to operate in the shadows, despite their assurances to Pike at the end of Star Trek: Discovery season 2.

1 Section 31 Is A Myth By The Time Of Star Trek: Deep Space Nine Was Philippa Georgiou responsible for the organization’s erasure?

Over a century later, Star Trek: Deep Space Nine’s Dr. Julian Bashir and Captain Benjamin Sisko (Avery Brooks) learned of the existence of Section 31. When Sisko investigated Bashir’s claims about a secretive Starfleet intelligence agency, he was obstructed at every turn. The organization was led by the enigmatic Luther Sloan (William Sadler), who twice attempted to recruit Dr. Bashir. How Section 31 went from being a well-known arm of Starfleet intelligence to a deeply buried secret is a question that is yet to be answered. Perhaps Philippa Georgiou had a key role in erasing Section 31’s existence from the Starfleet history books. Michelle Yeoh’s Star Trek: Section 31 movie will likely bridge the gap between the end of Star Trek: Discovery season 2 and Star Trek: Deep Space Nine. With Philippa Georgiou confronting the sins of her past, she may choose to bring down the morally bankrupt Section 31 once and for all. This would explain why Section 31 was a deeply buried secret by the time of the 24th century. Alternatively, Georgiou could do something so controversial that Starfleet chooses to bury any and all information pertaining to her actions. Whatever happens, the Paramount+ exclusive movie should provide a fascinating glimpse inside Starfleet’s shadiest department. Star Trek: Section 31 is now filming for release on Paramount+.

Star Trek: Section 31
In this Paramount+ exclusive movie event, Michelle Yeoh returns as Emperor Philippa Georgiou, who was first introduced in Star Trek: Discovery Season 1. Star Trek: Section 31 centers on Yeoh’s character as she faces her past sins and is recruited by Starfleet’s secret division that protects the United Federation of Planets.
