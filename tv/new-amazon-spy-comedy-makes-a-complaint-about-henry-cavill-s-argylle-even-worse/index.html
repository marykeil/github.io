Mr. & Mrs. Smith effectively utilizes its ensemble cast, giving them interesting storylines and making their characters more compelling. Mr. & Mrs. Smith subverts action comedy tropes, creating a more entertaining experience compared to Argylle’s exaggerated antics.

A new Amazon action comedy series succeeds in one certain aspect where Matthew Vaughn’s Argylle fails. Following the release of Argylle on February 2, 2024, Vaughn’s latest spy comedy has received poor critical reception, leading to an underwhelming $18 million opening weekend at the box office for the $200 million film. Vaughn has already announced plans to crossover Argylle with his Kingsman franchise as well as create various Argylle sequels and prequels across both film and television. Based on the poor reviews and box office performance, however, Vaughn may not be able to match his franchise ambitions with their financial expectations.

Argylle features a star-studded ensemble cast led by Henry Cavill, John Cena, Samuel L. Jackson, Dua Lipa, Bryan Cranston, Catherine O’Hara, Ariana DeBose, Sam Rockwell, and Bryce Dallas Howard. Just about half of these celebrity actors, however, only appear in the movie for a brief amount of time, which essentially minimizes their highly marketed performances to brief and hardly impactful cameos. This has been a major complaint of the new Vaughn film, especially since the Argylle trailer that played relentlessly in theaters throughout December and January implied a much larger presence of its main attractions.

Amazon’s new action comedy series exceeds where Argylle fails due to the proper use of the Mr. & Mrs. Smith cast. The series features leading roles from Maya Erskine and Donald Glover, who also co-created the adaptation of the 2005 blockbuster starring Angelina Jolie and Brad Pitt. The Amazon series also stars Paul Dano, John Turturro, Ron Perlman, Alexander Skarsgård, Eiza Gonzalez, Wagner Moura, Parker Posey, Michaela Cowl, and Sarah Paulson, who rounds out its ensemble. Mr. & Mrs. Smith better utilizes its ensemble cast than Argylle does by making their characters interesting and giving them great storylines.

The main attraction, Henry Cavill, shows up slightly more, but is similarly disregarded for a large majority of the 136-minute film.

Argylle’s characters are mostly flat and one-dimensional throughout the film, essentially being used to push forward a convoluted and over-the-top plot. Dua Lipa, John Cena, and Ariana DeBose hardly have any lines in the movie. The main attraction, Henry Cavill, shows up slightly more, but is similarly disregarded for a large majority of the 136-minute film. Mr. & Mrs. Smith does employ a bit of a marketing trick with its use of Skarsgård and Gonzalez. However, the other members of its supporting cast are given robust material to work with that contributes greatly to the story in the long run.

Argylle’s Celebrity Cameos Only Add Fluff To Its Convoluted Plot Cavill, Cena, & Dua Lipa hardly appear in the movie

Given the sheer amount of star power that Argylle was able to attract, it’s surprising that Vaughn and screenwriter Jason Fuchs chose not to carve out more screen time for Cena, Cavill, and even Dua Lipa. Rather than going entirely meta with its Elly Conway twist, Argylle could have simply followed Cena and Cavill and established a great new franchise without all the additional misleading elements and characters. Cena and Cavill certainly could have carried Argylle as a one-two-punch action combo, which feels closer to what fans were expecting from the movie based on the trailer.

To Argylle’s credit, the characters played by Sam Rockwell, Bryce Dallas Howard, and Bryan Cranston felt full and realized.

In handling and balancing its ensemble cast, the advantage that Mr. & Mrs. Smith over Argylle was its much longer runtime, spanning 8 total episodes, each between 45–60 minutes. Some of the supporting characters’ arcs were able to build alongside Glover’s John and Erskine’s Jane characters, while other actors were effectively featured in only one episode. To Argylle’s credit, the characters played by Sam Rockwell, Bryce Dallas Howard, and Bryan Cranston felt full and realized, but the overall amount of potential left on the table with its star-studded cast is one of the biggest disappointments of Vaugh’s latest film.

Mr. & Mrs. Smith Enhances The Action Comedy Genre By Subverting Its Tropes Amazon series offers a grounded and relatable take on an action classic

Mr. & Mrs. Smith has received some backlash from fans of the original 2005 movie despite its Rotten Tomatoes Certified Fresh rating of 88%. While Argylle tries to jam-pack just about every action trope in the book, sometimes self-seriously and other times out of parody, Mr. & Mrs. Smith subverts many of these expected tropes intentionally with great creative impact. The innovative approach to the modern action comedy genre seen in Mr. & Mrs. Smith has ultimately found greater success with audiences than Argylle’s exaggerated and oftentimes senesless antics. As a result, Mr. & Mrs. Smith makes for a much more entertaining use of its star-studded ensemble cast.
