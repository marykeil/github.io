Snowfall The 15 Best Quotes

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Snowfall depicts a dangerous and violent world with a gritty realism as Franklin navigates the drug-fueled streets of Los Angeles in the 1980s. The series explores larger-scale issues, such as government involvement and the crack epidemic, while showcasing compelling and nuanced characters. The Snowfall quotes from Franklin and other main characters offer rousing, realistic insights into their struggles and motivations within the chaotic world they inhabit.

Literature Review

Despite the criminal exploits of the characters on Snowfall, they show their underlying humanity through insightful quotes, quips, and lines.

FX’s highly acclaimed crime drama Snowfall is full of dangerous twists and turns, and the Franklin Saint quotes hold a mirror up to his chaotic world. Snowfall tackles the infamous crack epidemic in Los Angeles in the 1980s by utilizing young Franklin as a budding drug dealer rising the ranks. Saint and his crew become powerful forces in South Central. However, Saint isn’t the only focus, as Snowfall chronicles how his life intersects with that of CIA Operative Teddy, Mexican luchador Gustavo, and a crime boss’ niece Lucia. Snowfall depicts a dangerous and violent world with a gritty realism as Franklin undergoes the trials and tribulations of navigating his newfound drug-fueled world. The series pulls no punches considering how it chronicles the government’s involvement, and the best episodes of Snowfall deal with larger-scale issues. Snowfall is loaded with compelling, nuanced characters, steeling them for life where only the strong survive. The resulting Snowfall quotes are often articulated in rousing, realistic quips from the main characters.

Snowfall: 10 Main Characters’ Arcs, Ranked Worst To Best Of all the main characters in Snowfall, whose arc and growth is the best to watch, and which ones leave something to be desired?

15 “I Tried To Do Sh** The Right Way, Went To The Other Side.” Franklin Saint — Season 1, Episode 1, “Pilot”

“Know What I Learned? The Game Is Rigged. It Ain’t Made For People Like Us, So You Know What? I’m Rewriting The Rules.”

The theme of the show can be distilled down into one of the most trenchantly observed Franklin Saint quotes from the series. Hinting at the oppressive systematic racism that has entrenched Franklin and other Black people in the wake of the crack epidemic during the 1980s, Franklin becomes an empowered self-agent unwilling to let society cheat him out of his pursuit of life, liberty, and happiness. Snowfall introduces Franklin’s personal goals and beliefs in the pilot episode, resulting in one of his most poignant quotes in the series.

14 “See, It’s Not Just About Being In Charge. It’s About Deciding How You Want To Be In Charge.” Teddy McDonald — Season 2, Episode 4, “Jingle Bell Rock”

CIA operative Teddy McDonald gives a shrewd piece of advice to Franklin with one of the best Snowfall quotes on how to expand his drug empire. Teddy opens the screed with “I have what some people might call control issues,” coming around to say, “I have to empower the people I choose to work with.” The hypnotic diatribe proves the long-game strategy Teddy maintains throughout the series, essentially warning Franklin to work smarter, not harder while showing the importance of delegating authority. It’s advice Franklin wisely takes.

13 “I’m A Black Man In America, God D**n It.” " Franklin Saint — Season 2, Episode 5, “Serpiente”

“Hell Yeah, I’m Being Paranoid! You Know, People Of Your Complexion Don’t Exactly Have The Best Record When It Comes To Dealing With People Like Me.”

Another one of the most quintessential Franklin Saint quotes that cuts to the heart of the show’s theme, the war on drugs and the Reagan-era crack epidemic left an entire generation of people feeling exactly the way Franklin expresses himself. While discussing the specifics of how Avi managed to get off even though his house is full of drugs and guns, Franklin levels the sobering line at Teddy. This hints at the unease Franklin feels in a society that disenfranchises minorities and favors those who are white while committing the same crimes.

12 “Say A Prayer, MOTHERF***ER!” Khadija Brown — Season 4, Episode 6, “Say a Little Prayer”

Khadijah Brown delivers one of the most showstopping Snowfall quotes with such forceful credibility in season 4 that it cannot be omitted from the fray. Becoming Franklin’s enemy after her daughter Tianna’s drive-by murder, Khadijah presses Fatback for Leon’s whereabouts. When he brazenly refuses, Khadijah delivers the iconic line before stabbing Fatback to death, ending Franklin’s formidable enforcer in a particularly brutal way. The title of the episode is named “Say a Little Prayer” for this moment.

11 “You Gonna Need To Get That Hate Out Your Heart.” Skully — Season 4, Episode 9, “Sleeping Dogs”

Once Tianna is murdered and Khadijah seeks vengeance, her husband Skully makes an impassioned plea, reinforcing their bond as one of the best couples from the show. Skully often delivers lewd, foul-mouthed rants, but here he shows a much softer and compassionate side in advising his wife. The quote not only proves Skully has a moral center beneath the rough exterior, but the advice happens to be 100% correct. Khadijah didn’t heed her husband’s advice and paid the ultimate price.

10 “Mija, You’re Smart, Innovative, Fierce. But You Can’t Do This Alone.” Mauricio Villanueva — Season 1, Episode 8, “Baby Teeth”

“You Need People You Can Count On. That’s What Helps You Prosper. That’s What Makes A Life.”

The head of the powerful Sinaloan drug empire, Mauricio Villanueva is an influential criminal who has his competition killed without an ounce of compunction. Yet, when it comes to his brilliant daughter Lucia, he gives a speech that couldn’t be more uplifting and results in one of the best Snowfall quotes. The polarity between Mauricio’s daring criminality and caring fatherly advice shows that even the worst villains have glimpses of heartfelt humanity. Lucia takes the words of wisdom and becomes a drug-running queen.

9 “I Didn’t Build All Of This Just To Make A Bunch Of Money And Leave, Abandon Our People.” Franklin Saint — Season 3, Episode 10, “Other Lives”

In a candid admission that proves where his heart lies, Franklin Saint talks to Alton and Cissy when they suggest running when things get dangerous. Loyal and dedicated to improving his community, Franklin’s morality shines through despite selling drugs. Franklin, like many of the characters in Snowfall, is deeply community-focused, despite his actions sometimes suggesting otherwise. The quote reinforces the integrity of Franklin’s true nature and unwillingness to turn his back on where he came from.

8 “If We Win This War We Can Change The Course Of History. And This War, We Can Win.” Teddy McDonald — Season 2, Episode 1, “Sightlines”

Stationed in Central America to fight the communist threat, Teddy has a unique perspective on Snowfall. He sees how the war on drugs intertwines with communism in the region, and funds insurgents under the table. Despite his shady business dealings and moral ambiguity, Teddy describes the trajectory of America’s future while voicing proud confidence in getting the job done. There’s no question Reagan’s war on drugs was a disaster, but Teddy believes in the cause. It’s a grandiose quote with huge global ramifications.

7 “If I Ain’t Wanna Be In This Game, I Wouldn’t Be And Neither Would You, Louise.” Jerome Saint — Season 5, Episode 7, “Lying in a Hammock”

“All That Matters Right Now Is That We Survive. That’s It.”

For many drug dealers, circumstances dictate and trap them. In Snowfall, Jerome Saint insists to his wife Louise that he takes responsibility for his actions and expresses where his true priorities lie. Jerome owns up to his participation in the drug game with one of the most character-defining Snowfall quotes, expressing a profound sense of self-agency and actualization that is admirable. Moreover, the gravity of how Joseph delivers the “All that matters is we survive. That’s it,” is one of the most heartening moments on the show.

6 “See, That’s The Difference Between Me And You, Drew. I Don’t Give Up My People.” Franklin Saint — Season 4, Episode 5, “The Get Back”

In shows like Snowfall, the easiest way to lose street cred is to be a snitch. In Snowfall, the line between drug dealers and police officers is often a racial one, which somehow makes betrayal twice as damning. When Franklin stands up to Manboy and expresses how much integrity he has as a Black man committed to his community, it gets to the heart of the series’ social commentary. Franklin may deal drugs, but he isn’t so callous as to turn his back and help the police in any capacity, no matter how beneficial it may be for him.

5 “What, You Think We’re Making All This Money Because This Sh*t Ain’t Addictive?” Leon Simmons — Season 3, Episode 7 “Pocket Full of Rocks”

The likable Leon Simmons is Franklin’s fast-talking friend who is known for keeping things painfully real. Many of Leon’s Snowfall quotes are reminiscent of this personality trait, and many of the arguments he has border on humorous thanks to his inability to soften the blow. When Leon and Franklin discuss Mel and Wanda, Franklin refuses to believe Mel would follow the same path of addiction Wanda has. Leon reminds him Wanda’s struggles aren’t an indicator of weakness. Drugs change people and anyone is susceptible.

4 “Gangbanging About More Than Just Money And Colors For O.G.’s. It’s About Community. Respect!” Jerome Saint — Season 4, Episode 7, “Through a Glass, Darkly”

The Jerome Saint quotes reflect the troubling circumstances in which he was raised and the mantle he took up to get ahead in an unfair world. Jerome feels the need to impress upon increasingly chaotic Leon that what they do isn’t just about money. Organized crime is a complex system and the participants therein often justify their illegal activities through assisting their community. Oftentimes, drug cartels, while also responsible for horrific crimes, are celebrated by their communities for keeping them safe where the police can’t.

3 “Business Is Only As Good As The People You Work With.” Teddy McDonald — Season 2, Episode 3, “Prometheus Rising”

Teddy and Franklin successfully wrap up their very first deal in “Prometheus Rising.” Teddy manages to give Franklin sound advice at various points throughout Snowfall, which he heeds — most of the time. Unfortunately, Franklin forgets this adage by the time Snowfall season 6 rolls around and Ruben records their conversation, meaning the KGB is aware of all of Franklin’s crimes. Louie also betrays Franklin, cutting him out of a deal with Teddy which causes Franklin to retaliate brutally.

2 “Rule Number Two, Because There Ain’t No Winners.” Jerome Saint — Season 1, Episode 6, “A Long Time Coming”

When Franklin first approaches O.G. Jerome about getting in on the crack cocaine game, Jerome refuses. He has retired from life in gangs, content with selling marijuana and working out on his front lawn. Jerome gives Franklin a litany of reasons why he won’t be a part of the game anymore, but this one is the most poignant. He warns Franklin that it’s a risky business, with constantly turning tides and changing loyalties that can often result in jail or death. Jerome is right in saying that the business itself is a no-win situation.

1 “I Mean, They Ain’t Killed Me Yet.” Leon Simmons — Season 6, Episode 3, “Door of No Return”

While in Ghana, Leon weds Wanda and the pair enjoy a party afterward. When approached by Dodzi, Leon claims he has to go back to the States soon to get some business in order. When Dodzi questions Leon’s safety in the matter, he busts out one of the best Snowfall quotes and proclaims he’s still standing. With all the deaths in Snowfall, it is a testament to Leon’s character that he’s still around. Before Khadija was killed in season 4, she made it her mission to kill Leon, and had it not been for Jerome, she might have succeeded.
