10 Best Upbeat Anime Guaranteed to Cheer Fans Up

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Iruma-kun is a cheerful and funny anime that follows a human boy’s adventures in a demon school. The Way of the Househusband is a hilarious anime that flips gender roles as a former Yakuza member becomes a stay-at-home husband. A Place Further Than the Universe is an uplifting anime about four girls who form a strong bond while journeying to Antarctica.

Literature Review

When feeling down, nothing hits harder than a good upbeat and cheerful anime, from goofy isekai series to inspirational sports journeys.

The most upbeat anime guaranteed to cheer fans up are not only cheerful enough to clear away any lingering sadness, but will also be inspiring, motivational, funny, and sometimes just outright absurd. As a result, series of all sorts of genres can bring about cheer, from mainstream comedy, to sports, to isekai. As a medium, anime is fascinating because of its wide reach and the variety of relatability it offers. Whether one is feeling down, or if just a distraction is needed, there is an anime out there that will fulfill the role, it only needs to be found. As for cheerful and uplifting anime, regardless of how they do it, as long as the audience is left feeling better than when the show began, the job has been successful.

10 Iruma-kun Is a Pool of Unbounded Optimism Based on the manga by Nishi Osamu

Release Year: 2019 Number of Episodes: 65 Where to Stream: Crunchyroll, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video
Welcome to Demon School! Iruma-kun is a comedy isekai anime that plays into the unexpected, making it one of the best fantasy anime on Hulu. Produced by Bandai Namco Pictures, the series follows Iruma, the titular character, as he embarks on a new life in the demon world. However, the twist is that Iruma is human, and he must lie low in order not to be discovered. Despite all of Iruma’s obstacles, he never lets them affect his mood, as his optimism is his biggest strength. Even when his parents sell him to the chairman of the demon school, Babyls, Sullivan, he embraces his new life, which quickly transforms into laughter and new friendships at every turn. Even Sullivan provides Iruma with more love and doting care than he’s ever received before. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

9 The Way of the Househusband Hilariously Flips Gender Roles Based on the web manga by Oono Kousuke

Release Year: 2021 Number of Episodes: 15 Where to Stream: Netflix
The Way of the Househusband is a gag comedy anime on Netflix that takes up a novel but intriguing idea and expands it into situations of laughter and unexpectedness. Produced by J.C. Staff, this series, also known as Gokushufudou, follows former Yakuza member Tatsu, otherwise known as “Immortal Dragon,” as he navigates the life of a stay-at-home husband. Although the limited-style animation of The Way of the Househusband caused some controversy, nothing takes away from the anime’s declaration of reform. From cooking meals to cleaning the apartment to having everything ready to welcome home his hard-working wife, Tatsu does things that create a hilarious juxtaposition with his image. He’ll smile menacingly while holding a knife, only to eloquently dice up some onions. He can also be found occasionally arguing with a robotic vacuum cleaner. Such moments sum up the everyday wacky behavior that follows Tatsu, as his days of reforming his previous Yakuza antics for the sake of his wife are endearing to follow. WATCH ON Netflix

8 A Place Further Than the Universe Sets Sights on Antarctica An original series directed by Ishizuka Atsuko

Release Year: 2018 Number of Episodes: 13 Where to Stream: Crunchyroll, Amazon Prime Video
Produced by Studio Madhouse, A Place Further Than the Universe, also titled Sora Yori mo Tooi Basho, literally ventures towards places no anime has set foot before. The series follows four girls who have almost nothing in common, each bringing their own goofy form of eccentricity to a quickly budding friendship. Although it may seem the girls could not be any more different, their goal ultimately ties them together stronger than any ordinary friendship. Tamaki Mari and Kobuchizawa Shirase are the central figures in the group, as the passion to journey towards the icy lands of Antarctica started with these two. Kobuchizawa Shirase is adamant about going on an expedition to find her missing mother, and Tamaki Mari, a girl who has always wanted to do something amazing but never found the conviction, is inspired by Shirase. Her own passion even blossoms so much that it further attracts Miyake Hinata and Shiraishi Yuzuki to join in on their antics, crafting a wonderfully warming story despite the temperatures. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

7 Haikyuu!! Is All About Connecting: Tsunagu! Based on the manga by Furudate Haruichi

Release Year: 2014 Number of Episodes: 85 Where to Stream: Crunchyroll, Amazon Prime Video
Produced by Production I.G, Haikyuu!! is a sports anime that follows the energetic Hinata Shoyo through the ups and downs of his volleyball journey. Although he is short, Hinata has incredible jumping power, which he slowly learns to harness and take advantage of as a member of the Karasuno Volleyball Club. As volleyball is a team sport, the anime is not only about Hinata, but also his rival Kageyama Tobio, the team’s central figure, the setter. The two butt heads but eventually understand that, together, they make an ultimate combination. As they both develop further, they connect more and more with each of their teammates, who are all strong in their own rights. When the time for competition comes, tensions run high, setting the perfect stage for inspiring and uplifting stories. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

Haikyuu!!: Each Main Character’s Most Iconic Scene Haikyuu!! focuses on volleyball and as is the case with most sports shows, each character gets an iconic moment on the court.

6 Devil is a Part-Timer! Shows the Toils of the Fast Food Industry Based on the light novel by Wagahara Satoshi

Release Year: 2013 Number of Episodes: 37 Where to Stream: Crunchyroll, Funimation, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, Tubi
Devil is a Part-Timer! is an isekai anime that is both a satire of the genre, and a classic example of the isekai structure. Produced by White Fox Studio, Devil is a Part-Timer! resembles all the details, jokes, and characters in an isekai or fantasy, but the setting is modern-day Japan. In other words, the main character, who is transferred to another world, as well as the target “new world” is a very familiar and realistic place for fans watching. In addition, as the anime follows the demon king who has been isekai’d into the real world, quickly adapting to everyday life in Japan, but forced to take up a part-time job at a fast food chain in order to make a living. With a stature and standing resembling nothing of his former image, the newly named Maou Sadao clumsily tries his hand at civilian life, leading to relatable struggles and lighthearted banter. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

5 Saiki K Is a Universal Gag Comedy Based on the manga by Asou Shuuichi

Release Year: 2016 Number of Episodes: 56 Where to Stream: Netflix, Funimation, Amazon Prime Video, Tubi
Produced by J.C. Staff, The Disastrous Life of Saiki K is a gag comedy that stands out even in shonen anime, as it revolves around the first-person account of the omnipotent psychic, Kusuo Saiki, a pink-haired teenager who only wants to live life normally. Fortunately, such a goal is within his powers, but also comes with the downside of causing a ruckus and being exposed as abnormal. Through Saiki’s high school days, new friends pop up one after another, creating more and more trouble for Saiki, but giving him, as well as viewers, hilarious memories that will not be forgotten. Most of Saiki K’s plot is laid back, but the overarching narrative is not without deep themes, such as the perception of beauty, the grass is always greener on the other side, and lastly, that nothing in life is ever too serious. WATCH ON Netflix

4 Hikaru no Go Is a Lesson on Harnessing Talent Based on the manga by Obata Takeshi and Hotta Yumi

Release Year: 2001 Number of Episodes: 75 Where to Stream: Hulu, Vudu
Hikaru no Go is a classic sports anime produced by Studio Pierrot that thrives on all fronts of suspense, comedy, and tales of rivalries. The story follows a 6th grader named Hikaru who discovers the ghost of a go (Japanese checkers) master from the Heian period named Fujiwara no Sai, who takes root in Hikaru’s body. With such a helper by his side, Hikaru has every ability to take the professional world of go by storm. However, as he discovers more about the game that the moniker of Japanese Chess does not do justice, Hikaru is inspired to stop relying on Sai’s ability to win, but to come up on his own terms. As a character, Sai’s historical disconnect and his pure passion for the game of go creates cute and funny situations between him and Hikaru, and endless motivational stories of growth. WATCH ON Hulu

3 Nichijou Is About an Ordinary Life That Is Anything But Ordinary Based on the manga by Arawi Keiichi

Release Year: 2011 Number of Episodes: 26 Where to Stream: Crunchyroll, Funimation, Amazon Prime Video
Nichijou, meaning everyday life or ordinary life, is a gag comedy that veers as far from its title’s meaning as possible, making it one of the weirdest slice of life anime. Produced by the renowned Kyoto Animation that adapted series such as Violet Evergarden, Clannad, K-On!, and more, Nichijou is a light-hearted amalgamation of silly antics that disguise themselves among the portrayal of seemingly normal day-to-day life. The series follows a diverse cast of characters that range from high school girls to talking cats, but one thing they all have in common is that they all blend into the anything-but-ordinary world of Nichijou. Whether it’s the antics of a mischievous sister, or the laid-back life of a 10-year-old scientist and the sentient robot caretaker she created, Nichijou will provide laughter that distracts from all and any of life’s problems. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

2 Grand Blue Dreaming Hits the Jokes From All Angles Based on the manga by Inoue Kenji and Yoshioka Kimitake

Release Year: 2018 Number of Episodes: 12 Where to Stream: Amazon Prime Video
Produced by Studio Zero-G, Grand Blue Dreaming is the ultimate display of unhinged college life on a remote coastal island in Japan. The story follows newly admitted college first-year student, Kitahara Iori, after he moves to Izu, a small town where his uncle runs a scuba diving shop, which is the main hangout spot for the local university’s diving club. As a result, Iori is quickly dragged into all the out-of-pocket activities of the diving club members, but is also exposed to the beautiful art of scuba diving. Although Iori joins such an abnormal club with a multitude of members who enjoy sauntering around in comedic nakedness, he still holds on to the slowly fleeting goal of wanting to experience a normal college life. WATCH ON Amazon Prime

1 Spy x Family Is Too Cute to Pass Up Based on the manga by Endou Tatsuya

Release Year: 2022 Number of Episodes: 37 Where to Stream: Crunchyroll, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video
Spy x Family is one of the hit series of the decade, as it blends the excitement of an action comedy shonen format with the warmth of a chosen family. Produced by Wit Studio, Spy x Family follows an undersized elementary school girl named Anya, an orphaned esper who only wishes for a caring family. However, certain circumstances force the legendary spy, Lloyd Forger, to adopt Anya and marry Yor, who is secretly an assassin. As an esper, Anya hears all of her adopted parents’ thoughts, creating a stage for the ultimate dramatic irony as Anya tries her utmost to keep the family from falling apart, whether it’s helping with catching a purse-snatcher or enrolling in an elite institution to get closer to the son of Lloyd’s target. Despite Anya’s abilities, she still holds an adorable innocence that is impossible to erase, creating a story that is both endearing, but also hilarious when most needed. With these combinations, Spy x Family is guaranteed to cheer up any fans. WATCH ON Crunchyroll
