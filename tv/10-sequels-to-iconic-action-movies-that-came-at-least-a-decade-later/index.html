10 Sequels To Iconic Action Movies (That Came At Least A Decade Later)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Belated sequels can either benefit from decades of anticipation or disappoint fans when they fail to live up to the original. Successful long-awaited sequels include Top Gun: Maverick and Mad Max: Fury Road , while others are quietly released and only enjoyed by a few dedicated fans. Incredibles 2 doesn’t live up to the charisma of the original villain, but retains the retro feel and humor, making it a fun sequel.

Literature Review

Sequels to action movies have been known to come out more than a decade later and benefit from the anticipation – or make for an even bigger letdown.

With studios always in the market to capitalize on properties that they already own, there will consistently be belated sequels to iconic action movies that come out a decade or more after the original movie. Some of these sequels are treated as true movie events, and the results are popular among audiences and critically acclaimed. In other cases, a movie sequel is quietly released on video, for only a few dedicated fans to enjoy. In the best-case scenario of a long-awaited sequel, the movie benefits from the decades of anticipation. This was what happened with movies such as Top Gun: Maverick and Mad Max: Fury Road, both of which were also nominated for Best Picture at the Oscars. However, as most people know, legacy sequels always run the risk of disappointing fans when they fail to live up to a classic from the 80s or 90s.

10 Longest Gaps Between Movie Sequels
While successful movies usually have sequels quickly greenlit, there are some examples of decades-long gaps between one movie and the next.

10 Terminator 3: Rise Of The Machines (2003) Sequel to Terminator 2: Judgment Day (1991).

The third installment in the Terminator franchise is generally seen as the point where the movies started to go downhill — but it isn’t terrible yet. However, when the seven-year wait after the first Terminator movie resulted in Terminator 2: Judgment Day, Terminator 3: Rise of the Machines was a disappointment, as it also arrived after a long wait. Still, the action holds up, and the story continues to expand on the initial premise of Skynet sending Terminators into the past to prevent John Connor from becoming a threat in the future.

9 Timecop 2: The Berlin Decision (2003) Sequel to Timecop (1994).

Jean-Claude Van Damme stars in the original Timecop, which can be considered entertaining at its best, if not actually good. However, the movie was still popular and Van Damme’s acting and fight sequences were generally praised. Almost a decade after the original, a video sequel was released. While critics did not review Timecop 2 due to it not having a theatrical release, it has a lower audience score than the original on Rotten Tomatoes. Van Damme did not return for Timecop 2, deriving the sequel of what was the first movie’s main selling point. Timecop has a 36% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes, while Timecop 2 has 26%.

8 Incredibles 2 (2018) Sequel to The Incredibles (2004).

The Incredibles 2
PG Adventure Action Animation

The sequel to Pixar’s The Incredibles, Incredibles 2 follows the Parr family as they continue to fight crime as Supers. In a world that has only recently rediscovered its need for superpowered heroes, building trust is key. However, when Elastigirl uncovers a villainous plot to discredit the Super community, the family investigates, putting the Parrs in more danger than ever before. 

Release Date June 15, 2018 Director Brad Bird Cast Sarah Vowell , Samuel L. Jackson , Holly Hunter , Craig T. Nelson , John Ratzenberger , Catherine Keener , Huck Milner , Bob Odenkirk Runtime 2h 6m

Despite arriving 15 years after the original movie, the highly-anticipated Incredibles 2 picks up moments after the first movie ends. The sequel doesn’t quite live up to the original in terms of plot or the charisma of its main villain. However, it retains The Incredibles’ retro feel while continuing to depict the Parr family balancing their crime-fighting lives with mundane activities like school and work. With some hilarious slapstick sequences and a few more sassy lines from Edna Mode thrown in, Incredibles 2 makes for a fun sequel.

7 Tron: Legacy (2010) Sequel to Tron (1982).

Tron: Legacy brings back its original star Jeff Bridges, now playing the long-lost father of the movie’s young protagonist. Kevin and Sam Flynn contend with Clu, the villainous program taking over the virtual reality Kevin created. While Tron: Legacy received average reviews, no one can deny that the advancement of CGI technology served the setting well: The techno-inspired setting of the Grid is hauntingly futuristic. Tron: Ares is also now on the horizon, and will come out more than an additional 10 years after the previous movie.

10 Legacy Sequels That Are Sure To Be Developed In The Near Future
Top Gun: Maverick and many other legacy sequels have found success, so some other iconic movies deserve to get new follow-ups soon.

6 Escape From L.A. (1996) Sequel to Escape from New York (1981).

Following up the cult classic Escape from New York is Escape from L.A.Escape from New York was relatively well reviewed, claiming an interesting premise: In the future, all of New York has been converted into a massive prison. Kurt Russell stars as a former soldier turned convict who is recruited to rescue the president in exchange for a full pardon. This is also a premise that does not easily translate into a sequel. However, Russell returns for another movie, in which it is revealed that this world’s L.A. is a remote island where dangerous items are disposed of.

5 Independence Day: Resurgence (2016) Sequel to Independence Day (1996).

Independence Day: Resurgence
PG-13 Adventure Sci-Fi Action

Set 20 years after the original, this sequel to Independence Day sees the nations of Earth use the alien technology to develop an advanced defense system for the planet. But nothing can prepare them for an unprecedented force of alien attackers out for revenge. It’s up to a few brave men and women to save the world from extraterrestrials once more.

Release Date June 24, 2016 Director Roland Emmerich Cast Bill Pullman , Sela Ward , Vivica A. Fox , Jessie Usher , Charlotte Gainsbourg , Liam Hemsworth , Maika Monroe , Judd Hirsch , Brent Spiner , William Fichtner , Jeff Goldblum , Joey King Runtime 120 minutes

Independence Day takes an optimistic approach to the alien invasion plot that humanity will be able to fight back, with some comedy mixed in. Independence Day: Resurgence builds upon this theme, showing how humanity has built up its arsenal for 20 years since the last invasion. While Jeff Goldblum and Bill Pullman return for Independence Day: Resurgence, Will Smith does not. The first Independence Day is a fun blockbuster with plenty of action and destruction – most famously an alien spaceship destroying the White House – and its sequel delivers the same. There have also been discussions about Independence Day 3.

4 Hard Target 2 (2016) Sequel to Hard Target (1993)

Hard Target 2 is another follow-up to a Van Damme movie for which Van Damme does not return. However, Hard Target 2 finds another martial arts master as its star in Scott Adkins. The story depicts a fictional martial artist a few months after he accidentally kills his best friend in the ring as he is tricked into being the prey of people hunting him for sport. Despite poor reviews, Hard Target 2’s story has an emotional foundation about coming to terms with guilt, which is used as a vehicle for plenty of action.

3 Mad Max: Fury Road (2015) Sequel to Mad Max Beyond the Thunderdome (1985).

The Mad Max material was left untouched for decades before George Miller returned to the franchise. Even with the original director on board, no one could have predicted the critical and commercial success of Mad Max: Fury Road, now considered one of the best action movies of all time. The movie serves as a soft reboot, moving the story further into the future. The detailed setting is better than ever before, and the excellent performances tie it all together. Mad Max is recast as Tom Hardy, while Charlize Theron comes on board as the new but equally intriguing character Furiosa. Mad Max: Fury Road received a total of 10 Oscar nominations.

2 Top Gun: Maverick (2022) Sequel to Top Gun (1986).

Top Gun: Maverick continues the story of Top Gun by having Maverick progress from a student to a teacher. Additionally, Top Gun: Maverick coming out more than 35 years after the original allowed for a new, heartrending plot point where Maverick is confronted by Goose’s now adult son. It is widely acknowledged that the intense flight sequences were all fueled by Tom Cruise’s commitment to authenticity and the actors performing the stunts themselves. With some great nods to the original and the same thrilling action, Top Gun: Maverick turned out to be a very enjoyable movie.

10 Movie Sequels That Took Decades To Happen But Were Definitely Worth The Wait
More often than not, long-awaited movie sequels don’t work out for many reasons. However, there are a few notable examples of some being successful.

1 Blade Runner 2049 (2017) Sequel to Blade Runner (1982).

Blade Runner 2049
R Sci-Fi Action Drama Mystery

Set thirty years after the original film, Blade Runner 2049 is a sci-fi neo-noir film by the director of Dune, Denis Villeneuve. The film follows LAPD replicant officer K, who discovers a devastating secret that could throw society into total chaos. To stop a potential war between replicants and humans, he’ll need to locate missing former blade runner Rick Deckard and uncover the truth.

Release Date October 6, 2017 Director Denis Villeneuve Cast Ryan Gosling , Harrison Ford , Jared Leto , Ana De Armas , Robin Wright , Lennie James , Dave Bautista , Carla Juri , Hiam Abbass , Barkhad Abdi , David Dastmalchian , Mackenzie Davis , Sylvia Hoeks

Taking place 30 years after the original Blade Runner, Blade Runner 2049 continues the story in an organic way. It maintains the primary theme of concerns about AI technology, and the vibrant sci-fi world benefits from advanced CGI without being overwhelmed by it. Ryan Gosling is dynamic enough to take over as the protagonist, but Harrison Ford’s Rick Deckard is still a major character. Belated sequels might suffer because they simply came too late, but the long gap between the Blade Runner movies works since the story picks up decades later as well.
