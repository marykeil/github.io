8 Theories About What Happens To Tommy Shelby In The Peaky Blinders Movie

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Finn seeks revenge after being cast out by the family, setting up a rivalry that will be a major plot point in the movie. Tommy may use Dr. Holford against his enemies, leveraging his knowledge to gain an advantage in World War II. Tommy will continue to aid Winston Churchill and potentially contribute to the war effort, while also seeking to take down his last remaining enemy, Oswald Mosley.

Literature Review

Peaky Blinders season 6 left Thomas Shelby’s fate up in the air, leaving several potential options for his ending in the Peaky Blinders movie.

The Peaky Blinders sequel movie is confirmed, and there are many possibilities for where Tommy Shelby’s story will end. With its sixth season, the BBC’s hit crime series concluded with a somewhat vague, symbolic ending. The closing sequence was highly evocative and a powerful ending on its own, but it still begged the question of what would be next for Tommy, having once again barely escaped death. Borrowed time is a consistent theme in Peaky Blinders, meaning the most potent questions heading into the film are those regarding Tommy’s fate and purpose. Much of the Peaky Blinders cast is likely set to return for the film, exploring “a sort of untold story that happened in the Second World War, which Peakys are going to be involved in,” according to creator Steven Knight (via Radio Times). Seasons 5 and 6 established the rise of fascism as a central conflict through the antagonist, Oswald Mosley (Sam Claflin). Given Tommy’s previous efforts to undermine the fascist politician, the Peaky Blinders will likely continue to pose a threat to the Axis Powers once World War II begins.

8 Finn Will Return For Revenge Finn was cast out of the family in season 6 and wasn’t happy about it.

With Polly, Michael, and John dead, Tommy Shelby is running out of family heading into the movie. His youngest brother Finn has been cast to the side after botching the Oswald Mosley assassination in season 5 and turning on the family in season 6. Duke Shelby banishes Finn, and Finn swears that he’ll come for him in revenge. Given that Tommy has only recently discovered Duke as his firstborn son, Finn and Duke’s rivalry will likely be an integral plot point in the Peaky Blinders movie.

7 Tommy Will Use Dr. Holford Against His Enemies Tommy may flip Dr. Holford against Mosley.

Dr. Holford is a secondary antagonist in season 6, who’s tasked by Oswald Mosley to convince Tommy of a fatal illness, hoping he’d take his own life. On the cusp of death, Tommy realizes Holford has been conspiring with Mosley against him, yet decides to spare Holford, turning over a new leaf. With stakes heightening as World War II ensues, having Holford available to blackmail for information on Mosley and the Axis Powers will be a valuable asset and a potential plot point.

6 Tommy Will Be In Hiding After his supposed “going away” in season 6, Tommy may need to operate discreetly.

Like Alfie Solomons, after his supposed death, Tommy will likely have to go into hiding and wait for his moment to strike in the Peaky Blinders movie. If Mosley thinks Tommy is off the board, Tommy can operate and plan a counterattack on his enemies. This will leave the Peaky Blinders in a state of disarray, and it will have to be seen whether Arthur can hold himself together, Duke will take a leap and live up to his father’s reputation, and, perhaps, Ada will run for political office and fulfill that aspect of Tommy’s life in his absence.

5 Tommy Will Reconcile With His Son Charlie Shelby deserves a better ending.

Tommy Shelby’s relationship with his wife and kids has been tumultuous at best, with the tragic loss of Ruby a considerable narrative in season 6. If Tommy is presumed dead or still in hiding, Lizzie and Charlie could find themselves starting anew. While Tommy has never held Lizzie to the respect she deserves, he holds Grace’s memory with reverence, which may be a suitable motive for him to pursue a relationship with Charlie. While Tommy’s most honest path to redemption would be laying down his work and trying to be a reliable father, circumstances in the Peaky Blinders movie may push him toward more violence. Still, the film should offer some closure to Tommy and Charlie’s relationship, resolving Tommy’s feelings for Grace. Tommy owes the boy better, especially after Tommy’s Peaky Blinders farewell meal and the awkward dichotomy established between Charlie and Duke, where Tommy barely had anything to say to the son he’d helped raise.

4 Tommy Will Aid Winston Churchill In The War Tommy and Churchill have been working together for years.

Winston Churchill is a recurring character in Peaky Blinders who seems essential to the sequel movie. At the end of season 2, Churchill prevents Tommy’s death at the hands of Inspector Campbell’s hired assassins, and Tommy, in turn, owes him a favor. The two continued to conduct business together for years following, and now that Churchill is set to become a prominent figure in Britain for the duration of World War II, their business will likely continue. Churchill has utilized Tommy Shelby to carry out assassinations for Great Britain, turning the crime boss into a tactical hitman. With events escalating into war, Tommy and his crew could be used as assassins for the Allies. Especially if Tommy is presumed dead by his enemies, he’d be even more dangerous. Eliminating Mosley and contributing to the war effort could be one of the only ways for Tommy to find inner peace through redemption.

3 Tommy Will Take Down Oswald Mosley Oswald Mosley is Tommy’s last enemy left alive.

Oswald Mosley is the only main antagonist in Peaky Blinders left alive at the end of the series. He’s easily the most evil Peaky Blinders character and must be dealt with in the film. Given previous failed attempts on Mosley’s life, Tommy may have to deal with the man himself. Given that the real-life Oswald Mosley was imprisoned in 1940, the historical event could tie in perfectly with Thomas Shelby’s arc and redemption. Rather than murdering Mosley, Tommy could contribute information that leads to his legal arrest.

2 Tommy Will Deal A Blow To The Axis Powers The Peaky Blinders movie will navigate World War II.

Peaky Blinders season 6 explored the idea of Tommy’s redemption, and it seems that the best way for him to end as a morally justifiable TV character would be for him to stand up to the Axis Powers. While it’s unlikely that the Peaky Blinders movie will go into Inglourious Basterds mode and re-write history with an assassination of Hitler, Tommy could still greatly assist the Allies with information or by taking down Mosley. It will be interesting to see how Steven Knight navigates real-world history and implements the Peaky Blinders characters into those events.

1 Tommy Shelby Will Finally Die Death could be the only peaceful resolution for Tommy.

Death and time are two central themes throughout Peaky Blinders, particularly in season 6 with the seemingly impending death of Tommy Shelby and the death of Ruby Shelby. It’s absolutely vital to Tommy’s character that he’s a World War I veteran who survived as a tunneler. Not only does it contribute to his PTSD, but the job as a tunneler presents the idea that his life after the war is “borrowed time.” Tommy doesn’t believe he was meant to survive, having come so close to death. Therefore, he lives unafraid of death and can achieve unprecedented success. Season 6 presents Tommy with another situation of borrowed time. Up until the final twist, he once again believes death is imminent. The perfect ending to Tommy Shelby’s story must resolve his complicated feelings toward death, either by his finally passing or by him finding a path to inner peace in life. How Tommy chooses to spend his time with the final act he’s been given in Peaky Blinders will be up to his agency. Even after redemption, death may be the only option if his heroic final acts can’t resolve his inner turmoil. Sources: Radio Times
