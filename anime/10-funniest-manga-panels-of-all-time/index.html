10 Funniest Manga Panels of All Time

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Manga humor is often absurd and slapstick, contrasting with Western media’s ironic tone. This makes many panels feel abruptly humorous in their delivery. Some funny manga panels may be lost in translation due to puns and wordplay – which is the case with classics like Inuyasha and Azumanga Daioh! The funniest manga panels can still resonate despite cultural and language barriers, and can introduce fans into new style of comedy writing.

Literature Review

Manga is the perfect visual medium for humor. More often than not, it nails comedic timing to surprise and delight audiences.

Manga lends itself well to humor, which means the funniest manga panels of all time exist in a variety of genres. The visual medium is perfect for characters having overblown reactions, but manga also includes more sly, subtle humor on occasion. Even darker, grittier series make use of humor as a way to lighten the mood between scenes. Newcomers, especially Western audiences, may be surprised by the style of humor most prevalent in manga. It tends to lean more absurd and slapstick, which contrasts sharply with the ironic tone of Western media. Additionally, some panels that include puns or wordplay may be lost in translation. The funniest manga panels always stick the landing in spite of cultural and language barriers.

10 Kagome’s Friends Roast Americans in a Hilarious Mistranslation Inuyasha (Chapter 139 - “The Pair’s Feelings”)

Publisher Shogakukan Creator Rumiko Takahashi Where to Read Online Viz
Occasionally, even official manga translations miss the mark. This is hilariously evident in Inuyasha, when Kagome is describing the titular dog demon to her friends. “American” appears to be a mistranslation of “yankee”, which is another word for a delinquent in Japanese culture. As the translation stands, Takahashi appears to be roasting the stereotypical American personality. In addition to this unintentionally funny scene, Inuyasha is chock-full of physical comedy and snarky remarks between its leads that make this historical fantasy series a must-read. READ ONLINE

9 Patalliro Loves to Get Under Bancoran’s Skin Patalliro! (Chapter 1 - “Bishounen Killer”)

Publisher Hakusensha Creator Mineo Maya Where to Read Online Unavailable
An agent for the British MI6, Jack Barbarosa Bancoran is perpetually unruffled, true to his James Bond-esque nature. That all changes when he is assigned to protect the 10-year-old king of Malynera, Patalliro. Bancoran considers Patalliro to be the most annoying and off-putting kid he has ever met. The endless spats between Bancoran and Patalliro are, in addition to the manga’s LGBTQ+ themes, the heart of Maya’s series. It takes a special kind of person to make Bancoran lose his cool, and Patalliro can do it within seconds.

8 Guts and Casca Share a Playful, Palate-Cleansing Moment Berserk (Chapter 50 - “Infiltrating Wyndham, Chapter 2”)

Publisher Dark Horse Creator Kentaro Miura Where to Read Online Unavailable
For such a dark and emotionally-devastating series, Berserk manages to seamlessly weave in genuine humor. For example, during the mission to rescue Griffith from being tortured, Guts and Casca share this playfully slapstick interaction. Some consider it a cheeky throwback to volumes past, when these two did not always get along. Additionally, it’s a testament to Miura’s ability to balance angst and endearing scenes between characters. Fans who are aware of the pain that Guts and Casca have yet to experience in Berserk can look back on such panels as moments of respite.

7 Chiyo-chichi is Funny in Both Appearance and Dialogue Azumanga Daioh (Chapter 24.5 - “Consultation”)

Publisher Yen Press Creator Kiyohiko Azuma Where to Read Online Unavailable
Azumanga Daioh is one of the weirdest slice-of-life series and has long been praised for its comedic timing. In short, this manga has no shortage of funny panels. Some of the best are reserved for Chiyo-chichi, a cat-like creature who claims he is the father of the protagonist, Chiyo. He’s not a real cat, but he claims cat status when it suits him, such as when he’s asked for help in choosing the right college. Chiyo-chichi’s memorable quips, in addition to his off-putting gaze, make him one of the funniest critter companions in manga.

6 Fai Playfully Makes Fun of an Action Hero Trope Tsubasa: Reservoir Chronicle (Chapter 206 - “The Current Future”)

Publisher Kodansha Creator CLAMP Where to Read Online Unavailable
Fai always knows how to push Kurogane’s buttons. His ability to do so lies in their opposing personalities – Fai is playful and mysterious, while Kurogane is no-nonsense. In one hysterically funny panel, Fai pokes fun at the trope of shonen action heroes who announce their techniques before they perform them. Judging by Kurogane’s deep-set frown, he is not amused by Fai’s observation. Tsubasa is one of the best retro isekai, and that is in part due to its humor.

5 Kaoru Gets Overly Possessive of His AI Skateboard Sk8 Chill Out! (Line 1)

Publisher Kadokawa Shoten Creator Toriyasu Where to Read Online Unavailable
In the gag manga based on the Sk8 the Infinity anime, Kaoru takes his obsession with his AI skateboard, Carla, to a whole new level. When Kaoru meets Langa Hasegawa for the first time in the anime, he politely rejects Langa for a position at his calligraphy studio. In Chill Out’s version, Kaoru suspects the empty-headed Langa of something more nefarious. In other words, the anime’s running gag of Carla being Kaoru’s “robot girlfriend” is exaggerated here to hilarious effect.

4 Uramichi Can’t Maintain His Child-Friendly Persona for Long Life Lessons With Uramichi-Oniisan (Chapter 7 - “When Worry Becomes Anxiety”)

Publisher Ichijinsha Creator Gaku Kuze Where to Read Online Unavailable
Adulthood is soul-sucking – or so says Omota Uramichi, a children’s TV show host whose quarter-life crisis is the overarching theme of Kuze’s manga. Uramichi secretly cares a lot about the kids he teaches, which is perhaps why he can’t be phony with them about the realities of adulthood. The goofy nihilism of Uramichi-Oniisan is uncommon in a manga series, but has also helped make it more accessible to Western audiences, who are accustomed to that type of humor. Millennials and young professionals have praised the series for being uproariously funny and relatable.

3 Tamaki Thinks His Trauma Makes Him More Mysterious Ouran High School Host Club (Chapter 62)

Publisher Viz Media Creator Bisco Hatori Where to Read Online Viz
When forced to confront his tragic past, the self-absorbed Tamaki has the most in-character response ever – he thinks it only makes him more attractive and mysterious. Tamaki’s golden retriever energy is hardly that of the dark and brooding host he believes himself to be in this scene, making it a cheeky wink-wink moment for readers. There’s no denying that Tamaki’s melodramatic nature, paired with his lack of self-awareness, makes him one of the funniest bishounen anime heroes ever created. READ ONLINE

2 Tatsu Keeps Forgetting He Isn’t a Yakuza Anymore The Way of the Househusband (Chapter 9)

Publisher Viz Media Creator Kousuke Oono Where to Read Online Viz
Many of the gags in The Way of the Househusband revolve around how Tatsu, a former yakuza, brings the same intensity he had as a gangster to his new role as homemaker. The vocabulary and mannerisms of Tatsu’s former life tend to bleed into his domestic one, leading to plenty of social faux pas. The best example of this is when he asks a grocery store clerk where he can find “white powder”, when he really means flour. Thankfully, Tatsu’s neighbors find him endearing enough that he can usually get out of these situations unscathed. READ ONLINE

1 Yugi Plays the Role of the Mysterious Vigilante Yu-Gi-Oh! (Chapter 45 - “The Terror of Thirteen!!”)

Publisher Viz Media Creator Kazuki Takahashi Where to Read Online Viz
Even serious Yu-Gi-Oh! fans will admit that there is something inherently ridiculous about a bomber challenging a group of men to a card game. The way the mysterious silhouette hovers in the corner of the panel, with an iconic hairstyle that could only belong to one person, makes this scene all the more sidesplitting. It’s unclear whether Takahashi meant this panel to be funny or not, but framing Yugi as an enigmatic hero akin to Batman is certain to garner laughs. READ ONLINE
