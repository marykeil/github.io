The New Ultimate Spider-Man Officially Debuts a Classic Costume (But Its Not The One Fans Expected)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 800 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Peter Parker starts his career as Spider-Man in a new costume: his black Spider-Man suit. The new series explores a different take on Spider-Man where Peter chose to receive his powers and was given a costume by Tony Stark. This version of Peter Parker hasn’t earned the classic red and blue colors, highlighting his lack of tragedy and commitment so far in the series.

Literature Review

A preview for Ultimate Spider-Man #2 (2024) gives fans a full look at Spidey’s new costume, but it’s not the classic uniform readers expected.

Warning: Contains a preview for Ultimate Spider-Man #2 (2024)Now that he has been granted his powers, the Peter Parker of Marvel’s new Ultimate Universe is ready to swing into action as Spider-Man for the first time. Peter has already been given a superhero costume, allowing him to don a classic Spider-Man look right off the bat. This classic costume, however, is likely not the one fans were expecting.

A new preview of Ultimate Spider-Man #2 (2024) details Earth-6160’s Peter Parker starting his career as Spider-Man after accepting the radioactive spider (and attendant powers) gifted to him by Tony Stark.

The textless preview pages - which Marvel.com says take place “a month into Peter’s burgeoning super hero career” - reveal that the costume Peter acquired at the end of the first issue is not the classic red and blue fans are used to early Spider-Man wearing. Instead, Peter wears his black Spider-Man suit.

Ultimate Spider-Man #2 (2024) Writer: Jonathan Hickman Artist: Marco Checchetto Colorist: Matthew Wilson Letterer: Cory Petit Cover Artist: Marco Checchetto, Matthew Wilson

Ultimate Spider-Man Is Back in Black The Adult Peter Is Starting Out with Spidey’s 80 Costume

Ultimate Spider-Man (Vol. 2) #1 saw the saga of Marvel’s new alt-reality Ultimate Universe (Earth-6160) continue after the events of the four-part Ultimate Invasion and Ultimate Universe one-shot. In the issue, an adult Peter Parker (married to Mary Jane Watson and a father of two) is visited by a hologram of Tony Stark (this universe’s Iron Lad). Tony tells Peter that he was meant to become a superhero 20 years prior, but his run-in with the radioactive spider that gave him powers was prevented by the Maker as part of his plot to rule this new world.

Peter accepts the radioactive spider from Tony, viewing it as a chance to find what he feels is missing from his life. When the spider bites Peter, he dons a costume Tony gave him with the spider - a “picotech stealth suit to mask [his] identity.” The issue ends with an image of Peter wearing the costume on a rooftop, a fully black figure with Spider-Man’s eyes. This preview reveals that, one month in, Peter has still yet to adopt the classic colors.

Ultimate Spider-Man Hasn’t Earned the Red & Blue Is This Adult Peter Parker Really Destined to Be Spider-Man?

Spider-Man’s blank new costume mirrors the status of this new, radically different take on Marvel’s wall-crawler. Though Peter Parker has officially adopted the mantle of Spider-Man, the beginning of his superhero career is decidedly dissimilar from most versions of the character. Rather than being the product of fate or accident, Peter chose to receive his powers. Rather than creating his own costume, he was immediately given one by Tony Stark. He has, to this point, gone through the motions of his origin without fully embracing the Spider-Man mythos. He hasn’t earned his colors, setting up a very different identity where Spider-Man isn’t Marvel’s most dutiful, guilt-ridden hero.

This blank suit parallels Peter’s lack of tragedy or commitment thus far in the series. Though May died shortly before the events of Ultimate Spider-Man #1, this accident was in no way preventable by Spider-Man. He’s an early-stage hero who has a lot to learn when it comes to power and responsiblity, uncolored by the tragedy that many fans consider inherent to Spider-Man. There’s still plenty of time for Peter to earn the red and blue, however there’s a reason Marvel chose to explore an adult Spider-Man in this new series - it may be to see who Peter becomes in a world where he truly chooses to become a hero, but it may also be to show that without those formative experiences, this Pete isn’t actually worthy of the power he’s been given.

Ultimate Spider-Man #2 is available February 21 from Marvel Comics.
