Nightwing Has a Secret Healing Factor (Just Like His Great-Grandfather) - DC Theory Explained

    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Nightwing’s survival and ability to come out on top may be due to his connection to electrum, a rare metal capable of reanimating the dead. Electrum is primarily used by the Court of Owls to reanimate their assassins, and Nightwing was originally intended to be a Talon. Nightwing has sustained serious injuries, including being shot in the head multiple times, but has always made a miraculous recovery, suggesting he may have a superpower of regeneration - the same one the Court gave his assassin ancestor.

Literature Review

Nightwing keeps surviving huge injuries - is it because the Court of Owls changed his biology, just like they did to his assassin great-grandfather?

Nightwing is one of the most well-liked superheroes in the DC Universe. He is hugely respected by fans and his superhero allies alike, and part of that is because Nightwing manages to protect the world without any metahuman powers. Dick Grayson goes out there every day and risks his life to save people, relying purely on his skill… and, just perhaps, a supernatural healing factor even he doesn’t know he has.

Nightwing has survived countless near-death experiences with seemingly nothing but his skills and a minimally-armored costume. That’s not to say that Dick walks away without a scratch - he’s often badly injured, and yet somehow always pulls through. While luck could explain everything that Nightwing has managed to live through, there is another explanation - electrum.

In DC lore, electrum is a rare metal which is capable of reanimating the dead. During the events of Green Arrow #32 (from Benjamin Percy and Juan Ferreyra), Nightwing mentions that he still has Electrum running through his veins from his time with the Court of Owls. The Electrum in this story gives him visions of the Dark Multiverse, but it’s unlikely that’s the only benefit he’s been getting.

Electrum is primarily used by the Court of Owls, who utilize it to reanimate their Talon assassins. It’s no secret that Nightwing was originally intended to be a Talon, and that he was being mentally and physically prepared for the role over his childhood - apparently including electrum treatments (and even a false tooth containing the substance.) One person who has benefited from electrum in the past is William Cobb - a Talon who claims to be Dick Grayson’s great-grandfather, and who likewise had an electrum tooth which gave him regenerative healing. While Dick had his tooth removed, the mention from Green Arrow suggests the metal is still present in his blood - and if he is working with a major healing factor, that would actually explain a lot of his near-misses.

Nightwing has been shot in the head a lot and keeps surviving. Nightwing has never died in the main continuity, with the only exception being during the events of Forever Evil, where Lex Luthor stopped his heart for a matter of seconds. Electrum is capable of reanimating people after several minutes, so it’s possible that, given enough time, Dick might very well have come back to life on his own. Simon Hurt and the KGBeast both shot Dick in the head, and he’s also been shot by Joker and the GCPD, savagely beaten by Two-Face, stabbed by William Cobb, and even caught in a helicopter crash. Each time, Dick sustained serious injuries but made a miraculous recovery.

Dick has gone out and risked his life for years and managed to survive being shot in the head several times, having his apartment blown up, and facing off against super-powered beings. Despite this, he always comes out on top and the reason may not entirely be skill. With electrum flowing through his blood, Nightwing may have an actual superpower of regeneration. The Talons showed remarkable durability and self-healing, and Dick was prepped to be one. Nightwing may be capable of healing - and even returning from death - without ever knowing it, all because the Court of Owls changed his biology in order to try and make him their assassin.
