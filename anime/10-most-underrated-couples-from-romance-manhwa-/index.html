10 Most Underrated Couples From Romance Manhwa

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2000 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Underrated romances in manhwa often deviate from common tropes and showcase different relationship dynamics. Like Meldenique and Isaac from The Villainess’ Stationery Shop, some couples have wholesome relationships that overflow with positivity. While miscommunication is popular in romance manhwa, some relationships will subvert this trope with healthy communication and respect for boundaries.

Literature Review

These underrated couples featured in manhwa deserve attention for avoiding clichés and promoting positive relationship dynamics.

Over the years, manhwa has introduced many wonderful romances. Famous couples, such as Raeliana and Noah from Why Raeliana Ended Up At the Duke’s Mansion, usually follow tropes that are popular with readers. Obscure pairings, on the other hand, often deviate from the norm to showcase different relationship dynamics. Popular tropes from romance manhwa include miscommunication, love triangles, and office romances. Many of these motifs are used to create tension and give the couple new obstacles to overcome. While these patterns have become repetitive, they remain prominent in love stories. However, it’s always refreshing to see manhwa that doesn’t rely on common tropes to build romance.

10 Meldenique Babelloa and Isaac Matthew Reinhold The Villainess’s Stationery Shop, written by S. Moo and yeoroeun and illustrated by Minjeolmi

Meldenique Babelloa is the villainess of a romance novel and the male lead’s fiancée. After the protagonist transmigrates into her body, she avoids the character’s tragic fate by leaving her spiteful family. Meldenique’s new stationery shop has toys and treats for children and only exists to bring a smile to their faces. Her cheerful personality attracts several powerful figures, including Isaac, who is the enigmatic Master of the Magic Towers. Meldenique and Isaac’s relationship invokes feelings of a warm, sunny day. They show a mutual respect for boundaries and openly communicate with each other. The couple’s warmth causes children to gravitate toward them, and they often act as parental figures. Based on how Isaac raises the Tower’s youngest magician and how Meldenique takes care of the academy’s students, the two share a profound love for children. While their relationship has yet to evolve, the duo’s interactions are wholesome enough to brighten someone’s day. Read on Tapas

9 Joo Taeman and Kang Dahyeok Killer Crush, created by eresemo

Killer Crush follows Taeman, who is a professional assassin with 14 years of experience in the field. When the nation’s heartthrob appears in his life, the killer feels butterflies in his stomach for the first time. In a strange twist of fate, Dahyeok also finds himself drawn to the stoic man, who may be the key to living his film noir fantasy. Taeman and Dahyeok’s adorable interactions will have readers giggling in their seats. Taeman’s murderous aura makes him a huge contrast to Dahyeok, who is the physical embodiment of sunshine. Their hilarious misunderstandings are often followed by wholesome moments of enjoying each other’s company. Despite the many obstacles in their way, the couple’s affections are genuine, and they share a mutual concern for the other’s well-being. Read on Lezhin Comics

8 Karina Leopold and Milian Pastellio Karina’s Last Days, written by Chova and Jaeunhyang and illustrated by Kimpeul

Karina is the eldest daughter of the Leopold family, but she is overshadowed by her siblings and neglected by her parents. Since childhood, she has possessed the remarkable ability to create miracles by bringing her paintings to life. However, this ability weakens her body and leads to a deadly illness called “Art Disease.” Upon learning that she only has a year to live, Karina visits her fiancée and asks to spend the remainder of her life away from her family. As they spend time together, the couple’s frigid relationship quickly dissipates, and they develop a genuine fondness for each other. Milian’s warmth and kindness helps Karina appreciate her worth, while her compassion helps break down his walls. Even though Karina tries to hide her illness, Milian notices her declining health and does everything he can to search for a cure. As readers bask in the sweetness of their relationship, the story cruelly foreshadows tragedy for the couple. Read on Tapas

7 Tatiana Cartienne and Kynell Townsend Royal Marriage, written by Portofino and Hiyo and illustrated by Kangryeol

Tatiana Cartienne has the perfect life. Apart from her engagement to a handsome duke, she’s also the belle of high society and the apple of her family’s eye. When she discovers her fiancée’s infidelity, she immediately breaks up their engagement. Tatiana’s mother uses her influence to isolate her daughter from society and force her to reconcile with the duke. However, the young woman refuses and marries Kynell Townsend, who is a war hero scorned for his commoner status. Despite being insulted and publicly humiliated by her mother in the past, Kynell never blames Tatiana for her family’s actions. He treats his wife as an individual and respects her independence. At the same time, she encourages her husband to communicate his feelings and appreciate his worth. Tatiana’s assertiveness and charisma balance Kynell’s quiet stoicism, and their devotion to one another is heartwarming. Read on Tapas

6 Terryl Rihan and Cesio Debrik I Wasn’t the Cinderella, written by Kim Joon Kee, Juan, and G-ANNE and illustrated by Doba

Terryl Winterglass is called “Cinderella” for her romance with Jemon Debrik, the son of a marquess. After he insults her family’s status and breaks up with her, the young woman learns that her father is a powerful duke. As Terryl trains to become his successor, she reinvents herself as a confident aristocrat and swears vengeance against her former lover. She teams up with Jemon’s adoptive brother, Cesio, who plans to take revenge on his abusive family by claiming the marquess’ seat. Terryl’s noble-minded pursuits often challenge Cesio’s morally gray views. While they sometimes clash, their overall dynamic is balanced, and both partners are treated as equals in their relationship. Cesio’s unpredictability raises some red flags, but he constantly shows faith in Terryl’s judgment and respects her as an individual. The couple’s witty banter is often entertaining, especially when the lord’s flirtations are dismissed by his fiancée. Read on Tapas

5 Cha Jooahn and Yoo Dojin An Hour of Romance, created by Kim Myeongmi

Jooahn is known for being a cold person who is excellent at her job. Dojin, on the other hand, is known for being a sweetheart who is terrible at his job. When their company goes to a temple for their retreat, a mysterious monk causes them to switch bodies for an hour every day. This forces the pair to set aside their differences and work together to solve their problem. Since the switch occurs at random, the two leads are forced to learn about each other’s quirks and mannerisms. As Jooahn learns about Dojin’s life, she realizes she has judged him too harshly. The young man also starts seeing his boss as a human being with her own traumas. Since the manhwa is a slow burn, the characters walk several miles in each other’s shoes before falling in love. Read on TappyToon

4 Elena Taylor and Hugh Godwin Long After the Ending, created by Dorae and Wonderdream

After waking up as the villainess of a romance novel, Elena Taylor refuses to follow the story. As her relationship counseling business takes off, Duchess Godwin enlists her services to turn her son into a gentleman. Elena’s new assignment proves to be difficult, since Hugh Godwin is uncouth, burly, and the total opposite of a refined aristocrat. As they spend time together, the young woman learns that beneath his rough exterior, the duke houses a heart of gold. Elena’s outgoing nature, combined with Hugh’s gentle-giant personality, makes for an adorable romance. It’s refreshing to see a couple that can communicate openly and work together to solve misunderstandings. The two always want what is best for each other, and are prepared to make sacrifices for the other person’s well-being. This is shown by Elena’s willingness to distance herself after the duchess threatens to disown her son. It’s also seen when Hugh complies with his mother’s demands to keep her from shutting down Elena’s business. Read on Webtoon

Emotional and captivating love stories that perfectly capture the multitudes of romance, with all of its inherent difficulties and potential highs,

3 Valia Dean and Shuden Gartt Valia, Tribute to Shuden, written by Flowing Honey and illustrated by Hash and Nono

After dying a tragic death in her first life, Valia wakes up in the past as her younger self. To save her adoptive grandfather, she volunteers to be a tribute bride for the Guelle Empire. This leads her to marry Marquess Shuden Gartt, whose reputation as a cold man is nothing compared to her misfortunes. Even though he’s destined to love someone else, the young lord only shows kindness towards his wife. However, Valia is determined to save herself from heartbreak, so she tries her best not to fall for Shuden. Despite the circumstances of their marriage, Shuden is always kind and patient towards Valia, even reminding her that as spouses, they stand as equals. After constantly rejecting offers for a duchy, the young lord accepts the title to prevent other nobles from looking down on his wife’s family background. Valia’s affirmations remind Shuden that he’s worthy of being loved and that he doesn’t have to suffer alone. While it takes several months for them to confess, the couple has always maintained a stable relationship with each other. Read on Pocket Comics

2 Judith Maibaum and Ruediger Winterwald The Villainess Flips the Script!, written by Garin and illustrated by Ken

In this underrated isekai manhwa, a woman wakes up as the protagonist’s abusive aunt, Judith Maibaum. Unlike the original character, she plans to love her nephew and treat him with kindness until his aristocratic uncle arrives. When Ruediger Winterwald does come for Luca, the child refuses to let go of his aunt. This causes Judith to raise Luca alongside him, all while protecting the child from his tragic fate. Like the story itself, Judith and Ruediger’s relationship subverts many common isekai tropes. While he physically embodies the “cold male lead” archetype, Ruediger shows nothing but warmth towards his love interest. His expressionless delivery often leads to misunderstandings, but he is earnest and sincere. Judith is hilariously patient as she picks up on his mannerisms and blunt way of speaking. The couple shares a mature relationship with open communication and several heartwarming moments. Read on Tapas

1 Leah Hildebrandt and Roxant Kroemer Bring the Love, created by Zestkyo

Leah Hildebrant is a former knight and the eldest daughter of her family. After her brother fails to return from his travels, she steps up as head of the family. Due to the pressures from society, Leah is forced to marry in order to secure her position. When Roxant Kroemer, the intimidating eldest son of his family, proposes marriage, she accepts. Leah soon learns that her new partner is a kind man beneath his cold facade. While they express themselves differently, Leah and Roxant are both said to have strong personalities. Their romance develops organically as they appreciate each other’s flaws and work together to overcome their insecurities. One of the best aspects of their relationship is their open communication and honesty, which many stories often ignore for the sake of drama. Roxant and Leah have a mutual respect for boundaries and stand as equals in their relationship. Overall, the couple’s sweet love story and healthy communication make them the most underrated couple from romance manhwa. Read on TappyToon
