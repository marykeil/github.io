One-Punch Man Confirms Saitama s Greatest Pupil, & It Isn t Genos

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 3 minutes 
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Sonic’s repeated attacks on Saitama have paid off, as he displays his improved abilities in Chapter #197 of One-Punch Man. Sonic compares his opponents to Saitama and realizes they can’t even compare, motivating him to aim to surpass Saitama in strength. While Genos’ growth depends on his cyborg upgrades, Sonic has only improved through his attacks on Saitama, proving he is actually a better “disciple”.

Literature Review

One villain’s repeated attempts to challenge Saitama have led to him becoming phenomenally stronger as a result, and Saitama may not even know it.

Warning: Spoilers for One-Punch Man Chapter #197 (JP)/#195(EN)One-Punch Man chapter #197 puts recurring villain Speed-o’-Sound Sonic in the spotlight, but it turns out that Sonic has improved a lot throughout the series, and surprisingly, it’s all thanks to Saitama. In fact, based on his growth on display in this chapter, it’s fair to say that Sonic has been a more successful student to Saitama than even Genos.

Chapter #197 sees Sonic meeting Flashy Flash at a designated spot for what was intended to be a battle to the death. The two haven’t really seen each other since they were children at the Ninja Village together, and thus Flashy Flash has no idea of Sonic’s abilities in the present. As it turns out, neither do fans, as Sonic’s abilities are actually a match for Flashy Flash, who is a top-tier, S-Class hero.

Flashy Flash is impressed, and wonders if together their strength might be enough to defeat the Ninja Village’s leader. At that moment, however, a large group of ninja from the village appear, intending to kill Flashy Flash themselves.

The English version of One-Punch Man, published by Viz, has a different numbering due to the authors adding or re-doing chapters in the original Japanese version.

Sonic’s Desire to Defeat Saitama Has Driven Him to Greater Heights

When faced with the mob of highly-trained, elite ninja, Sonic stands up to them, claiming his duel with Flashy Flash supersedes theirs and threatens to fight them as well. They’re shocked at his audacity, but then it’s revealed that Sonic is comparing their strength with Saitama’s, and it doesn’t even begin to measure up. Saitama has set a new bar for what true strength is to Sonic, a bar that he intends to aim for and surpass. Flashy Flash notes that Sonic must have done a lot of training, and it’s clear in the context that this was training to take down Saitama.

Even Sonic’s most casual defeats at Saitama’s hands seem to have led to him learning about his deficiencies and training to overcome them. Since Saitama won’t kill Sonic, he can try again and again, improving in some way each time while discovering new weaknesses that he needs to patch. In a way, Saitama is behaving almost like an old master, with Sonic as his pupil, even though he doesn’t realize it. Sonic has grown up in the harsh Ninja Village and is particularly used to this kind of training. If Sonic’s abilities are now on par with an S-Class hero, then he’s already improved substantially from his first appearances.

Genos Has Not Gained Much Power From Being Around Saitama

Compared to Sonic’s progress, Saitama’s self-appointed disciple, Genos, seems to be lagging. Recently, Genos even had a sad realization about Saitama, concluding that their relationship was one-sided. In fact, Genos’ progress in terms of power depends on his cyborg upgrades, so there is not much he can learn from Saitama. While both have actually benefitted from each other’s presence in emotional terms, from a strength perspective it was Sonic who gained most from Saitama. Given Sonic’s progress, he can definitely claim that he, and not Genos, is Saitama’s most successful “disciple” in One-Punch Man.

One-Punch Man is available from Viz Media.
