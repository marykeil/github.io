10 Best Starter Manhwa to Read for Beginners

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Manhwa is becoming increasingly popular as a genre, and there are some series that function as the best introduction for new fans. The God of High School is a martial arts manhwa that combines realism and the supernatural, making it a solid introduction to the genre. Tower of God revolutionizes the tower-climbing story structure and offers deep characters, making it a wonderful introduction to the wide range of themes in manhwa.

Literature Review

The world of manhwa can seem daunting, but with the right introductory series, there is something for everybody.

The best starter manhwa for beginners of the genre consists of series that not only serve as the face of manhwa, but offer unique stories that once served as the foundation of their respective niches. Some are older series that set the tone for game-element stories, and some are romance-driven manhwa that are often overlooked but serve as defining series of the genre. Although manhwa and manhua (the Chinese equivalent) are often interchangeable, as their pronunciations are the same and they’re both full-color comics, when discussing entering the medium as a beginner, it is beneficial to focus on just Korean Manhwa first. As opposed to manhua, manhwa typically feature crisper art styles and follow a standardized chapter length, whereas manhua are less consistent. As a result, Korean manhwa is what first paved the way for non-Japanese source material to enter the anime industry, which is why it is best for budding fans.

10 Best Manhwa Every Fan of Isekai Anime Needs to Read
While isekai has been popular with anime and manga for years, there are a surprising number of manhwa that pose as formidable competition.

10 The God of High School Takes On Modern Martial Arts By Park Yong-Je

The God of High School is one of the best martial arts manhwa, as the series begins with realism and eventually incorporates the supernatural. The God of High School follows Jin Mo-Ri, who has been invited to a tournament to decide the strongest high schooler. Having learned Taekwondo from his grandfather, Mo-Ri shows great hope of succeeding in this mysteriously planned tournament. Although Mo-Ri is typically portrayed as an air-headed main character similar to Luffy from One Piece, when it’s time for him to get serious, it’s almost like another version of him comes into being. With heavy connections to Sun Wukong from the classic novel Journey To the West,The God of High School is a solid introduction to martial arts manhwa. READ ON Webtoon

9 Lookism Writes Into Understanding Insecurities By Park Tae-Jun

Lookism is a complex manhwa that gives a look into general sentiments concerning Korean beauty standards while doubling as an example of how drama and comedy often operate in manhwa. The story follows Park Hyung Suk, who is originally portrayed as insecure and called overweight, as he is clearly unsatisfied with his current standing. He transfers schools in hopes that things will change, but he is greeted with a metamorphosis much more extreme than ever expected. Waking up with the shell of his old body next to him, Hyung Suk realizes he has awoken in a new, more attractive body. Thus, Hyung Suk begins to explore the other side of beauty standards and the surprising problems that come with them. Overall, Lookism, which received an anime adaptation in 2022, is a great introduction to the drama and comedy manhwa genres. A story of many genres, Lookism’s anime ranks as one of Netflix’s best isekai series, and even challenges the boundaries of the otherworldly anime phenomenon.

READ ON Webtoon

8 Omniscient Reader’s Viewpoint Tries To Overlay Perspectives By sing N song and Sleepy-C

First released in 2020, Omniscient Reader’s Viewpoint follows the rising popularity of its source material web novel, and puts its story into potent images and color. The story follows Kim Dokja, the protagonist of the story portrayed to audiences, but not particularly the protagonist within the narrative. To clarify, this is because the premise of the manhwa follows that the world has turned into a post-apocalyptic landscape that resembles that of the web novel Kim Dokja finished reading called Three Ways to Survive the Apocalypse. The two worlds have overlapped, and even characters in the web novel make appearances in Kim Dokja’s reality, including the “real” protagonist, Yoo Joonghyuk. However, using his advantage as the only reader who’s finished reading the novel, Kim Dokja embarks on a treacherous road towards finding the optimal path to survival. READ ON Webtoon 1:56

7 Wind Breaker Is the Go-To Sports Manhwa By Jo Yongseok

Wind Breaker is an action sports manhwa that reads almost like a Korean rendition of the typical sports anime template, but with its own original twists and innovation. The story follows the multi-talented Jo Jahyeon, who’s also known as Jay. He’s a motivated student who isn’t disillusioned by his position as student council president and doesn’t have many friends. However, Jay has yet another passion and talent in his life: biking. Using unorthodox techniques with much more skill than he is made out to have, Jay attracts the attention of his classmate, Yoon Min woo, who urges Jay to join his biking group, but is met with rejection. As the boys have further interactions and Jay’s unique style is explored, a riveting story unfolds that gives a perfect lens into manhwa sports and realism. READ ON Webtoon

6 The Legend of the Northern Blade Tells a Complex Story of Revenge By Woogack and Hae Min

The Legend of the Northern Blade is a Murim martial arts manhwa that released its first chapters in 2019, taking the knowledge of various predecessors in the genre and refining it into a representative work of all martial arts manhwa. The rather underrated story of The Legend of the Northern Blade follows Moowon, the son of the former sect leader of the Northern Heavenly Sect, who ends his own life to protect Moowon in the tumultuous conflict between the sect and their enemy, the nefarious Silent Night. Moowon trains arduously in the mountains in hopes of exacting his revenge against the indirect killers of his father. He wishes further to even uncover the secrets that led to his father’s downfall, and cut down his enemies with the technique that he’s retained from his time in the sect. Although the premise is rather straightforward, The Legend of the Northern Blade is a substantial rendering of the Murim world in manhwa, giving a perfect sampling to any potential fans. READ ON Tappytoon

5 Who Made Me a Princess Takes a Crack At Shojo Isekai By Platus and Spoon

First released in 2017, Who Made Me a Princess is a completed manhwa series that aggregates and blends genres and demographics into a comprehensively entertaining story that takes the best from all worlds. Following the main character, Athanasia, the story not only takes the shojo perspective by focusing on a female protagonist, it also ties in the genre of isekai, meaning to travel to another world. Previously a Korean woman from the modern age, Athanasia plays the role of a princess in a tragic novel that she knows the ending to. As a result, she tries to do everything in her power to survive the inevitable bad ending, from playing palace politics to praying for a butterfly effect to flap its wings. This blending of genres and story influences creates a fresh look at the manhwa genre, and is perfect for any fans wishing to dip their fingers into a tantalizing story of suspense, drama, and comedy. READ ON Tappytoon

4 Noblesse Establishes a Vampire Classic By Son Jae-ho and Lee Gwang-su

Noblesse is a wonderfully crafted manhwa that has been around as early as 2007, with its stories of vampires and school days having cemented itself as one of the forebears of the manhwa medium, and even receiving an anime adaptation in 2020. The narrative follows Cadis Etrama Di Raizel, or Rai as some of the characters come to call him, who is a legendary being who awakens after 800 years. However, the world has changed and Rai’s title as the Noblesse brings with him more troubles within the modernized world that he has to work to adapt to. Luckily, he gets help from his servant, Frankenstein, who has been around, understands the world, and even runs his own school. From there, a riveting story of vampires unfolds, giving starting fans a taste of the dustier side of the manhwa industry. READ ON Webtoon

3 Tower of God Is the Tower-Climbing Series That Started It All By SIU

First released in 2010, Tower of God received a successful anime adaptation and is one of the biggest names in manhwa. The series has solidified itself as a household name through its focus and revolutionizing of the tower-climbing story structure. It also features deep and heartfelt characters who the main character, Twenty-Fifth Bam, meets along his journey. At first, Bam was all alone, without any semblance of human warmth, until the blonde-haired Rachel reaches into his life, and exits just as fast to pursue her dreams in the mysterious Tower. By perhaps sheer willpower or miracle that isn’t immediately revealed, Bam makes his way into the Tower as an irregular, someone who isn’t chosen to climb the Tower but finds their way in anyway. In this new environment, Bam meets obstacle after obstacle in the arduous climb filled with strange magic and even stranger people. Tower of God is a wonderful introduction to almost everything manhwa has to offer, at least on a thematic level. READ ON Webtoon

2 Eleceed Falls on the Fluffy Side of Manhwa By Son Jae-Ho and Kim Hye-Jin (ZHENA)

Eleceed is a unique action superpower manhwa that has taken the manhwa world by storm through its cute visuals and carefully constructed premise. The story follows main characters Jinwoo, a boy who has always hidden his abilities, and Kayden, a passing master taking the form of a fluffy cat due to his injuries, through a world of supernatural abilities and the awakeners that utilize them. By an interesting turn of events, Jinwoo, someone who cares deeply for the neighborhood’s stray cats, comes under the tutelage of Kayden, who introduces the boy to the broader world of awakeners that he’d been avoiding. Furthermore, Kayden teaches Jinwoo to more efficiently utilize his powers of superspeed and reflexes in the hopes that the pair can fight against the forces of evil that spring up at every turn. For beginners, Eleceed is a must-read not only because of its straightforward premise, but because of the often funny and cute interactions between the series’ characters. READ ON Webtoon

1 Solo Leveling Spearheaded Manhwa Popularization By Chugong, Jang-Sung-rak, and Disciples

Based on the immensely popular web novel of the same name, Solo Leveling released the first chapters of its manhwa in 2018, and established itself as one of the best-known names in the medium, and with good reason. The height of its prowess has even brought about an anime adaptation that began airing in January 2024. Although there are differences between Solo Leveling’s manhwa and anime, A-1 Pictures has done a stand-up job of creating a memorable experience for a wide audience, from long-time fans to new viewers. Solo Leveling follows the rise of Sung Jinwoo in an urban fantasy landscape that features dungeons, otherwise known as gates, monsters, deity-like figures, and more. However, where Solo Leveling goes above and beyond is with its close ties to the real world, as events all take place in recognizable districts in Seoul, and later other parts of the world. Both the anime and manhwa are amazing avenues for fans of any demographic to dive into the world of manhwa, starting with Sung Jinwoo’s monumental rise to strength.
