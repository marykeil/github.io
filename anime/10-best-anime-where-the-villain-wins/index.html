10 Best Anime Where the Villain Wins

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Some anime end with the villains winning, emphasizing the hopeless nature of the story or what’s best for the plot. Notable examples include Berserk , Hellsing Ultimate , Shaman King , and Code Geass . These victories can range from the villains achieving their goals to the villains actually being the good guys through unexpected plot twists.

Literature Review

There are plenty of great stories that end with the villains beating the heroes, and here are 10 of the best anime with that kind of storytelling.

Not every anime ends with the heroes defeating the villains. While the heroes winning is to be expected, as with all mediums in fiction, it’s very common for stories to end with the villains being the ones to get everything they want, while the heroes, unfortunately, end up losing to varying degrees. As for why so many end with the heroes losing, the reason tends to vary from story to story. Just like in any medium, an anime will have the villain win if it’s meant to emphasize the hopeless nature of the story, sometimes it will be a pyrrhic victory where both sides get what they want, and in some cases, the villains will win because that somehow ends up being what’s best for the story.

Whatever the case, there are plenty of anime where the villains are the ones to win, and a few in particular are especially worth highlighting.

The Most Satisfying Anime Villains Deaths Of All Time
Many anime feature terrifying villains who demonstrate frightening strength, abilities, & cunning – and there’s nothing better than their downfall.

10 Berserk Still Hasn’t Given Griffith His Just Desserts Based On the Manga By Kentaro Miura

One notable example of an anime ending with the villain winning is the 1997 Berserk anime. At the end of Berserk, Griffith, in his pursuit of power, sacrifices the Band of the Hawk to become the God Hand Femto, even going so far as to rape Casca in front of Guts, and the anime simply ends there with no explanation of what happened next. Even in the manga, this is still the case as Griffith has yet to be punished for his crimes, and at the rate the story moves, there’s no way of telling how long that will take.

9 Hellsing Ultimate’s Villains Got Exactly What They Wanted Based On the Manga By Kouta Hirano

Another anime that ended with the villains winning is 2006’s Hellsing Ultimate. The main goals of Millenium, the main antagonists of Hellsing Ultimate, were to fight the biggest war in history and to kill Alucard, and while Millenium is ultimately defeated, they still succeeded in both waging an incredibly massive and bloody war and defeating Alucard by using Schrödinger to negate his existence. Granted, Alucard managed to come back after 30 years, but Millenium still got everything they were hoping to get out of fighting Hellsing, so it can be seen as a pyrrhic victory for the heroes at best. Watch On Crunchyroll

8 Shaman King’s Villain Was Unstoppable From Day One Based On The Manga By Hiroyuki Takei

Many stories will have a villain win because of how utterly unstoppable, with a perfect example of that being 2021’s Shaman King. Hao was established early on to be so powerful that no one could beat him in a fair fight, and sure enough, Hao became Shaman King completely unimpeded, and the only reason no one dies is that Hao was talked into putting off his genocide of humanity. It’s unlikely that Hao will ever be a full-fledged villain again, but it’s still surprising that the original series had the heroes so utterly outclassed from start to finish. Watch On Netflix

7 Cyberpunk: Edgerunners’ Prequel Status Solidified Its Downer Ending Based On the Tabletop Game By Mike Pondsmith Adam Smasher in Cyberpunk: Edgerunners

Another anime where the villain wins by being untouchable is 2022’s Cyberpunk: Edgerunners. David and his mercenary crew continually drew more and more ire from the Arasaka Corporation, and when it seemed like they would win in the end, Adam Smasher, one of the major antagonists from Cyberpunk: 2077, appeared from basically nowhere to kill David and all of his remaining friends except for Lucy and Falco. Since the anime was a prequel to Cyberpunk: 2077, it makes sense that the Arasaka Corporation and Adam Smasher had to survive, but seeing it end with almost everyone dead was nonetheless heart-wrenching. Watch On Netflix

Cyberpunk: Edgerunners
David Martinez (Kenichiro Ohashi) is a streetwise kid living in the slums of Night City in Cyberpunk: Edgerunners. To survive, David becomes an edgerunner, also known as cyberpunk, taking on mercenary jobs outside the system. Together with a group of new and veteran edgerunners, David will shoot for the moon to escape the underbelly of the increasingly cyber-obsessed city. Unfortunately, the law isn’t the only group that the edgerunners will have to contend with, as other heavily cyber-augmented humans will pose an even more significant threat. 

6 Iron-Blooded Orphans Made Its Villains Good Guys Original Story By Tatsuyuki Nagai & Mari Okada

Stories can also end with the villains winning by suddenly making the villains the good guys, and one example of that is 2015’s Mobile Suit Gundam: Iron-Blooded Orphans. Despite their best efforts, Tekkadan was completely destroyed by Rustal and the Arianrhod Fleet, but surprisingly, the timeskip at the end of Iron-Blooded Orphans revealed that Rustal enacted all the reforms Tekkadan had wanted to have passed. It’s implied that Rustal did it largely out of pragmatism, but seeing the villains do everything the heroes wanted to do with no consequences for their crimes still ended the series on a sour note. Watch On Crunchyroll

5 Code Geass Had Lelouch Get Everything He Wanted Out Of His Death Original Story By Goro Taniguchi & Ichiro Okouchi

Another notable anime where the villain wins by being the good guy all along is 2006’s Code Geass. Lelouch definitively became thevillain of Code Geass’ final act as he engaged in increasingly heinous acts, but the Code Geass series finale revealed that it was all on purpose, as Lelouch wanted to unite the world against a common foe and usher in an era of peace upon his death. The movie continuity had things not go as smoothly as the anime, but it’s still a prime example of the villain winning by secretly being on the side of good.

The Most Shocking Anime Deaths No One Saw Coming
Anime is no stranger to sudden and shocking deaths, and there are some that stood out, making fans jump out of their seats.

Watch On Crunchyroll

4 Devilman Crybaby Perfectly Modernized Its Villains’ Victories Based On the Manga By Go Nagai

The Devilman manga is a classic example of a story ending with the villains winning, and almost 50 years later, that was perfectly modernized in 2018’s Devilman Crybaby. Just like in the manga, Devilman Crybaby saw Akira lose all his loved ones to Satan’s forces, and not only did Akira die fighting Satan, but God, heavily implied to also be evil, suddenly appeared at the end to destroy the Earth. There’s no way of viewing Devilman’s story as anything other than a complete victory for its villains, and Devilman Crybaby did a great job of bringing that into a modern day setting. Watch On Netflix

3 The End Of Evangelion Has One of the Biggest Downer Endings In Anime Original Story By Hideaki Anno & Kazuya Tsurumaki

Another older example of an anime ending with the villains winning is 1997’s The End of Evangelion. SEELE, the main villains of Neon Genesis Evangelion, wanted to unite humanity into a single consciousness through the Third Impact, and in The End of Evangelion, despite Shinji’s efforts to stop them, SEELE successfully initiated the Third Impact and destroyed the world and everyone in it, save for Shinji and Asuka. While it’s unclear if SEELE got everything they wanted, the film still ended with SEELE defeating the heroes, and it wouldn’t be until the Rebuild of Evangelion series that that would be rectified. Watch On Netflix

2 Panty & Stocking’s Villains Win Through an Utterly Ridiculous Twist Original Story By Hiroyuki Imaishi & Geek Fleet

Twist endings are one of the easiest ways villains end up winning, and a great example of that in anime is 2010’s Panty & Stocking with Garterbelt. The anime seemed to have a straightforward ending with the heroes defeating the villains one last time, but at the last second, Stocking suddenly killed Panty, revealed herself to be a demon, and cut Panty’s body into 666 pieces and spread them around the world. It completely undoes everything the heroes accomplished, but the absurdist nature of the series does a lot to keep the villains’ victory from bringing down the story too much. Watch On Crunchyroll

1 Madoka Magica Rebellion’s Villain Was Someone No One Expected Original Story By Akiyuki Shinbo, Yukihiro Miyamoto, & Gen Urobuchi

Another anime where the villain wins through a twist ending, and the last notable anime to discuss, is 2013’s Puella Magi Madoka Magica The Movie: Rebellion. While it initially seemed like Kyubey was the only villain in the film, after Kyubey was defeated in Madoka Magica: Rebellion, Homura was revealed to have been the true villain who used the events of the story to steal Madoka’s position as God and rewrite reality to be with Madoka. Making Homura the new villain completely changed the franchise, and that’s what makes the film one of the best anime where the villain wins.
