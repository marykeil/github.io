Hazbin Hotel s Alastor Accidentally Proved He s Capable of Redemption (He s Just Hiding It)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 800 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Alastor, the fan-favorite demon from Hazbin Hotel, has shown a glimpse of redemption by saving an innocent demon from being killed for meat. Although Alastor still has a long way to go, this act demonstrates a sliver of redeemable humanity within him. While Alastor’s true intentions and plans remain sinister, there is a possibility for his character to change and be influenced by Charlie and her friends in future seasons.

Literature Review

While the series seems to show him as irredeemable, Hazbin Hotel’s prequel comic actually showed that Alastor is secretly hiding a good side.

Fan-favorite demon Alastor from Hazbin Hotel has proven that he’s capable of redemption—he’s just hiding it. Premiering in January, Amazon Prime’s newest series, Hazbin Hotel, was met with record-breaking success by fans. There are many fan-favorites among the series’ well-written characters, but one stands out above the rest. Alastor, best known as the Radio Demon, has won fans over with his menacing demeanor hidden beneath an uncanny smile. His design and character have made him the most irredeemable demon Charlie has ever come across…or so fans would think.

In 2020, series creator Vivienne Medrano, alongside the talented creative team of Winters_Ichor, Faustisse, Lunetta and Magpie concocted a prequel webcomic to Hazbin Hotel starring Alastor titled “A Day in the Afterlife.” While the comic has since been taken down from the show’s official website, the story follows Alastor as he navigates a day in Hell. For the most part, the comic stays fairly true to his character as he hides a nefarious personality behind a misleading smile. However, near the end of the comic, one fleeting moment proves he’s as capable of redemption as the other demons.

After visiting a butcher shop for venison, Alastor hears the butcher attempt to kill an innocent lamb demon, presumably to use their flesh for meat. Rather than continue with his day, Alastor returns to kill the butcher and save the lamb, taking the butcher’s flesh to eat over the venison he purchased.

Alastor Can Be Redeemed, But He Has a Long Road Ahead of Him

While Alastor ultimately stays true to his character in this fleeting glimpse of good will by taking the butcher’s flesh, the fact that he actually goes back to save the lamb demon from being made into meat shows that he has a sliver of redeemable humanity still within him. However, this doesn’t necessarily mean that he will have an easy road to redemption. The first season’s finale ultimately gives viewers a glimpse at his ulterior motives. Plus, he tends to be a character who expects a fair exchange. The comic even shows this when he leaves the butcher shop with the butcher’s flesh. He could very well leave this altercation on a note of good will, but instead he takes the meat of the demon he kills as exchange for saving the lamb’s life. He’s a long way off from showing any growth as a selfless character, especially since he actively hides this side of himself from the rest of Hell, but that doesn’t make it impossible.

When Might Fans Start Seeing Change in Alastor?

The comic’s ending raises the question of whether Alastor can truly be a success story for Charlie’s hotel. However, Heaven likely won’t be seeing the Radio Demon at the Pearly Gates anytime soon. The series has been adamant about making Alastor a sinister character who has plans to unleash chaos at some point in the upcoming seasons, with his big solo number from the series finale showing he wants more than what fans expected. However, the series could see a change in Alastor’s character closer to its end as his nefarious plans begin to come to light. It’s entirely possible for Charlie and her friends to see what he has planned and attempt to make him see the errors of his ways.

Of course, only series creator Vivienne Medrano knows what lies in store for Alastor’s character as her story progresses. With the show greenlit for a second season before the show even premiered, it’s clear that she has a strong possibility of seeing the story through to the very end. It will be interesting to see how Alastor’s character changes over the course of the series, but based on his solo comic from before Hazbin Hotel even premiered, it’s clear that he has more of a chance of being redeemed than fans believed.
