12 Ways Power Rangers Comics Completely Redefined The Original Pink Ranger

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Kimberly Ann Hart’s legacy as the greatest Pink Ranger extends beyond the original show, with spin-off material and upcoming comics written by the original actress. The Power Rangers comics have recontextualized Kimberly’s character, adding new layers to her and showcasing her leadership skills. Future Kim, known as the Ranger Slayer, leads the resistance against an evil Tommy Oliver, proving her adept fighting skills and intelligence. She later breaks free from mind control and becomes a hero.

Literature Review

Kimberly Hart’s time as the original Pink Ranger has gone down in Power Rangers history as legendary, especially thanks to the BOOM! Studios comics.

Kimberly Ann Hart is fondly remembered in history as the greatest Pink Ranger in Power Rangers history. One could make an entire list out of Kimberly’s best scenes and moments from the show, and her legacy extends to the character getting her own spin-off material in other media, including one upcoming comic written by the character’s original actress. Speaking of comics, the Power Rangers franchise has found a new life through comic books, particularly when it comes to the original Pink Ranger. Comic series like Mighty Morphin Power Rangers and Go Go Power Rangers have gone out of their way consistently to expand the lore of the original series as much as they recontextualize old characters in new ways. Kimberly is one of the characters who seem to have benefited the most from such a renaissance. On top of reminding fans why they fell in love with her character to begin with, the comics add new layers to paint her in a new light.

12 Future Kim Leads The Resistance Go Go Power Rangers #9 by Ryan Parrott, Dan Mora, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

After the end of the previous issue teased the arrival of the Ranger Slayer, this issue starts by showing flashbacks of how the original Pink Ranger became the Ranger Slayer. In an alternate reality dominated by an evil Tommy Oliver (rechristened into Lord Drakkon), Kimberly leads a resistance group in an attempt to evade his dictatorship. She’s shown to think on her feet in dire situations, as well as still being handy with a bow and arrow without the need of a Morpher. Keep in mind that in this timeline, since Tommy becomes Drakkon before joining the team, Kimberly doesn’t experience many of the moments that would define her in later seasons of the show. Without the character development that would prove crucial to Kimberly, she still manages to shine in the World of the Coinless, proving that many of the leadership skills she develops in the main canon come natural to her. She becomes such a threat that Drakkon feels compelled to mind-control her into becoming his Ranger Slayer.

11 Taking Out the Whole Team as The Ranger Slayer Go Go Power Rangers #9 by Ryan Parrott, Dan Mora, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

In the same issue as the previous entry, future Kimberly makes an immediate impact on present-day Angel Grove. First, she takes out Goldar and his Putties, forcing the former into retreating. Then, just when the Rangers think they’ve met a new ally, she attacks them all, knocking them out one-by-one before fleeing the scene herself. She makes an example out of both the good guys and the bad guys, proving herself as an adept fighter no matter whose side she is on.

10 Present-Day Kim Breaks Future Kim’s Ranger Slayer Mind Control Go Go Power Rangers #11 by Ryan Parrott, Dan Mora, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

Future Kim’s first few issues in the comics aren’t just a showcase of her new run as the Ranger Slayer, but also a showcase of the original present-day Kim. Present-day Kimberly’s crowning moment during this arc comes when the Ranger Slayer attacks the Command Center, forcing a one-on-one between the two. Thinking fast on her feet, present Kim presses a button that electrocutes her future self so dramatically that it breaks Drakkon’s mind control, opening the door for future Kim’s hero turn.

9 Ranger Slayer Kim Relives the Juice Bar Go Go Power Rangers #11 by Ryan Parrott, Dan Mora, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

While most of the moments on this list showcase Kimberly’s best skills as a Ranger, this is admittedly just a wholesome moment that gives a traumatized alternate Kimberly a rare moment of peace. As future Kim takes time to repair her devices and reevaluate who she is now that she’s free of Drakkon’s control, she takes some downtime at the juice bar with present Kim. Whether it’s reuniting with her resistance co-leader Bulk or drinking dozens of milkshakes, a trained killer gets a moment to decompress for once, reminiscing on a life she’s long been deprived of.

8 Ranger Slayer’s Plan for Tommy Saves the Universe Go Go Power Rangers #12 by Ryan Parrott, Dan Mora, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

Before leaving the present, future Kimberly visits Tommy Oliver at a time before he becomes a Ranger. She unexpectedly shoots him with a green arrow, yet he’s unphased. It isn’t clear what has happened until the conclusion of the Shattered Grid storyline. That arrow is a shard of the Chaos Crystal that gives him his Green Ranger powers. When Lord Drakkon stabs him with a similar shard, it puts Tommy’s soul in limbo, allowing him to enter the Morphin Grid for a final battle with Drakkon, which he wins. By proxy, Tommy saves the world because of the Ranger Slayer.

7 Jason Gives Present-Day Kim the Dragon Dagger Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #30 by Kyle Higgins, Daniele Di Nicuolo, Simona Di Gianfelice, Walter Baiamonte, and Ed Dukeshire

Before the Ranger Slayer’s failsafe plan can go into effect, the present-day Rangers have to deal with the fallout of Tommy Oliver’s murder at the hands of Lord Drakkon. Drakkon is Tommy’s future self from an alternate reality where he not only remains evil after breaking Rita’s mind control, but also becomes a worldwide dictator. Since she watched his death happen on the night of their first date, Kimberly takes it the hardest, but there’s no time to grieve as Drakkon starts an all-out war with the Morphin Grid on the line. As the Rangers prepare for one final battle against Lord Drakkon’s army, Jason - who leads the charge for the Rangers, honoring the late Tommy Oliver by donning his Dragon Shield - gives Kimberly the order to pilot the Dragonzord. She’s confused about how she can do that with her Pterodactyl, until she turns around to see Jason handing her the Dragon Dagger. With the stakes being as high as they are, Jason wouldn’t offer such a big responsibility if Kim didn’t prove herself as a Ranger during this trying time, and it appears she does rightfully impress him.

6 Leading the Solar Rangers Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #31-39 by Marguerite Bennett, Simone Di Meo, French Carlomagno, Francesco Mortarino, Walter Baiamonte, Elenora Bruni, and Whitney Cogar

To defeat Lord Drakkon’s army, Rangers from multiple worlds, timelines, and eras join forces in the Beyond the Grid storyline to fight off his army. This Ranger team includes a returning Ranger Slayer. When Drakkon is defeated, the remaining Rangers find a whole new threat they have to neutralize in space. Joining forces with a mysterious Solar Ranger, future Kimberly gets to show off her skills as a leader by leading the ultimate Ranger dream team. Upon gaining new Solar powers and Zords, they take on an evil Morphin Master. Leaving her Ranger Slayer past behind her, Kimberly showcases she has what it takes to lead a team.

5 Becoming the Shazam of the Power Rangers Mighty Morphin Power Rangers: Pink by Brenden Fletcher, Kelly Thompson, Danielle Di Nicuolo, Sarah Stern, and Ed Dukeshire

The Mighty Morphin Power Rangers: Pink miniseries takes place after Kimberly leaves the team in the original show, though she now has to fight off monsters during the Pan-Global Games. Like what happens when trouble finds any Power Ranger, she suits up for action, proving she never lost any pep in her step. When she asks Zordon for help, she learns that, thanks to an untapped plot hole, she basically becomes Shazam by being able to not only still have her Ranger powers, but share them with others.

4 Future Kim Takes Over Drakkon’s Empire Power Rangers: Ranger Slayer #1 by Ryan Parrott, Dan Mora, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

Meanwhile, when the former Ranger Slayer returns to her timeline, a newly Drakkon-less world isn’t sunshine and rainbows just yet. The power vacuum created by Drakkon’s absence has allowed Scorpina to take over his regime. Even worse, Kim has to deal with a returning Rita Repulsa, thanks to the original Yellow Ranger, Trini. When both matters are dealt with in stride, the people are ready for Kimberly to lead them into the future. This Kimberly’s already shown that she knows how to lead, but becoming the leader the world deserves to undo Drakkon’s damage is the closest she gets to a happy ending at this point.

3 Kim Stops Herself From Killing Drakkon Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #52 by Ryan Parrott, Moises Hidalgo, Walter Baiamonte, Katia Ranalli, and Ed Dukeshire

Speaking of Drakkon, back in the present, a crazed version of the former Lord resurfaces. However, this version of Drakkon is attempting to warn the Rangers of a bigger threat on the horizon: the Empyreals. Naturally, they’re hesitant to trust him, so they keep him locked up in the Command Center’s quarters for the time being, but Kimberly especially hasn’t let go of how he tormented her friends. Still, though, when she has a kill shot on him, she doesn’t pull the trigger. She exhibits a heap of self-control and resistance towards a man she hates more than anything else on the planet.

2 Breaking Zordon’s Rule About Zords Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #103 by Melissa Flores and Simona Di Gianfelice

One moment where Kim does not exhibit self-control is when her ex, the new Green Ranger Matt, gets captured by Rita Repulsa in her new Mistress Vile era. Letting her emotions get the better of her and anxious to focus more on matter, she introduces her Firebird Zord almost immediately into a battle, despite the monster not growing yet and despite going against Zordon’s rules. This moment showcases just how scary a pissed-off Kimberly can be, but also how efficient she can be when she feels she’s run out of options and time is running out.

1 Leading the Ultimate Pink Ranger Team-Up Mighty Morphin #22 by Mat Groom, Moises Hidalgo, Raul Angulo, and Ed Dukeshire

Kimberly’s (arguably) greatest moment in either the show or the comics comes during a training simulation gone wrong where Kimberly’s forced to face down the rogues’ gallery ghosts of the Rangers’ past, present, and future. When all hope seems lost, the Pink Emissary appears, connecting her to the Morphin Grid to unlock her jaw-dropping true power and allowing her to summon the help of every Pink Ranger from the past, present, and future of Rangerhood. Kimberly Ann Hart’s leadership qualities aren’t debatable at this point, but being able to lead the ultimate Pink Ranger team-up proves why she’s one of the greatest Power Rangers of all time.
