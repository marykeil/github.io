10 Best Isekai Anime Every Beginner Should Watch Before Anything Else

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The best isekai anime gives beginners a taste of the genre without overwhelming them with complex premises. Different types of isekai anime exist, including reverse isekai, time travel reincarnation, hero summoning, and stuck-in-a-game. These introductory series cover common aspects and tropes of popular genres like shonen and adventure, creating a fresh world of fantasy and world-traveling premises.

Literature Review

Isekai anime is an ever-expanding genre of fantasy and innovation, but these series are perfect for fans who are looking for somewhere to start.

The best isekai anime every beginner should watch before anything else will give a hearty taste of the genre without dragging viewers into the rabbit hole of reincarnated swords and talking vending machines. Starter series tie common aspects and tropes of popular genres like shonen and adventure into a fresh world of fantasy and world-traveling premises. At the core, an isekai anime can be defined as any series that involves transporting a character, body, mind, or soul, to a world that does not fall within their established status quo. This results in an array of creative isekai anime, such as the reverse isekai or the time travel reincarnation, on top of the more common hero summoning or stuck-in-a-game isekai. The best introductory isekai series will cover these areas and more, all without making fans feel as if their understanding has been transported to another world like the characters.

10 Best Isekai Anime on Hulu Hulu boasts one of the biggest isekai anime catalogs, but the true gems will do more than just stand out.

10 Tsukimichi: Moonlit Fantasy Shows the Power of the Normal Human Based on the light novel by Azumi Kei and Matsumoto Mitsuaki

Tsukimichi: Moonlit Fantasy is a more recent isekai anime adaptation with its own modern innovations, but the series still serves as a solid introduction to the genre. The story follows Misumi Makoto, a normal boy on Earth whose family has a surprising secret. His parents have come from the Goddess’ World, a fantasy world that runs parallel to Earth. One day, Makoto is summoned to this fantasy world due to a deal his parents made with one of the world’s gods in the past, leaving Makoto to new adventures in this world where he has a physically overpowered isekai advantage from living on Earth, where the gravity and natural laws are much more demanding than those of the Goddess’ World. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

9 Ascendance of a Bookworm Dabbles In Medieval Rebirth Based on the light novel by Kazuki Miya and Shiina You

Ascendance of a Bookworm is the perfect isekai anime for lovers of all other genres, from shojo to literature, to historical fantasy, and more. The story follows the reincarnation of an unfortunate librarian who was crushed by her own beloved books. Reborn as Myne, the love for literature has not diminished, but bad luck seems to still be persistent because Myne is a 5-year-old girl of a commoner’s family in a period resembling medieval times. As a result, Myne’s immediate world is devoid of any books, as they are gatekept by the rich and influential. Not willing to resign herself to such a fate, Myne takes advantage of her memories of the modernized world and works towards making literature and books accessible to all, one invention at a time. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

10 Best Isekai Anime With Female Main Characters It may seem like the Isekai anime genre is shonen-dominated, but there are many amazing new world fantasies that follow female leads.

8 InuYasha Perfectly Blends Isekai and Shonen Based on the manga by Takahashi Rumiko

InuYasha is a classic shonen fantasy that inadvertently doubles as an isekai anime. However, it is difficult to catch at first because every supernatural event is smoothly blended into the overall narrative. When the main character, Kagome, makes her appearance in the first episode as a modern high school girl and falls into a magical well connected to a feudalistic world of demons and magic, an isekai adventure begins. Kagome meets the half-demon Inuyasha and the two embark on a magical journey to uncover the secrets of the world and reassemble the shattered Sacred Jewel. Throughout this entire process, which very much follows the mainstream shonen structure of the early 2000s InuYasha introduces elements of isekai as if they are sets of hors d’oeuvres to the genre. Kagome is questioned about her out-of-place clothing style, she worries about the possibilities of returning home, and later, there are even elements of Inuyasha exploring the modern world. These snippets will give any beginner a comfortable sampling of the isekai genre. WATCH ON Netflix

7 Log Horizon Is One of the Best Stuck-In-a-Game Anime Based on the light novel by Touno Mamare and Hana Kazuhiro

Log Horizon is an isekai anime that follows the popular template of characters getting stuck in a virtual reality video game. Because the series was released around the height of series like Sword Art Online, Log Horizon’s unique world structure and deep themes were inevitably overshadowed by the colors and sparkles of Kirito’s power trip and similar stories. Regardless, Log Horizon does an excellent job of constructing a realistic landscape of fantasy in which rational humans try to adapt to their situation. The game elements are still expertly wielded to push character and plot development, but the additional nuance of character relationships and their ability to reconstruct society in the video game world speaks to the work Log Horizon does for the isekai genre, as it creates a perfect introduction to the game isekai for beginner and veteran fans alike. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

6 The Devil is a Part-Timer! Is More Slice of Life Than Isekai Based on the light novel by Wagahara Satoshi

The Devil is a Part-Timer! is a light-hearted satire on the hero versus demon king story, and encapsulates all those ideas in a reverse isekai setting that introduces more real themes than ever expected. The concept of reverse isekai comes when the former Demon King of a fantasy world makes his way to modern-day Japan, a realistic and magic-restricting setting. In this new world, the Demon King, who renames himself as Maou Sadao, must adapt to modern life from the lowest rungs of society. He must fight tooth and nail just to land a part-time job at a fast food joint due to his lack of background and documentation. As he struggles to make ends meet, opportunities for humor explosively make their way into the narrative but still coincide with the potent adversity present in not only the fast food industry but any service-oriented jobs. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

5 Overlord Gives a Taste of a Villain Hero Based on the light novel by Maruyama Kugane and So-bin

Overlord is yet another twist on the stuck-in-a-game trope of isekai anime, as it follows a main character who gets stuck in his overpowered skeleton avatar in a world where it’s clear that he is the only player character, as opposed to being an NPC (non-player character). Despite this, Momonga, who later renames himself as Ainz Ooal Gown, quickly realizes his situation and that the world he is stuck in is much deeper than it appears. NPCs develop much more detailed emotions and variations, making them no different from the average person or character. As a result, Overlord is a story just as riveting as any other isekai anime, if not more. Furthermore, Overlord gives beginners a taste of both the overpowered protagonist archetype done well and the villain-esque main character portrayal that is often explored deeper in more niche series of the isekai genre. Overlord is slated to return with a 2024 film titled Overlord: The Holy Kingdom, which adapts additional sequel content from the light novel.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

4 Welcome to Demon School! Iruma-kun Is Trademark Isekai Humor Based on the manga by Nishi Osamu

Welcome to Demon School! Iruma-kun is an isekai anime that gives a casual but deep look into the demon world archetype of not only the isekai genre, but also the western fantasy genre. The story follows Iruma, a very unfortunate, but endlessly optimistic 14-year-old boy. Despite being exploited by his parents to embark on dangerous high-seas fishing journeys and similar excursions for money, Iruma is still able to smile. Even when events reach the boiling point of his parents selling him to a demon named Sullivan, Iruma is still able to keep his optimism intact despite his surprise. Luckily for him, Sullivan is actually a caring grandfather-type character who is the chairman of the Babyls Demon School and sends Iruma, a human, to attend his school. Iruma-kun’s premise not only creates suspense, but great humor, as Iruma learns more about his classmates, and they come to respect each other as peers. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

3 KonoSuba: God’s Blessing on This Wonderful World Is Gag Humor Meets Isekai Based on the Light Novel by Mishima Kurone and Akatsuki Natsume

KonoSuba: God’s Blessing on This Wonderful World is an isekai anime that pokes fun at the fantasy genre while also embracing its most glaring features. The story of Konosuba which has recently announced a third season, follows Sato Kazuma, who dies a death so embarrassing he is given the chance to be reborn in a fantasy world. The administrative goddess of Kazuma’s rebirth, Aqua, ridicules him on the way to offering him one wish he can take with him to his new life. Out of spite, instead of choosing a legendary sword or unmatched magic, Kazuma chooses the goddess herself, and she is forced to oblige by the restrictive rules of the deity system. As a result, the pair of a useless goddess and a reborn shut-in with no overpowered abilities embark on a hilariously magical journey that creates gags that coincide with hardship at every turn. Konosuba is an incredibly witty and fun isekai anime that any beginner would benefit from checking out. WATCH ON Crunchyroll

2 No Game No Life Can Capture Fans With Colors Alone Based on the light novel by Kamiya Yuu

No Game No Life is an ingenious isekai anime that single-handedly creates new dimensions of the genre through its unique interpretation of what a game is. Similar but much more accommodating than game isekai like Sword Art Online and Log Horizon, No Game No Life treats the game not as the world model, but as the world mode, meaning that the world in which the main characters, Sora and Shiro, are transported to is a fantasy world where games are the all-deciding law, whether it’s chess or a first-person shooter. Sora and Shiro began their legend as a player under the name “Blank,” as they played games of all types to their utmost extreme, all without ever losing. One day, the brother-sister duo receive an invitation from the God of Play himself, beckoning them to his world where they meet an endlessly entertaining cast of supporting characters and carve out a new path to gaming hegemony in a short 12 episodes. No Game No Life gives a brief but compact rendition of the modern isekai that exposes the true blinding colors of the genre to any budding fans. WATCH ON Hulu

1 That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime Aggregates All Isekai Strong Points Based on the manga by Kawakami Taiki and Fuse

That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime is a revolutionary anime for the isekai genre that any beginning fan must watch to understand the history and rise of the genre. The story is a unique repackaging of the reincarnation trope, as it flips the notion of the perfect rebirth. Although the main character, Rimuru, is given various advantages and eventually rises to power, he started with the most humble beginnings, that of a slime, a creator often associated with the utmost weakness in fantasy world construction. Despite this, Rimuru uses his ability to devour to become a singularity, and goes as far as to build an isekai-revolutionizing community around him that includes monsters and entities of all types, from orcs, to ogres, to demons, and more. The series creates a tangible sense of unity among the complications of an isekai fantasy, stemming from the creators’ uncanny ability to world-build and generate immersive premises. As a result, That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime is an the best introduction to the isekai genre for any fans who wish to dip their toes into otherworldly adventures. WATCH ON Crunchyroll
