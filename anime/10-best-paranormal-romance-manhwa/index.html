10 Best Paranormal Romance Manhwa

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Paranormal romance manhwa feature enchanting couples and explore the complexities of supernatural love. They blend fantasy and reality. Titles like Unholy Blood and A Good Day to Be a Dog showcase sweet romances that build naturally and incorporate paranormal elements creatively. Devil Number 4 and Swimming Lessons for a Mermaid offer unique love stories that feel grounded despite their supernatural elements. The characters’ development and relatability make them captivating.

Literature Review

These sweet and compelling paranormal romance manhwa explore the difficulties and wonders of falling in love with the unknown and the unfamiliar.

The best paranormal romance manhwa highlight some of the medium’s most enchanting and unique couples, which explore the difficulties and complications of falling in love with the supernatural. Staying relatively grounded while borrowing elements typically found in fantasy worlds, these romance stories find a way to feel relatable yet novel at the same time. Whether they involve vampires, ghosts, or even mermaids, paranormal romance manhwa boast the unique strength of having the creative freedom to leverage all the attributes of their starring imaginary or mythological beings. True love is difficult to find even among fellow humans, let alone unfamiliar beings with distinct circumstances, but these titles prove that love always prevails.

10 Unholy Blood (2020) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Lina Im

The world has become a dangerous place, threatened by converted vampires who greedily abuse their newfound powers to terrify and feast on the weak. As a pureblood vampire, Hayan Park has the strength and skill to overpower even dozens of half-blood vampires easily, but she wishes for nothing but to live a normal life. Yet as she becomes the target of the “Angels of Death”, Hayan finds herself with no choice but to fight back, driven by the desire to protect her loved ones. To this end, she partners up with Euntae Hwang, a dashing local police captain. Although it may not be the focus of this title, Unholy Blood prominently features a sweet romance that naturally builds up over time. As a vampire focused on her mission of protecting everyone, Hayan might not seem to have the time for romance, but she ends up developing feelings without even noticing it. Effortlessly blending different genres, Unholy Blood proves to be not only an exceptional action manhwa, but also a captivating romance title that should particularly appeal to fans of vampire stories. 9 A Good Day to Be a Dog (2017) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Hye Lee

Hana Han is an ordinary teacher, afflicted by a strange curse that has prevented her from finding true love. After first kissing someone, she is turned into a dog every midnight, with the only way to escape this situation being to receive a second kiss from the same person, but only in her dog form. After drunkenly kissing her coworker Seon Jin, who harbors a deep fear of dogs, Hana is faced with the seemingly impossible task of breaking her curse. As unserious as its premise might seem, A Good Day to Be a Dog provides an endearing romance story that is easy to fall in love with. Incorporating classic tropes while adding an unusual twist to the situation, this title ironically uses its paranormal setting to explore a surprisingly realistic relationship, that develops at a natural pace. Hye Lee, the author of A Good Day to Be a Dog, also created See You in My 19th Life, another fantastic title for fans of romance manhwa with bizarre twists.

8 Oh! Holy (2015) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Ah Hyun

Although Jamie Oh is not particularly popular or well-liked by his peers, he has no problem with his situation, as he much prefers the company of ghosts, whom he has always been friends with. However, his unusual yet comfortable life takes a sudden turn when the school’s most popular girl - and his old childhood friend - Holy Joo, begins to show her interest in him, thus drawing attention to a boy who was previously invisible. Oh! Holy tells a creative and original story revolving around the romance of two unusual high school students, one of whom has always lived with the paranormal. Some fans have expressed their disappointment with how the story progresses in the latter half, but the overall experience, which blurs the lines between romance and comedy, remains intriguing and enjoyable, particularly for fans of stories with ghosts and spirits. Ah Hyun, the author of Oh! Holy, also created My Life as an Internet Novel, an extremely entertaining isekai rom-com title.

7 Devil Number 4 (2018) Original Series Written by jangjin & Illustrated by woombeee

Meant to deceive humans into giving away their souls, Number 4 has had a difficult time closing deals lately, which might affect his reputation as one of Satan’s hardworking subordinates. He decides to go after Hanna Yoon, a student who works at a coffee shop to support herself, but she boldly states that she has no wish which could be granted in exchange for her soul. Determined to meet his goal no matter what, Number 4 decides to seduce her instead, without realizing how easily such a plan could backfire. Devil Number 4 thrives on its unique setting and compelling characters, whose love story proves simultaneously novel and familiar. Despite featuring a strange romance between a devil and a human, the realistic characters make the experience feel grounded, and their development throughout the series adds to the experience, making it remarkably entertaining and enjoyable. Some fans have expressed their dissatisfaction with the ending, but everything that leads up to it is simply outstanding. Read on Webtoon

6 Operation: True Love (2022) Original Series Written by Kkokkalee & Illustrated by Deuldeom

Having struggled with an unfulfilling relationship for years, Su-ae Shim is deeply familiar with the difficulties of high school romance. What’s more, she can’t help but compare herself to her popular stepsister, Ra-im. But when Su-ae discovers a sentient flip phone named Jellypop, who reveals to her that the amount of love everyone receives is predetermined, she makes up her mind to change her fate. Gamifying the concept of love by requiring the protagonist to earn hearts, Operation: True Love tells a truly unique story that both new and old fans of romance titles are certain to enjoy. The well-developed characters are easy to relate to, and their realistic personalities only serve to bring this complex story to life. Moreover, the remarkable artwork further elevates the experience, adding to the allure of this already captivating title.

5 Super Secret (2015) Original Series Written by & Illustrated by eon

Emma Ji and Ryan Nam have been friends forever, and their friendship has always been defined by an interesting dynamic. As Emma is virtually helpless by herself, Ryan has to be the responsible one, taking care of her much as a parental figure would. Unbeknownst to Emma, however, Ryan is keeping a huge secret that is becoming more difficult to hide from her dear childhood friend - he is a werewolf. Super Secret revolves around the adorable romance between a human and a werewolf, providing a criminally underrated and incredibly heartwarming experience. The cute and colorful artwork perfectly matches the atmosphere as well, elevating the experience beyond expectations. It should also be noted that, although the art style may deceive readers at first, the characters are actually of college age, making the story more mature and potentially more relatable to fans. The author of Super Secret, eon, is currently publishing How to Become a Dragon, an original and surprisingly underrated story that should particularly appeal to fans of comedy manhwa.

4 My Boo (2016) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Jeongseo

Despite having the ability to see and communicate with ghosts, Yeori So sees it more as a burden than a gift, thanks to certain unpleasant experiences. As such, when she discovers that the new apartment she moves into is haunted by a ghost, she decides to ignore his existence completely. Yet Joon Go, a lonely ghost who can’t accept his untimely death, realizes that Yeori can see him, and the two begin to accept the idea of living together. My Boo tells the story of a romance that cannot be, providing a bittersweet yet enchanting experience. Even though Yeori may want nothing to do with any ghost at first, Joon manages to change her perspective over time, as both of them share their stories with each other. Moreover, as this beautiful story is wrapped up within less than 50 chapters, this title also stands out as one of the best short manhwa, particularly for romance fans. Jeongseo, the author of My Boo, also created Happily Ever Afterwards, a refreshingly original and wholly enjoyable otome isekai title.

3 Orange Marmalade (2011) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Seok Woo Kang

Despite having become accustomed to modern society, and not needing human blood to survive, vampires continue to be discriminated against and forced to hide their identities. As a victim of this meaningless hatred, Ma Ri Baek, a young vampire girl who has never caused any harm, has grown disillusioned concerning humans, friendships, and relationships. But when she meets Jae Min Jung, whose sweet scent instinctively beckons her to bite his neck, she accidentally ends up turning him into her admirer. Orange Marmalade explores the complicated romance story of a vampire who struggles with discrimination and self-acceptance. Although the romance plot plays an undoubtedly important role, the title also emphasizes the difficult situation of vampires, making it one of the most interesting vampire stories out there. Moreover, the development of the characters feels organic and enjoyable, and the ending of the story beautifully wraps up the experience, making it that much more satisfying. Seok Woo Kang, the author of Orange Marmalade, also created Days of Hana, a similar supernatural romance title with an unexpected emotional impact.

2 Swimming Lessons for a Mermaid (2019) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Yongchan

Despite being a mermaid born in the ocean, Choa Shin has never figured out how to swim. To get away from mean mermaids and avoid unfortunate accidents, she receives human legs and begins living on land, together with her father. Still longing for the ocean, she watches her high school’s swim meets, admiring humans who can swim freely. When Soo Lee, a loud yet warm-hearted boy, accidentally discovers her secret, he offers to teach her how to swim, hoping to help her achieve her dream. Swimming Lessons for a Mermaid tells a simple yet charming romance story between an easy-going human boy and a reserved mermaid girl. Avoiding the unnecessary complications or pretentious drama scenes that sometimes serve to make such stories more complex, this title provides a sweet yet memorable experience. Naturally, the vibrant artwork enhances the experience as well, allowing the characters to be that much more expressive and recognizable. Read on Webtoon

1 Mystical (2020) Original Series Written & Illustrated by VAN.J

Garam has always been passionate about art, a passion that was further fueled by his mother’s kind encouragement. But after she tragically passed away and Garam started living with a new family, his relationship with art changed as well. All grown up, Garam moves into his grandmother’s empty house, in a rural town by the ocean, where he seeks to focus on drawing again. This is also where he has an unexpected encounter with Myst, a mystical water creature who is about to become his greatest muse. Mystical explores the idea of a human becoming familiar with the unknown, emphasizing the fact that mysterious beings can be beautiful rather than scary. The compelling story is sure to keep readers emotionally invested from the first chapter to the final one, and the whimsical and ethereal artwork turns this experience into a truly unforgettable one, making this one of the best paranormal romance manhwa out there. VAN.J, who created Mystical, is also credited for the art of Winter Woods, a title known for its vibrant and fluid artwork.
