10 Best Episodic Anime

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2600 words 13 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

There are many anime that are great for people looking for episodic stories. Some anime work as episodic stories for how each new installment reinvents the story by putting the characters in new situations. Other anime work as episodic stories for prioritizing character work over overarching plots, which can create deeo and interesting interpersonal relationships between characters.

Literature Review

Anime is hardly bereft of episodic stories, and plenty of series stand out for their incredible casts and isolated stories.

While most anime are known for their linear storytelling, it’s hardly devoid of episodic stories. Like Western cartoons, many anime are structured in a way that makes it easy to jump in at any point without ever being lost in what’s happening in the story; this is very common with anime geared towards children, but even more mature stories are sometimes structured like that, as well. Linear storytelling might be the most recognized form of writing in anime, especially with anime that serve as adaptations, but there are plenty of great episodic anime to watch, as well. Naturally, the ease with which a person can jump into the story at any point is of major importance in episodic anime.

But, just like any anime, the quality of said story, combined with its visuals and animation, is also an important factor. There are a lot of great episodic anime that keep to that no matter the genre, and a few, in particular, are especially worth highlighting.

10 Lupin the 3rd Never Fails To Reinvent Its Basic Formula Anime series by TMS Entertainment; based on the manga by Monkey Punch

The first great episodic anime for people to watch is TMS Entertainment’s Lupin the 3rd. Lupin the 3rd is a massive multimedia franchise centered around Lupin III, the grandson of the famous phantom thief Arsene Lupin who’s become a master thief in his own right. Every story sees Lupin and his friends, many of whom may or not betray him, working to pull off a major heist of some kind, all while avoiding being arrested by Inspector Zenigata of Interpol and occasionally saving the world. The reason why Lupin the 3rd is such a great episodic series stems largely from the way it’s structured. While there was a manga the original anime was adapted from, every season and movie largely follows its own continuity, and as such, viewers only ever need to know the basic story of Lupin the 3rd before jumping into a new story, no matter the age or genre. Add in the lovable cast that is always perfectly adapted to whatever story is being told, and Lupin the 3rd is one of the most easily accessible anime a person can watch. Watch On Crunchyroll

9 Gintama’s Parody Writing Make It Easy For Anyone To Jump Into It Anime series by Sunrise, based on the manga by Hideaki Sorachi

Another episodic anime that works because of the specific way it’s structured is Sunrise’s Gintama. In an alternate history to our own, aliens invaded Japan during the late Edo period and transformed it into a land where samurai are outlawed and aliens reign supreme. Gintoki, a former samurai who fought in the war with the aliens, is now living as an eccentric freelancer, and after unwittingly taking on an apprentice in Shinpachi, his freelancing becomes a full-fledged business known as the Yorozuya. While there are a lot of ongoing and plot-driven storylines in Gintama, the series is first and foremost an absurdist comedy. The majority of episodes and story arcs in Gintama are focused on rapid-fire gags and making fun of various aspects of life and pop culture, so except for a few instances, the show rarely expects anything out of its audience beyond laughing at whatever crazy joke is being told. There are few anime that are better for comedy lovers, and that’s a big part of what makes Gintama so great for episodic story lovers. Watch On Crunchyroll

8 Azumanga Daioh Is Still One Of The Best Slice Of Life Anime Anime series by J.C.Staff, based on the manga by Kiyohiko Azuma

On the opposite end of the spectrum, a more realistic anime that serves as a great episodic series is J.C.Staff’s Azumanga Daioh. Azumanga Daioh is a slice-of-life story focused on the daily lives of eight individuals: child prodigy Chiyo, the stoic Sakaki, the spacey Osaka, the goofy Tomo, Tomo’s straight man of a best friend Yomi, the energetic tomboy Kagura, and their teachers, Yukari and Nyamo. The series is largely grounded outside a few deliberately surrealist moments, and the comedy comes largely from the characters’ various quirks and how they play off each other.

10 Best Anime to Introduce Kids to the Medium

As with many slice-of-life anime, Azumanga Daioh focuses more on character-driven humor and stories than an overarching plot, but it ends up working here far better than in some of its contemporaries. While many slice-of-life anime have some sort of gimmick that’s used to justify their story, Azumanga Daioh is strictly about the regular lives of largely regular people, and that ends up helping the comedy hit even harder than normal. Even after over 20 years, there are very few anime with comedy like Azumanga Daioh, and that’s what makes it such a great episodic anime. Watch On HIDIVE

7 K-On!’s Anime Elevated The Original Manga To A Whole New Level Anime series by Kyoto Animation, based on the manga by Kakifly

Another slice-of-life anime that makes for a great episodic series is Kyoto Animation’s K-On! K-On! follows the daily lives of Houkago Tea Time, a band formed from the four, later five, members of a high school’s light music club. Naturally, many episodes of the anime focus on the girls’‘growth as musicians across the anime’s two seasons, but the primary focus of the series often ends up being the various antics the core cast ends up involved in that tend to be completely divorced from music.

K-On! is another slice-of-life anime where character-driven humor takes priority over any sort of plot, and it’s especially impressive to see it in K-On! While K-On! does get its material from a manga of the same name, the manga is mostly a framework for the anime to expand on plots and characterizations, and it’s to the point that K-On! season two is almost entirely original content. It’s rare for an anime to heavily diverge from the manga and end up better for it, and it works to make K-On! an anime that’s perfect for fans of episodic stories. Watch On HIDIVE

6 The Melancholy Of Haruhi Suzumiya Embodies The Best Of Kyoto Animation Anime series by Kyoto Animation, based on the light novel by Nagaru Tanigawa

Regarding episodic Kyoto Animation anime, one of the best for people to watch is easily The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya. Series protagonist Kyon finds himself mysteriously drawn to the eccentric Haruhi Suzumiya, and their odd friendship leads to him being dragged into the SOS Brigade, a club Haruhi formed for the sake of finding supernatural beings in the world. Ironically, all the other members of the SOS Brigade are the exact supernatural beings Haruhi is looking for, and they’ve all gathered together to have Kyon help them examine Haruhi’s godlike powers she’s blissfully unaware of. Like many Kyoto Animation shows, The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya is largely an episodic slice-of-life anime that’s focused more on character work than an overarching story. Every episode and story arc is largely self-contained and makes nearly any place a good starting point, and that aspect of the anime was sold even further by how The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya season 1 was originally broadcast in anachronic order. Add in some great writing and animation that still ranks among Kyoto Animation’s best, and The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya is easily one of the best episodic anime to watch. Watch On Crunchyroll

5 Pokémon Always Does A Great Job Of Making Things Easy On Its Audience Anime series by OLM, Inc, based on the video game franchise by Satoshi Tajiri & Game Freak

For long-running anime that serve as great episodic stories, one of the best to watch is undoubtedly OLM, Inc.’s Pokémon. Pokémon adapts the juggernaut of a video game franchise of the same name, with the focus being on Ash Ketchum’s journey to become a Pokémon Master with his trusty Pikachu by his side. Naturally, Ash faces many challenges across his journey; some are the various rivals he makes in every region, but more often than not, it’s the constant — albeit largely ineffectual — threat of Team Rocket trying to steal Pikachu and other Pokémon. Pokémon is one of the most notable long-running anime, having now been around for 27 years, and it’s easily one of the best episodic anime around. Every season of Pokémon sees Ash and Pikachu in a new region and meeting a new cast of characters, so just like other episodic shows, all a person needs to know is the basic premise before they’re good to jump in at any point they want. The anime even has a soft reboot in Pokémon Horizons, so whether it’s old stories or new ones, Pokémon is always easy to jump into at any point. Watch On Netflix

4 Detective Conan Is the Mystery Series That Will Never End Anime series by TMS Entertainment, based on the manga by Gosho Aoyama

Another long-running anime that serves as a great episodic series is TMS Entertainment’s Detective Conan. Also known as Case d in America, the series follows teen detective Shinichi Kudo who, after walking in on a crime being committed by two men in black, is turned into a child by a mysterious drug of theirs. Now, under the guise of Conan Edogawa, Shinichi works with what few people know the truth about him to take down the Black Organization and find a cure for his condition, all the while solving any case he happens to come across. Both the Detective Conan anime and manga have been going on for about 30 years, and a lot of that is owed to how the series is written. Every story arc in Detective Conan is a new mystery story largely disconnected from the one before it, so while there are ongoing story lines and character arcs, it’s easy for a person to jump into any mystery and get the full picture of what the series is about. That sort of writing is very common among classic detective stories, and that perfectly sells Detective Conan as an episodic series. Watch On Crunchyroll

3 Spy X Family Has A Perfect Handling Of Family & Action Comedy Anime series by Wit Studio & CloverWorks, based on the manga by Tatsuya Endo

For a more recent anime series that employs episodic storytelling, a great one to watch is Wit Studio and CloverWorks’ Spy x Family. Set in a fictional Cold War setting, Spy x Family stars Twilight, a secret agent whose latest assignment is to start a family to eventually get close to someone who could disrupt the delicate peace of the setting. The people he chooses as his wife and daughter, however, are an assassin and telepath, respectively, and the daughter, Anya, is the only one of the three ever aware of what everyone is truly up to. Spy x Family is one of the biggest anime to come out in recent years, and its episodic storytelling plays a big part in that. Similar to a Western cartoon, most episodes of Spy x Family are focused more on the comedic hijinks of the Forger family than advancing the main plot, and many episodes are even split into two or three vignettes as opposed to telling a single story. It’s a series that lives off of its great comedy and character work, and there are few shows in recent years that have as strong a handling on episodic content. Watch On Crunchyroll

Spy x Family
Created by Tatsuya Endo, Spy X Family is an action-adventure comedy anime that follows the misadventures of a found family with unique quirks. The story follows spy Twilight (known as Loid Forger), who, while on assignment, builds a fake family to get closer to his target. Unbeknownst to him, his new family carries their quirks. His wife, Yor, is a kind but deadly assassin, while his daughter, Anya, has telepathic powers - and is the only one who knows their secrets.

2 Space Dandy Is an Absurdist Masterpiece That Doesn’t Care About Continuity Anime series by Studio Bones; directed by Shinichiro Watanabe

Another relatively recent anime that’s great at episodic storytelling is Studio Bones’ Space Dandy. Space Dandy stars Dandy, a dandy guy in space who works as a largely unsuccessful alien bounty hunter alongside the robot QT and the cat-like alien Meow. The three of them travel across the galaxy in search of bounties and women, the latter being mostly Dandy’s priority, and their travels often get them involved in some sort of bizarre and abstract adventure. Shinichiro Watanabe’s works often use some form of episodic storytelling, and Space Dandy is one of the best examples of that. Every episode of Space Dandy is more or less completely disconnected from what came before it, and not only is there no real semblance of plot in Space Dandy until the final episode, but the finale is also largely standalone and ends on an incredibly bizarre note. Every episode of Space Dandy prioritizes telling an absurdist sci-fi story above all else, and as such, every episode serves as a perfect starting point for viewers.

1 Cowboy Bebop’s Episodic Writing Made It One Of The Most Iconic Anime Of All Time Anime series by Sunrise; directed by Shinichiro Watanabe

The last great episodic anime to watch, and another work from Shinichiro Watanabe, is the classic Sunrise series Cowboy Bebop. Similar to Space Dandy, Cowboy Bebop is a sci-fi story about bounty hunters, primarily Spike Spiegel and the crew of the Bebop. Unlike Space Dandy, however, Cowboy Bebop, while not devoid of humor, has a more serious writing style and tends to take itself very seriously, especially when delving into the backstories of the main characters. Cowboy Bebop is often heralded as one of the best anime of all time, and its writing style plays a big part in that. While there is something of an overarching story, the anime doesn’t really focus on it, and instead, Cowboy Bebop spends its time on episodic adventures that are always sold on thematic writing and the great chemistry of the cast. Every episode of Cowboy Bebop does a great job of selling the idea of people just trying to live their lives, and that helps it stand as one of the best episodic anime a person can watch.
