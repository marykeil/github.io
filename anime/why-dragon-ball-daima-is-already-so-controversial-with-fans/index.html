Why Dragon Ball Daima is Already so Controversial With Fans

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Dragon Ball Daima’s announcement has received backlash from fans who aren’t happy with the direction the series takes the franchise. Some fans were hoping for an adaptation of the Dragon Ball Super manga’s remaining story arcs. The decision to turn the entire cast into Chibi-style child versions has also received criticism from fans.

Literature Review

Dragon Ball Daima has only just been announced, but many fans are already upset at what this new series could represent for the franchise.

Dragon Ball Daima may have just been announced in October, but for many fans, the series is already a major disappointment. Those who aren’t upset by it can be left to wonder, though, just what exactly about this new Dragon Ball series has these fans so worked up?

Dragon Ball Daima was revealed at New York Comic-Con on October 13, 2023, to a surprising amount of backlash. The new series is supposedly set between Dragon Ball Z and Super, but features kid versions of the entire cast, not just Goku, which did lead to some small amount of confusion at first.

The description of the show at the panel accompanied with subsequent statements made by Dragon Ball producer Akio Iyoku show that series creator Akira Toriyama has had a large involvement in the production of the series, which would normally be enough to set fans’ minds at ease. However, it’s the similarities to past Dragon Ball endeavors that actually has some fans upset the most.

Fans Worry Dragon Ball Daima Will Be GT All Over Again

Among fans who have posted about their discontent online, there are three main arguments that seem to be offered as to why they already dislike Daima. By far the most widespread is that the series’ premise of having Goku return to being a kid again is far too similar to the series Dragon Ball GT, which also opened with Goku being returned to a young age.

    Dragon Ball GT's Best Upgrade Was Given to a Classic Series Artifact, Not Goku  The Dragon Radar has always been important, since the Dragon Balls would have never been found without it - & it got the perfect GT upgrade!

Dragon Ball GT was created with minimal input from Toriyama, and was highly controversial among fans, to the point where its status as canon has become dubious with the advent of Dragon Ball Super. This similarity only seems to be surface level, however, and Toriyama’s confirmed role in its production should ease fears of a GT repeat.

Many Dragon Ball Fans Want an Adaptation of Dragon Ball Super’s Remaining Manga

Another major concern among some fans is a desire to see the remaining story arcs of the Dragon Ball Super manga animated, which include the Galactic Patrol Prisoner Saga and the Granolah the Survivor Saga. These arcs are set in space and expand the lore and world of Dragon Ball Super to a broad degree, featuring amazing fights that fans can’t wait to see animated. Learning that a new, different anime project is under works means that it’s that much longer until Super’s remaining arcs are animated, which had some fans already grumbling from the moment the new trailer debuted.

Lastly, it seems that some fans simply don’t like the idea of turning the entire cast into Chibi-style child versions of themselves. This is a fair enough criticism on its own, and if one doesn’t like the premise or style, there’s not much that can ultimately be done to win them over. Some fans have behaved in a rational manner and realized that the series might not be for them, while others have been much more rude about their discontent.

Why Dragon Ball Daima Could Be a New Classic

Despite fan outcry though, there is a legitimate chance that Dragon Ball Daima could far exceed expectations. That animation in both trailers is far beyond even the best moments of Super and GT, the result looking far closer to the quality seen in Dragon Ball Super: Broly and Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero. Additionally, as previously mentioned, Toriyama’s direct involvement and the series’ focus on adventure are both positive signs. None of this is a guarantee of quality of course, but all of it suggests fans should give Daima a chance.

While Dragon Ball Daima’s debut is still a ways off, hopefully, its quality will be able to win back some of these displeased fans and have them regretting their early reactions.

Look for Dragon Ball Daima when it premiers in Fall 2024!

  Dragon Ball DAIMA  Dragon Ball DAIMA is the fifth overall series in the action-adventure anime franchise. It features most of the classic cast members as de-aged versions of themselves, including Goku, Vegeta, and Bulma. The series was announced at NYCC 2023, with creator Akira Toriyama returning to handle DAIMA's run.
