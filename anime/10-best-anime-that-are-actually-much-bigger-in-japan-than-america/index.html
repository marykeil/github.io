10 Best Anime That Are Actually Much Bigger in Japan Than America

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1700 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Despite anime’s global success, many anime are still far more popular in Japan than they are in the West, inlcuding America. Some anime are unpopular in America because of their age and how long it took for them to be licensed, such as classics like Doraemon . Other anime are unpopular in America because of bad timeslots, or for not being as appealing to what’s popular in America.

Literature Review

Anime has become a major phenomenon in America in recent years, but even with that, there are many anime that are still far bigger deals in Japan.

It’s impossible to deny how big anime has gotten in America over the years. The increasing ease and availability of streaming has made it easier than ever to watch anime both old and new. As such, when an anime starts to take off in Japan, chances are it’ll become just as big in America and other parts of the world, if not bigger. That being said, not every anime that’s successful in Japan will be successful in America. Whether it’s because of a lack of an official translation or something about the story not being appealing to foreign audiences, there are many cases in which an anime will fail at replicating the success it had in Japan, and will only be regarded as a cult hit, at best, in America.

While this is sometimes an understandable fate for many anime, it’s still a shame, especially for a few anime series whose overall quality is too high for the lack of attention they receive from mainstream audiences in America.

10 Most Underrated Anime Series Streaming on Crunchyroll Even with how easy it’s become to watch anime, there are still too many that go unnoticed, and here are 10 of the most underrated ones on Crunchyroll.

10 Dragon Quest: The Adventure Of Dai’s Bad Timeslot Killed Its Popularity Based on the manga by Riku Sanjo & Koji Inada

Release Year 2020 Number Of Episodes 100 Where To Watch It Crunchyroll, Hulu
The first notable anime to be more popular in Japan than in America is 2020’s Dragon Quest: The Adventure of Dai. The anime adapted the classic manga of the same name and had some of the best animation of any Toei production. However, The Adventure of Dai never managed to become a big hit outside of Japan. It’s possible that Dragon Quest: The Adventure of Dai’s late airtime meant that too few people would have the time to watch it. Even so, it’s a shame that the best version of such a classic series was so thoroughly ignored. Watch On Crunchyroll

9 JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure: Golden Wind Is The Most Divisive JoJo Part Based on the manga by Hirohiko Araki

Release Year 2018 Number Of Episodes 39 Where To Watch It Crunchyroll, Hulu, Netflix
Another adaptation of a classic manga that’s far more popular in Japan is 2018’s JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure: Golden Wind. JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure is one of the most popular anime to come out in the past decade, but while its fifth installment, Golden Wind, is often viewed as one of the best parts worldwide, it’s far more divisive among American fans. Golden Wind is often criticized for its monster-of-the-week format and for how little development Giorno and Diavolo get. Despite this criticism, it hasn’t affected its popularity in Japan in the slightest. Watch On Crunchyroll

8 Yu-Gi-Oh! Zexal Was Far Too Childish For American Viewers To Enjoy Based on the manga by Kazuki Takahashi

Release Year 2011 Number Of Episodes 146 Where To Watch It Crunchyroll, Hulu
Every Yu-Gi-Oh! anime is divisive for one reason or another, but no series has ever been rejected by American audiences as much as the underratedYu-Gi-Oh! Zexal. Zexal tried to capture a younger audience with both a younger cast and more simplistic writing, and while that worked in Japan, it was a major turnoff for American fans, especially since it came off the heels of the largely serious Yu-Gi-Oh! 5D’s. The second season of Yu-Gi-Oh! Zexal did showcase far more serious writing, but that came far too late, and Zexal has never been able to completely shake its poor reputation in the West. Watch On Crunchyroll

7 Sword Art Online Was Too Simplistic For Its Own Good Based on the light novels by Reki Kawahara

Release Year 2012 Number Of Episodes 96 Where To Watch It Crunchyroll, Hulu
Sword Art Online was quick to become one of the biggest anime franchises in Japan and the world, but in America, Sword Art Online is very divisive for how overpowered Kirito often is, and its overreliance on controversial themes like harem tropes and sexual assault. That isn’t to say that Sword Art Online isn’t a major hit in America, but because of some of the more clichéd elements, its popularity in America is nothing compared to Japan and other parts of the world. Watch On Crunchyroll

6 Gintama Has Never Been More Than a Cult Hit Outside Of Japan Based on the manga by Hideaki Sorachi

Release Year 2006 Number Of Episodes 367 Where To Watch It Crunchyroll, Hulu
Not every Shonen Jump adaptation ends up becoming a hit worldwide, and a perfect example of that is 2006’s Gintama. Both the manga and anime ran for over 10 years on and off, and while it was a big hit in Japan, it’s only ever been a cult hit in America, with Viz Media even dropping it after 23 volumes. Gintama’s lack of success overseas is most likely due to the absurdist and reference-based nature of its comedy not translating well into English, but it’s still one of the most noteworthy Shonen Jump stories of the past 20 years, regardless. Watch On Crunchyroll

5 Tokyo Revengers Completely Fell Off In America, Despite the Manga’s Success Based on the manga by Ken Wakui

Release Year 2021 Number Of Episodes 50 Where To Watch It Hulu
A successful manga doesn’t always translate to a successful anime in the West, and one noteworthy example of that is 2021’s Tokyo Revengers. Tokyo Revengers is one of the biggest anime franchises of the past few years, and the manga was number nine among the best-selling manga of 2023, but the anime has gotten progressively less popular in America. The franchise is outright despised in some circles. Some of the reasons for this include a lack of availability after Hulu obtained the streaming rights and some very controversial story decisions, but with any luck, a hypothetical fourth season will alleviate those issues. Watch On Hulu

4 Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha Premiered Too Early For Its Own Good Original anime series by Masaki Tsuzuki

Release Year 2004 Number Of Episodes 76 Where To Watch It Currently Unavailable
Sometimes, it’s very specific genres that hold an anime back in America, and one notable example is 2004’s Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha. Nanoha is a very successful franchise with a multitude of anime, manga, and movies, but Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha came out at a time when magical girl anime were seen as exclusively for girls, so it was a major flop in America, with only a few installments licensed. Nowadays, magical girl anime are more accepted by all audiences, so if Nanoha had come out a few years later, it might have had more success outside of Japan.

3 Kinnikuman Might Be Too Old To Take Off In the United States Based on the manga by Yudetamago

Release Year 1983 Number Of Episodes 183 Where To Watch It Currently Unavailable
Age is another big factor in why some anime never take off in America, and a prime example of that is 1983’s Kinnikuman. Kinnikuman is one of the biggest manga franchises in Japan and arguably started a lot of the common tropes of shonen manga, but due to being over 40 years old and never having an official English translation, the Kinnikuman fandom is virtually nonexistent in America and other places outside of Japan. 2024’s new Kinnikuman anime might help the series truly take off in the United States, but it’s unknown how successful it will be at doing so.

2 Saint Seiya Missed Out On The Anime Boom In The United States Based on the manga by Masami Kurumada

Release Year 1986 Number Of Episodes 113 Where To Watch It Crunchyroll
Another older anime that never became a big success in America is 1986’s Saint Seiya. Saint Seiya is one of the biggest shōnen anime of all time, and in Japan and other parts of the world, especially Europe, it played just as big a part as Pokémon, Dragon Ball, and Sailor Moon in helping anime take off in the 90s. In America, however, Saint Seiya wasn’t licensed until 2003, when it aired as the heavily edited Knights of the Zodiac, so it never managed to take off in America and can only be seen as a cult classic there, at best. Watch On Crunchyroll

1 Doraemon Is Virtually Unknown In America, Despite Being a Children’s Classic Based on the manga by Fujiko F. Fujio

Release Year 1973 Number Of Episodes 2973 Where To Watch It Currently Unavailable
Perhaps the most famous anime that is far more popular in Japan than in America is 1973’s Doraemon. Doraemon has been one of the biggest anime franchises in Japan for over 50 years, with a multitude of different shows and movies, and the manga is the fifth-best-selling manga of all time. However, nothing of the Doraemon franchise was ever officially licensed in America until the 2000s, so despite being a major franchise for decades, Doraemon has little to no presence in America and is one of the biggest examples of an anime that didn’t reach the same heights of popularity in America as it did in Japan.
