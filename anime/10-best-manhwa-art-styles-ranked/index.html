10 Best Manhwa Art Styles, Ranked

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The art styles manhwa enhance the storytelling, creating a deeper impact for the audience. Each manhwa has its own unique and captivating art style that leads to a greater connection with readers. The use of color, lighting, and texture in the artwork enhances the mood and atmosphere of the stories.

Literature Review

Visual design can be the main reason a story pulls in a reader, which is why the best manhwa art styles make their series so fascinating.

When choosing a new story to dive into, readers will undoubtedly be looking for the best manhwa art styles. How the panels are drawn and displayed are huge reasons why a reader may choose to continue or drop the story. Regardless of genre, drawing and layout are tools in themselves to drive the storyline forward, optically mirror its sentiment, and overall create a deeper impact for the audience. These Korean comics, also known as manhwa, all have differing and particular ways of capturing the story’s characters, movement, and mood. The authors and illustrators maximize themes and intentionality, knowing when to fragment, include, or remove certain artistic elements. These ten manhwas have fascinating narratives, dialogue, and twists, but their art styles alone are worth the read. Drawing you in with palpable texture, lively action, and emotive color and lighting, here are ten of the best manhwa art styles.

10 After School Lessons for Unripe Apples (2020) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Soonkki Hi

Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama Status: On Hiatus

Following Mi-ae, After School Lessons for Unripe Apples depicts her normal middle school life is quickly disrupted when an acquaintance from her past transfers back. Despite her efforts, they can not seem to bridge the gap between them, so the story follows this engaging slice-of-life and the youthful yet grating interactions these main characters share. The simple but dynamic style mirrors this vibe, standing out when compared to other more popular art styles manhwas use. Hi’s design adds to the story’s comedic genre and creates a sense of nostalgia inspired by cartoons and comics many grew up with. READ ON WEBTOON

9 The Horizon (2016) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Ji-Hun Jeong

Genre: Drama, Psychological, Adventure Status: Completed

The Horizon’s art style mirrors the gruesome and desolate energy of its story pointedly. This chronicle depicts a war-torn land, the lonely and anguish-filled path a boy takes following the death of his mother, and the hopeful friendship he finds along the way. It is not often viewers see a Korean webtoon in black and white, but this choice is thought-provoking when considering its content, and in turn, gives the rare use of color a great effect when seen. Although each panel does not include full details of the scene, the style is illustrative and simply gruesome. READ ON WEBTOON

8 A Stepmother’s Märchen (2019) Adapted From the Webnovel Written by Nyangiwa Hyangshinlo, Illustrated by ORKA

Genre: Drama, Fantasy, Romance Status: Ongoing

A Stepmother’s Märchen’s main character Shuri Von Neuschwanstein has gone through plenty of hardships throughout life, and when given the sudden chance to rewrite her history, a surprising and heartfelt story unfolds. Each panel of A Stepmother’s Märchen’s is moody, detailed, and filled with rich colors, all to add to the worldbuilding of this fantastical German setting. Each character has a distinct design which adds to their personality and makes it simple to follow. A major point of beauty in this manhwa is the lighting, as it seems to sparkle and give focus to the people and movement happening in each frame. READ ON TAPAS

10 Most Underrated Manhwa You Should Be Reading
A few lucky titles were popular enough to receive anime adaptations, but many more interesting manhwa are still waiting to be discovered.

7 Unholy Blood (2020) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Li-Na Lim

Genre: Action, Drama, Romance, Supernatural Status: Completed

This series ranks among the best paranormal manhwa, as Unholy Blood excels in capturing the graphic vampiric world of pure blood Ha-Yan Park and police captain Eun-Tae Hwang. Following their fight against half-bloods to restore their home in Korea to safety, the illustrator makes use of various digital art techniques, giving focus to panel details comparatively to a movie storyboard. Readers easily sense movement and emotion through shading and blur of clothes, hair, and the destruction of background sets during high-speed battles. The hot-blooded revenge and romance manhwa is full of beautiful character design, saturated blacks, and striking colors. READ ON WEBTOON

6 The Remarried Empress (2019) Adapted From the Webnovel Written by Alphatart, Illustrated by Chireon & Sumpul

Genre: Drama, Fantasy, Romance Status: Ongoing

The Remarried Empress’s art style is a wonderful compliment to its story of social obligation, betrayal, satisfying revenge, and the fantastical love that blossoms through it all. The main character, Navier Ellie Trovi, is the center of both narrative and design, as she embodies the opulence and rich feel of the webtoon. The texture of clothes and jewels is pulled straight from reality, filled with saturated color and sparkle. The background is not neglected, as the graphic artists include highly ornamental and ostentatious architecture in the Western Empire’s castles and manors. All of these details immerse readers into the manhwa’s fairy tale world-building and the calculated political play of high monarchy. READ ON WEBTOON

5 Doom Breaker (2021) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Cheong-Dam

Genre: Action, Fantasy Status: On Hiatus

Fighting against evil as the lone human in a world of gods, Zephyr of Doom Breaker is given a second chance at life and love as he is sent ten years back into his past. This manhwa’s style is fantastical and detailed, despite the complexities of the protagonist’s epic battles, mythical foes, and changing locations. With glowing hues and stark outlining, readers escape into the otherworldy and moody air of this story. Doombreaker’s comic style enhances the sense of magic, action, and heroism in this tale of swords, dragons, and redemption READ ON WEBTOON

4 Under The Oak Tree (2020) Adapted From the Webnovel Written by Suji Kim Under the Oak tree -  Cover art in center & panels from manhwa on either side 

Genre: Drama, Fantasy, Romance Status: Ongoing

Lady Maximilian and Sir Riftan may have wed under less-than-ideal conditions, but Under the Oak Tree’s story details their story of overcoming past trauma, gaining strength in their abilities, and ultimately learning how to be healthy loving partners and co-protectors of their land. This highly respected romance manhwa has a beautiful understanding of lighting, softening scenes with intentional sentiment and glow, as it ultimately introduces a delicate air to Anatol with the coming of the female protagonist. Under the Oak Tree’s detailed fabrics, foliage, and surroundings are on par with this era of fantasy, monsters, and the legendary Remdragon Knights. READ ON MANTA

3 When a Thousand Moons Rise (2021) Original Series Written & Illustrated by Hui-Jeong Park

Genre: Drama, Psychological, Romance, Supernatural Status: Ongoing

When perusing When a Thousand Moons Rise, the audience is consumed by its harrowing yet dark-romantic feel. Onlookers see the main character No-Gyung Lee’s doomed fate to be murdered every time she is reborn. This star-crossed supernatural romance is twisted and full of gore, but simultaneously makes way for beauty through it all. When a Thousand Moons Rise has rich and emotive coloring, even in primarily dark panels. As readers bounce back and forth between different times, the familiar future style is contrasted with the detailed and opulent past scenes, making it easy to appreciate Hui-Jeong Park’s artistic effort in her environment construction. READ ON TAPAS

2 Omniscient Reader (2020) Adapted From the Webnovel Written by Sing Shong

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy Status: Ongoing

The meta story of Omniscient Reader focuses on Dok-Ja, as he becomes the protagonist of one of his favorite novels come to life. It is drawn dark and with heavy contrast as it sets the atmosphere of fantasy and adventure. As the genre suggests, Omniscient Reader’s panels are filled with dynamic movement and action as the main character must do all he can to save the world. Illustrators also create interest with peculiar monsters and character design, mixed with surprisingly cute creatures. This manhwa is celebrated enough that it is now being adapted into a live-action Korean drama. READ ON WEBTOON

1 Solo Leveling (2018) Adapted From the Webnovel Written by Chugong

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy Status: Completed

Solo Leveling has caught the eye of many and has now been adapted into an animated series. It details a unique world of hunters protecting humanity, and weak Sung Jinwoo has luckily been revived from death to uncover the secrets behind his powers, the dungeon that created them, and defeat foes, man and beast alike. Panels use lighting that seems to be glowing off the page or screen, surrounded by moody cool tones and murky shadow. From its origin as a webnovel and manhwa, it makes use of eye-catching colors, thick outlines, and a comic style to portray the riveting atmosphere during his battles for survival, power, and ultimately breaking through limits. All of this adds up to Solo Leveling having the best manhwa art style to date. Read On Tappy Toon
