The Best Anime Soundtracks To Listen To While Studying

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2300 words 11 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Some anime soundtracks are perfect to listen to while studying because their mellow instrumentals and soothing themes promote focus. Doraemon ’s extensive soundtrack and movies offer diverse songs perfect for studying. “Stand By Me 2” has standout tracks for study sessions. Chihayafuru ’s soundtrack, resembling a quiet autumn, features high-tension instrumentals and light opening and ending themes.

Literature Review

There is a plethora of amazing anime soundtracks that are perfect for studying, even when taking Joe Hisaishi’s Ghibli scores out of the equation.

The best anime soundtracks to listen to while studying will provide a variety of vibes, from creating zone-in rhythms to soothing instrumentals. Furthermore, the comprehensive soundtracks will provide songs that are fit for all types of study, as different students work best under different situations, such as one who can’t focus when listening to lyrics, or one who thrives with upbeat tempos. For the most part, slow, mellow, but focused music is what works best for students. When considering which series or studios fit this model best, one name comes to the forefront: Studio Ghibli. However, the internet is already saturated with Ghibli playlists from the walls of YouTube to Spotify, so Joe Hisaishi and other Ghibli soundtracks will not be considered for the sake of exploration. Furthermore, this idea also aligns with the fact that Miyazaki Hayao himself views his work more as manga-eiga (meaning manga in motion) rather than “anime.”

10 Best Anime Songs to Listen to While Working Out Anime songs are known to be energetic and inspiring, so they’re great to listen to while getting a good workout.

10 Doraemon Features Great Tracks Through the Decades Based on the manga by Fujiko Fujio

Release Year: 1979 Number of Episodes: 1787+ Notable Tracks: Doraemon no Uta (Shunsuke Kikuchi), Niji (Masaki Suda), Himawari no Yakusoku (Hata Motohiro)
As a franchise, Doraemon has cemented itself not only in anime history, but in Japanese culture, as the lovable blue robot cat named Doraemon has entertained the masses for over 26 years, from time traveling to straight-up wacky antics. Within the long run of the main Doraemon series, there is just as robust a soundtrack collection, with many of the songs reminiscent of 80s Jpop with springy upbeats and mellow instrumentals, making them perfect for studying. Furthermore, the hegemon of a franchise has led to multiple notable Doraemon movies, with Stand By Me 2 released in 2020 boasting two of the greatest songs in the Doraemon franchise that are also perfect to study alongside: “Niji” by Masaki Suda and “Himawari no Yakusoku” by Hata Motohiro. Although Stand By Me 2 is experimental in its full use of 3D animation, the movie’s soundtracks can undoubtedly still be classified as anime soundtracks.

WATCH ON Disney Plus

9 World Trigger Has Rhythmically Soothing Ending Themes Based on the manga by Ashihara Daisuke

Release Year: 2014 Number of Episodes: 99 Notable Tracks: Ashita no Hikari (AAA), Ungai Doukei (FantasticYouth), World Trigger Main Theme (Kawai Kenji)
World Trigger is a sci-fi action shonen series that takes an awkward but effective route in connecting to its audience. Produced by Toei Animation, the same studio that adapted Dragon Ball and One Piece, World Trigger takes similar tropes and elements from those series, but applies them to a world of aliens called neighbors and battle suits called triggers, all while following a protagonist named Mikumo Osamu, whose path to strength is arduously slow. World Trigger also adapts an interesting format for opening and ending themes halfway through the first season. At around episode 27, the series begins to utilize one song to serve both purposes, placing it in the middle of an episode. One such song that stands out is “Ashita no Hikari” by AAA, which serves as a season one theme. This song gives off a quiet touch of tension that is perfect to listen to while gearing up for an upcoming exam. “World Trigger Main Theme” and “Ungai Doukei” also give off similar vibes.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

8 Chihayafuru’s Tracks Are Like An Autumn Sprinkle Based on the manga by Suetsugu Yuki

Release Year: 2011 Number of Episodes: 74 Notable Tracks: Chihayafuru Main Theme (Yamashita Kosuke), Karuta Biyori (Yamashita Kosuke), Soshite Ima (Seto Asami)
Chihayafuru is a heartstopping sports drama anime produced by Studio Madhouse, one of the biggest studio names in anime, and the same studio that adapted Frieren, Hunter x Hunter, and Death Note. However, the story of Chihayafuru differs greatly from those of other Madhouse series, as it follows a high school girl and her journey through the world of karuta, a Japanese sport and card game that is based on poetry. Similar to the grace of karuta that is described and depicted through animation, the soundtrack is similarly touching. Chihayafuru features songs from high-tension orchestral instrumentals, to light and airy opening and ending themes. As a whole, Chihayafuru’s soundtrack resembles the colors of a quiet autumn, with rainy days sauntering by as they please. The Chihayafuru full soundtrack can be streamed on Apple Music.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

7 The Monogatari Series Has One Song With Infinite Repeat Potential Based on the light novel by NISIO ISIN and VOFAN

Release Year: 2009 Number of Episodes: 100 + 3 films Notable Tracks: Renai Circulation (Hanazawa Kana)
The Monogatari Series is a complex world of supernatural mysteries with a touch of romance, as its fifteen separate titles that are in no way disjointed amalgamate into three coherent sections that can be called seasons. Produced by Studio Shaft, the same studio that adapted March Comes in Like a Lion and Puella Magi Madoka Magica, the first series of the Monogatari Series, Bakemonogatari, introduces Araragi Koyomi as he is caught by a vampire ambush. Following this, the world of apparitions and supernatural phenomena opens up to Araragi’s eyes. Throughout the Monogatari Series’ fifteen titles, there have been many chill themes and instrumentals that help one focus. However, one song stands out from the rest: “Renai Circulation” by Hanazawa Kana. The track’s upbeat and light melody matches perfectly with Hanazawa’s soft and soothing voice, which eventually boosted it into TikTok stardom. Unbeknownst to many users of the app, the song is a Monogatari soundtrack, and also the perfect song to throw on repeat for a chill study session.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

6 The Ancient Magus’ Bride Features Wonderfully Soothing Insert Songs Based on the manga by Yamazaki Kore

Release Year: 2017 Number of Episodes: 24 Notable Tracks: Iruna Etelero: Lindel’s Song (Guitarrista de Atena), Chise’s Theme (Matsumoto Junichi)
Produced by Wit Studio, The Ancient Magus’ Bride is an anime with themes and animation almost on the level of a Ghibli film, as it spins the nation of a typical fantasy on its head with deep emotional development and underlying romance beneath the world of magic. The story follows Hatori Chise, a human girl who puts herself up for auction, and is bought by a magus by the name of Elias Ainsworth, who takes care of Chise with surprising care, guiding her through her own powers that seemed to hurt her. Within this touching story of romance, there is also heartbreak and meditation on the power of time, all of which manifests wonderfully in the soundtrack of The Ancient Magus’ Bride. In particular, the series does a wonderful job with insert songs like the character Lindel’s song, titled Iruna Etelero, which is not only integrated perfectly into the narrative as he sings, but is also a song that soothes any listeners into a mindset of focus, which evidently coincides with many study styles.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

5 Suzume Brings Out the Best in RADWIMPS An original film directed by Makoto Shinkai

Release Year: 2022 Runtime: 122 minutes Notable Tracks: Suzume (RADWIMPS feat. Toaka), Kanata Haluka (RADWIMPS)
Suzume is yet another Makoto Shinkai masterpiece filled with trademark sparkles and riveting colors. The story is a fantastical adventure of strange magic, creatures, and portals, as the main characters, Iwato Suzume and Munakata Souta, try to save the world from invading evil. However, misfortune strikes when Souta is cursed by a small white cat and turned into an anthropomorphic chair.

In the realm of soundtracks, RADWIMPS is the leading force in Suzume, as they are in many of Makoto Shinkai’s films, such as Weathering With You and Your Name. These films are also commonly compared to Ghibli films, and if an analogy needed to be made, RADWIMPS would be to Makoto Shinkai as Joe Hisaishi is to Miyazaki Hayao. In particular, the titular theme song, “Suzume,” which features Toaka, a singer who had previously been making TikTok covers, is a mysterious and relaxing piece that can get anyone into the mood to study.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

Suzume Review: Makoto Shinkai’s Latest Film Has Serious Heart Makoto Shinkai’s latest film, Suzume, doesn’t reach the heights of his previous works, but it’s still a great film with plenty of heart.

4 Samurai Champloo Brings Hip-Hop To Anime An original series directed by Watanabe Shinichirou

Release Year: 2004 Number of Episodes: 26 Notable Tracks: Battlecry (Nujabes and Shing02), Shiki no Uta (Nujabes and Minmi)
Directed by the same man who directed the popular Cowboy Bebop, Samurai Champloo takes its own swing at injecting western influences into anime. However, compared to Cowboy Bebop with its jazz beats and space adventures, Samurai Champloo takes the more lowkey route, infusing elements of Hip Hop throughout the Edo period landscape that the series takes place in. Notable tracks include the opening theme, “Battlecry,” which was co-written and performed by Nujabes and Shing02. The song starts with a smooth beat, and uses English lyrics that sharply contrast with the samurai-centric plot of Samurai Champloo, but seems to fit in perfectly with the characters’ vibes. Even though this song is the opening theme, its mellow tunes and Shing02 fiercely soft vocals make for a solid study song, along with much of the anime’s comprehensive soundtrack.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

3 Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood Doesn’t Miss On Any Fronts Based on the manga by Arakawa Hiromu

Release Year: 2009 Number of Episodes: 64 Notable Tracks: Crime and Punishment (Senju Akira), Sorrowful Stone (Senju Akira), Rain (SID), Let It Out (Fukuhara Miho)
Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood is an anime produced by Studio Bones that has stood as one of the highest-rated and well-taken anime of all time. This shonen phenomenon follows the adventures of two teenage brothers, Edward and Alphonse Elric, as they travel around the world as government-hired alchemists trying to find the truths of the world, and perhaps the legendary philosopher’s stone. Along the ups and downs of the two boys’ journey, there is constantly beautiful music accompanying the action. From low-toned and deep instrumentals to heart-pumping opening theme songs, Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood has it all. Of course, this also includes studying music, as there is a song for any type of student. In particular, the last ending theme of the series, “Rain” by SID, is chock-full of motivational emotion that will inspire even the laziest of workers.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

2 Yuri On Ice Curates Music Just For Its Characters An original series directed by Yamamoto Sayo

Release Year: 2016 Number of Episodes: 12 Notable Tracks: Yuri on ICE (Umebayashi Taro), Theme of King JJ (Umebayashi Taro), History Maker (Fujioka Dean)
As one of the best anime produced by Studio MAPPA, Yuri On Ice took the anime world by storm at the time of its release, as the series unearthed underrepresented audiences on all fronts, from the LGBTQ+ community to the figure skating community. The story follows a professional figure skater named Katsuki Yuuri, as he approaches the end of his career at the age of 23. However, due to a surprising turn of events, Yuuri’s idol and role model, Victor Nikiforov, is inspired enough by a video of Yuuri to fly from Russia to become his coach. In terms of soundtracks, Yuri On Ice does an incredible job going above and beyond the typical OST (original soundtrack) structures, as each character who takes the ice will bring along their own personal pieces of music to dance to. This provides an amazingly wide variety of songs from all origins, as many skaters choose music representative of their national origin. Furthermore, many of the songs are smooth, but upbeat songs that are meant to be danced to, making them perfect for a high-intensity study retreat.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll

1 Your Lie in April Embodies Soundtracks From Classical to Contemporary Based on the manga by Arakawa Naoshi

Release Year: 2014 Number of Episodes: 22 Notable Tracks: Liebeslied (Sergei Rachmaninoff), Orange (Seven Oops), Introduction and Rondo Capriccioso Op. 28 (Saint-Saëns), Again (Yokoyama Masaru)
Produced by A-1 Pictures, the studio behind the recent success Solo Leveling, Your Lie in April, otherwise known as Shigatsu wa Kimi no Uso, is an anime of heartbreaking beauty and tear-jerking serenity. The story follows Arima Kosei, a genius pianist who has lost his way following his mother’s death, and Miyazono Kaori, a talented violinist who forces Arima to break out of his shell. All the happiness, conflict, and surprise that occurs throughout Your Lie in April and Arima and Kaori’s adventure is further accentuated by the impeccable soundtrack that perfectly blends contemporary Japanese pop elements with Classical music. Although, for classical music appreciators, it may be difficult to study while listening to classical music due to its riveting contours, many students find it relaxing. For those who can’t stomach neglecting beauty, there is still the masterful collection of OSTs from Yokoyama Masaru, as well as the popular opening and ending themes of the anime. Aside from its classical music features, the Your Lie in April OST full soundtrack can be streamed on Apple Music.

WATCH ON Crunchyroll
