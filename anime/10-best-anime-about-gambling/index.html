10 Best Anime About Gambling

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2300 words 11 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Gambling anime offer suspenseful mind games and unexpected strategies, making each psychological duel compelling. The creativity inherent in the anime medium allows for diverse gambling settings, from grounded ones to those that feature fantastical twists. From recent iconic titles like Kakegurui to classics like One Outs , these stories approach the idea of gambling from different angles.

Literature Review

Thanks to their shockingly high stakes and intriguing mind games, gambling anime deliver some of the most thrilling and entertaining experiences.

The best anime about gambling are some of the medium’s most engaging and thrilling titles, featuring suspenseful atmospheres and captivating mind games full of surprising tricks and unexpected strategies. Whether the stakes are high or low, these titles make each psychological duel as compelling as some of the best and most memorable anime fights. As niche as the gambling theme may seem, this medium is creative and diverse enough for creators to figure out a variety of ways to incorporate gambling elements in their storytelling. From grounded stories featuring realistic games and palpable tension, to those distinguished through their fantastical or supernatural twists, every good gambling title leverages its dramatic setting to deliver entertaining twists and thrilling resolutions. Although it has yet to receive an anime adaptation, Toshio Sako’s Usogui manga is widely regarded as one of the best gambling titles that fans of the theme ought to check out.

10 Kakegurui (2017) Produced by MAPPA and Based on the Manga by Homura Kawamoto & Tooru Naomura

Hyakkaou Private Academy is a prestigious school attended only by the elite, preparing rich students for their likely predetermined careers in business and politics. However, unbeknownst to outsiders, it turns into a completely different establishment at night, akin only to the most questionable gambling dens, replacing the learning subjects with bluffing, deceit, and manipulating people. When Yumeko Jabami transfers into the school, everyone is shocked by her strange personality - she plays not to win, but rather purely to enjoy the thrill of gambling. Kakegurui is easily one of the most iconic gambling anime, and rightfully so. Nothing about it is even remotely realistic, but the absurdity of the games, the stakes, and the characters’ reactions are exactly what makes it so fun to watch. Featuring a diverse cast of characters who are unapologetically addicted to the risk of gambling, as well as a variety of intriguing games and scenarios that surprise viewers with unexpected resolutions, this is undoubtedly one of the most entertaining and underrated anime titles on Netflix. Watch on Netflix

9 [C] CONTROL - The Money and Soul of Possibility (2011) Original Series Produced by Tatsunoko Production

Following Japan’s economic collapse, many citizens are suffering from the consequences, with both unemployment and crime rates constantly on the rise. In such a society, Kimimaro Yoga, a troubled university student, dreams of nothing but a stable life. When a mysterious stranger offers him a life-changing amount of money at the cost of his “future”, Kimimaro accepts, unaware of the fact that he is about to be pulled into the dangerous world of the Financial District, where the only way to keep his money and protect his future is winning weekly duels called “deals”. [C] CONTROL - The Money and Soul of Possibility shows the lengths that people are willing to go to for money, something that has become as essential to life as the air humans breathe. Despite the story’s sci-fi setting, the characters’ approach to financial motivations is realistic, and their development is likewise compelling. Moreover, the title also explores real-life economic concepts in a way that makes them simultaneously intriguing and entertaining, blending social commentary with the thrill of high-stakes games. Watch on Funimation

8 Saki (2009) Produced by Gonzo & Picture Magic and Based on the Manga by Ritz Kobayashi

Saki Miyanaga has a troubled relationship with mahjong, as her family would always be displeased whether she won or lost. As a result, she developed the rare skill of always maintaining a plus-minus zero score throughout the game, which should be impossible. After being dragged by a friend to her school mahjong’s club, Saki’s talent stuns her peers, and she ends up experiencing the joy of winning a proper game for the first time. With her newfound love for mahjong and a group of like-minded friends, Saki and the rest of the club aim for the nationals. Saki tells a surprisingly intense and wonderfully chaotic story about mahjong. Compared to other gambling anime, it features lower stakes and far less tension, opting for a more light-hearted approach instead, but this only serves to give it a more distinct identity. Furthermore, though most may not know it, mahjong is officially recognized as a sport, much like chess, making this one of the most unusual sports anime titles. Watch on Crunchyroll

7 Death Parade (2015) Original Series Produced by Madhouse

When people think of the afterlife, most would visualize the duality of Heaven and Hell. However, pairs who die at the same time are brought to neither of these places, but rather to Quindecim, a luxurious bar attended by the enigmatic bartender Decim, who also plays the role of the arbiter - deciding whether the souls of the dead are to be reincarnated or banished to the void. The catch is that, while players are convinced that the outcome of the games they play determines their fate, Decim bases his decisions on the behavior they exhibit. Death Parade focuses on the human nature in relation to gambling, showing the thoughts and emotions that people experience when facing the risk of losing games that may as well cost their lives. Beyond the unique setting and intriguing premise, however, it stands out as a thought-provoking title that doesn’t hesitate to discuss profound topics that are often ignored, such as existentialism, life and death, and the ambiguity of morality and the inherent difficulty of judgment.

6 Great Pretender (2020) Original Series Produced by Wit Studio

As a result of unexpected life-changing events, Makoto Edamura has had to turn to petty crime for a living, reaching a point where he takes great pride in his skills as a swindler. However, when he decides to target what appears to be a clueless French tourist, Makoto ends up as the victim instead, falling into the trap set by the more experienced con artist Laurent Thierry, and unwillingly joining him and his eccentric associates in a world of fraud that extends far beyond the scale Makoto is used to. Although it doesn’t revolve entirely around gambling, Great Pretender features similar high-stakes cases and thrilling developments that are often found in gambling anime. Moreover, gambling and betting on different competitions are activities that occur multiple times throughout the series, typically with the intent of showing how such activities can be linked to fraud and deceit. All in all, thanks to its clever writing and captivating action, Great Pretender reminds viewers of what makes the heist genre so entertaining. Watch on Netflix

5 No Game, No Life (2014) Produced by Madhouse and Based on the Light Novel by Yuu Kamiya

Bored with life’s uninspiring daily events and lack of interesting challenges, stepsiblings Sora and Shiro spend most of their time dominating competitive strategic games under the username “Blank”. After accepting the invitation of a mysterious stranger who’s taken an interest in their otherworldly skills, they find themselves before Tet, the god of a world named Disboard, in a place where everything is decided through games. Impressed by the would-be sovereign of Disboard’s human kingdom, Sora and Shiro decide to save humanity. As its title implies, No Game, No Life depicts a world where every decision, no matter how important, depends on games. Though it may not feature gambling in the traditional sense of the word, the psychological games portrayed therein are just as captivating as those of the best gambling anime, and the stakes are just as high, if not higher. Having gained a cult following thanks to its interesting, colorful world, and riveting strategy games, this is also one of the best isekai anime for fans who are new to the theme. Fans of the title shouldn’t overlook No Game, No Life: Zero, a prequel movie that adds depth and context to the world of the series.

Watch on HiDive

4 Legendary Gambler Tetsuya (2000) Produced by Toei Animation and Based on the Manga by Fuumei Sai & Yasushi Hoshino

As a consequence of World War II, Japan’s economic state was set back drastically, losing a large portion of the advancements they had made throughout the years in the form of impressive industrial plants and innovative infrastructure. In the Shinjuku of 1947, many could hardly afford even the necessities, resorting to anything to survive, including gambling. Tetsuya Asada spends his days traveling to different mahjong parlors and challenging various opponents. However, after encountering Boshu, Tetsuya realizes that skill is just as important as luck. Despite its age, Legendary Gambler Tetsuya remains a compelling old-school title about the complexities of mahjong. Though viewers who have a grasp on the rules of the game are more likely to understand and appreciate the ongoing events, anyone can enjoy the sight of a game that is progressively evolving in terms of difficulty and skill levels. Above all, this title seeks to depict the various methods that people often employ to cheat when gambling, as well as how to outsmart and gain the upper hand on such players.

3 Akagi (2005) Produced by Madhouse and Based on the Manga by Nobuyuki Fukumoto

Suffering from a gambling addiction that has caused him to accumulate a debt of over three million yen, Nangou proposes one final game to the yakuza, the outcome of which is to determine whether his debt is fully cleared, or if he will pay with his life instead. Unfortunately for Nangou, the odds do not seem to be in his favor as the game progresses. But his luck turns around as Shigeru Akagi wanders into the mahjong parlor by chance. After learning a few rules, the young boy quickly astonishes everyone with his unparalleled talent and self-determination. Despite not reaching nearly the same heights as the original creator’s more recognizable title, Kaiji: Ultimate Survivor, Akagi tells an equally compelling and suspenseful story about a genius mahjong player who is bound to surprise and keep viewers engaged throughout the series. To Shigeru Akagi, mahjong is but a chance to investigate and manipulate his opponents, as he resorts to unexpected methods, even going as far as to risk his own life, all to ensure that he can psychologically affect his rivals. 2 One Outs (2008) Produced by Madhouse and Based on the Manga by Shinobu Kaitani

When Hiromichi Kojima, the star batter of Saikyou Saitama Lycaons, a struggling baseball team, goes to Okinawa hoping to train himself, he comes across an unexpected diamond in the rough, namely Toua Tokuchi. Known for fastballs that are nearly impossible to hit, Toua has made a name for himself in a game known as “One Outs”, whereby a pitcher and a batter face off with money on the line. Hiromichi manages to recruit Toua, but only with a special condition - he is to earn five million yen for every out pitched, and lose fifty million for every point lost. One Outs effortlessly blends sports with gambling to tell an unprecedented story about an athlete who, despite proving inferior in terms of physical strength and conventional skills, manages to come out on top due to the way he relies on his understanding of the human psyche. To Toua, pitching is not a matter of simply throwing a ball as hard as he can, but rather a chance to play a mind game with his opponent, reading their emotions and manipulating them as if gambling against them. Although it has yet to receive an anime adaptation, Shinobu Kaitani also authored Liar Game, an equally thrilling and captivating high-stakes game manga title.

1 Kaiji: Ultimate Survivor (2007) Produced by Madhouse and Based on the Manga by Nobuyuki Fukumoto

Kaiji Itou lives a directionless existence, wasting his days away until a debt collector comes and changes the trajectory of his life forever. Supposedly, one of his former co-workers, whose loan Kaiji had co-signed, failed to repay his debt and ran away, leaving Kaiji as the sole person to suffer the consequences of this unpaid loan. Told that there is a chance for him to make money and escape this burden, Kaiji ends up boarding a ship where debtors like him have to participate in dangerous gambling tournaments to earn their freedom back. Despite its simple premise and distinctly rough art style, Kaiji: Ultimate Survivor remains an unparalleled masterpiece in the realm of gambling stories. Kaiji is a flawed yet realistic protagonist, whose journey is wholly captivating and full of thrilling scenes. Not only does the series take after classic titles like JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure, in the way that every scene feels absurdly serious, but it also includes everything fans love about gambling anime, from enthralling high-stakes games to shocking revelations, making it one of the best anime about gambling of all time. Although he is best known for his Kaiji series, Nobuyuki Fukumoto also authored Gambling Emperor Legend Zero, a manga title that features a similar premise but a distinct style.
