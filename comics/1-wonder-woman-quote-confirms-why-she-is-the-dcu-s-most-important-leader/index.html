Literature Review

Wonder Woman’s best quote solidifies her standing as the linchpin of the DC Universe, affirming why she is undeniably its most vital leader.

From the brilliant mind of Gail Simone, Wonder Woman’s best quote serves as a powerful testament to why Diana Prince is unequivocally the most crucial leader in the DC Universe. Within the confines of this beautifully crafted quote, Simone skillfully unveils the distinctive quality that sets Diana apart from other heroes, showcasing how this particular attribute has evolved into a defining characteristic of the Amazon princess.

“We have a saying, my people. Don’t kill if you can wound, don’t wound if you can subdue, don’t subdue if you can pacify, and don’t raise your hand at all until you’ve first extended it.” - Wonder Woman Vol. 3 #25 Gail Simone

Nestled within the pages of Wonder Woman Vol. 3 #25, by Gail Simone and Bernard Chang, is a profound and character-defining quote that has endured as a fan-favorite since its publication in 2008.

In this issue, Diana imparts a timeless piece of wisdom: “We have a saying, my people. Don’t kill if you can wound, don’t wound if you can subdue, don’t subdue if you can pacify, and don’t raise your hand at all until you’ve first extended it.” This powerful maxim showcases Wonder Woman’s innate wisdom and provides a glimpse into the mindset that makes her DC’s most paramount leader.

Best Wonder Woman Quote Proves Diana is a Diplomat Before Anything Else Wonder Woman Vol. 3 #25 (2008) by Gail Simone and Bernard Chang

Despite the lighthearted tone permeating Wonder Woman Vol. 3 #25, Diana’s character emerges vividly through the aforementioned quote, delving beneath the surface of her warrior persona. This profound expression serves as a testament to Wonder Woman’s multifaceted identity, highlighting her foremost role as a diplomat. While her exceptional fighting prowess, epic battles, and the assertion of being one of DC’s most renowned warriors often overshadow her true essence, the quote underscores Diana’s unwavering commitment to conflict resolution through words and compassion, prioritizing these over physical force. This profound dedication to diplomacy stands as the distinguishing factor that elevates Wonder Woman to the position of DC’s most crucial leader.

Diplomacy stands as a paramount trait for any leader, and Wonder Woman exemplifies its profound significance within the DC Universe. Diana’s ability to navigate complex challenges with a focus on dialogue, compassion, and conflict resolution positions her as DC’s preeminent leader. Her approach aligns with the belief that true leadership extends beyond physical strength to encompass the ability to forge connections, bridge gaps, and seek resolutions that endure beyond the immediate conflict. In embracing diplomacy as a guiding principle, Wonder Woman emerges as a symbol of enlightened leadership, making her a formidable superhero and also the quintessential leader within the rich tapestry of the DC Universe.

Tom King’s Wonder Woman Continues to Uphold Diana’s Diplomatic Nature

In Tom King’s ongoing Wonder Woman series, Diana’s enduring and loved characterization shines through, a testament to King’s skillful portrayal of this iconic superhero. The series captures the essence of Wonder Woman, depicting her as both a warrior and a hero dedicated to fostering peace—an ethos in line with Simone’s timeless characterization. Diana’s actions within this comic run serve as a continuation of Simone’s vision, emphasizing her commitment to resolving conflicts through diplomacy and compassion rather than resorting to warfare. King artfully integrates Wonder Woman’s strength, wisdom, and compassion into the narrative, ensuring that each stride she takes aligns with the enduring principles that make her beloved among fans.
