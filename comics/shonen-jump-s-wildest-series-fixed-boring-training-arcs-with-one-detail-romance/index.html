Shonen Jump s Wildest Series Fixed Boring Training Arcs With One Detail, Romance

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Dandadan’s training arc adds drama by incorporating a romantic misunderstanding, flipping the usual dynamic between characters. The relationship between Momo, Okarun, and Jiji is a central aspect of Dandadan’s storyline and adds intrigue to the intense action. While Momo often misreads situations, this time it is Okarun who misunderstands, making for an interesting and entertaining twist.

Literature Review

Training arcs are a big part of manga, and Shonen Jump’s Dandadan proves how the addition of romance can make them much more interesting.

Training arcs are notoriously boring in manga because they often follow strict formulas that can become repetitive, but Shonen Jump+‘sDandadan found a genius way to spice things up by adding in a classic trope from romantic dramas. A hero misreading a situation might come across as cliché in a romance, but in a shonen training arc, it makes a huge difference.

In the final panels of Dandadan chapter 92 by Yukinobu Tatsu, the day’s training has ended in response to Dandadan’s upcoming war, and what appears to be the ghost of Okarun, a character who has been incapacitated this whole time, sees his love interest Momo Ayase sleeping in the arms of his good friend Jiji.

While Okarun’s assumption and focus is undeniably on what this means for his and Momo’s relationship, he’s completely misreading the situation.

Dandadan’s Training Arc Uses a Romantic Misunderstanding to Add Drama

Although the scene doesn’t transpire during an active training exercise, the entire regimen continues for the next few days, so the aforementioned scenario that Okarun witnesses is still apart of Dandadan’s big training arc. The fact that there is quite a bit of history between all three characters puts the misconception in the forefront of the actual training, especially since it is usually Momo who finds herself in the situation that Okarun’s ghost was forced to contend with.

Momo and Okarun’s ever-fluctuating relationship is one of Dandadan’s most intriguing aspects, which is impressive considering the series’ intense action. Even though the duo has had cute moments together, their relationship took the greatest leap in chapter 78 when the impersonations of a yokai caused Momo to believe that Okarun was confessing his love for her, to which Momo responded by saying that she wanted him to tell her properly to her face – and all that implies.

Meanwhile, Momo has a history with Jiji, and even though Jiji wants her back, Okarun comes to cherish his friendship with him because Jiji is his first male friend. The fact that Okarun sees Jiji and Momo together as a ghost means that Momo and Jiji aren’t able to see Okarun’s strained reaction when they wake up together in Chapter 93. All of this interpersonal romantic drama goes far in making Dandadan’s training arc stand out. The misunderstanding also does a great job of flipping Okarun and Momo’s relationship.

Dandadan Innovates By Flipping One of its Central Dynamics

Though Okarun is on the receiving end of a misunderstanding in chapter #92, it’s usually Momo who misreads situations. Many chapters ago Momo caught Okarun and Aira in a compromising position. Although Aira was purposefully trying to get intimate with Okarun, Okarun wanted nothing to do with her. In what is always an enjoyable trope in romance stories, it would take Okarun some time before he was able to tell Momo what really happened. More recently, Momo found herself fuming at Okarun whenever Vamola courted him, a predicament that Okarun enjoyed, much to Momo’s chagrin.

As Okarun is clearly the level-headed one of the two, it is interesting to see their dynamic flipped. The artwork of Okarun as a ghost trying to get Momo and Jiji to see him is endlessly entertaining, especially coming from a character like Okarun. Dandadan is one of Shonen Jump+’s strangest manga, but its many innovations with things like romance and training arcs are clearly paying off.
