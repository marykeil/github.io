Marvel s New Hero Smasher Combines the Powers of His Avengers & X-Men Parents

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Dead X-Men #1 introduces a new Smasher, a powerful mutant with the abilities of both his X-Men father Cannonball, and his Shi’ar warrior mother Izzy Kane. Smasher is on a mission to honor the memory of his deceased parents by fighting against Orbis Stellaris and the Technarchy to save the galaxy. Although Smasher’s parents were killed when he was still very young, they would be incredibly proud of the honorable and heroic man he has become.

Literature Review

Dead X-Men #1 reveals a future Smasher, son of Cannonball and Izzy Kane’s Smasher, acting as a powerful legacy hero alongside the Starjammers.

Warning: Contains spoilers for Dead X-Men #1The epic start to a team of resurrected mutant’s reality-saving mission in Dead X-Men #1 has introduced an alternate timeline version of Josiah Guthrie, the powerful son of X-Men member Cannonball and former Avenger Smasher, as a legacy Smasher, complete with both his parents abilities. Fighting alongside Carol Danvers as Warbird and a revolutionary Starjammers crew, Smasher takes the battle directly to Orbis Stellaris, fighting for his future and to honor the memory of his parents.

Dead X-Men sees Prodigy, Jubilee, Cannonball, Frenzy, and Dazzler traversing through different timelines of Sinister’s Moira Engine, attempting to find a non-cyborg Moira so Prodigy can read her mind and gain necessary intel.

Dead X-Men #1, from Steve Foxe with art by Bernard Chang, Jonas Scharf, and Vincenzo Carratù, introduces Smasher in the context of an annihilated Earth, with the Sinister clone Orbis Stellaris attempting to steal mysterium as the Technarchy tries to wipe out the last stronghold of mutants.

Smasher Is The Powerful Son Of Sam Guthrie And Izzy Kane

While not explicitly named, it is reasonable to assume that the young Smasher introduced in Dead X-Men #1 is Josiah Guthrie, son of Sam Guthrie and Izzy Kane. First introduced in Avengers World #17, Josiah was born with Shi’ar supergestation technology on the planet Chandilar and is deeply loved by both of his iconic parents. Sam Guthrie, or the New Mutants hero Cannonball, is one of the most well-known X-Men of all time, with the power to propel himself with thermo-chemical energy, while also creating a blast shield around him, making him nigh-inpenetrable.

Izzy Kane’s Smasher is just as powerful if not more so than Cannonball, possessing the Shi’ar technology goggles called Exospex, which give her superhuman strength and durability among other abilities. Their son Smasher is not only a mutant with his father’s thermo-chemical energy abilities - said by Izzy to be even tougher than Sam’s in U.S.Avengers #5 - but also the wielder of the Exospex, giving him both of his parents’ powers. Dead X-Men revealed that Izzy and Sam were killed during the fall of Earth, either in the destruction of Krakoa or the intergalactic war that followed and Smasher is understandably vengeful over their deaths.

Izzy And Sam Would Be Incredibly Proud Of Their Superhero Son

Orbis Stellaris was revealed to be the puppet master behind the ensuing intergalactic war, pulling the strings of Earth and Arakko’s fall, and ultimately the reason that Smasher’s parents were both killed. As part of the new Starjammers team - including his father’s best friend Sunspot - Smasher asked Warbird to be the one to destroy Orbis’ base and his newly acquired M’Kraan crystal, which he successfully did by using the combined powers of his incredible parents. Sadly, Sam did not get to meet his teenage son, but if he did he and Izzy would be incredibly proud of the honorable and heroic man Josiah has become.

It is not clear whether the Dead X-Men will revisit Smasher’s reality, although the Moira from that timeline is teased to become a larger threat moving forward, so there is a chance Smasher could meet his deceased father Cannonball. Regardless, Smasher carries on the legacy of his X-Men father Sam, and his Shi’ar warrior mother Izzy, honoring their memory and fighting to save the galaxy.

Dead X-Men #1 from Marvel Comics is available now in stores.
