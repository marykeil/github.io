10 Longest Manga Hiatuses That Left Fans on the Edge of Their Seats

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Vagabond has been on hiatus for almost 9 years, testing the devotion of fans who eagerly await its continuation. Dogs: Bullets & Carnage has left fans hanging since 2015, with a major cliffhanger that needs closure. X by Clamp has been on hiatus for over 21 years, leaving fans wondering how the story will conclude.

Literature Review

Long hiatuses are an unfortunate part of the grueling manga industry, but these 10 series tested their readers’ patience like no others.

Many manga fans know the pain of seeing their favorite series go on a brief hiatus, but that can’t compare to how brutal the waits have been for the manga that have gone on the longest hiatuses. These hiatuses can be caused by many factors including health issues or cancelation, but no matter what the reason being left on the edge of their seats waiting for another chapter has tested the devotion of some fans and broken the wills of others. However, for those who manage to wait out the entire break, a potential continuation to the story can be incredible to read. Of course, not every series that goes on hiatus comes back. Sometimes the creators lose interest in the story or their health problems become too debilitating. Sometimes the creators tragically pass away, leaving their manga unfinished unless another group of creators continues the story, like with Berserk’s new creative team. Mostly however, in those situations the manga will just end, so series like High School of the Dead which released its last chapter in 2010 won’t be considered on hiatus. However, there are plenty of long hiatuses that aren’t the result of a creator’s death, some of which even beat the odds to end after decades.

10 Vagabond Length of Hiatus: 2015-Present

Manga Creator Hiatus Length Vagabond Takehito Inoue 9 Years
Vagabond is one of the most popular manga that has gone on a long hiatus. Created by Takehiko Inoue, the series follows the historical figure Musashi Miyamoto as he fights his way through Sengoku-era Japan. The story features great characters and art, but unfortunately, its creator seems uninterested in completing it soon. One of Inoue’s other series Real was able to return after a 5 year hiatus, so it’s possible that Vagabond will return. However, since the last chapter was published in May 2015, fans have waited with bated breath for almost 9 years for Musashi’s journey to continue. Read From Viz

9 Dogs: Bullets & Carnage Length of Hiatus: 2015-Present

Manga Creator Hiatus Length Dogs: Bullets & Carnage Shirow Miwa 9 Years
Dogs: Bullets & Carnage is a Seinen manga by Shirow Miwa that initially follows 4 separate morally gray protagonists who eventually meet in a futuristic and gritty city. Both the setting and the characters themselves are really interesting, but the action sequences are what make this manga so exciting. Unfortunately, the manga went on hiatus in March 2015 and while Miwa expressed interest in continuing the series in 2017, no new chapters have been released since then. This is especially unfortunate because the manga left off on a major cliffhanger that fans need closure for. Read From Viz

8 Glass Mask Length of Hiatus: 2012-Present

Manga Creator Hiatus Length Glass Mask Suzue Miuchi 12 Years
Glass Mask is a long-running series, created in 1976 by Suzue Miuchi. The series follows an aspiring actress Maya as she attempts to get her dream part of the lead in the famous play The Crimson Goddess. Along the way, she clashed with a rival Ayumi, who is a great character in her own right. Unfortunately, the compelling journeys of these two girls stopped in October 2012 when the series went into an indefinite hiatus. While The Crimson Goddess was actually adapted for the stage in 2020, no more chapters of the actual series have been released. Glass Mask is not currently available to read in English

7 Magic Kaito Length of Longest Hiatus: 1994-2007

Manga Creator Hiatus Length Magic Kaito Gosho Aoyama 13 Years
Magic Kaito is a bit of a strange case when it comes to keeping track of its hiatuses. Created by Gosho Aoyama, it follows the magician and thief Kaito on his various adventures. The series has gone on intermittent hiatuses since 1998, with the longest one being from 1994 to 2007. However, some of the characters in the series have occasionally crossed over into Aoyama’s more popular series Case d. This means that while fans haven’t always been able to read Kaito’s solo adventures, they could at least see what some of the cast was doing in the meantime. Aoyama has clarified that Case d and Magic Kaito are in separate continuities despite these crossovers. Magic Kaito isn’t available to read in English.

6 Bastard!! Heavy Metal, Dark Fantasy Length of Hiatus: 2010-Present

Manga Creator Hiatus Length Bastard!! Heavy Metal, Dark Fantasy Kazushi Hagiwara 14 Years
Bastard!! is an iconic dark fantasy series that takes place in a brutal and dystopian world. It follows the reincarnation of the wizard Dark Schneider as he battles to prevent the resurrection of the demon Anthrasax. Its creator Kazushi Hagiwara has recently made a redrawn version of the manga’s original one shot in 2023, but the actual series itself has been on hiatus since 2010, with unfortunately little indication of when if ever it will return. Still, its recent anime adaptation shows that there is still interest in the series, so hopefully, the manga’s hiatus won’t last much longer. Bastard!! is currently out of print in English.

5 Nana Length of Hiatus: 2009-Present

Manga Creator Hiatus Length Nana Ai Yazawa 15 Years
Nana is a grounded tale of two women named Nana who end up living together. Over its 9 years of regular serialization, series creator Ai Yazawa recorded their respective journies, as one pursued her music career and the other looked for love. However, due to health issues, Yazawa had to put the manga on indefinite hiatus in 2009. Still despite this, Nana has remained popular and Yazawa even released some new art for the series in a 2015 calendar. With almost 15 years on hiatus, many fans are still anxiously hoping that Nana’s story will someday reach a satisfying conclusion. Read From Viz

4 X Length of Hiatus: 2003-Present

Manga Creators Hiatus Length X Clamp 21 Years
X is a manga created by the iconic creative group Clamp following the young Kamui as he returns home during the last days of 1999 when an apocalypse is about to occur. Kamui must fight to keep the world alive while also coming into conflict with his former childhood friends. In 2003 the series went on indefinite hiatus when the magazine serializing it wouldn’t publish Clamp’s intended ending. While Clamp has expressed interest in ending the series at some point, over two decades later this ending hasn’t come leaving fans to wonder about how the story will conclude. Read From Viz

3 Hades Project Zeorymer Length of Hiatus: 1984-2007

Manga Creators Hiatus Length Hades Project Zeorymer Chimi Moriwo 23 Years
Hades Project Zeorymer is a mecha series from the 1980s following the son of a scientist Masaki, who is tasked with preventing his father’s invention from falling into the wrong hands. While this may not be the most compelling premise for anime fans who have grown up with Neon Genesis Evangelion, at the time it was a more revolutionary idea and this made it disappointing when Chimi Moriwo was unable to finish its initial run satisfactorily. Luckily in 2007, 23 years after the original serialization ended, Moriwo was able to write the end to the story. Moriwo also began a sequel in 2008 called Project Zeorymer Omega with the artist Watari U. Neither the main series nor the sequel are available to read in English.

2 Stop!! Hibari-kun! Length of Hiatus: 1983-2010

Manga Creators Hiatus Length Stop!! Hibari-kun! Hisashi Eguchi 27 Years
Stop!! Hibari-kun! is one of the weirdest cases of a manga returning from a hiatus. The series is a romantic comedy that was serialized in the early 1980s following the adventures of Kosaku and Hibari who is an early example of a trans character in manga. The manga initially ended when its creator Hisashi Eguchi couldn’t keep up with the gruelling pace of producing chapters weekly. This left the manga with an unsatisfying ending, so for a 2010 rerelease of the series Eguchi drew the originally planned ending and added it to the story, 27 years after it originally finished. Stop!! Hibari-kun! is unavailable to read in English.

1 Otoko-Zaka Length of Hiatus: 1985-2014

Manga Creators Hiatus Length Otoko-Zaka Masami Kurumada 29 Years
Otoko-Zaka is a fighting manga by Masami Kurumada, the creator of the influential classic Saint Seiya. The series was initially serialized from 1984 to 1985 before it was canceled. Almost thirty years later in 2014, with a slew of successful series under his belt, Kurumada returned to the manga and worked on it for 9 more years, concluding Otoko-Zaka in 2023. While it may be a bit obscure, this return to the series shows Kurumada’s passion for it and provides hope to fans of other series on long hiatuses. Even the longest manga hiatuses of all time can end, as Otoko-Zaka shows. Otoko-Zaka is currently unavailable to read in English.
