10 Best Web Novels Which Deserve Manhwa Adaptations

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Web novels like Warlock of the Magus World bring a fresh twist to the isekai genre, combining science and fantasy effectively. A Monster Who Levels Up offers an interesting perspective on good and evil in a fantasy world, with a game-like system that leads to perilous evolutions. Throne of Magical Arcana explores the weaponization of academia in a fantasy world, where knowledge of science and mathematics gives the protagonist a big edge.

Literature Review

With popular manhwa taking the anime stage by storm it’s time to look into the web novel industry that all renowned manhwa originate from.

The best web novels that deserve manhwa adaptations are often overlooked and underrated, but they provide just as much riveting content as currently serialized manhwa or manhua (the Chinese equivalent). With the recent rise and success of anime like Heaven’s Official Blessing, Tower of God, Solo Leveling, and more, there is greater exposure to each respective series’ source material and the differences between the source material adaptation structures of Korea, China, and Japan. The traditional anime structure in Japan begins with manga or light novels being adapted to anime, essentially for advertising purposes, with the occasional original series like Neon Genesis Evangelion or Gurren Lagann. However, the process of adapting manhwa and manhua to anime is much more arduous because the source materials almost all have source materials of their own in the form of web novels, which leads to many solid stories slipping under the radar.

10 Best Completed Manhwa For fans who are tired of cliffhangers and unexpected hiatuses, completed manhwa provide uncompromising experiences and satisfying conclusions.

10 Warlock of the Magus World Brings Science Into Fantasy Worlds A Chinese Web Novel by Wen Chao Gong

Warlock of the Magus World is a web novel first released in 2015 that puts a fresh twist on the isekai fantasy genre by bringing a touch of science into the world of mages and magic. The story follows the main character, previously Fang Ming and reborn as Leylin Farlier, who transmigrates with an advanced A.I. chip that has fused with his soul. With this remnant of the technological world with him, Leylin takes the world of magi by storm, as the chip helps him grow his powers through all sorts of analysis, memory support, and more. 9 A Monster Who Levels Up Takes a Monstrous Perspective A Korean Web Novel by Jee Gab Song

A Monster Who Levels Up is a completed web novel that began its serialization in 2017. One of the most notable things about A Monster Who Levels up is its interesting perspective on the dichotomy of good and evil in a fantasy world. The story takes place in a world similar to that of Solo Leveling with its urban fantasy balance, and follows main character Kim Sae-Jin, who one day wakes up as a monster. Although he is first confused, Sae-Jin quickly adapts to the mysterious game-like system that accompanied his transformation and works towards eventually regaining his humanity through a series of perilous evolutions. READ ON Webnovel

8 Throne of Magical Arcana Weaponizes Academia A Chinese Web Novel by Cuttlefish That Loves Diving

Written by the same author of the popular Lord of the Mysteries series that already has a manhua adaptation, Throne of Magical Arcana is an earlier showing of Cuttlefish That Loves Diving’s excellent writing abilities. Throne of Magical Arcana follows Xia Feng, whose soul travels from Earth to inhabit that of Lucius Evan’s in a fantasy world. However, Lucius quickly realizes that the Western fantasy he’d been isekai’d to is not exactly normal, as the laws of the world are very similar to Earth’s, from science, to mathematics, to the laws of relativity. The twist is that through this knowledge, the power of magic is conquered and harnessed; calculus can cast spells and the periodic table can shake the entire world. With his knowledge from his time on Earth, Lucius embarks on the path of magic and academia all in one, as he realizes that Earth’s high academic standards give him a big edge. READ ON Webnovel 1:56

7 Quickly Wear the Face of the Devil Writes a Questionable Redemption Story A Chinese Web Novel by Feng Liu Shu Dai

Quickly Wear the Face of the Devil is a reincarnation story repackaged as a redemption story, though as the narrative progresses, whether that is true remains to be seen. The novel follows Zhou Yunsheng, who was once a top-tier hacker, as he is reborn time and again through the Villain System that casts Yunsheng as an expendable villain in a fantasy world every time. However, Yunsheng finally struggles out of the grasp of the system and, with renewed freedom, he sets out to become a figure contrary to all of his previous roles, at least on the surface. READ ON Webnovel

6 Rebirth of the Thief Who Roamed the World Perfectly Balances Game and Reality A Chinese Web Novel by Mad Snail

Written by the same author of the popular manhua series Tales of Demons and Gods and Star Martial God Technique, Rebirth of the Thief Who Roamed the World is arguably one of Mad Snail’s best works, but it doesn’t get the recognition it deserves. The story follows Nie Yan, who dies for revenge but gets an opportunity to redo his life. The story’s setting is one that borders both sci-fi and fantasy, as most of the plot takes place in the virtual world of Conviction, a VRMMO that surprisingly serves as a foundation of Nie Yan’s modernizing world. In this world, Nie Yan plays the character of a thief, and through his past life’s memories he is able to gain advantages that no one else will even think of. However, the most interesting part of the story is the fact that outside of the game, there is an element of martial arts that Nie Yan eventually comes to realize has connections with Conviction that are much deeper than he thought. 10 Best Reincarnation Manhwa The best manhwa that utilize reincarnation will have regret-cleansing and escapism themes.

5 Emperor of Solo Play Makes Skeletons and Zombies Palatable and Much More A Korean Web Novel by D-Dart

First released in 2015, Emperor of Solo Play is a novel with similarities to the popular Solo Leveling, but focuses more on the necromancer occupation as it hones in much further on the fantasy video game genre. The story follows An Jaehyun, who was once an expert in the virtual reality game, Warlord, but was betrayed by his guild members. When he is given a second chance at life and travels back in time, Jaehyun decides not to trust anyone ever again. As a result, Jaehyun sets out to become the most overpowered necromancer, whose army of skeletons and zombies can take the place of any prospective teammate. Aside from his in-game adventures, the tales of Jaehyun uploading his gameplay and embodying the role of a YouTuber is just as amusing to follow as the main storyline. READ ON Webnovel

4 God of Crime Turns Misdemeanors and Felonies Into Game Skills A Korean Web Novel by Han Yeoul

God of Crime is an interesting reincarnation, or regression web novel that follows Seo Tae Hyuk in the reliving of his life that was originally slated for death row. Having been perfectly framed for a crime he didn’t commit, Tae Hyuk is filled with the utmost regret, and when he is reborn and given a game-like system that quantifies crimes into numbers and skills, Tae Hyuk sets off to uncover the secrets of the mysterious incident that landed him in jail. Wielding skills from robbery to spying, Tae Hyuk still finds time to play the vigilante along the way. READ ON Webnovel

3 King of Gods Is the Representative Cultivation Novel That Has It All A Chinese Web Novel by Fast Food Restaurant

Not to be confused with the manhua King of The Gods, King of Gods is the pinnacle of Xuanhuan world-building and cultivation narrative construction. Xuanhuan is a genre term that refers to the more magical side of cultivation novels that stems from Taoist and Buddhist influences, narrowing in on a subsection of Xianxia, which refers to the overall genre of immortal adventures, demon-slaying, and the like. Within this genre, what King of Gods does so well is creating a never-ending momentum that carries the main character, Zhao Feng, onto endlessly evolving adventures, and eventually the path of immortality. Throughout the series, every cultivation trope is touched on but far from overused, and characters are never wasted, as even the most unassuming figures often reveal their relevance later in the series, creating an amazing network of relationships. READ ON Webnovel

2 Birth of the Demonic Sword Takes Maximum Western Fantasy Influence Web Novel by Eveofchaos

Originally written in English, Birth of the Demonic Sword is an amazing series that reads like a traditional cultivation novel, but with sprinkles of contemporary and modern twists. The story follows Noah Balvan, who is a transmigrated soul that is reborn as an infant in a fantasy world that takes a lot of inspiration from the Xuanxuan genre. Noah strives for the pinnacle of the magical world through any means, from developing new martial arts to refining a legendary demonic sword. Although the series shares different roots than other Korean and Chinese series that receive manhwa or manhua adaptations, Birth of the Demonic Sword is just as deserving. READ ON Webnovel

1 Stop, Friendly Fire Creates New Systems and Fresh Perspectives A Korean Web Novel by Toika

Written by the same author of the popular series, Everyone Else Is a Returnee, Stop, Friendly Fire creates a world with a unique fantasy system that arguably tells a better story than the formerly mentioned Everyone Else Is a Returnee, which already has a serializing manhwa. The narrative of Stop, Friendly Fire follows Lee Shin Woo, who is summoned to a seemingly self-contained fantasy world. Unfortunately, the god that did the summoning made a mistake, resulting in Lee Shin Woo turning into a skeleton, much to his surprise and inconvenience. This invigorating premise creates wonderful grounds for misunderstandings between the other heroes that have been summoned to save the world and Lee Shin Woo, thus resulting in the title of the series, Stop, Friendly Fire. Although Shin Woo is capable of shouting these words to fellow humans, it is their prerogative to believe him or not, leading to a somewhat lonely, but endlessly exciting path to strength for the persevering skeleton Lee Shin Woo. Stop, Friendly Fire is an amazing web novel and proves why more entries in the medium deserve manhwa adaptations. READ ON Wuxiaworld
