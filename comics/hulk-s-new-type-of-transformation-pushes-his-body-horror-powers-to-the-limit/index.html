Hulk s New Type of Transformation Pushes His Body Horror Powers to the Limit

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 800 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Marvel Comics introduces a new type of transformation for the Hulk, adding a layer of horror to his already gruesome powers. The Hulk becomes a flesh and bone mech-suit for Bruce Banner in order to protect him from the eldritch creature War Devil. This transformation showcases a potential shift in Hulk’s relationship with Banner, as he actively chooses to protect him instead of inflicting pain.

Literature Review

The nature of the Hulk’s powers is disturbing, but the transformation process has never been as grisly as it is now - especially in this latest issue!

Warning: Contains SPOILERS for The Incredible Hulk #8! Body horror is arguably the cornerstone of Hulk mythos, as the very nature of Bruce Banner’s superpower to transform into the Hulk and become a being of immense strength is gruesome and grotesque. And now, Marvel Comics has added a new layer of horror to this already grisly aspect of Hulk’s powers, pushing them to their absolute limits with one new type of transformation.

In The Incredible Hulk #8 by Phillip Kennedy Johnson and Nic Klein, the Hulk is possessed by an eldritch creature called War Devil, otherwise known as the First Hunter of Men. This corrupted spirit took control over the Hulk’s body after creeping into his mind and embedding itself within Hulk’s psyche. And that’s where War Devil had quite the unexpected encounter with this body’s other inhabitant: Bruce Banner.

War Devil has no use for Banner, so it angles to kill him. However, the Hulk hasn’t been totally beaten despite momentarily losing control over his physical form, as his metaphysical form manifests between Banner and the attacking War Devil in an effort to save his alter ego from this intrusive monster - and the form Hulk takes is pretty shocking. Rather than taking his normal form, the Hulk becomes something of a mech-suit for Banner, one made from flesh and bone rather than machinery, with Banner himself finding refuge in Hulk’s hollow rib cage.

Hulk’s Latest Marvel Comics Run Has Pushed the Boundaries of His Body Horror The body horror in The Incredible Hulk Vol. 4 is matched only by The Immortal Hulk!

Hulk turning himself into a morbid mech-suit within the metaphysical plane of his and Banner’s shared psyche is far from the first example of extreme body horror in this comic run alone. The Incredible Hulk Vol. 4 has been all about Hulk’s relationship with Bruce Banner, and how he wants to make his alter ego suffer for the pain he inflicted during the ‘Starship Hulk’ storyline. In that effort, Hulk deliberately makes shifting as painful as possible for Bruce, and that leads to some gnarly visuals.

When the Hulk takes control of their shared body, he literally rips Banner apart as gruesomely as he can. Then, when Hulk reverts to Banner, he forces his body to contort horrifically as it shrinks back to Bruce’s original ‘puny Banner’ self. However, this latest transformation actually speaks to the opposite of what all the others represented, as this transformation wasn’t used to hurt Banner, but to protect him.

Hulk’s New Transformation Could Help Heal His Relationship with Bruce Banner Hulk actively tried to protect Banner rather than inflicting more pain!

While Hulk saving Bruce from War Devil was assuredly an act of self-preservation way more than it was done out of the goodness of his heart, Hulk still protected Banner nonetheless. This could have been Hulk’s chance to be rid of Bruce once and for all by letting this eldritch monster consume his essence - but Hulk didn’t let that happen. Hulk preferred twisting his body into a protective shell for Banner to hide within, to letting his alter ego be killed by War Devil, which seems like a step in the right direction in terms of healing their fractured relationship.

Whether this new grotesque form was something of an olive branch Hulk was extending to Banner, or if it was just the latest of the increasingly disturbing transformations this comic run has to offer, in the end, the result is the same, as Hulk’s new type of transformation pushes his body horror powers to the limit.

The Incredible Hulk #8 by Marvel Comics is available now.
