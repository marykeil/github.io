10 Power Rangers Moments That Prove the New Green Ranger Has Earned His Title

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

An all-new Green Ranger, Matthew Cook, debuts in BOOM! Studios Power Rangers series as a hero equal to the original. Matthew showcases bravery without powers, facing torture from Rita Repulsa and taking on Lord Zedd to save the Power Rangers. Matthew’s journey includes revealing his identity to the world, saving lives before becoming a Ranger, and helping boost fellow Ranger Rocky’s confidence.

Literature Review

BOOM! Studios’ Mighty Morphin Power Rangers series introduced Matthew Cook, a new Green Ranger who has earned the mantle once worn by Tommy Oliver.

The BOOM! Studios Power Rangers ongoing series introduced an all-new Green Ranger who earned the stripes originally worn by Tommy Oliver. Due to the nature of time and space being twisted after the events of Shattered Grid, the comic series was given free rein to introduce new elements never-before-seen in the television show. This includes a Green Ranger on the Mighty Morphin team who isn’t Tommy Oliver. Beginning in Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #55 and throughout the earliest issues of the following Mighty Morphin series, there is a mystery surrounding the identity of the new Green Ranger recruited by Mighty Morphin’s most controversial leader, Grace Sterling. Eventually, the mysterious Green Ranger’s identity is revealed to be Matthew Cook, a friend and classmate of the Rangers with an understandably vested interest in their superhero lives. As an original character created for the comics, the name may not stand out to longtime viewers unfamiliar with the comics, but even before he becomes a Ranger, he earns his stripes as a hero. Once he is a Ranger, he proves why he deserves his Morpher.

10 Escaping from Rita Repulsa Go Go Power Rangers #7 by Ryan Parrott and Dan Mora

Introduced in the Go Go Power Rangers series, Matthew is kidnapped by Rita Repulsa and replaced by her shapeshifter Putty on Earth, while she tries to squeeze intel about the Rangers out of him. He’s tortured and starved, but doesn’t relent. Meanwhile, the Rangers are none the wiser that their friend is actually an impostor. With no hope of rescue in sight, Matthew opts to save himself, which, for someone who’s never received a power coin, is impressive. While the Empress and her goons are preparing to attack the Rangers on Homecoming night, Matthew ambushes Squatt and forces him to send him back to Earth.

9 Surviving the Coinless World with No Powers Go Go Power Rangers #11 by Ryan Parrott and Dan Mora

It’s easy to forget Matthew Cook makes an appearance during the Shattered Grid storyline because his appearance is extremely brief, but nonetheless significant. He appears once in the Coinless world in Go Go Power Rangers #11, where the rechristened leader of the resistance, Kimberly, stumbles upon a one-armed, beaten, broken Matthew in their infirmary unit. He has just finished taking on an army of Lord Drakkon’s Sentries by himself to protect their hideout. Moments later, he dies with a smile on his face. Matthew never gets powers in this world, but he maintains his signature resilience and blind bravery stemming from a heart of gold.

8 Staring Down a Giant Zord Without a Suit or Zord of His Own Go Go Power Rangers #10 by Ryan Parrott and Dan Mora

Speaking of Matthew’s blind bravery, much of that bravery is on display at the end of Go Go Power Rangers #10, bleeding into Go Go Power Rangers #11. After surviving Rita, Matthew Cook is determined to find out why he keeps finding himself in the middle of the Power Rangers’ adventures, unaware that it’s because he’s friends with all of them. He confronts his suspicions by entering the thick of a Zord fight between the Dino Megazord and the Ranger Slayer’s Megazord. Matthew’s suspicions grow when the Ranger Slayer’s voice echoes from her Zord - because even under Drakkon’s mind control, she must acknowledge her ex - but he displays no fear whatsoever.

7 Figuring Out the Rangers’ Secret Identities Go Go Power Rangers #12 by Ryan Parrott and Dan Mora

In this issue, Matthew finally gets the truth that he’s been looking for, but at a tragic cost. Before returning to her timeline, the future, alternate reality version of Kimberly - free of her Ranger Slayer mind control - pays Matthew a visit, making peace with a lifelong regret of never telling him she was a Ranger. Furious that his friends have let him drone on aimlessly looking for answers, Matthew uses the newfound clarity to demand that the present-day Rangers tell him the truth, or else he’ll never speak to them again. When they remain silent, he exits from their lives. He remains gone from the series until he resurfaces as the Green Ranger.

6 Putting Lord Zedd in a Coma in His Ranger Debut Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #55 by Ryan Parrott and Moises Hidalgo

It looks like Lord Zedd’s wildest dreams of world domination have finally come to fruition. Having used the Green Chaos Crystal to rechristen Rita Repulsa and her favorite creatures into the Dark Power Rangers, all hope seems lost with the Mighty Morphin squad on the ropes. That is, until a new Green Ranger steps onto the scene. Matthew’s identity would be revealed in time, but for now, he leaves an immediate impression by being a formidable combat opponent for Zedd before destroying the Crystal. The impact of the blast is enough to put the Power Rangers’ final boss into a coma.

5 Debuting the New Dragon Strike Mighty Morphin #4 by Ryan Parrott and Marco Renna

Given that he is the second man to don the Green Ranger outfit, Matthew could easily coast through his new crime-fighting career by using the tried and true methods that made Tommy Oliver’s run such a success. Instead, Matthew continues to add new moves to his arsenal after training at Promethea. Case in point, during a fight with a new panda-based monster - appropriately and creatively named Pandamonium - Matthew finishes him off with a move that shocks even the Rangers: the Dragon Strike, something that Tommy never showed he could do before. Matthew is a new dog using new tricks instead of the same old ones.

4 Revealing His Identity to the World Mighty Morphin #4 by Ryan Parrott and Marco Renna

Secret identities have always been a staple of the franchise, but early into his run as Green Ranger, Matthew reveals his not just to readers, but to the entire world around him. It’s a big secret to give up, but he does so for a noble cause. In a time when the citizens are afraid after endless monster attacks, this new Green Ranger wants to assure the city he’s protecting that they can trust him by telling them his biggest secret. It speaks to the core of who Matthew is. He hates the secrets and lies that stemmed from the Rangers before he became one and doesn’t want to repeat that cycle.

3 Saving Someone Before He’s Even a Ranger Mighty Morphin #5 by Ryan Parrott and Marco Renna

Throughout the character’s run in the comics across the franchise, Matthew often tries to remind people around him that he’s no hero, even if his actions suggest otherwise. Even if he never believes it himself, Matthew Cook has always been worthy of being a Power Ranger. After he reveals his identity to the world, the next issue shows off the beginning of Matthew’s time as a Ranger, including why he’s chosen and his training. He still insists through the narration that he’s not brave, yet he is saying this as he leaps into action to save someone’s life before he even gets a Morpher. Even if he’s leaping into action scared, at least he’s leaping.

2 Giving Rocky the Dragon Shield Mighty Morphin #20 by Mat Groom and Moises Hidalgo

Matthew isn’t the only new Ranger on the scene skeptical of his abilities as one of the Power Rangers. Rocky has big shoes to fill in replacing Jason as the Red Ranger, thus essentially becoming the team’s co-captain next to Tommy before he’s ready. In the middle of a fight with King Aradon of the Automaton Dominion (direct enemies to Zeo’s Machine Empire), Rocky has a breakdown that he’s not good enough for this. Matt helps boost Rocky’s confidence not only with a pep talk, but by giving him his Dragon Shield to feel stronger (physically and emotionally) than he does at that moment.

1 Choosing to Be Good Again After Rita’s Mind Control Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #112 by Melissa Flores and Hendry Prasetya

In Mighty Morphin Power Rangers #102, Matthew once again finds himself kidnapped by Rita Repulsa (this time calling herself Mistress Vile), but she takes advantage of his newfound Rangerhood by making him into her minion. When the Rangers break the spell in #109, he’s too confused to rejoin their team, and all the more angry that the Rangers got him captured a second time. He re-joins Rita, nearly repeating history as the next Lord Drakkon, but thanks largely to a pep talk from Tommy, Matthew comes to his senses. As Green Ranger, Matthew does the one thing that Tommy couldn’t before becoming Lord Drakkon: rejoin the Power Rangers.
