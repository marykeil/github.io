The New Hulk s Identity is Finally Revealed, Confirming Bruce Banner Transformed a Major Ally

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Rick Jones is the Earth-65 Hulk, created by a sinister Bruce Banner in Spider-Gwen Smash #3! Bruce Banner turns allies into monsters and commits heinous crimes in this twisted Marvel reality. Rick Jones has a dark history with gamma radiation, now facing new challenges as Earth-65’s Hulk.

Literature Review

Marvel Comics recently introduced an all-new version of the Incredible Hulk who isn’t Bruce Banner, and now, their identity has finally been revealed.

Warning: Contains SPOILERS for Spider-Gwen Smash #3! Bruce Banner is the original Hulk, but that doesn’t mean he’s the only ‘Hulk’ in the Marvel Universe - far from it, in fact. A number of people have taken up that particular mantle in the past, and now, one more has officially been added to the list. However, this person is especially unique, as he’s not only a classic ally of the original Hulk, but he was created by Bruce Banner himself.

In Spider-Gwen Smash #3 by Melissa Flores and Enid Balám, Ghost-Spider and Dazzler are attacked by Earth-65 versions of the Hulk and Pixie, resulting in Mary Jane and even Dazzler herself getting kidnapped. Ghost-Spider goes back to her place to come up with a game plan to get her friends back while Natasha Romanoff takes to the streets to investigate the scene. That’s when there’s a breakthrough, as Gwen and Natasha discover the identity of the Hulk who assisted in the kidnapping of their friends: Rick Jones.

In this alternate reality, Rick Jones wasn’t rescued by Bruce Banner during a gamma bomb demonstration, but was actually cursed by Banner to suffer the very fate Bruce saved Rick from in Earth-616. In Earth-65, Banner befriended Rick when Jones was at his lowest, offering him treatment for a supposed substance abuse problem (which was just a ploy to gain medical access to him). Banner secretly turned Jones into the Hulk, and then proceeded to use him for his own nefarious (and downright creepy) purposes.

Rick Jones is the Hulk of Marvel Comics Earth-65, & Bruce Banner Created Him Bruce Banner is a sinister villain in Spider-Gwen Smash!

Rick Jones is someone who has always been there for both Bruce Banner and the Hulk during the early days of their adventures. Of course, that was in Earth-616, where Bruce Banner was still a hero at heart who always tried his best to protect those around him, and aim the Hulk at the bad guys whenever the Jade Giant took over. In Earth-65, Bruce Banner is a sick and twisted villain who would get someone to trust him only to turn him into a monster that he could then force to commit crimes in his stead.

Bruce Banner turned Rick Jones into the Hulk, but he also turned Mary Jane (after the Hulk and Pixie brought her to him) into this universe’s Carnage. However, as bad as these crimes of unwanted mutations are, arguably the worst part about this version of Bruce is his creepy obsession with Dazzler. The sight of Bruce having a sedated Dazzler strapped down while he plays her music in his laboratory is truly skin-crawling, and really hammers home how disgustingly villainous Banner is in this reality, on top of his sick scientific pursuits.

This Isn’t the First Time Rick Jones Has Gotten a Hulk-Level ‘Upgrade’ Immortal Hulk by Al Ewing and Joe Bennett

Rick Jones becoming the Hulk may be a new development, though this isn’t the first time he’s been mutated by gamma radiation. Due to his gamma irradiated DNA, Immortal Hulk revealed Rick Jones also had access to the Green Door, which meant he was both immortal and doomed to the eternal hell of the Below-Place. This also meant he was susceptible to borderline demonic entities, like the Leader, who possessed Rick Jones’ body for a time. Jones’ existence became truly hellish when his essence was merged with Del Frye, turning him into a twisted creature of perpetual anguish and pain.

While this isn’t Rick Jones’ first time being a gamma mutate, it’s certainly the first time he’s been any universe’s prime Hulk, and only time will tell what that will mean for him moving forward in the Spider-Gwen Smash series. However, regardless of what the future holds, the biggest takeaway is that fans now know the identity of Marvel Comics’ newest Hulk, as well as the extent of Bruce Banner’s newfound villainy.

Spider-Gwen Smash #3 by Marvel Comics is available now.
