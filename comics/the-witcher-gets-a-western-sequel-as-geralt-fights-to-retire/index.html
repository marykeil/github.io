The Witcher Gets a Western Sequel as Geralt Fights to Retire

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

A new The Witcher comic series is expanding on Geralt’s story and his struggle to retire while facing new challenges. Dark Horse Comics is partnering with CD Projekt Red for a new five-issue miniseries. The Witcher: Corvo Bianco will blend western and fantasy genres, providing a unique twist within The Witcher franchise.

Literature Review

Dark Horse Comics delivers a new adventure for Geralt in the upcoming miniseries, The Witcher: Corvo Bianco, set to release in May 2024.

Spoilers for The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt ahead

A new comic series for The Witcher is coming soon from Dark Horse Comics, expanding on Geralt’s story. The Witcher: Corvo Bianco will tackle the protagonist’s struggle to retire as new challenges keep being thrown his way. It will also feature Yennefer, Geralt’s love, in the newest comic adventure in The Witcher franchise.

Dark Horse Comics is partnering with CD Projekt Red to bring fans another miniseries inspired by the hit franchise, The Witcher. This upcoming series will span five issues and feature the creative team of Bartosz Szytbor, Corrado Mastantuono, Matteo Vattani, and Hassan Otsmane-Elhaou. The debut issue will also feature several variant covers.

Variant cover artists include Tonci Zonjic, Neyef, and Jorge Molina. Szytbor is known for his previous work on The Witcher franchise in comics, including Fading Memories, Wild Animals, The Ballad of Two Wolves, and Witch’s Lament. This is another addition to his partnership with CD Projekt Red, having written comics involving Cyberpunk 2099.

              Geralt Gets Caught Up in a Deadly Conflict in The Witcher: Wild Animals   Dark Horse Comics in collaboration with CD Projekt Red is releasing a miniseries, The Witcher: Wild Animals, which throws Geralt in a deadly conflict.

Dark Horse Comics Provides a Direct Sequel to The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

This new Dark Horse Comics miniseries is slated for release in May. Based on a few details, it will serve as a sequel to The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, as its title references a reward granted at the end of the Blood and Wine expansion. The game was released in 2015, seeming to mark Geralt’s retirement. However, based on the details surrounding the newest comic, that retirement isn’t lasting the way fans originally may have believed it might. Corvo Bianco is the name of a vineyard Geralt is given, where he chose to reside with Yennefer. The synopsis for The Witcher: Corvo Bianco is as follows:

For a witcher, the simple life can be hard to come by, and even harder to pass up. When Geralt acquires a taste for a slower pace - good wine, and good company - the routines of a witcher are easily eclipsed. With Yennefer at his side, one might hope that Geralt will truly get to enjoy a taste of the good life. But the stains of history are deep, and with blood and wine, every drop attracts those who want more.

This series will mark the U.S. comic debut of Corrado Mastantuono, an Italian comic legend known for his work on Disney Italia and Nick Raider. The Witcher is a renowned global franchise, with the book’s original author - and the video game adaptation’s developer - being from Poland. This series will also mark an intriguing departure from the usual storytelling traditions used in The Witcher franchise thus far. The next comic adventure for Geralt from Dark Horse Comics is described as a Western, which likely was not the most expected direction to take in his continuing journey.

The Witcher Meets Western in The Witcher: Corvo Bianco

The Witcher is best known as a fantasy franchise full of monsters that Geralt of Rivia slays, due to his titular profession. The series’ hero has faced war conflicts, magic users, and crime bosses within his world. However, he has never had a truly western-style adventure, which Bartosz Sztybor seems ready to change in the near future with the latest comic installment within the franchise.

Detailing his excitement on the genre blend, writer Bartosz Sztybor says:

For all who know Geralt, Corvo Bianco is his endgame. The end of his journey, one and only place where he can retire - taking a break from killing monsters and trying to figure out how to make his own wine. As a fan of pop-culture and postmodern genre mixing, that setup above sounded like a perfect premise for a western. A western in The Witcher world - that’s something that was never done before! So I quickly got excited and thought about getting on board someone who really knows what western is. Corrado Mastantuono is a comic book legend, one of the biggest names of all time, a person that created the most important titles in European comics, including a real western classic - Tex. With him mixing fantasy and western that was never easier and more beautiful. Geralt roaming the Continent with a wild bunch of mages, Elves and Dwarves is my homage to Andrzej Sapkowski, Clint Eastwood and Sergio Leone. Just imagine, Yennefer having a duel with another mage and Geralt attacking a stagecoach. Corvo Bianco was his dream, but he needs to fight for it!

The Witcher: Corvo Bianco #1 will be available on May 8 from Dark Horse Comics.
