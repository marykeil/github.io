10 Best Captain America Love Interests in Marvel History

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Captain America’s love life has been eventful, with surprising romantic pairings and meaningful relationships with superpowered peers and civilians. In an alternate reality, Captain America had a child with Rogue and had a family with Carol Danvers in the body of Rogue. Sharon Carter is recognized as Captain America’s true love in the comics and their relationship has endured, with Sharon always supporting him.

Literature Review

Captain America has had a number of surprising relationships throughout his history, from fellow Avengers to X-Men to civilian paramours.

The long life of Captain America has included wars, heroics, and a fair share of love interests. While Captain America may not be the most mushy-gushy guy, his love life has been full. Honestly, the guy has been alive so long, he’s bound to have an eventful relationship history. While some romantic partners are ones that most fans are familiar with, such as Sharon and Peggy Carter, there are also a select few that one could not see coming. For instance, the relationship between Steve Rogers and an X-Men member is one that stands out as surprising, even if it is in an alternate reality. Sharon Carter may be recognized as his true love in the comics, but he has still had meaningful relationships with many of his superpowered peers as well as some civilians.

10 Rogue & Cap Strike Up a Romance on Battleworld Dated Cap in What If…? #114 (1998)

While longtime Marvel fans are well aware of the events in Secret Wars and what occurred on Battleworld, there is an alternate world where things played out very differently from the Earth-616 reality. In one What If…? universe that focuses on Secret Wars 25 years after the original miniseries, the Marvel characters never escaped Battleworld and instead took up with one another. A perhaps unexpected pairing arose between Captain America and Rogue, who would go on to have a child together, Sarah Rogers. However, it should be noted that Rogue had the psyche of Carol Danvers, so it could be argued that technically he was in a relationship and had a family with Carol Danvers, just in the body of Rogue.

9 A Romance With Iron Woman Prevents a War Married Steve in Dark Reign: Fantastic Four #2 (2009)

In the Earth-616 reality, Tony Stark is the son of Howard and Maria Stark who goes on to be an Avenger. However, in the Earth-3940 reality, Howard and Maria instead have a daughter, named Natasha Stark. The Iron Woman marries Captain America, and surprisingly, the relationship actually prevents a war. Without the Iron Man-Captain America conflict that causes the Civil War, there is no dissension between Marvel’s superheroes. This vastly different outcome really brings meaning to the old ’60s saying, “Make love, not war.”

8 Blind Al Dated Cap Before She Met Deadpool Romance with Cap Revealed in Deadpool #25 (1999)

While many may know Blind Al as the spunky neighbor of Ryan Reynold’s Deadpool, something a lot may not know is that she once had a relationship with an Avenger, Captain America himself to be specfic. During World War II, Blind Al aka Althea was in a relationship with the Captain. While she saw his bravery and strength, witnessing him being able to withstand grenades, she also saw his vulnerable side. “Vulnerable” would not be a word used to describe Captain America often, so Althea got a different glimpse of the soldier who was still struggling with what it meant to be a hero and if he was truly up to the challenge.

7 Connie Ferrari Couldn’t Handle Steve’s Secret Identity Debuted in Captain America #15 (1999)

A defense lawyer, Connie Ferrari was in a long-term relationship with Steve Rogers. While they were together for a long time, that does not mean that their union was without its issues. Connie being a lawyer for criminals like AIM and her brother David put a strain on their relationship, as Steve disliked her defense of those on the wrong side of the law. Also, Steve was still hung up on Sharon Carter, which would put a damper on any relationship. They broke up when Connie found out that Steve was actually Captain America and could not stomach the revelation.

6 Bernice “Bernie” Rosenthal Was Briefly Engaged to Steve Debuted in Captain America #247 (1980)

Steve Roger’s neighbor and an uber-talented glassblower, Bernie Rosenthal found her way into the Captain’s heart. However, it did not occur without some hard work. Since Steve was still reeling from the death of Sharon Carter (supposed death, at least), he didn’t warm up to Bernie immediately. Eventually, they became closer and romantically involved. They actually got engaged at one point. It was not to last though, due to rising New York City costs pushing Bernie to study law in Wisconsin. The distance was too much and the pair went their separate ways but ultimately remained friends.

5 Golden Girl, Betsy Ross was Steve’s First Love Interest Debuted in Captain America Comics #1 (1941)

Starting out as a World War II government agent, Betsy Ross had a number of adventures before her days as the Golden Girl. An FBI Agent, she was a part of the Super Soldier program that saw Steve Rogers become Captain America. While many of Betsy’s early FBI missions required her to be rescued by Captain America and Bucky, she eventually became the partner of Jeff Mace’s Captain America when his right-hand man was injured. They would go from crime-fighting partners to romantic partners, and eventually they got married. Jeff and Betsy retired to normal jobs as a newspaper reporter and a teacher, respectively.

4 Wasp Romanced Steve in the Ultimate Universe Debuted in Ultimates #2 (2002)

In the Ultimate Universe, Janet Van Dyne helped a recently-revived Captain America acclimate to the modern world when the super strike force known as the Ultimates first assembled. Janet was married to Hank Pym in this universe, but things went sour when he got violent towards his wife and ran away. Consequently, Captain America tracked him down and beat him up. Some time later, after the Ultimates had fought off an alien invasion, Captain America and the Wasp started to date. It ended soon after, however, for a myriad of reasons, including a big generation gap and the Wasp still holding feelings for Hank Pym.

3 Diamondback Debuted in Captain America #310 (1985)

Originating as a mercenary antagonist, Diamondback is a surprising love interest for Captain America, who prizes justice and virtue. Meeting Captain America when she was sent to kill M.O.D.O.K, the two engaged in a tense confrontation. Interestingly enough, she left the fight smitten with him. Later on, Diamondback would require Captain America’s help and would also provide help for him in combat, bonding the two previous foes. They would go on to date, albeit for a short time, and Steve would take her on as a partner against evil. They separated when she killed Snapdragon and felt that Steve would never accept her as a result.

2 Peggy Carter Debuted in Tales of Suspense #77 (1966)

Much like in the 2011 film Captain America: The First Avenger, Peggy Carter met Steve Rogers when she was given the job of overseeing him after he received the Super Soldier serum, falling for him in the process. They were together a short while, until duty called and they each received separate missions. The nail in the coffin that was their relationship occurred when Peggy was captured by enemy forces. While being held captive, an artillery shell hit her and her captor, killing him and rendering her an amnesiac. Captain America searched for her to no avail until he disappeared himself.

1 Sharon Carter Debuted in Tales of Suspense #75 (1965)

The relative of a fellow love interest of the Captain’s, Sharon Carter is the niece to Peggy Carter. In addition to sharing a common love interest, both have also served with S.H.I.E.L.D. It was through her missions with the organization that Sharon met Steve Rogers when he rescued her. Shortly after, they fell in love. However, they had their problems, as Captain America thought that Sharon’s missions were too dangerous and wished for her to step down. Regardless, Sharon was always in Steve’s corner. When Cap was framed for killing Thunderbolt Ross, Sharon re-formed the Daughters of Liberty and worked tirelessly to clear his name. Their relationship has endured through the years, making her the strongest of all of Captain America’s love interests.
