10 Pieces of Darwyn Cooke Art That Prove He Was Born to Draw the Justice League

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Darwyn Cooke’s art captured the spirit and bond of the Teen Titans in a variant cover for Teen Titans #5, showcasing their energy and teamwork. Cooke’s playful sense of humor shines through in his cover for Ambush Bug: Year None #7, where the hero leaves Batman slipping on a banana peel. Cooke’s artwork in DC: The New Frontier #1 beautifully depicts the dynamic battle between Batman and Superman, showing off the amazing energy and intensity of their timeless conflict.

Literature Review

Comic legend Darwyn Cooke had some amazing illustrations of the Justice League, but only a few show how awe-inspiring the team truly is.

Comic creator Darwyn Cooke’s work with the Justice League and the DC Universe is considered legendary by many. The artist created hundreds of amazing and iconic pieces of art, many which highlighted the members of DC Comics’ premier hero team. It’s been nearly ten years since Darwyn Cooke passed away and the works he left behind continue to inspire comic book fans everywhere. Cooke was lauded for his unique style and the way he drew characters like Superman, Wonder Woman, and the Flash. While it’s hard to pick just a few of Cooke’s amazing works, the following list takes a look at some of Cooke’s finest pieces that show just how well his art captured the Justice League.

10 The Justice League’s Future Members Rock Out on One Cover Teen Titans #5 (2014)

It would be some time until the Fab Five founding members of Teen Titans grew up to join the ranks of the Justice League. But Cooke showed off their spirit and connection to one another with his variant for Teen Titans #5. Robin, Wonder Girl, Speedy, Aqualad and Kid Flash all play their hearts out to the cheers of fans. This image is filled with energy and captures the bond these heroes shared with one another. Maybe they didn’t actually have a rock band, but this image amazingly portrays the kind of teamwork the Teen Titans are known for.

9 Cooke’s Sense of Humor Came Though with Ambush Bug Ambush Bug: Year None #7 (2009)

Ambush Bug isn’t the kind of hero one would expect to be hanging out with the Justice League in any sort of capacity. But DC’s Deadpool is shown on Cooke’s cover for Ambush Bug: Year None #7. The green-suited hero is munching away on a banana as he walks away from Batman, who has just taken a spill on the peel Bug left behind. Cooke’s work is playful here, whether it’s the League having a laugh at Batman’s expense, Bug nonchalantly walking away, or the Dark Knight staring daggers at the person who just made him look like a fool.

8 Superman and Batman Fighting is a Timeless Battle DC: The New Frontier #1 (2004)

Batman and Superman may be allies, but the image of them fighting is a tableau that many artists have taken a crack at over the years. Cooke tried his hand at the Man of Steel fighting the Dark Knight on the back cover of DC: The New Frontier #1. Superman stands over Batman, who he’s punched into the ground. But the Caped Crusader has a few bombs at the ready to fight back. This piece has an amazing energy to it and brilliantly shows off just how dynamic a fight between Batman and Superman would really be.

7 Batman’s Villains Come to Life in One Artwork Batman #37 (2014)

They say a hero is only as good as their villains. But few heroes have as amazing a rogues’ gallery as Batman and Cooke proves it with his variant for Batman #37. This image pulls from several generations of Batman lore and shows the Dark Knight facing off a panoply of his worst and most memorable antagonists ever. The use of reds in this image adds a special layer of intimidation, as does the size manipulation. Cooke’s variant shows just how much of an uphill battle Batman faces trying to keep Gotham safe from the villains in his city.

6 The Green Lantern Corps Showed Cooke’s Amazing Designs Green Lantern Corps #37 (2014)

A good artist always has a bit of variety in their style. While the Justice League offers up several unique characters, they pale in comparison to the Green Lantern Corps, which Cooke marvelously illustrates in his variant for Green Lantern Corps #37. Not only does the piece put some of the best Lanterns front and center, but Cooke shows off a wonderful diversity with members of all shapes and sizes from the bird-like Tomar-Tu to the polygonal Chaselon. Not to mention the greens in this artwork are stellar and show just how impressive a force the Green Lanterns truly are.

5 The Flash Variant Redid a Classic Cover Perfectly The Flash #7 (2010)

It’s not unusual for artists to honor older covers by remaking them and putting their own spin on them. For The Flash #7, Dawryn Cooke modeled his art after the cover for The Flash #139, the story that introduced Barry Allen’s nemesis, the Reverse-Flash. Not only does Cooke perfectly remake the cover, but his particular style shines through. Between the softer colors and the perfect speed trails, Cooke gives the older artwork a cool update without losing anything that made the original The Flash #139 cover so iconic.

4 Superman’s Heroism Has Never Been Shown Better Than On One Variant Action Comics #37 (2014)

What other hero embodies heroism better than Superman who Darwyn Cooke beautifully portrays on the variant for Action Comics #37? This piece shows the Man of Steel after saving a young man from being hit by a train. Cooke excellently captures both the boy’s surprise and Superman’s satisfaction from another good day of work. It’s easy to show how great Superman is when fighting an alien conqueror or multiversal threat. But it takes true talent to show off just how confident and awesome Superman is when saving people in need.

3 Cooke’s Justice League Shines on Absolute New Frontier Absolute DC: The New Frontier (2006)

DC: The New Frontier captured the hearts of DC Comics fans everywhere and Darwyn Cooke’s art played no small part in that. The cover for the miniseries’ Absolute edition is a clean group shot of the old-school Justice League. While A-Listers like Wonder Woman and the Flash take center stage on the front, the wraparound cover also includes heroes like Hourman, Adam Strange, and the Challengers of the Unknown. There isn’t a Justice League hero that doesn’t look well drawn in Cooke’s charming and identifiable style.

2 The Trinity Looked Perfect in One Amazing Shot Justice League: The New Frontier Special #1 (2008)

The core of the Justice League has always, and will always be the Trinity: Batman, Superman, and Wonder Woman close out the short story “Fight to the Finish” from Justice League: The New Frontier Special #1. A skirmish between the World’s Finest is settled by Diana, and the three pose triumphantly together. Cooke recognizes that these three heroes are the pillars on which the DC Universe stands, and this page captures each DC hero’s indomitable spirit perfectly.

1 Cooke’s Modern Justice League Was Absolutely Perfect Justice League #37 (2014)

Cooke has made plenty of amazing pieces of the Justice League and its various heroes. But nothing shows how he was meant to draw the team more than his variant for Justice League #37. Much like The New Frontier, it showcases the biggest stars of the DC Universe, but with a more modern flair compared to his iconic story. Every hero on this cover looks positively amazing and one can’t look at this photo without feeling the might of this legendary team. If there’s an example of how well Cooke drew the Justice League, this is it. Darwyn Cooke is a comic book legend whose incredible talent is missed by fans to this day. But he left an amazing trove of work behind that shows that he was one of the best creators suited to bring the Justice League to life.
