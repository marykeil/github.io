Shang-Chi vs U.S. Agent Gives the MCU a Fight It MUST Adapt in Phase Five

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The Shang-Chi vs U.S.Agent fight in Thunderbolts #3 has the potential to be an incredible matchup for the MCU. Shang-Chi’s history of fighting iconic Marvel characters can be adapted into the MCU, making for exciting battles. This is the perfect time for the MCU to adapt the Shang-Chi vs U.S.Agent fight, given the characters’ rising prominence and recent affiliations in the MCU.

Literature Review

Shang-Chi and U.S.Agent are trading blows in the new preview for Thunderbolts #3, and it’s a fight so intense, the MCU needs to adapt it for Phase 5!

Warning: Contains SPOILERS for Thunderbolts #3! The MCU’s Phase Five is well underway, with Phase Four having introduced a slew of new characters that are prepped and primed to flourish - including Shang-Chi and U.S.Agent. While these two Marvel Comics characters being brought up in the same sentence may initially seem a bit out of place, their latest interaction changes that sentiment entirely, as the upcoming ‘Shang-Chi vs U.S.Agent’ fight gives the MCU a matchup it must adapt.

In a preview shared by Looper for Thunderbolts #3 by Jackson Lanzing, Collin Kelly, and Geraldo Borges, Shang-Chi (who is working with the Thunderbolts) approaches U.S.Agent in the hopes that John Walker will join them in taking down the Red Skull. Walker is currently on a mission for the U.S. government, but Shang-Chi tells him his mission is a lie, and only serves to help Red Skull. Needless to say, U.S.Agent doesn’t believe him.

After hearing his spiel, John Walker flatly says to Shang-Chi, “how ‘bout we stop [expletive] around… and get to the damn fight already?”. U.S.Agent brandishes his shield while Shang-Chi calmly raises his fists, and after the two square off in the empty bar for just a moment, Shang-Chi makes the first move. Shang-Chi delivers a devastating kick right at the center of Walker’s shield, sending him flying through the wall. The preview ends after that initial strike, but it’s safe to say that the fight is far from over.

U.S.Agent is Just the Latest of Shang-Chi’s Fights that NEED to be Adapted in the MCU Shang-Chi has battled the likes of Wolverine, Spider-Man, and the Avengers!

Shang-Chi is one of the greatest hand-to-hand fighters in the entire Marvel Universe, which means he’s spent a fair amount of time fighting iconic Marvel Comics characters throughout his extensive history. Shang-Chi battled Feral Wolverine in X-Men #62, Shang-Chi fought Spider-Man in Giant-Size Spider-Man #2 (and is about to again in Gang War), and he even took on all the Avengers at once in Shang-Chi #6.

Every one of those fights were amazing in unique ways, but the coolest thing about them is actually what they all have in common: they can all be adapted in the MCU. Shang-Chi fighting U.S.Agent is undeniably awesome, and the violent grit of it can be felt just from this preview alone, but that’s just the tip of the iceberg when considering potential MCU adaptations of Shang-Chi’s greatest ‘versus’ battles.

Now is the Perfect Time for the MCU to Adapt Shang-Chi vs U.S.Agent

While it’s true that Shang-Chi has a number of iconic Marvel characters he can fight in the MCU, there’s no better time to adapt his fight with U.S.Agent than right now. Fighting those other heroes would require buildup and explanation, but one with John Walker could happen as easily as it did in this comic. Given the up-and-coming nature of both characters in the MCU, and the fact that Shang-Chi recently became an Avenger while U.S.Agent has been recruited on the Thunderbolts, the timing is perfect for this fight to be brought to the big screen.

So long as the MCU sticks to the source material in terms of future Shang-Chi matchups, he will quickly become the most badass character in the MCU, matched only by those he fights - including U.S.Agent. But, even if the MCU doesn’t adapt this fight in the near future (despite the fact that it should), fans can still enjoy the epic battle between Shang-Chi and U.S.Agent when Thunderbolts #3 hits the shelves.

Thunderbolts #3 by Marvel Comics is available February 14, 2024.
