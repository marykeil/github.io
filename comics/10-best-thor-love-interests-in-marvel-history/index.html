10 Best Thor Love Interests in Marvel History

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Thor has had a diverse array of love interests, from mortal friends to Asgardian allies and even villainous enchantresses. His relationships often involve complicated circumstances and external influences, such as love spells and mistaken identities. Jane Foster, portrayed as the love of Thor’s life, is a strong and independent character who has had her own transformative journey.

Literature Review

While Jane Foster may be the most recognizable love interest of Thor due to the MCU, he has quite a few other meaningful relationships in the comics.

As an immortal Norse god, Thor has led an exciting, and sometimes tragic, life filled with interesting people; some he has loved, others not so much. Whether warring with his power-hungry brother Loki or being with his love Jane Foster, Thor has found himself in fascinating situations since his debut in 1962. Living in both Earthly and mythological realms, his love interests vary greatly from one to another. There are the mortals who help bring Thor a little closer to becoming down-to-Earth, not an easy feat for him. On the other hand, there are his Asgardian peers who are both sources of comfort and friendship, like Lady Sif, or treacherous spell casters who try to trick him into falling in love (ahem, Enchantress and Lorelei). Regardless, Thor has loved and lost, making a wildly unrelatable character understandable when it comes to affairs of the heart.

10 Roz Solomon First Apperance: Thor: God of Thunder #12 by Jason Aaron and Nic Klein

An environmental S.H.I.E.L.D agent, Rosalind “Roz” Solomon has been a fan of Thor from the beginning, even inviting him to her S.H.I.E.L.D Academy graduation via online video. Tony Stark makes sure that Thor sees the invitation, and the God of Thunder actually attends. Thor goes on to help Roz with her agent work multiple times. One example is when the pair fight off the evil Roxxon. He eventually asks Roz out on a date and even waits on Earth for her. Their time as a couple is not long-lasting, however, with Thor still not over the death of Jane Foster.

9 Shawna Lynde First Appearance: Thor #314 by Doug Moench and Keith Pollard

While not star-crossed lovers by any measure, Thor and Shawna Lynde’s relationship is important to the development of the character. When Thor still has the human alter ego of Donald Blake, he meets fellow medical student and, later, physician Shawna. They form a friendship and then, a relationship. However, juggling his two personas becomes complicated for Thor and his two worlds start to blend when Shawna thinks that Donald Blake is dating his cousin, who in reality is Lady Sif. Not able to tolerate the double life any longer, Donald Blake, as Shawna knows him, disappears and Thor has her memory erased.

8 Enchantress First Appearance: Journey Into Mystery #103 by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby

The sister of fellow Thor love interest, Lorelei, Enchantress is a villainous Asgardian who usually aligns herself with Loki rather than the more heroic brother. However, that does not mean that she has no interest in Thor. She has cast successful love spells on Thor multiple times. However, most of the time, the love spells do not last for long and Thor realizes he does not truly love her. In the 2003 storyline, “The Reigning” by Dan Jurgens and Scot Eaton, Thor and Enchantress are together without any enchantment over him and even have a child together. In spite of that, he does not like what his life has turned into, going back in time to prevent it from ever happening.

7 Bloodaxe First Appearance: Thor #392 by Tom DeFalco and Ron Frenz

During a weird period for Thor readers, the Asgardian is sent into the body of the human Eric Masterson by his father, Odin. An already bizarre concept gets more bizarre when Jackie Lukus is introduced. Jackie is the normal, human girlfriend of Eric Masterson, but when she comes across Scurge the Executioner’s axe, she becomes Bloodaxe. A vigilante, Bloodaxe is gifted her powers from the evil inherent in the axe, which is transferred to whoever touches it. The superhero realm offers no benefits to Jackie, first having a Norse mythology character enter into her boyfriend’s consciousness, and then she’s turned into a burly, ne’er do well. Let this be a lesson to all; never pick up an axe that isn’t yours!

6 Lorelei First Appearance: Thor #337 by Walter Simonson

A relationship created under false pretenses, the pairing of Lorelei and Thor is engineered by the mischievous Loki. Lorelei is the sister of Enchantress, and she has the hots for Thor. Loki persuades her to use her magical talents to cast a love spell on Thor. Hoping to get at Asgard’s throne, Loki uses Thor’s (fake) love for Lorelei to distract him. However, his attempts are for naught as Heimdall comes to the rescue. Consequently, Thor comes to realize that he has been under a spell and does not truly love her. In the ultimate twist of fate, Lorelei herself ends up falling for Loki.

5 She-Hulk First Appearance: Savage She-Hulk #1 by Stan Lee and John Buscema

Thanks to the Disney+ series, She-Hulk has become more familiar with audiences, but something not covered by the show is Jennifer Walters’ relationship with Thor Odinson. Working with the Asgardian to fight against the Dark Celestials, She-Hulk gets to know the Norse god better and forms a bond with him. At first, she thinks that Thor is only interested in hanging out with her as buddies in her Hulk form due to a misunderstanding on their first date, when Thor brings her to the Savage Land. However, Thor clarifies that he is actually interested in Jennifer Walters and just didn’t know how to go about a first date. Unfortunately, as much as Thor and She-Hulk have in common, they do not last due to having their hands full with superhero duties: being King of Asgard for him and fighting the Winter Guard for her.

4 Valkyrie (Ultimate Universe) First Appearance: Ultimates 2 #6 by Mark Millar and Bryan Hitch

Played by Tessa Thompson in the MCU, Valkyrie’s comics counterpart has a different trajectory, especially in regard to her love life. In the Earth-1610 timeline, she and Thor are an item. In this universe, Valkyrie does not have any powers of her own until she gets into a relationship with Thor. Meanwhile, the Thor of this timeline is much more aggressive and reckless than his responsible Earth-616 self. Nevertheless, she helps Thor to quell his less admirable qualities, making him a more amiable Norse god. However, Valkyrie dies during the Ultimatum event. Rather than take that lying down, Thor loves Valkyrie so much that he attempts to reclaim her soul in Valhalla.

3 Brunnhilde First Appearance: Defenders #4 by Steve Englehart and Sal Buscema

A seeming match made in Heaven (or Asgard), Brunnhilde comes across as a dream girl of Thor’s. She is a Valkyrie and a warrior while being of one mind with Thor, personality-wise. An interesting storyline sees Odin cursing Thor and Brunnhilde to be mortals on Earth, where they fall in love. They have no memory of where they came from, thinking that they have always been mortals. However, tragedy strikes and Thor, as a human, dies. Second guessing his decision, Odin turns Brunnhilde and his son back to their Asgardian forms, but takes away the memory of their time on Earth in the process. You can’t miss what you don’t know at the end of the day.

2 Sif First Appearance: Journey Into Mystery #102 by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby

While Sif is more known as being Thor’s close friend and comrade in battle, she was actually his first love. Sif has always been a support for Thor and shows him unconditional love while still calling him out when he deserves it. The pair were even betrothed at one point. Regardless, Thor tends to overlook his longtime pal and sometimes romantic partner for other people, especially Jane Foster. With how much these two have in common and the torch that Sif still carries for Thor, they could get back together if the timing was right. In the meantime, Sif is busy controlling the Bifrost, a role she took over from her brother Heimdall.

1 Jane Foster First Appearance: Journey Into Mystery #84 by Stan Lee, Larry Lieber and Jack Kirby

The love interest that is inescapable for Thor thanks to Marvel audiences, Jane Foster is arguably the love of the Asgardian’s life. A caring, down-to-Earth mortal, Jane has had a fascinating character trajectory that rivals that of her partner. She first meets Thor when he is living on Earth under the Donald Blake name. She begins as his nurse, but eventually becomes a doctor. Later, Jane takes up Thor’s mantle before eventually becoming Valkyrie. Consequently, Jane is a love interest who can stand on her own, being an interesting and autonomous character. Through it all, Jane has remained a pillar of warmth and strength for Thor, being like-minded with him on certain things while contrasting him in spots that help make him more level-headed and sentimental.
