Wolverine vs Weapon VIII Proves Logan s Successor Had Objectively Better Claws

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 800 words 4 minutes 
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Wolverine faces off against the spider-themed Weapon VIII, getting brutally injured by their sharp limbs and flamethrower. Weapon VIII is the official debut of the mainstream Weapon Plus’ “greatest previous success” before Wolverine, equipped with deadly weapons and an Iron Spider costume. The story hints at future plans for Weapon VIII, as Marvel often connects beloved heroes and villains to the Weapon Plus program.

Literature Review

As Wolverine takes on Weapon VIII, the Weapon X program’s biggest success lashes out with deadly claws, flamethrowers, and more.

As Wolverine finally faces off against his predecessor Weapon VIII, Logan is brutalized by a vicious take on his iconic claws. In Marvel lore, the Weapon Plus group have been creating living weapons for almost a century, with Wolverine actually being part of the Weapon 10 project (the ‘X’ in ‘Weapon X’ is a Roman numeral.) Various stories have revealed the group’s connections to major heroes and villains, however Weapon VIII has never been seen before… until now.

Additional weapons shown include palm blades, wrist-mounted flamethrowers, and some kind of miniaturized drone tech.

In a preview of Edge of Spider-Verse #1, Wolverine goes up against the spider-themed Weapon VIII. While Logan has his claws and healing factor, the preview art shows him losing hard, as Weapon VIII pins him with ultra-sharp prehensile limbs, then unleashes a flamethrower.

Also including the return of the digital hero Spider-Byte (last seen on the big screen, in Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse), Edge of Spider-Verse #1 is coming from Jackson Lanzing, Collin Kelly, Nilah Magruder, Travel Foreman, and Eric Gapstur.

: The Complete Guide to Marvel’s Weapon Plus Program

Wolverine Battles Weapon X’s Spider-Hero Weapon VIII Unleashes Claws & Flamethrowers

While Edge of Spider-Verse titles are multiversal anthologies, with each story taking place in a different alternate reality, fans have never actually seen the mainstream Weapon VIII, making this spider-villain the official debut of Weapon Plus’ “greatest previous success” before Wolverine. Wearing a deadly take on Spider-Man’s Iron Spider costume, Weapon VIII has four blade-like legs which cut through Wolverine’s body with ease. Additional weapons shown include palm blades, wrist-mounted flamethrowers, and some kind of miniaturized spider-tech drones. It’s easy to see why Weapon Plus considered Weapon VIII a major success story.

While Peter Parker’s Iron Spider had prehensile limbs, it’s clear that Weapon VIII has weaponized this feature in a new way, essentially creating a far more versatile version of Wolverine’s claws. These limbs can be used to hold an enemy in place, allowing Weapon VIII to get in close and unleash the rest of its arsenal. The biggest difference is likely that Wolverine’s bones were able to be coated in adamantium, making his claws unbreakable. Despite this, it’s one of Logan’s biggest vulnerabilities that he needs to get in close and soak up damage to be effective - something that’s only possible because his mutant healing factor picks up the slack.

Weapon Plus Debuts Yet Another Epic Antihero Weapon VIII Is the Latest Antihero to Share an Origin with Wolverine

Coming from Kelly, Lanzing and Foreman, this story is being teased as “the first appearance of Weapon VIII,” suggesting Marvel has future plans for the killer. That makes sense, as some of Marvel’s most beloved heroes and villains connect back to Weapon Plus. Currently, the canonical results of each Weapon Plus project are as follows:

Now, Weapon VIII joins that list, revealing a deadly Weapon Plus experiment which likely ties back to the Spider-Verse’s mystic animal totems. Given spider-heroes’ unique tendency to travel across the multiverse, fans shouldn’t be surprised to see Weapon VIII appear in Marvel’s mainstream reality soon - assuming of course that the assassin survives their inaugural battle against Wolverine.

Edge of Spider-Verse #1 is coming from Marvel Comics February 21.
