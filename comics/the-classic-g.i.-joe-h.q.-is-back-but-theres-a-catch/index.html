The Classic G.I. Joe H.Q. Is Back, But Theres A Catch

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 800 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Duke #2 introduces the Pit, an old base that serves as a safe house for high-risk prisoners, the Energon Universe incarnation of the Joes’ classic base. The classic G.I. Joe team may eventually form and move into the Pit in the Energon continuity, but for now, readers will have to wait to find out what happens, as Duke and Baroness team up to break out of the decommissioned facility Duke #3. Two more Energon G.I. Joe miniseries have been announced to follow Duke and Cobra Commander, potentially leading to an all-new ongoing G.I. Joe title once the team officially comes together.

Literature Review

The Pit makes its Energon Universe debut, but the longtime G.I. Joe headquarters is being used for an entirely different reason in the new continuity.

WARNING! Spoilers ahead for Duke #2Now that G.I. Joe has a new home at Skybound Entertainment, America’s elite fighting force will need a home-base to operate out of. Thankfully, the recently released Duke #2 may have just provided a headquarters in the Energon Universe for the new Joe team… if Duke and his current allies can escape from it first, that is.

Duke #2 – by the creative team of Joshua Williamson, Tom Reilly, Jordie Bellaire and Rus Wooten – features the title character on the run from the U.S. military after trying to uncover the secret of the Transformers.

Duke runs afoul of his Stalker and Rock’ N Roll, two of his teammates from the classic G.I. Joe continuity, and winds up being captured after a breathtaking ambush. After his capture, Duke is taken to an undisclosed location: the Pit, an old base that’s fallen on hard times.

The Pit: G.I. Joe’s Hi-Tech Headquarters

“I thought the Pit was decommissioned in the eighties?” Duke asks his captors, in a clever nod to the G.I. Joe: A Real American Hero series’ debut in 1982. Stalker texplains that the Pit now serves as “a safe house for high risk prisoners before they can be transported.” First appearing all the way back in 1982’s G.I. Joe: A Real American Hero #1, the Pit served as the official headquarters for the elite G.I. Joe team. Originally an underground base located beneath the Chaplain’s motor pool in Fort Wadsworth, no one knew about the top-secret facility, except the Joes.

The base was attacked by Cobra and relocated several times throughout the original run of the series, before ultimately settling down at a permanent location in Utah. While the Pit was decommissioned in 1995 when the original Marvel run came to an end with issue #155, the team returned to their old HQ when that series restarted in 2012. Now that the Pit has appeared in the Energon Universe, fans can assume this is all set up for when the G.I. Joe team officially forms in the new continuity.

Will Duke End With The Formation of G.I. Joe?

Neither Cobra nor the G.I. Joe organizations yet exist in Skybound’s brand-new Energon continuity, so watching both factions come together in the pages of Duke and Cobra Commander has been incredibly exciting for long-time fans. Most exciting of all is the ending scene of Duke #2, which promises an epic team-up between unlikely allies Duke and Baroness as the two attempt to break out of the decommissioned facility. With so many classic characters and concepts showing up in the new comics, fans are clamoring to know if the five-issue Duke miniseries will end with the official formation of G.I. Joe.

Two further miniseries have been announced to follow Duke and Cobra Commander, although specific details for either series have yet to be released, leaving little room for speculation as of now. It’s possible one of the subsequent series could be a team-up book, but more than likely, all the miniseries are leading to an all-new ongoing G.I. Joe title once the team officially comes together. Whanever the legendary organization finally does form, it’s good to know that G.I. Joe will have a familiar home-base in the Energon Universe.

Duke #2 is on sale now from Skybound Entertainment
