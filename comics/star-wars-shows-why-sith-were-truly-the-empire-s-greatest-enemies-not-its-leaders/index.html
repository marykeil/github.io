Star Wars Shows Why Sith Were Truly The Empire s Greatest Enemies, Not Its Leaders

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The plot of Darth Vader #43 reveals the extent of an anti-Palpatine conspiracy that aims to overthrow Emperor Palpatine and eliminate corruption from the Empire. The Schism Imperial, a group that believes in the Empire’s greater potential, recruits disgraced Imperials to join their cause in order to bring about true order. The Sith, who created the Imperial system, only use it to perpetuate their own power and might, while the Schism Imperial sees the Empire as something more than that.

Literature Review

Fresh off the ambitious “Dark Droids” crossover, Marvel’s Darth Vader series is uncovering the latest plot against Palpatine, the Schism Imperial.

Star Wars only just finished up its incredible Dark Droids crossover event, and it is now diving into the political intrigue at the heart of the Galactic Empire – as yet another plot to overthrow Emperor Palpatine makes its case that the Sith are the true threat to the Empire’s longevity and stability, rather than the guiding hands that will keep it stable…by shooting force lightning if necessary.

Darth Vader #43 – written by Grek Pak, with art by Adam Gorham, color by Federico Blee, and lettering by Joe Caramagna – recounts the story of an Imperial show trial, which ends with the defendants recruited to join the Schism Imperial, an anti-Palpatine conspiracy.

Used and abused, and then saddled with charges of treason and marked for disposal, the prisoners narrowly avoid execution, in exchange for being drafted into the plot. Each is depicted as a steadfast Imperial, and as a result, they willfully take Sly Moore’s offer to join the cover effort to overthrow Palpatine, for the good of the Empire.

The Emperor’s True Nature Makes Him An Enemy Of The Empire

As [Sly] Moore explains, the much touted order promised by Palpatine as the reason for the creation of the Empire is not for its subjects, or even its loyal servants – true order and prosperity are the domains of a select elite.

In Vader #43, three disgraced Imperials – Admiral Corleque, Agent Fabarian, and Governor Tauntaza – are brought before the Imperial Court, to be judged by sub-administrator Sly Moore. Each of them is more than willing to account for their actions, and protest their loyalty to the Empire – but there is no room for that in the Empire’s totalitarian system, where the system – and the Emperor’s – will is law, and opposition legally is not allowed to exist. A fourth defendant, an Imperial Council Member, guilty of embezzlement, professes unwavering confidence in Palpatine’s will – and is promptly electrocuted for it.

“You know there is no appeal for you,” Sly Moore tells the prisoners. “No reform. No justice. No order. That’s reserved for the few.” As Moore explains, the much touted order promised by Palpatine as the reason for the creation of the Empire is not for its subjects, or even its loyal servants – true order and prosperity are the domains of a select elite. Palpatine himself only ever considered the Empire as an extension of his own power, his own will, his own being. The Schism Imperial believe it to be so much more.

The “Schism Imperial” Are More Committed To The Empire Than Palpatine

As Darth Vader #43 makes evident, the Sith may have instituted the Imperial system, but it was always an edifice to their own might, and not a system that was meant to function beyond perpetuating that might, through any means necessary.

“You three know the Emperor’s true nature. Which means you see his Empire as it truly is. Not as it claims to be,” Moore tells her new recruits into the Schism Imperial, who are subsequently instructed to “accept penalties and demotions” and put in low-level positions, far from actual power, until the Schism is in need of them. Corleque is demoted to Captain; Fabarian is put in a meager, reduced role, where he’s shown being forced to suffer the indignity of having to delete a baron’s traffic ticket from the system.

The former governor, Tauntaza, is sent to a remote outpost – where the action of the issue shifts, as Sly Moore and Darth Vader come to collect her for service to the Schism Imperial. As Darth Vader #43 makes evident, the Sith may have instituted the Imperial system, but it was always an edifice to their own might, and not a system that was meant to function beyond perpetuating that might, through any means necessary. The Schism Imperial – and now Vader – consider the Empire’s potential so much greater, and will now seek to bring about true order.

Star Wars: Darth Vader #43 is available now from Marvel Comics.
