Marvel May Finally Make the MCU s Daredevil & She-Hulk Romance Canon

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Marvel fans might see a romance between She-Hulk and Daredevil in the comics, just like in the MCU. Daredevil #5 is dedicated to She-Hulk and Daredevil going on eating dates, but She-Hulk is possessed and turns the romance into a rivalry. The comic explores the chemistry between Daredevil and She-Hulk, possibly setting the stage for a future romance if well-received by readers.

Literature Review

Daredevil and She-Hulk’s MCU romance quickly garnered a cult following, but the two were never actually an item in the comics. But that could change.

Warning: Spoilers for Daredevil #5!Marvel Comics might finally be ready to catch up on bringing the MCU’s steamy love affair between She-Hulk and Daredevil to the page. In the span of just one episode of She-Hulk: Attorney at Law, audiences were quick to fall in love with the lawyer lovers. This is especially interesting because, before the MCU brought them together, Jen and Matt were never a romance in the comics, save for a brief flash in the House of M universe.

It seems as though Marvel is starting to lay the groundwork towards making that romance a reality in its comic universe, beginning in Daredevil #5 by Saladin Ahmed, Farid Karami, Jesus Aburtov, and VC’s Clayton Cowles. The issue is dedicated almost entirely to She-Hulk and Daredevil having a series of eating dates (platonic of course, since She-Hulk is dating Jack of Hearts in her comic).

While the twist in this comic turns the romance into a rivalry where She-Hulk is possessed, evidence suggests that this could be foreshadowing for the comics to pull the trigger on the real thing one day.

Daredevil and She-Hulk Reunite (Or Do They?)

In his recent history, Matt Murdock sacrificed his life while serving as the Hand of God, but he’s been resurrected in a second coming as Father Matthew, youth home director. With his new life comes a rewriting of Marvel history, creating a world where not even former close friends like Ben Ulrich remember Matt’s old life (hence why they’re feuding now) because that life never came to pass. This is why Matt is so surprised to get a visit from She-Hulk, who is eager to take him to a series of restaurants.

Matt is mostly interested in talking to She-Hulk about how someone mysterious has a vendetta against the foster home he resides over, St. Nicholas. Ms. Walters, on the other hand, only has interests in stuffing Matt’s face with food. Everything becomes clear when She-Hulk goes into a rage over their dessert being interrupted, revealing that She-Hulk isn’t herself at all - she’s been possessed by Gluttony of the Seven Deadly Sins demons gunning for Matt. As with the previous demons, Daredevil exorcises it away, saving the real She-Hulk’s life.

Could She-Hulk and Daredevil Still Become an Item?

Throughout the comic, a spark between Daredevil and She-Hulk is evident. It isn’t clear if their chemistry comes from a past romance fans don’t know about, or if it’s alluding to a past romance for fans of the MCU. But maybe it doesn’t matter for now, since She-Hulk is possessed the whole time. Plus, she’s in a relationship right now (albeit one that’s on the rocks), so a Daredevil romance isn’t likely for the near future. Still, that doesn’t mean it isn’t meant to be.

Despite everything destined to lead to the twist that Jennifer Walters is possessed, the comic spends considerable time on the pairing out of superhero duties, focusing on their chemistry. It is almost as if the comic is serving as a test pilot of sorts to show readers what such a romance could look like. Perhaps if the comic pairing is received as positively as She-Hulk and Daredevil were in their MCU hookup, then maybe Marvel will finally pull the trigger on an arguably overdue romance.
