10 Most Surprising Batcaves in Batman History

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Batman’s first Batcave was a simple garage for vehicles, not the high-tech hideout it is now. Batman has Batcaves all over the world to support him and his team during his global adventures. Batman has a secret second Batcave hidden within his main Batcave, where he keeps dangerous secrets and has even held the Joker.

Literature Review

One of the most iconic locations in the DC Universe is the Batcave, and Batman has had so many different versions of it over the years.

There are tons of iconic locations in the DC Universe, from Superman’s Fortress of Solitude to the Justice League Watchtower, but few places are as iconic or recognizable as Batman’s hideout, the Batcave. Batman has had many different versions of the Batcave over the years, and while it’s usually located underneath Wayne Manor, that’s not always the case. As Batman has traveled all over the planet and even through space, he’s used lots of different spots as Batcaves and these are the top 10 most surprising he’s ever used.

10 The First Batcave First Appearance: Batman #12 by Bill Finger, Bob Kane, Jerry Robinson, and George Roussos

The first iteration of the Batcave in comics appeared way back in Batman #12 in 1942. Batman’s first Batcave was essentially little more than a glorified garage. Instead of the high-tech crime lab and training facility it is now, the original Batcave really only existed for Batman to store his vehicles. It was less a “cave” and more of a “tunnel,” essentially just connecting Wayne Manor to a nearby barn, so Batman could drive to and from crime scenes without being directly connected to the manor.

9 Batcave West in the City of Angels First Appearance: Outsiders #19 by Mike W. Barr, Jim Aparo, and Adrienne Roy

While Batman primarily hangs out in Gotham City, he’s been known to travel all over the world when necessary. Because of this, Batman often needs to have different bases to operate out of when he’s away from Gotham City. This led Batman to construct an entirely new Batcave in the city of Los Angeles. Readers first got to see the Batcave West in 1987’s Outsiders #19. While traveling with the Outsiders and dealing with the villain team known as the Masters of Disaster, Batman reveals the unfinished Batcave to Metamorpho and Katana.

8 Batcave East Gives Red Hood His Own Batcave First Appearance: Batman Incorporated #7 by Grant Morrison, Chris Burnham, Jason Masters, Nathan Fairbairn, and Carlos M. Mangual

Batman has a whole team of allies in the Bat-Family and some of them are stretched out throughout America instead of operating solely in Gotham City. Because of this, Batman has set up Batcaves all over the United States that his allies can use. Readers were able to see the Batcave East in 2013’s Batman Incorporated #7. The Batcave East isn’t used by Batman in this story, but actually serves as the Batcave of Red Hood, during his time as Wingman.

7 Prague Batcave is One of Many All Over the World First Appearance: Batman and Robin Eternal #8 by James Tynion IV, Scott Snyder, Genevieve Valentine, Alvaro Martinez, Scot Eaton, Raul Fernandez, Wayne Faucher, Sandra Molina, and Corey Breen

While Batman primarily hangs out in Gotham City, he has traveled all over America and has Batcaves for when he does. But his adventures aren’t limited to just America, he’s traveled all over the world, and as such, has Batcaves all over the world to support him and his team. One of the first examples of this was revealed in the 2016 issue Batman and Robin Eternal #8. The Prague Batcave is first seen when it’s used by Dick Grayson and Harper Row after a particularly nasty encounter with the villain known as Mother.

6 The Flying Batcave Acts as a Mobile Base First Appearance: Detective Comics #186 by David Vern Reed, Lee Sayre Schwartz, and Charles Paris

Things were a lot more lighthearted in the early days of Batman comics, and there’s no better story to prove this than Detective Comics #186 from 1952. In this story, Robin is captured by a group of criminals, but instead of them killing Robin, they come up with an original plan to force Batman to sign a contract promising that Batman and Robin won’t step foot in Gotham for a whole week. With no other choice, Batman signs the contract. With the crime-fighting duo forced out of Gotham, Batman had to come up with a plan to keep fighting crime without breaking the contract. To achieve this, Batman simply builds a flying Batcave that he uses to fight crime from the sky, technically not setting foot in Gotham again.

5 Crime Alley Batcave is Set Up Where Batman Began First Appearance: Batman #0 by Scott Snyder, Greg Capullo, Jonathan Glapion, FCO Plascencia, Richard Starkings, and Jimmy Betancourt

Crime Alley is one of the most important locations in Bruce Wayne’s history. It’s where his parents were killed and where the idea of Batman was firstborn. When Batman was first starting out his career, he obviously felt very strongly about this place, and he wanted his mission as Batman to be as close to it as possible. This location of a Batcave was established in 2012’s Batman #0. It’s here that Batman first shows off his first iteration of the Batcave, which is only 40 feet from the place where his parents were brutally killed.

4 Lunar Batcave is Truly Out of This World First Appearance: Dark Days: The Forge by Scott Snyder, James Tynion IV, Jim Lee, Andy Kubert, John Romita Jr, Scott Williams, Klaus Janson, Danny Miki, Alex Sinclair, Jeremiah Skipper, and Steve Wands

Batman has visited the Moon a few times. The original Justice League base was on the Moon, and its there that Batman stored the Hellbat suit. But as it turns out, Batman even has a Batcave on the Moon. The Batcave is meant to be a place of solitude where Batman is mostly alone, but several enemies have broken in over the years. It makes sense Batman would try to make a cave in literally the most secluded place possible. It also allows Batman to investigate things from beyond Earth that he might have a harder time finding while on the planet. He uses his Lunar Batcave for this very purpose in 2017 during the Dark Nights: Metal event.

3 The Second Batcave is a Cave Within a Cave First Appearance: Dark Days: The Forge by Scott Snyder, James Tynion IV, Jim Lee, Andy Kubert, John Romita Jr, Scott Williams, Klaus Janson, Danny Miki, Alex Sinclair, Jeremiah Skipper, and Steve Wands

Another Batcave revealed during the 2017 Dark Nights: Metal event is the extra-secret second Batcave. It’s nothing new that Batman has secrets that he keeps from his friends and allies. Batman is easily one of the most secretive characters in comics, but still, Green Lantern and Duke Thomas aren’t expecting Bruce to go so far as having a second Batcave hidden inside his first. After Batman fights the Court of Owls, he becomes obsessed with the metal Electrum that the Court uses to revive their Talons. This leads Batman to create a hidden Batcave for him to put all the secrets he learned. He also uses it as a prison to hold the Joker, who has prior experience with Dionesium and its healing properties.

2 The Batman Who Laughs’ Lair is the Darkest Batcave First Appearance: Batman/Superman #1 by Joshua Williamson, David Marquez, Alejandro Sanchez, and John J. Hill

The Batman Who Laughs is one of the darkest versions of Batman to ever appear in comics. This evil monster hails from a universe where Bruce Wayne was infected by Joker Venom, turning into a twisted version of the Dark Knight. Naturally, considering he is a Batman, he also needs his own Batcave. The 2019 issue Batman/Superman #1 reveals this dark Batcave, which is located directly under Crime Alley, in the exact spot where Batman’s parents were killed. While this cave doesn’t play a major part in the story and has only been seen once, its design and location absolutely left an impression on readers.

1 Zur-En-Arrh’s Cave was Created by Batman’s Alternate Personality First Appearance: Batman #136 by Chip Zdarsky, Belen Ortega, Tomeu Morey, and Clayton Cowles

Batman has so many secrets that he even has secrets from himself. This includes a shocking secret Batcave that the Dark Knight only learned about in 2023. One of the major problems that Batman has recently had to face is his alternate personality of Zur-En-Arrh. This personality is a “pure” version of Batman that cares only for the mission, with no regard for friends, family, or Bruce Wayne. In order to accomplish his goals, Zur-En-Arrh created his own secret Batcave below Batman’s regular one. From here, Zur created things like the Android Failsafe that nearly tears Gotham apart. This Batcave is something Batman is entirely unaware of and only discovers it after Zur exposes it when Failsafe goes rogue.
