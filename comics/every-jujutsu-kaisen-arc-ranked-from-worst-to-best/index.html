Every Jujutsu Kaisen Arc Ranked From Worst to Best

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1800 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

While all Jujutsu Kaisen arcs have their merits, some stand out as being a cut above the rest. Some arcs succeed because of their excellent writing and action, whereas others fail because of their short length and adding little to the story. Not every arc in Jujutsu Kaisen is a standout story arc, but they all have something to offer, nonetheless.

Literature Review

After being serialized for over 5 years Jujutsu Kaisen has a variety of arcs, all of which are great but some of which are better than others.

Warning! Contains major spoilers for the Jujutsu Kaisen manga.Jujutsu Kaisen is currently one of Shōnen Jump’s most iconic battle manga, standing alongside the likes of My Hero Academia and One Piece. This is partially due to how long it has been serialized, spanning multiple intense arcs. Now that the manga is officially in its end game, it seems a perfect time to compare these storylines and see which Jujutsu Kaisen arcs are the best. Of course, as fans know, no Jujutsu Kaisen arc is abjectly terrible. They all feed into the manga’s overall narrative in interesting ways and are often filled with innovative fight scenes.

Every story arc is written with incredible care from start to finish, which leads these arcs to all of Jujutsu Kaisen’s arcs being incredibly satisfying, even if they may not always feature the best moments the series has to offer. However, some Jujutsu Kaisen storylines stand out as being a cut above the rest for their story and action, and there’s plenty of merit in examining why that is.

11 Itadori’s Extermination Arc Is A Poor Follow-Up To Jujutsu Kaisen’s Biggest Arc Jujutsu Kaisen Chapters #137-#143

As the first storyline after the Shibuya Incident arc, the Itadori’s Extermination arc had some big shoes to fill, but for the most part, it couldn’t maintain the same standard of quality as its predecessor. There isn’t much action outside of the fights with Yuta and Naoya, and what few story beats are featured in the Itadori’s Extermination arc mostly exist to set up developments for later story arcs, so it doesn’t have much going on for itself. What few moments are present are still great, but it’s hard to see it as anything other than Jujutsu Kaisen’s weakest arc.

10 The Death Painting Arc Is Unfortunately Forgettable Jujutsu Kaisen Season 1, Episodes #22-#24

While the Death Painting arc has two great fights, that’s pretty much all it has going for it. The arc does introduce the concept of Cursed Wombs, which would become important later on when Choso became more of a character, so it’s far from filler. However, the Death Painting arc is far too short to have any major impact, especially in the anime, making it one of the most forgettable arcs in the series. But forgettable isn’t bad, with moments like Itadori and Nobara’s dual Black Flash standing out; it just doesn’t compare to the later arcs of the series. Watch on Crunchyroll

9 The Fearsome Womb Arc Is A Very Basic Introductory Arc Jujutsu Kaisen Season 1, Episodes #1-#8

Being the first arc of the main series, the Fearsome Womb arc has a lot of exposition to do, and luckily it does it well, setting up the world of sorcery and curses quite nicely while also introducing the main characters of the series. It also fears the first fight between Gojo and Sukuna as well as Yuji’s shocking “death”. However, like the Death Painting arc, the Fearsome Womb arc is too short to make a lasting impact on the audience beyond hooking them into the story. Still, as an opener goes, few series could do better.

8 The Culling Game Was An Interesting Idea That Dragged On For Too Long Jujutsu Kaisen Chapters #159-#221

The Culling Game has been one of the most controversial arcs in Jujutsu Kaisen history. It features some of the highest highs the series has to offer, like Sukuna possessing Megumi and Hakari and Kashimo’s fight, but it also drags. Focusing on each colony meant that certain characters weren’t featured for a very long time, and the pacing of the Culling Game arc left many of the new characters and fights feeling inconsequential. However, the arc may read better in a reread after the series is over, as the Culling Game has set up ideas that haven’t paid off yet. Jujutsu Kaisen season 3 is set to adapt the Culling Game.

7 The Cursed Child Arc Is The Ideal Prequel Story Jujutsu Kaisen 0

The Cursed Child arc, while not originally written as such, is a prequel story to Jujutsu Kaisen focused on Yuta Okkotsu. Better known as Jujutsu Kaisen 0, its events are very important for the series as a whole, featuring the culmination of Geto’s plans and his death at the hands of Gojo. It also shows a variety of characters before their introduction in the main series like Gojo, Maki, Panda, and Inumaki. Jujutsu Kaisen 0’s greatest strength, however, is telling one relatively self-contained story about Yuta’s growth, which makes it more fulfilling than many other Jujutsu Kaisen arcs. First called Tokyo Metropolitan Curse Technical School, the manga version of Jujutsu Kaisen 0 was originally a standalone work that series creator Gege Akutami later turned into a prequel.

6 Hidden Inventory/Premature Death Was The Perfect Way To Start Season 2 Jujutsu Kaisen Season 2, Episodes #1-#5

The Hidden Inventory/Premature Death arc is another prequel arc, except this time the chapters were released as part of Jujutsu Kaisen’s regular serialization. It follows a young Gojo and Geto as they take on a mission to protect a young girl known as the Star Plasma Vessel, but their mission ends in failure and said failure is responsible for their present-day characterizations. The Hidden Inventory/Premature Death arc is satisfying on its own due to it being relatively disconnected from the main plot, which elevates it above many arcs on this list.

5 Perfect Preparation Finally Gave Maki The Spotlight She Deserves Jujutsu Kaisen Chapters #144-#158

The Perfect Preparation arc is largely just set up for the Culling Games, but it ends up being a top-tier arc thanks to Maki. The Perfect Preparation arc features Maki’s awakening and the incredibly cathartic slaughter of the Zenin clan, which is one of the best parts of the entire series. Sure, that sequence is a bit rough around the edges as Gege didn’t have time to polish it up before release, but the sheer emotional weight and epic scale of the destruction make up for that and make Perfect Preparation a top-tier Jujutsu Kaisen arc.

4 The Vs. Mahito Arc Perfectly Set Up One Of Jujutsu Kaisen’s Best Villains Jujutsu Kaisen Season 1, Episodes #8-#13

The tragic storytelling of the Vs. Mahito arc perfectly set the tone for Jujutsu Kaisen. The arc does a great job of establishing Mahito as a twisted villain with a personal connection to Yuji. Also, Yuji’s friendship with Junpei and his horror upon their death at the hands of Mahito leads to one of the coolest panels in the entire series. Adding Nanami’s introduction along with the multiple fights against Mahito that hinted at how cool Jujutsu Kaisen battles can be makes this easily one of the best arcs in the series despite how early into the manga it comes.

3 The Kyoto Goodwill Event Was Jujutsu Kaisen Action At Its Finest Jujutsu Kaisen Season 1, Episodes #14-#21

The Kyoto Goodwill Event arc features an incredible expansion of Jujutsu Kaisen’s cast of characters by introducing the Kyoto school sorcerers, including fan favorites like Miwa and Todo. Not only that, but the Kyoto Goodwill Event arc features some epic fights both between the sorcerers, themselves, and against the Disaster Curse Hanami who infiltrates the event. In addition to the high-octane fights, it also takes time to show off fun interactions between the high school students such as in the baseball game of the epilogue. All in all, this arc truly has everything that makes Jujutsu Kaisen special.

2 The Shinjuku Showdown Arc Is Set To End Jujutsu Kaisen On An Amazing Note Jujutsu Kaisen Chapters #222-Present

As the final arc of the series, the Shinjuku Showdown arc naturally ranks high as one of Jujutsu Kaisen’s best story arcs. Gojo and Sukuna’s fight was incredible in terms of story and choreography and more than lived up to the hype, and as oddly placed as it might have been, Takaba’s interlude with Kenjaku made for some of the best writing in the entire series. According to Akutami, The Shinjuku Showdown is set to end sometime in 2024, but as it stands, it’s hard to see it anything as one of the best arcs in all of Jujutsu Kaisen.

1 The Shibuya Incident Is Still Jujutsu Kaisen’s Best Work Jujutsu Kaisen Season 2, Episodes #6-#23

While the current Shinjuku Showdown arc could eventually dethrone it, the Shibuya Incident currently stands as the best arc in Jujutsu Kaisen. The arc forever changed Jujutsu Kaisen’s status quo and its revolutionary nature changed shōnen manga forever. It also featured some of the best and most chaotic fights in the series, with each battle bleeding over into the other ones and creating a beautiful whole. As the culmination of everything that came before it, the Shibuya Incident is in equal measure tragic and cathartic, easily making it the best arc in Jujutsu Kaisen. Read On Manga Plus

Jujutsu Kaisen
Jujutsu Kaisen is a supernatural action anime that follows high school student Yuuji Itadori as he battles against magical curses. After Yuji selflessly saves a classmate by taking a curse into his own body, he is discovered and trained by a powerful sorcerer named Satoru Gojo. Gojo, who also happens to be a faculty member at a high school, enrolls Yuji there to help him and other students prepare themselves against the supernatural forces that plague humanity.
