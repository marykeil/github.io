Another Survival Game Is Looking To Steal Palworld s Attention This February

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Nightingale offers a unique Victorian fantasy world with gaslamp fantasy elements, distinct from other survival crafting games. The game features procedural generation and realm cards to create diverse and mystical environments for players to explore.

Nightingale emphasizes co-op play and offers a variety of construction styles and combat options.

Literature Review

Palworld’s still enjoying a large share of the spotlight, but one new game with a unique angle has some exciting potential to attract players.

The survival crafting game Palworld has been getting plenty of attention since its release in January, but a new game called Nightingale could be poised to give it some serious competition. This isn’t the first legitimate rival to Palworld, as the fantastical Enshrouded quickly attracted a community only a week after Palworld’s launch. Nightingale also takes the approach of building a fantasy world for players to explore, but it does it differently and that could help it stand out in the increasingly crowded field.

Nightingale is the debut title from developer Inflexion Games, a company that’s full of industry veterans from studios like Bioware. Inflexion CEO Aaryn Flynn worked as a director and studio manager during the development of several Mass Effect and Dragon Age games, and his public insistence on delivering more value to players at Inflexion than was possible at Bioware should reassure anyone whose opinion of Bioware has soured in recent years. Nightingale joins projects like The Banner Saga and Stray Gods as part of a substantial list of interesting games from ex-Bioware employees.

Nightingale Has Victorian Fantasy Realmwalking How Nightingale Is Different From Other Survival Games

Nightingale’s aesthetic isn’t the standard approach to a world of magic, borrowing elements of the Victorian era to place itself squarely within the “gaslamp fantasy” niche. This isn’t quite steampunk, focusing more on otherworldly elements and abilities and less on an overabundance of clocks and cogs (although Nightingale still has its share). Filling out this alternate take on history are a number of characters both real and fictional, from Nellie Bly to Allan Quatermain, along with some that have been newly concocted for the game.

The 2004 book Jonathan Strange & Mr Norrell was one major source of inspiration for Nightingale , with a detailed look at a 19th-century take on magic through the eyes of rival magicians.

The setting of Nightingale distinguishes it from other survival crafting games in more ways than one, mostly because it doesn’t constrain itself to a single setting at all. Although players can set up camp in a home world called an Abeyance Realm, exploration can venture forth into a variety of Fae Realms conjured up by procedural generation. The key attributes of these realms are determined by the use of cards, which can dictate the biome and contents as well as unique and often mystical effects.

How Realm Generation Works In Nightingale

Any given realm in Nightingale falls into one of three basic biomes – forest, desert, or swamp – each of which has unique environmental hazards and enemies to face. Major Realm Cards then determine what points of interest or basic attributes can be found in each realm, making it easy to strike out with a specific goal. Minor Realm Cards provide the most unusual transformations, from altering gravity to randomizing what drops come from resource gathering. These are deployed once the player is already present inside a realm, and it’s possible to switch up the nature of one location any number of times.

Compared to something like Palworld, which features one discrete world, Nightingale might lack the same consistency of design but offers a much larger variety of possibilities in its place. It’s far from the first survival crafting game to use procedural generation, with Valheim being a major example of the same standard, but handing the streamlined recipes to generate to the player is an unusual approach. If any one feature is likely to attract a community when the game first hits early access, it’s this one, as its promise of experimentation and potentially chaotic results has an undeniable allure.

Crafting And Fighting With Friends In Nightingale How Crafting & Combat Works In Nightingale

Playing with friends is definitely another key component of Nightingale’s appeal, with co-op support for up to six players in a shared server. Some concepts like placing blueprints for building without assigning materials help support teamwork in developing a realm, although it’s still possible to tackle the game alone without trouble. At the moment, the game requires an always-online internet connection, both to support multiplayer and to assist with the realm generation features, but offline play could be possible in the future.

Nightingale also seems well-equipped on the basic tenets of survival crafting gameplay, with sufficient attention paid to both building and combat possibilities. Unlike the fairly limited aesthetic options for building in Palworld, Nightingale features a variety of construction styles that invoke different period-appropriate settings and architectural movements. Fans of the voxel-based systems in Enshrouded won’t find that extra level of construction and destruction detail in Nightingale, but the less typical fantasy player might provide an equal or greater allure depending on personal preference.

Combat in Nightingale plays out in first-person by default, which makes sense with the increasing emphasis on firearms that comes with general progress in the game. Enemies include everything from basic prey to complex automatons, which can be hunted with crude knives and crossbows or ornate rifles and intricately worked war hammers. One-handed weapons provide a handy dash feature, and having an offhand free makes it easy to heal, patch up injuries, or equip an umbrella that makes jumping from heights and gliding across the landscape possible.

Like Palworld and Enshrouded, Nightingale is entering an early access model rather than a full release, so player feedback should play a major part in shaping the game’s continued development. In Nightingale’s case, the planned early access period is 9–12 months, which could flex if more time becomes necessary to bring about a product that feels robust and polished. Nightingale already has enough ideas and content to be a reasonably attractive proposition for players, and watching it grow over time could be exciting if Inflexion Games can build upon its foundation for a truly special final product.
