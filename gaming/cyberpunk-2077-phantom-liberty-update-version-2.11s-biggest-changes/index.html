Cyberpunk 2077 & Phantom Liberty Update Version 2.11s Biggest Changes

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The 2.11 update for Cyberpunk 2077 brings bug fixes and improvements, making the game run smoother and more stable. Players can now change the paint colors of certain cars with the new CrystalCoat feature, hinting at potential future customization options. The update also adds new content, including new weapons, rewards, and settings, providing players with more options and a better overall gameplay experience.

Literature Review

Cyberpunk 2077’s 2.11 update is now live on all platforms. Here are all the major changes, fixes, and new content the latest patch adds to the game.

Cyberpunk 2077’s version 2.11 update is now live on PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X/S, bringing with it tons of changes to the base game and its Phantom Liberty DLC. This is the third major update since the DLC’s launch, and since the Cyberpunk 2.0 update that completely overhauled all its systems of progression, customization, and combat. Cyberpunk is a very different game today from what it was at launch, but there’s still work to be done.

The 2.11 update is largely focused on bug fixes, of which there are many both big and small. Whether they’ve been experiencing a common issue or a rare bug, most players who boot up Cyberpunk after the update will find it a more well-oiled machine. Even though the update mostly consists of bug fixes, there are also a few tidbits of new content in there. While they’re not earthshaking in and of themselves, they hint at an interesting future for Cyberpunk. Here are all the biggest changes in the 2.11 update.

Cyberpunk 2.11 Lets Players Change Certain Cars’ Paint Colors Plus Other Vehicle Fixes

Per the patch notes available on Steam, the most exciting change in Cyberpunk’s 2.11 update is the ability to change some cars’ colors. In previous versions of the game, all vehicles were locked to their default paint jobs in perpetuity - barring player-made mods, there was no way to pick a particular color or finish. That seems to have changed in the update. Switching colors with the new feature, called CrystalCoat, is easier than expected: it doesn’t require a mechanic, or even an AutoFixer payment, and can be done at will while behind the wheel of an eligible car.

The variety of paint colors available is pretty impressive. Players can pick anything they like from a complete color wheel, and also adjust the hue for lighter or darker pigments. However, at the moment, the CrystalCoat feature is exclusive to Rayfield vehicles owned by V. The in-universe explanation is that this is a Rayfield-developed technology, but this could be a sign of something bigger to come. Hopefully, future updates to Cyberpunk add more paint customization options for cars of other makes and models.

Extensive changes have come to some other Cyberpunk vehicles as well. Burn-Out Mode is one of the most affected driving mechanics: all-wheel drive cars finishing drifts will apply power through their front differentials more evenly, and Dynamic Stability Management should also kick in more smoothly. Some mid-engined cars, which previously struggled with bumpy Burn-Out Mode induction, should also go into and exit burnouts more easily. The affected vehicles include the Rayfield Caliburn, Quadra Turbo R, and Herrera Riptide, but other mid-engined cars may have also been tuned up.

And speaking of tune-ups, various cars have been tweaked in order to improve their overall handling and efficiency. The updated models include, but are not limited to:

Cyberpunk 2.11 Adds New Content New Weapons, Rewards, & Settings

Cyberpunk has also added a few new things for players to see, hear, and do in the 2.11 update. First and foremost, anyone who’s already completed the “Psycho Killer” gig for Regina Jones might want to check back in with the fixer. Her payment for the gig has increased, and even if they’ve already gotten the original reward, players can still swing by to get the bonus. Anyone who hasn’t already done “Psycho Killer” will get a bigger reward automatically on completing the job.

Players interested in getting the BFC 9000, one of Cyberpunk’s legendary weapons, were previously limited to a missable event in which they had to find a crashed aerial vehicle in Rancho Coronado. Patch notes mention that there’s now an alternate way to find the BFC 9000, but don’t elaborate further. So, while it’s possible to find the weapon without the AV, players who want it will still have to do a little experimentation.

Also, in a Phantom Liberty-specific change, players who have completed the DLC will now get a message from Reed over the end credits regardless of what ending they’ve achieved. This feature was previously limited to “The Tower,” Cyberpunk 2077’s new ending added with Phantom Liberty.

Finally, there’s a new suite of settings to affect how Radioports behave throughout Cyberpunk 2077. By default, music would previously stay on whenever V got out of a car. However, this is now adjustable. By disabling “Sync to Vehicle Radio,” the music will shut off as soon as V opens the door. There’s also another setting, “Cycle Stations with Button Press,” that allows players to switch radio stations without opening the menu.

Cyberpunk 2.11’s Other Changes & Bug Fixes Quest Fixes, Improved Keybinding, & Balance Changes

In the previous version of Cyberpunk 2077, several keybind options were bugged or broken. Sometimes, rebinding the right mouse button wouldn’t overwrite the aim controls, so V would still aim down their sights while doing whatever RMB was rebound to. Changing the “interact” key often didn’t work, and dashing would sometimes also activate crouching. All these keybind issues and more have been fixed in 2.11, so anyone who’s had difficulty with custom controls in the past might want to give it another try.

Issues regarding melee finishing attacks have also been addressed in the update. Melee finishers were broken in the previous update - when players tried to perform them, the animation wouldn’t trigger at all. This should work properly in the update.

And lastly, one balance change in 2.11 may affect certain Cyberpunk character builds. There’s now a strict cap on Cyberware Capacity at 450. However, once this cap is reached, Cyberware Capacity Shards will no longer drop, allowing players to obtain points in other skills instead. This is part of a more sweeping change that affects how Shards are dropped: once a player reaches max level in a certain category, Shards for that skill will cease to appear. This should ensure that Vs everywhere have a better spread of stats, and make every Skill Shard feel useful.

Those are all the major changes and new things in the Cyberpunk update, but certainly not everything there is. There are far more detailed quest fixes, visual bug patches, and much more also included in the changelog. But of course, the best way to experience the 2.11 update to Cyberpunk 2077 is to boot up the game and test out the new features.
