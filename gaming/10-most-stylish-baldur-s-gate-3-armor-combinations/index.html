10 Most Stylish Baldur s Gate 3 Armor Combinations

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Unique armor combinations in Baldur’s Gate 3 can provide both power and style, allowing players to create impressive outfits for their characters. Players will often want to prioritize stats over looks, but some armor combinations look too good to ignore. Players can mix and match different pieces of equipment to create the most formidable and stylish party in Baldur’s Gate 3 , combining style with power.

Literature Review

Baldur’s Gate 3 has a lot of armor pieces you’ll come across, some better than others. Here are the most stylish outfits you can use them to make!

Baldur’s Gate 3 has no shortage of equipment options for every member of the party to choose from. While some pieces are certainly better than others due to the stats and bonus effects they have, they don’t look the same. Since each piece of gear has a unique look, certain combinations of pieces can give the party both power and style. Baldur’s Gate 3 might not necessarily be about style, but that doesn’t mean that Tav and their party can’t create outfits based on appearance alone. Of course, this wouldn’t be the best idea for parties tackling the challenge of Honour Mode, where function needs to be more important than style for the sake of survival. But for a casual run or for some new choices in camp clothing, here are the most stylish armor combinations in BG3.

10 Helldusk Combination An Act 3 Style Choice

The Helldusk Armor is earned in the House of Hope in Baldur’s Gate 3, which is a particularly difficult section to get through. Ithas an imposing look to it with spikes and skull features as decorations, so combining it with other imposing items creates a Tav that nobody would want to mess with. Here, it’s combined with Bhaalist Gloves, the Deathstalker Mantle, and Spaceshunt Boots. The Deathstalker Mantle is unique to the Dark Urge, but red looks great to make a rather Bhaalist gear combination.

9 Bhaalist Combination Found In Act 3

The Bhaalist Armor from Baldur’s Gate 3’s third act isn’t over the top in its design, but the black with red detailing creates a piece that’s stylish without being loud. Of course, pairing them with Crimson Mischief and Bloodthirst is a natural combination since those weapons belong to Orin, who leads the Bhaalists. Although not necessary, the Disintegrating Night Walkers make the perfect boots with this set, and the Gloves of Dexterity make a good choice of gloves for them.

8 Obsidian Laced Robe Combination Comes With Fire Protection

This combination is really about the robe and weapon match-up, and neither of them are the best option for Gale. They do, however, look great on him. The robe is the Obsidian Laced Robe, which deals damage to enemies who successfully pass Saving Throws against the wearer’s spells, and it grants Fire Resistance. Then, Woe is the perfect weapon to pair with it in terms of appearance – it gives Gale a free use of Blight, which can be helpful depending on the enemies he’s fighting.

7 Half Plate Armor Combination With Shadowheart’s Silver Hair

The Half Plate Armor is decent enough, as the wearer takes two less Piercing Damage, but there are Medium Armor options that are stronger. However, the silver and blue design go great with Shadowheart’s silver hair, which she has if she frees Nightsong in the Shadowfell in Act 2. To finish the outfit, add Hoarfrost Boots and the Reverse Rain Cloak, which has no use outside its appearance.

6 Reaper’s Embrace Combination New Ketheric Style

Reaper’s Embrace looks similar to Ketheric’s armor, and on Shadowheart, it can be used to create a new Justiciar style, but in Selûne’s name. Itpairs well with the spiky style of the Gauntlets of Hill Giant Strength and Hoarfrost Boots. The best touch for this combination, however, has to be Selûne’s Spear of Night, which looks like it could’ve been designed to go with the Reaper’s Embrace armor. Of course, the Shar variation of the spear would also be fine for a party that decided to kill Nightsong.

5 Moon Devotion Robe From Isobel

The Moon Devotion Robe pairs well with the Incandescent Staff, or any weapon that’s lighter in color and not too flashy. The Moon Devotion Robe has a lot of subtle details in its design, but it’s also helpful for any caster in the party. It strengthens mage armour, and gives the wearer a free use of produce flame and lunar bulwark. Unfortunately, getting this robe means the party has to kill Isobel in Act 2.

4 Robe Of The Weave A Wizard’s Appearance

The Robe of the Weave is almost identical to Elminster’s outfit, which paints it as an outfit that’s designed for wizards in particular. The Robe of the Weave, however, is a white and tan variation of Elminster’s outfit, and like the Moon Devotion Robe, it pairs well with the Incandescent Staff. In general, any staff could look nice with this robe because it’s the hallmark of a Wizard to have a robe and staff, but with all the details on the robe, a simple staff design is best.

3 Wavemother’s Robe No Combination Needed

This armor piece oozes style to the point that characters don’t need to pair it with anything else. Its sleek, silver appearance looks great on a Shadowheart with silver hair, but even Karlach and Astarion can rock this robe. For pairing, a simple or elegant weapon would be perfect for the robe, such as Shadowheart’s Spear from Selûne or Shar, which has some detailing, but not too much to take away attention from the robe. However, getting the Wavemother’s Robe requires completing “Avenge the Drowned,” which isn’t until Act 3.

2 Dark Justiciar Set From Act 2

While in the Gauntlet of Shar, the party can pick up pieces of gear to create a full Dark Justiciar set. And the result is an outfit that’s sleek without too much detail, but it also masks the wearer’s identity. In a symbolic way, it’s perfect for Sharrans, who give up pieces of their own identity to serve Shar if they need to. Dying it black only makes its appearance more fitting for Shar’s elite warriors.

1 Githyanki Armor Set The Githyanki Default

The Githyanki Half-Plate and Githyanki Longsword are perfect for each other. While they give bonuses to Githyanki wearers, they’re stylish on any race, and they give off the vibes of a seasoned warrior. That part shouldn’t be surprising, however, because the Githyanki culture is very militaristic, so it only makes sense that the armor they wear reflects that. Baldur’s Gate 3 gives a few options for armor customization with the ability to dye items, but the most stylish outfits come from mixing and matching pieces of equipment together. As such, it seems like a system similar to World of Warcraft’s Transmogrification could go a long way in the world of Faerûn. Until the day that a system like that is added, the best solution is to mix style with power to create the most formidable party in Baldur’s Gate 3.
