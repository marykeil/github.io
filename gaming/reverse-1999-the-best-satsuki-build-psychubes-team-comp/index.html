Reverse 1999 The Best Satsuki Build (Psychubes & Team Comp)

SUMMARY

Literature Review

Satsuki is a niche sub-DPS in Reverse: 1999 who works best when paired with fellow Plant-Afflatus DPS Druvis III for a Petrify team comp.

Petrify prevents an enemy from acting until a certain amount of rounds pass or until they obtain Reality DMG. By reapplying Rigidity, Satsuki can also extend the duration of an existing Petrify status. This makes Satsuki a decent crowd-control unit, especially when paired with other characters who use Petrify. She just needs a good Psychube and specific team comps to utilize fully.

The Best Psychubes For Satsuki

When looking for good Psychube options for Satsuki in future updates of Reverse: 1999, keep an eye out for single-target Amplifications. Satsuki’s debuff and Ultimate are single-target abilities that could benefit from these bonuses.

Luxurious Leisure Of all the globally available ATK Psychubes in Reverse: 1999, Luxurious Leisure is Satsuki’s best-in-slot Psychube. The Psychube boosts Ultimate Might by 18% at Level 60, playing well with its Amplification. When Satsuki uses her Ultimate while using Luxurious Leisure, she will get up to a 9% DMG Dealt bonus. This bonus can be stacked three times throughout the fight. Since only a few characters have a single-target Ultimate that can trigger Luxurious Leisure, there shouldn’t be much competition for its use if you’re lucky enough to pull this Psychube.

Thunderous Applause Of course, if another character with a single-target Ultimate would benefit more from Luxurious Leisure, try giving Satsuki Thunderous Applause and turn her into a stronger sub-DPS. Thunderous Applause is both an ATK and Crit Psychube that gives you further incentive to send Satsuki on the attack. When Satsuki scores a critical hit on her debuff or her Ultimate, her Critical DMG increases by up to 32%. Thunderous Applause increases the chance of these critical hits by boosting Satsuki’s Critical Rate by up to 16%.​​​​​​​

An Afternoon Nap If you don’t have Thunderous Applause in Reverse:1999, An Afternoon Nap is the go-to lower-level replacement Psychube. An Afternoon Nap mimics Thunderous Applause in many ways, with a 13% Critical Rate bonus and an Amplification based on single-target critical hits, but the mechanics of this Amplification are slightly different. Rather than a flat Critical DMG bonus, An Afternoon Nap applies one stack of 3% Critical DMG with a maximum of 8 stacks possible.

              Reverse 1999: The Best Charlie Build (Psychubes & Team Comp)   Charlie is a performer in Reverse: 1999 who makes a show of her oddly balanced HP to boost her DMG with excellent buffs for every occasion.

Brave New World Brave New World is challenging to beat when you have a character like Satsuki with three attack abilities. This Psychube rewards Satsuki for using her Ultimate in two ways: by increasing her Ultimate Might by up to 18% and providing up to a 40% Critical DMG bonus for the next Incantation following her Ultimate.

His Bounden Duty If you don’t have any of these Psychubes available or want a support-oriented Satsuki, you can stick her with His Bounden Duty, a combination of ATK and Heal Psychube. His Bounden Duty has a general DMG Bonus of 12% at Level 60, but its healing capabilities appear with its Amplification. If Satsuki can defeat an enemy, she can heal by up to 120% of her ATK. This requires you to carefully time out other attacks so Satsuki delivers the final blow. The ability can also interfere with her Insight 1 bonus, but it’s still a solid substitute.

The Best Team Comps For Satsuki

Druvis III You’d want to build Satsuki to pair her with Druvis III in Reverse: 1999. Satsuki is essentially a less powerful version of Druvis III; the 6-Star character can apply Petrify to enemies, enjoys an increased Penetration Rate with her dual-target Incantation, and has a wider-reaching Ultimate. The main strategy with Druvis III and Satsuki is to apply the main Petrify status with Druvis III and use Satsuki’s Rigidity status to increase the duration of Petrify, keeping enemies eternally frozen.

Satsuki only truly shines when in a team comp with Druvis III. Without her Petrify synergy, Satsuki becomes awkward in other teams, especially since she doesn’t stand alone as a DPS unit. As such, if you don’t have Druvis III or another character who can apply Petrify, it is better to leave Satsuki behind.

Charlie If you really want to use Satsuki in a team comp but don’t have Druvis III, Charlie is the best DPS alternate. Thanks to her Insight bonuses, Charlie plays with her HP to apply various buffs. Her Mental DMG won’t break Satsuki’s Petrify or Rigidity debuffs while her Ultimate targets the entire enemy battlefield, picking up Satsuki’s single-target slack.​​​​​​​

Medicine Pocket Although Reverse: 1999’s Medicine Pocket does not have a wide-reaching target range to cover Satsuki and Druvis III’s single and dual-target Incantations, their healing cannot be beaten. When they heal the team, they also apply Sturdiness, reducing their DMG Taken. Meanwhile, they increase the DMG Taken against the target of their debuff. They can even inflict Daze with their Ultimate, which behaves similarly to Petrify without the Reality DMG break.

Regarding the perfect healer/shielder for a Satsuki & Druvis III team comp in Reverse: 1999, the ideal candidate would be a Plant-Afflatus character with Mental DMG. However, since Version 1.3 does not include a healer with both traits, you will have to settle for either a Plant-Afflatus healer or a healer with Mental DMG.

Dikke If you’re worried that Druvis III and Satsuki might get overwhelmed, pair them with Dikke from Reverse: 1999, a healer with sturdy combat skills. Her attacks deal both Mental and Genesis DMG, making the Incantation a perfect follow-up to an enemy’s Petrify debuff. Her healing is stronger when her allies have less than 50% HP, so if they can’t Petrify an enemy in time to shield themselves, Dikke can patch them up.

Tooth Fairy Tooth Fairy has healing abilities rival Medicine Pocket’s while opening up enemies to critical hits. When she heals the team, she also increases their Critical Rate by 30%. Her debuff Incantation applies Confusion to one target, opening them up to the team’s increased Critical Rate. If an enemy slips past the veil of Rigidity and Petrify statuses set up by Satsuki and Druvis III, Tooth Fairy’s Ultimate can fix them, removing all negative statuses from the team.

With Druvis III and the right healer on her side, Satsuki can be the lynchpin in a strong Petrify-oriented team comp for Reverse: 1999.
