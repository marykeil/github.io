You Probably Didn t Notice The Best Update In Genshin Impact 4.4

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Genshin Impact 4.4 update added item in the new Chenyu Vale area that provide extra Liyue-based materials for character Ascension. The items in Chenyu Vale are valuable because they offer limited resources that are hard to farm due to respawn limitations. The addition of this system in Chenyu Vale could be used in future map extensions in other regions.

Literature Review

Genshin Impact 4.4 added several new features, including two playable characters, but one of the best updates probably went unnoticed by players.

Most players probably missed the best Genshin Impact 4.4 update amidst the patch’s several notable ones, since this one was not so openly advertised by the developer HoYoverse. The current Version brought forth entertaining additions, such as the introduction of the playable version of Cloud Retainer, whose human form is named Xianyun, and the 4-star Pyro unit Gaming, as well as the extension of Liyue’s northern region with Chenyu Vale. The focus on Liyue coincides with the Nation of Contract’s annual Lantern Rite Festival, a flagship event that is also planned to occur during the early stages of Version 4.4.

Despite not being a x.0 update, which introduces a new nation and continues the Archon Quests of the Traveler in search of their lost sibling, Version 4.4 is a big and important patch, something that was foretold by the format of its presentation during its respective livestream. There is much to see and do during Version 4.4, including many hidden details to discover. In fact, Genshin Impact 4.4 added a funny secret to the game that can only be found through exploration. Amidst new playable content and an explosive chest with reward, one notable addition likely went unnoticed by players.

Chenyu Vale Hides Extra Liyue Materials In Genshin Impact

Yodie Valley, situated within Chenyu Vale, has several ruins and reminders of the Adepti. While exploring, players may stumble across broken vases that can be hit. These vases have a small glimmer, indicating the possibility of an interaction, much like boxes that spew out Wheat and Ore, for instance. The big difference is that these vases in Chenyu Vale actually offer extra Liyue-based materials, many of which are used in several characters’ Ascension processes. It is possible to find items like Cor Lapis, Jueyun Pepper, Violetgrass, Qingxin, Noctilucous Jade, and even Clearwater Jade in Genshin Impact 4.4 through these vases.

Some vases also give less valuable items, such as Butterfly Wings, Horsetails, Frogs, Lizard Tails, and Slime Condensate.

Hitting Vases In Chenyu Vale Is Extremely Valuable For Genshin Impact Characters

The fact that players can hit these vases in exchange for additional materials may seem simple, but is actually valuable. Most of the materials obtained from the vases in Chenyu Vale are Local Specialities. This means that their nodes are limited and often take about two full days to respawn. Hu Tao, for instance, needs 168 Silk Flowers to fully Ascend, though there are only 17 registered nodes present on the map. Players can purchase some from a vendor and obtain more from the planting system in Genshin Impact’s Serenitea Pot, but this limitation is not exclusive to her.

When there is enough of a material to gather in a single run and Ascend a character completely, as is the case with the 169 Noctilucous Jade nodes that supply Beidou’s 168 item requirement, for instance, players have to make sure they farm precisely, leaving virtually no node behind. Although the loot is limited, obtaining extra materials from the vases in Chenyu Vale can help address the lack of farmable resources or simply ease off the pressure of missing a few nodes when following farming routes. Even if subtle or unspoken, this is the best update in Genshin Impact 4.4.

It does not, however, come lacking flaws. While valuable and limited materials tend to be the majority when it comes to the general loot pool registered by Game8, there are also less useful items, such as Horsetail and Butterfly Wings. Though they can be used for cooking and crafting, they are less worthy of deserving a spot inside the vases of Chenyu Vale. Considering how easy it is to obtain them naturally and how quickly their nodes respawn, their addition is not very helpful to players farming for character Ascension in Genshin Impact.

All the vase locations in Chenyu Vale can be consulted on Game8.

Another issue with the vases in Chenyu Vale is the fact that they do not respawn, according to Game8’s entry on the subject. After players interact hit a vase, it drops materials and cannot be interacted with anymore. Once they interact with all 44 registered vases in Yodie Valley, their source of extra materials from Liyue comes to a halt. This may be disappointing, indeed, but it does not annul the vases’ value as one of the most important additions of Genshin Impact 4.4. In fact, this minor and unspoken update should be taken as a standard moving forward.

Other Regions Need Genshin Impact 4.4’s Vase System

The addition of these vases in Version 4.4’s Chenyu Vale paves the way for future updates that feature map extensions. The lack of the necessary amount of resources or the strict respawn time for Local Specialties is not an issue that is exclusive to Liyue. In fact, the limitation of Kalpalata Lotuses for Genshin Impact’s Nahida, for instance, was one of the most prominent problems in Sumeru. This continues to be true, as there are still only 66 nodes of the item available on the map, which essentially forces players to run three farming sessions to gather everything for Nahida.

HoYoverse tends to revisit its older regions and expand them with new areas, as is the case with Chenyu Vale. This could be an opportunity to implement a system that is similar to the vases in other nations, offering a plethora of Local Specialties and hard-to-obtain materials. Of course, these resources do not need to be added via vases per si, but simply interactable items. This would prove beneficial to players and not only help them but also encourage them to thoroughly explore any new area added to an older region in Genshin Impact.

Inazuma’s Tsurumi Island or Sumeru’s Desert of Hadramaveth, for instance, would have been great locations for the implementation of such a feature.

The hittable vases are but one of the many great additions that arrived with Version 4.4. Chenyu Vale is building up to be one of the best post-release map expansions in the game so far, in terms of playable content, exclusive mechanics, and overall design. The area to the north of Liyue is well worth exploring and the vases with limited materials make it even more worthwhile. Natlan’s release in Genshin Impact is still a few months away, but the game has a lot of playable content to entertain players while they wait to go to the land of Pyro.
