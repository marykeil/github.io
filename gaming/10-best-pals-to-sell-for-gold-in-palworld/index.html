10 Best Pals To Sell For Gold In Palworld

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Literature Review

Selling Pals will help players acquire hard-to-farm resources and materials for improving their base and their character in Palworld.

Many factors go into whether players will want to sell their cutest Pals in Palworld, but the biggest reason will be how useful they are in other ways. While Pals have the highest base gold value based on how strong or rare they are, players might not want to sell the Pal with the highest base value as it will likely be more valuable in other places. That means players will often want to sell Pals of lower base value, as there is less reason to keep them around.

One Clever Palworld Strategy Can Take Out The Game’s Toughest Alphas
Those having trouble taking out Alpha Pals in Palworld can use this clever building trick to overwhelm them with numbers instead.

10 Woolipop Alpha Gold

Woolipop is a fairly early game Pal that can be found and caught easily. While Woolipop will drop Cotton Candy in a Ranch, there are few uses for Woolipop for the Base or in a team, as this Pal’s typing makes it weak in Palworld. Therefore, if players find an extra Woolipop or even an Alpha version of Woolipop, it is a sure bet to sell it and earn some gold from the Woolipop instead of watching piles of cotton candy accumulate.

9 Mau Gold for Gold

While Mau has a strong element to make them a little more valuable, that is where most advantages end. Compared to Vixy, Mau’s ability to farm Gold Coins is not as strong in the Ranch, and there is no reason to keep both. Mau has no good stats other than the low farming level, so it would be better to get the Gold rather than watch it dig. That will help players clear out their Palbox for new and stronger Pals.

8 Lamball Fast Cash

While you will not get much gold, Lamball is easily found, and a whole bunch can be caught at once. This can make it easy to sell a large amount all at the same time. It will not get you the highest base value, but you are unlikely to need extra Wool or Lamball meat, and selling it will get more for your trouble than butchering your Pal would.

7 Cattiva Clearing Space

Many other early-game Pals in your early journey will become less useful as time goes on. This will mean you often end up with large amounts of low-level Pals taking up space in the Palbox, leading to crowded storage. This can be easily dealt with by selling off all those low-level Pals you had to catch at the beginning, such as Cattiva, and getting something from them. You can only sell Pals in the Palbox, so it is easy to clear out the box when you visit a merchant. Moreover, Pals that are not bosses or Alphas are often more useful for the Gold than for the resources that will drop from being defeated (or butchered).

All Dragons In Palworld, Ranked Worst To Best
Dragons are always cool, and the Dragon Element Pals in Palworld are no exception. They cover a large variety of strengths and are fun to have around.

6 Jolthog Electric Cash

While there are advantages to having an Electric-type Pal around your base, there are also a lot of better Pals to keep with that trait, such as Grizzbolt. Grizzbolt is excellent for combat, breeding, and resource farming, whereas Jolthog is not as lucky. While they are adorable, having many Jolthogs on hand will clog up the Palbox, so it would be better to sell them and get Gold for some High-Quality Pal Oil, which is far easier to buy.

5 Rushoar No Work No Play

While Rushoar is not the only Pal in this category, some of the low-level Pals, when you are beginning in Palworld, are less valuable later on, particularly with Pals like Rushoar, who start to slack or are constantly seeking breaks. Look for the UI text that will inform you if a particular Pal is lazy, and make sure to sell that one and replace it with another. Rushoar, when you have a decent mining replacement, you will have a higher level and, therefore, a bigger payout when you sell them to the merchant.

4 Chillet Easy Alpha

Very early in Palworld, an Alpha Chillet will continue to respawn near the Fort Ruins Great Eagle Statue and is around Level 11. This is likely a player’s first Alpha to beat, and it is an easy Alpha Pal to continue defeating repeatedly. By catching it, you can take advantage of the Alpha stat and sell the Alpha Chillet for more than you would be able to if it was a normal version.

3 Lifmunk Dungeon Boss

There are a few Level 13 dungeons around Palworld, and in one of the first, you will be directed towards a cave just before the Rayne Syndicate Tower. It is an easy dungeon to run for anyone with even a low-level flame-wielding Pal such as Foxsparks, and this should allow you to catch the dungeon’s boss, a Lifmunk, consistently. Since it is conveniently close to a fast-travel point, it is easy to travel back and forth and accumulate several boss-level Lifmunk to sell for a higher profit.

2 Dumund Mining For Gold

While having one Dumund to automatically farm ore in your base is good, having more than one doesn’t make much sense. They are not very good at fighting, and if you can breed them or find extras, they can fetch a high base value, depending on their level. Start catching Dumund in this location, as shown by the YouTube channel Game Guides Channel, once you have Heat Resistant Armor.

1 Relaxaurus Meta Gold

A base-level Relaxaurus can sell for around 2000 Gold, with more Gold possible as the level increases. Some of the meta around Pal selling currently includes breeding and selling them for as much as possible, allowing players to rack up Gold quickly. They are a potential breeder for many harder-to-find Pals, so you will likely end up with Relaxaurus eggs in Palworld. This can turn breeding mistakes into breeding Gold, which will help you finish your journey strong.
