Forget Scarlet & Violet, Pokémon TCGs Best Deck Uses A Bunch Of Old Cards

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The best deck in the Pokémon TCG is still composed primarily of older cards from previous expansions. The Giratina Lost Zone deck, known as Lost Tina, is currently one of the best decks due to its customization and adaptability. The Lost Tina deck will suffer an impact in 2024 due to the retirement of key cards, but may still remain strong with some modifications.

Literature Review

One of Pokémon TCG’s strongest decks at the moment is, curiously, composed of older cards from previous expansions from Sword and Shield.

Despite the release of the Scarlet and Violet series of expansions, Pokémon TCG’s best deck continues to be one composed primarily of old cards from previous expansion packs. Since early 2023, the trading card game initiated a new series to reflect the current moment in the Generation 9 games. The new expansions of the Scarlet and Violet series added creatures from Paldea and brought back older gameplay mechanics, such as Pokémon ex. The Pokémon TCG: Paldean Fates cards, for instance, highlight creatures from the Gen 9 region, as well as Shiny versions of several monsters, including Charizard and Palafin.

Even though the main focus as of late has been on this series of Scarlet and Violet expansions, the trading card game has not officially retired previous expansions and sets from the older Sword and Shield series for TCG. This means that players are currently allowed to build decks with varied mechanics, mixing the V, VMAX, and VSTAR systems with the recently returning ex cards. The larger Standard card pool allows for some very interesting combinations and various new deck formats. However, one of the best Pokémon TCG decks in 2024 continues to be built out of older 2022 cards.

Pokémon TCG’s Lost Origin Set Up One Of The Best Decks

The Lost Origin expansion for Pokémon TCG: Sword and Shield is a vital part of the best deck in 2024. The set’s returning Lost Zone mechanic along with Giratina V and Giratina VSTAR are crucial to drawing several cards and quickly eliminating the opponent’s Pokémon on the board. This deck was notably used by the Pokémon TCG player Jon Eng, who won the first place prize in the 2024 Pokémon Portland Regional Championships - TCG, as is registered in Limitless’ database. Although it utilizes some recent cards from Scarlet and Violet, the bulk of its composition is cards from 2022.

Why Giratina Lost Zone (Lost Tina) Is Currently The Best Deck In Pokémon TCG

This Giratina - Lost Zone deck, popularly known as Lost Tina, is very good because of its exclusive Lost Zone mechanics and a little less on how tightly knit the card composition is. In fact, this deck is open to a lot of customization and can be deeply modified. This customization factor and adaptability are precisely what made the deck continue as one of the best in the competitive scenario for so long. The cards that rotated out of the Standard mode so far have not heavily impacted the structure of the Giratina - Lost Zone deck in Pokémon TCG.

Of course, like any other deck, Lost Tina is not immune to failures. The deck’s limited Energy pool can often be problematic, and some cards lose their effects quite quickly – though they can serve as fuel for the Lost Zone mechanic. Lost Zone is pretty difficult to counter, as it allows players to discard their cards there and use those to empower certain Pokémon. Giratina VSTAR, for instance, can activate its VSTAR Power in Pokémon TCG after ten cards have been put in the Lost Zone – this allows Giratina VSTAR to instantly knock out the opposing Active Pokémon.

Cramorant, on the other hand, can strike for 110 points without any Energy attached to it when there are four cards in the Lost Zone. The deck’s structure requires sacrifices but provides a lot of horsepower. Another factor is how the Lost Tina deck is built around drawing a lot of cards and switching the Active Pokémon with some frequency to hit the opponent’s cards and recharge. With several cards in the Standard format offering such effects, so far it has been easy to find substitutes whenever Pokémon TCG sunsets some of these Trainer cards.

Pokémon TCG’s Lost Tina Deck Will Suffer An Impact In 2024

This deck has had its reign for over a year and a half, but it may soon lose its potency. In 2024, the game is retiring several key cards in Lost Tina’s composition. Battle VIP Pass, which is a crucial start-of-match card, will no longer be usable as of April, which should heavily impact how the deck sets up its early-game board. The card allows players to search and put two Basic Pokémon from the deck into the Bench on their first turn. This Pokémon TCG deck is mostly composed of Basic Pokémon, which makes Battle VIP Pass vital.

The only current substitute for it would be Nest Ball, but these are already part of Lost Tina’s composition. The release of the Temporal Forces expansion will bring a card called Buddy Poffin, which is considered something of a substitute, but it will operate in a suboptimal way, only serving to add Comfey and Manaphy to the board, as it requires finding Pokémon with 70 HP or less. The Stadium card Path to the Peak is also being retired in 2024, which will likely disrupt a part of Lost Tina’s structure, along with several other decks in Pokémon TCG.

This card helps keep the player safe from losing 2 Prize cards when their Giratina V or VSTAR are knocked out. Players may have to lean more into Lost City or find a more generic Stadium substitute. 2024’s losses also include Escape Rope, but this is easily replaceable. The Lost Zone mechanic should prove to continue reliable as long as Giratina, Comfey, and Cramorant are usable in Pokémon TCG, but the quick flow of the deck’s build-up will likely sustain damage.

For now, this strong deck suffers some very significant losses, but there are still ways of mitigating the damage done to it. If players find a way to circumvent the loss of Battle VIP Pass and Path to the Peak, Lost Tina will prove itself once again one of the strongest and most adaptable decks in Pokémon TCG so far.
