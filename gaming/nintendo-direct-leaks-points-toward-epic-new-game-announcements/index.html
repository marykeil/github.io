Nintendo Direct Leaks Points Toward Epic New Game Announcements

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 900 words 5 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

A Nintendo Direct may be on the horizon, with leakers suggesting big reveals from third-party developers on Nintendo consoles. Speculation continues around potential reveals, like Xenoblade Warriors, an Ecco the Dolphin reboot, or GameCube games for NSO. Game Freak’s new IP, Project Bloom, may also make an appearance, but all details remain purely speculative until the Direct airs.

Literature Review

A leaker claims that a February 2024 Nintendo Direct is still coming - and it has some big reveals in store. Here’s everything their hints suggest.

With rumors of a Nintendo Direct fast approaching, leakers already claim to know what it’ll reveal. Nintendo Directs are irregularly spaced, airing whenever the company has enough big reveals to fill the time slot. A Nintendo Direct was initially predicted for February 15, but was likely delayed so as not to be overshadowed by the end of Xbox exclusivity also announced that week. However, rumor has it a Direct is still coming later this month - and some fans hint at the games it’ll allegedly feature.

Leaker Pyoro on X (formerly Twitter) recently asked the loaded question, “Is Nintendo a partner?” This refers to a specific kind of Nintendo Direct - a Partner Showcase - that focuses on games developed by third parties, but published on Nintendo consoles. The wording of this tweet seems to suggest that 2024’s first Nintendo Direct could reveal a spin-off of a Nintendo game, developed by a third party. Many in the replies suggest this could be Xenoblade Warriors, a crossover between the Xenoblade RPG series and the Warriors hack-and-slash franchise. However, Pyoro’s other tweets suggest something else entirely.

What Else The Nintendo Direct Could Reveal Game Freak’s Project Bloom, GameCube Online, & Ecco The Dolphin Rumors Explained

Warriors has already adapted two Nintendo properties in Fire Emblem Warriors and the Hyrule Warriors series, and with its real-time RPG combat and diverse cast of characters, Xenoblade is a prime candidate for the next spin-off. However, Pyoro’s direct reply states, “Sometimes Nintendo can be a Partner because they love dolphins,” complete with a dolphin emoji. This could refer to a series revival of Ecco the Dolphin, a formerly Sega-exclusive action-adventure series later ported to Nintendo systems. Series creator Ed Annunziata has expressed interest in an Ecco game for the Switch, but so far, nothing concrete has materialized.

But Nintendo has another association with aquatic mammals: during the GameCube’s development, it used the internal codename “Project Dolphin.” So, the dolphin in this tweet could instead reference something related to the GameCube - perhaps GameCube titles coming to Nintendo Switch Online. The addition of the GameCube library to NSO, a subscription service that grants players access to a plethora of NES, Genesis, and N64 games, has long since been rumored. But there’s been no official confirmation from Nintendo on the matter.

And in another tweet thread entirely, Pyoro asks whether Game Freak, the development studio best known for the Pokémon series, counts as a Nintendo partner. Game Freak has a better claim to the title than Nintendo itself; although the two are closely related, Game Freak is an independently owned, third-party developer. Game Freak also happened to announce a brand-new IP, an action-adventure game known only as Project Bloom, in May 2023.

All anyone outside Game Freak knows about Project Bloom is the single piece of concept art above, that Private Division is attached as a publisher, and that it’s likely a few years out from release. Still, that could change soon: if Game Freak really is set to showcase something in an upcoming Nintendo Direct, it could reveal a first trailer or announce a release date for Project Bloom.

Still, all of this is little else but speculation at this point. Nintendo does have a history, especially in recent years, of putting out Directs each February to announce the games coming to its consoles throughout the first half of each year. Pyoro has a history of correctly leaking Nintendo announcements and releases, but no leaker has an absolutely perfect track record. These events tend to be announced only at the last minute, and Nintendo plays its cards close until announcements are officially made. So, until a Nintendo Direct actually airs, players are left to wild speculation.
