10 Games Releasing This March You ll Want To Add To Your Wishlist

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

March 2024 brings a diverse lineup of games covering popular genres. March will contribute to the many RPGs releasing in 2024, but action-adventure and simulation are among the genres also seeing releases during the month. Among others, Princess Peach: Showtime! and Dragon’s Dogma 2 show that there is something for everyone looking to enjoy new experiences in March.

Literature Review

March 2024 has a lot of variety in video game releases, from expansive action RPG experiences to titles that focus on intricate strategy.

March 2024 is bringing a fresh new slate of games to the table, with titles from Dragon’s Dogma 2 to Princess Peach: Showtime releasing across the full range of modern platforms. Not every 2024 game has been a hit so far, but between the launch of games like Prince of Persia: The Lost Crown and Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth has complemented a boom in popular survival crafting experiences to prove plenty of market strength. March 2024 could keep that momentum up, and some of the most anticipated titles feel like sure bets for fun. The diversity of subject and style in March 2024 releases looks strong, with major games covering just about every popular genre aside from first-person shooters. 2024’s emphasis on RPGs is continuing with a couple of key titles, while action-adventure games start to pick up steam and simulation games get a nice bump. It shouldn’t be hard for anyone to find something that fits their niche, whether it’s a sequel, reboot, or a new IP altogether.

10 The Outlast Trials (March 5) Red Barrels, Co-op Horror

Platform(s) PC Released May 18, 2023 Developer(s) Red Barrels Genre(s) Survival Horror , Multiplayer

The Outlast Trials first entered early access in May 2023 on PC, but after nearly 10 months, it’s ready for a full release across platforms. As a prequel to the prior Outlast games, this psychological horror story takes on a Cold War setting and expands the fear to allow some friends in co-op gameplay. The focus in The Outlast Trials is on stealth, as players lack the power to defeat enemies outright.

9 South Park - Snow Day! (March 26) Question, Co-op RPG

Platform(s) Nintendo Switch , PC , PlayStation 5 , Xbox Series X/S Released March 26, 2024 Developer(s) South Park Digital Studios , Question Games Publisher(s) THQ Nordic Genre(s) Action Franchise South Park ESRB M

Like The Stick of Truth and The Fractured But Hole, South Park - Snow Day! looks to be an irreverent take on the RPG genre starring the memorably crude protagonists of South Park. This one has an emphasis on co-op gameplay, although it’s still possible to treat it as a standard single-player campaign. A switch to 3D graphics and a snowfall covering the town invoke the Nintendo 64 game South Park, as does the return to more action combat.

8 Horizon Forbidden West (March 21) Guerrilla Games, Action RPG

Platform(s) PlayStation 4 , PlayStation 5 Released February 18, 2022 Developer(s) Guerrilla Games Publisher(s) Sony Interactive Entertainment Genre(s) Adventure , RPG , Action ESRB T

Horizon Forbidden West isn’t exactly a new game for March 2024, but the PC port arriving on March 21 does mark the first time that anyone without a PlayStation console will be able to leap into the adventure. Like Horizon Zero Dawn, it follows Aloy on a journey of discovery that’s fraught with danger from mechanical creatures and unknown threats. The tropical, desert, and mountainous regions to the setting should truly shine on high-end hardware, as developer Guerrilla Games has always been good at tech showcases.

7 The Thaumaturge (March 4) Fool’s Theory, RPG

Anyone looking to scratch the itch that Baldur’s Gate 3 satisfied could do a lot worse than taking a look at The Thaumaturge, which also follows in the classic isometric CRPG tradition. In this case, however, the story takes on the unique setting of a magically-infused 20th-century Warsaw. It promises plenty of rich choices and branching possibilities along with turn-based combat with some twists, giving it a strong mix of classic appeal and unique ideas.

6 Unicorn Overlord (March 8) Vanillaware, Tactical RPG

Platform(s) Nintendo Switch , PC , PlayStation 4 , PlayStation 5 , Xbox Series X/S Released March 8, 2024 Developer(s) Vanillaware Publisher(s) Atlus , Sega Genre(s) RPG , Tactical

Developer Vanillaware has attracted a strong following for its memorable art style and polished game design, with titles like 13 Sentinels: Aegis Rim and Odin Sphere building up a legacy of acclaim. Unicorn Overlord takes these strengths and marries them with gameplay fundamentals more familiar to Final Fantasy Tactics and Fire Emblem for a gorgeous tactical experience. Conveniently for console players, it’s available across all modern systems but PC.

13 Sentinels: Aegis Rim Switch Review - Sublime Story & Strategy
13 Sentinels: Aegis Rim remains compelling on Switch, with a seamless transition onto the platform making it well worth a look for new players.

5 Prison Architect 2 (March 26) Double Eleven, Management Simulation

Prison Architect has been a popular choice for construction and management simulation for nearly a decade now, marrying the fundamental silliness of older tycoon games with a significant helping of depth. Prison Architect 2 takes all of that to 3D, making the building possibilities more robust than ever. It still maintains a signature cartoonish style, with inmates and guards alike looking more like children’s toys than actual people.

4 Princess Peach: Showtime! (March 22) Nintendo, Action-Adventure

Franchise Super Mario Platform(s) Nintendo Switch Released March 22, 2024 Developer(s) Nintendo Publisher(s) Nintendo Genre(s) Action , Adventure

Princess Peach hasn’t gotten the spotlight all to herself since Super Princess Peach for the DS, but Princess Peach: Showtime! looks ready to break her out of that dry spell with style. A variety of transformations for the princess shake up the gameplay, giving her opportunities to investigate, fence, cook, and more. With an obvious surplus of charm and the expectation of Nintendo’s typical level of polish, Princess Peach: Showtime! should be a fun one for Switch owners to check out.

Princess Peach Is Finally Getting What She Deserves
Princess Peach will get her very own Nintendo Switch game next year to explore a classic 3D Mario world in a color-changing dress.

3 Alone In The Dark (March 20) Pieces Interactive, Survival Horror

Platform(s) Microsoft Windows , PlayStation 5 , Xbox Series X/S Released January 16, 2024 Developer(s) Pieces Interactive Publisher(s) THQ Nordic Genre(s) Action , Survival Horror ESRB M

Alone in the Dark is one of the original titans of the survival horror genre, getting its start as an MS-DOS title in 1992. The 2024 re-imagining takes a similar approach to the Resident Evil remakes and Bloober Team’s upcoming Silent Hill 2, going for an over-the-shoulder third-person perspective and more realistic graphics. It’s been delayed several times to avoid crunch, but the March 20 release is looking like a lock.

2 Rise of the Ronin (March 22) Team Ninja, Action RPG

Platform(s) PlayStation 5 Released March 22, 2024 Developer(s) Team Ninja Publisher(s) Sony Interactive Entertainment , Koei Tecmo Genre(s) Action-Adventure , RPG ESRB M

Rise of the Ronin brings the focus on fluid combat that developer Team Ninja is known for to an open-world setting, which explores Japan at the time of the Boshin War, a significant historical point of political upheaval. There’s also a focus on traversal options, from standard horseback riding to Assassin’s Creed-style runs across rooftops to a glider. Rise of the Ronin is a PlayStation 5 exclusive, so if it sticks the landing it could be one of the console’s biggest selling points for 2024.

1 Dragon’s Dogma 2 (March 22) Capcom, Action RPG

Platform(s) Microsoft Windows , PlayStation 5 , Xbox Series X/S Released March 22, 2024 Developer(s) Capcom Publisher(s) Capcom Genre(s) Action , Adventure , RPG ESRB M

The original Dragon’s Dogma has become a certified cult classic over the years, with a complex combat system serving to attract fans willing to overlook some bugs and various issues. Dragon’s Dogma 2 revisits the original’s strengths with an interesting world, narrative focus, and multitude of possibilities in combat. It could easily be another notch in Capcom’s recent winning streak, and all the gameplay that’s been showcased to date certainly looks suited for an impressive March 2024 release.
