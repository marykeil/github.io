Pokémons Indigo Disk DLC Creates A Paradox For Scarlet & Violets Legendaries

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1100 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The DLC in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet introduces Paradox Pokémon and highlights the use of time travel and paradoxes in the game’s plot. The origin of Paradox Pokémon is uncertain, adding a new dimension to the franchise’s existing themes of time travel and parallel worlds. Interactions with characters like Sada and Turo create bootstrap paradoxes and multiple timelines, showcasing the complex nature of the game’s narrative.

Literature Review

A certain encounter in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet’s Indigo Disk DLC has created a paradox centered on the games’ mascot Legendary Pokémon.

A brand new and very real paradox has been introduced to Pokémon Scarlet and Violet by the events of the Indigo Disk DLC. The DLC has introduced several new Paradox Pokémon to the franchise, but a more conceptual contradiction is also clear to see. As a result, Scarlet and Violet feature some undeniable impossibilities in their plot. Scarlet and Violet’s use of time travel and the resulting Paradox Pokémon makes it clear that the games are far from unfamiliar with the concept of Paradoxes.

Name aside, the actual origin of Paradox Pokémon is itself somewhat uncertain, with descriptions of them arising long before Professor Sada or Turo built their time machine. Whether primal and savage or futuristic and calculating, these Pokémon introduce a strange new dimension to a franchise that already features time travel and parallel worlds. However, it now seems that there are more than just these physical paradoxes at play in Scarlet and Violet.

A Bootstrap Paradox Is Created At Kitakami’s Crystal Pool During The Indigo Disk

When the original Sada or Turo appears at the Crystal Pool in Kitakami as part of The Indigo Disk, the player character tells them the modern name of Scarlet or Violet’s mascot Legendary Pokémon. Meanwhile, the professor refers to it by its Paradox name (Winged King or Iron Serpent), indicating that the name ‘Koraidon’ or ‘Miraidon’ has not occurred to them. As a result, this encounter is presumably what prompts the professor to use the name themselves when they return to the past. However, the way this information is actually provided makes this encounter the root of a major paradox.

As an example of this, in Pokémon Scarlet, the protagonist learns about Koraidon from Arven, who presumably learned its name from his mother Sada. However, the encounter at the Crystal Pool shows that Sada herself learned the name from the player character. As a result, nobody seems to have actually come up with the name ‘Koraidon’ to begin with. This creates a time loop with no discernable start or end, a sequence of events that sustains its own existence whilst being logically impossible. It is a brilliant example of a bootstrap paradox, also known as an ontological paradox.

Talking To Sada And Turo Can Create More Than One Paradox

Of course, it is worth noting that Sada and Turo raise the possibility that they have come from an alternate timeline, suggesting a more linear spread of information. The fact that they traded their copy of Heath’s book for Briar’s own book would otherwise be a paradox itself; the same book was the key to the time machine, which would be impossible if the professor had already traded it away. Although considering that this conversation generates a paradox anyway, an additional one is not out of the question.

If Arven is spoken to after this event in his dorm room, then he will see the book carried by the protagonist but will not realize it is the exact same copy his parent owned.

Considering their journal in the Area Zero Underdepths describes the encounter at the Crystal Pool, it is clear that the particular version of the professor who died in that game has experienced some version of this meeting in Kitakami. As a result, even if the professor did travel to another timeline entirely, they are still met with an analog of the protagonist, and likewise presumably received the name of Koraidon or Miraidon from them. As a result, a similar paradox unfolds regardless, although this one is arguably more complicated with the various alternate professors traveling the Pokémon franchise’s multiverse.

In addition to all this, the same conversation can generate yet another paradox, although this one is optional. When the protagonist first sees the professor emerge from the mist, one of the possible responses is to ask if they are the AI version. The professor’s reaction makes it clear that they have not built their AI double yet but find the idea intriguing. As such, speaking about the AI here technically creates another bootstrap paradox. However, given that the professor obviously creates the AI regardless, they arguably would have had the idea either way, effectively nullifying the paradox to some extent.

The Indigo Disk’s Paradoxes Build Upon Scarlet And Violet’s Time Travel Narrative

It should be noted that despite the contradictions, the time loops established by this scene are self-contained and evidently stable. There is no mention of any threat to the stability of the timeline or world as a result of these paradoxes, and so, presumably, there is none. Instead, they are simply relatively subtle but interesting additions to the scene and very thematic additions to the narrative of Pokémon Scarlet and Violet as a whole. However, with that being said, it is quite sad that the protagonist has no option but to tell Arven about the encounter.

Paradoxes like these are, by their very nature, impossible to reconcile. This can be frustrating at times, especially if the paradox’s illogical nature is seen as a detriment to the wider story. However, in this case, the existence of so many paradoxes surrounding the Professor seems very fitting. Professors Sada and Turo are strongly associated with their favored Paradox Pokémon and the concept of time travel in general, in that context these additional complications to their existence seem only natural in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet.
