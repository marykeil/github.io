There s One Major Thing Suicide Squad Kill The Justice League Actually Gets Right

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Rocksteady’s approach to live-service in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is player-friendly, ensuring that no one misses out on content or rewards if they take a break from the game. Players have the freedom to play the game at their own pace, without feeling the pressure of a “life commitment” or needing to spend excessive amounts of time or money to progress. Unlike other live-service games, players can revisit completed seasons and unlock rewards even after they have ended, allowing new players to catch up and enjoy the full experience of the game.

Literature Review

Rocksteady is being player-friendly with its approach to Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s post-launch content and free battle pass’ rewards.

There has been plenty of skepticism surrounding Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League since the reveal of live-service components, such as Battle Passes, paid cosmetics and episodic post-launch chapters. Rocksteady seems to be taking an interesting approach to this model, however, and it’s one more live-service titles should consider.

After several delays, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is finally releasing on January 30th for those who pre-ordered the Deluxe Edition with a general launch on February 2. In the build-up to this release, Rocksteady has been offering more of an insight as to what players can expect from the latest entry in the Arkhamverse through several Suicide Squad Insider videos detailing gameplay mechanics, storylines and the game’s post-launch content.

Rocksteady Is Taking A “No FOMO” Approach To Suicide Squad: KTJL Players Are Able To Play Suicide Squad: KTJL’s Chapters At Their Own Pace

On January 26, Rocksteady held a Q&A session with some of the game’s developers on the official Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League Discord (which has been recapped on the official SuicideSquadRS Instagram page). In this session, the team behind the game addressed some of the feedback from the d Alpha and the Press Preview events in recent months, and explained exactly how the game’s Battle Pass - which only contains cosmetics - would work.

As is the case with many live-service titles, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s Cosmetic Battle Pass will have a free and a premium model, with players also able to use points acquired through the game to purchase Battle Pass tokens if they don’t wish to buy them with real-world money. The studio has also taken an approach to the battle pass rewards and post-launch seasons in which no players miss out should they take a break from the game or don’t complete the episodes within a set time-frame.

Premium Battle Pass tiers are available via an optional in-game purchase, but most of the Battle Pass tiers will be free with each new season.

Each Season is free and will be released every 3 months, and is split into 2 episodes lasting 6 weeks. Even if players miss this window, they can revisit and play the story at any time so no player gets left behind. This is particularly useful if a player skips a season and jumps back in later because a particular character they want has been announced, for example. As the game’s director, Axel Rydby stated, “We all love playing games, but we also have lives.”

Rydby continued to explain that a big part of Rocksteady’s design philosophy was that the studio doesn’t want Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League to feel like “a life commitment", referencing the concerns that players would need to “sacrifice a lot to see all the content on offer, or feel like [they’re] not making good progress in the game if you can’t play hundreds of hours.” These comments suggest that although there are approximately 80 tiers per Battle Pass, each with new cosmetic rewards etc, no reward is missable.

              Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League - Every Confirmed Character   Suicide Squad: KTJL is set to have an impressive roster of DC characters, with several characters also returning from Rocksteady's Arkhamverse.

Players Can Revisit Seasons Once They’ve Ended Allowing Them To Revisit The Story And Unlock Every Reward

Unlike other live-service games like Fortnite, once one of Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s seasons has ended, players can still go back and play these seasons and get all the rewards on offer, with each battle pass remembering their progress. One of the major criticisms surrounding live service games is that unless a player has been playing since day one and is prepared to sink all of their time or money into the game, they can miss some very cool rewards. This is the case with the aforementioned Fortnite where some of its best skins, such as 2021’s Spider-Man, or various DC characters, are no longer attainable as their Battle Pass event has long since ended.

Players will also be able to revisit completed levels from the main campaign and post-launch seasons at any time with new characters they’ve unlocked.

With this in mind, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s more player-friendly approach to the live-service model is one that more games in that genre should follow. If a player has spent money - and with AAA titles such as Suicide Squad already costing approximately $70 - $90 depending on the standard or deluxe edition, it’s a pretty large entry fee for many - they should have access to everything they’ve paid for, rather than spending money and still grinding to no avail.

              Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League Roadmap May Have Just Spoiled The Ending   Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League's post-launch roadmap has been revealed and may have provided the biggest clue as to how the game's story ends.

The Player-Friendly Approach To Live-Service Could Help Suicide Squad: KTJL’s Longevity New Players Aren’t Being Penalized

Considering some of the negativity surrounding the title, many potential players are likely holding off pre-ordering the game until they’ve seen more of it in action. By giving players the option to complete Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s Battle Pass tiers in their own time, Rocksteady could benefit the game’s longevity as it won’t penalize players who were weary and didn’t buy the game at launch.

The general consensus from those who played the d Alpha Playtest, or went to the Preview event was that Rocksteady has done a good job with the story and character work, with the banter between the core members of Task Force X being a highlight. Although Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is a shooter and is a departure from the melee-based, Freeflow combat of the Batman: Arkham games, Rocksteady still demonstrates that it knows how to chain combat together, allowing players to pull off some satisfying, fluid combos as they take on large groups of enemies.

Rocksteady has already addressed some of the criticisms from the d Alpha and previews and confirmed that there will be several customization features to clean up the sometimes crowded and overwhelming Heads Up Display at launch, showing that the studio is intent on listening to its fans and supporting its title over time. With this mentality surrounding its community and support, and the notion that everything is accessible regardless of when they first pick up the game, there’s a possibility Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League could draw some players in and grow post-launch, and hopefully, more live-service titles will take note of a more player-first approach.
