Every D&D Book Releasing In 2024

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Dungeons & Dragons will release five new books in 2024, including new editions of the Player’s Handbook and Dungeon Master’s Guide .

Vecna: Eve of Ruin will be a campaign starting at level 10 and taking players to level 20, and Quests from the Infinite Staircase will be an anthology of adventures.

The Making of Original Dungeons & Dragons: 1970-1977 will provide insight into the game’s history and include never-before-seen details.

Literature Review

Dungeons & Dragons has multiple new books to look forward to in 2024 as it celebrates its 50th anniversary, including new adventures and core updates.

Dungeons & Dragons players will have five new books to look forward to in 2024 with a great mix of adventures and resources to dive into. 2024 is set to be a very big year for DnD as the game celebrates its 50th anniversary and concludes its playtesting for the latest DnD edition. So what exciting new goodies can be expected in 2023?

Every D&D Book In 2024 Vecna: Eve of Ruin May 21, 2024 The Making of Original Dungeons & Dragons: 1970-1977 June 18, 2024 Quests from the Infinite Staircase July 16, 2024 Player’s Handbook (2024) September 17, 2024 Dungeon Master’s Guide (2024) November 12, 2024

Among the new releases coming in 2024 are the new Player’s Handbook and Dungeon Master’s Guide to make it easier than ever to start playing DnD. Those hoping to dive straight into creating new encounters will have to wait a little longer, until February 18, 2025, for the Monster Manual to be released. This revised Monster Manual looks set to have an even greater selection of foes for adventuring parties to face off against and provide more options for all levels of play.

    How D&D Can Help You In Real Life  D&D has many skills that transfer well into the real world, and by using D&D and those skills, players will have the tools to tackle many problems.

Vecna: Eve Of Ruin - May 21, 2024

Kicking off the year will be Vecna: Eve of Ruin, a new campaign for starting at level 10 and taking the party all the way through to level 20. There are a lack of high level adventures in DnD, and Vecna: Eve of Ruin looks set to fill that gap wonderfully in a multiverse-spanning adventure. While the party will start out in the traditional setting of the Forgotten Realms, as the story unfolds, they will find themselves traveling to other worlds such as Eberron, Dragonlance, Planescape and Greyhawk as they attempt to track down the terrifying Vecna.

Giving one of the most iconic DnD villains his own adventure campaign during the 50th anniversary year is a smart move. Thanks to Stranger Things featuring Vecna, the nefarious lich is more well known than ever and this Plane-hopping adventure can act as the perfect gateway to other DnD settings for newer players. As the Whispered One attempts to bend the multiverse to his will and destroy existence, it will be up to the party to team up with legendary heroes to stop him in Vecna: Eve of Ruin.

The Making Of Original Dungeons & Dragons: 1970-1977 - June 18, 2024

As DnD reaches its 50th birthday, The Making of Original Dungeons & Dragons: 1970-1977 is a great way to kick off the celebrations by taking a look at the beginnings of the game. This gorgeous book will contain never-before-seen details from DnD’s inception, including the first handwritten draft of DnD’s rules by Gary Gygax. Jon Peterson, author of Dungeons and Dragons Art and Arcana: A Visual History, has curated a fantastic collection to tell the story of this world-famous game.

The Making of Original Dungeons & Dragons: 1970-1977 will include articles from magazines and fanzines to explore the earliest years of DnD in detail. Exploring the history of DnD will provide fascinating insight into the game itself and the time and circumstance under which it was created. Hopefully, this will the first of many books to delve into the history of DnD, and will no doubt be a must-have for collectors.

Quests From The Infinite Staircase - July 16, 2024

The second DnD adventure book of 2024 is Quests from the Infinite Staircase, releasing July 16 and is intended for levels one through 13. Just like the successful Journeys Through The Radiant Citadel and Keys From The Golden Vault, Quests from the Infinite Staircase will be an anthology of shorter adventures that can be played individually or as a larger campaign. What makes this more exciting is this anthology will weave together six classic DnD adventures, updated for fifth edition.

    Ranking Every Heist In Dungeons & Dragon’s Keys From The Golden Vault  Keys From The Golden Vaults has 13 excellent adventures to choose from for D&D players and DMs to embark on their heist-themed adventures.

The premise of this anthology is the titular Infinite Staircase, which holds doors to other realms. A genie named Nafas makes their home there, hearing wishes from throughout the multiverse and recruiting heroes to help fulfill them. Previous DnD anthologies have typically had one adventure per player level, so it will be interesting to see what other adventures are available when Quests from the Infinite Staircase releases.

Player’s Handbook (2024) - September 17, 2024

Perhaps the most anticipated DnD release for 2024 will be the brand-new Player’s Handbook. Released as Player’s Handbook (2024) this revised version of the core rules will be available September 17, 2024 and will incorporate the changes made from the One D&D playtests. This new sourcebook will contain new versions of main classes, with each having at least four subclasses to choose from.

There have been plenty of tweaks and changes made throughout the playtesting process, resulting in changes to DnD’s rules, new spell classifications, and new mechanics for all classes. Backgrounds look to play a more important role in character creation going forward, giving players more options to create truly unique adventurers. Wizards of the Coast has stated that this new 2024 Player’s Handbook will be completely backward compatible with fifth edition rules and adventures, which should help with the transition into this next iteration of the game.

Dungeon Master’s Guide (2024) - November 12, 2024

The new and improved Dungeon Master’s Guide (2024) will arrive on November 12, 2024, the last DnD book of the year, and promises to make life easier for DMs everywhere. While the previous edition of the core rulebook was more than a little inaccessible for new DMs, this new revised guide should hopefully address some of those issues. The team at Wizards of the Coast has emphasized from the beginning that with this new Dungeon Master’s Guide, their goal was to ease people into the role of DM and answer their questions in an order that makes sense.

The Dungeon Master’s Guide (2024) aims to teach the craft of DMing and provide all the necessary tools needed to run the game. This reorganized book was designed with ease of use in mind to make sure everything a DM could need is at their fingertips and laid out in a much more logical way. With new magic items, player handouts, a glossary and an appendix of maps, the Dungeon Master’s Guide (2024) should make it easier than ever to be DM and start running new adventures in Dungeons & Dragons.

  Dungeons and Dragons  Dungeons and Dragons is a popular tabletop game originally invented in 1974 by Ernest Gary Gygax and David Arneson. The fantasy role-playing game brings together players for a campaign with various components, including abilities, races, character classes, monsters, and treasures. The game has drastically expanded since the '70s, with numerous updated box sets and expansions.
