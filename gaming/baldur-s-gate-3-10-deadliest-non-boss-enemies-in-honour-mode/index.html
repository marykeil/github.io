Baldur s Gate 3 10 Deadliest Non-Boss Enemies In Honour Mode

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Normal enemies can pose a deadly threat to an unprepared party in Baldur’s Gate 3 ’s Honour Mode. The adjusted Action Economy in Honour Mode can turn normal creatures into deadly threats. Preparation, including using the environment and resources like scrolls and potions, is crucial for a successful Honour Mode run.

Literature Review

Bosses are extra deadly with Legendary Actions, but they aren’t the only dangers that can wipe out the party in Baldur’s Gate 3 Honour Mode.

Baldur’s Gate 3 has plenty of difficult bosses that the party will end up facing and possibly dying against, especially in Honour Mode. However, bosses aren’t the only threats that can end a run when it just takes a single party wipe to get a game over screen. Even normal enemies can be enough to kill Tav and their party, depending on how the battle is approached and how well the attack rolls go. One big difference that’s added in Honour Mode is the threat of permadeath. If the party is wiped out, then the game ends, and the only option is to try again from the beginning or continue in the Normal game mode. Depending on the enemy type and the point when the party encounters them, the adjusted Action Economy in Honour Mode can be enough to turn normal creatures into deadly threats. 4:08

10 Goblins Pose An Early-Game Threat In The Blighted Village

When entering the Blighted Village, Tav can be spotted by Goblins on the rooftops. Depending on the dialogue options, this can lead to a battle where the enemies have the advantage of high ground. The initial positioning can make it difficult for the party to attack while also making it easier for the Goblins to deal damage. As a result, the party might die to the Goblins here.

9 Gnolls Will Launch Surprise Attacks On The Risen Road

The Gnolls can hit hard on their own, but the biggest issue is that they’re grouped up in ways that make it tough to fight one or two at a time. It doesn’t help that there are hyenas nearby that are on the verge of becoming Gnolls, meaning that they can join in on already busy battles. If the party isn’t prepared to deal with a bunch of enemies at once, it’s easy to end up dying.

8 Gremishka Limits Spell Use In The Mountain Pass

The Gremishka are a punishing group of invisible enemies that are found in the Mountain Pass inside Rosymorn Monastery. Not only are they invisible, they have a magic allergy that makes them tough to fight, especially when Tav has any magic users in their party. Using magic causes the Gremishka to have a reaction, and those reactions can include effects like growing in size and strength or exploding. And the threat of those reactions limits what abilities the party can use against them since even healing will cause a reaction.

10 Most Difficult Baldur’s Gate 3 Boss Fights, Ranked Baldur’s Gate 3 is packed with tough enemies to challenge the party, but some boss fights require more preparation and tactics to take them down.

7 Death Shepherds Are Formidable Tanks In The Mountain Pass

If the party uses the Mountain Pass to progress to Act 2, then they’ll have to fight a group of Death Shepherds and Ghouls. The Death Shepherds are fairly tanky on their own, but their abilities can resurrect Ghouls and the other Shepherd if it’s killed. Also, they hit hard, which overall creates a nightmarish fight for a party in Honour Mode and can pose a difficult threat even in Normal Mode.

6 Shadows Will Make You Lose Advantage Found In Act 2

Act 2 has plenty of Shadows that the party can encounter while exploring. What makes them particularly dangerous is that they can surprise the party. And characters who are surprised are skipped in the first round of combat, which means that the enemies can deal free damage without the party being able to retaliate or heal if needed.

How To Find (& Rescue) Zevlor In Baldur’s Gate 3 The Tiefling Prince Zevlor is someone you can find and rescue in Baldur’s Gate 3 once you reach the Mind Flayer Colony area near the end of Act 2.

5 Undead Justiciars Prevent Spellcasting In Gauntlet Of Shar

There are multiple types of Undead Justiciars that appear in the Gauntlet of Shar, and none of them are particularly easy to fight. In the library, the fight can be deadly because of the orb that casts Silence, preventing the party from casting spells. Like many enemies, they can be tough to separate and fight one at a time, so the numbers and inability to cast can be a run killer in Honour Mode.

4 Githyanki Attack In Numbers In Rosymorn Monastery

After fighting the Inquisitor, all Githyanki in the monastery turn hostile, which means the party might have to fight their way out since safe zones for teleporting are limited. Without managing lines of sight, more Githyanki can keep joining the fight. They aren’t all overly tanky or heavy hitters, but the continuous combat can lead to death if the party isn’t cautious. And having to go through several fights in a row without a Long Rest can add another layer of difficulty.

3 Sharrans Operate In Darkness In House Of Grief

While there’s a boss among the Sharrans, the non-boss enemies are deadly on their own. Even if the party burns down Viconia in the fight, they’re left with a room of elite Sharrans. They continually cast Darkness and cause Necrotic Damage, so being able to handle both of those is critical when dealing with them. And that means having a way to get out of Darkness quickly and take down the caster to interrupt it, or a way to see in the Darkness, which Wyll can do with Devil’s Sight.

2 Hell Spheres Are Heavy Damage Dealers In House Of Hope

The House of Hope in Baldur’s Gate 3 is full of dangerous enemies and Raphael as a boss. But the Hell Spheres alone are deadly, dealing damage by rolling into characters and spreading fire in the process. And because they’re made of fire, cantrips like Firebolt don’t work, meaning the group needs to be prepared with options like Eldritch Blast or Ray of Frost. Otherwise, the damage can be too much and enough to end a run in Honour Mode.

1 Steel Watchers Are The Ultimate Enemy Found In Act 3

Steel Watchers are found throughout Act 3, and it’s tough to make it through the game without having to fight at least a few of them. They have a few features that make them difficult, such as their weapon attack hitting multiple characters if they’re grouped up or their self-destruct explosion radius that hits anybody around them when it goes off. Since their weapon attacks can make characters prone, it’s more dangerous when the Watcher’s health is getting low since it traps them in the explosion when they self-destruct. Honour Mode puts Tav and their companions to the test in Baldur’s Gate 3, but that also means that even normal enemies can be a deadly threat to an unprepared party. With the threat of having to start from the beginning again with a single party wipe, preparation is more important than ever. However, using the environment and other resources like scrolls and potions can make the difference between a successful Honour Mode run and an early death in Baldur’s Gate 3.
