Honkai Star Rail 2.0 Review Sets The Groundwork for Another Impressive Year”

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 introduces a new destination called Penacony, with new assets, puzzles, and gameplay mechanics. The story in Version 2.0 subverts expectations and takes a dark turn, leaving players curious for more. The new playable characters in Version 2.0 are not essential additions but can add value to existing team compositions.

Literature Review

The arrival of Version 2.0 for Honkai: Star Rail sets up the second year of content for the RPG with several great and a few lackluster additions.

The release of Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 is an important milestone for the popular turn-based RPG developed by HoYoverse. With the update, the game introduces a new destination called Penacony, also known as the Planet of Festivities and the Land of Dreams. This is a completely new location, much like the Xianzhou Luofu flagship or Jarilo-VI. In addition to Penacony, the game kicks off its second year of content following its original release back in 2023. Honkai: Star Rail 2.0’s release sets in motion what is the beginning of another promising year of playable content, especially in terms of narrative.

Although there are limitations to the amount of content added with Version 2.0, with a lot of the story pushed into upcoming patches, this is one of the largest updates of the year. The arrival of Penacony not only brings the destination itself but an entirely new set of assets that allow the iconic location to take shape, as well as exclusive puzzles and gameplay mechanics that can only be found on the Planet of Festivities. The next updates in Honkai: Star Rail will build up on top of what was introduced with Version 2.0, both for gameplay and story.

Honkai: Star Rail Subverts Expectations (& That’s Good) 2.0’s Story Starts With Dreams & Descends Into Nightmares

The premise of an idyllic dreamland resort for the Astral Express is but a facade and this is exposed quite quickly when diving into the Penacony storyline. The presence of the Nameless in the Reverie Hotel does not come without complications, but there is a slow-burn element to how threats are presented. Players are gradually introduced to this entirely new destination and its particular elements, from goofy puzzle-like challenges to the colorful world design, both in reality and in the Dreamscape scenarios. Penacony is beautiful in Honkai: Star Rail 2.0, if not overwhelming in its design.

That is the intention, after all. The idea is to make players comfortable with their lush and brilliant surroundings, which obfuscates the deeper and darker truth of the story being told. What players see in the Dreamscape at first covers something that will likely be explored as the second year of content progresses and players venture further into the Land of Dreams, overruled by the Family. Curiously enough, the Family and the destination’s alignment is to the Path of Harmony in Honkai: Star Rail, yet harmony is something missing here.

Without going into details, the story presented by Version 2.0 is exactly what is needed for an update that initiates a new year in a live-service title. The narrative subverts expectations drastically, transforming the dreamlike qualities of the Dreamscape into a nightmare to be explored. There are surprising twists and turns, especially during the latter portions of the main Trailblaze Missions, which will leave players at the edge of their seats and with their jaws dropped.

A certain pivotal moment in the story could have been postponed into Honkai: Star Rail 2.1, so as to improve the emotional attachment created.

Nevertheless, the story gets very dark really quickly, and it incites players’ curiosity to continue exploring and see the outcome. The best and worst part of it is that the main campaign missions end on a gigantic cliffhanger and leave several unanswered questions. This comes as no surprise since the live-service game is expected to continue the story of Penacony in the following patches. Nevertheless, the construction of the campaign during Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 is superb and lives up to the franchise’s reputation of taking risks and making sacrifices.

New Characters Add Value To Existing Team Comps Additions Do Not Feel Like A Necessity

The playable characters in Version 2.0 continue the game’s trend of expanding the roster with heroes that present exclusive abilities. Misha and Black Swan are great additions and feel different from most other characters so far. Nevertheless, their gameplay kits are simultaneously dispensable if players already have a Destruction or a Nihility DPS, respectively. They may add value to certain team comps; Misha’s damage potential increased by Skill Point consumption pairs well with Hanya and Sparkle, while Black Swan’s focus on multiple DoT debuffs can work well with Kafka in Honkai: Star Rail, for instance.

These are not, however, must-pull characters, which makes the first batch in Version 2.0 a bit underwhelming. The game’s release in Version 1.0 was accompanied by Seele, a strong Quantum DPS hero. She has since lost a lot of her value in the face of other DPS characters, but Seele made quite an impact at launch. As a 5-star character, Black Swan does not leave that impression. She is still a powerful character, but is not one fitting the arrival of a complete new year of content in Honkai: Star Rail.

In this author’s opinion, pulling for the rerun banner of Imbibitor Lunae is a much better option than Black Swan.

Penacony Is A World Worth Exploring A Mix Between Great & Lackluster Puzzles, Secrets, & Rewards

With its ambiguous presentation flickering between a colorful Jazz Age-inspired metropolis and the dark corners of an inconceivable nightmare, the world of Penacony is worth exploring. Penacony is filled with exclusive new puzzles, secrets, and rewards to solve, uncover, and claim, respectively. All the new puzzle types added with Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 lean into the dreamlike aspect of the overarching theme.

The puzzles in Penacony play around with the idea of reality and perception, as those two are easily distorted in dreams. Some puzzles are a bit lackluster, such as the ones that see players altering the emotions of those they speak with, namely Clockwork: Emo Tuning, or the all-too-simple Dream Jigsaw puzzles that reveal treasure chests and open up new paths. In contrast, Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 also adds Hanu’s Adventure, which is a pleasant stealth-based exploration mini-game, as well as the Dream Ticker mode, which somewhat resembles Monument Valley.

The addition of the Origami Birds in Penacony gives players an extra reason to thoroughly explore the world, though the collection-based mini-game is simple and does not entice players to actively look for feathers on the map. The best mini-game is called Dream’s Eye, and they are located on the Dream’s Edge map. This exploration-based mode is the one that is most aligned with the dreamlike properties of Penacony in Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 and has players forge new paths and uncover secrets by having them explore areas through a perspective that distorts reality.

Of course, the Dreamwalker mechanic is likely the most notable, as it allows players to walk on walls and the ceiling. The way the puzzles around the Dreamwalker are presented is mind-boggling and will have players thinking before acting, which makes it a fantastic new addition. There is a lot to see and do in Penacony and, ultimately, the Land of Dreams is already a pretty complete destination at launch, which is surprising since it will mostly likely continue to expand with future updates, well beyond Honkai: Star Rail 2.0.

Power Creeps Rule In Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 Lack Of Combat Challenges Is Concerning

While the setting and the exploration aspects of Version 2.0 are remarkable, the combat factor is a bit concerning. Most of the new opponents introduced with Penacony are not much of a challenge and, in most fights with level 80 teams, players will likely feel like their characters are all power creeps. Even without the proper elements to inflict Weakness Break on certain enemies, it is easy to defeat them and continue playing. Their designs are fantastic, and they fit the theme of Honkai: Star Rail 2.0 perfectly, but a little more challenge would have been nice.

It is likely that players will only feel a sense of challenge in two or three fights, at best. While the majority of the combat instances in Version 2.0 lack the aforementioned challenge, there is one particular fight that gets players on their toes. The battle against Something Unto Death is a fun one since it can one-shot some of the players’ characters and provide a sense of threat. This is being built as one of the big villains in Penacony, so the final fight against it in a future Honkai: Star Rail update should be memorable.

The fight against another big boss, which will not be named to avoid potential spoilers, is also fun. It is one of the few enemies that provides a challenge and a risk of wiping the player’s party, but it is unfortunately cut short. It is likely this opponent will be faced again in an upcoming patch, yet players conclude the instance with a feeling of emptiness. Until now, Honkai: Star Rail had not struggled with such a problem, with Version 1.6 introducing a full set of new and challenging insect-like opponents.

Characters operating as power creeps in the majority of Penacony’s fights are not a good indicator, as HoYoverse needs to develop a way of continuing to challenge parties without simply boosting enemies’ Max HP. Overall, Version 2.0 is a fairly positive release for the game. While it does have some issues in combat and is occasionally lackluster in some aspects, it does offer a wide range of content and hits the nail on the head with its campaign, which sets the groundwork for another impressive year of stories told in Honkai: Star Rail.
