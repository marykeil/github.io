SUMMARY

The finale of Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League paves the way for a far greater adventure far beyond the world of Rocksteady’s Arkhamverse. Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League sees Task Force X, a rag-tag group of supervillains and antiheroes, sent on a deadly mission by Amanda Waller to stop an alien invasion led by Brainiac in Metropolis.

[Warning: The following article contains major spoilers for Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League.]Shortly after arriving, Task Force X discovers Brainiac has abducted 99% of Metropolis’ population already and has turned them into brainwashed creatures as part of his attempt to create a New Colu. To assist him in acquiring the remaining 1% of survivors and in ruling his new world, he has also used his mind control on the Justice League, forcing Waller to issue a kill order on some of the DC universe’s most beloved heroes.

Colu was home to the most intelligent race in the universe. In the Arkhamverse, this planet has been destroyed, and Brainiac is determined to rebuild the advanced civilization he has lost at any cost.

Brainiac’s First Meeting With Task Force X Is Cut Short Brainiac Teleports Task Force X Aboard His Skull Ship

After successfully killing the Justice League, Brainiac teleports Task Force X aboard his giant Skull ship to meet them face-to-face for the first time. Here he tells them how he sees their potential and could transform them to make them superior as he continues trying to make Earth into a New Colu. He then mentions his wider plans to conquer the multiverse, asking “Why stop at one Earth?”

The multiverse had been established early on in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League. Soon after killing Flash, Task Force X is teleported to Earth 2, a world already decimated by Brainiac, where they find a surviving Lex Luthor. As the Luthor of Task Force X’s main universe (Earth 1) has been killed, they bring him back to their universe to assist them with their mission.

Although many would think this conversation would lead to a boss battle aboard Brainiac’s ship, it is abruptly cut short, as Task Force X is suddenly transported back to LexCorp. Here, Lex Luthor tells them that the Brainiac they met is one of thirteen, and the team will also have to defeat the remaining twelve versions of him across other universes in what Deadshot deems “an eternal conflict.”

              Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League Cast: Who Plays Which Character   Suicide Squad: KTJL, has an impressive roster of DC characters, with many actors reprising their roles from previous games.

The Brainiac Boss Battle Takes Place On Earth 2 Brainiac Uses Flash’s Powers

The main boss battle against Brainiac comes later, as Brainiac can only be fought after stopping his Terraforming plans via a certain number of side missions. Once players have acquired enough Promethium points from these activities, they can use them to unlock a trip to Earth 2, with a 25-minute timer to get activities done on that planet, such as data collecting for Toyman, and killing a Bug Farm to lure enemies away from Brainiac’s base.

Once inside Brainiac’s base, transforms himself into a Brainiac/Flash hybrid, utilizing Flash’s powers from his boss battle earlier in the game, and commanding a legion of Flash-enhanced enemies. Much like the Flash boss fight earlier in the game, Task Force X must use counters to dampen his speed force abilities, although the window to shoot him in after is much shorter than it was in the Flash fight.

Once Brainiac has been countered and shot so many times, he disappears and summons a horde of enemies in his place. To fight them, Task Force X receives support from the Support Squad, with Ivy and her plant Daphne spawning plants to deal massive toxic affliction clouds. As Brainiac absorbs his minions’ data to help him regenerate, this toxic damage is carried over to him. After a few rounds of this, Gizmo delivers some of his weaponized vehicles to counter the hordes and assist in capturing Brainiac.

Soon after this Brainiac is brought back to Earth 1, Waller stabs Luthor’s Dataspike into his head. As explained by Lex Luthor, the Dataspike downloads a fragmented branch of Brainiac’s network through Coluan technology and kills that version of Brainiac, blowing up a chunk of the Skull ship in the process. This marks the end of the main campaign, and the credits begin to roll.

The “No One Defeats Brainiac!” Trophy/Achievement is unlocked for killing Brainiac.

Suicide Squad: KTJL’s Ending Sees Task Force X Embark On A Multiversal Mission One Brainiac Down, 12 More To Go…

While the death of this Brainiac triggers Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s end credits, it is only the beginning of a much larger mission. Players still have to embark upon various Elseworld missions to kill the remaining Brainiacs, with missions to other universes becoming available upon meeting set criteria.

There is also the introduction of new, increasingly more difficult challenges in the game’s “Finite Crisis” mode. This encourages players to raise their mastery levels to gain higher rewards by doing jobs for the Support Squad, embarking upon further Incursion missions, and more.

              Is Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League A Live-Service Game?   Developer Rocksteady has been sending mixed messages on Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League's live-service elements, but an answer is out there.

Does Suicide Squad KTJL Have A Post-Credits Scene? Suicide Squad: KTJL Has A Tribute To Kevin Conroy

After the credits have rolled, Lois Lane’s eulogy for Batman plays. In it, she talks about both Bruce Wayne and Batman, and the kind of man he was before Brainiac’s brainwashing. This clip also doubles up as a tribute to Kevin Conroy, who passed away in November 2022, ending with the message “Thank you, Kevin” alongside a bat emblem.

After first appearing in 1992’s critically acclaimed Batman: The Animated Series, Conroy portrayed Batman in various forms of media for over three decades, including all three of Rocksteady’s previous Batman: Arkham games. Conroy’s depiction of the character was incredibly well-received and has been heralded as the best version of the Dark Knight by many.

The image of Batman standing atop a rooftop illuminated by lightning used in Lois Lane’s tribute is reminiscent of a moment in Batman: The Animated Series’ opening sequence.

              Where To Find the Kevin Conroy Memorial In Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League   A tribute to legendary Batman voice actor Kevin Conroy can be found in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League if you explore the city of Metropolis.

Suicide Squad: KTJL Has A Post-Game Roadmap Reveal Four Playable Characters Are Coming To Season 1

Following this video, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s roadmap for Season 1 appears with on-screen text promising “The fight against Brainiac will continue in Season 1… And Beyond!” The idea of a multiversal post-game narrative had already been revealed by Rocksteady before Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s launch, with the arrival of another universe’s Joker in the roadmap’s first chapter.

Four images appear, teasing each of the Elseworlds and the playable characters coming to the game over the coming year. The first character is clearly the aforementioned Joker, but the other four are silhouetted. There is enough information for DC fans to figure out who they are, however.

The second character is an ice-based one, and while rumors circulated that it could be an Elseworld version of Victor Fries’ wife, Nora, leaks have revealed that it will actually be a female variant of Mr. Freeze called Victoria Fries, who goes by the alias Mrs. Freeze. The third character is Deadshot’s daughter, Zoe Lawton, a.k.a. Lawless. Zoe had appeared as a voice only in Deadshot’s fear toxin-induced hallucination earlier in the game, but here, she will be an anti-hero with some high-tech gadgetry that allows her to fight alongside Task Force X.

The fourth character is hardest to make out due to them being on the edge of the image, in the smallest panel, and being impacted most by the vignetting on the screen. It does seem most likely to be Slade Wilson, a.k.a. Deathstroke though, with the silhouetting showing a gold and black outfit. Deathstroke’s katanas and the distinct flowing tassels on the back of his mask can be seen as part of his silhouette to support this theory.

Deathstroke has been teased a few times in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League, mainly to set up a rivalry between him and his fellow assassin, Deadshot. Considering Waller approached Wilson regarding Task Force X at the end of Batman: Arkham Origins, which takes place 15 years before the events of Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League, his arrival is long overdue.
