Zelda Could Introduce New Features By Bringing Back A Controversial Character

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Reintroducing a controversial character in a future Legend of Zelda game could lead to incredible results and redeem her reputation. This character could make a comeback in a new game, potentially granting the Master Sword new powers and abilities. The inclusion of the character would provide an opportunity for them to learn about their predecessors and uncover lost lore, offering a heartwarming development.

Literature Review

The return of one controversial character could have an incredible effect on The Legend of Zelda, prompting the introduction of new features.

With the next Legend of Zelda game likely to introduce a new range of features to the franchise, one intriguing route for doing so actually relies on the reintroduction of a controversial character. There is an incredible range of characters in The Legend of Zelda but not everyone has proved to be popular. However, even the most annoying characters in past games can hold a vast amount of potential for future games.

Not every Zelda character is well-received, with even some major NPCs having huge amounts of detractors. One of the best examples of this is Navi, Link’s constant companion in Ocarina of Time. Despite being a prominent part of one of the most popular and influential games of all time, the fairy is often written off as being one of the most irritating characters in the entire series. Her constant cries of ‘Hey, listen!’’ can quickly sour opinions of her, no matter how many useful hints she gives to Link. However, reputations like Navi’s can be redeemed, with potentially incredible results.

              Zelda Sidekicks More Annoying Than Navi   Fans of The Legend of Zelda will likely know Link's irritating fairy companion, Navi, but the hero has had more annoying sidekicks over the years.

Sword Spirit Fi Could Return To The Legend Of Zelda

Fi, the spirit of Master Sword, was a hugely important character in Skyward Sword but features such as her constant commentary and warning people about their controller batteries meant that many also quickly developed a dislike for her. However, the Nintendo Switch’s Skyward Sword HD fixed Fi’s more annoying attributes, allowing her contributions to the plot and lore of the game be more easily focused on. What’s more, Fi has maintained a minor presence in BOTW and TOTK, making her return far from impossible despite being originally set to slumber inside the sword after sealing Demise.

Fi’s familiar chime can be heard at crucial moments related to the Master Sword in BOTW and TOTK, including evidently communicating with Zelda after Link falls on Blatchery Plain, proving that she remains aware even millennia later. Moreover, since the sword itself has now been repaired and recharged by Zelda in the form of the Light Dragon for countless years, Fi could arguably have enough strength to manifest herself once more in times of need. As such, although the return of a prior companion character would be unusual for Zelda, Fi’s unique position makes her doing so very believable.

              The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom Review - Building Excellence   Tears of the Kingdom takes Legend of Zelda into mostly familiar ground, but it remains an excellent, must-play title with plenty of innovation.

As the spirit of the Master Sword, Fi returning to the series could also easily allow the blade to gain new powers, either by her revealing new innate capabilities or guiding upgrades. It is typical for companion characters to have a strong influence on Link’s abilities in any given Zelda title, with Fi herself included. For example, Fi enables Link to Dowse for various objects and also telepathically calls for Scrapper on several occasions. These features could easily return in a new game, along with any number of new powers for the Master Sword.

Fi’s Return Could Accompany The Master Sword Gaining New Abilities

In terms of game mechanics, there is a strong precedent for the Master Sword having a large range of abilities. The Sheikah Slate and Rauru’s arm have already demonstrated the effectiveness of having items with a range of interconnected functions. Because of this, a new title focused on the Master Sword could easily follow suit. TOTK even left one Master Sword mystery in the form of Zonai writing along its blade; this perhaps implies the Zonai were involved in its initial forging and that the Master Sword might be able to retain a version of Fuse for power-ups in future games.

The Master Sword was originally the Goddess Blade, the personal weapon of Hylia in the war against Demise.

Fi and the Master Sword could offer plenty of functions in a new game, including abilities that have been firmly established by Skyward Sword, like Dowsing and the Skyward Strike. In addition to this, entirely new abilities for the Master sword could include elemental enchantments for Link to switch between, allowing him to target specific weaknesses for various monsters. If such monsters are made to be only beatable with the right element, then this would further integrate the mechanic into gameplay, as would having each element come with its own attack style.

The elemental trio of fire, ice, and electricity featured in some of BOTW’s and TOTK’s weapons demonstrates just how effective this kind of mechanic can be, and the Master Sword could take this to new heights. For example, each element could grant the Master Sword new extra abilities and attacks, such as the ability to freely fire fireballs or summon a bolt of lighting on a designated target. It would undoubtedly be an interesting way for the next Zelda game to learn from its predecessors.

Fi Interacting With Link Once More Would Be A Heartwarming Development

Having Fi manifest once more provides a unique opportunity for that game’s version of Link and Zelda to learn about their predecessors. For example, the history of Hyrule is extremely complicated, especially when taking the series’s split timeline into account and having Fi give a reliable retelling of what she has experienced throughout the ages would be incredibly informative. What’s more, given her close relation to Hylia, Fi would presumably be able to give reliable exposition on all the Goddesses of Hyrule, as well as reveal other lore apparently lost to Hyrule, such as the existence of the Triforce.

Fi has gone from being deeply annoying to somewhat redeemed by the updated Skyward Sword HD, but some traces of her poor reputation can remain, especially for those who did not play the remake of the original Skyward Sword. Reintroducing her in a brand new title, building upon all of the lessons learned with both her prior treatment as a companion and the successful abilities of the most recent The Legend of Zelda games, could, therefore, be just what the character needs. Moreover, with Fi arguably being Link’s oldest and most faithful companion, reuniting the two could also prove to be extremely heartwarming.
