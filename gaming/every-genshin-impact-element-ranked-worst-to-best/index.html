Every Genshin Impact Element, Ranked Worst To Best

SUMMARY

The seven elements in Genshin Impact affect gameplay and mastering them unlocks new opportunities in combat and exploration. The seventh element, Dendro, was added in the 3.0 update, and there are hints of new elements to come. Hydro is considered the best element in the game for its high damage capabilities and supportive abilities.

Literature Review

Genshin Impact’s seven elements are the core pillars of the game, and while each is strong in its own right, there is a ranking they fall under.

Genshin Impact’s seven elements are crucial parts of the game, and although each contributes to gameplay in its own way, there is a hierarchy to them. The seven multi-faceted elements affect numerous parts of the combat and exploration systems, and are some of the first things players should learn to master. Doing so unlocks new opportunities in combat and exploration, making Genshin Impact much easier than it would be otherwise. While learning how each element works alone is essential, it is equally so to understand the reactions and interactions they share with each other. Genshin Impact initially launched with only six elements, but there were signs that a seventh would eventually be added to the title. This seventh element was Dendro, which launched with the 3.0 update that added the Sumeru region. There have since been hints that Genshin Impact will add new elements, such as Light and Dark, as there are hints of their presence in the mystical world of Teyvat - but any playable inclusion of them would likely come far down the line. This leaves the current seven elements as the foundational pillars of the elemental system, each with their own strengths and weaknesses.

Genshin Impact: The Best Characters For New Players Genshin Impact can be intimidating to newcomers. This guide will provide a list of the best characters to unlock in the game for new players.

7 Geo Is The Worst Element In Genshin Impact Best Characters: Zhongli And Noelle

Though it may have some fantastic designs and potential, Geo is undoubtedly the worst element in Genshin Impact. Geo has a single elemental reaction, Crystallize, where most other elements have three or four. Crystallize combines with Cryo, Pyro, Hydro, and Electro, but even so, it’s not all that useful. All Crystallize does is give the player a shield, and it doesn’t last long. With the right build, Genshin Impact’s Geo characters can do the trick. However, apart from all-Geo teams that take advantage of Geo resonance, which increases the party’s shield strength and damage output, they’re not very helpful. The only two outliers in this are Zhongli, the game’s most powerful shielder, and Noelle, who’s good for players just starting out. Exploration is another area where Geo offers little to the player. Outside certain areas where Zhongli and the Geo Traveler can help speed up traveling, no other Geo character offers a beneficial exploration ability. Past that, the best use of Geo abilities is to mass break ores scattered across the world.

6 Electro Is One Of The Weaker Elements Best Characters: Raiden And Fischl

Electro may include some of the most remarkable characters in Genshin Impact, but the element itself feels weak in comparison. It has many reactions, many of which can be convenient in combat. Superconduct deals AoE damage while reducing enemy physical resistance, and Quicken is an important step in inflicting a wide variety of status effects. The problem Electro has lies more in the lack of characters who can use these powerful reactions to their full potential - heavy hitters like Raiden or Fischl are usable in most teams, but others like Yae Miko or Beidou require more niche comps. Many Electro characters saw a rise in popularity with the inclusion of Dendro, which created more reactions for Electro, but it still falls short of other elements. In regard to exploration, Electro offers slightly more than Geo since Sumeru’s addition. Before, the only use for Electro was solving the puzzles sprinkled around Inazuma, but now Electro also helps navigate around the lush landscape of Sumeru. Outside those two uses, Electro doesn’t offer much more in terms of overall exploration of Teyvat.

5 Anemo Is Highly Flexible Best Characters: Venti, Kazuha, And Sayu

Anemo is undoubtedly the most flexible element in Genshin Impact, playing well with other elements while having some benefits on its own. The Swirl reaction, which does elemental damage, synergizes incredibly well with Cryo, Pyro, Hydro, and Electro and has led to the high standings of great supports like Venti and Kazuha. Anemo also has excellent crowd-control capabilities that speed up combat against multiple enemies, eventually becoming a crucial part of clearing the higher floors of the Spiral Abyss. Overall, Anemo can be a vital part of most team comps, but still relies on the strengths of other elements. In addition, Anemo just happens to be the best exploration element in the game. Anemo resonance gives reduced stamina composition and increased movement speed, making it the best for quick travel in any region. This has made characters like Sayu and Kazuha great for moving around the world. Anemo is also used in solving many of Genshin Impact’s original puzzles that are hidden throughout the world.

4 Cryo Is Good, But Hasn’t Gotten Any Better Best Characters: Ayaka, Ganyu, And Kaeya

Fundamentally, there is nothing wrong with Cryo, as it is home to many of the game’s most used characters and deals plenty of damage. The popularity of characters like Ayaka, Ganyu, and Kaeya is a testament to this fact. Cryo’s reactions are also very useful in their own right, especially Superconduct and its defense reduction, or Frozen and its immobilizing crowd control capacity. Cryo’s biggest weakness is that the elements above it have gotten stronger while, for the most part, Cryo has stood still. Exploration-wise, Cryo has one great use that makes it handy for quickly getting to hard-to-reach places. Ice bridging across bodies of water saves stamina and speeds up the whole venture, and is useful across many different regions. This process is excellent for players who have not invested in the Statues of The Seven in Genshin Impact and is one of the chief methods of reaching the Spiral Abyss.

3 Dendro Requires Work, But Is Incredibly Powerful Best Character: Nahida

Genshin Impact’s newest element takes a bit of thinking and building, but once that is done, it becomes one of the strongest in the game. Dendro’s reactions are more complicated than the others, but each is incredibly strong and has allowed other characters to rise back to prominence. Besides that, Dendro can pump out immense damage, and its team comps provide an excellent fun factor. Genshin Impact has many good Dendro characters, but not quite as many as other elements. That’s mostly a result of its newness. However, that problem is solved if players have Nahida. Exploration with Dendro is a bit weird, and can be hit or miss depending on the location. Inside Sumeru, it is crucial to solving puzzles and navigating across the landscape. Outside Sumeru, Dendro has little use in exploration, which is a bit of a letdown considering its potential.

2 Pyro Does High Damage And Is Highly Flexible Best Characters: Hu Tao And Yoimiya

Since the beginning, Pyro has often been the most popular element when it comes to dealing damage. Initially, Klee and Diluc dominated the fiery element, but in recent versions, it has moved to Hu Tao and Yoimiya. Pyro’s three significant reactions, Vaporize, Melt, and Overload, are some of the easiest to use and understand, but also the most robust in the game, making them a large part of Pyro’s strength. Pyro has also been blessed with insanely strong character kits, making the element even better, even if indirectly. Sadly, Pyro has no exploration capabilities or benefits to be used in traveling. In fact, one could argue Pyro is a hindrance to exploration, as lighting oneself on fire and having health tick away becomes an annoyance. However, this is a small setback in the grand scheme of things, so it’s not like avoiding Pyro during exploration is a necessity.

1 Hydro Is The Best Element In Genshin Impact Best Characters: Furina, Neuvillette, And Yelan

While it was once one of the weaker elements, Hydro is now Genshin Impact’s best element. It has high damage capabilities, especially in the hands of characters like Furina and Neuvillette, while also having the supportive abilities of Mona, Xingqiu, and Yelan. All of Hydro’s reactions are strong, and it can even be used to trigger Vaporize, which increases the damage of the attack that inflicts the status effect. Vaporize is even stronger if Hydro is the one that activates it, doubling damage instead of multiplying it by 150%. Hydro and its characters continue to dominate the combat aspect of Genshin Impact. Hydro may not have the same exploration buffs that Anemo has, but it is still useful. Yelan and Mona have untargetable dashes that make avoiding enemies a breeze. Meanwhile, Barbara and Kokomi can be portable healers that, along with being better than food, prevent needing to teleport to a statue to heal. The seven elements and their reactions are one of the core features this gacha game has been built around, and they have proved their worth for that. Every element in the game is fun and usable, and at the end of the day, that is what’s most important in a video game. However, some elements are better than others, and in the interest of optimization, it’s important to know how Genshin Impact’s elements rank from worst to best.
