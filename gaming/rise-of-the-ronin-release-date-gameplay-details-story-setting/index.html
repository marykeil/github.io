Rise Of The Ronin Release Date, Gameplay Details, & Story Setting

SUMMARY

Rise of the Ronin is an action RPG game set in 19th century Japan, offering an open world and intense combat. The game releases on PlayStation 5 on March 22, 2024, competing with other highly anticipated titles. The game emphasizes choice and allows players to shape the story’s outcome, with diverse combat styles and traversal options.

Literature Review

Here’s everything to know about Rise of the Ronin, an upcoming action RPG that takes players to an intriguing period of Japanese history.

Rise of the Ronin is an upcoming game that explores the path of a lone warrior in 19th century Japan, and it definitely has the potential to be a memorable action RPG experience. Developed by Team Ninja, Rise of the Ronin builds on the studio’s trademark polished action that’s been honed across the course of games like Nioh, Wo Long: Fallen Dynasty, and Ninja Gaiden. This time, it also shakes things up by taking that gameplay to an open world setting that focuses on mobility and exploration in addition to the usual intense showdowns.

Rise of the Ronin was originally announced in September 2022 as a PlayStation 5 title, following up on a pattern of Sony exclusivity that applied to both of the Nioh games. While those games helped bolster the PlayStation 4’s appeal as the home of Bloodborne and other challenging Soulslikes, Rise of the Ronin could attract fans of games like Ghost of Tsushima and the Assassin’s Creed series. Team Ninja isn’t turning its back on the hardcore fans, however, offering the choice of a higher difficulty setting that’s intended to recapture the difficulty that the studio is known for.

When Rise Of The Ronin Releases

Rise of the Ronin releases for PlayStation 5 on March 22, 2024, a date initially confirmed by the title’s 2023 Game Awards trailer. It’s landing in a spot that goes toe-to-toe with some tough competition. The PC port of Horizon Forbidden West launches one day ahead on March 21, and Capcom’s big action RPG venture Dragon’s Dogma 2 shares the date of March 22. On the Switch, Princess Peach Showtime is also squeezing into the same busy date, although its vastly different points of appeal make it a somewhat less direct competitor.

The game is currently available for pre-order both digitally on the PlayStation 5 and through physical retailers, with several bonuses on offer for anyone who wants to lock in a purchase ahead of time. These are all focused around getting early access to combat styles and equipment that presumably unlock later in the game otherwise. Like most flagship PlayStation 5 games, pre-ordering the standard edition comes to a total of $69.99, with no option or upcharge for any early access scenario.

Opting for the Rise of the Ronin Digital Deluxe Edition does raise the price to $79.99 in exchange for a share of goodies, and these ones don’t seem to be available in-game through other means. These include a staff, paired swords, and both an armor and formal wear set. In addition to the in-game rewards, the Digital Deluxe Edition also unlocks a digital art book and soundtrack that can likewise be accessed on the PlayStation 5. Physical collectors are out of luck, as there’s no option for a shelf-friendly collector’s edition.

Rise Of The Ronin Gameplay Details

Combat is a big forcus of Rise of the Ronin, and it centers around fast-paced dueling that makes heavy use of timed parries and counters. Different combat styles that have varying degrees of efficacy against specific enemies can be swapped on the fly. The more grounded historical aspects of the game place the focus on fighting human enemies rather than the more fantastical foes that appear throughout the Nioh games. Traditional Japanese swords and spears are Rise of the Ronin staples, but the game also takes advantage of the cultural and technological shifts of the era to provide diverse options.

Guns can be used in various ways, like dueling with a musket’s bayonet before pulling back to fire or whipping out a pistol for a finishing shot. An inventor character named Igashichi can supply some more fanciful choices, such as a proto-flamethrower to roast enemies or set off explosions. Gadgets likewise play a role in traversal, and zipping across rooftops with a grappling hook chains into glider flight that makes it easy to move across a city at high speeds.

Rise Of The Ronin’s Story And Setting

Rise of the Ronin takes place in the Bakumatsu period of Japanese history, a period ranging from the early 1850s to the late 1860s that saw Japan undergo major shifts in government and foreign policy. The arrival of Commodore Matthew Perry in 1853 marked a turning point in Japan’s generally isolationist approach, leading to 1854’s Treaty of Kanagawa and 1858’s Harris Treaty that opened select ports to American ships, first for fuel and provisions and then for trade. Subsequent economic instability and friction between political factions led to the collapse of the Tokugawa shogunate and beginning of the Meiji Restoration.

The civil war that precipitated this transition is the focus of Rise of the Ronin, which puts the spotlight on the factions struggling for control at the end of the Bakumatsu era. Following a masterless ronin gives the game the opportunity to examine the conflict from multiple angles, and trailer footage showing the port city of Yokohama in 1858 makes it clear that some of the surrounding context will also make the cut. Rise of the Ronin emphasizes choice, so the decisions that a player makes can plot a unique course for Japanese history.

Rise of the Ronin is an ambitious project for Team Ninja, but it doesn’t look like it’s abandoning the elements that the studio has refined in its other games. If the open-world progression and story prove to be compelling, the interesting setting and challenging combat are unlikely to be serious letdowns. It isn’t long now before Rise of the Ronin will make its debut on the PlayStation 5, bringing a unique window into Japanese history to the gaming market.
