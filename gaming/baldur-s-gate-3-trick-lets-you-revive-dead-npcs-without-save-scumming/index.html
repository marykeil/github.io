Baldur s Gate 3 Trick Lets You Revive Dead NPCs Without Save Scumming

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Without save scumming an NPC getting killed in Baldur’s Gate 3 is permanent. Siding with Sovereign Glut in the Underdark gives access to his Animating Spores ability, allowing the revival of certain NPCs as powerful minions. Glut’s ability is only available during part of the game, so parties should make use of it until better spells become available.

Literature Review

Save scumming isn’t possible in Honour Mode, so here’s an alternative trick that can revive dead NPCs in Baldur’s Gate 3 without extra save files.

Baldur’s Gate 3 has a popular tactic called save scumming that makes it so choices don’t necessarily matter. However, Honour Mode effectively removed this strategy by giving the party a single save file to work with for the entire campaign. This means that parties need to get creative if they want to help NPCs cheat death, or find ways to bring those NPCs back from the dead.

Save scumming is the practice of creating a save file before important fights or dice rolls, then going back to that save file if Tav receives an unfavorable outcome during that fight or doesn’t succeed on the roll. In modes other than Honour Mode, there’s nothing stopping parties from making extra save files that could be used to save scum, since they aren’t limited to a single file. However, even parties using Baldur’s Gate 3’s other difficulties might make it a personal challenge to avoid save scumming by finding new ways to work around any unfavorable outcomes.

Reviving NPCs In Baldur’s Gate 3 In An Overlooked Way

At the end of Act 1, the party has two options for how to reach Act 2: the Underdark or the Mountain Pass. It’s possible to go through both areas for the content in them, then choose one as an entrance to the Shadow-cursed Lands. Parties that go through the Underdark will likely come across the Myconid Colony, where there’s a bit of tension between Sovereign Spaw and Sovereign Glut. While this tension can be ignored, the party can benefit by choosing to side with one or the other.

Siding with Sovereign Glut provides the party access to his Animating Spores ability, but only while the Underdark is available. This means that once the party enters Shadowfell, they can no longer return to the Underdark and make use of Glut’s revival ability. After that point, Tav has to rely on animate dead and Fungal Infestation if they want to revive NPCs who aren’t companions, in which case they’d be resurrected by Withers instead. Revived NPCs also don’t gain any dialogue options or give the party a chance to complete quests they might’ve been given if the NPCs weren’t killed.

Using Glut’s Power After Siding With Him

When killing the Duergar at Glut’s request, he accompanies the party as a follower, giving Tav the chance to use Animating Spores. Animating Spores’ greatest strength is that it can be used every time Glut’s turn comes around. Then, after overthrowing Spaw, Tav can bring corpses to Glut to have them revived. NPCs that Glut can reanimate include Priestess Gut, Dror Ragzlin, Bulette, Kith’rak, the Inquisitor, and more. These revived characters also keep at least some of their abilities, making them powerful minions.

Avoid dropping corpses right in front of Glut, as this has a chance to make him hostile toward the party.

However, Glut’s reanimation ability isn’t a resurrection like Withers’ power. If Tav brings a character like Minthara to Glut, he won’t revive her. So, parties that might’ve killed her in Honour Mode that now want to find a way to have her as an ally at their camp would be out of luck here. For that, Tav would need to follow the current method to recruit Minthara in Baldur’s Gate 3, which can now be done without sacrificing the Emerald Grove and giving up Halsin, Karlach, and Wyll as companions.

Like Minthara, there are other NPCs that Tav and their party encounter in Act 1 and Act 2 that can’t be revived by Glut’s power. Among them are generally creatures that are important to the story, or those that don’t fall into the category of being humanoids. So, Death Shepherds, which are already undead, can’t be revived by Glut. And characters like Elminster can’t be revived either, which is understandable given his importance in the lore of BG3 and Dungeons and Dragons. It’s also possible that he doesn’t have any combat abilities programmed into the game, since he isn’t generally part of combat.

Glut’s Animating Spores Can’t Be Used After Act 1

Right now, there’s no way built into BG3 to naturally recruit Glut to the party and bring him along for the rest of the game. The only option would be to use mods or find glitches that have Glut tag along with the party. However, the risk to those options is that it’s possible for Glut to not function properly, since he’d be dragged into places and circumstances that he wasn’t intended to be part of. As such, it’s best to use his ability while it’s available, then switch to animate dead when it’s available.

An alternative to Glut’s ability that’s especially strong would be a wizard with the School of Necromancy as their subclass. This can be achieved through respeccing Gale or another Origin character, creating a wizard Tav, or getting a Hireling from Withers and making them into a School of Necromancy wizard. Otherwise, a wizard of any subclass with the animate dead ability learned through a scroll still has benefits, even if they don’t match the quality of undead minions that come from the School of Necromancy.

Glut’s Power Is A Nice Trick In Early Game

Glut can be used to give Tav and their party a decent power boost to get through a chunk of the Underdark, and provide access to undead minions with his spores that might make the difference between dying and surviving some of the tougher fights. After leaving the Underdark, it becomes bothersome to grab corpses and haul them back to Glut for a revival instead of using animate dead. But animate dead takes over in terms of power and accessibility shortly after the party leaves the Underdark, especially if there’s a School of Necromancy wizard in the party.

Baldur’s Gate 3 is full of hidden tricks that can be crucial for completing runs, particularly for parties that take up the challenge of Honour Mode. But even when playing on regular difficulties, it can be more satisfying to make it through all three acts without save scumming for a second chance to survive a battle or succeeding on a dice roll. Since Glut’s ability is only available during part of the game, it’s great to use it until upgrading to better spells in Baldur’s Gate 3.
