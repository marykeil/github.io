10 Best Rare Items You Probably Missed In Baldur s Gate 3 (& How To Get Them)

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1900 words 9 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Literature Review

There are so many items in Baldur’s Gate 3 that it is easy to miss a lot of secrets. There are some top-tier weapons and armor hidden in the game.

There are so many items in Baldur’s Gate 3 that you can be on your fourth or fifth playthrough and still find new weapons and equipment. There are a few items that only a small selection of players have managed to collect, whether due to their inconspicuous locations or the difficult process involved in obtaining the item. Some of these secret items are worth sidetracking.

One Highly-Overlooked Baldur’s Gate 3 Action Will Add Flavor To Any Campaign One action in Baldur’s Gate 3 can make a huge difference, but it may be difficult to come by. Here’s the most underrated action, and where to find it.

10 Worgfang (Act 1)

The Worgfang dagger is a simple weapon, dealing 1d4 Piercing damage with every strike. The dagger’s charm shines through with its special feature, Goblin’s Undoing. When Goblins attempt to attack the wielder, they’ll get Disadvantage on their Attack Rolls. This is great for when the Tav and their party are trekking through the Goblin Camp in Act 1. If you want this Goblin-resistant dagger, head to the Worg Pens in the Goblin Camp, where Halsin is trapped in his bear form. There is a pile of bones at X: 386, Y: -15 you can loot through. Wargfang rests within the pile, ready to use against the goblins tormenting Halsin nearby. While you may not keep this dagger around when you’re done with the Goblin Camp, it’s still a great companion for this stretch of the campaign.

9 Ring of Poison Resistance (Act 1)

The Ring of Poison Resistance is another rather simple piece of equipment for the early game, but useful when you come across enemies with a passion for Poison attacks. The ring grants Resistance to Poison damage. Poison attacks are common among magical characters like Wizards or any Green Dragonborn you may just so happen to piss off. The ring is often lumped in with the Worgfang because both are found around the Worg Pens, just outside the area. You’ll need to open up a nearby sarcophagus and loot the skeleton inside to collect the ring. The sarcophagus can be found at X: 338, Y: 14.

8 Club of Hill Giant Strength (Act 1)

This Club of Hill Giant Strength lives up to its name with its 1d4 Bludgeoning damage and its incredible perk. With this club, the holder’s strength is increased to 19. Considering the club increases its own damage based on a Strength modifier, this is a beautiful Act 1 weapon for character builds with a reliance on Strength like the Barbarian class. It may not look fancy, but it sure is useful. If you want this Uncommon weapon, hidden on the highest floor of Arcane Tower, you’ll need to fix the Arcane Tower elevator in Baldur’s Gate 3. Doing so will allow you unfettered access to the area. Head to the top floor and look for a toppled stool around X: -30, Y: -272. This is the Stool of Hill Giant Strength. If you or anyone in your party sits on the stool, they’ll comment on the sense of power they get from the seat. Destroy the stool to obtain the Club of Hill Giant Strength.

7 Spineshudder Amulet (Act 2)

The Spineshudder Amulet is a pretty necklace studded with blue gems that works well with spellcaster characters. With this Amulet equipped, whenever the wearer hits someone with a Ranged Spell Attack, they’ll get two turns of Reverberation. Reverberation applies a -1 penalty for Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution Saving Throws for each turn inflicted on them. If they can get five turns of Reverberation, they’ll take 1d4 Thunder damage and fall Prone if they fail a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. While Reverberation doesn’t affect those with Thunder damage Immunity, it’s still a great effect. This amulet is notoriously easy to miss when you get to the upper floor of Moonrise Towers in Act 2. When you enter Isobel’s old room full of Selûnite items, you’ll see a chest at X: -171 Y: -195 which turns out to be a Mimic. If you want the Spineshudder Amulet, you’ll need to kill this Mimic. For a long time, the amulet only dropped if you one-shotted the Mimic, but this glitch was fixed in Patch #5.

10 Baldur’s Gate 3 Dice Roll Buffs To Increase Your Chances Of Succeeding Baldur’s Gate 3 has a number of ways to provide buffs that help tip the dice in the party’s favor. Here are some of the best and easiest to use.

6 Smuggler’s Ring (Act 1)

The Smuggler’s Ring is a Rogue’s best friend and a necessary part of the best Rogue builds in Baldur’s Gate 3. With this triangular ring equipped, you’ll get +2 Stealth and +2 Sleight of Hand. Considering these are two of the most important skills a Rogue could know, you’ll want your go-to pickpocket to wear this item. It will also give then -1 Charisma, but that is a small price to pay for a great Rogue. The Smuggler’s Ring is hidden within The Risen Road at X: 58, Y: 512. Head to the broken bridge and go to the path following the river. There is a skeleton stuck inside a bush nearby. This skeleton has the Smuggler’s Ring in its possession. It’s easy to walk past, considering the size of The Risen Road, but it is worth the stop if you want a good Rogue in your party.

5 Everburn Blade (Act 1)

The Everburn Blade is more well-known than other items on this list, but can still be difficult to obtain. Dealing 2d6 Slashing damage and 1d4 Fire damage, this blade is, as the name implies, ever-burning. The blade is constantly on fire, bestowing Fire damage with every attack. It is considered one of the best greatswords in Baldur’s Gate 3 and a great weapon for Karlach, Lae’zal, or a melee-oriented Tav. If you want to give this prized greatsword to your party, however, you’ll need to beat Commander Zhalk in Baldur’s Gate 3. Commander Zhalk appears on the crashing Nautiloid at the very beginning of the game. You’ll find him at the helm, fighting a Mindflayer. Considering this is the very beginning of the game, many players simply activate the transponder on the other side of the room and move on. However, if you can take down Commander Zhalk before the ship crashes, you can get his Everburn Blade.

4 Band of the Mystic Scoundrel (Act 3)

The Band of the Mystic Scoundrel is a Rare ring that fits characters with a love of Illusion or Enchantment spells. Slip on this gem-studded ring and once you’ve hit a creature with a simple weapon attack, you can cast an Illusion or Enchantment spell as a Bonus action. This is a great way to pull out a combat-oriented spell like Mirror Image or Vicious Mockery when you’re low on actions. Getting this ring requires upsetting Akabi, a djinni working at the Circus of the Last Days found in Rivington during Act 3. You’ll need to win Akabi’s jackpot in Baldur’s Gate 3 by pickpocketing his magic ring. Upon winning the jackpot, he will teleport you to the Jungle of Chult. While you’re there, head to X: -1566, Y: -1522. There should be a cliff above an alioramus den where a skeleton sits under a canvas shelter. Loot the backpack by the skeleton to obtain the Band of the Mystic Scoundrel.

3 Svartlebee’s Woundseeker (Act 1)

Svartlebee’s Woundseeker seems like a typical greatsword at first glance, dealing 2d6 + 1 Slashing damage. However, if this greatsword just so happens to strike an already injured enemy, their attack rolls will get a +1d4 bonus. Because of this, Svartlebee’s Woundseeker is great to use in long boss battles for any character proficient with a greatsword. As you rescue citizens from the burning inn at Waukeen’s Rest in The Risen Road, there will be an NPC, Gauntlet Yeva, holding open a door. She is the original owner of Svartlebee’s Woundseeker. If you want to peacefully take this greatsword, cast Command (Drop) on her as she holds the door open. She’ll drop Svartlebee’s Woundseeker, leaving it free for the taking. Make sure you take her greatsword before you leave Waukeen’s Rest, as you won’t see Yeva again for a while.

2 Hellfire Engine Crossbow (Act 3)

The Hellfire Engine Crossbow is a beast of a weapon with 1d10 + 2 Piercing damage and a magical appearance. It is a Heavy Crossbow that allows the wielder to cast Lightning Arrow once per Long Rest. Even if you don’t want to use a bulky crossbow as a martial ranged weapon, it sells for up to 960 gp, so you might as well craft it. Yes, unlike other weapons on this list, you have to craft the Hellfire Engine Crossbow. First, you’ll need to get into the Steel Watch Foundry in the Lower City of Baldur’s Gate itself. Once inside, you need to collect three items scattered around the main floor of the foundry; the Steel Watcher Arm at X: -280 Y: -165, the Targeting Module at X: -307 Y: -157, and the Watcher Crossbow Blueprint at X: -330 Y: -157 in the Security Office. You can then combine these pieces at a table in the Security Office to obtain the Hellfire Engine Crossbow.

10 Baldur’s Gate 3 Inventory Management Tricks To Make Your Life Easier With so much cool loot to find in Baldur’s Gate 3 keeping on top of the party’s inventory can become a full time job, luckily these tips will help.

1 Bonespike Gloves (Act 3)

The Bonespike Gloves are part of a great set scattered around the world. With these gloves equipped, your attacks ignore Resistances to Slashing, Piercing, or Bludgeoning damage. This bonus effects both melee and ranged weapons, making it a great fit for almost any weapon-wielding character. You may not get it until Act 3 of Baldur’s Gate 3, but it is worth the wait.

How To Get The Bhaalist Armor Set In Baldur’s Gate 3 Those who accept dark urges to murder in Baldur’s Gate 3 have a chance to get the Bhaalist Armor set, which has magical properties to help in combat.

The Bonespike Gloves show up as you journey to the Bhaal Temple through the Undercity Ruins. As you progress through the quest “Get Orin’s Netherstone”, you should get jumped by a character named Strangler Luke, a Human cultist. He drops the Bonespike Gloves upon defeat, but you have to fight him prior to fighting Farslayer of Bhaal Ghislev. If you complete his trial and defeat this boss, Strangler Luke will despawn and the gloves with him. Therefore, if you want this rare item in Baldur’s Gate 3, you’ll need to pace yourself.
