10 Hidden Features In Suicide Squad KTJL That Will Improve Your Experience

SUMMARY

Literature Review

There are many additional features in Suicide Squad: KTJL that will improve players’ overall experiences, but not all of them are obvious.

While Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League has a tutorial for each character, and can provide prompts to let players know what to do, there are many additional features in the game that will improve players’ experiences. These could be as simple as changing settings in the game’s menu, or simply selecting specific objectives to suit a preferred playstyle. Despite taking place in the same universe as the Batman: Arkham games, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is a different kind of game. Rocksteady has eschewed the melee and stealth-focused gameplay of its previous DC titles to create a looter-shooter title that can be played solo or in teams of up to four players online, and therefore, some of the franchises’ pre-existing players may not be as familiar with these mechanics. With some of the customization Rocksteady has included, however, it’s not hard to pick up Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League and tailor it to suit an individual’s preferences.

10 Customize Suicide Squad: KTJL’s HUD To Reduce Clutter Rocksteady Addressed A Major Pre-Launch Concern

Before Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s launch, clips began to surface from the closed Alpha playtest and a press preview event that drew a lot of negative attention due to how cluttered the screen seemed. Throughout the game, the HUD shows the mini-map, aiming reticule, and health bars for enemies and players, as is fairly standard for a shooter game. However, the screen can also be filled with other messages informing players how many collectibles have been picked up, what their other teammates’ scores are, mission objectives, combo multipliers, or repeatedly showing “Dynamic tutorial” messages for special moves, despite players likely mastering these early on. Fortunately, Rocksteady took this criticism on board and added the option to customize or completely remove the HUD via the Interface tab in the Settings menu. Each of the aforementioned options can be toggled on and off at any time, alongside several other features, and players can pick and choose if they want features like tutorials at first to ease them into the key gameplay, but don’t want the likes of damage numbers flying out of their opponents from the start. Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League also doesn’t have a Photo Mode as of the time of writing, so switching off the HUD is the best way to get cleaner, action-packed screenshots of the game.

9 Changing Camera Settings Can Improve Outdoors Combat Seeing More Of Metropolis At Once Is Extremely Useful

Another key feature within the Settings menu that will improve the gameplay experience is increasing the Field of View and turning off Camera Shake and Motion Blur in the Video tab. Although the closer camera is more common for the story-driven action-adventure genre of the other Batman: Arkham games, with the amount of enemies that can be shooting at players at once across the Metropolis map in Suicide Squad, seeing more of an area gives players a great advantage. This is also the case with boss battles with Flash and Superman, as they move around at such a fast speed that they can be harder to keep track of with a smaller field of view. It is also easier to find the paths to an incubator’s connected crystals when clearing out a Bug Nest, especially with the crystals that can reactivate if players don’t clear them all within a certain time. For a character like Deadshot who can provide aerial support with his jetpack, this wider view of a whole area can be optimized with a sniper to get a better lay of the land and pick off enemies with ease.

8 Check Upcoming Talent Choices For Each Character Planning Ahead And Not Bailing On A Character Too Soon Can Help In The Long Run

It can be easy to write off a character too early if certain elements don’t seem to suit a preferred playstyle. However, every potential Talent can be seen from the moment Hack makes this tab available to players, long before they may be able to unlock them. Players should use this to their full advantage and check each character’s full Talent tree perks to plan ahead, as what may be annoying earlier in the game such as Deadshot’s jetpack running out of fuel quickly, could be fixed by a Talent (in this case, one that unlocks around Level 6 for the character) that increases the Traversal meter and slows down the rate in which it runs out.

7 Respec Specific Talents At Any Time Suicide Squad: KTJL Doesn’t Penalize Players For Changing Their Minds

Hack’s Talents upgrades are made up of three trees per character, each with 10 levels. While some of these levels only offer one upgrade, others can see players choosing between up to three different options. Fortunately, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League lets players respec and fine-tune their specific Talents at any time. Unlike some other games, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League also doesn’t penalize players for doing so, as there’s no cost and no limit on how many times this can be done, enabling players to tweak their skills if they find their preferred playstyle tends to be changing to favor other tactics or weapons as they progress in the game. Once in the Talents menu, simply select “Edit Talents” and select a different one for any level in the three talent trees.

6 Keep An Eye On Who Gets Psyched Up Perks Early In The Game But Be Wary Of Playing As A Weaker Character In The Mid-To-Late Chapters

When going to play a story or Support Squad mission, one of Task Force X’s members will essentially state that they want to take charge. A notification will appear to let players know that, if selected, that character will be “Psyched Up”. Swapping to the highlighted character can provide certain perks such as XP, and also ensures that they take less damage during the mission in question. While these are useful earlier in the game for balancing out characters, and they do provide a lot of much-needed XP upon completion, players can find themselves opting into a harder experience later in the game should they blindly follow these prompts. Other characters don’t level up alongside the selected character, so unless players constantly switch between all four characters outside of missions, they can end up playing as a much weaker character in the mid or late section of the game if they just follow the Psyched Up prompts after spending a lot of time as their favorite character.

5 Use Secondary Weapons As Stat Sticks Even If There’s No Intention Of Using Them In Combat

Even if players find their ideal weapon type and upgrade it or select more powerful versions of it, there is still plenty of use for other weapons in a secondary role, primarily as Stat Sticks. Equipping certain secondary weapons with additional passive perks can boost players’ general stats, even if they never intend to use that weapon in combat. This ensures that players get all the bonuses of using a variety of weapon types while sticking with the one that best suits them.

4 Assign More Powerful Weapons To AI Teammates Players’ Loadouts Aren’t The Only Ones That Matter

The unused weapons can be used to further benefit the whole team when playing with AI teammates. Assigning he weapons players don’t want to other AI teammates can create more powerful synergized attacks. For example, if the player prefers to have King Shark as their main character with a minigun, but acquire an extremely powerful, Infamy-level sniper, it’s best to give it to a character like Deadshot who is better suited to sniper combat to make the overall team more powerful in combat. Some characters may equip more powerful weapons automatically if the option to “Auto Equip Gear” is turned on in the Loadout tab. This can be toggled on and off to allow more control over this.

3 Slides Make Suicide Squad: KTJL’s Traversal Far More Fluid Sliding Also Grants Additional Perks

Although Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s tutorial covers sliding when landing, there are many more benefits to doing so that shouldn’t be overlooked. Slides make traversal far more fluid in general, which is a huge bonus considering the size of the Metropolis map. For example, when landing into a sliding maneuver, not only can players keep moving, but it resets the cooldown on the Traversal Meter and can add an extra boost, extending the time before it next runs out. If players chain this with boosted wall runs - which also reset the Traversal Meter - they can get across Metropolis quickly without going near street level.

2 Don’t Manually Grab Every Resource Resources Are Automatically Added To Inventories When killing an enemy type such as a Tank, Helicopter, or Drone in Metropolis, several glowing resources will be seen falling to the ground. Fortunately, players don’t need to rush to them to collect them immediately afterward and can continue exploring the city or mission as planned. A notification will likely appear on the bottom right of the screen informing the player that these items and resources have been collected automatically and have been added to the inventory. It’s worth noting this does not apply to ammo, grenades or Damage boosters. Make sure to keep an eye on the inventory to ensure that it doesn’t fill up automatically through this feature.

1 Don’t Worry About Playing Every Contract Pick Contracts Best Suited To A Preferred Playstyle

During Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s story, Hack will open up separate communications to the Support Squad. This lets Task Force X accumulate rewards for doing side objectives called “Contracts”. These can be selected from the Contracts tab in the main menu, or a shortcut window on the map. Most contracts involve tasks that can be done passively while exploring the game or progressing through its story or side missions. These involve killing a certain amount of enemies, completing Critical Reloads (which are similar to the reload function in Gears of War that players should use anyway to speed up reload times), destroying Rooftop Emplacements, and more. While many will feel they have to do every contract available, this is not the case. Some contracts require killing enemies with a certain weapon type which may not suit a players’ preferred playstyle, for example, if a contract wants a certain amount of kills with a pistol or SMG, and players have a Deadshot sniper build set up as their primary. If these appear, there will often be a different contract available that’s more passive to any play style, such as Localized Kills, which involves simply killing a certain number of enemies in one of Metropolis’ districts. These will often offer a similar kind of payout in terms of rewards, and after a day, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s Contracts tab will reset and any that aren’t selected as Active will be reset anyway.

Suicide Squad Kill The Justice League
Set in the Arkham game universe, Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is an action third-person shooter live-service game from the developers at Rocksteady. Players will choose from one of four members in the Suicide Squad, headed by Amanda Waller, as they’re forced to take on their most difficult challenge yet - to face a corrupted slate of the world’s greatest heroes, including the Flash, Batman, and Superman. The game allows for teams of up to four players as characters collect gear to improve their chances of survival.
