Even Bloober Team Agrees With Silent Hill 2 Remake s Latest Controversies

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 700 words 4 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Developers of the Silent Hill 2 remake acknowledge the flaws in its recent trailer.

Silent Hill 2 ’s Combat Reveal trailer depicts slick combat mechanics that deviate from the original game’s horrifying realism. Bloober Team’s CEO suggests the trailer doesn’t accurately represent the final product.

Literature Review

Bloober Team’s CEO says Silent Hill 2 fans were right to criticize the remake’s recent trailer. Here’s why, and how Bloober plans to improve it.

Without so much as a release date, the Silent Hill 2 remake is already marred by controversy - and as it turns out, even the developers agree. The remake, developed by Bloober Team, is part of a wider attempt by publisher Konami to revive the defunct franchise. It follows the disastrous release of Silent Hill: Ascension and the half-decent preview provided by Silent Hill: The Short Message. The latest trailer for the remake, released as part of January 2024’s PlayStation State of Play, was immediately lambasted on release for failing to understand why people loved the original SIlent Hill 2.

In an interview with Polish investors’ channel inwestorzy.tv (which has since been made private on YouTube, but is partially preserved in a translation by Eurogamer), Bloober Team CEO Piotr Babieno agreed with fan criticisms of the Silent Hill 2 remake trailer. “It’s not the spirit of what used to be, or what we’re creating now,” Babieno says of the trailer. He admits that the trailer is flawed, but emphasizes his and Bloober Team’s desire to “capture the spirit of the game.” However, he assures fans, it’s not totally his fault.

What Was Wrong With The Silent Hill 2 Trailer? Silent Hill 2’s Combat & Konami Controversy Explained

The issues with Silent Hill 2’s most recent trailer begin with its title. It’s a “Combat Reveal” trailer, but combat is far from the most exciting aspect of the original. Silent Hill 2 tells a gut-wrenching story about grief, guilt, and abuse, against the backdrop of a uniquely terrifying survival horror game. And the combat is…there. It’s easy, it’s clunky, and it’s blasé. But the awkwardness is the point. James Sunderland isn’t a badass action hero with elite special forces training like Leon Kennedy in Resident Evil. At least physically, he’s a regular guy trapped in a waking nightmare.

Now compare that to the Silent Hill 2 remake’s Combat Reveal trailer above. In it, James is seen jumping between buildings and lining up headshots on bubble nurses, underscored by an epic battle theme. Monsters previously iconic for their sluggish, ragdoll-like movement mantle obstacles and grapple James, some of them without arms. The awkward realism of Silent Hill 2’s action is part of what makes it so scary, and this trailer suggests the remake eschews that in favor of slick, snappy combat.

But Piotr Babieno says it’s not to be trusted, and blames Konami for representing the project so poorly. Marketing is Konami’s job, Babieno says, and this trailer was made by its team, not Bloober’s. This is the second time Bloober has thrown Konami under the Silent Hill 2 bus, with a post by Bloober Team on X (formerly Twitter) in November 2023 decrying its poor communication with regard to remake news.

So, while there’s clearly some tension between Bloober Team and Konami, Babieno reminds Silent Hill 2 fans not to worry. He promises the final product is different from what’s been presented in early trailers, and urges players to wait until they have the game in hand to judge it. Bloober Team’s priority, he says, is putting out a faithful remake of Silent Hill 2 - the rest is just static.
