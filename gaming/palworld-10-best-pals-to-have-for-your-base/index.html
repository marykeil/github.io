Palworld 10 Best Pals To Have For Your Base

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Literature Review

Out of the 111 current Pals, some are better to work on the base in Palworld that can maximize resources and defend them from harm.

Comparing Palworld to Pokémon is a common critique of the game, but the base building mechanics are much more similar to games like Minecraft, where the player must build up resources in a survival situation. Unlike in Minecraft, players can utilize their Pals to help them generate some of these resources automatically, which is the part that players should look at closest when assembling their working force. Additionally, players should consider what Pals can help defend their base from Raids the best and keep the base going without a lot of oversight.

Palworld Preview: “For All Its Faults, It Teeters on the Edge of Early Greatness” Palworld wears its many inspirations on both sleeves, but this monster-catching open-world survival sim could have some serious legs.

10 Lamball Get the Ball Rolling Early

In terms of early-game help around the base, a Lamball is one of the better options to help you get started. Lamball can help with things like crafting, carrying things to storage chests, providing food through the Berry Plantation and Wheat Farm, and it also provides Wool in Palworld to help you get the cloth you need for all of your outfits, among other necessary items. While it may not stay working on the base for your entire Palworld journey, it will help you get an excellent foundation to work with. Skills Handiwork, Transporting, Farming
If no number is listed after the skills, such as in the example above, the skill level is 1. Otherwise, the skill level is noted in parentheses after the skill, e.g., Farming (2) would be Farming Level 2.

9 Vixy Ranching Out

While most of the Pals who can work the Ranch and drop items from it can only drop one item, Vixy has the potential to drop three. While you may still want a Lamball or Cremis in the Ranch to farm Wool, Vixy will drop Pal Spheres, Gold Coins, and Arrows, making it a valuable way to make money, automate Sphere production, and gain free arrows to help you generate cheaper ammo. Later in the game, the arrows might be less helpful, but Gold Coins and Spheres will always be useful for players to have a steady supply. Skills Gathering, Farming

8 Beegarde A Working Bee

For a jack-of-all-trades who can do a little bit of everything around the Base, a Beegarde is the Pal to get. It can help with almost everything around the Base and will never run out of things to do. Considering it can also drop Honey and do a fair job of protecting the Base in a Raid, the Beegarde could be a great addition to have on the Base. There might be better dedicated workers later on, but having a few workers who can keep themselves busy is always useful. Skills Planting, Handiwork, Lumbering, Medicine Production, Transporting, Gathering, Farming

7 Petallia Plant-based Medicine

Medication, particularly early, takes a long time to craft. This will make a Pal with higher levels of medication production, such as Petallia shown in the Game Guides Channel video above, important. Petallia can help around the base in other ways when it isn’t needed for medication. Flopie and Bristla have the same skills, although at lower levels, so having either of them around until you find and catch a Petallia is a good idea. Vaelet also has a similar skill set but with Level 3 Medicine Production, so if that is more important, pick Vaelet instead. Skills Planting (3), Handiwork (2), Medicine Production (2), Transporting, Gathering (2)

6 Flambelle Get Fired Up

While Flambelle has lower stats than other Pals on this list, what makes them valuable beyond their traits is that they can drop Flame Organs in the Ranch. This means players won’t have to farm or butcher any Pals to acquire Flame Organs to make their fire weapons, such as a Fire Bow and fire ammo. This is especially useful since Fire is a type with multiple elements it is strong against. Flambelle can move its own Flame Organs to a storage chest afterward. Skills Kindling, Handiwork, Transporting, Farming

5 Penking Watered Down

Pengullet and Penking have similar statistics, with Penking having the better statistics. Both of them can help with crafting, moving items, watering plants on a farm, or turning a mill, and if that is not enough, they can also keep your food fresh in a Cooler. Of note, Pengullet can also help with Mining, so it is a good all-rounder who can help out with odd jobs while waiting on the watering tasks that it will be committed to otherwise. Better yet, his skills are excellent at defending the base from enemy attacks. Skills Handiwork (2), Transporting (2), Watering (2), Mining (2), Cooling (2)

4 Warsect Wood Cutting

Warsect will not often be available to players just beginning Palworld, but it will be an excellent member to add to the base. Lumbering is a reasonable and very necessary skill to have, and Warsect can both do logging tasks and transport the wood to chests, so the player does not have to. Until you find a Warsect, Eikthyrdeer is also a notable addition to the base and has stats that can increase the mining potential of other Pals. However, Eikthyrdeer doesn’t have any other skills, so Warsect will be a better worker in the long term. Skills Planting, Handiwork, Lumbering (3), Transporting (3)

3 Mammorest Bigger Is Better

A Mammorest can usually be seen, if not caught, from the game’s starting area. While it may take a player a while to level up to the point where it can be caught, it will be worth it for the defensive abilities Mammorest can bring to the base. The size is a considerable factor, but the added Power Bomb attack is an AOE with devastating effects that can eliminate enemies quickly. Between these, it’s later more powerful moves and being able to mine and log materials, making Mammorest a keeper for the long haul. Skills Planting (2), Lumbering (2), Mining (2)

2 Blazamut Blazing Fury

Kindling will be an important skill for cooking food as well as making ingots, which will be a very important thing to have in the beginning hours of Palworld. Arsox is a good early-game version of this, but Blazamut has the advantage of having a very high score in Kindling and Mining, allowing him to produce resources quickly and efficiently without much fluff. The enhanced Fire skills are also a key part of base defense that will keep your valuable resources safe from Raids. Skills Kindling (3), Mining (4)

1 Grizzbolt Electric Energy

Players will soon need a Pal to generate Electricity to provide Power for the base. Like the Cooler, a Pal working a Power Generator can walk away from the task after charging it, so a Pal that can do other things in the meantime is important. This is where the Lumbering trait is handy, as Grizzbolt can always generate resources. Orserk has a better Electricity Generating level for pure energy production in Palworld. However, the added versatility and its defensive skills make Grizzbolt a slightly better worker for the base. Skills Handiwork (2), Lumbering (2), Transporting (3), Generating Electricity (3)
