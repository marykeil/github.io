How To Get (& Use) The Resonance Stone In Baldurs Gate 3

SUMMARY

Literature Review

The Resonance Stone is a unique purple orb found in Act 2 of Baldur’s Gate 3. This item gives a strange condition with both pros and cons for using.

Steeped in Bliss gives everyone within its range a boost in energy to correspond with their euphoria. However, the condition also makes its victims open to psychic suggestions. Mechanically, the condition provides an advantage for physical checks but a disadvantage for mental Saving Throws and a new vulnerability to Psychic damage. If you manage to collect this simple artifact, you will need to be strategic while using it.

Where To Find The Resonance Stone In Baldur’s Gate 3 (BG3)

You won’t be able to collect the Resonance Stone until Act II of Baldur’s Gate 3. Players should their party into the Mind Flayer Colony during this act while hunting Ketheric Thorm. Upon fighting Ketheric on the top of Moonrise Towers, you will follow him underneath the prison to where the colony awaits. The colony includes a Morgue, a Necrotic Laboratory, a Tadpoling Center (where you can either save or kill Mizora in Baldur’s Gate 3), Barracks, and a boss room where players encounter Ketheric once more.

The Resonance Stone is not a guaranteed item. Some playthroughs of Baldur’s Gate 3 may not include the Resonance Stone as loot. Make sure to search all areas of the Mind Flayer Colony when looking for this item.

The Resonance Stone can spawn in various locations within the Mind Flayer Colony. Some players get the Resonance Stone as loot after fighting a Mind Flayer, whereas other times, the Resonance Stone spawns randomly in the area as a pick-up item. If so, the Resonance Stone is likely near one of the area’s puzzle rooms.

Should You Use The Resonance Stone In Baldur’s Gate 3?

The Resonance Stone takes effect if you have it in your inventory, affecting yourself and all beings in range (except for the undead or constructs). The Advantage it gives on physical checks can be useful if your character and your party prefer to focus on swinging swords and axes about. However, your enemies will also get this bonus when they come in to swing at you.

On the other end, the Resonance Stone will give you a disadvantage on mental Saving Throws in Baldur’s Gate 3, which could lead to death with a poor roll of the dice. Your heightened vulnerability against Psychic damage will make fighting enemies with such attacks difficult. If you want to turn this disadvantage into a battlefield strategy, you can get in range of an enemy and use your own Psychic/mental attacks against them.

For most characters, keeping the Resonance Stone around probably isn’t a good idea. However, if you want to challenge yourself or think you can use the item’s condition in your favor, head to the Mind Flayer Colony and see if it spawned into your current playthrough of Baldur’s Gate 3.
