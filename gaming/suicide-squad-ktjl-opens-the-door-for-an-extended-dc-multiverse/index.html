Suicide Squad KTJL Opens The Door For An Extended DC Multiverse

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League introduces the concept of the multiverse, expanding the scope of the game’s story beyond Arkhamverse. Task Force X faces the challenge of not only stopping Brainiac’s plan to terraform Earth but also defeating 13 Brainiacs across different dimensions known as “Elseworlds.” There is potential for crossover and exploration of other DC universes, including the possibility of connecting the Arkhamverse with James Gunn’s DC Universe and NetherRealm’s Injustice universe.

Literature Review

Although Suicide Squad: KTJL’s story is a continuation of Rocksteady’s Arkhamverse, several plot points open the door to an extended DC Multiverse.

Although Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s story is a continuation of Rocksteady’s Arkhamverse, several plot points open the door to an extended DC Multiverse. The main campaign follows A.R.G.U.S. and Task Force X as they are deployed into Metropolis in the midst of an alien invasion led by Brainiac. In this time, Brainiac has captured and brainwashed 99% of Metropolis’ citizens, including the majority of the Justice League, creating plenty of additional enemies for Task Force X to overcome on their mission to stop Brainiac’s plans to terraform Earth and to create a New Colu.

Colu was home to the most intelligent race in the universe. In the Arkhamverse, this planet has been destroyed, and Brainiac is determined to rebuild the advanced civilization he has lost at any cost.

[Warning: The following article contains major spoilers for Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League]This mission seems straightforward initially, as Amanda Waller puts out a kill order on the Justice League and Brainiac, and it seems like Task Force X’s only challenge will be overcoming some of the DC Universe’s most powerful heroes. However, it soon becomes clear that the scale of this assignment is far larger than it initially seemed when the multiverse is established soon after killing Flash. Task Force X is teleported to another dimension referred to as “Earth 2” in the game. Here, they find a world already decimated by Brainiac, where they find a surviving Lex Luthor. As the Luthor of Task Force X’s main universe - which is deemed “Earth 1” - has been killed, they bring him back to their universe to assist them with their mission and refer to him as “Lex-2”.

Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League’s Ending Explores The Multiverse Post-Launch Content Will Visit New Elseworlds

After successfully killing the Justice League, Brainiac teleports Task Force X aboard his giant Skull ship to meet them face-to-face for the first time. Alongside detailing his plans to create a New Colu, he also tells the team what an asset they could be if they submitted and agreed to fight alongside him. It then becomes clear that Brainiac’s ambition is far greater than terraforming one world, and that he has wider plans to conquer the multiverse.

Task Force X is teleported out of this situation by Lex-2, and explains that there are 13 Brainiacs all connected via a network across various dimensions. While they’re unable to kill the one on their Earth at present, Task Force X do find a way to travel to Earth 2 and capture the Brainiac there, enabling Amanda Waller to kill him with a Dataspike developed by Luthor, which also downloads a fragmented branch of Brainiac’s network through Coluan technology destroys part of Brainiac’s Skull ship in the process.

It’s then explained how Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s endgame will unfold, with the team sent to face the remaining 12 Brainiacs across other dimensions known as “Elseworlds.” The introduction of Elseworlds also opens the door for more characters who have already died in the Arkhamverse to return, albeit as new incarnations. This is the case with Joker, who died in Batman: Arkham City, but will be appearing in a new form from another dimension in the game’s first post-launch chapter.

    Every Character Who Dies In Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League  As is the case with most of the stories centered around Task Force X, Suicide Squad: KTJL sees many characters killed throughout its campaign.

Could Suicide Squad: KTJL Explore Other DC Universes? The DC Multiverse Is An Extremely Vast Place

As established across several comics, TV shows, movies, and games, the DC Multiverse is an extremely vast place. Along with 52 confirmed Earths, there are several other realms where gods and other supreme, immortal beings exist, such as Jack Kirby’s New Gods, Wonder Woman’s Greek-inspired patrons, Neil Gaiman’s Dream of the Endless, and Lucifer Morningstar and his legions of Hell. There’s also the Monitor Sphere, where the Monitors reside and conduct their oversight, the Source Wall, which contains everything in the Multiverse, and much more. There are also stories that fall under DC’s official “Elseworlds” banner, such as Gotham by Gaslight, Kingdom Come and Superman: Red Son, and the Earth One continuity.

The Earth One comics form a different continuity that features re-imagined and modernized versions of popular DC characters such as Batman, Superman, Wonder Woman, Green Lantern, and the Teen Titans. This is separate from the “Earth One” title assigned to the Arkhamverse in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League , as that was just a name given by Lex-2 for the sake of simplicity when explaining the Multiverse.

While not the first time the DC Multiverse was featured - that came in the 1961 story “Flash of Two Worlds” - Marv Wolfman and George Perez’ 1985 comic event, Crisis on Infinite Earths is one of the most famous books in DC history, and one many comic fans immediately think of when discussing the Multiverse in general. Two characters who are present in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League, Brainiac and Lex Luthor, team up in this storyline and gather many of the super villains from across the multiverse, which ultimately fails.

Considering the relationship between the two in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League, however, it’s unlikely this will occur in Rocksteady’s game. After the Arkhamverse’s Lex Luthor died, the Lex Luthor of another Earth assisted Task Force X and worked against Brainiac after seeing what could happen if his invasion and subsequent terraforming were to succeed firsthand.

Crisis on Infinite Earths concluded with the destruction of many Earths across the DC Multiverse, leaving just one remaining in an attempt to simplify the DC Universe for readers. Since then, however, many stories have attempted to explore a wider Multiverse again. With how many DC universes exist again now there’s some great potential for Rocksteady to let players explore some of the more established ones from other media and see how they’d cope with fending off Brainiac’s invasion, as well as the original ones that have already been designed for the game.

    A New Batman: Arkham Game Is Possible After Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League  Despite the destructive storyline in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League, there are still ways for Rocksteady to make a new Batman: Arkham game.

What Other DC Universes Could Be Explored Across The Multiverse? The Arkhamverse’s Task Force X Could Travel To Pre-Existing And Upcoming Universes

Custom image by Glenn Bunn

Brainiac has appeared in recent games such as NetherRealm’s Injustice 2 alongside a “Multiverse” mode that saw players travel through a series of parallel worlds within the DC Universe and battle against variants of some of their previous opponents. Considering Warner Bros. owns both Rocksteady and NetherRealm, this would be an obvious collision point for the two games, as both already feature interdimensional travel in some capacity. It would also be interesting to see how different versions of these characters interact based on their own experiences, such as the Injustice universe’s reformed version of Harley Quinn and her slightly less reformed Arkhamverse counterpart.

There’s also the possibility that Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s Multiversal exploration could see the Arkhamverse interlink with the previously announced games set within James Gunn and Peter Safran’s DC Universe. While nothing has been revealed about these games yet other than the fact they will tie into the shared universe’s movies and shows and will feature the same actors, characters such as Superman have already been confirmed to be a part of this universe with Superman Legacy, which has a release date of July 11, 2025. There was some speculation that Brainiac may even make his cinematic debut in Superman Legacy, but Gunn addressed these rumors on Threads and BlueSky, saying, “Who knows what the future will bring?” before reiterating that right now all he cares about is Superman Legacy, which suggests that a Brainiac appearance may not be on the cards right now.

In a series of Threads comments (which have since been removed from the platform, but have been preserved by DCU Updates on X), Gunn did express support for the Arkhamverse, stating that he is a fan and was looking forward to Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League. Gunn also addressed a fan’s concerns about it being the final Arkhamverse game in the wake of the DC Universe’s upcoming titles and reassured them that there are “No plans of it being the last.” Considering how Brainiac is often one of Superman’s major adversaries, this would be a good way to cross-promote the titles via a potential tie-in that also organically continues Task Force X’s new interdimensional mission.
