There s One Easy Way To Take Out Bosses In Palworld

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Palworld is a hit on Steam’s concurrent session charts despite its glitches and bugs. Tamers have found a creative method using the Pal Sphere glitch to defeat bosses in one hit. Pocketpair plans to address glitches and exploits in future updates, but the Pal Sphere glitch may not be its top priority.

Literature Review

Palworld players have already found countless glitches to use in-game, and this one makes bosses easy, but only to defeat instead of capture.

Palworld is now available on Early Access, and it’s a hit that’s taken over the concurrent session charts on Steam. And although it’s fairly polished, it’s far from being glitch-free. From infinite loading screen bugs to using the guard AI against bosses because they follow a Wanted tamer indefinitely, there’s no shortage of ways to make use of the game’s issues before they get patched out.

It hasn’t taken long for Tamers to find creative ways to take down the bosses featured in Palworld. Considering that these bosses can present a tough fight, it’s understandable that Tamers want to use any methods available to them to make the process of defeating - or capturing - boss Pals easier. The latest method for defeating bosses involves a Pal Sphere glitch and the power of gravity to turn a dangerous battle into a one-hit kill.

Defeating Bosses In Palworld The Easy Way Using The Pal Sphere Glitch

This method seems limited to bosses that have smaller Pals with them and have the ability to leap or jump at the Tamer. The first step is to engage the boss in combat. When the boss is getting ready to leap at the Tamer, position one of the minion Pals between the boss and Tamer, then throw a Pal Sphere at it. The boss needs to leap and land on top of the floating sphere that’s trying to capture a Pal. When the Pal Sphere completes its capture, the boss is shot into the air so high that hitting the ground usually results in a one-hit kill.

Additionally, this trick lets the Tamer capture a few new Pals in Palworld, assuming the Pal Sphere they use successfully holds the Pal long enough to add them to their collection. Timing is essential to pulling off this method, so the Tamer will want to know the boss’ abilities and when it’s likely to use a leaping attack.

The Danger Of Palworld Fall Damage Fall Damage Can Kill A Pal Or Tamer

Fall Damage is a killer force in many titles, and Palworld is just another one among them. But depending on the Tamer, the Pal Sphere glitch isn’t the only way to use Fall Damage as an advantage. Or a disadvantage, depending on how the fight plays out.

Using knockback abilities on a Pal that’s close to a cliff can push it off the edge and cause Fall Damage. Unlike the Pal Sphere glitch, this deals varying amounts of damage depending on how high the cliff is. It could also make it difficult to grab the Pal’s loot at the bottom of the cliff without having a glider or Normal Parachute, which is unlocked at base level 5. Overall, it’s not the best option for fighting, but it might be necessary for a Tamer in early game who finds themselves in a fight with limited resources or against an especially tough Pal.

Farming Bosses In Palworld The Benefits Of Boss Drops

The Pal Sphere glitch can be a particularly useful tool for Tamers looking to farm bosses for their items or experience. But Tamers who want to farm bosses for the sake of collecting them as Pals will want to try other methods, considering this one results in the Pal’s death. While this makes killing bosses quick, it doesn’t make the respawn timer on them move any faster.

              Palword: Every Pal Strengths, Weaknesses, Resistances, & Elements   Pals in Palworld will each have their strengths and weaknesses based on their type, which players will need to take advantage of to form their team.

Every boss in Palworld respawns, so a Tamer accidentally killing one they wanted to collect isn’t that big of a deal. The downside is that the respawn timer for bosses is about an hour. Also, that hour has to be spent in-game for Tamers who are in a solo world instead of a server. The server’s timer continues even if the Tamer who killed the boss logs out. But that isn’t the case for solo Tamers. Tamers in a solo world need to stay in-game for an hour in real-time before the boss respawns.

Being able to farm bosses easily with the Pal Sphere glitch helps Tamers try to get items that have a lower drop rate from those bosses. As an example, one of the bosses that are close to the starting area is the Mammorest. It has a chance to drop a ring that gives resistance to Grass Damage, the Ring of Grass Resistance +2. However, items like that have a much lower drop rate compared to the other possible drops, so Tamers will need to defeat these bosses multiple times if they’re after a specific item.

Pocketpair Addressing Palworld Glitches And What That Means For Tamers

In the latest patches, Pocketpair has mentioned its intent to crack down on the glitches and cheats being exploited by players. While the Pal Sphere glitch mentioned here isn’t exactly game breaking, it will likely be patched out of the game in the future. However, it probably isn’t as high of a priority compared to other glitches and exploits that are more game breaking. This is because the Pal Sphere glitch requires timing, bosses with leap abilities, and a bit of luck to pull off, making it more difficult to use in-game.

              What Is The Highest Level In Palworld?   Survival crafting and creature collecting game Palworld has a level cap for both characters and Pals, and its not the same as in some competitors.

Other Palworld bugs and glitches to be fixed in updates are likely to include tricks that make the game a bit too easy for the survival crafting genre. Glitches that make the game easier include the food glitch that resets the expiration timer on food by swapping it from one container to another, and the glitch that uses the PIDF guards to reset Boss AI and make it easier to capture them with Pal Spheres because the guards follow a Wanted tamer indefinitely.

Palworld has opened its Early Access period to huge success, but it’s not free from glitches that Tamers are exploiting to make the game easier. While not every popular glitch has been patched out yet, Pocketpair’s focus on countermeasures for them will certainly reach the most common ones soon. But until then, it’s worth trying some of them out, like this boss glitch using the Pal Sphere, even just for a bit of fun in Palworld.
