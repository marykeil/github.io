Everything We Know About D&D s New Player s Handbook In 2024

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Dungeons & Dragons 5e is being updated in 2024 for the game’s 50th anniversary, starting with the release of the Player’s Handbook. The release date for the 2024 Player’s Handbook is still unknown, but it may be released on May 21, 2024 according to leaks. The 2024 Player’s Handbook will include changes to classes, spells, abilities, and races (now called species), and will consolidate rules from previous supplements into the core rulebook.

Literature Review

An updated version of the Dungeons & Dragons 5e Player’s Handbook is due out in 2024, and there are a lot of changes heading to the 50-year-old RPG.

Dungeons & Dragons is a living game, meaning that it grows and changes as time passes, and that includes the ever-useful Player’s Handbook. Whether that is from basic DnD to Advanced Dungeons & Dragons to third, fourth, or fifth edition, the game has never stayed the same for long. The last full edition of DnD, 5e, was released in 2014 after being playtested under the name D&D Next.

Now, a decade later in 2024, Dungeons & Dragons 5e is being updated just in time for the RPG’s 50th anniversary. Playtesting has been going on under the codename One D&D since 2022. The first book of the 2024 update is going to be the Player’s Handbook (or PHB), and it will be followed quickly by a new Monster Manual and Dungeon Masters Guide.

D&D 2024 Player’s Handbook Potential Release Date & Price

It appears that the 2024 Player’s Handbook may be released on May 21, 2024. Tentative dates for all of 2024’s DnD books were mistakenly posted on social media during PAX Unplugged in late 2023. These posts were subsequently pulled, so the actual date for the 2024 Player’s Handbook is still officially unknown, and may be subject to change. What is known, however, is that the Player’s Handbook is the first of the core rulebooks slated to come out.

Wizards of the Coast’s online store currently sells the 2014 digital/physical bundle for $59.95, and the 2024 update will likely be around the same price. There will most certainly be a D&D Beyond version of the rulebook, probably priced near $29.95 to maintain consistency. It is also likely that other virtual tabletop platforms like Roll20 will have the new 2024 Player’s Handbook adapted and for sale around the same price the current one sells for.

What Is In the Player’s Handbook 2024?

The 2024 PHB will contain the same information that Player’s Handbooks have always had. There will be character classes, spells, abilities, items, combat rules, races (which are going to be called species in the 2024 edition, according to One D&D playtest packets), and more. As always, the Player’s Handbook will be the one book that is indispensable to have at the table.

The Player’s Handbook, Dungeon Master’s Guide, and Monster Manual are together considered DnD’s core rulebooks, providing rules on everything a group needs to run a campaign.

What Changes Are In The New Player’s Handbook?

The point in releasing a new Player’s Handbook, however, are the changes included. Some of the most radical appear to be alterations to familiar classes, such as druids and rangers. No class will be untouched, though, with spells being broken into Arcane, Divine, and Primal categories instead of simple class-based lists, as well as martial characters gaining a new system called Weapon Mastery. There are also tweaks to how abilities like the barbarian class’ Rage work.

There are no new classes being introduced in the 2024 Player’s Handbook. As of PAX Unplugged in late 2023, the artificer class will not be included in the update. Each class that is included will have four subclasses to choose from.

Races will be called species in the 2024 update, removing some bioessentialism from the game. New mechanics for player character abilities coming from backgrounds and origins are being introduced, removing them from being inherent based on biological factors. Additionally, half-races such as half-orcs and half-elves are being removed from the 2024 version of 5e.

2024 Player’s Handbook Is All-Inclusive

Because DnD 5e is 10 years old, a lot of updates and additional rules have been published. Two of the most vital are in Tasha’s Cauldron of Everything and Xanathar’s Guide to Everything. Many of the rules from these books that have become 5e standards over the years, such as custom lineages, spell lists, and subclasses, are sure to be consolidated into the new 5e core. This consolidation will be the same as how Monsters of the Multiverse used and updated parts of Volo’s Guide to Monsters and Mordenkainen’s Tome of Foes.

The new PHB can’t incorporate every single rule written over the past decade, though. This is why it is essential that the new core rules will be fully backward compatible with 5e books released previously. Even if a rule players love is not included, it’s still part of the system and available for use.

There Will Be New Art & A New Structure

It is impossible to separate DnD from its artwork. Since the 80s, with Larry Elmore and Jeff Easley and Brom, art has been a major part of the Dungeons & Dragons experience. The 2024 Player’s Handbook is not going to disappoint in that area. All-new art has been commissioned for the core rulebook updates, and the plan is to make it feel as important as the rules it compliments.

At PAX Unplugged, it was revealed that every class would have a whole page of art to introduce it. Also, for the first time in 50 years, every single subclass would have an image depicting it. This is a huge improvement over past volumes.

If a new player comes to DnD and wants to learn how to play, the Player’s Handbook is probably the one book they will buy before anything else. Unfortunately, the PHB has traditionally not been very good at teaching players how to play the game. There are starter sets and special boxes for that. According to Wizards of the Coast, the 2024 Player’s Handbook will rectify that and have a structure that makes more logical sense in terms of both navigation and instruction.

Is the 2024 Player’s Handbook Still Fifth Edition?

Wizards of the Coast has stated that however the 2024 update to DnD is branded, it will be fully compatible with 5e. Players have taken to calling it 5.5e based on the official 3.5 rules update in the early aughts, but that is only a community moniker. In reality, it will still be a part of 5e, based on the 5e ruleset, and work with 5e adventures, supplements, and even unofficial rulebooks.

Originally, the playtest being released under One D&D was taken to mean that the 2024 update was going to be the first non-edition edition of DnD. Rather than split the userbase into groups, such as those who play 5e and those who play 5.5e or even 6e, there would only be Dungeons & Dragons players. While there has been no announcement of whether there will be any official name, branding, or edition changes, it’s pretty safe to say that the 2024 Player’s Handbook will still be considered some version of 5e.
