One Scarlet & Violet DLC Character Proves Pecharunts Mind Control Isnt Perfect

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Pecharunt’s mind control abilities are terrifying and can easily ensnare and control both people and Pokémon, potentially creating a devastating army. Despite Pecharunt’s power, Nemona’s love for Pokémon battles allows her to resist the mind control to some extent, demonstrating the importance of passion and willpower. Nemona’s actions and ability to catch new Pokémon even while under mind control suggest that Pecharunt may not have been able to fully subvert her will in the epilogue of Pokémon Scarlet and Violet.

Literature Review

One character in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet’s new epilogue proves that Pecharunt’s Binding Mochi does not enable absolutely perfect mind control.

Although the epilogue to Pokémon Scarlet and Violet’s DLC paints a terrifying picture of Pecharunt’s capabilities, one character also proves that the franchise’s new Mythical Pokémon’s powers are not absolute. The story of Pecharunt provides the other side to Ogerpon’s own backstory, effectively bringing The Hidden Treasure of Area Zero full circle as it returns to Kitakami. In the process, the effects of Pecharunt’s mind control are given a prominent place in the story, which both emphasizes the potential threat that the new Pokémon poses while also demonstrating a weakness.

Pecharunt’s poisonous abilities are clearly very potent, given that it is able to ensnare almost the entirety of Mossui Town with ease in a single night. What’s more, it was able to both control and boost the abilities of the Loyal (or Lousy) Three; this means that it either could control three Legendaries from the outset, or elevate three random Pokémon to Legendary status. As such, there is no doubt that the Binding Mochi and Toxic Chains it creates, whether used separately or combined, have the potential to create a devastating army if Pecharunt desires it.

Nemona’s Mochi Mayhem Actions Defy Pecharunt’s Powerful Mind Control

There also seems to be a flaw in Pecharunt’s mind control, even when the Pokémon is clearly asserting itself as much as possible. Hilariously enough, Nemona’s obsession with Pokémon battles is evidently able to overcome Pecharunt’s will, even if only in part. For example, while most mind-controlled people are only able to cry “Mochi,” Nemona instead says, ‘“Mochi match,” alongside other verbal exclamations when the protagonist battles her. Although Kieran’s grandmother ensnaring her husband does show that mind-controlled humans can still speak, this seems to be a deliberate choice on Pecharunt’s part, while Nemona’s own dialogue seems much more natural.

Yukito and Hideko, Kieran and Carmine’s grandparents, are currently the only Pokémon NPCs with the Grandpa and Grandma Trainer classes.

Passion and willpower are clearly the keys to resisting Pecharunt’s mind control. Although throwing off the effects of the Binding Mochi completely doesn’t seem possible for Nemona, the amount of her own personality bleeding through the enforced dancing and shouts for mochi is proof that people can still assert themselves to some extent. This can be seen with other NPCs in the Pokémon DLC as well, such as Penny’s own shouts becoming a mix of “Mochi” and “Eevee.” However, it is not just Nemona’s dialogue that proves just how strong her love of battles is, even when under mind control.

Mind Control Cannot Stop Nemona From Following Her Instincts

When Nemona is battled during Mochi Mayhem, she has two new Pokémon in her party: a Kommo-o and Ribombee. This is especially notable because both Pokémon are available to catch only with the addition of DLC content; both appear in Tera Raid battles in The Hidden Treasure of Area Zero, and Ribombee can also be found roaming the wild in Kitakami. As such, it seems that Nemona must have caught these new Pokémon in Kitakami. However, given the time frame of the group’s visit, the only real opportunity she has to do so is after being mind-controlled.

Prior to leaving the community center in the evening, Nemona is accounted for at all times, and has little opportunity to catch Pokémon. She does admittedly leave the community center twice, the first time to buy soda at Peachy’s, but a brief trip to the nearby shop seems unlikely to have led to Nemona catching two Pokémon. It would have been a significant diversion, and hardly a priority when looking for drinks for herself and her friends. As such, Nemona must have caught these Pokémon after falling under Pecharunt’s influence, suggesting that the Binding Mochi could not stop her battling.

Of course, it is worth noting that Pecharunt could have directed Nemona to capture and train some new Pokémon. At the very least, there is no evidence to suggest that it doesn’t do so. However, given that its powers would make it simple to capture a multitude of Pokémon for Mossui residents to use, if Pecharunt was acting so tactically throughout, then Kieran would undoubtedly have had a much harder time holding off the mind-controlled mob by himself. In the end, it seems more reasonable for Nemona to have been acting on her own instincts to some extent.

Nemona’s Love To Battle Shines Through Pecharunt’s Possession

This epilogue to Pokémon Scarlet and Violet makes it clear that Nemona is obsessed with battling at any opportunity. The marathon of battles she has with Kieran and Carmine after Pecharunt has been dealt with is a perfect example of this. But her actions while being mind-controlled, and the extent to which her love of battling has evidently been preserved during this time, perhaps demonstrate the core of her personality even better. A desire so strong it overrides the control of a Mythical Pokémon is an incredible force, and Nemona demonstrates this in practice perfectly.

Whether mind-controlled or not, it is clear that Nemona has her priorities firmly fixed in favor of battling. In fact, her desires are so strong that they can partially overcome the effects of Binding Mochi, resulting in what she believes to be a fun, battle-filled ‘dream’. Pecharunt’s mind control is a fearsome force, but Nemona has proved that it isn’t necessarily impossible to beat it. In fact, given that all she does is battle under the Pokémon’s influence, Pecharunt may not have been able to properly subvert her will in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet’s new epilogue to begin with.
