Latest Switch 2 Update May Confirm Two Rumors About Nintendo s Next Console

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Nintendo Switch 2 will feature an LCD screen, which might disappoint some, but will help keep costs down for a competitive price point. The new console will have an 8-inch display, making it slightly bulkier than its predecessor, but still portable. The release of the Nintendo Switch 2 is expected in 2024, supported by increased shipments of “amusement displays” that year.

Literature Review

A new report backs up some current expectations for the Nintendo Switch 2, making it seem like a lot of speculation may indeed be accurate.

A new report offers more clarity on the future of the Nintendo Switch 2, and the key piece of news that it delivers actually supports two major rumors about the upcoming console. Nintendo has generally kept quiet about plans for a Switch successor, but that hasn’t stopped speculation from running wild for years. In more recent months, things have transitioned from more random conjecture to reasonably grounded analysis of reports about the console, which seems set to arrive in the near future despite the lack of any official announcement from Nintendo.

The Nintendo Switch is coming up on its seven-year anniversary, a healthy lifespan for a console that’s significantly less powerful than its competitors. An appealing form factor and strong marketing helped propel it beyond the disappointing performance of the Wii U, and it’s held strong despite the lack of any major mid-generation upgrade. Although a Switch Pro may have been in development at some point, any plans for overhauling the system have merged into the development of its successor. Getting the Switch 2 right will be crucial, as losing the momentum generated by the console would be an enormous waste.

Rumor #1: The Switch 2 Is Reported To Have An LCD Screen It’s Likely To Keep Costs (& Final Price) Down

One piece of information that’s been bouncing around for a while is the idea that the Nintendo Switch 2 will have an LCD screen, and a new report from Bloomberg backs up this theory with support from analyst Hiroshi Hayase. Hayase’s market research on small and medium display panels apparently indicates the significant supply chain shifts necessary to provide these panels for a Switch successor. Although Nintendo declined to comment on the situation, actual production shifts like this are generally a significantly more reliable indicator of plans than random rumors.

The fact that the Nintendo Switch 2 will presumably be employing an LCD panel will no doubt be disappointing to some, as the minor mid-generation Switch refresh saw it receive an upgrade to an OLED screen. The decision isn’t surprising, however, as keeping costs down on the panel will make it easier to target a competitive Switch 2 price point necessary to sell the device to families. The original Switch’s LCD panel is also a strong display despite its lack of contrast compared to an OLED, with better color accuracy and uniformity than some past Nintendo offerings.

The Nintendo Switch 2 Screen Will Be Bigger

Another interesting tidbit about the new LCD panel is that it’s expected to be an 8-inch display, which outdoes both the 6.2-inch LCD panel of the standard Nintendo Switch model and the 7-inch OLED panel of the upgraded version. Presumably, the design will look more similar to the smaller bezels on the OLED model, as a lot of extra space went to thick bezels on the original iteration. Even so, an 8-inch diagonal is slightly bigger than the overall dimensions of a Switch, so the successor seems like it will be a bit bulkier.

The Nintendo Switch isn’t intended to be pocketable regardless, so it’s likely that most of the community will welcome a larger screen at the expensive of a very slight difference in portability. Competitors like Valve’s Steam Deck have shown the viability of substantially larger handhelds than the Switch, and it’s likely that the next Nintendo system will still come in at a size that’s closer to the Switch than it is to the Steam Deck. There’s always a chance of another smaller mid-generation variant like the Nintendo Switch Lite, which jettisoned official docking capability in favor of purely handheld function.

Rumor #2: The Report Backs Up A 2024 Nintendo Switch 2 Release A 2024 Release Date Seems Ever More Likely

Another piece of information that keeps cropping up with Nintendo Switch 2 rumors and news is the idea that a Switch successor will release in 2024, and the newest analysis provides further support to that theory. Shipments for “amusement displays” are expected to double in 2024, confirming the idea that the system is coming sooner rather than later. The general expectation is a fall window, which would give Nintendo some time to get momentum going while moving into the holiday season to maximize sales.

The current lineup of games announced for the Nintendo Switch generally supports this idea, with an apparent shift toward prioritizing remakes and remasters rather than new flagship titles. Nintendo reportedly demoed the Switch at Gamescom 2023 for developers, and other reports indicate that some partners have had dev kits in their hands for a while. The company has tended toward short windows between announcements and releases in recent years, so the lack of an official reveal isn’t an especially big concern or any real indication that the system won’t be launching this year.

In terms of performance and overall function, the Switch 2 is expected to make significant advancements on the capabilities of the original while sticking to a similar fundamental design. The Gamescom demo apparently showcased the PS5 proof-of-concept The Matrix Awakens running on the system through the use of Nvidia’s DLSS upscaling, backing up reports that the system will be comparable to a PlayStation 4 or Xbox One overall. Although it obviously won’t be on the same level as the Xbox Series X/S or Playstation 5, a 2024 release of the Switch 2 could help Nintendo attract more third-party titles.

Nvidia DLSS is currently only available on PC, but the technology renders games at a lower resolution before upscaling through AI to achieve higher visual fidelity at a lower performance cost.

It’s still ultimately impossible to know anything about the Nintendo Switch 2 with complete certainty, but as more reports back up the same ideas, it’s easy to have increased confidence in the projections regarding its launch. Anyone who’s been waiting to upgrade a Switch or jump on board for the first time shouldn’t have to wait much longer, and the larger LCD screen is an unsurprising feature that should adequately deliver even if it fails to inspire. Hopefully, Nintendo will provide official updates on the Nintendo Switch 2 in the near future and confirm current speculation once and for all.
