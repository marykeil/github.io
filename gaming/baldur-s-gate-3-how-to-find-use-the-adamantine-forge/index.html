Baldur s Gate 3 How To Find (& Use) The Adamantine Forge

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments

SUMMARY

Literature Review

To explore the Adamantine Forge in Baldur’s Gate 3, the party must find a safe route, and locate gear molds and Mithral Ore to craft Adamantine gear.

Your party has two possible routes into the Shadow-Cursed Lands and Act Two: the Mountain Pass and the Underdark, which have some well-hidden Act One secrets. Your Underdark adventures will eventually lead you to the Grymforge, where you can explore further to find the Adamantine Forge. This involves four main objectives: finding the Forge, locating gear molds, obtaining Mithral Ore, and finally using the Forge itself.

How To Find The Adamantine Forge In Baldur’s Gate 3

After arriving at the Grymforge by boat, you will encounter a group of Underdark dwarves, working in an uneasy partnership with Absolute cultists who’ve enslaved a group of gnomes. This can include Barcus Wroot if you free him from the windmill in the Blighted Village. The fate of the gnomes has consequences for Acts Two and Three, so you may want to deal with the Duergar and cultists first, potentially by recruiting Elder Brithvar and killing True Soul Nere.

Whether to save or kill Nere depends on your character; either way, it’s recommended that you clear the Grymforge out for some extra XP before tackling the Adamantine Forge.

The Adamantine Forge is a challenging gauntlet that mixes dangerous exploration with a few challenging combat encounters. Having a solid party composition with the right companions is essential, and you will need party members capable of ranged and melee combat backed up by spellcasting support. Proficiency in Perception is also highly recommended.

To start your journey, head up the stairs from the docks past the Underdark – Grymforge fast travel sigil and turn right at the top, following the path. If the Duergar are still present, chat to Stonemason Kith along the way and use your skills to help him survey the nearby stonework. If players succeed, he will hand over some Infernal Iron: a key item in a personal quest for Karlach, one of Baldur’s Gate 3’s best companions.

At the end, your party will come across a staircase blocked by rubble and will need to find a way past it. There are several options for breaking through:

If the Duergar are still present, Herdmaster Skarjall can be found trying to force some Deep Rothe to break through the rubble. You can use the Speak with Animals spell to get the Rothe to attack the Duergar or destroy the rubble. Break the rubble with Bludgeoning damage, such as having a friendly Barbarian crush it with a blunt weapon. Break the rubble with Force damage by using smoke powder bombs or a Warlock’s Eldritch Blast cantrip.

Afterward, head past the rubble and follow the staircase. The path at the top is littered with traps that can seriously damage your party, but a high-Dexterity character with Sleight of Hand Proficiency can disarm everything and clear the way forward. Roughly halfway along the path, your party can drop down to find two levers and a bridge segment. Use the levers to lower the bridge segment and jump the gap to reach the iron walkway. Continue along, making sure to collect the Adamantine Longsword Mold here.

If you hit the other lever, you can move the bridge segment across and jump to the opposite side of the upper level. There are several rooms to explore here, one of which contains the Adamantine Shield mold and another which contains Philomeen the Gnome’s hiding spot.

Eventually, you will reach the end of the walkway. To cross, look to your left to find a set of levers off in the distance. You can hit the levers with ranged attacks or control them with Mage Hand to bring over another bridge segment. Jump on, ensure your whole party follows, and use the levers to move the bridge segment.

Continue along the final section of the walkway at the end and jump down to find yourself in the Adamantine Forge. At this point, you need to track down more gear molds and Mithral Ore for the forging process.

Where To Find All Adamantine Forge Item Molds (And Which To Craft) In Baldur’s Gate 3

You can find molds for six pieces of Adamantine Gear: a longsword, shield, mace, scimitar, medium-scale mail, and heavy splint armor. However, the catch is that only two chunks of Mithral Ore can be found, so you can only craft two out of six items.

    All Adamantine Forge Items in Baldur's Gate 3, Ranked Worst To Best  Six different items can be crafted at the Adamantine Forge in Baldur's Gate 3, and it's important to make the most of this exclusive opportunity.

The scale mail, splint armor, and shield are your best options. The Adamantine Shield provides useful defensive benefits, including inflicting the Reeling status effect on any melee attackers that miss, immunity to critical hits, and +2 to Armor Class. Shields are great for classes that can use them, such as Fighters or Paladins.

Humans and Half-Elves have Shield Proficiency as a race feature and can equip shields in their offhand regardless of their chosen class. For example, if you’re running a Half-Elf race build , you can have your Half-Elf Bard equip a shield for a quick Armor Class boost.

The Adamantine Scale Mail and Adamantine Splint Armor are also recommended over the Adamantine weapons. Both sets are challenging and particularly resistant to melee damage, making them excellent options for frontline warriors and tanks. Additionally, armor tends to stay competitive for longer when compared to weapons in Baldur’s Gate 3. You could craft the Adamantine weapons, but, likely, your party will quickly find equal or better replacements in Act Two.

Where To Find Mithral Ore In Baldur’s Gate 3

There are two veins of Mithral Ore in the Adamantine Forge, as shown on the map above.

The first is located southwest of the forge entrance. As you head southwest, some hostile Animated Armor enemies will appear on a small balcony and trigger a short combat encounter. After defeating them, you can follow the path down behind the balcony towards a lava pit with a Mithral Ore node located just across a small gap. You can break it with weapons or spells to find a chunk of Mithral Ore.

The second is off to the left as you descend the stairs toward the Adamantine Forge platform. However, as you approach, a swarm of Magma Mephits will spawn. This can be a surprisingly tough combat encounter as these creatures explode when killed, and you’ll likely be in close quarters when they spawn.

When facing the Magma Mephits, Elixirs of Fire Resistance can be very helpful. When consumed, they give your character resistance to Fire damage and immunity to Burn - a useful quality, as Magma Mephits can spray fire and set the ground ablaze.

Now, you should have everything you need to forge Adamantine items. However, once you reach the Adamantine Forge platform, you can explore a small area to the right to defeat a Lava Elemental and pick up Baldur’s Gate 3’s Sentient Amulet.

How To Use The Adamantine Forge In Baldur’s Gate 3

To use the Adamantine Forge, insert the item mold of your choice into the Mold Chamber and Mithral Ore into the Crucible. You can then pull the Forge Lever so the platform descends to a lower level.

If you want to swap molds, click the mold release lever to eject the mold and replace it.

Interacting with the Lava Valve on this level will flood the platform with lava, and you can trigger the Forge Hammer to forge your Adamantine Gear. However, this also triggers a boss fight with Grym, the Adamantine Golem. After defeating Grym, you can claim your first item and repeat the process to retrieve a second piece of Adamantine Gear and complete Baldur’s Gate 3’s Adamantine Forge quest.
