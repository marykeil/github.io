Ark Survival Ascended Scorched Earth - Release Date, New Dinosaurs, Skins, & Gameplay Features

Contents

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Ark: Survival Ascended is expanding with the addition of Scorched Earth, a remake of the first paid DLC for the original game. The release of Scorched Earth has faced delays but is now set to launch on April 1, 2024. The remake will feature new dinosaurs, including the Fasolasuchus, and new skins, with some cosmetic options available through additional purchases.

Literature Review

The upcoming DLC for Ark: Survival Ascended isn’t too far off, and here’s everything that players can expect to see from it when it arrives.

Ark: Survival Ascended is going to be expanded with the addition of Scorched Earth, a remake of the first paid DLC for the original Ark: Survival Evolved. As Survival Evolved underwent years of expansion from a comparatively slim early access release, the new Unreal Engine 5 iteration of the game known as Survival Ascended is now taking on a similar path to restore the full sum of content piece by piece. It isn’t all going to be a one-to-one comparison, however, with Scorched Earth making some changes and additions for its new implementation.The original announcement of Ark: Survival Ascended came as something of a surprise at the end of March 2023, with the news dropping right before April Fool’s Day and coinciding with another delay for the planned sequel Ark 2. It’s no surprise that Survival Ascended has received a mixed response in some regards, generating frustrations with the restarted cycle of updates along with some performance issues and the general lack of major news about Ark 2. Nonetheless, it’s managed to ride the popularity of the IP to a large and active user base, so there shouldn’t be any concern about planned content drying up any time soon.

When Ark: Survival Ascended’s Scorched Earth Expansion Releases

The Scorched Earth expansion for Ark: Survival Ascended is currently set for release on April 1, 2024, almost exactly one year after the original announcement of Survival Ascended. Keeping with the general theme of Ark releases, Scorched Earth has already been subject to a series of delays. The original Ark: Survival Ascended roadmap had the DLC set to launch alongside the base content before being pushed back to December and ultimately to the current date. This is the first time that a specific date has been offered, however, and with a comfortable window of time in front of the planned release, another delay doesn’t seem likely.Although Scorched Earth originally cost $19.99 when it released as DLC for Ark: Survival Evolved, the basic content of the Survival Ascended iteration will fortunately be free. The Steam page for Ark: Survival Ascended clarifies that purchasing the game provides access to all the worlds featured in Survival Evolved, and although only The Island was available at launch, the rest are coming “at no additional cost.” The retail price for Ark: Survival Ascended itself is $44.99, although it launched at a slightly discounted $39.99.

New Ark: Survival Ascended Scorched Earth Dinosaurs

The Ark: Survival Ascended remake of Scorched Earth is making one big addition to the dinosaur lineup that wasn’t present in the original game with the Fasolasuchus. The fourth winner in a series of Creature Votes held for the game, the Fasolasuchus ultimately generated 28,810 votes to beat out the runner-up, Gigantoraptor Regalimaia, which managed 20,455 according to an official Ark: Survival Ascended blog post. Although the Fasolasuchus is an ancestor of crocodilian animals, it’s actually the perfect fit for the dry wastelands of Scorched Earth, and Studio Wildcard is billing the creature as the “ultimate desert steed” for Ark: Survival Ascended.

              One Of The Biggest Ark: Survival Ascended Changes Is Actually A Good Thing   Ark: Survival Ascended might be a remake of Survival Evolved, but it’s making changes from the original, and that seems to be an improvement here.

The original release of Scorched Earth featured a significant roster of new creatures, all of which should also presumably return in the Ark: Survival Ascended version. These range from small desert animals like the Jerboa to mystical beasts like Wyverns, with the only real common ground being the adaptability of every creature to the hostile Scorched Earth environment. The full list of Scorched Earth additions is as follows:

Death Worm Jerboa Jug Bug Fasolasuchus Lymantria Mantis Morellatops Rock Elemental Thorny Dragon Vulture Wyvern Manticore

New Skins In Ark: Survival Ascended Scorched Earth

Scorched Earth will also be bringing new and returning skins to Ark: Survival Ascended, although some key cosmetic options are going to be locked behind an additional purchase. Cowboy and Cowgirl outfits and accessories will be coming to the Frontier Adventure Pack, a paid addition that launches alongside Scorched Earth on April 1. The full contents and price of the Frontier Adventure Pack haven’t yet been officially revealed, but it shouldn’t cross over into any content that was included with the original Scorched Earth DLC for Ark: Survival Evolved.

Adventure Packs are currently planned for each Ark: Survival Ascended DLC, offloading the monetization from the main expansion content to a supplementary model.

Assuming that the general lineup of Ark: Survival Ascended Scorched Earth skins will be identical to Survival Evolved, half of the available options are based on the fearsome boss, the Manticore, with skins for boots, chest pieces, gauntlets, helmets, leggings, and shields all available in the style. “Scorched” skins should be included for spikes, swords, and torches. The Thorny Dragon offers a saddle skin, and the Wyvern provides one for gloves. A “Safari” hat skin rounds things out with a traditional pith helmet look.

ASA Scorched Earth Gameplay Features

The big fundamental change between The Island and the map of Scorched Earth is obviously the environmental shift, with Scorched Earth focusing on a desert with only sparse sources of water. The survival focus consequently shifts in major ways, with hydration becoming a significant priority and heat stroke emerging as a concern. There are also some additional threats of inclement weather, with Sandstorms, Superheat, and Electrical Storms all making life a little bit harder. All the problems that they pose can be effectively countered or prepared for with some survival knowledge, but traversing the sands of Scorched Earth for the first time definitely has a learning curve.Scorched Earth isn’t for everyone, as its challenges have a different appeal from many other worlds and popular game modes in Ark, but it’s certainly worth a try as a free update for Ark: Survival Ascended. It should be followed by further expansions Ragnarok and Aberration, which were originally slated for Q1 2024 releases but now seem likely to come later in the year. It might still take some time for Ark: Survival Ascended to become a comparably content-rich experience when held up against the original Survival Evolved, but Scorched Earth is the first major step in that direction.
