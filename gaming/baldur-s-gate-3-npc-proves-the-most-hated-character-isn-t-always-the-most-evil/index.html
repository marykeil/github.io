Baldur s Gate 3 NPC Proves The Most Hated Character Isn t Always The Most Evil

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

One Baldur’s Gate 3 NPC is often cited as the most hated character in Baldur’s Gate 3 , attracting widespread antipathy within the community. This character’s dismissive attitude towards Barcus and lack of appreciation for the party’s help make him deeply annoying. The quality of writing and performance in the game is evident in intentionally creating a character that people love to hate.

Literature Review

Baldur’s Gate 3 has plenty of despicable villains, but the worst offender of all isn’t one of the most fundamentally wicked or morally bankrupt.

The world of Baldur’s Gate 3 is full of scheming villains and horrific evildoers, but sometimes, the characters that are the easiest to hate aren’t the ones that are doing the most wicked deeds. Although General Ketheric Thorm is one of the game’s most diabolical characters, for example, the iconic vocal performance from J.K. Simmons and crackling dialogue make it easy for him to go down as a favorite. On the other hand, some equally well-portrayed characters just feature such annoying characteristics that comparatively good intentions can’t save them from being the punching bags of the community.

One of the more memorable side characters in Baldur’s Gate 3 is Barcus Wroot, who isn’t a stranger to being the butt of Baldur’s Gate 3 jokes. Barcus is an innately pitiful character, and his tendency to land himself in hot water could easily prove irritating to a party that has to keep saving him. Somehow, though, Barcus’s sincerity tends to start winning parties over, activating the basic instinct to protect someone who can’t protect themselves. Developing warm feelings for Barcus tends to come with a flip side, as there’s one character associated with him who’s impossible not to hate.

Wulbren Bongle Is The Most Hated BG3 Character

If the Baldur’s Gate 3 community has united around opposition to any one character, it’s Wulbren Bongle, who’s managed to attract a level of hatred that might make the Absolute jealous. Wulbren is a name before he’s an actual character in Baldur’s Gate 3, as Barcus explains that he’s searching for his friend Wulbren when the party first encounters him in trouble. Barcus goes through hell on his quest for Wulbren, showcasing a level of loyalty that echoes Samwise Gamgee’s steadfast devotion to Frodo Baggins.

The same, however, cannot be said for Wulbren, a fact that makes itself exceptionally clear when the party finally meets the other gnome in Act 2 of Baldur’s Gate 3. Wulbren clearly views Barcus as an annoying hanger-on and acts innately dismissive of Barcus’s valiant attempts to ensure that he’s safe. His supercilious attitude tends to strike the wrong chord, and any prior negative feelings that a party might have held toward Barcus tend to evaporate in the face of the obvious injustice of Wulbren failing to appreciate the efforts of his loyal follower.

Even for those who despised Wulbren from the moment they met him, it can still sometimes come as something of a surprise to see just how passionate the community is about this subject. The widespread hate for Wulbren recently reached a new level with the creation of a subreddit dedicated purely to hating him, which has attracted over 500 members so far. The subreddit takes its name from a diss track focusing on the character, available on the YouTube channel of the Space Bards, who have crafted a number of video game raps that are usually less venomous.

Wulbren is still an example of the quality of writing and performance found throughout Baldur’s Gate 3, as intentionally creating a character that people love to hate might be even harder than making one that they just love. Wulbren’s attitude is so annoying because it’s deeply believable, and most people have likely encountered someone with a similarly dismissive demeanor. In a world where the biggest villains invoke the powers of gods, having characters that stand for more banal offenses helps ground the fantasy in a painfully authentic reality.

Further Crimes Of Wulbren Bongle In Baldur’s Gate 3

Of course, Wulbren Bongle’s sins extend beyond his callous attitude toward Barcus. For one, his lack of appreciation ties in with a generally narcissistic personality. His confidence that all of Baldur’s Gate will know his name echoes the behavior of the arrogant Tiefling Rolan, but unlike Rolan, he doesn’t seem as willing to re-evaluate his perspectives after the events of the story shed some light on their error. He also has no immediate inclination to thank the party for their help, although he’s more willing to acknowledge their talent than he is to accept that Barcus has anything to offer.

Wulbren’s overall aim in the game is to take down the Steel Watch Foundry in Baldur’s Gate, an objective fueled by an old grudge between the Ironhand Gnomes and the religious group of craftspeople known as the Gondians. Although it’s easy to empathize with his stance against the Steel Watch, which happens to be employed by the villainous Lord Gortash, Wulbren’s interest is clearly fueled more by bigoted anger than justice. If the party blows up the Steel Watch Foundry in Act 3, Wulbren is insistent upon killing any Gondians who escape and turns on the party if they disagree.

Finding Wulbren and the Ironhand Gnomes outside of the city requires some underground exploring, as the trapdoor to their hideout is blocked from above.

How To Deal With Wulbren Bongle In Baldur’s Gate 3

Killing Wulbren might not exactly be justice, but it could be the catharsis that a lot of parties are looking for. Unfortunately, it tends to come with a cost, as the other Ironhand Gnomes will leap to his defense if the party attacks him while they’re in sight. There’s also Barcus to consider, as although a life free of Wulbren’s shadow would shoot him well, actually killing the object of his admiration might not exactly be the way to pave the healthiest path forward for him.

The perfect solution lies in Barcus himself, who can be persuaded to stand against Wulbren and ultimately take over the leadership of the Ironhand Gnomes, ensuring a future for the group under the hands of a nobler leader. As long as the party has managed to keep him alive up to this point, it’s the perfect conclusion for the hapless gnome’s arc and the most satisfying way to put Wulbren in his place. Wulbren Bongle might be a cause for general frustration, but with the right choices, his inclusion can lead to a truly triumphant moment in Baldur’s Gate 3.
