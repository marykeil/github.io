All Jobs In Like A Dragon Infinite Wealth, Ranked Worst To Best

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2000 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth features a variety of jobs that party members can take on, similar to classes in other games. Party members can change between jobs to inherit skills from different classes, adding versatility to their abilities. Each job has its own unique weapons and skills, allowing players to customize their party members’ playstyles.

Literature Review

Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth has 25 different jobs that can be chosen for the ten party members, although some are exclusive per character.

Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth follows the traditional job system pattern that Yakuza: Like A Dragon and others have had, with a large variety of jobs that party members can take on. The job opportunities cover a wide range of possible skills, being similar to classes in other games, and often are limited to just certain characters being able to qualify for them. Unlocked jobs will carry over into New Game Plus mode, so after the job system is unlocked in Chapter 6 of the main story, it is worth finding all the jobs. Party members in Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth can change between jobs during the game by going to the clothes-changing booth. It is important to note that the Skill Inheritance Mechanic in the game will help provide benefits for having more than one job leveled. When a character gets a new skill from leveling up in a job, it can be applied to skill inheritance in other classes. Therefore, even when a character is classified as a certain job, they may be able to use some skills from another job that they have learned.

Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth Review - “A Tremendously High Bar” Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth takes many of the best elements of the franchise’s past, expands on them, and adds more into sheer brilliance.

25 Freelancer (Exclusive To Ichiban Kasuga) Weapon: Fists

At the beginning of Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth, Ichiban Kasuga has the Freelancer job as the default starting job. It provides a strong attack and defense with wrestling moves and punching, but otherwise, it is nothing too impressive. Soon after starting the game, he can take on the much more impressive Hero job.

24 Barmaid (Exclusive To Saeko Mukoda) Weapon: Bags

Although not the most powerful job in the game, the Barmaid job is very fun to see used. Exclusively available to Saeko, the job includes several skills that provide fun animations, including spraying enemies with champagne bottles or hitting them with purses. Additionally, her Essence of Remotivation skill can revive a fallen ally.

When Do Jobs Unlock In Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth? There are plenty of jobs to use when building a party in Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth. It does, however, take a while to unlock them.

23 Detective (Exclusive To Koichi Adachi) Weapon: Batons

Koichi Adachi’s starting job is that of Detective. It suits this powerhouse well by essentially creating a Tank class with a combat-focused style and very high HP. However, the agility stat for it is the lowest in the game, which leads to him being pretty slow. Although it is a good job for protecting other party members, Adachi is generally better off taking on a new job, such as Samurai, when it is unlocked.

22 Hitman (Exclusive To Joongi Han) Weapon: Knuckles

The Hitman job has the subname of “Expedient Kill Machine”, and for good reason. Though it is Joongi Han’s starting default job, he can use it well to quickly take out several enemies before they even have a chance to react. Depending on a player’s style of play and how they build their party, it can be very effective to leave him as a Hitman, although Samurai and Desperado are also good choices for Joongi.

21 Pyrodancer (Males Only) Weapon: Axes

Any male party member may take the Pyrodancer job after reaching Passion Level 3. It offers beautiful skills to watch, with the fire included in dance-like combat moves. The class can provide strong damage and magic buffs, yet it also offers no defense. Any character with the Pyrodancer job is very weak against foes, which is difficult without much in terms of ranged attacks.

20 Heiress (Exclusive To Chitose Fujinomiya) Weapon: Chokers

Chitose’s default job, Heiress, uses ballet moves, fashion, and more to gracefully take down enemies in a high-class feminine style. It is a fairly well-rounded job that can be used to great effect. Its only downfall is that it is limited to Chitose, who is stronger and more effective when using the Idol or Housekeeper jobs instead.

Every Returning Character For Yakuza: Like A Dragon 8 (So Far) Like a Dragon 8 will feature characters from previous games, including past protagonists. A few characters have already been confirmed to reappear.

19 Assassin (Exclusive To Seonhee) Weapon: Throwing Needles

Seonhee’s initial job is that of being a deadly Assassin, making great use of simple-looking throwing needles to debilitate her enemies and using whips to paralyze them. It is a very straightforward and even class that she could easily carry with her through the entire game and be very effective. That said, combining her Assassin skills with a Kunoichi job can take Seonhee to another level altogether.

18 Homeless Guy (Exclusive To Yu Nanba) Weapon: Umbrellas

The title of Homeless Guy does not lend itself to an idea of strong class skills, yet Nanba makes this default job work by flaunting his magical skills to trick and take out enemies. Of course, it also features humorous skills such as Malodorous Stench, which causes a decrease in attack and defense of nearby enemies, and Pigeon Raid, which calls upon local pigeons to carry the magic damage to foes.

17 Gangster (Exclusive To Tianyou Zhao) Weapon: Sabers

Tianyou Zhao begins with the Gangster job, which has a strong emphasis on melee damage. Though providing little in agility stats, the job is surprisingly graceful, with skills such as Rapid Crane Kick and Whirling Blade Dance providing a little bit of choreography. When applying Skill Inheritance, combining aspects of Gangster and Desperado works well to complement each other for Zhao.

16 Aquanaut (Males Only) Weapon: Surfboards

Aquanaut is most certainly the most fun and vibrant of all the jobs in Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth’s repertoire. Using a surfboard, any male character who has the Aquanaut job can cause some totally tubular damage and even call upon the aid of dolphin friends. The job doesn’t offer much for HP stats; otherwise, it is a fairly even split on all the stat changes.

Infinite Wealth Can Fix Like A Dragon’s Biggest Combat Mistake Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth should completely skip one needlessly frustrating part of the previous game’s turn-based combat. Here’s why.

15 Geodancer (Female Only) Weapon: Maracas

Hula dancing combined with an extraordinary amount of magic can make the Geodancer job a powerful one for any female party member. Sadly, the HP stats for this job are very low, but with the help of some protection from other party members, it can cause a lot of damage to enemies. Additionally, the Essence of Natural Healing skill can recover all allies’ HP and remove status ailments.

14 Night Queen (Female Only) Weapon: Whips

One of the reasons Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth has the warning label of not being appropriate for all ages is that the Night Queen job certainly appears to have other things in mind. Despite the attire and judgmental ideas, Night Queen is the strongest damage-dealing job for female party members. Given to Seonhee with the added Skill Inheritance benefits from her Assassin background, Night Queen can be an essential job for successful combat.

13 Cabbie (Exclusive To Eric Tomizawa) Weapon: Wrenches

Eric Tomizawa’s starting job as Cabbie is both powerful and fun to watch in action. During fights, Eric can throw tires at enemies, fight with wrenches, and even douse enemies in gasoline to lower their resistance to fire. The Scrappy Technician uses a significant amount of magic in his job and can add a layer of levity to the action.

12 Breaker (Male Only) Weapon: Anklets

The Breaker job is a very agile, performance-focused skill set that provides a lot of buffs to nearby allies. Through the use of a lot of flair mixed with magic and a heavy dose of cartwheeling action added in, the Breaker not only damages enemies but also applies critical chances and more to allies. The element of the anklet that the Breaker character is wearing will define much of how well it works in certain situations.

11 Housekeeper (Female Only) Weapon: Cleaning Equipment

Requiring Kindness Level 4 and $200 to unlock in Alo-Happy Tours, the Housekeeper job likes to keep things polished in Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth. This job is a very powerful magic-wielding class, with many opportunities to magically damage enemies as well as remove buffs from them. However, it has a very weak basic attack, so any Housekeeper character would need to rely on magic to be useful.

10 Samurai (Male Only) Weapon: Katanas

The Samurai is a powerful job to have in a party, offering a very strong offense and defense with its swords and bows, making them invaluable as both a tank and damage dealer. The job is a good one for many of the men in the game and scales very well for late-game combat. Due to its tank-heavy qualities, the Samurai is perhaps most effective as a job for Adachi, yet it works well for any of the characters.

9 Chef (Male Only) Weapon: Kitchen Utensils

Subnamed the Tactical Gourmet, the Chef job provides a strong attack and magic ability but little in terms of defense or willpower. However, watching a Chef at work in battle can be fun to see with his weapons, including cast iron pans, pressure cookers, and other handy kitchen gadgets. The job is a good one for many of the male characters, particularly for Nanba and Tomizawa, due to its fire elements.

8 Host (Male Only) Weapon: Bottles

Another good choice for Nanba is the eye-catching job of Host because of its heavy emphasis on magic. Many of the attacks that the Host can use are similar to those of the Saeko-exclusive job, Barmaid. This gives another opportunity to have the fun of spraying enemies down with sparkling liquid from bottles while letting Saeko take on other roles.

7 Action Star (Male Only) Weapon: Nunchakus

The Action Star may be dressed in the flashiest of attire in Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth, but the high amount of HP and attack damage they can do proves their star power. The powerful attacks used by characters in this job will not use much magic but offer an entertaining display of agility and attack. The most impressive of skills is the Action Star’s Relentless Dragon’s Dance skill, which can double the damage to the next physical skill used. Follow it up with an Essence of Improvisation to do a heavy amount of damage, and the Star will heal back a percentage of the damage dealt.

6 Desperado (Male Only) Weapon: Guns

No frying pans or purses for weapons here. The classic Desperado job is a deadly sharpshooter with good, old-fashioned guns. Of course, it wouldn’t be a Like A Dragon game without some magic added to that firepower, and so the magic stat is also notably high. Combined with Joongi’s Hitman job Skill Inheritance, a Desperado’s bullets can make quick work of any enemy.
