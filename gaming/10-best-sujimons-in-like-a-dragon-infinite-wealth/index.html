10 Best Sujimons In Like a Dragon Infinite Wealth

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1600 words 8 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Literature Review

The Sujimancer job in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth lets you have special summons called Sujimon which are best used in unique fighting mini-games.

There are five types of Sujimons in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth: Blaze, Frost, Nature, Light, and Shadow. Each of these categories has different dynamics with each other that determine a Sujimon’s strengths and weaknesses. For example, a Blaze Sujimon is powerful against Nature types, but ultimately vulnerable to any enemy wielding a Shadow-type on their team. The Sujimancer class is one of the best jobs for Ichiban Kasuga in the game, not only for the ability to participate in Sujimon battles but also for the added benefit of a summoner role that creates many new allies during a normal fight.

All Jobs In Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth, Ranked Worst To Best
Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth has 25 different jobs that can be chosen for the ten party members, although some are exclusive per character.

10 Suji Rider (Blaze) Slow Survivor

HP ATK SPD 415 145 90

Suji Rider is a Blaze-type Sujimon with a ridiculous amount of health in exchange for poor speed and only decent attacking power in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth. This summon works best as a supportive Tank that can survive for far longer against a rival’s team unless they are a Frost-type specialist that takes advantage of Suji Rider’s one weakness. Some Suji League bosses in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth may stand a better chance against this Sujimon, especially the Champion or someone from the Discreet Four. Thankfully, this Sujimon has cheap special attacks like Scorching Breath that only cost 1 MP. The Insecticide Inferno skill may cost 8 SP to activate, but it packs quite a punch if your opponent uses Nature-types.

9 Suji Bear (Nature) Primal Instincts

HP ATK SPD 358 157 110

The Nature-type Suji Bear has fairly balanced stats that lean heavily on offensive prowess rather than speed or health. Raw power is not normally a focus of this type, so this Sujimon is a rare, but welcome exception to other green summons in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth. The above-average HP of Suji Bear allows it to survive longer than you might expect compared to other Sujimons of its type. The main weakness of Suji Bear is not only the Blaze-type but also the lack of any Special Attack. Despite this, Suji Bear has a truly devastating special skill called Home Run Swing, which costs 10 SP to activate. Any battle against Frost-type Sujimons will be quite easy when you have Suji Bear ready to attack any opponent in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth.

8 Super Dungeon Troll (Shadow) Well-Rounded Explorer

HP ATK SPD 358 133 140

Similar to Suji Bear, Super Dungeon Troll has the same amount of health as the previous Sujimon, but this time sacrifices offense for speed. Shadow-type Sujimons have an easier time avoiding bad match-ups against Light-type opponents early in your Sujimancer career. The increased agility allows you to act ahead of your rivals and use attacks like Suspicious Smoke or skills such as Serial Slash. According to YouTube creator Rozalin, it can be hard to predict which Sujimons enemy trainers use in battle. The cheap MP cost of a reliable Shadow-type attack allows you to prepare for anyone wielding a Light-type before they can take advantage of Super Dungeon Troll’s vulnerability. Planning for Sujimon battles can turn desperate struggles in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth into easy victories.

Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth Review - “A Tremendously High Bar”
Like A Dragon: Infinite Wealth takes many of the best elements of the franchise’s past, expands on them, and adds more into sheer brilliance.

7 Suji Surfer (Frost) Wave Crasher

HP ATK SPD 350 122 170

The Suji Surfer has above-average health paired with decent attacking power and the largest speed stat among Frost-type Sujimons. Anyone struggling against Blaze-type trainers can use this wave rider to knock out enemy Sujimons before their opponent blinks. Super Surfer Crash may be one of the best Frost skills in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth, even if it does cost 10 SP to use. The incredibly cheap 1 MP cost Shark Tornado attack for Suji Surfer also gives the Sujimon utility as a fast-acting warrior in every round of combat. While you may have some trouble receiving this Sujimon from Basic or Type Gachas, the reliability of the Surfer cannot be underestimated in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth.

6 Suji Sumo (Frost) Intimidating Presence

HP ATK SPD 459 118 110

Arguably the strongest Frost-type Sujimon in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth is Suji Somo, a summon with an extraordinary HP stat. Unlike Suji Rider, Sumo has decent speed to make up for their bulk, even though their attack may be a little lackluster. The Intimidating Tremor 9 SP special skill defines this Sujimon since you won’t be using any special attacks akin to Suji Bear.

5 Super Executioner (Nature) Forest Hunter

HP ATK SPD 327 132 160

The versatility of Super Executioner puts this Sujimon at the apex of the Nature-type in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth. The remarkable speed of this Sujimon makes them faster than any other green summon, and even quicker than those of other types too. You may miss a rare evolution like the Super Executioner at first until you see the summon’s potential in the hands of your rivals. The 2 MP Rotten Blast special attack and 8 SP Soul Reaper special skill tie synergize well to give the Super Executioner interesting tools. Managing to assign a party member the Sujimancer unlocked job in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth allows you to explore each of these treats in a team dynamic. A video made by YouTube creator Purplatypus shows off just how deep certain Sujimon pairings can go in this mini-game.

4 Suji Delinquent (Shadow) Master of Swiftness

HP ATK SPD 323 111 210

When you start fighting higher and higher level Sujimons, you’ll quickly realize that those with the highest speed like Suji Delinquent have a better chance at winning. 210 Speed puts this Shadow-type above nearly all other Sujimons in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth. Their attack and health may be lower than others, but there’s no denying the advantages of someone with pure agility. The only skill on Suji Delinquent is Jawbone Rush, an 8 SP cost ability that may be useful against enemy Light-type summons. Like Suji Bear and Suji Summo, there are no special attacks on this Sujimon in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth. You’ll have to craft your team around this Sujimon’s absurd swiftness by adding characters with larger health and damage to make up for the Delinquent’s shortcomings.

3 Kiwami Violet (Shadow) Hellbound Darkness

HP ATK SPD 384 176 170

The Kiwami Sujimons in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth are the strongest summons in the game due to their Platinum rarity among their peers. Kiwami Violet is perhaps the weakest, but you wouldn’t be able to tell with its high health, attack, and speed in equal measure. Despite the expensive cost of this Shadow-type’s moves, spending the MP and SP is worth it to see your enemies eradicated. The special attack of Kiwami Violet is Deathly Dance, which costs 3 MP to use. On the other hand, the special skill Ticket to Hell costs 9 SP total to activate in battle.

2 Kiwami Red (Blaze) Flaming Firestarter

HP ATK SPD 480 165 120

Kiwami Red is the Sujimon with the highest health in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth out of any type. The absurd attacking power combined with this summon’s bulk makes them a reliable member of any Sujimancer’s team for challenging trainer battles. The only Sujimon that rival this kind of Kiwami are the DLC-exclusive Platinum characters like Joongi Han or Kazama Kiryu. This Blaze-type has moves that are easier to use than some of the Legendary Sujimons you might find elsewhere. The special skill Dual Hammer costs 8 SP, but the special attack Flaming Footwork only needs 2 MP to activate. Having a Sujimon that has cheaper abilities and the stats that Kiwami Red has makes them a strong contender against nearly any opponent.

1 Kiwami Gold (Light) Giver of Life

HP ATK SPD 426 134 220

The fastest Sujimon in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth is Kiwami Gold, a Platinum rarity Light-type summon. The character perhaps has the strongest abilities in the game, with the 10 SP cost Life-or-Death Fist being capable of knocking out nearly any other Sujimons that isn’t Kiwami Gold’s level. The Hula Hullabaloo special attack provides consistent offense as well, only costing 2 MP to use. Obviously, the big draw to this Sujimon comes from the combination of extremely high health and speed. The attacking power may be a bit underwhelming, but you’ll hardly need it as Kiwami Gold tanks attacks and dish back their own at a moment’s notice. The best Sujimons in Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth define trainer battles with better stats and reliable strength that leads your team to success.
