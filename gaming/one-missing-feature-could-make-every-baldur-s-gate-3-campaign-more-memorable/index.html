One Missing Feature Could Make Every Baldur s Gate 3 Campaign More Memorable

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1200 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Baldur’s Gate 3 lacks a photo mode, limiting players’ ability to capture and showcase the beautifully detailed world and interesting designs. A dedicated photo mode, like those found in many AAA titles, would provide greater control over capturing images and allow for creative shots. Implementing a photo mode would not only enhance the player experience but also serve as a way to memorialize memorable moments and characters in the game.

Literature Review

Baldur’s Gate 3 is in a great state already, but one thing that could be in the works would add a special aspect of longevity to the experience.

Baldur’s Gate 3 is a notably feature-rich game, but one element in particular that’s failed to make the cut so far feels like a major shame. Developer Larian Studios has attempted to deliver both the complexity and content of older computer RPGS and the immersion and quality-of-life of modern AAA titles, a balancing act that the company manages to pull off to surprising success in most regards. This best of both worlds approach doesn’t include everything, however, and sometimes missing features can be the ones that are most often taken for granted in other games.

Although Baldur’s Gate 3 left early access for its official release in August, development on the title has been going strong post-launch, with Larian Studios dropping frequent patches and hotfixes. Many of the best Baldur’s Gate 3 changes simply address bugs and problems, but others introduce new requested features or add options to play Baldur’s Gate 3 according to individual preferences. Fixing issues that are making the game less fun for everyone obviously has to take priority, which can explain why some heavily requested features haven’t arrived yet, but the overall stability has been significantly improved in the months since its release.

Baldur’s Gate 3 Deserves A Photo Mode

Baldur’s Gate 3 has a beautifully detailed world full of bespoke content and interesting designs, but there’s currently no better way to capture it than simply disabling the UI overlay and taking screenshots. Although this isn’t a particularly laborious method, and the key to toggle the UI can easily be rebound on PC, it’s strongly limited in its capabilities. It’s possible to zoom in to a reasonable degree in Baldur’s Gate 3, but screenshots are always bound to a certain distance and range of camera angles due to the basic restrictions for the in-game camera.

The situation seems especially restrictive when compared to many other modern games, as having a dedicated photo mode has become a staple feature of the majority of AAA titles. Photo modes allow similar control over the image to what an actual camera would offer, with the added benefit of a freer range of placement possibilities due to the lack of any real-life physical restrictions. From setting up shots of beautiful landscapes to snapping dynamic action scenes, photo modes make it possible to show off games in ways that would otherwise be difficult or impossible.

It does seem like a Baldur’s Gate 3 photo mode could be on the horizon, as the Larian Studios director of publishing responded on Twitter to a query about a potential mode with “It’s on the list.” This was posted in September 2023, so must not be especially high on the list, but the fact that the studio would apparently like to include one is a generally good sign. As photo mode is more of a luxury than a critical necessity, it’s not surprising that it hasn’t already been addressed with the important changes that have been made so far.

What A Baldur’s Gate 3 Photo Mode Would Entail

The most basic necessary functions of a Baldur’s Gate 3 photo mode would be a camera that can be re-positioned, which might actually be the most challenging aspect to implement. Due to the vaguely isometric angle of the game, Baldur’s Gate 3 interiors might not always be designed to look their best from low angles. Angle restrictions on in-door photography could solve this, and simply letting players judge whether a photo was worth taking or not wouldn’t be the end of the world either.

Beyond the basics, modern photo modes often implement a number of more sophisticated features that often mimic aspects of manual photography. Controlling the exposure and adjusting the focal distance and depth-of-field are a couple of major options that can help produce more appealing images. Other features might apply editing effects, like filters, or change the world itself by adjusting the time of day or lighting. The world controls might not work well in Baldur’s Gate 3, which doesn’t have a natural day-night cycle, but the rest of the standard suite would be reasonable to attempt.

A BG3 Photo Mode Would Memorialize Campaigns

As a traditional party-based RPG, Baldur’s Gate 3 places a big emphasis on an exciting journey with memorable companions, which makes it the perfect fit for a photo mode. Being able to document the steps along the way helps preserve some of the emotional heights and humorous moments that can occur throughout the game, making for the perfect photo album at the end. Games like Final Fantasy 15 show just how much value this can add to the experience, even if a Baldur’s Gate 3 camera couldn’t be incorporated as an actual in-game item due to the medieval fantasy influence.

The beautiful character models and environmental details of Baldur’s Gate 3 often don’t get to truly shine in Baldur’s Gate 3 due to the distanced perspective, which would also make photo mode a valuable way to get more mileage out of these assets. At the moment, up-close views are mostly restricted to dialogues with characters, which ultimately feels like an under-utilization of the world. While many photo modes are about preserving images of what a game world looks like, Baldur’s Gate 3’s could also be the best way to really look at it to begin with.

Getting characters into corners can be a good way to manipulate the camera into interesting close-ups in Baldur’s Gate 3.

Adding a photo mode to Baldur’s Gate 3 could certainly be a dangerous venture, as it would no doubt lead to some players sinking even more hours into the addictive game. It’s hard not to imagine just how much it could add to the experience, however, especially when it does seem like there’s a real possibility of the feature being implemented at some point in the future. A Baldur’s Gate 3 campaign is one of the most memorable journeys in modern gaming, and a photo mode would be the perfect way to keep those memories around forever.
