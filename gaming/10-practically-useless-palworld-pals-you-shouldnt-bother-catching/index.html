10 Practically Useless Palworld Pals You Shouldnt Bother Catching

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1400 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Some Pals in Palworld are so useless that there’s no point in catching them, with at least 10 falling into this category. Each Pal has an active ability and set worker skills, which determine their usefulness in combat or as a worker in a player’s base. The game requires a balance of combat and worker Pals to maximize a player’s time and effort both away from and on their base.

Literature Review

Palworld is filled with 111 Pals to catch for combat or base work, but some are so useless that catching them holds little to no value.

Palworld is filled with various catchable creatures that help lighten the load, but some Pals are so useless that there is no point in catching them. Palworld currently has a roster of 111 unique Pals to catch, with more confirmed for later releases. Technically, there are a few more, but the same Pal with a different type is officially the same number as the initial Pal. Each Pal also has an active ability, such as being used as a weapon or a rideable mount. Along with an active skill, Pals come with set worker skills and random passive skills. The latter is important when deciding if a Pal will be used for combat or as a worker. Worker skills come into effect regarding a player’s base, as some Pals may not be good in combat but have beneficial worker levels that make them great to catch. It is vital to have a good balance of combat and worker Pals to maximize a player’s time and effort away from and on base.

10 Rushoar (#020) Type: Ground

Rushoar is a Pal that can be used for the first two hours of Palworld but then drops into obscurity. It’s one of the first mounts available to players, but a level later surpassed by the fastest mount in Palworld​​​​​​, the Direwolf. The only worker skill it has is mining, but only at level one, and is beaten out by other easy-to-catch Pals. Even its active skill of increasing mining efficiency, when mounted, is negligible as mining is not that tedious of a process to begin with.

9 Vixy (#014) Type: Neutral

Vixy is much like Mau in that it has one very niche reason to bother with capturing it. It has weak stats and attacks, making it pointless to attempt combat. Worker skill-wise, it can gather or farm, but other Pals can fill that role and supply more. The benefit of Vixy is that its active skill allows it to occasionally dig up Pal Spheres at a ranch. Though this is incredibly useful in the early game, late-game when better Pal Spheres are required, Vixy becomes more pointless to use.

8 Grintale (#052) Type: Neutral

Grintale is a mixed bag, but even as a boss, it is better killed for parts than capturing. It has decent enough overall stats that are useful in combat, but being a Neutral type hurts it. This is somewhat saved by its skill that boosts its Neutral attacks while mounted but exposes the player to more danger. Grintale’s biggest drawback is that it has no appeal as a worker, only helping with gathering.

7 Hangyu (#032) Type: Ground

Hangyu, along with being one of the more controversial Pals in the game, is also useless. It boasts one of the weakest stat combinations in the game, making it pointless for combat. The active skill allows it to be used as a glider that will raise the player into the air, but this can be done with the glider or a more useful flying mount. The only saving grace of Hangyu, and its Ice variant Hangyu Cryst, is that the worker skills are decent. That said, the Pal is still beat out by other, better, late-game Pals.

6 Depresso (#017) Type: Dark

Depresso has been a fan-favorite Pal, but its usefulness in the game is slim despite its appeal. Like other early-game Pals, Depresso has stats so weak that using it in combat is pointless. The active skill is also very situational, as it will occasionally increase its movement speed. For an early-game Pal, the worker skills it can cover are helpful, but once again is quickly surpassed by other Pals.

5 Fuddler (#022) Type: Ground

Fuddler is another Ground Pal with low stats, making it useless for combat. Its active skill is not much better, as it will help players locate ore veins. This would be helpful in other games, but Palworld veins are easy to find and have common spawnpoints that are utilized often. The skill could still be helpful while traveling, but if traveling lightly, ore veins will be ignored anyway. Fuddler is also a decent early-game worker but, like Hangyu, will fall off quickly.

4 Fuack (#006) Type: Water

Fuack is one of the starter choices in Palworld, and while it can satisfy as an early-game worker, that’s all it can do. Though Fuack’s attack is usable, the rest of its stats make it too weak to consider for any fights after level 10. Its active skill, sliding into and knocking an enemy away, is situational and a copy of Penking’s exclusive move, Emperor Slide. Overall, Fuack is another Pal that can be usable in the first hours of play but then falls off hard enough that it is not worth catching in the first place.

3 Cawgnito (#044) Type: Dark

Cawgnito, while physically having a great design, has one niche purpose and is useless outside of that. That purpose is that its active skill allows it to launch a surprise attack on a target Pal. However, this skill is in the middle of the pack damage-wise and is not helped by Cawgnito’s average stats. Coupled with Cawgnito’s lack of worker skills outside of lumbering, its only purpose in the game is to farm Neutral Pals, which others will still do better.

How To Breed Pals in Palworld Stronger and rarer creatures can be found while breeding Pals in Palworld ultimately making some of the best workers and fighters that can be used.

2 Mau (#024) Type: Dark

The Egyptian-inspired Mau is another Pal with a purpose so niche it’s pointless to catch. Mau’s stats are horrific, and while it does have some good attack moves, it won’t stay alive long enough to use them. It only has one good worker skill, meaning players need to build around it. This skill is Mau’s saving grace, as when deployed at a ranch, it will occasionally dig up a gold coin. However, to make this a reasonable venture, players need to dedicate a whole base to multiple Maus, with the return being a measly amount of coins.

1 Woolipop (#034) Type: Neutral

As it stands now, Woolipop is the most useless Pal to catch in Palworld. Even though its design may be fantastic, it has no benefit for players. It is not good for combat; it can only work in ranches, and its key drop is useless. When on ranches, Woolipops will drop cotton candy, and while that is an interesting idea, the consumable gives little nutrition or sanity. This makes it so, unless given a future purpose, Woolipop will forever be the most useless Pal. Overall, Palworld is still a game that gives players the freedom to make what they want work if the time is put in. However, not everyone wants to put that commitment in, and the cooler Pals with stronger abilities or uses will outshine those below them. This leaves Palworld in the unfortunate situation of having at least 10 Pals so useless that there is no point in catching them.
