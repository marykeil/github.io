Trading This Legendary Pokémon Is About To Get A Lot Easier In Pokémon HOME

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

A rare Legendary Pokémon is losing its value in Pokémon HOME’s trading system due to its release in Pokémon GO. The Legendary creature was highly sought after in Pokémon HOME because it was difficult to obtain in the mainline games. Other Legendaries in Pokémon HOME have also lost their trade value when they became available in Pokémon GO.

Literature Review

One of the most valuable Legendary Pokémon for trades in HOME’s GTS is losing its value as a token with its upcoming release in Pokémon GO.

A Legendary Pokémon that is currently considered a valuable trading token in Pokémon HOME will get a lot easier to trade following a new release. While HOME is mostly a storage service where players can deposit their creatures from the various games in the franchise, it does have some helpful features, such as trading systems and connection between Pokémon HOME and Scarlet and Violet, for instance. One of the best systems in the app is called Global Trade System, or GTS. This allows players to deposit a Pokémon they want to get rid of and ask for a specific creature.

Alternatively, players can search for a Pokémon that they want and see what players are asking for – if they have what is solicited, they can consolidate the trade. In GTS, however, there is an unspoken tier of creatures that are considered premium and are often offered for rare creatures. Due to its rarity and being locked behind a paid DLC, Raging Bolt from Pokémon Indigo Disk is a valuable trading token that can be used to acquire offered Shiny Legendaries and other hard-to-catch creatures, such as Iron Crown. Now, a valuable Legendary is losing its worth in Pokémon HOME.

Enamorus’ Release In Pokémon GO Reduces Its Value In HOME

The dual Fairy- and Flying-type Legendary, Enamorus, is going to become a lot easier to trade in Pokémon HOME, thus reducing its value in GTS trades. The Legendary, originally introduced in Generation 8’s Pokémon Legends: Arceus, is being released in Pokémon GO. The announcement came through an official post on the Pokémon GO page on X. Enamorus is being added to Elite Raids in Pokémon GO in February. Despite Elite Raids being harder to participate in than regular ones, being able to obtain multiple copies from GO will eliminate Enamorus’ reign as a highly sought trading token in GTS.

The exact date when Enamorus will be added to Pokémon GO remains to be announced by developer Niantic. It will arrive in the game in its Incarnate Form.

Enamorus’ Rarity Put It In A Two-Year Reign In Pokémon HOME

What made Enamorus so valuable as a trading token for the rarest of Pokémon in HOME’s GTS was the fact that it was so difficult to obtain. In Legends: Arceus, Enamorus is a late-game acquisition that can only be unlocked after a gruesome set of tasks. Not only does the player have to catch Landorus, Tornadus, and Thundurus, but they also have to level each of these Pokémon’s entries in the Pokédex to level 10. This is a very time-consuming feat. Only after they have accomplished this will they be able to unlock the Request to catch Enamorus.

              Pokémon’s Indigo Disk DLC Creates A Paradox For Scarlet & Violet’s Legendaries   A certain encounter in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet’s Indigo Disk DLC has created a paradox centered on the games’ mascot Legendary Pokémon.

The creature’s value was somewhat reduced in Pokémon HOME’s GTS when it was released in another game: Pokémon Scarlet and Violet. Its value did not drop significantly, however, due to some notable factors. In the Gen 9 games, Enamorus can only be unlocked if players have purchased the Indigo Disk DLC. This means that Enamorus is locked behind a paywall in Gen 9. Additionally, players also have to reach the end of The Indigo Disk, which means they have to conclude the story for the base game and the campaign for the Teal Mask before even starting The Indigo Disk.

Another worthy detail is that, as a Legendary creature, there is only one copy of Enamorus per save file. As such, both in Pokémon Legends: Arceus and Pokémon Scarlet and Violet’s Indigo Disk DLC, players could only capture one Enamorus after dozens of hours of gameplay. The large time investment is one of the important factors when establishing Enamorus as one of the most valuable trading tokens in Pokémon HOME’s GTS. With Enamorus’ addition to Pokémon GO’s Elite Raids, its trade value should fall significantly. When it reaches normal raids in the mobile game, its value will likely collapse.

Of course, Enamorus will still be a Legendary creature. Considering the unofficial trading market in GTS, Enamorus will still be tradable for other less rare Legendary creatures and perhaps even some Shiny Pokémon. It will, however, not be requested for high-value trades, even if continues to be offered by some players. Nevertheless, Enamorus’ two-year reign as one of the most valuable Pokémon in HOME’s trading system will come to an end – this is, however, the natural flow of things over the past years and Enamorus is just another trading token that has lost its shine.

Before Enamorus, Other Legendaries’ Values Fell In Pokémon HOME

This is not the first time a Legendary has lost its trading value in GTS. In the recent past, creatures like Gen 8’s Legendary mascots were used as a token to obtain hard-to-catch Pokémon and Shiny Legendaries, for instance, but as soon as they became available in Pokémon GO, their high value in Pokémon HOME was eliminated. Zacian and Zamazenta are still requested for lesser Legendary trades and, on occasion, some Shinies, but are not used for grabbing a Raging Bolt or Iron Crown, for instance. Instead, these Paradox creatures are currently some of the most valuable Pokémon in GTS.

It is impossible to trade Mythical Pokémon in GTS.

Similarly, some of the most recent Legendaries to lose their high trade value are Regieleki and Regidrago. The two creatures, which were first introduced with Sword and Shield’s Crown Tundra DLC, were some of the rarest creatures in GTS and would be requested for the most valuable trades. They gained their value from only being obtainable in Crown Tundra, the second paid DLC for the Gen 8 games. After they both debuted in Pokémon GO’s raids, however, their worth in Pokémon HOME also collapsed, transforming them into trivial Legendary trade currency.

It should come as no surprise that Enamorus is losing its worth as a valuable trade token in GTS, as such a thing is natural with time and more releases across the several Pokémon games. Nevertheless, Enamorus held its spot for over two years, so having it lose its value is still memorable. Other creatures recently became valuable for trading, like Terapagos and the Legendary-inspired Paradox creatures added with Indigo Disk, as well as the Legendaries from the Teal Mask. As more releases occur, some Legendaries will lose their worth in Pokémon HOME, while others will rise to the occasion.
