Remaking This Pokémon Spin-Off Would Be A Perfect Switch 2 Launch Title

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

The rumored Gen 5 remakes for the Nintendo Switch 2 could include a remake of an old Pokémon spin-off series. The Nintendo Switch’s touchscreen and gyroscopic controls make it a perfect fit for a remake or sequel. The series could potentially be developed by a secondary team like Creatures or HAL Laboratory, allowing Game Freak to focus on main series titles.

Literature Review

If the Nintendo Switch 2 rumors are true, then this Pokémon spin-off series desperately needs a remake or a sequel to fit the hardware’s features.

There is one specific Pokémon game spin-off that would be perfect for the rumored Nintendo Switch 2 and its leaked specifications. Currently, the majority of the rumors are about an eventual Generation 5 remake, which would see Pokémon Black and White and Pokémon Black 2 and White 2’s Unova region remade for the current console generation. Other than the numerical logic of remaking Gen 5 Pokémon games after the release of the Gen 4 remakes, namely Pokémon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl, there are also several other hints at the rumored games within the Gen 9 titles.

The Blueberry Academy of the Indigo Disk DLC for Scarlet and Violet, for instance, is confirmed to be located in the Unova region and several of its students are related to key characters from the area, such as important Gym Leaders. The past and future theme is also recurrent in Gen 9 and Gen 5, which sparks even more speculation. Though not officially announced, Gen 5 remakes are almost considered a certainty, but one specific Pokémon spin-off could be a perfect remake for the highly-rumored Nintendo Switch 2 console, should it be announced soon.

Pokémon Ranger Series Could Be Remade For Nintendo Switch 2

A spin-off title in the franchise that should be remade for the rumored new console should be Pokémon Ranger. This series, composed of three Nintendo DS games, has players control a Ranger, whose mission is to preserve the locations they patrol. Rangers subdue menacing Pokémon with a device called a capture styler, which is essentially a special spinning top. Though the Nintendo DS games used the console’s touch screen and the special stylus, this gameplay feature could be translated to the Nintendo Switch and, given the rumors about the new console, it even makes sense.

Pokémon Ranger Remake Would Be A Perfect Nintendo Switch 2 Launch Title

The Nintendo Switch console has a touch screen, which could be used in case the rumored second version of hardware comes with a stylus of sorts, in an attempt to recreate the popular Nintendo DS. The latest Nintendo Switch 2 rumors indicate that the alleged console will have an 8-inch LCD screen, which is significantly larger than the 6.2-inch panel of the standard Switch and even the 7-inch display of the Switch OLED model. Such a screen, if it continues to be touch-sensitive, would be perfect for receiving Pokémon Ranger remakes or, perhaps, even a sequel in the series.

The titles were not met with critical acclaim – the original game’s critic score on Metacritic, for instance, is 69 out of 100, while the user score is 6.7 out of 10. However, they were innovative and brilliantly utilized the Nintendo DS’ capabilities. The spin-off series could get a second chance with a remake or a sequel for the current or next generation of consoles by Nintendo. A new Pokémon Ranger could even serve as a launch title for the rumored Switch 2. The original games weren’t developed by Game Freak, and this could be the case with the remake.

The Ranger series games were co-developed by Creatures and HAL Laboratory, both Nintendo subsidiaries, but not the main engine fueling new Pokémon games. That is Game Freak’s responsibility and the developer is likely already occupied with the next title for the main series, be that the rumored Gen 5 remakes, or a new Legends: Arceus-like game. It could even be working on the first Gen 10 titles, as they are probably set to arrive at some point in late 2025. Nintendo and The Pokémon Company could allocate a Pokémon Ranger remake to a secondary dev like Creatures or HAL Laboratory.

If the companies did that, then they would not have to sacrifice or postpone any of the releases planned for the mainline series, as Game Freak would likely be in charge of those. Something similar occurred during Gen 8. Though they are remakes of main series games, Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl’s development was actually outsourced and set in the hands of ILCA. This action likely allowed Game Freak to focus on its work on Pokémon Legends: Arceus, an experimental title with a different gameplay style that proved to be a success, both commercially and in terms of reception.

Nintendo has recently been revisiting its older Pokémon titles and either giving them remakes or sequels. New Pokémon Snap and Detective Pikachu Returns are simply two of the most recent examples of this deed, and none of them actually involves Game Freak. The former was outsourced to Bandai Namco Studios, while the latter was developed by Creatures, Nintendo’s subsidiary. A few years before, Pokémon Mystery Dungeon also received a new title, called Rescue Team DX.

Like the previously mentioned games, Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Rescue Team DX was outsourced, as it was developed by Spike Chunsoft with no registered involvement of Game Freak.

How Pokémon Ranger Could Work On Nintendo Switch 2

Other than the obvious usage of the touchscreen, the Ranger series could easily work on the rumored Nintendo Switch 2 as much as it could work on the current generation of consoles. The hardware is well-known for its versatility in gameplay, with its docked and undocked forms. In its docked form with Joy-Cons detached from the console, players could use the controllers’ gyroscope system to rotate and control the spinning top used in Pokémon Ranger games to control creatures. Of course, this could also be done with the console undocked and the controllers separated from the rest of the hardware.

In handheld mode, with both Joy-Cons attached to the Nintendo Switch or rumored Switch 2 console, the rotating movement could be achieved by using both of the analog sticks to control the motion of the spinning top. There are many ways of making Pokémon Ranger work on a Nintendo Switch-styled console, even if its main focus is not its touchscreen. This is mostly due to the hybrid hardware’s versatility and what grants it its popularity.

There have been no indications so far that Nintendo plans on remaking the Ranger series for the Pokémon franchise, but there are many factors that show it should. The games are a perfect fit for the current and alleged following generation of consoles due to how the gameplay can be translated into new mechanisms, as well as the company’s intent on remaking or giving sequels to older spin-off series. Even if it is pure speculation at this point, Pokémon Ranger would be a perfect game for an eventual Nintendo Switch 2 console.
