10 Crucial Baldur s Gate 3 Camp Cutscenes That Add More Context To The Story

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 2100 words 10 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Cutscenes in Baldur’s Gate 3 provide important information and advance quests and can lead to romance, adding depth to the game’s story. Many cutscenes involve the party’s companions, and interacting with them regularly is necessary to trigger these scenes and learn more about their characters. Key cutscenes occur at the party’s camp, especially during nighttime rests, revealing secrets, introducing key characters, and shaping the overall narrative of the game.

Literature Review

Baldur’s Gate 3 has tons of cutscenes to introduce characters and further its epic plot, but some are vital to add context to the complex story.

Throughout Baldur’s Gate 3, cutscenes are used to further the story, allowing the plot to be conveyed clearly in beautiful cinematic scenes. Playing out like a movie, these scenes will deliver important information, move questlines along, and sometimes lead to romance. While these cutscenes can take place in many different locations, some of the most important will happen back at the party’s camp as the team beds down for the night. [Warning: The following article contains spoilers for Baldur’s Gate 3.]More often than not, cutscenes in BG3 will involve one or more of the many companions who have joined the party to face off against the Absolute. To trigger many of them, the characters need to be spoken to on a regular basis to get them to open up and share their companion quests during conversation. While some cutscenes are particular to specific characters, the ones here are key as they add vital context to BG3’s overall story.

10 Learning Astarion’s Secret Act 1 Cutscene Reveals His Vampiric Nature

Very early in Act 1 the party will come across a boar that has been drained of all its blood. If Astarion is in the party when this happens, he will have something to say about it, cryptically mentioning vampires. However, at the next long rest, the player character will wake up, just in time to find Astarion about to take a bite out of them. It turns out Astarion is a vampire spawn, and this is the beginning of his personal quest. This is also the starting point for anyone looking to romance the pale elf, as letting him drink will grant a decent amount of approval. The nighttime cutscene will pick back up in the morning as the rest of the companions discover Astarion’s true nature, which marks a turning point in him becoming more open.

9 Meeting Wyll’s Patron Act 1 Cutscene Introduces Mizora As Key Character

When not playing as Wyll one key camp cutscene is the first time his patron is revealed. In a swirling mass of black, a cambion named Mizora will appear, greeting the party with a smirk. When she arrives, there are two possible scenes that can play out that depend on whether or not the party killed Karlach as requested. In one scene, Wyll is rewarded with a new armor, the Infernal Robe, and Mizora will praise her ‘pup’ for doing a good job. However, if the party chose to spare Karlach, Mizora will be very unhappy and punish Wyll with a new devil form. This scene adds context to Wyll and makes it clear that his patron is not a passive one, indicating that Mizora will show up later in BG3.

8 Magic Lessons With Gale Learning More About Gale’s Connection to Mystra And Magic

At some point, after enough approval has been gained, Gale will appear to be looking at an illusion in camp. Speaking to him will reveal more about his backstory and his link to Mystra, the goddess of magic. While it is more often encountered in Act 1, this cutscene can also trigger in Act 2 after Mystra has instructed Gale to use the orb to destroy the Absolute. This version is slightly more reflective, as Gale notes he is pondering what he lost. This is a crucial scene to understand Gale, both who he was before the events of BG3 and who he is now, as he speaks of his devotion and love for magic. Gale will also offer a magic lesson which all classes can participate in with mixed results. As well as offering insight into Gale’s connection to Mystra and the Weave, this scene is also the opportunity to start a romance with Gale.

7 An Alliance With Voss Late Act 1 Cutscene Is The Catalyst For Lae’zel’s Personal Quest

After visiting the Rosymorn Monastery and completing the first part of Lae’zel’s personal quest, the camp will get an unexpected visitor. When attempting to leave the Mountain Pass area for the first time after meeting with Vlaakith and entering the Astral Prism, Kith’rak Voss will arrive at camp. He wishes to speak with Lae’zel and explains that he knows Vlaakith has been lying to the githyanki people.

Voss wants to team up to overthrow Vlaakith and believes that the key to doing so is the strange artifact the party carries. Here, Voss can be killed or listened to, after which he will arrange to meet up once again at Baldur’s Gate, where he will explain more. This scene is key to helping Lae’zel see the truth about Vlaakith, as Voss backs up what has already been learned from the Dream Guardian, and adds context to the artifact protecting the party.

6 Elminster’s Important Message Act 2 Cutscene With Famous D&D Character Sent By Mystra

At the beginning of Act 2, as the party enters the Shadow-Cursed Lands, an old man will approach the group. He will explain that he wants to speak with Gale, and he can be invited to come to camp, where it is revealed that this is Elminster, the famous wizard. Elminster has a very important and grave message for Gale from Mystra regarding the Netherese Orb he has inside him. Elminster has been sent by Mystra to tell Gale to use the orb to kill the Absolute, adding that if he does this, she will consider forgiveness. Elminster shows Gale a spell to stabilize the orb temporarily before leaving to allow him to digest this request. This scene does a good job of showing how even the gods are concerned with the threat of the Absolute and how Gale’s relationship with Mystra is still very strained. As well as adding a new way to end the game, this cutscene also adds another layer to Gale, who must now weigh his own life against the fate of the world.

5 Learning About Thaniel Act 2 Cutscene Is Critical To Lifting The Shadow Curse

When entering the Shadow-Cursed Lands in Act 2, it is important to speak with Halsin as soon as possible. He will explain his connection to the region and about the spirit of the lands, who takes the form of a boy named Thaniel. Halsin will explain that he has known Thaniel his whole life but lost contact with him after the Shadow Curse fell on the area. As Thaniel is key to lifting the curse and recruiting Halsin as a full companion, this is an important conversation to have. Without this initial conversation, it might not be clear that Halsin and Thaniel play a role in helping this region. Although Art Cullagh can be spoken to before talking with Halsin, this can sometimes lead to the quest not progressing properly, so it’s best to talk to Halsin first. This questline is notoriously bugged and also has many ways in which it can fail. One of the most common is Art dying when the inn is attacked after speaking with Isobel. For this reason, it is best to avoid speaking with Isobel, as her blessing is not needed to progress in the area.

4 Meeting The Dark Urge’s Butler The First Clue For Many Dark Urge Characters To Their Past

For those playing as the Dark Urge, there are many cutscenes that add context and further the personal story of the character. However, one of the most important comes after the murder of Alfira or the dragonborn bard Quil, if Alfira was knocked out. One night, as the rest of the party sleeps, a strange fiend will appear, claiming to be the Dark Urge’s butler. He introduces himself as Sceleritas Fel and provides the first hints as to who the Dark Urge was before the events of BG3. He explains that he’s been searching for his lost master, and it was the smell of the murder that led him back. Sceleritas will actively encourage more violence and gift the character with the Deathstalker Mantle, one of the best cloaks in BG3.

3 Aylin Helps Shadowheart Remember Act 2 Cutscene Moving Shadowheart’s Personal Quest On

At the end of Act 2, if she has rejected Shar and helped save the Nightsong, then Shadowheart has a very important cutscene with Dame Aylin. Once Ketheric Thorm is defeated and the celebrations have been had, then Aylin and Isobel will appear at camp. Speaking to them with Shadowheart in the active party will lead to a conversation between the aasimar about Shadowheart’s past.

Aylin is able to help Shadowheart unlock memories that had been partially stolen and changed by Shar and her worshipers. This reveals that Shadowheart and her parents were followers of Selûne and that Shadowheart had been stolen as a child, her memories of the event altered, and that her parents are both still alive, being held prisoner by Shar. This will set Shadowheart on a new mission to rescue her parents and abandon Shar for good.

2 Orin’s Kidnap Reveal Five Different Scenes To Set Up Orin’s Nefarious Plan

In Act 3, after entering Wyrm’s Rock Fortress, then either long resting or entering the sewers, an event will trigger. Orin the Red, the Chosen of Bhaal, will appear at camp, taking the form of one of the characters there. Orin’s form can be one of five characters, Gale, Halsin, Lae’zel, Minthara, or young orphan Yenna, based on whoever the player character has the lowest approval with. The scene will play out differently depending on who Orin is impersonating. For example, ‘Lae’zel’ will grab Yenna and attempt to slit her throat, while ‘Halsin’ appears to as those he is about to kill Scratch. Whatever the cutscene, Orin will play with the party before revealing her true form, explaining that she has taken the character and will kill them unless the party kills Gortash for her.

1 First Meeting With The Dream Guardian Adds Context And Reframes The Party’s Mission Going Forward

Early in Act 1, a cutscene will trigger with the party beginning to feel ill, with Lae’zel suggesting an extreme response. However, as the party sleeps, a new cutscene will unfold, introducing one of the most important characters in the game: the Dream Guardian. This mysterious figure, which can be customized at character creation, will say that they have arrived just in time to stop the party from transforming into mind flayers. Then, they will go on to explain that they are the ones who have been protecting the group from the influence of the Absolute and well as catching them when they fell from the Nautiloid. This is really the start of the main questline in BG3, giving more context to the stakes and clarifying goals beyond getting rid of the tadpoles. As far as cutscenes that add context, this is by far one of the most important in Baldur’s Gate 3.
