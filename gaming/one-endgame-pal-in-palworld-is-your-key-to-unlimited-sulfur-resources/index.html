One Endgame Pal In Palworld Is Your Key To Unlimited Sulfur Resources

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1300 words 6 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

With the right combination of skills, Astegon makes mining in Palworld faster and more efficient by shattering multiple sulfur deposits at once. In order to catch Astegon, players must defeat it as a boss, or breed it from other Pals. Even after Astegon is in the party, players will need to teach it a certain skill and craft some gear to improve its mining efficiency.

Literature Review

Mining can be exhausting in Palworld, but one endgame Pal can be a huge help in an unexpected way. Here’s how to speed up a sulfur operation.

Tired of constant sulfur mining in Palworld? One endgame Pal can be a big help - and not in the way one might expect. As an open-world survival game with heavy emphasis on crafting, Palworld inevitably involves a lot of resource gathering. It’s a big part of the gameplay loop: set a goal, track down the materials for it, craft it, and reap the benefits. This becomes especially prevalent in the mid-late game, when players start regularly crafting ammo and need a constant supply of sulfur.

It’s an intensely satisfying, if familiar formula. But sometimes, it’s easy to get bogged down. Especially when crafting many items at once, or high-level items like those unlocked by Legendary Schematics in Palworld, tons of materials are required. Even if a player has easy access to a few convenient resource nodes, they’ll have to burn through energy gathering, then wait for the nodes to respawn before repeating the process all over again. It’s tedious, but there is a way around it. However, players will have to wait until the late game to employ this strategy.

Palworld: Astegon Helps With Easy Mining

With the right combination of gear and abilities, Astegon can make mining in Palworld as easy as pie. Simply fly Astegon over a mining target, activate the Lightning Strike skill, and watch as all the ore veins below burst into collectible drops. Then, land Astegon, hop off, and gather as much as the player character can carry before doing it all over again. This makes mining go much more quickly. Instead of focusing on one node at a time and mining until they drop, the player character can shatter multiple sulfur deposits at once with the press of a single button.

But why Astegon, and why Lightning Strike? Surely there are stronger Palworld Pals who work just as well, or at least others who are a little easier to obtain. It may take a little more work to catch or breed Astegon, but that extra effort is justified by its unique skills. Firstly, Astegon has the ability to fly. The utility of flight in mining cannot be overstated: many sulfur (and other mineral) nodes are located atop mountains, or on high outcroppings that would otherwise be difficult to reach.

Flight also allows Astegon to find distant mining nodes from a high vantage point. It can move from one mining node to another more quickly, and strike them with pinpoint accuracy from the air. In particular, it’s easiest to find sulfur veins on and around Mount Obsidian, the giant volcano in the southwest of the Palpagos Islands. A trip here aboard Astegon is quick, easy, and immensely rewarding, no matter where the player’s Palworld base is located. Players don’t have to worry about fast traveling when their Pals can fly.

Astegon also has a useful Partner Skill called Black Ankylosaur, which, per its in-game description, “improves the player’s mining efficiency while riding Astegon.” In practice, this means that the player’s damage to mining nodes is increased when riding Astegon. In essence, all mining nodes will break more quickly when the player character is astride Astegon - whether they use Lightning Strike or a regular old pickaxe. With this skill, Lightning Strike should do enough damage to break up any ore vein instantly.

Palworld: How To Make Astegon A Mining Powerhouse Lightning Strike, Breeding Choices, & Gear Crafting

Now, it’s worth noting that Astegon doesn’t know Lightning Strike naturally. There are two ways to learn the skill, but the most reliable is breeding. Breeding Astegon in Palworld is by no means easy, and will require a couple of valuable late-game Pals. However, breeding Astegon can increase the chances of it spawning with Lightning Strike already known. Bred Pals inherit skills, and while nothing is guaranteed, a little luck can save players a lot of time. Cryolinx and Grizzbolt can be combined to create an Astegon, and since Grizzbolt learns Lightning Strike naturally, it may pass the skill down.

Otherwise, if a player catches the Alpha Boss version of Astegon, they’ll have to teach it Lightning Strike manually. Astegon naturally occurs in the Destroyed Mineshaft, which is conveniently located on the slopes of Mount Obsidian. The closest waypoint is the Mount Obsidian Midpoint; just head west from there. Astegon is a level 38 boss, which means the player and their equipped Pals will need to be around the same level to beat it comfortably. As a Dark/Dragon-type Pal, it’s especially weak to Ice-type skills. Players will also need a Legendary Sphere to catch it.

But the Alpha Boss Astegon doesn’t learn Lightning Strike as it levels up. Luckily, Pals can be taught skills they don’t normally have with the appropriate piece of Skill Fruit. Palworld takes the concept of a skill tree very literally: as players wander the Palpagos Islands, they’ll find gnarled trees bearing brightly patterned fruits. These are Skill Fruit Trees, and their output can be fed to Pals in order to teach them skills they wouldn’t otherwise learn.

Unfortunately, the Skill Fruits that grow on each particular tree are random; there’s no way to guarantee a particular skill. Players using this method will either have to be persistent enough to check Skill Fruit Trees repeatedly, or be lucky enough to have a Lightning Strike Fruit squirreled away.

Once Astegon is in the party and knows Lightning Strike, the final step is to craft the Astegon Saddle so the player character can ride it. This is a level 47 item unlockable on Palworld’s Technology tree, requiring four Technology points to learn. With the recipe unlocked, players will also need a Pal Gear Workbench, and the following materials to craft Astegon’s Saddle:

30 Leather 50 Ingots 55 Paldium Fragments 50 Refined Ingots

But with all that out of the way, there’s no further barrier to Astegon’s mining. Simply fly it to the desired outcropping, hover over a gathering node, and blast away with Lightning Strike until the ground is covered with mineral pickups. Naturally, this should work just as well for other resources, but with Astegon’s bonus to mining damage, clearing out rocks and minerals may go a bit faster than everything else. No matter where or how it’s used, this unique combination of skills and gear will make Astegon a miner’s best friend in Palworld.
