10 Important Features Helldivers 2 Doesnt Tell You About

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Helldivers 2 ’s tutorial fails to provide important details, leaving players lacking crucial information about the game’s features. The game introduces new gameplay from its predecessor, requiring even returning players to adapt and learn new strategies for combat. Players should be cautious of certain hazards, prioritize certain enemies, and take advantage of hidden opportunities to maximize their success in the game.

Literature Review

Helldivers 2 has a basic tutorial, but it leaves players in the dark about a variety of helpful mechanics, some of which are crucial for survival.

Fighting on the front lines of Super Earth’s war against the Terminids and Automatons in Helldivers 2 requires significant combat prowess, but the game’s tutorial leaves out a number of details pertinent to each mission. The third-person co-op shooter is off to an incredible start, rocketing to the top of Steam’s bestsellers, but there may be some players frustrated by the game’s trial-and-error methodology for teaching how to dish out managed democracy. A tutorial delineates the most basic controls, but a noticeable lack of tooltips leave many Helldivers 2 features unexplained or even entirely obscured. Helldivers 2’s gameplay is a significant departure from its predecessor, despite its shared premise. The first Helldivers is a top-down shooter released in 2015, so while the general understanding of the setting and similar game mechanics like Stratagems can help longtime fans get a head start, the switch to a third-person perspective introduces a lot of new quirks to the series. Helldivers 2 eases players into higher difficulty settings, but it doesn’t go so far as to even mention certain maneuvers are possible, how certain currencies are obtained, and more.

10 Selecting A Mission Opens A Multiplayer Lobby When The Hellpods Are Ready, Other Players Can Join

Quickplay is a readily apparent option on the Galactic War map, but that will only let players join others’ missions. If sights are set on a certain mission or operation, random teammates can be found by selecting a mission, and simply waiting. Helldivers 2 is always online, but it doesn’t necessarily make it clear when players are searching for teammates. Once the Hellpods – where players select their Stratagems and equipment prior to a mission – are ready, waiting around on the deck of the Super Destroyer should eventually result in teammates joining.

Helldivers 2 Matchmaking: Co-Op & Quickplay Multiplayer Explained
Fighting on the frontlines to spread democracy is best while in a full squad, but Helldivers 2 doesn’t really make it clear how matchmaking works.

9 Automatons Are Harder Than Terminids There Are Two Fronts & Two Enemies In The Galactic War

At Helldivers 2’s launch, Super Earth’s enemies are two-fold: the bug-like Terminids, and the robotic Automatons. The game seems to push players toward the former with Galactic War objectives for recapturing Terminid space, and likely for good reason. The bugs aren’t necessarily pushovers, especially on higher difficulties, but the Automatons pose a much greater threat just by them having firearms. Some Terminids have ranged attacks, but a consolidated push by a few dozen robots slinging lead is significantly more likely to increas Helldiver mortality rates.

8 Helldivers Aren’t Great Swimmers Drowning Costs A Life

There’s not really much of a reason to ever get in a body of water during a Helldivers 2 mission, but if trying a quick swim ever looks like a convenient shortcut, players are better off going around. Helldivers can swim, they’re just not very fast, nor do they have much stamina for the activity. Only the shortest swims should be attempted, as in, none longer than a few feet. Only a few seconds after diving in, the player character will flounder, sink below the surface, and will have to be called back in as a Reinforcement.

7 Weapons Can Be Fine-Tuned & Their Flashlights Turned On Hold The Reload Button

Weapons in Helldivers 2 don’t necessarily have much customization, but they do have adaptability. However, the game never makes it apparent that certain weapon stats can be tweaked on the fly, including fire mode, rate of fire, and zoom level. Holding the reload button (default square on PS5 and R on PC) will allow these to be changed, and the weapon’s flashlight to be turned on and off. Flashlights may be especially helpful when attempting nighttime missions. A mission’s time of day can be seen from the Galactic War map when selecting it.

6 Be Careful Around Undetonated Hellbombs Damage Causes Hellbombs To Explode

Hellbombs are objective Stratagems available for certain missions, and as the name implies, they pack quite a punch. However, it’s also possible to find undetonated Hellbombs lying around, and these should be noted, because a stray bullet or even an errant melee attack can end in disaster. The Hellbomb will give a couple warning flashes before detonating, vaporizing any Helldivers in the blast radius. A bit of tactical thinking might make these latent explosives a useful tool, though.

5 POIs Have Samples, Medals, & Super Credits Valuable Currencies For Upgrades & New Equipment

Holding off swarms of enemies while trying to complete main and bonus objectives is enough to keep track of, but squads looking to really be rewarded should be on the lookout for points of interest, aka POIs. POIs often contain Samples, Medals, or Super Credits, all invaluable in Helldivers 2’s meta-progression. Samples are required for new Ship Modules, Medals are spent in Warbonds for new gear, and Super Credits are the game’s premium currency, otherwise needing to be purchased with real-world money.

4 Half-Buried Containers Can Be Opened Use Grenades Or Explosive Barrels

There’s a certain type of storage container, found half-buried in the ground, that Helldivers 2 should keep an eye out for. There is seemingly no way to open their doors, but explosive barrels next to some is a pretty good indication that they’re meant to be blown open. Grenades work as well, and they’ve usually got pretty lucrative rewards inside, including Support Weapons, Medals, various kinds of Samples, Requisition points, Super Credits, and extra ammo.

3 Samples Are Dropped When You Die, Super Credits Are Not Always Try To Pick Up Your Lost Resources

Getting dismembered in the heat of battle is just part of the Helldiver routine, but with so many different pick-ups, resources, and currencies in the game, it can be hard to keep track of them all throughout a mission. Samples are crucial for unlocking new Ship Modules, which are important for meta-progression to be effective in Helldivers 2’s higher difficulties. They can be picked up all over the map, but are dropped when the player is killed. The same is not true for Super Credits – once Super Credits have been picked up, they cannot be lost. Players hoping to recoup their Samples will have to return to where they died and retrieve them.

2 Stims Can Be Used On Squadmates Heal A Teammate At Critical Health

Stims are a vital resource on the battlefield, typically used to heal one’s own health. However, they’re in finite supply, and approaching enemies aren’t always courteous enough to allow a Resupply Stratagem. Once a teammate reaches critical health, their health bar in the lower left corner will turn red, and once this happens, other players with available Stims are able to approach them and press the interact button (default X on PS5 and E on PC) to administer one of their Stims.

1 Hellpods Can Kill Enemies Well-Placed Reinforcements Can Instakill Tough Foes

The multitude of firepower in the Helldivers’ arsenal is a distinct highlight of the game’s combat, but even unconventional means of dispensing managed democracy can be highly effective. Hellpods for respawning act as giant projectiles, and will crush any enemy they land on. It’s an especially risky maneuver, though, since there’s only a modicum of control over the drop pods, and missing a target will put the player immediately in danger. Friendlies be warned: Helldivers 2’s universal friendly fire includes getting squashed by an errant Hellpod too. Landing a Hellpod on a giant bug is one of the more satisfying strategies in Helldivers 2, and one of the many features the game doesn’t make readily apparent.

Helldivers 2
Helldivers II is the follow-up to the 2015 multiplayer top-down single-player/co-op shooter from Arrowhead Game Studios. The sequel, which has shifted gears to a third-person shooter format, drops players into the boots of elite super-soldiers who fight to protect Super Earth from invading alien forces while taking the fight to those who threaten the “democracy of Super Earth.”
