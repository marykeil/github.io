10 Early Act 3 Things To Do Before Entering The Lower City In BG3

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Rushing into the Lower City in Baldur’s Gate 3 might make you miss out on the best content in the game, so exploration is key. Rivington has plenty of opportunities for combat encounters and scuffles, so make sure your party is well-rested and prepared. Don’t miss out on important side quests and interactions, as they can lead to valuable rewards and story progression.

Literature Review

The outskirts of the city in Act 3 of Baldur’s Gate 3 have quite a surplus of hidden secrets and sidequests to pick up before moving on.

Rushing into the Lower City might seem tempting in Act 3 of Baldur’s Gate 3, but there are plenty of things to do before ever stepping foot inside Wyrm’s Rock Fortress. Burdened with a refugee crisis, the Outer City region of Rivington is at a boiling point with interesting tensions and conflicts. It’s also full of memorable characters and locations, although some are easier to stumble across than others. Proceeding onward before hitting a number of key points makes it easy to miss out on some of the best content in Baldur’s Gate 3. It might seem like major combat encounters are unlikely to arise in the midst of civilization, but it’s best to explore Rivington with a well-rested party. Whether ticking off guards or stumbling on hidden shady dealings, opportunities to get into a scuffle for potential rewards are numerous. On the other hand, a bit of tact can also go a long way in several stories within the Outer City area, and a party looking to do as much good as possible before progressing into the Lower City will have many chances as well.

Baldur’s Gate 3: Act 3 Checklist (Everything You Don’t Want To Miss)
In Act 3 of Baldur’s Gate 3, players finally arrive in the big city. With dozens of quests and encounters, keeping track of everything can be tricky.

10 Check Out The Well Venturing Downward In Act 3 Is Useful

Proceeding down the main road in Rivington reveals a well by the roadside, located across from the Open Hand Temple and in between The Rivington General and Sword Coast Couriers. Walking close enough to the well will trigger a perception check, highlighting it if any characters succeed. It’s possible to descend into the well through the bucket mechanism and find some bodies beneath, which both point to the full extent of the troubles in Rivington and provide some decent loot.

9 Find A Sharran Lookout Shadowheart Has A Baldur’s Gate 3 Arc To Complete

Any party with Shadowheart will be well-served by staying on the hunt for a Sharran lookout in the Lower City. Baldur’s Gate 3 doesn’t offer terribly specific instructions regarding where to find one, but if Shadowheart is in the active party, she’ll make a remark about looking for one when the party comes into the general vicinity of the Requisitioned Barn, where refugees are gathering in makeshift camps. The name to watch for is Ferg Drogher, a man with trim facial hair and two swords across his back.

8 Return The Missing Letters Fetch Quests Don’t Have To Be Dull

Sword Coast Couriers are in a spot of trouble thanks to some letters that have gone missing, which the party can offer to help find in Act 3. This Baldur’s Gate 3 sidequest is mostly a fairly standard affair, but there’s a little more than gold in it for Gale fans. Searching out the letters on top of the Open Hand Temple roof, which can be accessed by vine or simply misty step, will reward a party with a nice little interaction if Gale is present. For those who aren’t as focused on Gale, there’s still the gold, after all.

7 Enter The Abandoned Windmill A Secret Baldur’s Gate 3 NPC Is Inside

Wandering on up the gradated fields near the center of Rivington reveals an abandoned windmill, which luckily does not have a gnome attached to it. It does, however, have someone inside. If the party should end up wanting to kill the windmill’s occupant, the situation makes it easy, but it’s also possible to help. Regardless, it’s a choice that’s worth making and not ignoring, so although the windmill isn’t one of the most crucial locations in the Outer City, it shouldn’t be skipped.

6 Speak To The Ironhand Gnomes Wulbren Bongle Has A Big Quest To Offer

A party’s standing with the Ironhand Gnomes may be somewhat dependent on their behavior toward a couple of key gnome characters in the past, but seeking out their headquarters is a must for anyone who rescued Barcus Wroot and Wulbren Bongle. This hideout can be accessed from underneath when exploring the subterranean parts of Rivington, as the trapdoor above it is blocked from below. Wulbren is a man on a mission to destroy the Steel Watch Foundry, and he’ll happily recruit an agreeable party to it. Whether or not a party plans to ultimately go along with this scheme, it’s worth checking out, as even choosing to diverge from his instructions can result in both loot and information.

5 Meet The Harpers Find The Potion Ship In A Lower City Hideout

A group of Harpers has a Lower City hideout in the potion ship, which can be found on the left side of the road right before Wyrm’s Rock Fortress. Stopping by with Jaheira is more important than it may seem, as nefarious deeds are afoot in the city that can first be exposed by speaking to the Harpers there. The potion shop itself is worth poking around in for a recipe to brew Greater Healing potions that can be found on one of the bookshelves there.

4 Deal With The Squatters Arfur’s House Hides A Baldur’s Gate 3 Secret

The situation with the squatters in Baldur’s Gate 3 isn’t one that many parties are likely to miss in Act 3, as some refugees have taken up residence in a house at the forefront of Rivington. When the homeowner Arfur returns, he creates something of a scene in the name of reclaiming his home. What’s especially important about paying attention to the situation is that the home relates to another side activity that houses some more significant reveals. Whichever way a party chooses to deal with the squatters, it’s a good idea to snoop around the house a bit when the task is done, as Arfur has some figurative skeletons in the closet (or basement, in this case).

3 Visit Raphael The Cambion Has A Big Act 3 Story

Sharess’ Caress has much more to offer than just pleasure, and speaking to as many people as possible, there can open some interesting doors in Baldur’s Gate 3. Most important, however, is speaking to the devil Raphael, who makes a particularly interesting offer. Even if parties don’t choose to side with Raphael in Baldur’s Gate 3, conversing with him reveals some key information relevant to the main story and the different possible ways to approach the endgame.

2 See Dribbles The Clown The Baldur’s Gate 3 Circus Is A Major Destination

The circus might seem like a fun diversion in Baldur’s Gate 3, but it actually contains some of the biggest side content in Rivington. The most important thing to do at the circus is to see the performance from local legend Dribbles the Clown — and to be prepared for anything. The druid Zethino is another circus attraction that’s best not to miss, with a love test that can be a good bonding experience with a romantic interest and provide a little bit of a tie-in to a larger Act 3 plot. Stoney and Boney sell a personalized statue of the protagonist that provides a buff to anyone able to shell out some serious coin for it. Popper the Kobold can also be an interesting visit, particularly after the performance from Dribbles has already concluded.

1 Solve The Open Hand Temple Murder Baldur’s Gate 3 Has Its Own Murder Mystery

Father Lorgan of the Open Hand Temple is murdered not long before the party arrives in Rivington in Baldur’s Gate 3. Solving this mystery requires venturing into the kitchen cellars and beyond, but it’s possible to get some other work done while exploring the temple’s underbelly. Any party that kept the Sentient Amulet can finish that quest beneath the temple, and exploring this area is equally necessary to follow up on the story that Dribbles the Clown’s performance starts in Baldur’s Gate 3.
