All Skull And Bones Pets, Ranked Best To Worst

 Ella
2020-10-16 About 1500 words 7 minutes  - views   - comments  
Contents
    • SUMMARY
    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Skull and Bones offers limited cat and lemur pets, with potential for expansion. Special pets like Death’s Hand Lemur and Snow Leopard Cat are exclusive. Various pets in the game are available for purchase with in-game currency or real money.

Literature Review

Skull and Bones has a variety of pets available to keep the crew company on pirate ships; with choices of cats and lemurs, it’s hard to pick just one.

Skull and Bones allows pirating players to have more than just a simple parrot as a pet aboard their pirate ships, with parrots not even being an option at launch. This naval combat game currently only has a couple of cat or lemur animal companion options, with most of those being limited to special events. Therefore, many players are hoping that the list of available in-game pets will expand quickly. Having a pet on board a pirate ship may not be a necessity in the game, but having this option available is a fantastic feature that needs to be expanded on. Cats and lemurs in Skull and Bones are labeled as cosmetic ship items and do not do anything for the game mechanics, although one could feasibly roleplay that they are helping keep rodents out of the ship or are at least increasing the ship’s morale. The game itself focuses on naval combat, battling and looting other ships while traveling across the Indian Ocean, and can be played single-player or in multiplayer online. Of course, having a little cat with a peg leg or a lemur with an eye patch makes everything much more satisfying.

Skull And Bones: Release Date, Gameplay & Beta Access
After a long development cycle and many delays, Skull and Bones has finally received a final release date, along with a beta and pre-order options.

9 Death’s Hand Lemur - Exclusive To The Premium Edition Unlocked After Completing The Bloody Bones Legacy Quest

Only players who ordered the Premium Edition of Skull and Bones will have access to this spooky girl known as Death’s Hand. The lemur is fully equipped for a pirate life, with her own pirate hat and vest, a pistol strapped to her back, and a skull mask covering her face. She is unlocked only after completing Bloody Bones’ Legacy in the exclusive series of quests called the Ballad of Bloody Bones, which is limited to the Premium Edition. As she is safely situated aboard the player’s ship, she is sure to be a fearsome sight for anyone looking on.

8 Snow Leopard Cat - Twitch Exclusive Was Only Available As A Limited Time Twitch Drop Reward

Sadly, if players have not previously unlocked the Snow Leopard Cat, it is not currently possible to get this sweet feline. The only way to have gotten it was to have earned it as a Twitch Drop when Skull and Bones was in open beta, although many hope that it will make a return appearance in future exclusive drops. The peg leg kitty shares the same basic character model as the other cats, but something about this sweet and cuddly face and spotted fur makes it extra special.

7 Meilin The Lemur And Jyn The Cat Twitch Extension Rewards Only Available As A Limited Time Twitch Extension Reward

Two animal companions, Meilin the Lemur and Jyn the Cat, are exclusive pets available as part of the Twitch Extension Rewards program for Skull and Bones. Streamers who set up and installed this extension on their Twitch accounts were given codes for a variety of cosmetics, including these two animal friends, that they could give out to their communities.

The limited-time program began on February 13 and will not be around for very long. Of the two, Meilin is an easier pet to acquire, with streamers only needing to have everything installed and launch a stream with the extension activated. In order to get Jyn, the description reads that streamers must become a Kingpin of Twitch. Presumably, as streamers are playing Skull and Bones on Twitch with the extension there are multiple redemption codes that will be provided.

All Ship Blueprints In Skull And Bones, Ranked By Design
There are multiple ship blueprints to obtain in Skull and Bones, allowing you to craft your ideal vessel. Here are the best, ranked by their design.

6 First Mate Whiskers The Cat Purchasable From In-Game Store

First Mate Whiskers has caused a bit of a stir online as the sweet feline friend costs real-world money to purchase. While the game has Silver in-game currency that can be earned while playing, the Gold currency that is needed for First Mate Whiskers and other cosmetics must be purchased using real money. Microtransactions in a game that costs money to purchase, such as Skull and Bones, can be a bit off-putting, yet the sweet scarred face of First Mat Whiskers with its little eye patch, peg leg, and earring makes it hard not to succumb to the temptation to press buy.

5 Mr. Maroon The Lemur Purchasable From The Black Market

How he came to be on the black market is a mystery that may be best left unknown, but Mr. Maroon the lemur is for sale on this dark trade for players who want to get him on their ships. This well-dressed lemur comes with his own maroon pirate hat and outfit to always be at the peak of fashion. In the in-game black market, he costs 1000 Pieces of Eight, which is a unique currency specific to the black market.

4 Clumsy Feno The Lemur Purchasable From In-Game Store

He may be named Clumsy Feno, but this one-eyed lemur at least seems to be stable enough to remain on his little perch in the pirate ship. Clumsy Feno is available from the in-game store, but unlike most of the other pets, he is available for Silver rather than Gold. Silver is the currency that is acquired simply by playing the game and does not require real money. Clumsy Feno is also adorable with his tiny eye patch and looks well-suited for pirate life.

3 Bravo Neko The Cat Purchasable From The Black Market

Bravo Neko is a beautiful, fluffy, orange cat with a peg leg that has the sweetest facial expressions and will keep everyone on the ship company in Skull and Bones. This little friend is available on the black market in the game for a cost of 500 Pieces of Eight, the unique currency used on the black market. Although that is a lot of money on the black market, the idea of leaving such a sweet kitty friend in the hands of black market thugs seems wrong in every way.

2 Cookie The Lemur - Beta Reward Unlocked In Open Beta

The adorable pet lemur named Cookie was another limited-time reward that is not available any longer. To unlock Cookie, prospective pirates needed to play Skull and Bones during its open beta period, and more specifically, they needed to use a medium-sized ship in the beta. For those who missed out on the chance to get Cookie, at least this cute lemur looks fairly similar to Maurine the Lemur, only with a slight change in coloration. It is unknown if any of the animations are changed for the exclusive Cookie.

1 Maurine The Lemur Quest Reward

While poor Maurine doesn’t have much special going for it, Maurine is still a fun animal companion to have on the pirate ship and is much more cost-effective. Maurine is a lemur that is given as a quest reward for completing the Dressed To Kill quest. The contract is given by the Blacksmith and is very simple, only requiring a new shirt and hat for the main character. It is a simple quest and gives players a chance to have their very own pet companion in Skull and Bones. Sources: Skull and Bones Game/X
