Suicide Squad Game s Harley Quinn Is In The Worst Kind Of Danger

    • Literature Review

SUMMARY

Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League will feature free post-launch content, including new storylines and characters. The game’s story will continue through Brainiac’s exploration of the multiverse and the introduction of different versions of characters, such as Joker. The inclusion of an Elseworld Joker may impact Harley Quinn’s character development and potentially revert her back to her toxic relationship with the Joker.

Literature Review

Rocksteady has revealed Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s first post-launch character, and his inclusion may not look great for Harley Quinn.

Rocksteady has revealed Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s first post-launch playable character, and his inclusion may not look great for Harley Quinn. It had long been confirmed that the studio would be adding free post-launch content to the game with new storylines, environments, and characters, but it wasn’t clear how these would play into the game’s main campaign, considering its titular premise and the finality that comes with killing the Justice League.

[Warning: This article may contain spoilers for Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League]Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s story will continue due to Brainiac’s exploration of the multiverse, with each post-launch chapter covering a new “Elseworld”. As explained by actor Jason Issacs, Brainiac’s core motivation is to rebuild the advanced civilization he has lost, and so he attempts this by experimenting with DC characters’ DNA to open paths to different universes and allow variants of particular characters to be pulled into the Arkhamverse.

An Elseworld Joker Will Appear In Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League The Arkhamverse’s Joker Died In Batman: Arkham City

The first character to be brought in from an Elseworld is Joker. Joker appeared in every previous Batman: Arkham game, but died in the series’ second entry Batman: Arkham City. Joker’s blood was poisoned after he injected himself with a modified version of Bane’s Venom serum called Titan in the finale of Batman: Arkham Asylum, turning him into a giant hulking monster.

While Batman seemingly defeated him and returned him to normal after a boss battle, this proved not to be the case as the side effects slowly killed Joker throughout Batman: Arkham City. While Batman found a cure for his nemesis, Joker’s desperation was his hubris, and when he attacked Batman, he inadvertently led to the cure’s destruction.

The new, younger version of Joker who appears in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is different from the Arkhamverse’s, and comes from a universe where he is part of the Suicide Squad. This immediately makes him more cooperative to fit him into the game’s team-based dynamic, although the Suicide Squad Insider episode that revealed the game’s take on the character - which can be found on Warner Bros. Games’ YouTube channel - hinted that he may be “unhinged in a different way”.

              Suicide Squad: Kill The Justice League - Every Confirmed Character   Suicide Squad: KTJL is set to have an impressive roster of DC characters, with several characters also returning from Rocksteady's Arkhamverse.

Joker And Harley Quinn’s Relationship Is Different In The Arkhamverse Harley Didn’t Choose To Leave Joker Like She Did In Other Media

One of the main characters in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League is Harley Quinn, the Joker’s once psychiatrist-turned-partner-in-crime. Across nearly every form of DC media, the relationship between Harley Quinn and Joker is deeply disturbing and toxic at its core, with Joker emotionally manipulating Harley into falling in love with him during therapy sessions - something which often doesn’t seem to be fully reciprocated due to Joker’s obsession with Batman - before inflicting other forms of physical and emotional abuse over the years.

In other DC media, such as the comics, the Harley Quinn TV show, and the DCEU movies, Harley grows to understand that Joker is abusive, their relationship is toxic, and she chooses to leave him. This has resulted in more empowering storytelling surrounding the character that has mirrored many real-world stories of women escaping domestic abuse. These stories are also often cited as one of the reasons the character has become more relatable for many, despite her criminal exploits.

As Joker died in Batman: Arkham City, Harley didn’t seem to have this arc in the Arkhamverse, meaning her relationship with Joker is fundamentally different from how it is in other media. Batman: Arkham City’s Harley Quinn’s Revenge DLC and Batman: Arkham Knight showed a distraught Harley grieving Joker and seeking revenge on Batman, but she appears to have moved on somewhat by the time we see her in Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League.

What Could An Elseworld Joker’s Arrival Mean For Harley Quinn? And Could It Undo Years Of Development For The Character In Other Media?

The arrival of a new Joker, however, could change this and see Harley reverting to her old ways. Although this version of Joker isn’t the same one Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s Harley fell in love with, seeing a Joker variant could give her some hope she rekindle her lost love once again. If the Elseworld Joker has a Harley Quinn in his own universe, he may try to manipulate the Arkhamverse’s one for his own personal gain within Task Force X.

The addition of this Joker seeming more cooperative and potentially more charming on the surface could further suggest to Harley that things won’t be like they were before, and may even be better. However, Rocksteady’s warning about Joker being “unhinged in a different way” should be a large enough indicator that it won’t, and may even be worse for Harley than her relationship with the Arkhamverse’s Joker.

This kind of arc would be a betrayal of Harley Quinn’s character progression that fans have seen over the years, and it would be deeply disappointing for Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League to include it. Harley has grown to become stronger and more independent as she’s carved out her own identity detached from Joker in other media, and the game’s design seems to suggest the Arkhamverse’s version has finally done the same in the years following Arkham Knight.

The best outcome would be for Harley to see the Elseworld version of Joker’s behavior for what it is after years of being separated from her own, letting her finally come to terms with how toxic her relationship with Joker was. As a result, this would put the decision not to get romantically involved with or manipulated by Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League’s new Joker squarely upon Harley and would bring the character closer to her counterparts in other media and mirror their decision to leave Joker.
